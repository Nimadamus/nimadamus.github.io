6-Game Thursday Slate

NBA Previews Today

Thursday's six-game slate features a Prime Video doubleheader headlined by two of the most compelling matchups of the season. Anthony Edwards and the 46-29 Timberwolves visit Cade Cunningham's league-leading 55-21 Detroit Pistons, followed by Luka Doncic's surging 50-26 Lakers traveling to Oklahoma City to face Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and the 60-16 defending champion Thunder. Victor Wembanyama's 57-18 Spurs close out the night at the Clippers, the Cavaliers are massive 10.5-point road favorites at a depleted Golden State squad, Charlotte hosts Phoenix, and Portland's play-in hopes hang in the balance against New Orleans.

Game 1

Suns @ Hornets

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Spectrum Center, Charlotte, NC
Spread
CHA -5.5
Moneyline
PHX +170 / CHA -205
Total
O/U 220.5

Charlotte being a 5.5-point home favorite here is a testament to how far this franchise has come in a single season. The Hornets at 38-34 are in the thick of the Eastern Conference playoff race, sitting just 2.5 games behind Toronto for the fifth seed and making a legitimate push to end the franchise's playoff drought that stretches back to 2016. LaMelo Ball has been sensational this season, averaging 19.7 points and 7.1 assists per game while shooting 49.6% from the field, and his impact goes way beyond the box score. Charlotte generates 13.6 more points per 100 possessions when Ball is on the floor, a number that puts him in the company of the league's absolute elite in terms of on-court impact.

Phoenix has navigated a transitional season after shipping their biggest star to Houston and reaching a buyout with Bradley Beal over the summer. The Suns rebuilt around Devin Booker and younger pieces, and while they've been competitive enough to hold the 7th seed in the West, this is a team that's still finding its identity without the star power that defined the last few years. Coming into Charlotte on the road, Phoenix faces a Hornets team that has been one of the league's best stories since the turn of the calendar. Charlotte is 22-11 in their last 33 games with the NBA's most efficient offense during that stretch, and their starting lineup has been the most potent unit in basketball over that span.

The 220.5 total feels relatively low for a game featuring Ball's pace-pushing Hornets, but it reflects Phoenix's more deliberate offensive approach and Charlotte's quietly improved defensive numbers. The Hornets rank sixth in defensive rating over their recent surge, which is a dramatic departure from the porous defenses that defined this franchise for years. Ball's ability to score at all three levels, create for teammates off the bounce, and control the tempo gives Charlotte a clear offensive advantage in this matchup, and the home crowd at Spectrum Center has been electric this season as the fanbase senses something special is building.

Phoenix will compete because Devin Booker is still one of the most dangerous offensive players in the league, and the Suns have the kind of veteran savvy that keeps games from getting out of hand. But the Hornets' combination of offensive efficiency, defensive improvement, and home-court energy should be too much for a Suns team that's been inconsistent on the road all season. Charlotte has earned this spread, and the way Ball has elevated his game in the second half of the season suggests the Hornets are going to be a handful for whichever team draws them in the postseason.

Timberwolves @ Pistons

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Spread
DET -2.5
Moneyline
MIN +114 / DET -134
Total
O/U 224.5

This is the game of the early window, and it might be the most fascinating matchup on the entire Thursday board. Detroit at 55-21 owns the best record in the Eastern Conference and has already clinched its first 50-win season since 2007-08, a milestone that seemed like a pipe dream just two years ago when this franchise was the laughingstock of the NBA. Cade Cunningham has been the engine behind everything, averaging 25.7 points, 9.7 assists, and 5.7 rebounds per game before his recent lung injury sidelined him. His absence is the elephant in the room here, because while the Pistons have built enough depth and culture to win without their star, they lose a dimension of playmaking that's nearly impossible to replicate. Cunningham was in serious MVP consideration before the pneumothorax diagnosis, and Detroit needs him back for the postseason.

Minnesota at 46-29 is locked into the Western Conference's top tier and has legitimate championship aspirations with Anthony Edwards leading the charge. Edwards is back in the lineup after dealing with patellofemoral pain syndrome in his right knee, and his return to the floor in Monday's blowout over Dallas was a welcome sight for Wolves fans. He posted 17 points in 23 minutes, clearly still easing back into game shape, but Edwards at 29.5 points per game on 49.2% shooting and 40.2% from three is one of the most terrifying offensive weapons in basketball when he's fully healthy. The Timberwolves are fighting to stay in the top five out West and avoid the play-in tournament, and every game down the stretch carries massive seeding implications with a potential first-round matchup against the Nuggets looming.

The Pistons being only 2.5-point favorites despite having the best record in the East speaks to the uncertainty around Cunningham's availability and the respect that Minnesota commands as a road opponent. The Timberwolves have the kind of defensive length and versatility that can smother opposing offenses, and their ability to switch everything on the perimeter makes life difficult for teams that rely on pick-and-roll creation. Detroit's offense has been elite all season with Cunningham orchestrating, but without him, the Pistons need other players to step into creative roles they haven't been asked to fill consistently. That's a real concern against a Minnesota defense this talented.

The 224.5 total is moderate and reflects the defensive capabilities of both teams. Minnesota's defense has been one of the best in basketball this season, and Detroit's defensive identity under head coach has been a revelation compared to the Pistons teams of recent years. This should be a physical, competitive, playoff-atmosphere game in Little Caesars Arena, and the Prime Video cameras picked the right matchup for the early window. Edwards getting extended minutes as he ramps back up could be the difference in a game that projects to come down to late-game execution, and the Wolves' star has shown a flair for the dramatic that makes him appointment television in moments like these.

Lakers @ Thunder

Thursday, 9:30 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK
Spread
OKC -8.5
Moneyline
LAL +240 / OKC -295
Total
O/U 228.5

The headline matchup of Thursday night, and the one that basketball fans across the country will be locked into. Oklahoma City at 60-16 became the first team to hit 60 wins this season, and it marks the first time in franchise history they've recorded back-to-back 60-win campaigns. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has all but wrapped up his second consecutive MVP award, and the numbers are staggering: roughly 32 points per game with elite efficiency, highlighted by a 40-point, 11-assist masterpiece against Milwaukee on 16-of-19 shooting and a clutch 35-point, 15-assist performance that sank the Nuggets in March. The Thunder are 15-1 in their last 16 games and playing the kind of basketball that makes defending a championship look effortless.

Los Angeles at 50-26 has been one of the season's best stories, and the Luka Doncic acquisition has turned the Lakers into a legitimate Western Conference powerhouse. Doncic is averaging a ridiculous 33.7 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 8.2 assists per game, including a season-high 60-point explosion in Miami during the Lakers' current hot streak. The Lakers have won 13 of their last 14 games and clinched the Pacific Division title, and the combination of Doncic and LeBron James, who just broke the all-time career wins record with his 1,229th on Tuesday night, gives LA a one-two punch that can hang with anyone in the league. James scored 14 points in that historic win over the Cavaliers, a reminder that even in Year 23, he remains a factor in meaningful basketball games.

The 8.5-point spread is substantial for a game between two teams this talented, but it reflects Oklahoma City's dominance at home and the sheer gap in their overall body of work this season. The Thunder's defense has been suffocating all year, and their ability to switch everything, contest shots at the rim, and force turnovers creates problems even for elite offensive players like Doncic. Luka will get his points because he always does, but the question is whether the supporting cast can produce enough against OKC's swarming defensive schemes to keep this game competitive into the fourth quarter. The Thunder's depth advantage is real, and their bench units have outscored opponents consistently all season.

The 228.5 total is the highest on the board and reflects the offensive firepower on both sides. When Doncic and SGA share the floor in the same game, points are going to be scored in bunches, and both teams play at a pace that generates possessions and creates opportunities in transition. This has the feel of a potential Western Conference playoff preview, and while the Thunder should control the game at home, don't be surprised if Doncic has one of those nights where he puts 40 on the board and single-handedly keeps the Lakers within striking distance. Prime Video got the marquee matchup right with this one, and the Paycom Center is going to be electric for what projects to be one of the most entertaining games of the regular season's final week.

Game 4

Cavaliers @ Warriors

Thursday, 10:00 PM ET | Chase Center, San Francisco, CA
Spread
CLE -10.5
Moneyline
CLE -460 / GSW +360
Total
O/U 226.5

Cleveland at 47-29 rolls into Chase Center as a commanding 10.5-point road favorite, a spread that would have been unthinkable in this building during the Warriors' dynasty years but feels entirely appropriate given the current state of both rosters. Donovan Mitchell has been phenomenal this season, averaging 28.0 points, 5.9 assists, and 4.5 rebounds on 48% shooting through 61 games, and he's coming off a 42-point eruption against Orlando and a 34-point showing in a victory over Utah. The Cavaliers also made a significant trade-deadline addition in James Harden, and the question hanging over Cleveland is whether that move provides the missing piece for a deep playoff run. Mitchell has made the playoffs nine times in his career without getting past the second round, and this season feels like his best chance to break through.

Golden State at 36-39 is enduring the painful end of an era. Stephen Curry has missed 25 games this season dealing with a knee injury, and his absence has exposed the harsh reality that the Warriors' entire competitive existence revolves around the greatest shooter in basketball history. Without Curry, and with Jimmy Butler done for the season with a torn ACL, Kristaps Porzingis sidelined, and several other rotation pieces unavailable, the Warriors are running out a roster that lacks the talent, depth, and star power to compete with legitimate playoff teams. Golden State is 23-16 when Curry plays, which is respectable, but the games without him have been a disaster that's dragged the overall record below .500.

The 10.5-point spread is enormous for a road team, but Cleveland's motivation here is clear. The Cavaliers are locked into the fourth seed in the East and are trying to build momentum heading into the postseason, and Mitchell's history of postseason disappointment has lit a fire under this team to enter the playoffs playing their best basketball. Golden State's defense without Curry's offensive gravity is disjointed and easy to scheme against, and Cleveland's combination of Mitchell's scoring, Harden's playmaking, and a deep supporting cast should generate quality looks on virtually every possession. The Cavaliers are one of those teams that can beat you in multiple ways, and the Warriors simply don't have the bodies to match up.

The 226.5 total is interesting because Chase Center games this season have been unpredictable in terms of pace and scoring. Cleveland prefers to play at a controlled pace and execute in the halfcourt, which could keep the score from getting out of hand offensively. But the Warriors' defensive breakdowns tend to give up easy transition baskets and open threes, which inflates the score regardless of the intended tempo. This should be a game where Cleveland builds a comfortable lead, gives Mitchell and Harden a rest in the fourth quarter, and the only drama is whether Golden State's young players can make enough shots to push the final margin under that 10.5-point number.

Pelicans @ Trail Blazers

Thursday, 10:00 PM ET | Moda Center, Portland, OR
Spread
POR -6.5
Moneyline
NOP +198 / POR -240
Total
O/U 231.5

Portland at 39-38 is in survival mode with the play-in tournament picture still very much undecided. The Trail Blazers sit in the 9th spot in the Western Conference, and every game from here until the end of the regular season carries massive implications for a franchise that desperately needs postseason basketball. Damian Lillard's return to Portland on a three-year deal last summer was supposed to be the catalyst for a competitive resurgence, and the Blazers have delivered a season that's been fun, dramatic, and frustratingly inconsistent in equal measure. The interim coaching situation with Tiago Splitter replacing Chauncey Billups has added another layer of turbulence to a season that's been defined by its unpredictability.

New Orleans at 25-51 has been one of the most disappointing teams in basketball this season, and the Pelicans' record is a stark reflection of what happens when injuries devastate a roster beyond recognition. What was supposed to be a competitive year in the Western Conference has instead turned into a lost season, and New Orleans is playing out the string with nothing but draft positioning and player development on the agenda. The Pelicans will compete because professional basketball players always do, but the talent gap between these two rosters is significant, and Portland has far more to play for on a Thursday night in the Moda Center.

The 6.5-point spread is substantial but reasonable given the context. Portland needs this game badly, and the Moda Center crowd will bring the energy that comes with a team fighting for its playoff life. The Trail Blazers' offense has been productive all season, and their ability to score in bunches through Lillard and the supporting cast should allow them to control the game against a Pelicans defense that has been one of the league's worst. The 231.5 total is the joint-highest on Thursday's board and reflects the expectation that both teams will push the pace and generate scoring opportunities, though for very different reasons.

This feels like a game Portland should win comfortably, but the Trail Blazers have had a habit of playing down to their competition this season, and a 25-win Pelicans team on the road isn't exactly the opponent that brings out a team's best effort. Portland's defensive inconsistency could keep New Orleans in the game longer than the spread suggests, and the Pelicans' young players have nothing to lose and everything to prove with audition games for next season's roster spots. But ultimately, the stakes matter. Portland's play-in hopes are on the line, and teams fighting for their postseason lives tend to find an extra gear that lottery-bound opponents simply can't match. The Blazers should take care of business at home and keep their playoff dreams alive for at least one more night.

Game 6

Spurs @ Clippers

Thursday, 10:30 PM ET | Intuit Dome, Inglewood, CA
Spread
SA -4
Moneyline
SA -172 / LAC +144
Total
O/U 231.5

The nightcap features Victor Wembanyama and the 57-18 Spurs, who are in the midst of one of the most historically dominant regular seasons in recent NBA memory. San Antonio sits just three games behind Oklahoma City for the top seed in the West with seven games remaining, and the Spurs' 9-game winning streak heading into the week has them playing their best basketball at exactly the right time. Wembanyama has been everything and more this season, averaging 24.5 points, 11.4 rebounds, 3.1 assists, and 3.1 blocks per game while shooting 50.4% from the field and 35.1% from three. The 7-foot-4 alien continues to do things on a basketball court that the sport has never seen from a single player, and his pairing with De'Aaron Fox has given San Antonio a dynamic backcourt partner who averaged 19.4 points and 6.3 assists since arriving from Sacramento.

The Clippers at 39-37 have authored one of the most remarkable turnaround stories of the season. After a historically bad 6-21 start that included a 2-13 November, the franchise's worst month ever, the Clippers caught fire and won 16 of 19 games to climb back into the playoff picture. They became the first team in NBA history to reach .500 after being 15 games below it, a feat that speaks to the resilience of the roster and the coaching staff's ability to find solutions when the season looked lost. Bradley Beal signed with the Clippers after his buyout from Phoenix, and his addition has provided offensive stability and veteran leadership that the team desperately needed during those early-season struggles.

San Antonio being a 4-point road favorite at the Intuit Dome reflects the enormous gap between the Spurs' overall body of work and the Clippers' inconsistent season. The Spurs have the kind of two-way dominance that championship teams are built on, with Wembanyama's defensive presence turning the paint into a no-fly zone and Fox's speed and playmaking providing the offensive engine that keeps defenses on their heels. The Clippers have been competitive since their turnaround, but facing a team as complete as San Antonio is a different challenge than the opponents they've been beating during their surge. Wemby doesn't need to score 30 to dominate, his shot-blocking, rim protection, and ability to switch onto guards make him the most impactful defender in basketball.

The 231.5 total projects a relatively high-scoring affair, which makes sense given both teams' offensive capabilities. San Antonio can score through Wembanyama in the post, Fox in the pick-and-roll, and shooters spotting up around the floor, while the Clippers have enough offensive weapons to keep the game interesting even against the Spurs' elite defense. But the difference in this game will be on the defensive end, where San Antonio's length, discipline, and rim protection should create problems that the Clippers haven't consistently solved against elite competition. The Spurs should extend their winning streak and continue their push for home-court advantage throughout the Western Conference playoffs, while the Clippers will try to use this as a measuring stick game to prove their turnaround is sustainable against the league's very best.
