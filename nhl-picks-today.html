This is a game between two teams heading in drastically different directions. The Winnipeg Jets (28-29-11, 67 points) have had a deeply disappointing season after entering the year with legitimate contender expectations. At 67 points through 68 games, Winnipeg's playoff hopes are virtually dead, and the energy inside that locker room has to be deflating. Pittsburgh being a -162 home favorite feels right for a Penguins team that has been excellent at PPG Paints Arena all season, posting a 16-9 home record that reflects a roster that knows how to protect its building.

Pittsburgh's resurgence this season has been built on a balanced attack and a defense that has tightened up considerably. Sidney Crosby continues to defy Father Time, producing at an elite level and dragging this team through stretches where the depth hasn't been there. The supporting cast has responded, and the Penguins are a team that plays with structure and purpose on home ice. Their 16 overtime/shootout losses tell you they've been in a ton of close games, but the fact that they've accumulated 84 points despite those extra-time defeats speaks to how many regulation wins they've racked up. Winnipeg, by contrast, has been maddeningly inconsistent on the road, posting a 17-13 away mark that's been propped up by a few hot streaks bookended by extended slumps.

The 6.5 total is an interesting number here. Pittsburgh has shown the ability to score in bunches at home, and a Jets team with nothing to play for could either mail it in or play loose and carefree. If Winnipeg comes out swinging without any fear of consequences, this game could get track-meet-ish in a hurry. But if the Penguins control the pace and lean on their home structure, they could suffocate a Jets team that struggles to generate offense when they can't push the tempo. The puck line at PIT -1.5 (+160) is worth considering if you believe the Penguins have another dominant home performance in them, because Winnipeg hasn't shown the ability to stay competitive in games where they fall behind early on the road.

Minnesota isn't far behind at 90 points, and the Wild have been outstanding at Xcel Energy Center all year.
