7-Game Friday Slate

NBA March 6, 2026

Friday's seven-game slate is headlined by a near pick'em showdown in Denver where the 40-23 Knicks visit Nikola Jokic and the 39-24 Nuggets. The dominant 45-17 Spurs, featuring Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox, host the Clippers. Luka Doncic and the 37-25 Lakers welcome the rebuilding 15-47 Pacers. Kevin Durant and the 38-23 Rockets host Portland. The post-Luka Mavericks (21-41) travel to Boston to face the 41-21 Celtics. Miami visits Charlotte, and New Orleans heads to Phoenix.

Game 1
Marquee

Knicks @ Nuggets

Friday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Spread
NYK -1.5
Moneyline
NYK -120 / DEN EVEN
Total
O/U 230.5

This is the game of the night, and the spread tells you everything about how the market views it. The New York Knicks at 40-23 are actually slight road favorites in Denver, a place where visiting teams historically struggle with the altitude and the hostile crowd. The fact that New York is laying 1.5 points at Ball Arena tells you that the Knicks have earned a level of respect this season that transcends home-court advantage. This is a team that has been among the most consistent in the Eastern Conference, and they've done it with a grinding, physical style that travels well to hostile environments.

Denver at 39-24 still has Nikola Jokic, which means they're always dangerous. Jokic's ability to control every facet of the game from the center position makes the Nuggets a threat regardless of matchup or circumstance. He'll get his numbers, he'll find open teammates, and he'll make the Knicks work for every possession on both ends. But Denver's supporting cast has been inconsistent this season, and the Nuggets have dropped games at home that they shouldn't have. At 39-24, they're still a playoff team, but they're not the dominant home force they were during their championship run.

The 230.5 total suggests the market expects a competitive, mid-paced game with both teams scoring in bunches. New York has the defensive personnel to slow down Jokic's supporting cast, but containing the big man himself is a challenge that no team in the NBA has consistently solved. The Knicks' physical style on the perimeter could disrupt Denver's shooters, but Jokic's passing will find the openings regardless. This feels like a game that will be decided in the final five minutes, and the team that executes its half-court offense better down the stretch will walk away with the win.

The moneyline at NYK -120 / DEN EVEN is the most compelling number on the board tonight. Denver at even money at home against a team that's only one game ahead in the standings represents genuine value if you believe the altitude and Jokic's brilliance are enough to overcome New York's road swagger. On the flip side, New York at -120 is a modest price for a team that has shown it can win anywhere, against anyone, with the kind of blue-collar toughness that doesn't wilt in hostile environments. This one comes down to execution, and both teams have the talent and coaching to deliver a game worth staying up for.

Game 2
Marquee

Clippers @ Spurs

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX
Spread
SAS -7.5
Moneyline
SAS -310 / LAC +250
Total
O/U 224.5

The Spurs are the best story in the NBA this season, and Friday night in San Antonio should be another demonstration of why. At 45-17, San Antonio has the best record in the entire league, and the combination of Victor Wembanyama's generational two-way dominance and De'Aaron Fox's explosive scoring and playmaking has transformed this franchise into a legitimate championship contender. Fox, acquired from Sacramento in February 2025, has been the catalyst for everything the Spurs have become. His speed, his court vision, and his ability to attack downhill give San Antonio a dynamic backcourt presence that unlocks Wembanyama's skill set in ways that no previous point guard could.

The Clippers at 30-31 are a .500 team with .500 problems, and walking into Frost Bank Center against the best team in basketball is not the assignment they want on a Friday night. LA has been competitive this season but inconsistent, unable to string together the kind of runs that separate playoff contenders from also-rans. Against a Spurs team that's 7.5-point home favorites with a -310 moneyline, the Clippers are massive underdogs for a reason. San Antonio's defense, anchored by Wembanyama's rim protection and perimeter versatility, is elite, and their offense flows through Fox and Wembanyama with a precision that makes them nearly impossible to game-plan against.

The 224.5 total reflects the Spurs' defensive identity. San Antonio doesn't just beat teams. They suffocate them. Wembanyama's ability to alter shots at the rim, combined with the team's commitment to switching and contesting perimeter looks, makes scoring against the Spurs an exhausting exercise. The Clippers will need to be incredibly efficient offensively to stay competitive, and against a defense this good, efficiency is hard to come by. San Antonio's home record has been outstanding this season, and the Frost Bank Center crowd feeds energy into a team that plays with the kind of joy and purpose that championship-caliber teams exude.

The SAS -7.5 spread is significant, but the Spurs have been covering big numbers at home all season. When you have the best player in basketball in Wembanyama and the most explosive point guard in the league in Fox, 7.5 points is a manageable margin. The Clippers have the veteran savvy to keep games competitive, but talent gaps this wide tend to manifest in third and fourth quarters when the Spurs' defense tightens the screws and their offense executes with precision. This feels like a game where San Antonio pulls away late and covers comfortably.

Game 3

Mavericks @ Celtics

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Spread
BOS -15.5
Moneyline
BOS -1000 / DAL +650
Total
O/U 224.5

BOS -15.5 is the biggest spread on the NBA board tonight, and one look at the records explains why. Dallas at 21-41 has been one of the most disappointing teams in basketball since trading Luka Doncic to the Lakers in February 2025. The Mavericks are in full rebuild mode, and the results have reflected the talent void left by Doncic's departure. They're playing out the string, developing young players, and positioning themselves for the draft lottery. Boston, despite losing Jayson Tatum to an Achilles injury earlier this season, has remained a 41-21 force in the Eastern Conference, relying on their depth, defensive versatility, and the collective talent of a roster that was built for sustained excellence.

The Celtics have navigated the Tatum absence better than anyone expected, and their 41-21 record speaks to the organizational depth that Boston has built. Jaylen Brown has stepped into the primary scoring role, and the supporting cast has elevated their play to compensate for the loss of a former MVP candidate. TD Garden is one of the toughest environments in the NBA for visiting teams, and Dallas doesn't have the firepower to stay competitive in that building against a team this motivated. The Celtics are fighting for seeding in a loaded Eastern Conference, and every home game is an opportunity to build momentum heading into the playoffs.

At 15.5 points, the question isn't whether Boston wins. It's whether they win by enough to cover a historically large spread. Dallas has shown flashes of competitiveness this season, and young players sometimes play with the kind of fearlessness that keeps games closer than the talent gap suggests. But the Mavericks have been losing games by double digits regularly, and Boston at home against a bottom-five team is a recipe for a lopsided outcome. The 224.5 total is moderate, reflecting Boston's defensive strength and Dallas's inability to consistently score against quality opponents. This is a blowout waiting to happen.

Game 4

Pacers @ Lakers

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Spread
LAL -10.5
Moneyline
LAL -485 / IND +370
Total
O/U 235.5

Indiana at 15-47 is having the kind of season that makes you wince, and the Achilles injury to Tyrese Haliburton has been the defining blow. Without their franchise point guard and the emotional leader of the team, the Pacers have cratered into one of the worst records in the league. They're not competitive on most nights, and the talent gap between Indiana and the league's contenders has become a chasm. Heading into Crypto.com Arena against the Lakers is the kind of Friday night assignment that results in blowouts, and the LAL -10.5 spread reflects the market's expectation that this game will be over early.

The Lakers at 37-25 have been one of the best stories in the Western Conference, and Luka Doncic has been the primary reason. Since arriving from Dallas, Doncic has transformed the Lakers into a legitimate contender with his ability to control games from the point guard position. Paired with LeBron James in what may be the most fascinating backcourt experiment in NBA history, the Lakers have the offensive ceiling to blow out anyone on a given night. Against a Pacers team that doesn't have the personnel to defend Luka's step-back three, his floater game, or his passing vision, the Lakers should be able to name their score.

The 235.5 total is the highest on the board tonight, and it makes sense when you consider that Indiana doesn't play defense and the Lakers have one of the most prolific offenses in basketball. Luka's ability to generate points for himself and others means the Lakers' offense should hum, and Indiana's inability to get stops means the Pacers will need to try to run with LA in a shootout they can't sustain. This has all the makings of a comfortable Lakers victory where Luka and LeBron might not even need to play the fourth quarter.

Game 5

Trail Blazers @ Rockets

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -6.5
Moneyline
HOU -238 / POR +195
Total
O/U 220.5

Houston at 38-23 has been one of the biggest power shifts in the NBA this season, and the blockbuster acquisition of KD was the move that elevated this franchise from promising young team to genuine contender. Durant gives the Rockets a proven championship-level scorer who can operate from anywhere on the floor, and his presence has accelerated the development of Houston's young core by giving them a veteran superstar to play alongside. The Rockets have been rolling at home, and Toyota Center has become one of the tougher arenas to play in across the Western Conference.

Portland at 30-33 is a team stuck in the middle of the Western Conference, not bad enough to tank and not good enough to seriously contend. The Trail Blazers have competitive pieces and play hard most nights, but they lack the top-end talent to compete with the league's best teams. Walking into Houston and facing Kevin Durant, along with the Rockets' talented young roster, is a significant challenge. Portland will need to be at their absolute best to keep this within the 6.5-point spread, and even then, Houston's home-court advantage and offensive firepower make them prohibitive favorites.

The 220.5 total is the lowest on the board tonight, and it reflects both teams' defensive capabilities. Houston has been solid defensively under their coaching staff, and their length and athleticism make them a tough matchup for teams that rely on perimeter shooting. Portland's defense has been league-average at best, but their pace tends to slow games down enough that totals don't get out of hand. This feels like a game where Houston's superior talent manifests in the second half, pulling away from a competitive Portland team that simply doesn't have the star power to match Durant and the Rockets down the stretch.

Spread

Moneyline

Total
O/U 229.5

Charlotte being a 7.5-point home favorite over Miami is one of the more surprising numbers on Friday's board, especially when you consider that the Heat have the better record at 34-29 compared to Charlotte's 32-31. The market is telling us something here, and it likely comes down to Miami's current form, health situation, and the reality that the post-Butler Heat have been a different team in recent weeks. His departure stripped Miami of their emotional leader and best closer, and while the Heat have found ways to win, their identity has shifted in ways that make them vulnerable against teams with home-court advantage and motivation.

Charlotte at 32-31 is right on the playoff bubble, and every home game in March takes on outsized importance. The Hornets have been building something legitimate this season, and their young core has shown enough growth to suggest this isn't a flash-in-the-pan season. Playing at Spectrum Center with playoff positioning on the line, Charlotte will bring a level of intensity and purpose that could overwhelm a Miami team that's been inconsistent on the road. The 7.5-point spread is hefty, but if Charlotte's young players are locked in, the Hornets have the offensive talent to blow games open.

The 229.5 total suggests the market expects scoring from both sides, and these two teams play at a pace that generates offense. Neither defense is elite, which opens the door for a high-scoring affair where the home crowd gives Charlotte an emotional edge. Miami has the veteran know-how to keep games close, but without Butler, their fourth-quarter execution has been unreliable. Charlotte's growth this season has been one of the more underappreciated stories in the Eastern Conference, and Friday night at home is their chance to prove they belong in the playoff picture.

Game 7

Pelicans @ Suns

Friday, 9:00 PM ET | Footprint Center, Phoenix, AZ
Spread
PHX -6.5
Moneyline
PHX -230 / NOP +190
Total
O/U 225.5

New Orleans at 20-44 is in full tank mode, and the departure of Brandon Ingram to Toronto has left the Pelicans without their most consistent offensive weapon. This season has been a disaster from start to finish for New Orleans, and the Pelicans are now focused entirely on player development and draft lottery positioning. There's no shame in rebuilding, but walking into Footprint Center against a motivated Phoenix team is not a recipe for competitive basketball. The Suns at 35-27 are fighting for playoff seeding in a loaded Western Conference, and they can't afford to take a game off against anyone, even a bottom-five opponent.

Phoenix's season has been fascinating since the Kevin Durant trade to Houston. The Suns have had to reinvent themselves without their most dangerous scorer, and the results have been mixed but ultimately successful enough to keep them in the playoff picture at 35-27. Devin Booker has shouldered a larger offensive burden, and the supporting cast has stepped up in different ways. At home against a depleted Pelicans squad, Phoenix should be able to control this game from the opening tip. The 6.5-point spread reflects the talent gap, and the Suns' home-court advantage should make this a comfortable victory.

The 225.5 total is moderate, reflecting New Orleans's inability to score consistently without their best players and Phoenix's defensive improvements since the Durant trade. The Suns have become more balanced on both ends, and their defensive effort at home has been notably better than their road defense this season. New Orleans will struggle to generate offense against a Phoenix team that can switch and contest, and the Pelicans' young players, while talented, don't have the experience to exploit the Suns' defensive weaknesses consistently. This is a game that Phoenix should win by double digits, and the 6.5-point spread gives them plenty of room.
