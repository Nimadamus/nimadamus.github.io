6-Game Monday Slate

NHL March 2, 2026

Monday delivers a compact but compelling 6-game NHL slate. Columbus is the rare road favorite at the struggling Rangers on NHL Network. Colorado at 39-10-9 carries the best record in hockey into Los Angeles. Carolina's dominant 38-15-6 squad heads to Seattle. Dallas brings the league's second-best power play at 30.06% to Vancouver. Detroit visits Nashville for an afternoon matinee on NHL Network. Philadelphia and Toronto meet in a cross-conference clash of eerily similar records.

Red Wings @ Predators

Monday, 2:00 PM ET | Bridgestone Arena, Nashville, TN
Puck Line
DET -1.5 (+230)
Moneyline
DET +114 / NSH -110
Total
O/U 6.5

This afternoon matinee on NHL Network kicks off the Monday slate with a fascinating Central Division-adjacent matchup, and the moneyline tells you a story that the records alone might not. Detroit at 34-20 has been one of the pleasant surprises of the 2025-26 season, and yet they're listed as slight underdogs at +114 here against a Nashville team that sits at 27-24 and has been wildly inconsistent for much of the campaign. The Predators' home-ice advantage at Bridgestone Arena is the likely explanation for the market's lean, because Nashville has traditionally been a nightmare venue for visiting teams, and the crowd for an afternoon start should still be loud and engaged.

Detroit's record speaks for itself. At 34-20, the Red Wings have established themselves as legitimate contenders in the Eastern Conference, and their balanced approach on both sides of the puck has been the key to their success. This is a team that doesn't rely on one player or one unit to carry the load. They generate offense through depth scoring, they defend with structure and discipline, and they've found ways to win in all types of games, whether it's a high-scoring affair or a tight-checking grind. That kind of versatility is what separates contenders from pretenders in the NHL, and Detroit has earned its place among the former.

Nashville at 27-24 is a team stuck in the muddled middle of the Western Conference, and the Predators have spent most of this season trying to figure out exactly what they are. They're too talented to tank, too inconsistent to be considered a serious threat, and their underlying numbers have fluctuated wildly from month to month. The home record at Bridgestone has been one of the few constants, and the crowd energy in Nashville for afternoon games is typically strong, which helps explain why the books are making the Predators a slight favorite despite Detroit's significantly better overall record.

The 6.5 total is right on the standard number for most NHL games, and it suggests the market expects a game that could go either way in terms of pace and scoring. Detroit has been solid defensively this season, but Nashville's offensive talent, when clicking, can create problems for any team. The puck line at DET -1.5 (+230) offers a significant payout if you believe the Red Wings' superior record will translate to a comfortable road win, but the tight moneyline reflects the reality that afternoon games in Nashville are never easy, regardless of how good the visiting team has been playing.

Game 2
NHL Network

Blue Jackets @ Rangers

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Puck Line
CBJ -1.5
Moneyline
CBJ -130 / NYR +109
Total
O/U 6.5

If you told anyone before the season started that Columbus would be a road favorite at Madison Square Garden in March, they would've laughed you out of the building. And yet, here we are. The Blue Jackets at -130 on the moneyline are the favored team walking into the most famous arena in hockey, and the Rangers, a franchise that entered this season with serious championship aspirations, find themselves as home underdogs against a team that was supposed to be rebuilding. That's how bad things have gotten on Broadway, and the NHL Network cameras will be rolling for what has become a genuinely compelling storyline.

Columbus has been one of the great surprise stories of the 2025-26 season. This was a team that was widely expected to be in the lottery conversation, and instead they've turned into a legitimate playoff contender through a combination of improved goaltending, an aggressive system that creates turnovers in the neutral zone, and a young core that has developed faster than anyone anticipated. The Blue Jackets play with an energy and confidence that you rarely see from a team this young, and their road record this season has been particularly impressive, which explains why the books trust them to walk into MSG and come out with a win.

New York's struggles this season have been well-documented and painful to watch for Rangers fans. This was supposed to be a team built for a Stanley Cup run, and instead they've been dealing with inconsistency at nearly every position. The goaltending hasn't been as sharp as it needs to be, the defensive structure has broken down at inopportune moments, and the offense has gone through extended stretches where it simply can't generate enough quality chances to keep up. When a team with this much talent underperforms this significantly, the issues are usually systemic rather than individual, and that's a problem that doesn't get fixed overnight.

The 6.5 total is standard, but the real story here is the moneyline. CBJ -130 means the market gives Columbus roughly a 56% chance of winning this game at Madison Square Garden, which would have been unthinkable in the preseason. The Rangers at +109 offer value if you believe the talent gap between these rosters should eventually assert itself, but recent performance suggests that talent alone isn't winning games for New York right now. Columbus plays with a structured identity and a collective belief that has been missing from the Rangers' game for much of this season, and that intangible edge matters in a one-game situation like this.

Here's a cross-conference matchup that looks a whole lot different on paper than you'd expect. Philadelphia at 27-21-11 and Toronto at 27-24-9 have nearly identical records, and the Maple Leafs are only favored at -133 because of home-ice advantage at Scotiabank Arena. Strip away the venues and you're looking at two teams that have produced remarkably similar results this season, which makes the 7:30 PM start on ESPN+ a genuinely intriguing watch for anyone trying to find value on Monday night's board.

The Flyers have been one of the more interesting teams in the Eastern Conference this season. At 27-21-11, Philadelphia has accumulated 65 points, and while they haven't been dominant, they've been competitive in nearly every game they've played. The 11 overtime losses tell you this is a team that hangs around, fights until the final buzzer, and often comes out on the wrong end of close games. That kind of profile can be frustrating for fans but enticing for bettors, because it means the Flyers rarely get blown out and almost always keep games within a goal or two heading into the third period.

Toronto's season has been a study in frustration for a fanbase that expects so much more from this roster. At 27-24-9, the Maple Leafs have 63 points and sit in a precarious position in the Eastern Conference playoff picture. The talent is undeniable, but the consistency has been maddening. Toronto is the kind of team that can look like a Stanley Cup contender one night and a lottery team the next, and that unpredictability has been the defining characteristic of their entire campaign. Scotiabank Arena should be buzzing for this one, but the crowd energy can cut both ways for a team that's been under this much scrutiny all season.

The PHI +1.5 puck line offers some security given how close these teams are in the standings, and the 6.5 total suggests the market expects a game with a fair amount of scoring. Both teams have the offensive talent to put pucks in the net, and neither defense has been stingy enough this season to consistently shut down quality opponents. This is a genuine toss-up that could go either way, and the TOR -133 moneyline feels like the market is giving Toronto credit for home ice and not much else, because on paper, these are two teams that are virtually mirror images of each other in terms of overall production and results.

Stars @ Canucks

Puck Line
DAL -1.5 (-170)
Moneyline
DAL -191 / VAN +158
Total
O/U 5.5

This is a matchup between a legitimate Stanley Cup contender and a team that has been living through an absolute nightmare of a season. Dallas comes in as a heavy -191 favorite on the road, and the puck line at -1.5 (-170) tells you the market fully expects the Stars to win this game comfortably. When the books are laying -170 on a road team to win by two or more goals, that's about as confident as the line gets in the NHL, and Dallas has earned every bit of that respect with their performance this season.

The Stars' numbers are staggering. They're scoring 3.3 goals per game, which ranks 11th in the NHL, and their defensive structure has been absolutely elite, allowing just 2.7 goals against per game, the third-best mark in the entire league. But the stat that really jumps off the page is that 30.06% power play, which ranks second in the NHL. Think about that: nearly one out of every three times Dallas gets a man advantage, they score. That kind of power play efficiency is a cheat code in the NHL, and it means that Vancouver can't afford to take penalties in this game, because every trip to the box is essentially giving Dallas a free goal.

Vancouver's numbers paint the picture of a team in freefall. The Canucks are scoring just 2.5 goals per game, which ranks a dismal 30th in the league, and their 3.7 goals against per game is the worst mark in the entire NHL, dead last among all 32 teams. When you're being outscored by more than a goal per game on average, you're going to lose a lot of hockey games, and that's exactly what's happened to Vancouver this season. The Canucks have been porous defensively, their goaltending hasn't been able to bail them out consistently, and the offense simply doesn't generate enough to compensate for all the chances they give up on the other end.

The 5.5 total is lower than you might expect given Vancouver's defensive struggles, but it reflects the reality that Dallas is one of the best defensive teams in the league and tends to control the pace of play in their favor. The Stars don't need to engage in a track meet to win games. They're perfectly happy to slow things down, suffocate the opposition with their structure, and let their power play and opportunistic offense do the rest. Vancouver simply doesn't have the tools to compete with that kind of approach right now, and the DAL -1.5 (-170) puck line, while expensive, reflects a market that sees a significant talent and structure gap between these two teams.

Carolina at 38-15-6 owns the second-best record in the entire NHL, and the Hurricanes are heading into the stretch run as one of the favorites to represent the Eastern Conference in the Stanley Cup Final. This is a team that does everything well: they defend with ferocity, they transition the puck with speed and precision, they generate chances through their relentless forecheck, and they've got the goaltending to slam the door when they need to. Everything about this roster screams championship-caliber, and their consistency throughout the season has been the hallmark of a truly elite team.

Seattle at 28-22-9 has had a solid season by Kraken standards, but there's a significant gap between "solid" and what Carolina brings to the table every night. The Kraken have been competitive and have carved out a respectable record, but they haven't shown the kind of dominant stretches that suggest they can hang with the league's best teams over a seven-game series. Their home record at Climate Pledge Arena has been decent, and the Seattle crowd always brings energy, but the question is whether that energy can compensate for the talent and structural advantages that Carolina holds in virtually every facet of the game.

The CAR -188 moneyline makes Carolina a heavy road favorite, which is a testament to just how good the Hurricanes have been this season. Being priced that heavily on the road in the NHL is rare, and it speaks to the market's belief that Carolina is simply a different class of team compared to most of the league. The Hurricanes have been particularly dangerous in late-season situations where the games start to carry more weight, and their ability to ramp up their intensity when the stakes rise is what separates them from good teams that eventually hit a wall in the playoffs.

The 5.5 total reflects two things: Carolina's suffocating defensive system and the likelihood that this game will be played at a controlled pace dictated by the Hurricanes. Carolina doesn't need to win 5-4 shootouts. They're built to win 3-1 and 2-1 games where their defensive structure and goaltending ensure that the opposition simply can't generate enough quality chances to keep up. Seattle will have their moments, especially in front of their home crowd, but sustaining offensive pressure against a team this disciplined defensively is a tall order for any team in the NHL, let alone one that doesn't have the top-end talent to match up with Carolina's best players.

Avalanche @ Kings

Monday, 10:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Puck Line
LAK +1.5
Moneyline
COL -171 / LAK +143
Total
O/U 5.5

Save the best for last. Colorado at 39-10-9 has the best record in the entire NHL, and the Avalanche are on a pace that would make them one of the great regular-season teams in modern hockey history. Just let that record sink in for a moment: 39 wins, only 10 regulation losses, and 9 overtime losses through nearly 60 games. That's an 87-point team with roughly 25 games still to play, and they're on track to challenge for the Presidents' Trophy and enter the playoffs as the undisputed best team in hockey. This is a roster that is firing on all cylinders, and the NHL has taken notice.

What makes Colorado so terrifying is the combination of high-end talent and depth. This isn't a team that relies on one line or one goaltender to carry the load. The Avalanche score from every position, defend with commitment from the forwards all the way back to the goaltender, and they play with a pace and intensity that simply overwhelms opponents over the course of 60 minutes. Their ability to turn defensive zone exits into transition offense in the blink of an eye is the most dangerous weapon in the NHL right now, and teams that try to open up against them get burned by the counter-attack, while teams that try to sit back get suffocated by relentless offensive zone pressure.

Los Angeles at 24-21-14 has been a frustrating team this season. The Kings have 62 points, but that 14 in the overtime loss column tells you everything you need to know about their ability to close out games. LA has been competitive in the vast majority of their games this season, which is why they have so many overtime losses, but competitive isn't the same as winning, and the Kings have too often found themselves on the wrong end of tight games that playoff teams find ways to win. The home-ice advantage at Crypto.com Arena gives them a boost, and the crowd should be energized for a matchup against the league's best team.

The COL -171 moneyline and the LAK +1.5 puck line tell the story. Colorado is a significant road favorite, and the market is essentially saying that the Avalanche's overall quality will override whatever home-ice advantage the Kings enjoy. The 5.5 total suggests a tighter, more defensive game than you might expect from two Western Conference teams, but Colorado has proven all season that they can win in any style. Whether it's a 5-3 track meet or a 2-1 defensive grind, the Avalanche have the adaptability and depth to impose their will on the opposition. LA will compete and make this interesting, because that's what they do, but Colorado's ceiling is simply higher than what the Kings can match on their best night.
