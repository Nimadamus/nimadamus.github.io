Thanksgiving Day Triple-Header

The two-time defending champions host a Texans team that won the AFC South last year but has struggled with consistency in 2025. Kansas City's 11-1 record is the best in the AFC, and Arrowhead Stadium remains one of the toughest venues in football.

Chiefs' Championship Pedigree: Patrick Mahomes has 2,900+ passing yards with 22 TDs and only 8 INTs. Travis Kelce (750+ yards) remains elite, and the defense has been dominant, allowing just 15.8 PPG (1st in NFL). Kansas City has won 24 of their last 25 regular-season home games.

Stroud's Sophomore Season: C.J. Stroud has 3,200 passing yards but the Texans offense hasn't been as explosive as last year. Nico Collins (1,100+ yards) has been the go-to target, but inconsistency has plagued Houston. They've lost 3 of their last 5 games.

Arrowhead Factor: The Chiefs are 6-0 at home and have covered in 5 of 6 home games. December night games at Arrowhead are a different animal - the cold, the noise, and the moment tend to favor Kansas City. Houston is 2-4 on the road this season.

The Chiefs are 8-1 ATS when favored by 7+ at home under Andy Reid. This is a get-right spot for Kansas City after their only loss of the season. Expect Mahomes to control the clock and the defense to suffocate Stroud.

Philadelphia Eagles (10-2) @ Los Angeles Chargers (8-4)

Two of the best-coached teams in football square off in what should be a fantastic Week 14 finale. Philadelphia's 10-2 record includes a dominant rushing attack (175.2 YPG, 1st in NFL), while Jim Harbaugh has transformed the Chargers into a disciplined, defense-first team at 8-4.

Eagles' Ground and Pound: Saquon Barkley leads the NFL with 1,650+ rushing yards and 12 TDs. He's averaging 6.2 YPC and has run for 100+ yards in 10 of 12 games. Combined with Jalen Hurts' rushing ability, Philadelphia's ground attack is nearly unstoppable. The Eagles have rushed for 200+ yards in 6 games.

Harbaugh's Defense: The Chargers allow just 16.5 PPG (2nd in NFL) and have a front seven built to stop the run. Joey Bosa and Khalil Mack combine for 16+ sacks. LA has held opponents under 100 rushing yards in 7 games. This is the ultimate test for Philadelphia's rushing attack.

Justin Herbert's Efficiency: Herbert has a 97.5 passer rating with 20 TDs and 6 INTs in Harbaugh's system. He's not putting up massive numbers, but he's been incredibly efficient and turnover-averse. Ladd McConkey (800+ yards) has emerged as a reliable WR1.

Philadelphia is 5-1 on the road but faces their stiffest defensive test yet. The Chargers are 5-1 at home. This game could easily come down to a field goal in the final minutes. Both coaching staffs will have their teams prepared for a physical battle.
