The Final Saturday: 15 Games With Playoff Spots on the Line

On the last big Saturday of the regular season, a 15-game slate carries enormous weight, with wild-card berths and seeding still up for grabs from Boston and Ottawa in the East to the tight Western chase featuring the Kings, Predators, and a Vegas trip to the league-leading Avalanche.

Lightning at Bruins

TD Garden, Boston

Tampa Bay arrive around 49-25-6, locked into a strong Atlantic Division seed and chasing Montreal for second in the division, with Brandon Hagel enjoying a career year up front. The Lightning are one of the East's most dangerous teams and will treat this road trip as a tune-up for seeding, looking to stay sharp and keep pace near the top of a deep Atlantic.

Boston enter holding one of the East's wild-card spots but mired in a rough patch at home, having dropped several in a row, with their playoff position tied on points and hanging on a tiebreaker. The Bruins badly need to steady themselves before the postseason. The watch point is whether Boston can shake its home slump against a quality opponent, with their playoff seeding still very much in flux on the final Saturday.

Senators at Islanders

UBS Arena, Elmont

Ottawa arrive around 43-27-10, surging late and riding strong goaltending from Linus Ullmark, tied on points with Boston in the chase for an Eastern wild-card berth. The Senators have found their game at the right time and will look to keep their winning run going on the road, with a playoff spot within reach in the final days of the season.

The Islanders, near 43-32-5, have stumbled at the worst possible time, losing several of their last six and slipping behind in the Metropolitan seeding race with little margin left. New York need points to stay alive. The watch point is whether the Islanders can halt their slide at home against a red-hot Ottawa side, in a game with direct playoff-positioning consequences for both clubs.

Capitals at Penguins

PPG Paints Arena, Pittsburgh

This renewal of a classic rivalry carries late-season stakes. Washington, around 41-30-9, have been pushing to firm up their position behind the ageless Alex Ovechkin, who continues to add to his all-time goal total, and they arrive having won several of their last handful of games. The Capitals will lean on their experience and depth scoring in a meaningful road test.

Pittsburgh, near 41-23-16, have been solid in the Metropolitan and are fighting to lock down divisional seeding, with their veteran core still driving results although they have managed some injuries down the stretch. The watch point is whether Pittsburgh can hold serve at home against a rival, with both Pennsylvania-Capital rivals treating the rivalry game as a high-intensity dress rehearsal for the postseason.

Oilers at Kings

Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles

A potential first-round preview with seeding implications. Los Angeles, around 34-26-19, have been one of the West's stingiest teams, riding a strong recent run to hold a wild-card spot by a slim margin over Nashville, leaning on low-event hockey and reliable goaltending. The Kings badly want home points to protect their position in a tight Western race.

Edmonton, near 41-30-11, remain dangerous behind Connor McDavid even as they navigate the stretch run, and they are fighting to secure their own playoff seeding in the Pacific. The watch point is whether the Kings' defensive structure can contain Edmonton's star power, in a divisional clash that could shape the Western bracket and carries real weight for both sides on the final Saturday.

Devils at Red Wings

Little Caesars Arena, Detroit

New Jersey, around 41-36-3, have the high-end skill of Jack Hughes and Jesper Bratt to trouble anyone, though their own season has been uneven. The Devils will look to play spoiler on the road against a team with everything on the line, leaning on their top-six talent to control play and bank a result.

Detroit, near 41-30-9, arrive in a desperate spot, clinging to the edge of the Eastern playoff race with their margin for error all but gone in the final week. Every point is essential for the Red Wings. The watch point is whether Detroit's urgency and home crowd can overcome a skilled Devils side, with the Red Wings' postseason hopes hanging in the balance on the final Saturday.

Blues at Blackhawks

United Center, Chicago

St. Louis arrive still clinging to the fringes of the Western wild-card race, needing a near-perfect finish and plenty of help, leaning on their physical forecheck and a hot stretch to stay mathematically alive. The Blues will treat this as a must-win and look to take care of business against a non-contender on the road.

Chicago, near 28-38-14, are well out of the playoff picture and playing out the string, but Connor Bedard continues to produce as the face of the rebuild and can make any game interesting. The watch point is whether the Blackhawks' young talent can spoil St. Louis's slim postseason hopes, or whether the Blues' desperation carries them in a game that means far more to the visitors.

Wild at Predators

Bridgestone Arena, Nashville

Minnesota, around 45-23-12, have locked up a strong Central position and are set to finish third in the division, playing the heavy, structured game that makes them a difficult postseason matchup. With their seeding largely settled, the Wild will look to stay sharp while denying a direct rival valuable points.

Nashville arrive in the thick of the Western wild-card chase, sitting roughly a point behind the Kings for the final spot with the season running out, making every home game a four-point swing. The Predators need points desperately. The watch point is whether Nashville can keep pace in the race against a quality divisional opponent, with their playoff hopes hanging on results like this one.

Rangers at Stars

American Airlines Center, Dallas

Dallas, one of the West's elite teams, arrive fighting to lock down the No. 2 seed in the Central, with a deep, balanced lineup that can beat opponents in every style. The Stars will treat home seeding games seriously down the stretch and are expected to control play against a team on the outside of the race.

The New York Rangers, near 33-37-9, have played better of late, winning several of their last seven, but their own postseason math has run out, leaving them in a spoiler role. The watch point is whether the Rangers' recent form can trouble a top Western contender on the road, or whether Dallas's depth and seeding motivation prove decisive in a clear mismatch of stakes.

Hurricanes at Mammoth

Delta Center, Salt Lake City

Carolina, around 51-22-6, have been one of the best teams in the league and are pushing for the East's top seed, riding a strong recent run built on their relentless forecheck and depth. The Hurricanes are a complete team and will look to keep building toward the postseason with a road win against a Western club.

Utah, near 42-30-6, have enjoyed a strong sophomore campaign in Salt Lake City and are battling for Western seeding, holding a wild-card position with a cushion as the season winds down, powered by a young core. The watch point is whether Utah's home form can keep pace with one of the league's elite, in a cross-conference clash that matters to both teams' positioning.

Panthers at Maple Leafs

Scotiabank Arena, Toronto

Florida, near 37-38-4, have endured a disappointing, injury-affected season and arrive having struggled through a rough recent stretch, well off the pace they set in recent years. The Panthers still have championship-tested talent capable of a strong individual night, but consistency has eluded them down the stretch in a brutal Atlantic.

Toronto, around 32-33-14, have also fallen short of expectations and slid in a deep division, dropping several of their last handful of games. The watch point is whether either of these underachieving Atlantic clubs can find a spark in a head-to-head meeting, with both teams playing out a frustrating campaign and looking to finish on a positive note rather than chasing postseason stakes.

Blue Jackets at Canadiens

Bell Centre, Montreal

Columbus arrive still clinging to the edges of the Eastern wild-card race, needing points and help in the final stretch after a strong second half kept them relevant. The Blue Jackets play a fast, aggressive style and will treat this road game as a near-must-win to keep their slim postseason hopes breathing.

Montreal, around the top of the Atlantic wild-card and divisional mix, sit a couple of points ahead of Tampa Bay for second in the division with games to play, making home points valuable as they push for the best possible seed. The watch point is whether the Canadiens can hold serve at the Bell Centre against a desperate divisional-rival hopeful, with seeding implications on both sides.

Flyers at Jets

Canada Life Centre, Winnipeg

Philadelphia, around 41-27-12, have pushed into the Metropolitan picture with a strong campaign and are holding a playoff-relevant position, playing a fast, north-south game that has served them well. The Flyers will look to keep their seeding hopes on track with a road result against a fading Western club.

Winnipeg, near 35-32-12, have slipped out of the Western race after an inconsistent season and are running low on time, leaning on their top forwards to try to play spoiler. The watch point is whether the Jets can summon a strong home effort against a playoff-bound Flyers side, or whether Philadelphia's positioning motivation carries the day in a game that means more to the visitors.

Flames at Kraken

Climate Pledge Arena, Seattle

Both of these clubs arrive on the outside of the Western playoff picture, making this a meeting of teams playing out the string. Calgary, near 32-38-9, have battled but fallen short in a deep conference, and they will look to finish strong and build something for next season with their younger players getting opportunities down the stretch.

Seattle, around 34-34-11, have hovered around .500 and faded from contention, leaning on their structure and goaltending to remain competitive game to game. The watch point is which non-contender brings more urgency and pride on a night without postseason stakes, with both teams using the final stretch to evaluate their rosters and finish the season on a positive note.

Golden Knights at Avalanche

Ball Arena, Denver

This is the marquee Western matchup of the slate. Colorado, around 52-16-11, have been the class of the league all season, having locked up the top spot in the West behind Nathan MacKinnon and a deep, star-driven lineup. With their seeding secured, the Avalanche can rotate their roster and manage minutes heading into the postseason while still defending home ice.

Vegas, near 37-26-17, are fighting to firm up their own playoff position behind Jack Eichel and Mark Stone, and they will treat a road game against the league leaders as a measuring stick for the postseason. The watch point is whether the Golden Knights can match the Avalanche's pace in a potential playoff preview, with Vegas needing to keep banking points while Colorado fine-tunes for a deep run.

Canucks at Sharks

SAP Center, San Jose

This West Coast matchup pits two clubs near the bottom of the Western Conference, with neither in the playoff picture. Vancouver, near 23-48-8, have endured a difficult season and arrive in a spoiler role, though they still have individual talent capable of a strong night against a fellow non-contender.

San Jose, around 37-34-8, have shown flashes from a promising young core but ultimately fell short of the postseason chase, fading in the final stretch. The watch point is which rebuilding club finishes stronger in a low-stakes meeting, with both teams using the closing games to develop younger players and assess where they stand heading into the offseason.
