Thursday Night Football - December 4

@

Dallas Cowboys (6-6) @ Detroit Lions (11-1)

Thursday Night Football 8:15 PM ET Ford Field Prime Video

This is the marquee matchup of Week 14. The Detroit Lions are the best team in the NFC at 11-1, riding one of the most dominant offensive attacks in the league. Jared Goff has been surgical, the running game with Jahmyr Gibbs and David Montgomery is punishing, and the offensive line is elite. Ford Field is going to be absolutely rocking for this one.

Dallas has been inconsistent all season, sitting at 6-6 and fighting for playoff positioning in a crowded NFC. Dak Prescott needs to be at his best against a Lions defense that's been opportunistic and aggressive. The Cowboys have the talent to compete, but they've struggled against elite competition this year.

Detroit's home-field advantage is significant. The Lions are 6-0 at Ford Field this season, and the crowd creates one of the loudest environments in the NFL. Dallas will need to handle the noise, execute in the red zone, and limit turnovers to have any chance of pulling the upset. The Lions should control this game, but Dak has a history of big performances in primetime.

Sunday Early Games - December 7 (1:00 PM ET)

@

Pittsburgh Steelers (9-3) @ Baltimore Ravens (8-4)

1:00 PM ET M&T Bank Stadium CBS

AFC North battles don't get much bigger than this. Pittsburgh leads the division at 9-3, but Baltimore is right on their heels at 8-4. Lamar Jackson has been playing at an MVP level again, and the Ravens' rushing attack is the most dominant in football. This is a rivalry game with massive playoff implications.

The Steelers have been winning with defense and timely offense. Russell Wilson has stabilized the quarterback position, and Pittsburgh's defense remains one of the best in the league at creating turnovers and pressuring quarterbacks. The key will be containing Derrick Henry and Lamar's rushing attack while controlling time of possession.

@

Cincinnati Bengals (4-8) @ Buffalo Bills (10-2)

1:00 PM ET Highmark Stadium CBS

Joe Burrow vs. Josh Allen is always must-watch football, even with Cincinnati's disappointing 4-8 record. The Bengals' season is effectively over, but Burrow is still one of the best quarterbacks in the league and can go toe-to-toe with anyone. Buffalo at 10-2 is the class of the AFC and looking to secure home-field advantage throughout the playoffs.

The Bills are rolling right now. Josh Allen is in the MVP conversation, the defense has been opportunistic, and Buffalo plays with a swagger that contenders carry. Playing at Highmark Stadium in December is a significant advantage - the cold weather and hostile crowd make life difficult for visitors. Expect Buffalo to handle business.

@

Indianapolis Colts (6-7) @ Jacksonville Jaguars (3-9)

1:00 PM ET EverBank Stadium CBS

AFC South divisional matchup between two teams heading in opposite directions. Indianapolis is still in the playoff hunt at 6-7 and needs every win they can get down the stretch. Jacksonville's season has been a disaster at 3-9, with Trevor Lawrence struggling and the defense unable to stop anyone.

Anthony Richardson's health and development remain the key storylines for the Colts. When he's healthy and making plays with his arm and legs, Indianapolis has the ceiling to compete with anyone. The Jaguars' dysfunction should give Indy a favorable matchup, and a win here would be crucial for their wild card hopes.

@

Tennessee Titans (3-9) @ Cleveland Browns (3-9)

1:00 PM ET Huntington Bank Field CBS

Two teams at 3-9 fighting for draft positioning more than wins at this point. Tennessee's season has been plagued by quarterback inconsistency and defensive struggles. Cleveland has dealt with the Deshaun Watson situation and injuries that have derailed any hopes of contention. This is a battle between two franchises looking toward 2026.

@

Seattle Seahawks (7-5) @ Atlanta Falcons (6-6)

1:00 PM ET Mercedes-Benz Stadium FOX

Two teams fighting for wild card positioning in their respective conferences. Seattle at 7-5 is in a good spot in the NFC West, with Geno Smith continuing to prove he's a legitimate starting quarterback. Atlanta at 6-6 is in a crowded NFC South and needs wins to stay in the hunt.

The Falcons have been inconsistent but dangerous when clicking. Bijan Robinson is one of the most dynamic running backs in the league, and Michael Penix Jr. has shown flashes of the talent that made him the 8th overall pick. Seattle's defense will be tested by Atlanta's explosive playmakers.

@

Minnesota Vikings (10-2) @ Washington Commanders (8-5)

1:00 PM ET Northwest Stadium FOX

The Vikings are one of the best stories in the NFL this season. At 10-2, Sam Darnold's resurgence has been nothing short of remarkable. Minnesota's defense is elite, and their offensive weapons with Justin Jefferson are devastating. This is a team built to make a deep playoff run.

Washington's rebuild under Jayden Daniels has been ahead of schedule. The rookie quarterback has been sensational, and the Commanders at 8-5 are firmly in the playoff picture. This is a measuring stick game for both teams - Minnesota looking to prove they're legitimate Super Bowl contenders, Washington trying to show they belong with the NFC elite.
