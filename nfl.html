AFC Wild Card Games

#1 Denver Broncos (14-3) receive first-round bye

BYE: Denver Broncos (14-3) - AFC's top seed for the first time since their 2015 Super Bowl championship

Bills @ Jaguars

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | EverBank Stadium | CBS
Line: JAX -1.5 | O/U: 52.5 | BUF: 12-5 | JAX: 13-4

Here's a sentence nobody expected to type this season: the Jacksonville Jaguars are hosting a playoff game as favorites. After winning the AFC South at 13-4, Doug Pederson's squad has transformed into a genuine contender, rattling off eight straight wins heading into the postseason. The Bills? They're stumbling into January after barely holding on for the sixth seed.

Let's talk about the elephant in the room - Buffalo's run defense is an absolute disaster. They've given up 5.1 yards per carry, third-worst in the NFL, and surrendered 100+ rushing yards in 11 of their 17 games. Travis Etienne and Tank Bigsby are licking their chops. Jacksonville's ground game has been methodical during their winning streak, controlling time of possession and keeping opposing offenses on the sideline.

Josh Allen being Josh Allen keeps the Bills dangerous. The guy has been carrying this franchise on his back for years, and his playoff experience is undeniable. But Allen has looked human lately, throwing four picks over his last three games as the offensive line has given up pressure at an alarming rate. Trevor Lawrence, meanwhile, has found his groove - the Jags haven't scored fewer than 23 points during their hot streak.

The total sitting at 52.5 tells you oddsmakers expect fireworks. Both teams can score, but Jacksonville's defense has been quietly dominant, allowing just one opponent to crack 20 points in two months. Something's gotta give, and playing in front of a rocking crowd in Duval might be the difference maker.

Chargers @ Patriots

Sunday, 8:00 PM ET | Gillette Stadium | NBC
Line: NE -3.5 | O/U: 46.5 | LAC: 10-7 | NE: 12-5

New England is BACK. After years of post-Brady purgatory, the Patriots have stormed back to relevance with a 12-5 record and the AFC's second seed. Their turnaround represents a +10 swing in wins from last year, tying the NFL record for year-over-year improvement. Gillette Stadium in January hits different, and the ghosts of playoff past are ready to haunt another visitor.

Jim Harbaugh has the Chargers playing disciplined football, which is saying something for a franchise that's historically found creative ways to choke in big moments. Justin Herbert's been dealing all season, but road playoff games are a different animal. The Bolts went 4-1 ATS as underdogs this year, including 3-1 on the road, so they're clearly capable of rising to the occasion.

Here's where it gets interesting: New England went 12-5 ATS this season, tied for best in football. But as favorites of 2-5 points? Just 1-2 against the spread. The Pats know how to win, but covering short spreads has been tricky. Meanwhile, Harbaugh's been an underdog whisperer throughout his career, going 13-13 straight up in those spots.

The weather could be a factor - January nights in Foxborough are brutal. That typically favors the home team and running games. New England's defense has been suffocating lately, but Herbert with something to prove is scary. The all-time series favors the Patriots 27-17-2 including postseason, and playoff experience matters. This has the makings of a classic January slugfest.

NFC Wild Card Games

#1 Seattle Seahawks (14-3) receive first-round bye

BYE: Seattle Seahawks (14-3) - NFC's top seed awaits the lowest remaining seed in the Divisional Round

49ers @ Eagles

Sunday, 4:30 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field | FOX
Line: PHI -4.5 | O/U: 44.5 | SF: 12-5 | PHI: 11-6

Look, I know the records say San Francisco has the better record at 12-5 versus Philly's 11-6. But this 49ers team isn't the juggernaut we've seen in recent years. Nick Bosa's season-ending ACL tear in Week 3 ripped the heart out of their pass rush. Fred Warner's fractured ankle landed him on IR. This is a patchwork version of Kyle Shanahan's squad limping into January.

The Eagles opened as 3.5-point favorites and the line has climbed to 4.5 - the market clearly likes Philadelphia here. The Linc in January is one of the toughest environments in football, and Philly's home record speaks for itself. Jalen Hurts has been phenomenal down the stretch, and the return of their offensive line to full health couldn't have come at a better time.

San Francisco's recent history as underdogs isn't pretty - they've gone 1-7 ATS in their last eight games getting points. Even more concerning: they're 3-9 ATS in their last 12 meetings against Philly. There's something about this matchup that the 49ers just can't figure out, and facing them without their best defensive player makes it even harder.

The total has dropped from 46.5 to 44.5, suggesting the sharp money expects a defensive grind. Both teams can control the clock with their running games, and January football tends to favor the team that can move the chains on the ground. Saquon Barkley against this depleted San Francisco front seven? That could be a problem.
