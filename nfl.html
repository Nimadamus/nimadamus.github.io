Dolphins @ Steelers

Monday, 8:15 PM ET | Acrisure Stadium | ESPN
Line: PIT -3 | O/U: 42.5 | MIA: 6-7 (2-4 Road) | PIT: 7-6 (4-3 Home)

Matchup Overview

The Miami Dolphins (6-7) travel to Pittsburgh to face the Steelers (7-6) in a Monday Night Football showdown with major playoff implications for both teams. Miami has struggled on the road this season with a 2-4 record away from Hard Rock Stadium, while Pittsburgh has been solid at home posting a 4-3 mark at Acrisure Stadium. With both teams fighting for wild card positioning, this primetime clash carries significant weight.

Pittsburgh's defense has been the backbone of their season, ranking among the league's best in several categories. T.J. Watt continues to terrorize quarterbacks, and the secondary has tightened up in recent weeks. The Steelers' offense has been inconsistent but has shown flashes of explosive play when the running game gets going.

Key Storylines

Miami's passing attack led by Tua Tagovailoa will face a stern test against Pittsburgh's aggressive pass rush. The Dolphins' success has hinged on their ability to create explosive plays through the air, but the Steelers' secondary has been opportunistic all season. Tyreek Hill and Jaylen Waddle will need to win their matchups to move the chains consistently.

For Pittsburgh, ball security will be paramount. The Steelers have been prone to turnovers at critical moments, and Miami's defense has been creating havoc in the takeaway department. The cold December weather at Acrisure Stadium could favor Pittsburgh's physical style of play.

What To Watch

The trenches will decide this game. Miami's offensive line has been inconsistent in pass protection, and Watt and company will look to collapse the pocket and force Tua into quick decisions. Pittsburgh needs to establish the ground game early to control clock and keep Miami's dangerous offense on the sideline. Weather conditions could also play a factor with temperatures expected to be in the low 30s at kickoff.
