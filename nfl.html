Chicago Bears (8-3) at Philadelphia Eagles (8-3) - Black Friday football brings us a clash of NFC division leaders with massive playoff implications. The defending Super Bowl champion Eagles host a Bears team that has exceeded all expectations under first-year coach Ben Johnson. Both teams enter with identical 8-3 records, but the paths they've taken couldn't be more different.

Caleb Williams: The Franchise QB Emerges

The No. 1 overall pick has been everything Chicago hoped for and more. Through 11 games, Caleb Williams has thrown for 2,568 yards (10th in NFL), 16 touchdowns, and just 4 interceptions. He's completing 59.2% of his passes with a 55.3 QBR. Williams has set the Bears franchise record for most fourth-quarter comebacks by a QB and tied the NFL record for most fourth-quarter comebacks by any QB in their first two seasons (7). He's on pace to break Erik Kramer's 1995 franchise record of 3,838 passing yards. Most impressively, Williams ranks 6th in the league with 917 passing yards off play action. In last week's 31-28 win over Pittsburgh, he completed 19 of 35 passes for 239 yards with 3 touchdowns and 0 interceptions.

Saquon Barkley's Sophomore Slump?

The reigning NFL Offensive Player of the Year has been mortal in 2025. After setting NFL records last season with 2,504 rushing yards and 2,857 scrimmage yards, Barkley has just 684 rushing yards through 11 games this season - averaging 62.2 yards per game compared to 143.1 last year. He's averaging just 3.6 yards per carry, down from 5.8. In three games since the bye week, Barkley has produced only 265 yards from scrimmage with zero touchdowns on 69 touches. He also lost a fumble in the Cowboys loss. The explosive plays have vanished - just 2 rushes over 20 yards compared to 17 last season.

Jalen Hurts: Efficient but Limited

The Eagles QB has been incredibly efficient with 2,284 passing yards, 17 touchdowns, and just 1 interception on 67.2% completion. However, his rushing has declined significantly - just 298 rushing yards with 8 TDs on 80 attempts, on pace for his lowest rushing total as a starter. The Eagles offense ranks top-10 in scoring but has shown vulnerability, particularly in their 24-21 loss to Dallas where they allowed 17 points in the final 18 minutes.

Defensive Matchup Analysis

Chicago's defense ranks 25th in DVOA but leads the NFL with 22 takeaways. They've allowed 264 points (24.0 PPG) and 3,991 total yards. Opponents are averaging 5.9 yards per play with 5.2 yards per carry - the Bears remain vulnerable against the run. Philadelphia's defense dropped out of the "elite tier" after allowing 473 net yards to Dallas. The Eagles have allowed 1,266 rushing yards (4.3 YPC) and 2,588 passing yards with 22 sacks recorded. Their secondary has a clear vulnerability that Dallas exploited.

Head-to-Head History

This is where it gets ugly for Chicago. The Eagles have won the last 6 meetings against the Bears, outscoring them 94-52 in their last 4 games. Philadelphia is 4-0 SU and ATS against Chicago recently. The Bears are 1-5 ATS in their last 6 vs the Eagles. Historical dominance at this level doesn't disappear overnight.

The Verdict

The market has this at PHI -7 with -336 moneyline for the Eagles and +269 for Chicago. The spread opened at -7 and hasn't moved despite public action. This is a classic case of trends vs current form. The Bears have been incredible in close games (6-1 in one-score games) but face a team that owns them historically. Philadelphia's defensive vulnerability and Barkley's struggles give Chicago a puncher's chance. The UNDER 44.5 looks appealing given the 4-of-5 under trend in this series. Lean Eagles -7, but the under is the stronger play.
