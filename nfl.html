Chicago Bears at Philadelphia Eagles - Black Friday Showdown

Friday 3:00 PM ET | Amazon Prime Video | Lincoln Financial Field | PHI -7 | O/U 44.5

Chicago Bears (8-3) at Philadelphia Eagles (8-3) - Black Friday football brings us a fascinating matchup between two 8-3 teams fighting for playoff positioning. Look, this game has massive implications for both franchises. The Bears have been one of the biggest surprises of the 2025 season, sitting atop the NFC North after shocking everyone with their turnaround. Meanwhile, the Eagles are trying to stay in the NFC East race and secure home field advantage for the playoffs. This is the kind of game that separates contenders from pretenders.

The Bears' Improbable Season

Here's the thing about Chicago that nobody wants to talk about. This team has won seven of their eight games by one-possession margins. They're 7-2 ATS in their last nine games and 8-1 straight up over that same stretch. That's not luck anymore, that's a team that knows how to win close games. But here's the concerning part: they've done it despite having one of the worst statistical defenses in football. The Bears rank 25th in defensive DVOA while allowing the sixth-most points per game and third-most yards per play. They're winning games in spite of their defense, not because of it.

Why Philadelphia is a Different Animal at Home

The Eagles at Lincoln Financial Field are a completely different team than the one you see on the road. Philadelphia has covered the spread in nine of their past 12 home games and has an absurd 15-1 outright record in their last 16 home contests. That's not random variance. That's a team that knows how to win at home. Jalen Hurts has been electric all season, and the addition of Saquon Barkley has completely transformed this offense. Chicago's defense ranks 28th in the NFL, allowing an average of 138.1 rushing yards per game. Barkley is going to feast.

Historical Dominance by Philadelphia

The Bears may lead the all-time series 29-17-1, but the Eagles have absolutely owned this matchup recently. Philadelphia has won the last six meetings against Chicago. More importantly, the Bears are 1-5 ATS and 0-6 straight up in their last six games against the Eagles. That's complete domination. When these two teams meet, the Eagles just have Chicago's number. Home field advantage, historical trends, and defensive matchups all favor Philadelphia here.

The Betting Breakdown

The spread opened at Eagles -7, and it's held steady despite sharp money probing both sides. The total opened at 44 and has ticked up slightly to 44.5. The Eagles have covered in 14 of their last 17 games and are 4-1 ATS in their last five contests. Philadelphia has been rock solid all season. Chicago is 7-4-0 against the spread this year, but their road ATS record tells a different story. The Bears have struggled to cover as road underdogs in meaningful spots, and this is absolutely a meaningful spot.

Weather and Game Script Considerations

Late November football in Philadelphia means we need to think about weather. Temperatures will be in the low 40s with potential for wind. That's running weather, and that plays directly into Philadelphia's hands. The Eagles want to pound the rock with Barkley and control the clock. If they can build an early lead, Chicago's one-possession formula becomes much harder to execute. The Bears need to stay close to have a chance, but getting down early to a team that's 15-1 at home is a death sentence.

The Final Verdict

This is a spot where I trust the home team. Philadelphia has too many advantages here. They're dominant at home, they've owned this series recently, and they have a clear schematic advantage in the running game. Chicago's close game magic can only last so long, and facing a physical Eagles team in a hostile environment feels like the spot where it runs out. The spread is fair at -7, but I lean Eagles here. If you want to play it safer, take Philadelphia on the moneyline at -336. Sometimes you have to pay for quality, and the Eagles are the superior team.
