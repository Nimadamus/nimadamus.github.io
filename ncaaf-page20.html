Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

Bowl season is officially here and the Gasparilla Bowl in Tampa kicks things off with a solid matchup. Memphis at 8-5 brings their high-octane offense to Raymond James Stadium to face NC State at 8-5. Two evenly matched teams, two very different styles - this should be a lot of fun to watch.

Memphis lives for these shootout opportunities. Seth Henigan has been outstanding all year, racking up over 3,400 passing yards and making highlight-reel throws look routine. The Tigers' tempo can overwhelm defenses that aren't prepared, and their skill players are dangerous in space. When Memphis gets rolling, it's hard to stop them.

NC State's defense is going to have to play disciplined football to slow down this attack. The Wolfpack have been solid against the run and their secondary has made plays all season. If they can force Memphis into third-and-longs and get off the field, their more methodical offense can control the clock. The battle of tempo versus ball control is going to be fascinating.

Cougars @ Aggies

2025-12-22T19:00Z | Albertsons Stadium | ESPN
Line: USU -1.5 | O/U: 49.5 | WSU: 6-6 | USU: 6-6

Two .500 teams meet at SoFi Stadium in the LA Bowl, and this one has all the makings of a back-and-forth affair. Washington State at 6-6 has been through the wringer after the Pac-12 dissolved, but they're still standing and they've got a chance to finish with a winning record. Utah State at 6-6 wants to make a statement in the Mountain West.

Washington State's going to air it out - that's just their DNA. The Cougars throw the ball more than almost anyone in the country, and when their receivers are making plays, they're fun to watch. The flip side is they're prone to turnovers and bad decisions. Ball security is going to be everything for them.

Utah State's defense is opportunistic. They might not dominate you statistically, but they force turnovers at the worst possible times for opponents. If they can bait the Washington State quarterback into a few bad throws, this game could flip in a hurry. The Aggies being slight favorites feels about right - this is as close to a pick'em as you'll find in bowl season.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

The Celebration Bowl at Mercedes-Benz Stadium is one of the most unique events in college football. HBCU pride is on full display as South Carolina State from the MEAC takes on Prairie View A&M from the SWAC. The atmosphere is going to be absolutely electric with legendary marching bands and passionate fans from both programs.

SC State at 10-3 has earned this moment with their dominant play this season. Their defense has been the backbone - they make life miserable for opposing quarterbacks and they don't give up cheap points. On offense, they're efficient and smart with the ball. They might not blow you away, but they execute their game plan and avoid costly mistakes.

Prairie View at 10-4 brings the SWAC's explosive style to Atlanta. The Panthers have playmakers who can break a game open with one touch. Their speed at the skill positions is legit, and when they get the ball in space, watch out. The key for them is not falling behind early - if SC State gets a lead and makes them one-dimensional, it becomes harder to utilize their athletes. This is going to be an amazing spectacle.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

Ashton Jeanty. That's the story. The Heisman finalist brings his incredible season to SoFi Stadium as Boise State at 9-5 faces Washington at 9-4. Everyone knows what's coming - Jeanty is getting the ball 25+ times - but stopping him is another matter entirely. The dude has over 2,400 rushing yards and he's built different.

Boise State's entire offense flows through Jeanty. When he's rolling, the Broncos can beat anyone. He breaks tackles, he makes defenders miss, and he has elite vision to find the crease. Washington's defensive front is going to need to play their best game of the year just to slow him down. Tackling in the open field is going to be crucial.

Washington needs to make this a track meet. The Huskies have the weapons to put up points through the air, and if they can jump out to a lead, it forces Boise State to become one-dimensional. Their passing game has been solid all season and they've got playmakers on the outside. This is going to come down to whether the Huskies can stay in a shootout or if Jeanty grinds them down with 40 carries. Should be a fun one either way.

Trojans @ Gamecocks

2025-12-17T02:00Z | Cramton Bowl | ESPN
TROY: 8-6 | JVST: 9-5

The Camellia Bowl is going to be a war. Troy and Jacksonville State are Sun Belt rivals who know each other well, and there's genuine dislike between these programs. The Trojans at 8-6 want to salvage what's been a disappointing season. The Gamecocks at 9-5 are looking to validate their rapid rise to FBS relevance.

Troy's got the experience and the pedigree. They've played in bowl games before, they've won big games before, and they've got experienced players who can handle the moment. When their offense is clicking, they can score with anyone in the Sun Belt. The question is whether they show up with the right intensity from the jump.

Jacksonville State has been the upstart all year, exceeding expectations at every turn. The Gamecocks play with an edge because they feel like they have something to prove. Their defense is physical and relentless - they're going to hit you in the mouth from the opening kickoff. The Cramton Bowl atmosphere is going to be intense for this regional rivalry. Whoever wants it more is going to win.

Monarchs @ Bulls

2025-12-17T22:00Z | Camping World Stadium | ESPN
ODU: 10-3 | USF: 9-4

The Cure Bowl brings together two of the best Group of Five stories of the season. Old Dominion at 10-3 has been sensational - they've beaten good teams and they've done it with a physical, old-school approach that's hard not to love. USF at 9-4 has found their footing under their coaching staff and they're building something real.

ODU is going to try to pound the rock. The Monarchs win with their offensive line - they run the ball effectively, they control the clock, and they wear you down. Their defense has been opportunistic as well, creating turnovers when games are on the line. They're not flashy, but they're extremely well-coached.

South Florida has more dynamic playmakers. The Bulls can hit big plays when their guys get the ball in space, and they've got a quarterback who can make things happen. If they can establish an early lead and make ODU chase points, that changes the complexion of everything. But if the Monarchs get ahead and start grinding clock, it's going to be a long day. Camping World Stadium should have a nice crowd for this one.

Cajuns @ Hens

2025-12-18T01:40Z | Hancock Whitney Stadium | ESPN
UL: 6-7 | DEL: 7-6

An FBS vs FCS showdown in Mobile as Louisiana takes on Delaware. The Ragin' Cajuns at 6-7 had a frustrating season filled with close losses and inconsistent performances. Delaware at 7-6 is treating this game like their championship - taking on an FBS opponent is a huge opportunity for the Blue Hens program.

Louisiana should have the talent advantage, but that only matters if they show up ready to play. The Cajuns have had too many games this season where they started slow and dug themselves holes. If they come out flat against an inspired Delaware team, they're going to be in trouble. Bowl games are all about motivation, and Delaware is going to be absolutely fired up.

The Blue Hens aren't going to be scared. They've played tough competition in FCS and they believe they can compete with anyone. Their defense will be physical and they're going to make Louisiana earn every yard. If the Cajuns don't establish their identity early and assert themselves, this could turn into one of those embarrassing losses that follows a program for years. Louisiana needs to treat this seriously.

Bears @ Wolves

2025-12-19T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN2
MOST: 7-6 | ARST: 7-6

The Xbox Bowl at The Star in Frisco is a true 50-50 matchup. Missouri State at 7-6 from the Missouri Valley Conference takes on Arkansas State at 7-6 from the Sun Belt. Neither team had the season they envisioned, but a bowl win would go a long way toward making it feel worthwhile. Both programs are treating this as a chance at redemption.

Missouri State is playing with house money as an FCS program in an FBS bowl. The Bears have earned this spot by playing physical, disciplined football all year. They don't beat themselves with turnovers and penalties, and that kind of execution matters in close games. Arkansas State can't take them lightly.

The Red Wolves have more natural talent, but they've been their own worst enemy at times. Too many mental errors, too many lapses in concentration. If they clean that up and play to their potential, they should win this game. But Missouri State is going to make them earn everything. Expect a tight, hard-fought game that could come down to the final possession.

Tide @ Sooners

2025-12-20T01:00Z | Memorial Stadium (Norman, OK) | ABC, ESPN
ALA: 11-3 | OU: 10-3

This is must-see television. The College Football Playoff brings us Alabama at Oklahoma in what promises to be one of the most electric atmospheres in college football history. The Crimson Tide at 11-3 hit the road to Norman to face the Sooners at 10-3, and the stakes couldn't be higher. Winner advances, loser goes home.

Alabama has been here before - they've played on the biggest stages and won. That experience is invaluable when the pressure ratchets up. Their defense has been elite all season, creating havoc and forcing mistakes. On offense, they've got the firepower to hang with anyone. The Tide are battle-tested and they're ready for this moment.

Oklahoma desperately wants to validate themselves among college football's elite. Their home crowd is going to be absolutely rabid - the energy at Memorial Stadium is going to be off the charts. The Sooners have earned this moment with a strong season, and they believe they can knock off the giant. But playoff football is different, and experience matters. This game is going to come down to who handles the pressure better. I genuinely can't wait for this one.

Owls @ Broncos

2025-12-19T16:00Z | Brooks Stadium (SC) | ESPN
KENN: 10-4 | WMU: 10-4

The Myrtle Beach Bowl brings together two 10-4 programs with very different identities. Kennesaw State runs that triple-option attack that makes coaches lose sleep during bowl prep. Western Michigan plays a more conventional style with a balanced offense. This schematic clash is going to make for fascinating viewing.

Kennesaw's offense is all about execution and discipline. They're going to run the same plays over and over, daring you to stop them. When their option reads are on point, they can move the chains all game long and keep Western Michigan's offense on the sideline. Time of possession is going to be a huge factor.

Western Michigan needs to make this a shootout. The Broncos have playmakers who can create explosive plays, and if they can score early and force Kennesaw out of their comfort zone, that's a massive advantage. Their defense has a week to prepare for the option - can they stay disciplined and not bite on the fakes? Brooks Stadium should provide a nice neutral site atmosphere for what promises to be a chess match between two well-coached programs.

Dukes @ Ducks

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Autzen Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
JMU: 12-2 | ORE: 12-1

The College Football Playoff brings Cinderella to Autzen Stadium. James Madison at 12-2 has shocked the world this season, going from FCS powerhouse to legitimate FBS playoff team in what feels like the blink of an eye. Oregon at 12-1 is one of the best teams in the country and they're massive favorites at home. But JMU didn't come all this way to lose quietly.

Oregon's offense is elite by any measure. They've got speed, they've got playmakers, and their quarterback can make every throw. The Ducks don't make mistakes, they protect the football, and they score in bunches. Autzen Stadium is going to be deafening, and that crowd advantage is real. They're going to be heavily favored for good reason.

But write off JMU at your own peril. The Dukes have an edge about them - they genuinely believe they can beat anyone, and they've proven it all season. Their quarterback is fearless in big moments, their defense swarms to the ball, and they play with a chip on their shoulder that's intoxicating to watch. Is an upset likely? No. But JMU is going to make Oregon earn every single point. The Ducks cannot afford to sleepwalk through this one.

The Rebels at 12-1 don't think so - they're rolling and Lane Kiffin has this team playing their best football of the year heading into the playoffs.

The Rebels' tempo creates chaos for opposing defenses, and their skill players have the speed to turn any play into a touchdown. When they're clicking, they're unstoppable.

Tulane at 11-3 has had an excellent season, but they're simply overmatched here. The Green Wave got embarrassed in September and there's no indication they've closed the talent gap. Maybe they're more competitive this time, maybe they keep it closer, but asking them to overcome a 35-point beatdown from three months ago is a lot. The atmosphere at Vaught-Hemingway is going to be incredible. This feels like it's going to be a statement game for the Rebels.

The Brawl of the Wild takes on even more significance with a championship on the line. Both teams know each other inside and out, and this is going to be an absolute war.

Montana has the playoff pedigree. The Grizzlies have been to the big dance before, they know what this stage feels like, and they've got players who've made big plays in pressure moments. Their balanced offense can attack in multiple ways, and their defense has been suffocating in the postseason. They're going to be loose and confident.

Montana State has home field at Bobcat Stadium and their crowd is going to be absolutely deafening. The Bobcats have been dominant all season, particularly on the ground where they just wear teams down physically. If they can control the clock and keep Montana's offense on the sideline, they can grind out a championship. This is rivalry football at its best - expect emotion, intensity, and a game that comes down to the wire.

Redbirds @ Wildcats

ILST: 12-4 | VILL: 12-3

FCS semifinal action as Illinois State heads to Villanova with everything on the line. The Redbirds at 12-4 have fought through the bracket to get here, grinding out wins with physical, tough-minded football. The Wildcats at 12-3 have the home field advantage and they've been playing inspired football in the postseason. This is going to be a battle of wills.

Illinois State's defense has been the story of their playoff run. The Redbirds don't give up easy yards - you have to earn everything against them. They tackle well, they're physical at the line of scrimmage, and they create turnovers at opportune moments. On offense, they're efficient and mistake-free. Not flashy, but effective.

Villanova's advantage is playing at home where they've been excellent. The Wildcats have playmakers who can flip momentum with one big play, and their crowd creates genuine energy. They've got more speed at the skill positions, but they need to match Illinois State's physicality. If the Redbirds bully them up front, speed won't matter. Expect a low-scoring, defensive struggle where every possession is precious.
