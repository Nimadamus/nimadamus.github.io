World Cup - Tuesday, June 23

England Meet Ghana In A Group L Showdown, And Group K Leaders Colombia Face DR Congo On Matchday Two

A four-match Tuesday at the 2026 World Cup completes the second round of group games in Groups K and L. England and Ghana meet at Foxborough with both sides on three points, Panama face Croatia in Toronto chasing a first point, Portugal look to recover against Uzbekistan in Houston after a draw, and Group K leaders Colombia face DR Congo in Guadalajara. Group standings and matchday-two storylines below, analysis only.

Marquee Matchup
FOX

England vs Ghana

Tuesday, 4:00 PM ET | Boston Stadium, Foxborough | Group L

The headline match of the Tuesday slate is a Group L meeting at the Boston Stadium in Foxborough, where England and Ghana arrive level on points and both in control of their fates. England opened with a 4-2 win over Croatia, a result built on a Harry Kane brace alongside goals from Jude Bellingham and Marcus Rashford, the kind of attacking statement that marks them as one of the group favorites. Three points and a strong goal difference put England in a commanding early position.

Ghana are right alongside them after a 1-0 win over Panama in their opener, a tight, disciplined performance settled by a late goal. That result gives Ghana three points and real belief heading into the toughest test of their group, and they will look to defend with structure and strike on the counter against an England side that will see the majority of the ball. The challenge for Ghana is the familiar one against a possession-heavy favorite: stay compact without inviting relentless pressure.

For England, the focus is on backing up a strong opener and taking a major step toward the knockout rounds while keeping Kane and Bellingham sharp. For Ghana, a result against one of the group favorites would be a statement and could decide top spot. With both sides on three points, this is the match most likely to settle the order at the top of Group L, and the team that wins takes a firm grip on qualification.

Croatia Chase A First Point
FOX

Panama vs Croatia

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | Toronto Stadium, Toronto | Group L

A Group L clash at the Toronto Stadium sends Panama in against Croatia in a match both sides badly need, with each arriving on zero points after opening-round defeats. Panama were edged 1-0 by Ghana in a tight contest, while Croatia were beaten 4-2 by England in a more open game. Both teams now sit behind England and Ghana in the group, and a defeat here would leave the loser facing near-elimination heading into the final round of group play.

Croatia carry the greater tournament pedigree and will expect to control possession through their experienced midfield, but their opening loss exposed defensive frailties that England punished. They will look to be more solid at the back while leaning on their technical superiority to break Panama down. The pressure is real for a side with deep World Cup history that cannot afford to fall further behind.

Panama, for their part, showed enough organization against Ghana to suggest they will not simply sit back, and their pace on the counter makes them a threat if Croatia commit numbers forward. Expect a cautious, tense affair early, with both teams wary of conceding first and effectively ending their own hopes. For a Panama side chasing a breakthrough result and a Croatia team desperate to recover its standing, the margins could not be finer.

Portugal Look To Recover
FOX

Portugal vs Uzbekistan

Tuesday, 1:00 PM ET | Houston Stadium, Houston | Group K

A pivotal Group K meeting at the Houston Stadium sends Portugal in against Uzbekistan in a match that could shape the order behind leaders Colombia. Portugal were held to a 1-1 draw by DR Congo in their opener, taking the lead through Joao Neves before Yoane Wissa equalized late in the first half for DR Congo's first ever World Cup goal. A single point has left one of the tournament favorites needing a win here to take control of its group.

Uzbekistan, making their World Cup debut, are under pressure after a 3-1 loss to Colombia, a game in which Abbosbek Fayzullaev pulled one back but the result never seriously threatened the South Americans. With no points on the board, Uzbekistan need a response, and their disciplined, hard-working approach makes them a tricky opponent for a Portugal side that will be expected to dominate possession.

The contrast is clear: a Portugal team with far greater quality but a point to prove after dropping points in its opener, against a debutant nation that must avoid defeat to keep its hopes alive. Portugal will look to control the tempo and break Uzbekistan down, while the Central Asian side will defend in numbers and look for a moment on the counter. With Portugal needing the win and Uzbekistan needing to stay compact, this profiles as a test of patience for the favorites.

Leaders Look To Advance
FS1

Colombia vs DR Congo

Tuesday, 10:00 PM ET | Guadalajara Stadium, Guadalajara | Group K

The Tuesday nightcap at the Guadalajara Stadium is a Group K meeting between the group leaders and a DR Congo side that has already announced its presence. Colombia top the group after a 3-1 win over Uzbekistan, a performance keyed by a Daniel Munoz strike and the creativity of Luis Diaz, and a second victory here would put them into the knockout rounds with a match to spare. They arrive as the form team of the group and the side to beat.

DR Congo, meanwhile, earned a creditable 1-1 draw against Portugal in their opener, with Yoane Wissa heading home the nation's first ever World Cup goal to claim a valuable point against one of the tournament favorites. That result gives DR Congo a foundation to build on, and they will fancy their chances of springing another surprise against a Colombia side they will look to frustrate.

Colombia will expect to control the game through their technical midfield and the threat of Diaz on the flank, but DR Congo showed against Portugal that they can defend with organization and punish lapses. The leaders will want to assert themselves early and avoid the kind of complacency that lets a confident opponent settle. For a Colombia side eyeing early qualification and a DR Congo team chasing another upset, this is a compelling close to matchday two in Group K.
