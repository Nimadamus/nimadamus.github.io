World Cup Group Finale: England, Argentina And Portugal Close Out Groups J, K And L

The 2026 World Cup reaches the decisive final matchday for Groups J, K and L, and the headliners are heavyweights. England meets Panama at MetLife Stadium, Colombia faces Portugal in Miami, and Argentina takes on Jordan in Arlington, with Croatia, Ghana, Algeria, Austria, DR Congo and Uzbekistan filling out a coast-to-coast Saturday. The two matches in each group kick off at the same time to keep the standings honest. Kickoff times, venues, and storylines for all six below. Analysis and breakdown only, no picks.

England closes out Group L against Panama at MetLife Stadium, and the gulf in resources is wide. The English carry the depth, individual quality, and tournament pedigree that make them one of the favorites to go deep, a side comfortable controlling possession and breaking teams down through pace and movement in the final third. With seeding and a knockout-round path on the line, England will want a confident, controlled performance to top the group and set up the most favorable bracket route.

Panama represents the kind of organized, hard-working underdog that has made CONCACAF sides respected on the world stage, a team that defends in numbers, stays compact, and looks to punish any lapse on the counter. The Panamanians will be intent on frustrating a fancied opponent and springing forward when the chance arrives. Because both Group L matches kick off at the same time, Panama will be scoreboard-watching the result in Philadelphia as it tries to shape its own qualification scenario on the final day.

The other Group L fixture runs simultaneously in Philadelphia, where Croatia and Ghana close out their campaigns with qualification implications in the balance. Croatia brings the technical, midfield-driven style that has carried it deep into recent World Cups, a side that values possession, intelligent positional play, and the kind of game management that wins tight knockout-stage matches. The Croatians will look to control tempo and dictate the rhythm against a more athletic opponent.

The Black Stars will want to turn this into a physical, end-to-end contest that disrupts Croatia's preferred rhythm. Group K delivers the most evenly matched heavyweight clash of the day as Colombia meets Portugal at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami, with first place very much in play. Colombia brings a fluid, technically gifted attack and a midfield comfortable on the ball, a South American side that blends flair with the physical resilience to compete against anyone. With a partisan crowd expected in Miami, the Colombians will fancy their chances of topping the group with a strong showing.

Portugal counters with one of the deepest talent pools in the tournament, a side stacked with elite attacking options and the tactical maturity to control matches in the closing rounds. The Portuguese can hurt any defense through individual quality and well-rehearsed combinations in the final third. This is the marquee fixture of the evening, a genuine test of two contenders, and with both Group K matches running at the same time, the standings could swing on a single late goal in either city.
