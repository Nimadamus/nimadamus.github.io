6-Game Saturday Slate

NBA February 21, 2026

Saturday brings a compact but compelling 6-game slate highlighted by Victor Wembanyama and the surging Spurs hosting the free-falling Kings in Austin, the Rockets visiting the Knicks on ABC at Madison Square Garden, and Phoenix hosting Orlando with Devin Booker sidelined by a hip injury.

Game 1
NBA TV

Magic @ Suns

Saturday, 5:00 PM ET | Mortgage Matchup Center, Phoenix AZ

This is a game where both teams are dealing with significant absences, and whoever adjusts better on the fly is going to walk away with the W. The Suns are without their franchise cornerstone Devin Booker, who's sidelined with a hip injury that's kept him off the court and left a massive void in Phoenix's offensive identity. When Booker isn't out there, the Suns become a fundamentally different team. The shot creation, the gravity he demands from defenses, the clutch shotmaking, all of it evaporates. Phoenix has to find other ways to generate offense, and that's never easy when your best player is in street clothes.

On the other side, Orlando is also dealing with the ongoing absence of Franz Wagner, which has been a theme for the Magic all season. Wagner's versatility as a scorer and playmaker from the wing is something you can't just replace, and Orlando has had to lean heavily on Paolo Banchero and their defensive identity to stay competitive without him. The Magic are one of the best defensive teams in the league when healthy, and even shorthanded, they bring a physicality and length that makes life miserable for opposing offenses. That defensive intensity is going to matter here against a Suns team that's searching for offensive answers.

Phoenix is hoping that the return of Grayson Allen from a four-game absence provides some much-needed perimeter shooting and floor spacing. Allen's ability to knock down threes and play within the flow of the offense is exactly what the Suns need when Booker is out, and his presence should open up driving lanes for Jalen Green, who has been tasked with carrying more of the scoring load. Green has the talent to be a 25-point scorer on any given night, but consistency has been the question, and his shot selection under pressure will be crucial in this one.

This game has the feel of a grind-it-out, half-court battle. Orlando wants to play in the mud defensively and force you into tough shots, and without Booker, the Suns don't have the firepower to simply outscore that kind of effort. If Phoenix's role players step up and Allen looks sharp coming back, they've got the home court advantage working in their favor. But if Orlando's defense clamps down and makes life uncomfortable for Green and the rest of Phoenix's rotation, the Magic have the discipline and defensive backbone to steal one in the desert.

Game 2
League Pass

76ers @ Pelicans

Saturday, 7:00 PM ET | Smoothie King Center, New Orleans LA

It's hard to overstate just how catastrophic this season has been for the Philadelphia 76ers. Without Joel Embiid, this team has cratered, losing eight of their last nine games and looking like a squad that's completely run out of ideas. The Embiid-less 76ers are a fundamentally broken product on both ends of the floor. Their defense can't protect the rim without his massive presence, and their half-court offense devolves into isolation ball and contested mid-range jumpers. It's been ugly, and there's no sugarcoating it.

Tyrese Maxey has been doing everything humanly possible to keep Philadelphia afloat, and the dude deserves a medal for his effort. He's been shouldering an absurd scoring burden, pushing the pace, creating for others, and playing heavy minutes night after night. But one guy can only do so much, and Maxey is being asked to be a number-one option on a roster that wasn't built for him to carry alone. The wear and tear is starting to show, and the supporting cast hasn't given him nearly enough help to turn this losing streak around.

New Orleans, meanwhile, has Zion Williamson as their centerpiece, and when Zion is engaged and healthy, he's one of the most unstoppable forces in basketball. His combination of power, explosion, and finishing at the rim is unlike anything else in the league, and he should have a field day against a Philly frontcourt that has been getting bullied without Embiid. Zion's ability to get to the basket at will and draw fouls could turn this into a free throw parade, and the Pelicans' supporting cast has enough shooting to punish the 76ers if they collapse the paint.

The Smoothie King Center crowd should be juiced for this one, and New Orleans has the talent advantage at multiple positions tonight. Philadelphia's road woes have been well-documented, and traveling to New Orleans on the tail end of a brutal stretch doesn't bode well for a team that's already mentally fragile. The Pelicans should be able to control the tempo, get the ball to Zion in advantageous spots, and exploit a defense that's been hemorrhaging points all month. For the 76ers, this feels like another painful chapter in what's been a season to forget.

Game 3
League Pass

Pistons @ Bulls

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | United Center, Chicago IL

The Chicago Bulls are in freefall. A seven-game losing streak, the worst slump of their entire season, has exposed every crack in this roster and raised some serious questions about the direction of the franchise. The United Center has been a joyless place lately, and the energy from the home crowd has dried up as the losses pile up. When you're in the middle of a skid like this, everything compounds. Defensive rotations are a step slow, shots that used to fall rim out, and the body language on the bench tells the whole story. Chicago is a team that desperately needs a win, and they're hoping a visit from Detroit is the cure.

Cade Cunningham has been the engine for a Pistons team that's shown real growth this season. His ability to create offense from the point guard position, whether it's breaking down defenders off the dribble, finding shooters in rhythm, or pulling up from mid-range, has been the catalyst for everything good that Detroit does. Cunningham has the size and skill to be a matchup nightmare, and against a Bulls defense that's been leaking points at an alarming rate during this losing streak, he should have plenty of room to operate. The Pistons have been building something, and games like this against struggling teams are where you see that development translate into wins.

For Chicago, Josh Giddey has been the primary offensive initiator, and his blend of size, passing vision, and basketball IQ gives the Bulls a different look at the lead guard spot. Giddey's ability to find cutters, push the pace in transition, and facilitate from multiple positions on the floor is the kind of playmaking Chicago needs to snap out of this funk. But the Bulls' issues go deeper than any one player. Their defense has been porous, their rebounding has been inconsistent, and the overall cohesion that you need to close out games just hasn't been there during this skid.

This is a game between two teams in very different places mentally. Detroit has been playing with confidence and purpose, building chemistry and learning how to win games in crunch time. Chicago, on the other hand, is a team that's lost its mojo and is searching for answers anywhere it can find them. The United Center crowd will show up hoping the Bulls can end the bleeding, but the Pistons are the kind of young, hungry team that smells blood in the water against a reeling opponent. Cunningham versus Giddey is a fun individual matchup, but it's the overall team energy and execution that will decide this one.

Grizzlies @ Heat

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | Kaseya Center, Miami FL

Memphis has gone from one of the most exciting young teams in the league to one of the most concerning. Losing 11 of their last 14 games is the kind of tailspin that changes your entire trajectory, and the Grizzlies have looked lost on both ends of the floor during this stretch. The defensive intensity that once defined this team has vanished, the offensive execution has been inconsistent at best, and the collective confidence is clearly shaken. When you're in a nosedive like this, the hardest thing to do is find the one thing that stops the bleeding, and Memphis hasn't found it yet.

Now they've got to travel to South Beach and deal with a Miami Heat team that always makes life difficult at the Kaseya Center. The Heat have retooled their roster and brought in some serious scoring punch with Andrew Wiggins and Norman Powell, two veterans who know how to put the ball in the basket in a variety of ways. Wiggins brings the length, athleticism, and two-way versatility that Miami values, while Powell is one of the most efficient shot-makers in the league when he gets into a rhythm. Together, they give the Heat a wing scoring tandem that can torch you from all three levels.

And then there's Tyler Herro, who is getting back into the swing of things after dealing with fractured ribs. This is just his second game back, and while he might not be at 100% conditioning-wise, Herro's shot-making ability is undeniable. When he's on the court, Miami's offense gets another dimension of three-point shooting and pick-and-roll creation that makes them significantly harder to guard. The question is how many minutes he can handle and whether the rust factor limits his effectiveness, but even a 70% Tyler Herro is a problem for opposing defenses.

For Memphis, the challenge is enormous. They're a team that's lost its identity during this brutal stretch, and walking into Miami against a team that prides itself on toughness, conditioning, and defensive effort is a tough ask. The Heat culture demands maximum effort every night, and that's something Memphis hasn't been able to match consistently during their slide. If the Grizzlies can't match Miami's physicality and compete on the glass, this could get away from them quickly. Memphis needs a signature road win to stop the spiral, but the Kaseya Center is one of the toughest places in the league to find one.

Kings @ Spurs

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | Moody Center, Austin TX

This is the game of the night, and it isn't particularly close. The San Antonio Spurs are riding a scorching seven-game win streak and playing the best basketball of any team in the league right now, while the Sacramento Kings are trapped in a horrific 15-game losing streak that has completely destroyed their season. These two franchises are headed in completely opposite directions, and tonight's matchup at the Moody Center in Austin is going to be a showcase of just how dramatic that gap has become. Victor Wembanyama has turned the Spurs into must-watch television every single night, and this is a chance for San Antonio to flex in front of their home crowd against a team that can't get out of its own way.

The storyline that everyone will be watching is De'Aaron Fox suiting up for the Spurs against the Kings, the franchise that he called home for years before being traded to San Antonio. Fox has been an incredible fit alongside Wembanyama, giving the Spurs a backcourt partner who can push the pace, get to the rim at will, and create offense in the half-court when things break down. His speed and decisiveness have unlocked another dimension of the Spurs' offense, and opposing teams have struggled to deal with the combination of Fox's penetration and Wemby's ability to finish, shoot, and block shots. Tonight is personal for Fox, and you can bet he'll be motivated to put on a show against his former squad.

Sacramento, meanwhile, is living through a nightmare. A 15-game losing streak is the kind of thing that gets people fired, and the Kings have been unable to generate any positive momentum whatsoever. Without Fox running the show, Sacramento's offense has lost its primary engine, and the results speak for themselves. The roster that looked so promising in the preseason has been completely unable to adapt, and the defensive effort has been nonexistent on most nights. When you're losing at this rate, it seeps into everything, the practice court, the locker room, the fourth-quarter decision-making. The Kings are a broken team right now, and there's no quick fix on the horizon.

Wembanyama has been posting monster numbers all season, and his continued evolution as a two-way force of nature has the Spurs looking like legitimate Western Conference contenders. His shot-blocking, his three-point shooting, his playmaking from the elbow, all of it has been elite, and he's only getting better with each passing week. The Moody Center crowd is going to be absolutely electric for this one, and the Spurs have every reason to be confident that this win streak can extend to eight. For Sacramento, the question isn't really whether they can win tonight. It's whether they can show any signs of life at all against the best team they've faced in weeks. The talent gap between these two rosters right now is staggering, and this game should reflect that from tip to buzzer.

Game 6
ABC

Rockets @ Knicks

Saturday, 8:30 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York NY

Saturday night on ABC from the world's most famous arena. It doesn't get much better than this. The Houston Rockets bring one of the best records in the Western Conference to Madison Square Garden, where the New York Knicks will be waiting with a raucous crowd that turns the Garden into one of the loudest buildings in professional sports. This is the kind of regular-season game that feels like a playoff atmosphere, and both teams have the talent and the motivation to put on a show for the national television audience.

Houston has been one of the most impressive teams in the NBA this season, combining elite defense with a young core that plays with unrelenting energy and aggression. The Rockets' ability to suffocate opponents defensively while generating easy offense in transition has been the formula all year, and they've beaten enough good teams to prove that this isn't a mirage. Their depth is a weapon, their athleticism is suffocating, and they've got the kind of competitive fire that scares teams in late-game situations. Coming into the Garden on national TV is exactly the kind of spotlight game where Houston can make a statement.

The Knicks, of course, aren't going to just roll over for anybody at MSG. New York has been a strong Eastern Conference contender all season, and their combination of physicality, toughness, and half-court execution makes them a nightmare matchup for anyone. The Garden faithful expect a fight every night, and the Knicks' ability to grind out games with their defense and rebounding has been a hallmark of their identity. The crowd factor at MSG is real, especially for a Saturday night ABC game, and the energy from the building can swing momentum in the Knicks' favor during critical stretches.

This should be one of the best games on the entire Saturday slate, and it deserves the national spotlight. Houston's defensive intensity against New York's physical half-court style is a clash of identities that should produce a competitive, hard-fought game from start to finish. Both teams have the depth and the coaching to make adjustments on the fly, and the fourth quarter could be a chess match worth staying up for. The Rockets want to prove they belong among the league's elite, and there's no better place to do it than under the bright lights at Madison Square Garden. The Knicks, meanwhile, want to protect home court and send a message that they're not giving an inch to anyone. This one's going to be fun.
