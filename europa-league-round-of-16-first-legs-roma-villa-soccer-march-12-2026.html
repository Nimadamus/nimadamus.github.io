Europa League R16 First Legs

Soccer March 12, 2026

Thursday's soccer slate is headlined by four Europa League Round of 16 first legs. Bologna host Roma in an all-Italian affair, Lille welcome Aston Villa to France, Panathinaikos face Real Betis in Greece, and Celta Vigo take on Lyon in Spain. First legs are leverage matches, where composure and tactical discipline set the tone for the entire tie.

Match 1

Bologna vs Roma

Thursday, 12:45 PM ET | Europa League R16 First Leg

Thursday's Europa League round of 16 begins with a set of first legs that reward composure more than spectacle, and Bologna against Roma is a strong example. ESPN had Roma priced as the slight market lean for the opening leg in Bologna, with the total sitting at 2.5. That usually tells you the expected script: a more tactical match, fewer assumptions about open space, and a stronger chance that the tie remains alive deep into the second leg.

That is what makes this one attractive from a preview standpoint. Roma carries the larger

European profile, but first legs away from home are often about managing risk without giving away momentum. Bologna's opportunity is obvious. Use the home crowd, keep the structure tight, and make Roma work for every clean look. In these ties, one disciplined 90 minutes can be more valuable than trying to win the whole matchup in one night.

Match 2

Lille vs Aston Villa

Thursday, 3:00 PM ET | Europa League R16 First Leg

Lille and Aston Villa meet in another first leg that looks balanced on paper and in the market.

ESPN's schedule page listed Villa as a slight lean and kept the total at 2.5, which fits the sense that this tie could turn on which club controls the transitions rather than which one dominates the ball for long stretches. Lille at home will want the match to feel measured. Villa will want to find moments where its quality in the final third can make the difference.

That is why the first phase of the game matters so much. If Lille can settle the crowd and keep

Villa from countering into space, the French side can make this a chess match. If Villa starts winning second balls and creating fast attacks, the away goal pressure changes the emotional tone of the whole tie. First legs are rarely pure entertainment pieces. They are leverage matches, and this one should feel that way from the opening whistle.

Match 3

Panathinaikos vs Real Betis

Thursday, 12:45 PM ET | Europa League R16 First Leg

Panathinaikos against Real Betis looks like one of the more stylistically interesting ties of the night because it pairs a difficult away environment with a visiting team that usually wants to play rather than merely absorb. Betis was the market lean on the ESPN board, and again the posted total sat at 2.5, pointing toward a match where one clean spell of control could decide everything.

The preview lens here is pressure management. Panathinaikos at home will want to make the stadium matter and turn the match into a physical, emotional first leg. Betis will want to quiet that atmosphere with possession and patience. If the visitors can slow the game and keep the ball moving, they should feel good about their chance to bring the tie back home on favorable terms. If they get dragged into a frantic first hour, Panathinaikos has the kind of stage where momentum can become the biggest player on the field.

Match 4

Celta Vigo vs Lyon

Thursday, 3:00 PM ET | Europa League R16 First Leg

Celta Vigo and Lyon close the selected soccer slate as one of the later Europa League first legs, and the market had Celta as a narrow favorite at home. With the total again set at 2.5, the expectation is less about chaos and more about which side can own the cleaner attacking sequences. These are exactly the ties where one full-backs' duel, one set piece, or one lapse in buildup can end up defining the night.

That is what makes the preview worthwhile. Lyon has enough European experience to stay calm in a hostile road setting, but Celta gets the first chance to shape the tie in Spain and should want to make the game feel territorial. The home side does not need to force everything. It needs to spend enough time in the right areas to make Lyon defend with discomfort. For a first leg, that is often the entire job.
