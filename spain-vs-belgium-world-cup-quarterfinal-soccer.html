Spain vs Belgium: A World Cup Quarterfinal At SoFi Stadium

The tournament's stingiest defense meets one of its most dangerous attacks. Unbeaten Spain has not conceded a goal in five matches, Belgium arrives fresh off a 4-1 dismantling of the United States, and a semifinal against France is the prize. Verified form, key players and keys to victory below. Analysis only, no picks.

The best defensive story of the 2026 World Cup meets one of its most explosive attacks in the Inglewood afternoon. Spain arrives at SoFi Stadium unbeaten and, remarkably, uncrossed, having gone five matches without conceding a single goal, the tightest defensive run left in the tournament. Belgium comes in on a wave of momentum after taking the Round of 16 apart, and the winner books a semifinal date with France.

These are two of the most talented squads still standing, arriving from opposite emotional places. Spain has been methodical and suffocating, controlling matches and shutting the door. Belgium has been dramatic and dangerous, surviving one scare and then blowing the doors off the next opponent. That contrast in styles, control against chaos, is what makes this quarterfinal such a compelling watch.

Two Roads To The Last Eight

Spain topped Group H and has looked like a side built for a deep run, capping the group stage and then edging Portugal in a tense Round of 16 on a late Mikel Merino goal. The headline number is the clean sheets: five matches, zero goals conceded. A team that does not give up goals in knockout football is always a nightmare to eliminate.

Belgium's path has been far more turbulent and, lately, far more emphatic. After winning Group G, they needed a dramatic late rally to survive Senegal, scoring twice at the death to force extra time before winning it in the final moments. Then came the statement: a 4-1 demolition of the United States in the Round of 16, powered by a Charles De Ketelaere brace, that announced Belgium as a genuine contender rather than a lucky survivor.

Spain: Unbeaten And Uncrossed

Spain's identity is control. Manager Luis de la Fuente has his side dominating possession and squeezing the life out of opponents, with Pedri and Rodri orchestrating in midfield and a young, composed back line marshaled by teenage center back Pau Cubarsi alongside Aymeric Laporte, all protected by goalkeeper Unai Simon. That structure is the foundation of the five-match shutout streak.

The threat going forward is layered. Mikel Oyarzabal leads the team with four goals, 18-year-old winger Lamine Yamal is back to his best after an early-tournament injury, and Dani Olmo gives them another creator. When a side defends this well and still carries this much attacking talent, it is the profile of a tournament favorite.

Belgium: Firepower And A Comeback Streak

Belgium, under manager Domenico Tedesco, are all about their attacking weapons. Leandro Trossard has been the standout, chipping in goals and assists, and Charles De Ketelaere has caught fire at exactly the right time with two goals and an assist, including his brace against the USA. Add the pace of Jeremy Doku and this is an attack that can hurt anyone in transition.

The wild card is Kevin De Bruyne, who is expected back in the starting XI after being rested against the United States. He has not hit top form this tournament, but a player of his class against a Spain side that commits numbers forward is exactly the kind of match that can unlock him. If De Bruyne finds his range, Belgium's ceiling rises considerably.

Team News And Availability

The most significant absence is Belgium midfielder Amadou Onana, who is out for the rest of the tournament with a torn knee ligament, a real blow to the balance of their midfield. Up top, Romelu Lukaku is only fit enough for a bench role, leaving Tedesco to lean on his creators rather than a traditional target man from the start. Spain, by contrast, reported no injuries or suspensions in pre-match coverage, giving de la Fuente a fully stocked squad to choose from.

Keys To Victory: Spain

Keep doing what has worked. Spain's route is to dominate the ball, starve Belgium's dangerous forwards of transition opportunities, and protect the clean-sheet streak that has carried them this far. With Yamal and Oyarzabal capable of deciding tight games, the plan is control first and let the quality tell in the final third, while never letting Doku or De Bruyne spring a fast break.

Keys To Victory: Belgium

Break the seal early and ride the momentum. Belgium have to test that Spanish back line and find the goal that no one else has managed in five matches, ideally by catching Spain on the counter through Doku's pace and De Ketelaere's finishing. Getting De Bruyne on the ball in dangerous areas, and defending well enough without Onana to keep it level late, is the blueprint for the side that has thrived on drama.

What Is At Stake

The prize is enormous: the winner advances to a World Cup semifinal against France on July 14, after Les Bleus reached the last four by beating Morocco on July 9. For Spain, it is a chance to prove that the most complete team in the tournament can go all the way. For Belgium, it is a golden opportunity for a golden generation to finally break through on the biggest stage. Ninety minutes, and possibly extra time, separate one of these sides from the final four.

Final Thoughts

This is a heavyweight quarterfinal with a clear stylistic tension at its heart. Spain bring control, structure and a defense no one has solved, while Belgium bring firepower, momentum and the kind of match-winners who can crack any game open. If Spain impose their tempo, the shutout streak and their attacking depth make them formidable. If Belgium land an early blow and let their forwards run, the drama that has defined their tournament could strike again. Either way, SoFi Stadium should host one of the matches of the round.
