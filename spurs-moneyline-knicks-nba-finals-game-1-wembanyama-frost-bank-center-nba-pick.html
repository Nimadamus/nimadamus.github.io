Spurs Moneyline vs Knicks Game 1 Pick: Wembanyama Opens The Finals At Home June 3, 2026

Published June 3, 2026 | NBA Betting Pick | By BetLegend
Our Pick
San Antonio Spurs Moneyline
-185 | 3 units

The Finals Open In San Antonio

The official BetLegend play is the San Antonio Spurs moneyline at -185 for 3 units over the New York Knicks in Game 1 of the 2026 NBA Finals at Frost Bank Center, tipping at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. The market has the Spurs as 4.5-point favorites with the Knicks at +154 and the total at 218.5, and we are happy to lay the price with the best player in the series opening at home.

San Antonio earned this spot the hard way, going through the defending landscape of the West and beating the Thunder 111-103 in Game 7 of the Western Conference Finals in Oklahoma City. The Knicks swept the Cavaliers to win the East and reach their first Finals since 1999. Both teams belong here. Only one of them has Victor Wembanyama.

Wembanyama Is The Whole Equation

Wembanyama just went through a seven-game war with Oklahoma City averaging 27.3 points and 10.9 rebounds and walked away as the unanimous Western Conference Finals MVP. Across the entire playoff run he is putting up 23.2 points, 11.8 rebounds, and 3.5 blocks a night, which means he is dominating both ends of the floor at a level nobody in this series can answer. New York simply does not have a body to throw at him.

The rest spot matters too. The Spurs closed out Oklahoma City on May 30, so they come in with a few days off after the emotional Game 7, while New York has been waiting since its sweep ended. Long layoffs cut both ways, but San Antonio gets the better blend of rest without rust, and they get the first two games on their own floor.

Where The Value Sits

At -185 the Spurs need to win roughly 65 percent of the time to profit, and a home Game 1 with the most dominant two-way player in basketball is exactly where you accept that bar. Game 1s historically favor home favorites because the visitor is adjusting on the fly, and the Knicks have not faced anything resembling Wembanyama's rim presence in three rounds of Eastern Conference basketball. Cleveland did not prepare them for this.

Three units reflects conviction. This is the best player on the floor, at home, with the deeper recent test behind him. San Antonio survived a seven-game series against the West's best while New York was sweeping a softer path. Battle-tested beats well-rested in openers more often than not.

The Honest Counterpoint

The case for New York is real. A team that sweeps its conference final is playing elite basketball, the Knicks are loose with house money in their first Finals since 1999, and a long rest means fresh legs against a Spurs team that just emptied the tank in a Game 7. If New York controls the glass and turns this into a physical grind, +154 will look like a steal.

But the read is simple: when the series' best player is this much better than anyone else on the floor and he is opening at home, you lay the number. One transcendent talent decides close playoff games.

Final Verdict

The official play is the San Antonio Spurs moneyline at -185 for 3 units. Wembanyama is coming off a unanimous WCF MVP performance, the Spurs are home, and the Knicks have no answer for him inside. Take San Antonio in Game 1.

The Pick: Spurs ML (-185, 3 units)
