Why The Cubs Stay Quiet

Skenes is the entire reason this number is reachable. Even with a couple of bumpy starts mixed in, his 3.00 ERA, double-digit strikeout upside, and ability to work multiple times through a lineup without losing velocity make a Cubs four-spot a genuine lift. Chicago has to push at least one big inning to clear 3.5, and Skenes is built to deny exactly that. He lives in the strike zone, controls the run game, and keeps damage to solo shots when he gets touched.

What -145 Is Really Pricing

The -145 price is a notch firmer than the under in the first-five market, and it should be. With Skenes on the mound, the Pirates run-prevention scaffolding is at its sharpest before any bullpen leverage kicks in. The Cubs do not have to be shut out for this to cash; they have to be the lineup Skenes makes them into for six-plus innings, then run into a couple of high-leverage outs late. That is a much narrower path than chasing the full game total under and is the reason the sheet attached three units to it.

Risk Checklist

The risk is the same risk every team-total under carries against a frontline arm: one bad fastball locator in a key spot, or a Pittsburgh bullpen meltdown that hands Chicago a five-run inning after Skenes exits. The hedge in the design is that the bet is already paying juice; Cubs three runs or fewer is the realistic range against this matchup, and as long as Skenes finishes his outing without disaster, the under stays in striking distance.
