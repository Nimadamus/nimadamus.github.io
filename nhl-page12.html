Penguins @ Senators

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Canadian Tire Centre
Line: OTT -175 | O/U: 6.5 | PIT: 14-9-9 | OTT: 15-13-4

Crosby Watch: One Point From History

All eyes on Sidney Crosby tonight as he sits just one point shy of tying Mario Lemieux's franchise record of 1,723 regular-season points. Crosby currently has 1,722 points (644 goals, 1,078 assists) in 1,383 games. The 38-year-old legend has been on fire lately, recording points in five of his last six games (1 goal, 5 assists) and eight of his last 10 (6 goals, 6 assists). Remarkably, Crosby already passed Lemieux for the most total points (regular season and playoffs combined) in franchise history with 1,896 compared to Lemieux's 1,895.

Pittsburgh enters on a six-game losing streak despite producing offense at a respectable rate. Bryan Rust has scored in each of his last four appearances, tying for the league's longest active streak. The Penguins rank third in the NHL with a 31.11% power-play percentage, which could prove crucial against Ottawa's league-worst 70.45% penalty kill.

Ottawa has won seven of their last eight home games against Pittsburgh and claimed victory in their last four meetings at Canadian Tire Centre. Tim Stutzle continues to shine with 15 goals and 18 assists in 32 games. The historical trends favor Ottawa, but all hockey fans will be watching to see if Crosby makes franchise history in enemy territory.

Oilers @ Bruins

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | TD Garden
Line: EDM -141 | O/U: 6.5 | EDM: 16-12-6 | BOS: 20-14-0

The NHL's most electrifying offense visits TD Garden in a marquee matchup. Connor McDavid is playing the best hockey of his career, logging an astonishing 20 points in his last seven games while leading the league with 36 assists and ranking first overall with 56 points (20 goals, 36 assists). His linemate Leon Draisaitl recently celebrated his 1,000th career point and has accumulated 16 points over the same seven-game stretch.

The Oilers rank first in the NHL with a blistering 32.58% power-play conversion rate, and they'll face a Bruins team that leads the league in penalty minutes, potentially creating numerous man-advantage opportunities. Edmonton is coming off a 6-4 victory over Pittsburgh, where newly acquired goaltender Tristan Jarry won his second straight start since arriving from the Penguins.

Boston sits second in the Atlantic Division with a 12-5-0 home record. Jeremy Swayman has been solid in net with a 2.74 GAA and .909 save percentage. Edmonton has dominated this series recently, winning the past three meetings and claiming six of the last 10 head-to-head battles, including five straight victories at TD Garden. The Oilers are favored at -141 with the total set at 6.5 goals.

Wild @ Blue Jackets

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Nationwide Arena
Line: MIN -150 | O/U: 6.5 | MIN: 20-9-5 | CBJ: 14-13-6

The Minnesota Wild (20-9-5) bring a five-game winning streak into Nationwide Arena to face the struggling Columbus Blue Jackets (14-13-6). Minnesota is riding high after blanking the Washington Capitals 5-0 on Tuesday, showcasing the offensive firepower that has them third in the Central Division and just 10 points behind the NHL-best Colorado Avalanche.

Kirill Kaprizov continues to dazzle with 21 goals and 38 points in 34 games, recently scoring his 200th career NHL goal to become the fourth-fastest fifth-round-or-later draft pick to reach that milestone. His linemate Matthew Boldy matches his production with 38 points (19 goals, 19 assists), giving Minnesota one of the league's most dangerous one-two punches. The Wild's success has been built on elite goaltending from Filip Gustavsson and Jesper Wallstedt.

Columbus enters with only three wins in their last 10 games and the worst record in the Metropolitan Division. Zachary Werenski leads the team with 36 points, while Kirill Marchenko has contributed 26 points. The betting odds reflect Minnesota's momentum at -150 with a 52% win probability.

Blackhawks @ Canadiens

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Bell Centre
Line: MTL -160 | O/U: 6.5 | CHI: 13-14-6 | MTL: 17-12-4

The Blackhawks are reeling from a devastating blow - superstar center Connor Bedard was placed on injured reserve on December 15 with a separated shoulder sustained in the final seconds of a 3-2 loss to St. Louis. Bedard, who leads Chicago with 43 points (19 goals, 24 assists) in just 31 games, will miss the remainder of December and be reevaluated in January. Without their generational talent, the Blackhawks have lost 12 of their last 20 games and scored just 93 goals this season (24th in the NHL).

Montreal sits fourth in the Atlantic Division with 38 points, just three behind Detroit for the top spot. The Canadiens boast the league's fourth-best power play at 26.09% and have scored 104 goals (13th in NHL). Nicholas Suzuki leads the offense with 37 points (9 goals, 28 assists), while Cole Caufield has contributed 33 points (17 goals, 16 assists).

The Canadiens are -160 favorites at home with a 60% win probability. Montreal has won three straight meetings against Chicago and covered the puck line in each, including a 3-2 road victory on October 11.

Maple Leafs @ Capitals

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena
Line: WSH -154 | O/U: 5.5 | TOR: 15-12-5 | WSH: 18-11-4

Toronto has found their road legs, winning four of their last five away from Scotiabank Arena, including a 3-2 victory over Chicago on Tuesday where Auston Matthews, Oliver Ekman-Larsson, and Dakota Joshua each found the net. Matthews is heating up with goals in each of his last two games and three in his last four, bringing his season totals to 14 goals and 20 points in 24 games after missing five contests in November with a lower-body injury. William Nylander leads the Leafs with 36 points (11 goals, 25 assists).

Washington enters desperate for a bounce-back performance after dropping three straight games, including back-to-back shutout losses capped by a humiliating 5-0 defeat at Minnesota. This is a prime spot for the Capitals to respond at home, where they've been solid all season with a 10-5-2 record. Goaltender Logan Thompson has been outstanding with a 2.09 GAA and .922 save percentage, while Tom Wilson leads the attack with 17 goals and 32 points.

The Capitals have won the last three meetings between these teams, including a 4-2 home victory on November 28. Washington is favored at -154 with a 61% implied win probability.

Kings @ Lightning

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Amalie Arena
Line: TB -128 | O/U: 5.5 | LA: 14-9-9 | TB: 18-12-3

The Kings are mired in a four-game losing streak, most recently falling 3-2 to the Florida Panthers, and averaging just 2.58 goals per game (30th in the NHL) while maintaining strong defense at 2.55 goals against (2nd in NHL). Goaltender Pheonix Copley will get the start for Los Angeles, while the team deals with significant injuries: Darcy Kuemper is on injured reserve with an upper-body injury, and both Phillip Danault and Quinton Byfield are day-to-day.

Tampa Bay welcomes back star goaltender Andrei Vasilevskiy, who hasn't played since December 2. Defenseman Ryan McDonagh also returns after missing since November 8. Their availability is a massive boost for the Lightning, who rank eighth in goals scored at 3.21 per game and fifth in goals against at 2.64.

Los Angeles has struggled mightily against Tampa Bay historically, posting a dismal 3-13 record in their last 16 meetings and losing nine straight games at Amalie Arena. Tampa Bay is favored at -128 with most experts leaning toward the Lightning covering the -1.5 puck line.

Flyers @ Sabres

Thursday, 7:30 PM ET | KeyBank Center | ESPN
Line: BUF -136 | O/U: 6.5 | PHI: 17-9-6 | BUF: 14-14-4

ESPN Feature Game

Tonight's ESPN exclusive features the Philadelphia Flyers visiting the Buffalo Sabres in a rematch of their December 3 meeting, which Philadelphia won 5-2 as +110 home underdogs. The Flyers have been one of the season's surprise stories, sitting well above .500 despite losing star forward Tyson Foerster (10 goals, 3 assists) for the remainder of the regular season with an arm injury. Philadelphia's defensive structure has been excellent, allowing just 2.72 goals per game (8th in the NHL).

Trevor Zegras leads the team with 33 points (14 goals, 19 assists), while Travis Konecny has contributed 29 points. Goaltender Daniel Vladar has been stellar with a 2.41 GAA and .910 save percentage.

Buffalo welcomes back defenseman Michael Kesselring, who returns after missing 14 games with a lower-body injury. Tage Thompson leads the offense with 29 points (16 goals, 13 assists), and Rasmus Dahlin continues his excellent two-way play with 22 assists. Buffalo has won three straight including victories over Edmonton, Vancouver, and Seattle. The Sabres are favored at -136 at home with the total set at 6.5 goals.

Rangers @ Blues

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Enterprise Center
Line: NYR -142 | O/U: 6.5 | NYR: 16-15-4 | STL: 13-15-7

The second and final meeting of the season between these teams. New York won the first matchup 3-2 on November 24 and needs to build on their strong road record (12-5-1) after suffering a shutout loss and dropping four of their last five games. Despite the recent struggles, the Rangers have been far more reliable away from Madison Square Garden than at home.

Artemi Panarin drives the offense with a team-high 34 points (11 goals, 23 assists), while Igor Shesterkin remains one of the league's elite goaltenders with a 2.55 GAA and .908 save percentage. The Rangers are 13-5 against the spread in road games, making them a strong value play as -142 favorites.

St. Louis comes in riding positive momentum after a 1-0 home victory over Winnipeg, taking two of their last three contests. The Blues sit at 13-15-7 but have been dangerous at home, winning five straight matchups against the Rangers at Enterprise Center. Robert Thomas leads the team with 23 points. Despite being underdogs, the Blues have won 10 of 18 games as underdogs this season (55.6% upset rate).

Kraken @ Flames

Thursday, 9:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Saddledome
Line: CGY -178 | O/U: 5.5 | SEA: 12-13-6 | CGY: 13-17-4

The Seattle Kraken travel to Calgary desperately seeking to end their skid after losing nine of their last 10 games, most recently falling 5-3 to Colorado on Tuesday. The Kraken's injury situation is dire: Jared McCann, Berkly Catton, Jaden Schwartz, and Matt Murray are all out, while Brandon Montour is questionable. Seattle has mustered just 77 goals this season (32nd in the NHL) while allowing 95, ranking last in the league offensively.

The Calgary Flames enter off a disappointing 6-3 home loss to San Jose on Tuesday but have won four of their last six overall and claimed 10 of the last 15 head-to-head meetings against Seattle. Despite ranking 32nd in the league with a 13.76% power-play conversion rate, the Flames possess enough offensive firepower to exploit Seattle's defensive weaknesses.

Calgary is -178 favorites at home with expert predictions largely favoring the Flames in a 3-2 or 4-3 victory. The Kraken's brutal recent form makes Calgary the clear side, though Seattle's offensive struggles could lead to an under on the 5.5 total.

Stars @ Sharks

Thursday, 10:00 PM ET | SAP Center
Line: DAL -190 | O/U: 6.0 | DAL: 22-7-5 | SJ: 17-14-3

Celebrini Watch

The Dallas Stars close out Wednesday's slate with a late-night visit to face the surprising San Jose Sharks. Dallas enters as one of the NHL's elite teams, ranking fourth in offense at 3.29 goals per game and fifth in defense at 2.59 goals against. The Stars boast the league's second-best power play at 31.4% and have won 15 of their last 20 games. Mikko Rantanen has been sensational with 44 points (14 goals, 30 assists), while Jason Robertson has contributed 40 points. Jake Oettinger (14-6-2) has been solid in net with a 2.5 GAA.

San Jose has been the feel-good story of the season thanks largely to 19-year-old phenom Macklin Celebrini, who ranks third in league scoring with 51 points (18 goals, 33 assists). Celebrini is on pace for 118 points, which would make him the first teenager to reach that mark since 19-year-old Sidney Crosby posted 120 in 2006-07. He's coming off a monster four-point performance (2 goals, 2 assists) in a 6-3 victory over Calgary and became the fastest Sharks player to reach 50 points.

Dallas is favored at -190 but the Sharks have lost seven straight against Dallas and eight of the last nine meetings. San Jose's current form and Celebrini's brilliance make them live underdogs in what should be an entertaining offensive showcase.
