Liverpool @ Arsenal

2026-01-08T20:00Z | Emirates Stadium | Peacock
Line: ARS -170 | O/U: 2.5 | LIV: 10-4-6 | ARS: 15-3-2

Everyone's circling Arsenal as the Premier League's golden boys, but I'm seeing a team that's been living off reputation while Liverpool quietly gets written off despite being just five points back with a game in hand.

The revenge narrative here cuts both ways - Arsenal's still nursing wounds from last season's title collapse, while Liverpool remembers getting embarrassed 3-1 at Anfield earlier this year. But here's what the public's missing: Arsenal's home fortress isn't as impenetrable as the 15-3-2 record suggests. They've been grinding out narrow wins against inferior opposition while Liverpool's road form has been criminally underrated.

Klopp's side thrives as underdogs, and getting +170 odds at Emirates feels like free money. Arsenal's defensive vulnerabilities get exposed when facing elite attacking pace, and Liverpool's front three is hitting peak form at the perfect time.

The line screams "bet Arsenal because they're at home and have a better record." I'm doing the opposite.

**Take: Liverpool +170**

Cagliari @ Cremonese

Line: CRE +140 | O/U: 1.5 | CAG: 4-6-8 | CRE: 5-6-7

Listen, both these teams are garbage right now - 0% win rates away and at home tell you everything. But here's what matters: Cremonese is desperate at Giovanni Zini. When you're this bad, home crowd becomes your lifeline, not just background noise.

I've been in those spots where the fans are the only thing keeping you upright. The bookies have Cremonese as underdogs at +140, but that's disrespectful to what home desperation can do.

**Take Cremonese +140 straight up.** Sometimes the worst teams play their best football when their backs are against the wall at home.

Milan's -225 pricing is justified but offers no value. The total at 2.5 is where the action lives. With Milan's attacking urgency at home and Genoa's defensive fragility on the road, goals should flow.
