Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

The Gasparilla Bowl in Tampa pits two 8-5 teams with very different identities. Memphis runs one of the fastest offenses in college football - they get to the line, they snap the ball, and they dare you to keep up. NC State is more methodical, grinding out possessions and wearing down opponents. This clash of styles makes for an intriguing matchup.

Seth Henigan has been the catalyst for Memphis all year. The dude can sling it - over 3,400 yards through the air and he makes tough throws look routine. The Tigers spread you out, attack the middle of the field, and create mismatches with their speed. When they get rolling, they're almost impossible to stop. The question is whether NC State can disrupt their rhythm early.

The Wolfpack counter with a stout defensive front that can get after the quarterback. If NC State can collapse the pocket and force Henigan into quick throws, they neutralize a lot of Memphis's explosiveness. On offense, the Wolfpack need to sustain long drives and keep the Tigers' offense on the sideline. Ball control is going to be crucial. Raymond James Stadium should have a nice bowl crowd for what promises to be an entertaining game.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

The Celebration Bowl in Atlanta is more than just a football game - it's a celebration of HBCU excellence. South Carolina State represents the MEAC while Prairie View A&M carries the banner for the SWAC. The atmosphere at Mercedes-Benz Stadium is going to be electric, with marching bands, passionate fans, and two programs giving everything they've got.

SC State at 10-3 has been built on defensive dominance. They swarm to the ball, they create turnovers, and they make life miserable for opposing offenses. Their ability to get stops in crucial situations has been the difference in close games all year. On offense, they don't need to be spectacular - they just need to be efficient and avoid turnovers.

Prairie View at 10-4 brings more firepower offensively. The Panthers can score in bunches when their playmakers get the ball in space. Their speed at the skill positions creates problems for defenses, and they're not afraid to take shots downfield. If they can stay disciplined and not give SC State short fields, they've got the weapons to win this game. This is going to be a dogfight with incredible energy from start to finish.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

SoFi Stadium hosts a fascinating bowl matchup between Boise State and Washington. The Broncos may be 9-5 but everyone knows about Ashton Jeanty - the Heisman finalist has been otherworldly this season with over 2,400 rushing yards. Washington at 9-4 has had a solid Big Ten campaign and they want to end on a high note with a bowl win.

Everything for Boise State runs through Jeanty. When he's cooking, the Broncos are one of the most dangerous teams in the country. He breaks tackles, he finds creases, and he can turn a 3-yard gain into a 30-yard gain in a blink. Washington's defensive front is going to be tested in ways they haven't been all season. Can they consistently tackle the most elusive back in college football?

The Huskies need to make this a shootout. Their passing game has been solid and they've got playmakers on the outside who can win one-on-one matchups. If Washington can jump out to an early lead and force Boise State to throw more than they want to, that's a win. But if this turns into a grind-it-out running game, Jeanty is going to feast. The total is set high for a reason - expect points in this one.

Trojans @ Gamecocks

2025-12-17T02:00Z | Cramton Bowl | ESPN
TROY: 8-6 | JVST: 9-5

Sun Belt rivals clash in the Camellia Bowl, and you know these teams are going to go at each other. Troy at 8-6 has had moments of brilliance mixed with frustrating losses. Jacksonville State at 9-5 continues to impress in their FBS transition. The Cramton Bowl in Montgomery provides a perfect setting for this regional showdown.

Troy's been here before - they know what bowl games are about and they've got experienced players who can handle the moment. Their offense has playmakers at the skill positions and a quarterback who can make plays with his arm and his legs. When they're clicking, they can hang with anyone. The issue has been consistency.

Jacksonville State has been the feel-good story of the Sun Belt. The Gamecocks have embraced the challenge of FBS football and they've exceeded expectations. Their defense plays with an edge and they're physical at every level. If they can control the line of scrimmage and make Troy one-dimensional, they'll be in great shape. This game is going to be won in the trenches by whoever can impose their will.

Monarchs @ Bulls

2025-12-17T22:00Z | Camping World Stadium | ESPN
ODU: 10-3 | USF: 9-4

The Cure Bowl at Camping World Stadium features two programs heading in the right direction. Old Dominion at 10-3 has been one of college football's best stories - they've beaten quality teams and done it the right way. USF at 9-4 has found stability and they're building something sustainable in Tampa. This should be a quality matchup.

ODU is going to try to impose their will in the trenches. The Monarchs win with physicality - they run the ball effectively, they control the clock, and they play smash-mouth football. Their offensive line has been dominant all season and they're not going to change who they are for this game. If USF can't stop the run, they're in trouble.

South Florida has more speed at the skill positions. The Bulls can hit big plays when their playmakers get the ball in space, and they've got a quarterback who can make defenses pay for mistakes. Their defensive front needs to hold up against ODU's physical attack - if they can force third-and-longs, they can get off the field and create opportunities. This is a classic strength vs. speed matchup and it should be entertaining.

Cajuns @ Hens

2025-12-18T01:40Z | Hancock Whitney Stadium | ESPN
UL: 6-7 | DEL: 7-6

An interesting cross-divisional matchup in Mobile as Louisiana from the Sun Belt takes on Delaware from FCS. The Ragin' Cajuns at 6-7 didn't have the season they hoped for, but they've got pride and they want to end on a positive note. Delaware at 7-6 is making the leap to face an FBS opponent and they're treating this like their Super Bowl.

Louisiana should have the talent advantage here, but bowl games are about motivation and preparation. The Cajuns have been inconsistent all year and there's a real question about where their heads are at. If they come out flat and disrespected, Delaware is going to punch them in the mouth early. The Blue Hens aren't going to be intimidated.

Delaware's been battle-tested in FCS play and they're going to bring everything they've got. Their defense has been solid all season and they're physical enough to compete. For the Cajuns, this has to be about execution from the opening whistle. No slow starts, no turnovers, no giving Delaware life. If Louisiana plays to their potential, they win. But that's been a big if all season long.

Bears @ Wolves

2025-12-19T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN2
MOST: 7-6 | ARST: 7-6

Two 7-6 teams meet at The Star in Frisco for the Xbox Bowl, and this should be a competitive game from start to finish. Missouri State from the Missouri Valley Conference takes on Arkansas State from the Sun Belt. Neither team had the season they wanted, but a bowl win can salvage a lot and provide momentum into the offseason.

Missouri State is making noise in their first FBS bowl appearance. The Bears have earned this spot with tough, physical play all season. They're not going to wow you with flashy plays, but they execute their assignments and they don't beat themselves. That disciplined approach can be the difference in a close game against a team you've never faced.

Arkansas State has the athletes and the experience at this level. The Red Wolves know what bowl games are about and they've got playmakers who can change games. Their issue has been putting together complete performances - too many mental errors and lapses in concentration. If they clean that up, they should win. But Missouri State is going to make them earn every single yard. Expect a physical, low-scoring affair.

Tide @ Sooners

2025-12-20T01:00Z | Memorial Stadium (Norman, OK) | ABC, ESPN
ALA: 11-3 | OU: 10-3

CFP First Round football at its finest. Alabama traveling to Norman to face Oklahoma is the kind of matchup college football fans dream about. The Crimson Tide at 11-3 have championship pedigree - they've been here before, they've won these games, and they know what it takes. Oklahoma at 10-3 is hosting their first ever playoff game and the atmosphere is going to be absolutely insane.

Alabama doesn't flinch in hostile environments. They've played in the biggest games on the biggest stages for decades, and that experience is invaluable. Their defense has been elite all season, creating pressure and forcing turnovers. On offense, they've got the balance to attack you in multiple ways. Nick Saban's successor has continued the winning tradition and the Tide are dangerous.

Oklahoma's crowd is going to be the loudest it's ever been. The Sooners are desperate to prove they belong among the elite, and their fans are going to will them to victory. Their offensive line has been a strength and if they can protect the quarterback and establish the run, they can hang with anyone. The key is handling the pressure - playoff football is different, and inexperience can be costly. This is appointment television.

Owls @ Broncos

2025-12-19T16:00Z | Brooks Stadium (SC) | ESPN
KENN: 10-4 | WMU: 10-4

The Myrtle Beach Bowl at Brooks Stadium features a fascinating stylistic clash. Kennesaw State at 10-4 runs that unique triple-option attack that's a nightmare to prepare for in limited bowl practice time. Western Michigan at 10-4 has had a strong MAC campaign and they want to end on a high note.

Kennesaw State's offense is unlike anything Western Michigan has seen all year. The Owls run the ball on almost every single play and they dare you to stop it. Their option scheme requires precise discipline from the defense - one wrong read and it's a big gain. The Broncos are going to need their linebackers to play their best game of the season.

Western Michigan's traditional offense should provide a contrast that favors them if they can stay in a shootout. The Broncos can throw the ball around and they've got playmakers who can create separation. If they can score early and force Kennesaw to abandon their methodical approach, that's a huge advantage. The key is winning the time of possession battle - whoever controls the clock likely wins this game.

Dukes @ Ducks

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Autzen Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
JMU: 12-2 | ORE: 12-1

David meets Goliath at Autzen Stadium. James Madison at 12-2 has been the Cinderella story of college football, going from FCS dynasty to legit FBS contender in record time. Oregon at 12-1 is one of the most complete teams in the country and they're playing at home where they've been nearly unbeatable. This is going to be an incredible atmosphere.

Oregon's offense is a well-oiled machine. They've got speed, they've got playmakers, and they've got a quarterback who can make all the throws. Their offensive line gives him time to work and their skill players create matchup nightmares. On defense, they're opportunistic and aggressive. The Ducks are heavy favorites for a reason.

But don't count out JMU. These Dukes play with an edge and a belief that they belong with anyone in the country. They've beaten good teams to get here and they're not going to be intimidated by the stage. Their quarterback is fearless, their defense swarms, and they genuinely believe they can pull the upset. Is it likely? No. But JMU has earned the right to prove themselves, and they're not going out quietly. The Ducks better bring their A-game.

Wave @ Rebels

CFP First Round action at Vaught-Hemingway Stadium as Ole Miss hosts Tulane in a rematch of their September meeting. The Rebels won that one 45-10, and while Tulane has improved since then, the talent gap remains significant. Ole Miss at 12-1 is one of the most explosive teams in the country, and they're playing at home with everything on the line.

Lane Kiffin has this offense humming. Jaxson Dart has been phenomenal all season, making throws that most quarterbacks can't even attempt. The skill position players have elite speed and playmaking ability, and the offensive line gives Dart time to operate. When Ole Miss gets rolling, they're almost impossible to stop. The Rebels smell a deep playoff run.

Tulane at 11-3 deserves credit for an excellent season, but they're simply outmatched here. The Green Wave don't have the athletes to match up with Ole Miss's playmakers, and their defense got shredded in the first meeting. Maybe they've made adjustments, maybe they'll be more competitive, but asking them to overcome a 35-point deficit from their first meeting is a lot. The atmosphere in Oxford is going to be electric. This should be a Rebels showcase.

Hurricanes @ Aggies

2025-12-20T17:00Z | Kyle Field | ABC, ESPN
MIA: 11-2 | TA&M: 11-2

The 12th Man is going to be fully unleashed at Kyle Field for this CFP First Round showdown. Texas A&M at 11-2 hosts Miami at 11-2 in a matchup between two programs that have been waiting for this moment. The Aggies are finally back in the playoff conversation, and the Hurricanes are proving "The U is back" isn't just a slogan anymore.

Cam Ward has been the story of Miami's season. The transfer quarterback has put up Heisman-caliber numbers and he's got weapons all around him. The Hurricanes can score in a hurry and their offense has carried them through tough games all season. But Kyle Field at night is a different animal - the crowd noise can be deafening and it disrupts communication.

Texas A&M's defense has been the strength of this team all year. They've got NFL talent at every level and they're going to make Ward uncomfortable. The Aggies live for these primetime moments and their home field advantage is real. If they can rattle Ward early and get the crowd into it, this could get out of hand. But if Miami survives the initial storm and keeps scoring, their offense can match anyone. This is going to be an absolute battle.

I can't wait.

Cougars @ Aggies

The LA Bowl at SoFi Stadium brings together two 6-6 programs looking to salvage their seasons with a bowl win. Washington State has navigated unprecedented challenges after the Pac-12 fell apart, and getting to a bowl game at all is an accomplishment. Utah State has had an inconsistent Mountain West campaign but they're excited to play on this stage.

Washington State's air raid offense is going to put the ball in the air 50+ times. That's just who they are. The Cougars have receivers who can win one-on-one matchups and a quarterback who will take shots downfield. The risk is turnovers - when you throw it that much, bad things can happen. If they protect the football, they've got the weapons to outscore anyone.

Utah State being a slight home favorite tells you this is a coin flip. The Aggies have a more balanced approach that could grind down Washington State if they control the tempo. Their defense has created turnovers at opportune times, and if they can force the Cougars into mistakes, that's the ballgame. This should be a fun, high-scoring affair with plenty of big plays on both sides.
