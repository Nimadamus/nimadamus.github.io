Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

Two 8-5 squads collide in a Pop Tarts Bowl matchup that should produce some fireworks. Memphis brings one of the most explosive offenses in the Group of Five, averaging over 35 points per game and relying on a dynamic passing attack. NC State's defense has been stingy at times this season but has also shown inconsistency, particularly against mobile quarterbacks and up-tempo schemes.

The Tigers' ability to stretch the field vertically could be the difference here. NC State will need their run game to control the clock and keep Memphis' offense on the sideline. This has all the makings of a high-scoring affair with both teams looking to end their seasons on a positive note heading into the offseason.

Bears @ Warriors

2025-12-25T01:00Z | Clarence T.C. Ching Athletics Complex | ESPN
CAL: 7-5 | HAW: 8-4

The Hawaii Bowl is always a unique atmosphere, and Cal faces a tough road trip to the islands against a Hawai'i team that's exceeded expectations this year. The Rainbow Warriors' 8-4 record represents one of their best seasons in recent memory, and they'll have the home crowd fully behind them for this Christmas Eve matchup.

Cal's transition to the ACC hasn't been seamless, but they've shown flashes of competitiveness. The key for the Bears will be handling the long travel and the emotional environment. Hawai'i thrives in these late-night games at Clarence T.C. Ching Complex, and opposing teams often struggle with the atmosphere and time zone challenges. Expect the Warriors to come out swinging early.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

The Celebration Bowl showcases the best of HBCU football, and this matchup between South Carolina State and Prairie View A&M promises to deliver. Both programs have had outstanding seasons, with SC State's 10-3 record representing MEAC dominance while Prairie View's 10-4 mark shows SWAC excellence. The atmosphere at Mercedes-Benz Stadium will be electric.

SC State brings a physical, ground-and-pound approach that's been effective all season. Prairie View counters with a balanced attack that can strike through the air or on the ground. In big games like this, turnovers and special teams often decide the outcome. Whichever team protects the ball and wins the field position battle should come away with the trophy.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

Boise State vs Washington is a fascinating clash of styles at SoFi Stadium. The Broncos have been the class of the Mountain West for years, and their 9-5 record includes some quality wins. Washington, adjusting to life post-Kalen DeBoer, has had an up-and-down campaign but still boasts serious talent, especially on defense.

The Broncos' running game has been their bread and butter, and they'll look to establish it early against a Huskies defense that can be vulnerable between the tackles. Washington needs to generate a pass rush and force Boise State into uncomfortable third-and-long situations. This should be a physical, low-scoring affair with the winner likely controlling time of possession.

Trojans @ Gamecocks

2025-12-17T02:00Z | Cramton Bowl | ESPN
TROY: 8-6 | JVST: 9-5

Sun Belt Conference rivals meet in the Camellia Bowl, and this one has some extra spice. Troy and Jacksonville State have developed a solid rivalry, and both programs take pride in being among the best in the Group of Five. The Trojans' 8-6 record belies a team that's been competitive in almost every game, while Jacksonville State's 9-5 mark shows continued program growth.

Troy's defense has been the story of their season - aggressive, opportunistic, and capable of creating turnovers at key moments. Jacksonville State counters with a balanced offense that doesn't beat itself. The neutral-site setting at Cramton Bowl should provide an intimate atmosphere. Expect a tight, physical game that could come down to the final possession.

Monarchs @ Bulls

2025-12-17T22:00Z | Camping World Stadium | ESPN
ODU: 10-3 | USF: 9-4

The Gasparilla Bowl features an Old Dominion team that's had a remarkable turnaround season. At 10-3, the Monarchs have exceeded all expectations and look to cap things off with a bowl victory. South Florida, meanwhile, has been steady under Alex Golesh, building a program that's becoming increasingly competitive in the AAC.

ODU's offensive line has been excellent all season, creating lanes for their running backs and keeping their quarterback clean. USF needs to find a way to generate pressure and disrupt timing in the passing game. Camping World Stadium should have solid attendance with both fanbases making the trip to Orlando. This could be the game of the early bowl season.

Cajuns @ Hens

2025-12-18T01:40Z | Hancock Whitney Stadium | ESPN
UL: 6-7 | DEL: 7-6

An interesting cross-divisional matchup as FBS Louisiana takes on FCS powerhouse Delaware. The Ragin' Cajuns limped into bowl eligibility at 6-7, but postseason games offer a chance to end the season on a positive note. Delaware, meanwhile, has been solid all year and won't be intimidated by the FBS competition.

Louisiana needs to establish their ground game early and avoid the kind of turnovers that plagued them in their losses. Delaware has playmakers who can take advantage of mistakes, and their coaching staff will have this team well-prepared. Don't sleep on the Blue Hens - they're capable of pulling the upset if Louisiana takes them lightly.

Bears @ Wolves

2025-12-19T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN2
MOST: 7-6 | ARST: 7-6

Two 7-6 teams meet at Ford Center at The Star in a matchup of evenly matched squads. Missouri State has made strides in the Missouri Valley, while Arkansas State continues to compete in the Sun Belt. Both programs are looking to build momentum heading into the offseason, and a bowl win would do wonders for recruiting.

This game will likely come down to execution in key moments. Missouri State's offensive line has been inconsistent, which could be a problem against Arkansas State's active front seven. The Red Wolves, however, have had their own issues protecting the quarterback. Whichever team wins the turnover battle and converts in the red zone should emerge victorious.

Tide @ Sooners

2025-12-20T01:00Z | Memorial Stadium (Norman, OK) | ABC, ESPN
ALA: 11-3 | OU: 10-3

A pair of blue-blood programs meet in what feels like a New Year's Six-caliber matchup. Alabama's 11-3 record includes some signature wins, though the Tide have also shown vulnerability at times this season. Oklahoma, in their second SEC season, has found their footing with a 10-3 mark that suggests real progress under Brent Venables.

The key matchup to watch is Alabama's offensive line versus Oklahoma's front seven. The Sooners have been disruptive all season, generating pressure and stopping the run. Alabama counters with elite skill position talent that can make plays in space. This should be a physical, old-school SEC battle with big implications for program momentum heading into 2026 recruiting.

Owls @ Broncos

2025-12-19T16:00Z | Brooks Stadium (SC) | ESPN
KENN: 10-4 | WMU: 10-4

The Myrtle Beach Bowl features two 10-4 programs looking to cap impressive seasons with a victory. Kennesaw State's transition to FBS has been remarkably smooth, with the Owls showing they can compete at this level. Western Michigan, meanwhile, has been one of the MAC's best stories, building on consistent success.

Kennesaw State's triple-option scheme will present challenges for a Western Michigan defense that doesn't see this look often. The Owls control the clock and limit possessions, which can frustrate explosive offenses. WMU needs to score when they have the ball because opportunities may be limited. This should be a fun, contrasting-styles matchup.

Dukes @ Ducks

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Autzen Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
JMU: 12-2 | ORE: 12-1

James Madison's incredible rise continues with a Fiesta Bowl appearance against one of the nation's elite programs. The Dukes' 12-2 record speaks to continued excellence in their transition to FBS, while Oregon's 12-1 mark has them knocking on the door of the College Football Playoff. This is a major opportunity for JMU to prove they belong.

Oregon's speed and depth should be significant advantages, but JMU won't be intimidated. The Dukes have a proven winning culture and believe they can compete with anyone. Oregon needs to avoid looking ahead and respect this opponent. If JMU can hang around early and make this a four-quarter game, Autzen Stadium could get nervous.

Wave @ Rebels

2025-12-20T20:30Z | Vaught-Hemingway Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
TULN: 11-3 | MISS: 12-1

The Sugar Bowl brings Tulane back to New Orleans for a showdown with SEC powerhouse Ole Miss. The Green Wave's 11-3 record caps another strong season under Willie Fritz, while Ole Miss' 12-1 mark represents one of the best seasons in program history. Playing in the Superdome with a local crowd should give Tulane extra juice.

Ole Miss has been explosive offensively all season, averaging over 40 points per game. Tulane's defense will need to bend but not break, keeping the Rebels out of the end zone and settling for field goals. On offense, the Green Wave need to control the clock and limit Ole Miss' possessions. It's a tall order, but the atmosphere could create chaos.

Hurricanes @ Aggies

2025-12-20T17:00Z | Kyle Field | ABC, ESPN
MIA: 11-2 | TA&M: 11-2

Two 11-2 powerhouses clash in what might be the best matchup of the bowl season. Miami has been electric under Mario Cristobal, with a high-powered offense that can score from anywhere on the field. Texas A&M, playing at Kyle Field, will have the 12th Man behind them in what promises to be a hostile environment for the Hurricanes.

This game features two of the better defensive lines in the country, so the trenches will be critical. Miami needs to protect their quarterback against A&M's pass rush, while the Aggies must contain Miami's playmakers in space. Whoever wins the turnover battle likely wins the game. Expect a physical, intense contest with playoff implications for both programs.

Grizzlies @ Bobcats

2025-12-20T21:00Z | Bobcat Stadium (MT) | ABC
MONT: 13-2 | MTST: 13-2

The Brawl of the Wild takes on championship implications as Montana and Montana State meet with FCS title aspirations on the line. Both programs enter at 13-2, representing the best of Big Sky football. This rivalry needs no extra motivation, but with everything on the line, expect an absolutely electric atmosphere in Bozeman.

Montana State's home-field advantage could be significant in this one. The Bobcats have been dominant at home all season, and their physical running game wears opponents down. Montana counters with a balanced attack and a defense that creates turnovers. In a game this tight between evenly matched rivals, special teams and turnovers will likely decide the outcome.

Redbirds @ Wildcats

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Villanova Stadium | ESPN2
ILST: 12-4 | VILL: 12-3

Two FCS playoff stalwarts meet in a matchup featuring teams with nearly identical records. Illinois State's 12-4 mark shows consistent excellence in the Missouri Valley, while Villanova's 12-3 record represents CAA dominance. Both programs have deep playoff experience and know how to win in December.

Illinois State's ground game has been their calling card all season, controlling the clock and wearing down opposing defenses. Villanova needs to get off the field on third down and create short-field opportunities for their offense. This should be a classic FCS playoff battle - physical, low-scoring, and decided in the final quarter. Experience and composure will be the difference.
