Utah Mammoth @ Detroit Red Wings

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena | TNT
Line: DET -125 | O/U: 6.5 | DET: 19-12-3 | UTA: 16-16-3

Alex DeBrincat has been absolutely ridiculous in December, and tonight he gets a nationally televised stage to keep the momentum rolling. He became the fastest Red Wing to reach 20 goals since Henrik Zetterberg in 2007-08, and he's currently on pace for a 49-goal season. DeBrincat has four goals and an assist in his last two games, and 13 points in eight games this month. Tuesday night he scored twice in a 3-2 win over the Islanders, moving into first place in the Atlantic Division.

Detroit has climbed from two points out of a playoff spot on November 28 into first place in the Atlantic by going 6-1-2 over their last nine games. The turnaround has been real. DeBrincat leads with 9 power play goals (second in the NHL), and the whole team seems to have found another gear. They've collected points in 8 of 10 games, and the Little Caesars Arena crowd is buzzing again.

Utah comes in as a .500 team trying to stay relevant in the Western Conference playoff race. The Mammoth have been inconsistent all season, and a trip to face a rolling Detroit squad isn't ideal timing. This has the feel of a Red Wings win that keeps their hot streak alive.

Los Angeles Kings @ Florida Panthers

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Amerant Bank Arena
Line: FLA -148 | O/U: 5.5 | FLA: 17-13-2 | LA: 14-9-9

The back-to-back Stanley Cup champions are heating up at the right time. Florida has won five of their last six games, and Sam Reinhart has been the catalyst. He scored twice Sunday against Tampa Bay and has 8 points in his last 6 games (3 goals, 5 assists). Reinhart leads the team with 17 goals and 32 points in 32 games, and he's cracked the scoresheet in 18 of his last 23 games. The consistency is remarkable.

Los Angeles is a solid team with a lot of ties, sitting at 14-9-9 with 37 points through 32 games. They're maddeningly consistent but rarely dominant. Florida at home is a different animal, especially with the championship pedigree they've built over the last two seasons. The Panthers finished 3-1-0 on their recent road trip and look ready to make a push up the standings.

Carolina Hurricanes @ Nashville Predators

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Bridgestone Arena
Line: CAR -180 | O/U: 6.5 | CAR: 21-9-2 | NSH: 13-15-4

The Carolina Hurricanes are riding a four-game winning streak and hold the top spot in the Eastern Conference, and rookie goaltender Brandon Bussi is the biggest reason why. Bussi tied the NHL record for most wins in a goaltender's first 10 NHL games with 9 victories, joining Igor Shesterkin, Frederik Andersen, Bob Froese, and Frank Brimsek. During his 9-game winning streak, Bussi posted a 1.95 GAA and .911 save percentage. He's 11-1-0 overall and playing with supreme confidence.

Nashville has been a disappointment this season at 13-15-4. The Predators made big offseason moves but haven't been able to put it together. Playing at home against a hot Carolina team that's won three straight via shootout isn't an ideal spot. The Hurricanes have the kind of depth and defensive structure that gives Nashville problems, and the way Bussi is playing right now, scoring goals against Carolina is going to be difficult.

Winnipeg Jets @ St. Louis Blues

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Enterprise Center
Line: WPG -142 | O/U: 5.5 | WPG: 15-15-2 | STL: 12-15-7

This is a first-round playoff rematch from last season, when the Jets rallied to beat the Blues 4-3 in double overtime of Game 7. Jordan Binnington and Connor Hellebuyck could face each other tonight for the first time since that epic series. Both goalies were incredible in that playoff matchup, and there's legitimate bad blood between these Central Division rivals.

Neither team has lived up to expectations this season. Winnipeg started red-hot but has cooled off significantly to 15-15-2. St. Louis is struggling at 12-15-7 and looking like a team that might be sellers at the trade deadline. But divisional games have their own energy, especially when there's recent playoff history involved. This feels like a game where both teams will play above their recent level just because of what happened last April.

New Jersey Devils @ Vegas Golden Knights

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | T-Mobile Arena
Line: VGK -185 | O/U: 5.5 | VGK: 16-6-9 | NJ: 18-14-1

Mitch Marner has been steadily finding his game in Vegas, and his playmaking numbers are starting to pop. He became the fastest Golden Knight to reach 20 assists in franchise history (25 games), and he's second on the team in scoring behind Jack Eichel with 32 points (6G, 26A). His three-assist game against Columbus on Saturday was his first with Vegas, and he's looking more comfortable in the system every night.

Vegas owns the best points percentage in the league for December at .917 (5-0-1). They're playing their best hockey of the season at the perfect time. New Jersey is solid at 18-14-1 but faces a difficult road trip to the desert. The Golden Knights are heavy favorites at home, where they've been nearly unbeatable this month. Carter Hart has been reliable in net, extending his point streak to four games against Columbus.
