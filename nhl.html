Jackets @ Senators

2025-12-30T00:00Z | Canadian Tire Centre | ESPN+, FanDuel SN OH
Line: OTT -205 | O/U: 6.5 | CBJ: 16-15-6 | OTT: 18-14-5

The Blue Jackets continue their road trip in Canada's capital, and this one feels like a sneaky Over spot. Columbus has been scoring at will lately, putting up 3.4 goals per game over their last 10, but they've also been leaking goals at an alarming rate. Goaltending has been inconsistent for both squads, with neither starter inspiring much confidence right now.

Ottawa's offense has found its groove at home, where they're averaging 3.2 goals per game. Tim Stutzle is having a breakout campaign with 42 points already, and Brady Tkachuk continues to be a menace in front of the net. The Senators are 5-2 in their last 7 at Canadian Tire Centre, and they need to keep banking points to stay in the wild card hunt.

Don't overthink this one - take the Senators at home. They're the better team, they're playing with urgency, and the Blue Jackets have won just twice in their last 8 road games. Ottawa's puck luck has to turn around eventually, and this feels like the spot.

Capitals @ Panthers

2025-12-30T00:00Z | Amerant Bank Arena | ESPN+, MNMT
Line: FLA -130 | O/U: 5.5 | WSH: 20-13-5 | FLA: 20-15-2

This is a massive measuring stick game for both teams. The Capitals are one of the league's biggest surprises this season, with Alex Ovechkin's pursuit of Wayne Gretzky's goal record capturing headlines before his recent injury. Even without the Great 8, Washington has found ways to win, going 4-2 in games he's missed. Tom Wilson has stepped up, and the depth scoring has been remarkable.

Florida's been battling through their own adversity. The back-to-back champions started slow but have righted the ship with a 9-4-1 run over their last 14. Sergei Bobrovsky is looking like himself again, posting a .918 save percentage over his last 10 starts. Matthew Tkachuk and Sam Reinhart continue to dominate, and the Panthers' power play is clicking at 26% at home.

The Panthers are the rightful favorite here, but I'm intrigued by the Capitals' resilience. They've covered in 6 of their last 8 road games against teams with winning records. This game should be tight - lean toward the Over in a track meet between two teams that can score.

Rangers @ Hurricanes

2025-12-30T00:00Z | Lenovo Center | ESPN+, MSGSN
Line: CAR -185 | O/U: 5.5 | NYR: 19-17-4 | CAR: 23-11-3

The Rangers are in crisis mode. What was supposed to be a Cup-contending roster has floundered, sitting outside the playoff picture in late December. Igor Shesterkin's numbers are down, the power play has been anemic, and there's genuine concern about whether this core can get it together. Chris Kreider has just 9 goals - a massive disappointment for a guy who usually has 15 by now.

Carolina, meanwhile, is doing what Carolina does. The Hurricanes are grinding opponents into submission with their relentless forecheck and suffocating defensive structure. Sebastian Aho is on pace for a career year, and Andrei Svechnikov has found his game again after last year's struggles. Pyotr Kochetkov has emerged as a legitimate starter, posting a .911 save percentage.

I don't trust the Rangers at all right now. They've lost 6 of their last 9, and the Hurricanes are 15-4-1 at home. Carolina's too deep, too well-coached, and playing with too much confidence. Take the Hurricanes and lay the juice.

Oilers @ Jets

2025-12-30T00:30Z | Canada Life Centre | ESPN+
Line: EDM -130 | O/U: 6.5 | EDM: 19-14-6 | WPG: 15-17-4

What happened to the Jets? Winnipeg started 15-3-0 and looked like the class of the Western Conference. Since then? An utterly stunning 0-14-4 stretch that's dropped them from first to the playoff bubble. Connor Hellebuyck is still elite, but the team in front of him has completely fallen apart. The defense is in shambles, and the offense has gone ice cold.

Edmonton is the opposite story. The Oilers took their lumps early but have been building momentum, going 11-4-2 in their last 17. McDavid and Draisaitl are doing McDavid and Draisaitl things - they've combined for 45 points in December alone. Stuart Skinner has stabilized in net, and the power play is lethal at 31%.

This feels like a statement game for Edmonton. The Oilers need to take care of business against slumping teams, and Winnipeg is as slumping as it gets. McDavid usually dominates the Jets - he has 54 career points in 35 games against them. Take Edmonton straight up.

Sabres @ Blues

2025-12-30T01:00Z | Enterprise Center | ESPN+, MSGB
Line: BUF -115 | O/U: 5.5 | BUF: 19-14-4 | STL: 15-16-8

The Sabres are finally showing signs of life after years of disappointment. Tage Thompson is having an MVP-caliber season with 28 goals already, and the supporting cast is actually contributing for once. Rasmus Dahlin has been elite on the back end, and Buffalo's penalty kill is top-10 in the league. They're a legitimate playoff contender for the first time in over a decade.

St. Louis is stuck in neutral. The Blues aren't bad enough to tank and aren't good enough to compete, which is the worst place to be. Jordan Kyrou has been inconsistent, Robert Thomas is hurt, and the goaltending tandem has been shaky. They're just 6-9-4 at home - that's not going to cut it in a competitive Central Division.

Buffalo's been excellent on the road, going 10-6-2 away from KeyBank Center. The Sabres have the better goaltending, the better star player, and the better structure right now. Take the slight dog and grab the plus money - the Sabres are for real this year.

Kings @ Avalanche

2025-12-30T02:00Z | Ball Arena | ESPN+, FanDuel SN West
Line: COL -238 | O/U: 5.5 | LA: 16-12-9 | COL: 28-2-7

How good are the Avalanche? 28-2-7 is absolutely ridiculous. Nathan MacKinnon is running away with the Hart Trophy, Cale Makar is the best defenseman in hockey, and even without Val Nichushkin, this team just keeps rolling. They've won 12 straight at home and are scoring 4.3 goals per game over that stretch. It's video game stuff.

The Kings are a solid team that's stuck in the shadow of powerhouses. Anze Kopitar and Drew Doughty are still effective, Adrian Kempe is having a career year, and Darcy Kuemper has been dependable. But "solid" doesn't cut it against a team this dominant. LA has lost 5 of their last 6 against Colorado, and most of those games weren't close.

Here's the thing - even at -238, you have to wonder if that's enough for Colorado at home. The Avs are 14-1-3 at Ball Arena and haven't lost in regulation since early November. The Kings will compete, but MacKinnon will probably have 3 points and Colorado wins 5-2. Don't overthink it.

Predators @ Mammoth

2025-12-30T02:00Z | Delta Center | ESPN+, Utah 16
Line: UTAH -155 | O/U: 5.5 | NSH: 16-17-4 | UTA: 18-18-3

Nashville's season has been a massive disappointment. All that money spent on free agents in the summer, and they're sitting at .500 in late December. Juuse Saros has been good but not spectacular, the power play is 30th in the league, and the defensive breakdowns have been alarming. Barry Trotz's system isn't working the way it should.

Utah is still finding its identity in its inaugural season, but they've been competitive. Clayton Keller continues to be a dynamic playmaker, and the home crowd has embraced this team. They're 11-7-1 at Delta Center, which is impressive for a first-year franchise. The energy in that building is electric.

This is a toss-up game between two mediocre teams, and I'll take the home side. Utah has the slight edge in goal differential, and Nashville has been brutal on the road at 5-10-2. The Mammoth at -155 is fair value - they've earned the right to be favored here.

Bruins @ Flames

2025-12-30T02:00Z | Scotiabank Saddledome | ESPN+, NESN
Line: CGY -130 | O/U: 6.5 | BOS: 20-18-1 | CGY: 16-18-4

The Bruins are a team in transition. Gone are the days of Patrice Bergeron and David Krejci anchoring the middle - now it's up to a young core to carry the load. David Pastrnak is still elite with 24 goals, but the depth scoring has dried up. Charlie McAvoy has been inconsistent, and the goaltending tandem has been a question mark.

Calgary's rebuild is progressing faster than expected. The Flames have a nice mix of young talent and veteran leadership, with Connor Zary emerging as a legitimate top-six center. Dustin Wolf has taken the starting job and run with it, posting a .912 save percentage. The Saddledome crowd is as hostile as ever for visiting teams.

This is a dangerous game for Boston. They're on a West Coast swing, facing some travel fatigue, and Calgary plays well in front of their home fans. The Flames are 9-7-3 at the Saddledome and have covered in 4 of their last 5 home games. I like Calgary here - they're getting better while Boston treads water.

Wild @ Knights

2025-12-30T03:00Z | T-Mobile Arena | ESPN+, Scripps Sports
Line: VGK -112 | O/U: 5.5 | MIN: 23-10-6 | VGK: 17-8-11

The Wild are legit. Kirill Kaprizov is a superstar, Matt Boldy has taken a massive leap, and Marc-Andre Fleury's retirement has been offset by Filip Gustavsson's emergence as an elite starter. Minnesota is 23-10-6 and firmly in the Central Division race. They play a grinding, structured game that frustrates opponents.

Vegas is dealing with the injury bug. Mitch Marner has fit in seamlessly since arriving from Toronto, but the Golden Knights have been without key pieces for stretches. Still, they're 17-8-11 and sitting pretty in the Pacific. Adin Hill has been rock solid when healthy, and Jack Eichel is playing the best hockey of his career with 48 points.

This is an elite matchup between two well-coached teams. Vegas has home-ice advantage at T-Mobile Arena, one of the toughest buildings in the league. But the Wild are playing at an elite level right now, and Kaprizov has 14 points in his last 10 games. Take the Under - both teams defend well and goaltending should be strong. This feels like a 3-2 type of game.

Sharks @ Ducks

2025-12-30T03:00Z | Honda Center | ESPN+, NBC Sports BA
Line: ANA -185 | O/U: 6.5 | SJ: 18-17-3 | ANA: 21-15-2

The Sharks' rebuild is ahead of schedule, and it's all because of Macklin Celebrini. The rookie phenom has lived up to every bit of his hype, dominating games with his combination of skill and hockey IQ. He's already got 38 points and is the runaway Calder Trophy favorite. San Jose is playing meaningful hockey in late December - nobody saw that coming.

Anaheim has been one of the league's pleasant surprises. The Ducks rebuilt through the draft, and now those picks are paying off. Leo Carlsson, Mason McTavish, and Trevor Zegras form an exciting young core. They're 21-15-2 and in playoff position - a year ahead of schedule. The home crowd at Honda Center has been electric.

This is a fun matchup between two ascending teams. Anaheim has the home-ice advantage and the slightly better record, but San Jose has been competitive on the road. The Ducks are 12-5-1 at home and playing with confidence. Take Anaheim, but don't be shocked if Celebrini takes over and steals one. He's that good.

Canucks @ Kraken

2025-12-30T03:00Z | Climate Pledge Arena | ESPN+, KONG
Line: VAN -108 | O/U: 5.5 | VAN: 15-19-3 | SEA: 16-14-6

What happened to Vancouver? The Canucks were in the second round of the playoffs last spring and looked like a team on the rise. Now they're 15-19-3 and staring at a lottery pick. Elias Pettersson has been inconsistent, J.T. Miller's been the subject of trade rumors, and the vibes are just off. It's been a massively disappointing campaign.

Seattle is trying to find their footing after a rocky start. The Kraken have stabilized with a 9-5-3 run over their last 17, and Matty Beniers is finally looking like the player they drafted. Philipp Grubauer has been serviceable in net, and the power play has clicked recently at 23% over the last two weeks.

I'll side with the home team in this Pacific Division clash. Seattle's been playing better hockey, the Canucks are a mess, and Climate Pledge Arena will be rocking. Vancouver has lost 5 of their last 7 and doesn't show signs of turning it around. Take the Kraken and enjoy the chaos across the border.
