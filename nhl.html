Avalanche @ Kraken

10:00 PM ET | Climate Pledge Arena | TNT
Line: COL -275 | O/U: 5.5 | COL: 23-2-7 | SEA: 12-12-6

The Colorado Avalanche are playing hockey from another dimension right now. At 23-2-7, they're the best team in the NHL by a mile, and the numbers back it up. They lead the league in goals scored with 128, they have the fewest goals allowed at just 70, and their plus-58 goal differential is absurd. Nathan MacKinnon is orchestrating the offense like a conductor who's had too much coffee, and everyone around him is thriving.

Seattle is in survival mode. They're 1-4 in their last five games, averaging just 2.2 goals per game during that stretch while giving up 3.6. Missing Jaden Schwartz and Jared McCann from the lineup doesn't help. The Kraken haven't beaten the Avalanche in five straight meetings, and nothing about tonight's matchup suggests that streak is in danger. Colorado is a freight train, and Seattle is standing on the tracks.

Oilers @ Penguins

7:30 PM ET | PPG Paints Arena | TNT
Line: EDM -130 | O/U: 6.5 | EDM: 15-12-6 | PIT: 14-8-9

This one has storyline written all over it. Tristan Jarry, traded from Pittsburgh to Edmonton, returns to face his former team for the first time. In his Oilers debut, he stopped 25 of 28 shots. Now he'll be staring down Sidney Crosby and company from the other end of the ice. You know there's going to be some extra juice in this one.

The Oilers have won four of their last six and are sitting in a Wild Card spot in the Western Conference. Pittsburgh has dropped two straight in overtime, which is frustrating but at least shows they're competitive. Crosby and Bryan Rust have combined for 30 goals and 31 assists this season, while the blue line of Erik Karlsson and Kris Letang has chipped in 4 goals and 35 assists. This game has the potential to be a track meet.

Ducks @ Blue Jackets

7:00 PM ET | Nationwide Arena
Line: CBJ -143 | O/U: 6.5 | ANA: 20-12-1 | CBJ: 13-13-6

This is a sneaky good game for pure entertainment value. The Ducks and Blue Jackets are the two highest-scoring teams in the NHL, both averaging around 3.44-3.45 goals per game. Defense? Optional for both squads. Columbus allows 3.47 per game, Anaheim gives up 3.34. Add it all up and you're looking at a total of 6.5, which feels like it could get crushed.

Anaheim's youth movement is ahead of schedule. Leo Carlsson leads with 41 points (17G, 24A), Cutter Gauthier has 36 points with 18 goals, and rookie Beckett Sennecke leads all NHL rookies with 27 points. Columbus has Zachary Werenski (33 points) and Adam Fantilli showing out. Here's a wild stat: Columbus hasn't beaten Anaheim at home since December 1, 2017. They're 0-2-4 since then. Can they finally break through tonight?

Capitals @ Wild

8:00 PM ET | Grand Casino Arena
Line: MIN -122 | O/U: 5.5 | WSH: 18-10-4 | MIN: 21-10-3

This is a clash of two of the better defensive teams in the league. The Capitals rank 2nd in goals allowed, the Wild rank 4th. Minnesota has been rolling lately, winning 8 of their last 10 games including four straight. Their 6-2 demolition of Boston in their last game showed they can put up offense when needed.

Washington's 9-game point streak was snapped by Winnipeg, but this is still a dangerous team. Alexander Ovechkin continues his chase of Wayne Gretzky's all-time goals record with 14 goals this season on 85 shots (16.5% conversion). Tom Wilson has been excellent with 32 points in 32 games and 17 goals on 70 shots at a 24.3% clip. The Wild's power play has been deadly (3rd most goals, 8th in percentage), so staying out of the box is crucial for the Caps.

Islanders @ Red Wings

7:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena
Line: DET -127 | O/U: 6.0 | NYI: 19-11-3 | DET: 18-12-3

The Islanders have been rolling, winning 6 of their last 7 games including a 3-2 shootout win over Tampa Bay that extended their home winning streak to 5 games. Bo Horvat has been outstanding with 31 points (19G, 12A) in 32 games, and Ilya Sorokin continues to be one of the league's better goalies at .912 save percentage.

Here's the problem for Detroit: they've been dominated by the Islanders this season. New York won both previous meetings by a combined score of 12-2 (7-2 on Oct 23, 5-0 shutout on Nov 20). John Gibson has been hot for Detroit, going 5-0-0 in December with a 2.00 GAA, .940 SV%, and 2 shutouts. Alex DeBrincat leads with 18 goals and 19 assists. Something has to give in this series.

Blackhawks @ Maple Leafs

7:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena
Line: TOR -180 | O/U: 6.5 | CHI: 13-13-6 | TOR: 14-12-5

Chicago stunned the Maple Leafs earlier this season, winning 3-2 on November 15 when Colton Dach scored a late winner. Toronto needs to win to avoid being swept by the rebuilding Blackhawks, which would be an embarrassing addition to their already frustrating season. The Leafs went 4-1 in their last 5 games but were just crushed 6-3 by Edmonton.

Both teams can score at an elite level, sitting in the top 7 in goals per game. But here's the difference: Chicago's defense (2.55 GA/G) has been significantly better than Toronto's (3.26). The Maple Leafs' power play is also struggling at 30th in the league. Connor Bedard (13 points: 6G, 7A) leads the Blackhawks' young core, while William Nylander and John Tavares carry the offensive load for Toronto.

Canucks @ Rangers

7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden
Line: NYR -135 | O/U: 5.5 | VAN: 12-17-3 | NYR: 16-14-4

Two struggling offensive teams meet at Madison Square Garden. The Rangers rank 26th in goals scored, the Canucks rank 27th. Vancouver also ranks dead last in goals allowed at 30th. The Rangers are an abysmal 4-9-3 at home this season, which is shocking for a team with this much talent. Their 4-1 loss to Anaheim in their last game was another ugly showing.

Vancouver beat New Jersey in their last outing, but they're still last in the Pacific at 12-17-3. Kiefer Sherwood leads with 13 goals and 4 assists, but this team needs more production across the board. Artemi Panarin (11G, 23A) and Mika Zibanejad (4G, 6A in last 10) need to carry the Rangers. First meeting this season went to the Rangers 2-0 when Jonathan Quick posted a 23-save shutout.

Utah @ Bruins

7:00 PM ET | TD Garden
Line: BOS -160 | O/U: 5.5 | UTAH: 16-15-3 | BOS: 19-14-0

Utah's road trip brings them to TD Garden after a 5-4 win over Pittsburgh. The expansion club (well, relocated from Arizona) has been competitive in their first season, sitting around .500 with a 3.14 goals per game average. Their power play has been a weakness at 15.63% (27th), but the penalty kill has been solid at 81.9% (12th).

Boston has been inconsistent, going 4-4-2 in their last 10 games. Their last outing was a brutal 6-2 loss to Minnesota. David Pastrnak (13G, 23A in 27 GP) and Morgan Geekie (22G, 12A in 32 GP) lead the offense, and the power play has been clicking at 26.7% (4th in NHL). Utah won the last meeting 2-1 at home, so Boston will want revenge on home ice.

Flyers @ Canadiens

7:00 PM ET | Bell Centre
Line: MTL -145 | O/U: 6.5 | PHI: 16-9-6 | MTL: 17-11-4

The Flyers are on a 3-game losing streak and head into Montreal with some concerns. Their power play is one of the worst in the league at 16.47% (30th), which is a problem when facing a Canadiens team with an elite PP at 26.37% (4th). Trevor Zegras (32 points: 13G, 19A) and Travis Konecny (27 points: 8G, 19A) lead Philadelphia's attack.

Montreal is coming off a 4-1 win over Edmonton and looks like a team building toward something. Nicholas Suzuki (37 points: 9G, 28A) and Cole Caufield (33 points: 17G, 16A) are having excellent seasons. Here's a stat for totals bettors: 24 of Montreal's 32 games have gone over 5.5 goals this season. This one's set at 6.5, and given the Flyers' defensive struggles on the road, it could sail over.

Flames @ Sharks

10:00 PM ET | SAP Center
Line: CGY -132 | O/U: 5.5 | CGY: 13-16-4 | SJ: 16-14-3

The Sharks are holding onto a Wild Card spot in the West despite being without some key forwards. Will Smith and Philipp Kurashev are out, which puts more pressure on Macklin Celebrini to carry the load. And he's doing exactly that, leading the team with 47 points (16G, 31A) in 33 games for a 1.4 points-per-game average. The rookie is legit.

Calgary won the first meeting between these teams 2-0 and comes in off a 2-1 overtime win over the Kings. Nazem Kadri (6G, 22A) leads the Flames, while Dustin Wolf has been serviceable in net at 10-12-2 with a 2.9 GAA and .899 SV%. San Jose is on a 2-game win streak after beating Pittsburgh 6-5 in overtime. Yaroslav Askarov has been solid at 11-8-1 with a .901 save percentage.
