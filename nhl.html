Sharks @ Red Wings

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena | NHL Network
ML: SJ +200 / DET -245 | Puck Line: SJ +1.5 (-125) / DET -1.5 (+105) | O/U: 6.5

This is the game that might tell us everything we need to know about Macklin Celebrini's Calder Trophy case. The 19-year-old phenom has been nothing short of spectacular, sitting third in the entire NHL in scoring with 70 points in 45 games, including 24 goals and 43 assists. He's posted a plus-17 rating despite playing on a team that was expected to be a lottery contender. Washington's Spencer Carbery even said he'd give Celebrini the Hart Trophy if he had a vote right now. That's not Calder talk, that's MVP talk for a teenager.

The Sharks (23-19-3) have completely transformed from the league's worst team last season (20-50-12) into a legitimate wild card contender, currently holding the second wild card spot in the Western Conference. Celebrini's 12-game point streak earlier this season tied Wayne Gretzky, Joe Sakic, and Jimmy Carson for the third-longest point streak by a teenager in NHL history. His 20 even-strength goals lead the team by a mile. He's not just carrying, he's dragging this franchise into relevance.

Detroit (28-16) has been one of the pleasant surprises in the Atlantic Division, finally playing like a playoff team after years of rebuilding. They've scored 147 goals this season (12th in NHL) while allowing 146 (21st). The Red Wings at -245 is steep, but their home record has earned that price. However, this feels like a spot where the Sharks could steal one. Celebrini has a way of elevating his game in nationally televised matchups, and the NHL Network spotlight creates prime upset conditions. The 6.5 total is the highest on the board tonight.

Advanced Trends: San Jose has covered the puck line in 5 of their last 7 road games as underdogs of +180 or more. Detroit is 8-3 in their last 11 home games, but only 4-6-1 against teams with winning records. The over has hit in 4 of the last 5 Sharks road games. Celebrini has recorded at least one point in 9 of his last 11 games.

Panthers @ Hurricanes

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Lenovo Center | ESPN+
ML: FLA +142 / CAR -170 | Puck Line: FLA +1.5 (-180) / CAR -1.5 (+150) | O/U: 5.5

Look, when back-to-back Stanley Cup champions walk into your building, you better be ready to play. The Florida Panthers (24-18) have been frustratingly inconsistent this season, far from the dominant force that lifted consecutive Cups in 2024 and 2025. Sergei Bobrovsky's numbers tell the story: 19-12-1 with a 2.90 GAA and an .881 save percentage through 32 starts. That's not championship-caliber goaltending, and the Panthers know it.

Carolina (28-15-4) is quietly putting together another elite season, playing the structured, suffocating hockey that's become their trademark. The Hurricanes are among the East's best teams, and Lenovo Center has been a house of horrors for opposing offenses. Carolina's defensive system forces opponents into low-percentage shots, and their penalty kill has been excellent. Their 28 wins reflect a team that grinds out results night after night.

The historical series between these teams has been tight. Out of 163 all-time meetings, Carolina holds a slim 79-73-11 edge. Both teams know each other intimately from years of playoff battles. Florida's 23 goals from their top scorer this season shows they have the firepower, but Carolina's defensive identity should win out at home. The 5.5 total is appropriate for a game that should be tight throughout.

Advanced Trends: Carolina is 15-6-2 at home this season. The under has hit in 4 of the last 6 Panthers road games. Florida is 2-4 in their last 6 games against teams with winning records. Carolina has held opponents to 2 or fewer goals in 8 of their last 12 home games. The Hurricanes' power play conversion at home is 26.3%, well above their season average.

Lightning @ Blues

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Enterprise Center | ESPN+
ML: TB -162 / STL +150 | Puck Line: TB -1.5 (+145) / STL +1.5 (-175) | O/U: 5.5

Tampa Bay (29-13) continues to look like a legitimate Stanley Cup contender, and Jon Cooper's squad has their eyes firmly set on another deep playoff run. The Lightning have been among the league's most balanced teams this season, capable of winning high-scoring shootouts and grinding defensive battles alike. When you've got the championship pedigree that Tampa possesses, every game feels like preparation for the postseason.

St. Louis (18-21) is in a tough spot. The Blues are below .500 and fighting just to stay relevant in the playoff conversation. Their 39 points through 39 games puts them on the outside looking in, and they're running out of time to make a move. The Enterprise Center has been a difficult place to play, but the Blues simply haven't had the consistency to capitalize on their home-ice advantage this season.

Tampa's -162 price reflects the gap between these teams. The Lightning have been stellar on the road, and their offensive depth creates matchup problems for struggling defenses like St. Louis. The Blues will compete hard since this is a pride game, but there's a real talent disparity here. The 5.5 total suggests a tighter, defensive-oriented contest, though Tampa's offense has the ability to blow games open quickly when they get rolling.

Advanced Trends: Tampa Bay is 12-6 on the road this season. The Lightning have covered the puck line in 6 of their last 8 games against Central Division opponents. St. Louis is 1-5 in their last 6 home games against Eastern Conference teams. The under has hit in 5 of the last 7 meetings between these teams. Tampa's power play is clicking at 28.4% over their last 10 games.

Predators @ Avalanche

Friday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena | ESPN+
ML: NSH +245 / COL -285 | Puck Line: NSH +1.5 (-105) / COL -1.5 (-115) | O/U: 6.5

Here's the stat that will make your jaw drop: Colorado (33-4-8) remains unbeaten in regulation at home with a 19-0-3 record more than halfway through the home schedule. No other NHL team has fewer than five regulation losses at home. The Avalanche had won 17 straight home games before a 4-3 overtime loss to Toronto on Monday, and they're coming off that rare home blemish with a vengeance. This is the most dominant home-ice advantage in hockey, and it's not particularly close.

Nathan MacKinnon is having a season for the ages. He leads the way with 81 points (36 goals, 45 assists) and sits second in NHL scoring while ranking first in goals. Here's what's truly absurd: MacKinnon has 26 multi-point games in 45 team games, which is the most by any NHL player through 45 games of a campaign since Mario Lemieux in 1995-96. That's generational company. Cale Makar leads all defensemen with 53 points (14 goals, 39 assists), and Mackenzie Blackwood's .924 save percentage leads all NHL goalies.

Nashville (22-20-4) comes in off a 4-3 win over Edmonton, so they have momentum. Steven Stamkos leads the Predators with 20 goals, bringing that championship pedigree from his Tampa days. But the Preds are 9-10-2 on the road, and walking into Ball Arena against this Avalanche team is a nightmare assignment. Colorado leads the NHL in points (74), wins (33), and goals per game (4.02 per game) while allowing the fewest goals per game (2.20).

Advanced Trends: Colorado is 33-4-8 overall and 19-0-3 at home in regulation this season. The Avalanche have covered the puck line in 9 of their last 11 home games. Nashville is 3-7 ATS in their last 10 road games against teams with winning records. MacKinnon has recorded a point in 15 of his last 17 home games. The over has hit in 6 of the last 8 Avalanche home games.

Ducks @ Kings

Friday, 10:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena | ESPN+
ML: ANA +142 / LA -170 | Puck Line: ANA +1.5 (-180) / LA -1.5 (+150) | O/U: 6.5

The Freeway Faceoff brings Southern California bragging rights to the line as Anaheim travels to LA for the late-night showcase. The Ducks (22-21) have been surprisingly competitive this season, playing .500 hockey when many expected them to be firmly in rebuilding mode. Their 13-8 road record is actually one of the better marks in the Pacific Division, showing they can win in hostile environments. This young Anaheim team has fight.

Los Angeles (19-16) has been maddeningly inconsistent, especially at home where they're just 7-10. That's a red flag for any team with playoff aspirations, and the Kings need to figure out their Crypto.com Arena struggles quickly. Anze Kopitar continues to be the heart of this franchise at 38 years old, but the supporting cast needs to step up. The Kings have the talent to be a playoff team, but they haven't played like one at home.

This is the most competitive game on the board tonight by the betting market's estimation. The Ducks at +142 represents genuine value since Anaheim has shown they can hang with anyone on the road. LA's home-ice advantage has been non-existent this season, negating much of what should be a talent edge. The 6.5 total reflects both teams' ability to score. Expect a close, competitive battle that could go either way.

Advanced Trends: Anaheim is 13-8 on the road this season, while LA is just 7-10 at home. The Ducks have won 3 of their last 4 games overall. The over has hit in 5 of the last 6 Ducks road games. LA is 2-4 in their last 6 home games against Pacific Division opponents. The home team has lost 4 of the last 5 meetings between these rivals.
