5-Game Slate - Wednesday, December 03, 2025

Wednesday, December 03, 2025

@

Dallas Stars (17-5-4) @ New Jersey Devils (16-9-1)

7:00 PM ET Prudential Center ESPN+

The Dallas Stars are on an absolute tear right now, and the advanced metrics confirm what the eye test shows. At 17-5-4, they've got the second-best record in Dallas history and third-best in franchise history through 26 games. The Stars are averaging 3.26 goals per game (10th in NHL) while allowing just 2.79 goals against (9th). Their power play is operating at a blistering 33.3%, ranking 2nd in the entire league.

Jason Robertson was just named the NHL's Second Star of the Month for November, and his production has been elite. He led the entire league with 13 goals in November and tied for the lead in points with 26 across 15 games. On the season, Robertson ranks 2nd in the NHL with 109 shots on goal, tied 5th with 16 goals, and tied 3rd with 15 power-play points. His seven-game goal streak (Nov. 11-25) was the longest by a Stars player since the franchise relocated to Dallas in 1993-94. Robertson is currently on pace for 50 goals and 110 points, both career highs.

The Devils are 16-9-1 and sitting 3rd in the Metropolitan Division, but they're dealing with a devastating injury situation. Jack Hughes is out approximately eight weeks after finger surgery following a freak accident at a team dinner on November 15th. Before the injury, Hughes had 10 goals and 20 points through 17 games. Without Hughes, New Jersey started 1-3-0 and struggled to generate offense, often limping to one or two goals per game. The good news: they've scored 3+ goals in each of their last five games, with Nico Hischier and Timo Meier combining for 16 points during that stretch.

Here's where the numbers get interesting for bettors: Dallas has gone UNDER in 4 of their last 5 road games, and UNDER in 5 of their last 6 games against Metropolitan Division opponents. Meanwhile, New Jersey has gone UNDER in 11 of their last 14 December games. With the Devils' offensive struggles without Hughes and Dallas playing road-tested hockey, this projects as a lower-scoring affair.

Casey DeSmith has been outstanding for Dallas lately, posting a 5-0-1 record with a 1.46 GAA and .943 save percentage across his last six appearances. The Stars have 17 different players with goals in November, and all 22 skaters have tallied a point. That depth is elite. New Jersey's special teams are solid (25.0% PP, 5th; 82.7% PK, 7th), but they also have Brett Pesce, Johnathan Kovacevic, and several other key players on the injury list.

The matchup heavily favors Dallas. The Stars are playing championship-caliber hockey with elite depth scoring (17 players with November goals), while the Devils are missing their best player and haven't found consistency without Hughes. The Under trends (Dallas 4-of-5 road, NJ 11-of-14 December) align with a lower-scoring projection given the Devils' offensive struggles.

@

Buffalo Sabres (11-11-4) @ Philadelphia Flyers (14-8-3)

7:30 PM ET Wells Fargo Center TNT

This is your nationally televised game of the night on TNT, and the Flyers are defying every preseason expectation. Philadelphia sits at 14-8-3 with five wins in their last seven games, and they're in legitimate playoff position in the Metropolitan Division. The defensive numbers tell the story: 2.88 goals against per game, 26.1 shots against, and an 82.1% penalty kill (11th in NHL). Nobody saw this coming, and yet here we are.

The Flyers are 8-4-2 at home this season, and Wells Fargo Center has become a fortress. Daniil Vladar has been the mainstay starter, posting a 2.59 GAA and .906 save percentage. Their power play struggles at 17.1% (20th in NHL), but the Flyers make up for it with grinding, physical hockey. Trevor Zegras has been thriving since his change-of-scenery trade, and the entire roster has bought into the system. Philadelphia ranks 28th in goals (69) but 10th in goals against (72), which speaks to their identity as a defensive-first team.

Buffalo comes in at 11-11-4 and is starting a six-game road trip tonight. The Sabres just beat Winnipeg 5-1 on December 1st, with Josh Norris scoring twice and adding an assist in his first game back from injury. Zach Benson and Josh Doan each had two assists in that game. Buffalo has 79 goals for (14th) but allows 87 goals against (26th), showing inconsistency at both ends. Ukko-Pekka Luukkonen has been solid in net, making 23 saves in that last outing.

Here's the betting angle: Philadelphia's home record and defensive structure make them a tough out at Wells Fargo. The Flyers rank 7th in the league with 269 penalty minutes, showing they play physical hockey that can throw opponents off their game. Buffalo's road splits have been mediocre all season, and walking into a nationally televised game against a motivated Flyers team is a tough draw.

The Flyers' defensive discipline is going to be the difference. They block shots, limit high-danger chances, and make teams earn everything they get. Buffalo can score when clicking (as they showed against Winnipeg), but Philadelphia's structure should keep this game close.

Philadelphia's home record (8-4-2) and defensive structure (2.88 GAA, 82.1% PK) make Wells Fargo a difficult place for road teams. The Flyers have been one of the surprise stories of the season, while Buffalo starts a six-game road trip in a hostile environment on national television. The numbers favor the home team in this spot.

Winnipeg Jets (13-12-0) @ Montreal Canadiens (13-9-3)

7:30 PM ET Bell Centre ESPN+

The Jets have been wildly inconsistent lately, and the numbers tell the story. Winnipeg is 13-12-0 overall and 7-7-0 on the road. They just lost 5-1 to Buffalo on December 1st, got outplayed in every facet, and have won only 1 of their last 4 games. The volatility has been alarming: a 5-2 win in Nashville, but three straight defeats before that against Carolina, Washington, and Minnesota.

Here's the critical issue for Winnipeg: Connor Hellebuyck had arthroscopic knee surgery on November 23rd and won't return until early January. Before the injury, Hellebuyck was 8-6-0 with a 2.51 GAA and .913 save percentage in 14 games. Losing arguably the best goalie in the world changes everything. Backup Eric Comrie has struggled with a 3.02 GAA and .897 save percentage, while prospect Thomas Milic was recalled from AHL Manitoba but hasn't proven himself at the NHL level yet.

Mark Scheifele continues to lead the offense with 32 points (13G, 19A), and the Jets' power play operates at 22.1% (above average). But defensively they're allowing 3.00 goals per game and 29.2 shots against, which is concerning without their number one goalie. The goaltending situation is a massive vulnerability bettors need to factor in.

Montreal sits at 13-9-3 and is 5th in the Atlantic Division. The Canadiens are averaging 3.36 goals per game (84 GF, 9th in NHL) but also allow 90 goals against (25th). Here's the critical Over/Under trend: Montreal has gone OVER in 14 of their last 16 games. That's not a typo, 14 of 16. Cole Caufield is on a six-game point streak, Nick Suzuki continues to lead as captain, and Ivan Demidov has been contributing offensively. Bell Centre is always a difficult environment.

The betting context here is significant: Winnipeg's goaltending issues and Montreal's propensity for high-scoring games create an interesting total dynamic. The Canadiens at home should be able to capitalize on whoever Winnipeg throws in net, and the Jets have enough offensive talent to score against Montreal's porous defense (90 GA).

The data points to a high-scoring affair. Montreal's OVER trend (14 of 16 games) combined with Winnipeg's backup goaltending situation (Comrie: 3.02 GAA, .897 SV%) creates a compelling total narrative. Both teams can score, neither team is stopping anybody right now, and Bell Centre's atmosphere adds another layer. The numbers suggest goals in this matchup.

Utah Mammoth (12-12-3) @ Anaheim Ducks (16-9-1)

10:00 PM ET Honda Center TNT

This is a showcase of two of the best young talents in the NHL, and the numbers are staggering. Leo Carlsson leads the Anaheim Ducks with 36 points (14G, 22A) through 26 games, putting him tied with Connor McDavid for 4th in the entire NHL. The 20-year-old is on a seven-game point streak with 3 goals and 7 assists during that span, has a +13 rating, and has scored in 11 of his 25 games this season. Carlsson's on pace for over 100 points, which would be historic for a player his age.

Logan Cooley leads the Utah Mammoth with 23 points (14G, 9A) and just signed an eight-year, $80 million contract extension. Cooley's franchise-record four-goal game against Vegas on November 20th made him just the fourth player in NHL history to record multiple natural hat tricks before turning 22, joining Wayne Gretzky, Filip Forsberg, and Auston Matthews. He was questionable earlier this week with a lower-body injury but avoided missing his first game of the season.

The Ducks are 16-9-1 and playing winning hockey at Honda Center. Beyond Carlsson, Chris Kreider has been contributing offensively, and the team has found a winning formula this season. Utah sits at 12-12-3 with 27 points, 4th in the Central Division (just one point behind Minnesota). The Mammoth are in their first season after rebranding from Utah Hockey Club, and they're still establishing their identity on the road.

Here's the betting angle: Anaheim has all the advantages tonight. They're at home, they've got the better record by 6 points in the standings, and Carlsson is playing at an elite level. Cooley's injury situation adds uncertainty for Utah, even though he's expected to play. The Ducks' young core is playing with confidence and cohesion that Utah is still searching for.

This late-night matchup on TNT features elite young talent on both sides. Anaheim has the edge with home ice, a better record by 6 points, and Carlsson producing at an elite level (tied 4th in NHL with McDavid). Cooley's injury situation adds uncertainty for Utah. The Ducks' young core is playing with confidence and cohesion, making Honda Center a challenging road environment.

@

Washington Capitals (16-9-2) @ San Jose Sharks (13-11-3)

10:00 PM ET SAP Center ESPN+

Alex Ovechkin is hunting history, and the numbers in December are eye-popping. The Great Eight has 909 career goals, the most in NHL history, and he's been on fire lately. After a slow start (just 4 goals in his first 17 games), Ovechkin has exploded with 8 goals and 13 points over his last 9 contests. He just recorded his 33rd career hat trick, tying Brett Hull for 4th all-time, and moved past Joe Sakic into 10th on the NHL's all-time points list with 1,643.

On the season, Ovechkin has 12 goals and 15 assists (27 points) through 27 games with a +9 rating and 67 shots on net. At 40 years old, he's on pace for 30-plus goals for the twentieth time in his NHL career. The consistency, durability, and sheer will to score is unmatched in hockey history. Washington enters this game on a four-game winning streak and won 7 of their last 8 games in November.

The Capitals are 16-9-2 and playing confident hockey. Beyond Ovechkin, the young core has stepped up. Washington's rising stars have fueled this surge while Ovi shook off his slow start. This is a team that knows how to win, and they're dangerous when clicking on all cylinders.

San Jose is 13-11-3 and 4th in the Pacific Division. The Sharks have 79 goals for (19th in NHL), 86 goals against (22nd), a 19.2% power play (17th), and an 81.7% penalty kill (12th). They just got shut out 6-0 by Colorado on December 1st, as Mackenzie Blackwood stopped all 26 shots. Macklin Celebrini is the exciting young talent giving fans hope for the future, but the present is about development and growth. San Jose is overmatched against a veteran Capitals team riding momentum.

Here's the betting angle: Washington's the better team by a significant margin and playing their best hockey of the season. Ovechkin is locked in after his slow start, the Caps are winning games, and San Jose just got blanked at home. The Sharks don't have the depth to compete for sixty minutes against this version of Washington.

The statistical edge belongs to Washington across the board. Ovechkin's hot streak (8 goals in last 9 games), the Caps' four-game winning streak, and San Jose's struggles (6-0 shutout loss to Colorado) create a significant gap in form. The experience and momentum favor the visitors in this West Coast matchup.
