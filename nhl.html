Sabres @ Hurricanes

Monday, 1:30 PM ET | PNC Arena | TNT
Puck Line: BUF +1.5 (-142) / CAR -1.5 (+120) | ML: BUF +164 / CAR -205 | O/U: 6.5 (O +110 / U -118)

The Hurricanes have been one of the league's most consistent home teams all season, and they'll need that advantage against a Sabres squad that's proven surprisingly competitive on the road. Buffalo's 16-6 away record suggests this isn't the pushover it once was, but Carolina's 17-8 home mark and third-best goal differential in the Eastern Conference paints a clear picture of who controls this matchup.

Ukko-Pekka Luukkonen gets the start for Buffalo, and he's been solid between the pipes. The Sabres' goaltending has quietly been respectable, but facing Carolina's transition game presents unique challenges. The Hurricanes generate chances in bunches, and their forecheck creates turnovers that lead to high-danger opportunities. Brandon Bussi will defend the home crease for Carolina.

Look for Carolina to control possession and dictate tempo. The Hurricanes don't just outshoot opponents; they outchance them. Buffalo's defensive structure has improved, but containing Carolina's speed and puck movement is a different beast. The 6.5 total reflects the expectation that both teams can score, but Carolina's defensive metrics at home suggest they'll limit Buffalo's opportunities.

Capitals @ Avalanche

Monday, 4:00 PM ET | Ball Arena | ESPN
Puck Line: WSH +1.5 (-142) / COL -1.5 (+120) | ML: WSH +180 / COL -218 | O/U: 6.5 (O +105 / U -115)

This is the marquee matchup of the afternoon. Colorado sits at 33-5 overall with an absolutely absurd 19-1 home record. That's not a typo. The Avalanche have lost exactly one game at Ball Arena all season, and they've done it behind Nathan MacKinnon's MVP-caliber campaign. With 82 points in 46 games, MacKinnon is on pace to shatter his career-best 140-point season from 2023-24.

Washington comes in at 24-19 with a respectable 14-10 road record, but they haven't faced anything like this Colorado juggernaut. Logan Thompson starts in net for the Caps, while Scott Wedgewood gets the call for Colorado. The goaltending edge might favor Washington on paper, but the Avalanche's offensive firepower overwhelms that advantage entirely.

The Avalanche lead the NHL in goals per game and their power play has been lethal. Washington's penalty kill ranks middle of the pack, and staying out of the box becomes priority number one. MacKinnon's line creates chances at will, and Colorado's depth scoring has been equally dangerous. The Capitals need Thompson to steal this game because outscoring Colorado at altitude feels impossible.

Penguins @ Kraken

Monday, 5:00 PM ET | Climate Pledge Arena | ESPN+
Puck Line: PIT -1.5 (+175) / SEA +1.5 (-210) | ML: PIT -130 / SEA +110 | O/U: 6.0

Sidney Crosby brings his Penguins to the Pacific Northwest for an afternoon clash with Seattle. Pittsburgh enters as slight favorites, suggesting the market still respects Crosby's ability to elevate his team in any environment. The Penguins' inconsistency this season has been maddening for bettors, but their ceiling remains high when Crosby and company click.

The Kraken have been a difficult read all season. They play well at home but struggle to score consistently. Seattle's goaltending has kept them in games they had no business being in, and that could be the story here. The Kraken's system emphasizes limiting high-danger chances, which forces Pittsburgh to execute their cycle game rather than relying on pure skill.

The 6.0 total is notably lower than most games on this slate, reflecting both teams' struggles to score at times. Pittsburgh's power play has been hit or miss, and Seattle's penalty kill could frustrate the Penguins if they take penalties in the offensive zone. This feels like a grind-it-out affair that comes down to special teams and goaltending.

Sharks @ Panthers

Monday, 6:00 PM ET | Amerant Bank Arena | ESPN+
Puck Line: SJS +1.5 (-165) / FLA -1.5 (+138) | ML: SJS +154 / FLA -185 | O/U: 6.0

The defending back-to-back Stanley Cup champion Panthers host the rebuilding Sharks in what should be a mismatch on paper. Florida sits at 22-16-3, currently fourth in the Atlantic Division. Sergei Bobrovsky has struggled in January, posting a brutal 2-4-0 record with a 4.42 GAA and .810 save percentage this month. The workload of 228 games over three seasons, regular season and playoffs combined, appears to be catching up with the 36-year-old.

San Jose enters in full rebuild mode but has shown flashes of competitiveness despite their record. The Sharks' young players have talent, and they've pulled off upsets against better teams when their goaltending delivers. This matchup hinges on whether Bobrovsky can shake his January funk or if Florida needs to ride their depth and forechecking pressure.

The Panthers rank 12th in goals per game at 3.13 but 21st in goals against at 3.20. That defensive regression from their championship form has been the story of their season. San Jose can make this interesting if they catch Bobrovsky on another off night, but Florida's championship pedigree should prevail at home against a team in the middle of a long rebuild.

Wild @ Maple Leafs

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena | Sportsnet
Puck Line: MIN +1.5 (-190) / TOR -1.5 (+160) | ML: MIN -110 / TOR -110 | O/U: 6.0

A true pick'em in Toronto. The even moneylines reflect two evenly matched teams heading in different directions. Minnesota's goaltending tandem has been among the league's best, while Toronto's offensive firepower needs no introduction. Auston Matthews and the Leafs' top six can score on anyone, but the Wild's system limits high-danger chances as well as anyone.

Minnesota thrives on structure and discipline. They don't beat themselves with penalties, and they make opponents earn every inch of offensive zone time. Toronto's been guilty of playing loose defensively at times, which creates opportunities for Minnesota's counter-attacking style. The Wild transition quickly and punish turnovers.

This game should showcase contrasting styles. Toronto wants pace and skill; Minnesota wants structure and patience. The team that imposes their identity will likely win. With Mitch Marner now in Vegas, the Leafs' lineup construction has changed, and they've adjusted their approach. Watch for Toronto to play more direct hockey than in years past.

Flyers @ Golden Knights

Monday, 8:00 PM ET | T-Mobile Arena | ESPN+
Puck Line: PHI +1.5 (-152) / VGK -1.5 (+128) | ML: PHI +180 / VGK -218 | O/U: 6.0

Vegas added Rasmus Andersson from Calgary via trade, further bolstering their blue line depth for the playoff push. The Golden Knights have been Cup contenders since their inception, and they're building another run. At home, Vegas is particularly dangerous, feeding off T-Mobile Arena's energy and their trademark speed through the neutral zone.

Philadelphia's in a different place in their development. The Flyers are building something, but they're not ready to compete with Vegas consistently. The youth movement has shown promise, and they've surprised better teams this season, but the road challenges mount when facing organizations like Vegas that are designed to win now.

The Golden Knights' depth becomes the story here. Vegas can roll four lines effectively, and their defensive structure under Bruce Cassidy limits odd-man rushes. Philadelphia will need their goaltender to be exceptional and capitalize on the few chances Vegas allows. That's a tough ask against a team this well-constructed.

Jets @ Blackhawks

Monday, 8:30 PM ET | United Center | NHL Network
Puck Line: WPG -1.5 (+148) / CHI +1.5 (-178) | ML: WPG -142 / CHI +120 | O/U: 6.0

Winnipeg has been one of the pleasant surprises of the NHL season, and they bring that momentum to Chicago. The Jets' balanced attack and solid goaltending have positioned them as legitimate contenders in the Western Conference. Connor Hellebuyck's knee surgery earlier this season raised concerns, but the Jets' backup situation has steadied the ship.

Chicago's in full rebuild mode, and nights like this can be tough. The Blackhawks' young core is developing, but competing against playoff teams exposes the gap in depth and experience. The home crowd will be into it early, but Winnipeg's veterans know how to quiet road buildings with efficient hockey.

The Jets should control this game through two periods. The question is whether they get complacent against an inferior opponent. Chicago's been competitive in spots this season, pulling off upsets when their goaltending steals games. Winnipeg needs to stay professional and not look ahead to more challenging opponents on their schedule.

Devils @ Flames

Monday, 9:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Saddledome | ESPN+
Puck Line: NJD -1.5 (+165) / CGY +1.5 (-195) | ML: NJD -120 / CGY Even | O/U: 6.5

Jack Hughes and the Devils head to Calgary in what should be an entertaining affair. New Jersey's speed and skill make them dangerous against any opponent, and their power play ranks among the league's best. The Devils have embraced an up-tempo style that creates goals in bunches, though it leaves them vulnerable defensively at times.

Calgary just traded Andersson to Vegas, signaling they're sellers at the deadline. The Flames are in transition, and games like this become about development and evaluation more than results. That said, the Saddledome faithful will expect compete, and Calgary's remaining roster has enough talent to make this interesting.

The 6.5 total suggests both teams can score, and that feels right. New Jersey plays fast but sometimes loose, which opens up transition opportunities for Calgary's remaining skill players. This should be entertaining hockey with end-to-end action. New Jersey's talent should prevail, but Calgary won't go quietly.

Islanders @ Canucks

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Rogers Arena | ESPN+
Puck Line: NYI -1.5 (+172) / VAN +1.5 (-205) | ML: NYI -142 / VAN +120 | O/U: 5.5

The Islanders close out a West Coast trip in Vancouver, bringing their structured defensive system to Rogers Arena. New York under Patrick Roy has maintained their defensive identity while showing more offensive creativity. Ilya Sorokin remains one of the league's elite netminders, and he can steal games when the Islanders' offense struggles.

Vancouver's had an inconsistent season after their surprise run last year. The Canucks' depth scoring has dried up at times, and they've relied heavily on their top line to generate offense. Against a team as defensively sound as the Islanders, that top-heavy approach becomes problematic. The Isles eliminate time and space, and they don't beat themselves.

The 5.5 total is the lowest on the board, and it makes sense. Both teams prefer low-scoring, physical hockey. The Islanders won't open up, and Vancouver's struggled to score against teams that play tight. Expect a grind where the first goal carries significant weight. This feels like a 3-2 or 2-1 final.

Rangers @ Ducks

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Honda Center | ESPN+
Puck Line: NYR +1.5 (-190) / ANA -1.5 (+158) | ML: NYR +114 / ANA -135 | O/U: 6.0

An interesting price here with the Rangers as road underdogs in Anaheim. New York's had a disappointing season relative to expectations, but the Rangers remain talented enough to beat anyone on a given night. Igor Shesterkin's presence alone makes them competitive, and the Rangers' top-six forward group can score against any defense.

Anaheim being favored speaks to the oddsmakers' respect for their home ice advantage and recent form. The Ducks have surprised people this season with their young core taking steps forward. Their speed and skill have improved dramatically, and they play with an energy that makes Honda Center a tough building for visitors.

The Rangers might be catching Anaheim at the wrong time. The Ducks have won key games against better teams this season, and they feed off their home crowd. New York's inconsistency could doom them here, especially if they come out flat on the back end of a road trip. This line suggests value on the Rangers, but Anaheim's earned the favorite tag.
