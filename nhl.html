Devils @ Jets

Sunday, 2:00 PM ET | Canada Life Centre
Line: WPG -145 | O/U: 6.0

The Jets have been one of the league's most pleasant surprises this season, and Canada Life Centre is an absolute fortress. Winnipeg's combination of elite goaltending and a balanced offensive attack makes them dangerous against anyone. The crowd is always engaged, creating an energy that road teams struggle to match.

New Jersey came into this season with massive expectations after their breakout 2023-24 campaign, but inconsistency has been the story. Jack Hughes is still electric when healthy, but the Devils have had too many nights where they can't find the back of the net. Their power play has been frustrating - loads of talent, not enough execution.

Here's the travel angle: the Devils are on the second half of a Western Canada trip, and these games always seem to catch East Coast teams off guard. The time zone changes mess with sleep patterns, and Winnipeg in January is brutally cold. All those little factors add up when the Jets are already the better team on paper.

Penguins @ Bruins

Sunday, 5:00 PM ET | TD Garden
Line: BOS -165 | O/U: 5.5

TD Garden for an Original Six matchup on a Sunday evening? Sign me up. Boston's home ice advantage is among the best in hockey, and the Bruins have been stingy defensively all season. Their goaltending tandem has been rock solid, and the defensive pairings know exactly how to play in Jim Montgomery's system.

Pittsburgh is in a weird transition phase. Sidney Crosby is still producing at an absurd rate for his age, but the supporting cast isn't what it used to be. Evgeni Malkin has battled injuries, and the depth scoring has been inconsistent at best. The Penguins can still beat anyone on a given night, but stringing together quality performances has been the issue.

The low total at 5.5 tells you everything - expect a defensive chess match between two veteran-laden rosters who know how to play playoff-style hockey. Boston at home in these spots typically grinds you down with their structure. Pittsburgh will need Crosby to be superhuman to steal one here.

Blue Jackets @ Utah

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Delta Center
Line: UTA -130 | O/U: 6.0

Utah's inaugural NHL season has been a rollercoaster, but the home support in Salt Lake City has exceeded every expectation. Delta Center is LOUD, and fans who've never had professional hockey are showing up like it's Game 7 every night. The atmosphere creates problems for road teams who aren't prepared for the intensity.

Columbus has been quietly competitive this season, playing above expectations despite a thin roster. Johnny Gaudreau's playmaking keeps the offense afloat, and their young goalies have shown flashes of potential. The Blue Jackets won't lay down for anyone, but they're still a year or two away from seriously contending.

This is an intriguing matchup of two franchises in different stages of development. Utah is trying to establish an identity in their first season, while Columbus is building toward the future. The energy advantage at Delta Center should be significant - these fans have been waiting decades for NHL hockey.

Capitals @ Predators

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Bridgestone Arena
Line: NSH -125 | O/U: 5.5

Alex Ovechkin continues his pursuit of Wayne Gretzky's goal record, and every game feels historic when The Great Eight takes the ice. Washington has surrounded Ovi with enough talent to remain competitive, and their power play is still lethal when he's parked in his office on the left circle.

Nashville's had their struggles this season, but Bridgestone Arena remains one of the league's toughest environments. The crowd is always engaged, the catfish fly, and the Predators feed off that energy. Their defensive system can frustrate skilled teams, even when the offense isn't clicking.

This one feels like a coin flip. Both teams are in that middle tier of their respective conferences - good enough to make noise, not quite elite. The home-ice edge probably gives Nashville the slight advantage, but Washington has the superstar factor that can flip any game in an instant.

Golden Knights @ Sharks

Sunday, 8:00 PM ET | SAP Center
Line: VGK -210 | O/U: 6.0

Mitch Marner's trade from Toronto to Vegas in July 2025 has been an absolute home run for the Golden Knights. The chemistry between Marner and Jack Eichel is absurd - they're reading each other's minds on the ice, and Vegas's offense has become even more lethal. The Golden Knights are legitimate Cup contenders again.

San Jose is in the midst of a painful rebuild, and it shows. Macklin Celebrini, the first overall pick, is getting his NHL education the hard way on a team that's going to lose a lot of games. The Sharks are playing for the future, which means the present is pretty rough to watch some nights.

That -210 line is steep, but it's justified. Vegas is a significantly better team in every area, and the Sharks don't have the defensive structure to hang with elite opponents. The Golden Knights should roll here, though laying that juice always makes you nervous. San Jose's young guys might keep it respectable for a period or two.
