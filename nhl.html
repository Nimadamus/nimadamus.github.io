Islanders @ Rangers

Puck Line

Moneyline

Total
O/U 5.5

The Battle of New York comes to Madison Square Garden, and these rivalry games always deliver chaos. The Islanders blanked the Flyers 4-0 in their last outing, showcasing the defensive structure that has defined Patrick Roy's first season behind their bench. Roy has the Isles playing disciplined hockey, limiting high-danger chances and frustrating opponents into perimeter shots. Their 2.48 goals against per game ranks 6th in the league, a massive turnaround from last season's defensive struggles.

The Rangers snuck past Boston 4-3 last time out, continuing their home dominance at MSG where they're 18-6 this season. Igor Shesterkin remains the backbone of this team, posting a .921 save percentage that keeps New York in every game. The Rangers' power play (24.8%, 4th in NHL) is lethal when given opportunities, and the Islanders' penalty kill will be tested by New York's skilled forward group. Artemi Panarin (58 points) and Mika Zibanejad continue to carry the offensive load.

The 5.5 total reflects the tight-checking nature of these rivalry games. The Islanders are 6-3 in their last 9 meetings with the Rangers, and they consistently play their best hockey against the crosstown rival. Spencer Martin gets the start for the Rangers with Shesterkin resting, which changes the dynamic slightly, but this Rangers team at home is still a formidable opponent. Expect a physical, low-scoring affair decided by a goal or two in the third period.

David Pastrnak continues his MVP-caliber campaign with 61 points, and the Czech sniper finds space against even the tightest defensive schemes. The 5.5 total might look low, but both teams prioritize structure over run-and-gun offense. Expect a methodical game where Boston's superior depth eventually wears down the Flyers.

The back-to-back Stanley Cup champions roll into St. Louis as road favorites, and the Panthers look every bit as dangerous as the teams that hoisted the Cup in 2024 and 2025. Sergei Bobrovsky continues to defy age with a .918 save percentage, and Florida's forward depth remains the deepest in hockey. Matthew Tkachuk (54 points) brings his trademark blend of skill and agitation, while Aleksander Barkov anchors one of the league's best two-way lines.

St. Louis is fighting for playoff positioning in a tight Central Division race. Joel Hofer has emerged as a reliable starting goaltender, and the young Blues core is growing up before our eyes. Jordan Kyrou leads the offense, but St. Louis needs more contributions from their supporting cast to compete against elite opponents. Their 3.15 goals against per game suggests defensive vulnerabilities that Florida will exploit with their relentless forechecking system.

The 6.0 total is the highest among Thursday's early games, reflecting Florida's ability to generate offense in transition and St. Louis's tendency to give up quality chances. The Panthers are 7-2 in their last 9 road games, traveling exceptionally well as champions tend to do. St. Louis has the talent to hang around, but Florida's experience and depth should prevail in a game that could see multiple goals in the third period.

This one features two teams heading in opposite directions. Tampa Bay at 33-14 is one of the league's best, riding the continued excellence of Andrei Vasilevskiy between the pipes. Brayden Point (52 points) provides the secondary scoring Tampa needs, and their power play is clicking at 25.2%.

Winnipeg at 21-24 has been a massive disappointment this season. The Jets entered the year with playoff expectations, but injuries and inconsistent goaltending have derailed their campaign. Connor Hellebuyck has returned from knee surgery, but he's still shaking off rust, and the defense in front of him hasn't provided much help. Winnipeg ranks 24th in goals against, and their inability to keep pucks out of their net has made every game an uphill battle.

The -205 moneyline on Tampa is steep, but it accurately reflects the gap between these teams right now. The Lightning at home are 14-9, and Amalie Arena provides a hostile environment for visiting teams. The 5.5 total should stay under if Vasilevskiy is sharp, as Tampa's defense has been excellent at limiting high-danger chances. Winnipeg needs Hellebuyck to steal this one, and that's asking a lot from a goalie still working his way back to form.
