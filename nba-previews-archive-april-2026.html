Game 1 - Featured
ABC

76ers @ Celtics

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA

The 2-seed Boston Celtics (56-26) host the 7-seed Philadelphia 76ers (45-37) to open the Eastern Conference first round at 1:00 PM ET on ABC. Boston opens as a 12.5-point home favorite at -800 on the moneyline with Philadelphia at +550. Total 213.5. The storyline bigger than the line is Jayson Tatum's return. The Celtics' cornerstone tore his Achilles in the 2025 Eastern Conference Semifinals, missed the balance of that postseason and the front half of the 2025-26 regular season, and worked his way back to the floor in late February. He's been building minutes since, and Game 1 is his first real playoff basketball in eleven months.

The injury picture breaks hard toward Boston. Philadelphia is without Joel Embiid, who has been ruled out with a lingering abdominal issue that has cost him stretches all season. No Embiid means no rim-protecting center capable of matching the Celtics' Kristaps Porzingis inside, and no interior scoring hub capable of punishing Boston's smaller lineups. Tyrese Maxey is going to have to carry the primary creator load with Paul George as the secondary offensive option, and that tandem has been together enough this year for the fit to be functional. But asking the two of them to outscore the Jayson Tatum-Jaylen Brown-Porzingis triple at TD Garden is a significant ask, especially against the best playoff-ready defensive infrastructure in the East.

Boston's identity hasn't changed. Joe Mazzulla's offense pushes the league-leading three-point volume, Brown has carried more shot-creation responsibility in Tatum's absence and will slide back into the secondary role now, and Jrue Holiday and Derrick White remain the best defensive backcourt in any playoff bracket. The Celtics finished 30-11 at home this year and their collective playoff experience from the 2024 title run and the 2025 run before Tatum's injury is as deep as any roster in the league. Philadelphia's path to a cover is through Maxey producing 35-plus points and George hitting five-plus threes. Without Embiid to open drop-coverage looks, both of those outcomes become harder to engineer.

The 12.5 spread sits wider than most Game 1 home-favorite numbers, which is what happens when the road team is missing its All-Star center and the home team has every rotation piece available. The total of 213.5 reflects Boston's league-leading three-point volume balanced against the defensive grind Mazzulla demands in the half-court. Series odds have Boston at a heavy favorite to win in four or five games. The realistic underdog path for Philadelphia is a Game 1 ambush where Maxey goes nuclear and one of Tatum or Brown has a rusty first playoff half. First possession is 1:00 PM ET. ABC has the call. Game 1
ABC

Suns @ Thunder

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK

The defending champion and 1-seed Oklahoma City Thunder (64-18) host the 8-seed Phoenix Suns (45-37) to open their title defense. Oklahoma City is a 14.5-point home favorite at -1100 on the moneyline with Phoenix at +700. Total 215.5. This is the largest spread of the four Sunday openers, and it reflects the gap between the most complete roster in the league and a Suns team that backed into the 8-seed via the play-in tournament. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is the reigning NBA MVP. Jalen Williams is an All-Star. Chet Holmgren anchors the frontcourt. The Thunder went 64-18, finished with a league-best net rating, and have spent the last two weeks preparing specifically for this opener.

Phoenix's season has been uneven from the opening tip. Devin Booker has been the constant, but the collective rotation around him has lacked cohesion with Grayson Allen questionable with a knee issue and Jalen Green out with a hamstring strain. The one bright spot for Phoenix is that they actually beat OKC 135-103 on April 12 in what was effectively a Thunder load-management night, so the Suns at least know they can score in this building. The Thunder are resting multiple bench pieces with minor issues, with Isaiah Hartenstein (soleus), Nikola Topic (surgery), Jaylin Williams (heel), and Ousmane Dieng (calf) all confirmed out. Thomas Sorber is out for the postseason with an ACL tear.

The Suns' path to a Game 1 cover is through Booker creating 40-plus points and the secondary guard rotation producing any kind of shot-making. The flaw in that path is Cason Wallace and Luguentz Dort, the two most physical perimeter defenders in the West, who are going to take turns hounding Booker for 40-plus minutes. The Thunder's defensive system has finished top-three in defensive rating for three straight seasons. Their ability to switch one through five, apply ball pressure without fouling, and force Phoenix into late-clock isolation possessions is the structural problem the Suns have no answer for.

The 14.5 spread asks OKC to win a Game 1 by 15-plus against an 8-seed missing multiple rotation players. That's aggressive for a playoff Game 1 environment, but the Thunder have won their home games this season by an average of 15.5 points. The total of 215.5 is manageable if both teams settle into a half-court grind. Series odds have OKC at -1100 to advance, and realistically the only way the Suns push this to six games is if elite isolation scoring returns to Booker's bag for a full series. The Thunder roll out a preview of the deepest roster in the bracket. Tipoff is 3:30 PM ET on ABC.

Game 1
NBC

Magic @ Pistons

Sunday, 6:30 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI

The 1-seed Detroit Pistons host the 8-seed Orlando Magic in one of the most unlikely Eastern Conference openers in a decade. Detroit is an 8.5-point home favorite with Orlando priced at +340 to win the series and Detroit at -425. Total 218.5. The Pistons had a season-long breakthrough under JB Bickerstaff built around Cade Cunningham's continued ascent into legitimate All-NBA territory, Jalen Duren's interior dominance, and the addition of veteran wing pieces who turned Detroit's half-court offense into a legitimate top-ten unit. The franchise's first 1-seed playoff run since the Chauncey Billups era gets its Game 1 at Little Caesars.

Orlando gets into the series as the 8-seed after surviving the East play-in, and the roster takes a massive hit before Game 1 even tips. Paolo Banchero is ruled out with a left groin strain, which removes Orlando's best player, leading scorer at 21.7 points per game, and primary offensive hub. Moritz Wagner is also out continuing his recovery from a knee procedure. Jamahl Mosley is going to lean heavily on Franz Wagner to carry the creation load, with Jalen Suggs as the secondary ball-handler and Anthony Black filling the Banchero-sized hole at the forward spot. Orlando beat Detroit 123-107 on April 6, so the Magic know they can win a possession battle in this building, but that game was at full strength.

Cunningham is the series-defining player. His recovery from a collapsed lung in the late regular season is the kind of injury that leaves a window for re-aggravation, and while he's expected to play and has been ramping up practice participation, his Game 1 conditioning is a legitimate question mark. If he's at 100%, the Pistons' offensive ceiling is closer to 120 than 105. Duren's interior work against Orlando's perimeter-oriented frontcourt minus Banchero is the structural matchup Detroit exploits for four quarters. Ausar Thompson's defensive impact on Franz Wagner is the other central storyline.

The 8.5 spread reflects both the talent gap and the Banchero absence, but first-round Game 1s played at home by a team with no playoff experience can lean tight due to nerves. The Pistons haven't won a playoff game since 2008, and home-court adrenaline can cut both ways in young rosters. The 218.5 total sits right at the playoff Game 1 average and reflects two teams that play legitimate defense. Detroit should win outright and take Game 1 comfortably. Whether the 8.5 holds depends on Cunningham's conditioning and whether Orlando's young secondary pieces can offset the Banchero absence for one game. Tipoff is 6:30 PM ET on NBC.

Game 1
NBC

Trail Blazers @ Spurs

Sunday, 9:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX

The 2-seed San Antonio Spurs (62-20) host the 7-seed Portland Trail Blazers (42-40) in the prime-time closer of the Sunday slate. San Antonio is a 10.5-point home favorite at -470 on the moneyline with Portland at +360. Total 221.5. The Spurs' run from lottery afterthought to 2-seed in two years has been the most satisfying franchise build in the league. Victor Wembanyama's second season has been the anticipated leap into superstar territory. He's averaging over 24 points, 11 rebounds, and 4 blocks, anchoring what finished as a top-five defense by rim-protection metrics. De'Aaron Fox arrived in the February 2025 trade that reshaped the West, signed his four-year, $229 million extension, and has been the ideal backcourt partner for Wembanyama.

The injury picture for San Antonio is the variable. Wembanyama is day-to-day with a ribcage issue, though he's expected to play in Game 1. Luke Kornet is day-to-day with a shoulder issue. David Jones is out for the postseason with an ankle injury. The Spurs have the depth to absorb Kornet's absence, and Wembanyama suiting up at anything close to 85% is still the most impactful single player on the floor. San Antonio finished 32-8 at home. They closed the year at 30-4 in their last 34 games. They have won seven of their last ten matchups against Portland by double digits. Every structural advantage in this series starts with the home team.

Portland gets into the 7-seed having battled through a season defined by Damian Lillard's Achilles tear. Lillard is out for the postseason, which removes the scoring gravity the Blazers' offense has oriented around for years. Scoot Henderson is going to carry the primary creator load, with Deni Avdija as the secondary scorer and Shaedon Sharpe's shot-making as the X-factor. Chauncey Billups' rotation has leaned younger all year, and the playoff adjustment curve is significant. Getting one game on the road against Wembanyama's defensive gravity, without Lillard, is the closest Portland comes to a realistic path to a series win.

The 10.5 spread reflects the Wembanyama effect combined with Portland's Lillard-less offense. The 221.5 total is higher than you'd expect for a defense-heavy matchup because the Spurs play at a top-ten pace, and Wembanyama's transition offense generates easy looks when he's running. Series odds have San Antonio at -2000 to advance versus Portland at +1000. The most likely outcome is a four or five-game series with San Antonio comfortably winning. The Blazers' best path is Henderson and Sharpe combining for 50-plus points in a single game to steal one on the road. First possession is 9:00 PM ET on NBC. Game 2 is Tuesday at Frost Bank Center.
