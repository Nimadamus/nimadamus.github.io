Myrtle Beach Bowl, Gasparilla Bowl, and CFP First Round: Alabama vs Oklahoma

Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

Two 8-5 teams looking to end their seasons on a high note. Memphis has been one of the more entertaining teams to watch with their high-powered offense, but the Tigers have struggled on the road this year. Ryan Silverfield's squad relies heavily on the passing game and can put up points in bunches when clicking.

NC State has been inconsistent but dangerous at home. The Wolfpack have the defensive line to create pressure and could disrupt Memphis' timing. This should be a competitive back-and-forth affair - expect points and plenty of them. The team that forces turnovers late will walk away with the W.

Rebels @ Bobcats

2025-12-24T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN
UNLV: 10-3 | OHIO: 8-4

UNLV has had a breakout season at 10-3, making serious noise in the Mountain West. The Rebels have a dynamic offense led by their quarterback who's been one of the best in the conference. Barry Odom has completely transformed this program into a legitimate contender.

Ohio at 8-4 has been solid but they're catching a hot team at the wrong time. The Bobcats are well-coached and won't go down without a fight, but UNLV's talent advantage should show up in the second half. Look for the Rebels to control the tempo and pull away late.

Rockets @ Cardinals

2025-12-23T19:00Z | Flagler Credit Union Stadium | ESPN
TOL: 8-5 | LOU: 9-4

Louisville gets a favorable bowl matchup against a Toledo team that's feisty but outmatched. The Cardinals have been playing inspired football under Jeff Brohm, who has this program trending in the right direction. They've got playmakers on offense and a defense that can create havoc.

Toledo had a nice MAC season but the step up in competition is significant. The Rockets will compete and might keep it close early, but Louisville's depth and talent will wear them down. Expect the Cardinals to cover and win comfortably in the second half.

Hilltoppers @ Eagles

2025-12-23T22:30Z | Caesars Superdome | ESPN
WKU: 9-4 | USM: 7-6

Western Kentucky at 9-4 has quietly put together a solid season in Conference USA. The Hilltoppers have one of the better offenses in the Group of Five and can score on anyone. They're a nightmare to prepare for with their tempo and scheme diversity.

Southern Miss at 7-6 is just happy to be bowling after some rough years. The Golden Eagles have shown improvement but lack the firepower to keep up with WKU's offense. This should be a Hilltoppers win, though Southern Miss could keep it close if they control the clock and limit possessions.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

It's the Celebration Bowl and these are two of the best HBCU programs in the country going at it. South Carolina State at 10-3 has been dominant in the MEAC and comes in with championship pedigree. The Bulldogs are physical and play old-school power football.

Prairie View A&M at 10-4 won the SWAC and has their own high-powered attack. This matchup features two proud programs playing for bragging rights in the HBCU world. It's always an emotional game with incredible atmosphere. Expect a close, hard-fought battle decided by turnovers and special teams.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

Boise State at 9-5 had an up-and-down season but still finished strong in the Mountain West. Ashton Jeanty has been an absolute monster at running back, and the Broncos' ground game is their identity. When they commit to the run, they're tough to stop.

Washington at 9-4 is rebuilding after losing a lot of talent from last year's CFP team. The Huskies have shown flashes but inconsistency has plagued them all season. At home, they should have the crowd advantage. This is a close game that likely comes down to whether Washington can stop the Boise State run game. If Jeanty gets going, it could be a long night for the Huskies.

Trojans @ Gamecocks

2025-12-17T02:00Z | Cramton Bowl | ESPN
TROY: 8-6 | JVST: 9-5

This is a Sun Belt showdown between two programs headed in different directions. Troy at 8-6 has been decent but not spectacular, while Jacksonville State at 9-5 has made serious strides in their transition to the FBS level. The Gamecocks are ahead of schedule in their rebuild.

Jacksonville State plays at home and should have the edge in energy and motivation. Troy will compete but the Gamecocks have the better roster and home field advantage. Expect JSU to control the trenches and grind out a physical victory.

Monarchs @ Bulls

2025-12-17T22:00Z | Camping World Stadium | ESPN
ODU: 10-3 | USF: 9-4

Old Dominion at 10-3 has had a sneaky great season that nobody's talking about. The Monarchs play solid football on both sides of the ball and don't beat themselves. Ricky Rahne has done excellent work building this program into a consistent winner.

South Florida at 9-4 is also having a bounce-back year under Alex Golesh. The Bulls have rediscovered their offensive identity and can put up points. This should be an entertaining matchup of two evenly-matched teams. Home field gives USF a slight edge, but ODU is battle-tested and won't be intimidated.

Cajuns @ Hens

2025-12-18T01:40Z | Hancock Whitney Stadium | ESPN
UL: 6-7 | DEL: 7-6

Louisiana at 6-7 limps into this bowl after a disappointing season. The Ragin' Cajuns had higher expectations but couldn't find consistency. They've got talent on the roster but haven't put it together for a full game all year.

Delaware at 7-6 is transitioning to FBS and this is a big stage for the program. The Blue Hens are excited to show they belong and should play with a chip on their shoulder. In a game between two mediocre teams, motivation matters - and Delaware wants this one more.

Bears @ Wolves

2025-12-19T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN2
MOST: 7-6 | ARST: 7-6

Two 7-6 teams that are mirror images of each other. Missouri State has been solid in their transition but still finding their footing at the FBS level. The Bears are physical and play hard but lack the depth of more established programs.

Arkansas State is the more experienced team at this level and that should matter in a close game. The Red Wolves play at a neutral site but are the more polished squad. Expect a low-scoring defensive battle where the team that avoids mistakes wins.

Tide @ Sooners

2025-12-20T01:00Z | Memorial Stadium (Norman, OK) | ABC, ESPN
ALA: 11-3 | OU: 10-3

Now THIS is a bowl game. Two traditional powers with a combined 17 national championships going at it. Alabama at 11-3 had a "down" year by their standards but still won double-digit games. Kalen DeBoer's first year has had growing pains but the talent is still elite.

Oklahoma at 10-3 has had a resurgent season under Brent Venables. The Sooners are physical on defense and have found an identity in their second year in the SEC. This game is all about pride and brand - neither program wants to end the season with a loss to a rival. Expect an intense, physical battle between two teams that know how to win big games.

Owls @ Broncos

2025-12-19T16:00Z | Brooks Stadium (SC) | ESPN
KENN: 10-4 | WMU: 10-4

Kennesaw State at 10-4 is another FBS transition story, and they've done it impressively. The Owls came from the FCS and haven't missed a beat, proving they can compete at this level. Their option-style offense is a nightmare to prepare for in bowl season with limited practice time.

Western Michigan at 10-4 had a great MAC season and is a well-coached program. The Broncos are used to winning and won't be caught off guard. This is a fun matchup of two hot teams. The difference might come down to scheme - can WMU prepare for Kennesaw's unique attack in two weeks?

Dukes @ Ducks

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Autzen Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
JMU: 12-2 | ORE: 12-1

James Madison at 12-2 has been one of the best stories in college football this year. Bob Chesney took over when Curt Cignetti left for Indiana and hasn't missed a beat - the Dukes just keep winning. They're physical, well-coached, and play with a chip on their shoulder.

Oregon at 12-1 is an elite program with CFP aspirations. The Ducks have the talent advantage at every position and play at an incredibly fast tempo. As good as JMU is, this is a significant step up in competition. Oregon should win comfortably, but don't be shocked if the Dukes keep it closer than expected early. JMU is fearless.

Wave @ Rebels

2025-12-20T20:30Z | Vaught-Hemingway Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
TULN: 11-3 | MISS: 12-1

Look, I get it - laying points against Tulane feels wrong after the season they've had. But Ole Miss is just on another level. The Rebels already destroyed Tulane 45-10 back in September, and I don't care how much they've improved since then. You don't overcome a 35-point beatdown in a few months.

Ole Miss at 12-1 is playing for CFP positioning and won't take their foot off the gas. Pete Golding's squad has been dominant at home all year and the crowd at Vaught-Hemingway will be rocking. Tulane's a good team but this is a bad matchup. The Green Wave don't have the athletes to hang with the Rebels' speed.

Hurricanes @ Aggies

2025-12-20T17:00Z | Kyle Field | ABC, ESPN
MIA: 11-2 | TA&M: 11-2

This is the marquee bowl matchup everyone wanted to see. Two 11-2 teams with legitimate talent going head-to-head. Miami has been electric on offense all season, with Cam Ward putting up video game numbers. The Hurricanes can score on anyone and have playmakers everywhere.

Texas A&M has been physical and tough in the SEC, which is no easy feat. The Aggies play smashmouth football and will try to control the tempo and keep Miami's offense on the sideline. This game comes down to style - can A&M slow Miami down and make it a grind? Or will the Canes' speed and skill overwhelm the Aggies? This should be an instant classic with points and drama.
