Pittsburgh visits the Islanders in a tight matchup. Vegas hosts Vancouver with one of the deepest forward groups in hockey. Toronto travels to Anaheim where the surprisingly strong Ducks await. Game 1 - Marquee
Massive Favorite

Flames @ Avalanche

Monday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Puck Line
COL -1.5 (-140) / CGY +1.5 (+120)
Moneyline
CGY +345 / COL -390
Total
O/U 6.5

This is a mismatch on paper and everyone knows it. Colorado at -390 is one of the biggest moneyline favorites you'll see on any given night in the NHL, and the 48-14-10 Avalanche have absolutely earned that price through a dominant campaign that has them positioned as legitimate Stanley Cup contenders. Ball Arena has been a nightmare for visiting teams all season, and the altitude, the crowd noise, and the sheer depth of talent on this roster combine to create an environment that overwhelms opponents before the first puck drops. Calgary at 31-34-8 is walking into a buzzsaw, and the +345 moneyline tells you exactly how the market feels about the Flames' chances tonight.

Nathan MacKinnon and Cale Makar continue to be the most devastating one-two combination in hockey, and their ability to control games from both ends of the ice makes Colorado nearly impossible to contain when they're rolling at home. MacKinnon's vision and acceleration through the neutral zone create chances out of nothing, and Makar's ability to jump into the rush as a fourth attacker gives the Avalanche an offensive dimension that most teams simply can't replicate. The supporting cast has been excellent this season too, with the depth scoring providing insurance on nights when the top line isn't clicking at its peak. This roster is built for a deep playoff run, and games like this against inferior opponents are opportunities to fine-tune the machine heading into April.

Calgary's season has been a slow unraveling. The Flames don't have the defensive structure or the goaltending consistency to hang with elite teams for 60 minutes, and their road record reflects that reality. Nazem Kadri provides some offensive punch, but the depth behind him drops off significantly, and when you're facing a team as deep as Colorado, you can't survive on one line carrying the load. The Flames' penalty kill has been a liability all year, and that's a death sentence against an Avalanche power play that converts at an elite rate with MacKinnon and Makar quarterbacking from the point. The altitude factor in Denver compounds the problem, as tired legs in the third period against Colorado's relentless forecheck is a recipe for a lopsided scoreline.

The 6.5 total reflects the expectation that Colorado is going to put up goals in bunches, and the question is really whether Calgary's offense can contribute enough to push the total over. The Avalanche have the firepower to score four or five on their own, and if the Flames' defense breaks down early, this could turn into a rout by the second intermission. The puck line at COL -1.5 (-140) is the number that sharp bettors will focus on, because when the Avalanche win at home against weaker opponents, they rarely do it by a single goal. Colorado's depth, their home-ice dominance, and the massive gap in talent between these two rosters make this one of the most predictable outcomes on the Monday slate.

ESPN+

Penguins @ Islanders

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | UBS Arena, Elmont, NY
Puck Line
NYI -1.5 (+205) / PIT +1.5 (-230)
Moneyline
PIT +105 / NYI -125
Total
O/U 6.5

The Islanders are modest home favorites at -125, but getting Sidney Crosby at +105 in any building is going to attract attention from bettors who understand the value of a future Hall of Famer on the road with something to prove. Pittsburgh's season hasn't gone according to plan, but Crosby continues to play with the kind of fire and intensity that makes the Penguins competitive every single night regardless of the overall roster talent. UBS Arena should be rocking for this one, and the Islanders have been a tougher team to beat at home than their record might suggest, with Mathew Barzal providing the kind of game-breaking speed that can tilt a tight matchup in New York's favor.

The -125 line on the Islanders tells you the market doesn't see a massive gap between these two clubs, and that's probably the right read. New York has been frustratingly inconsistent this season, capable of looking like a legitimate playoff team one night and a lottery team the next. Their defensive structure under Patrick Roy has improved, but the goal scoring comes in waves, and when Barzal is quiet, the Islanders don't have the depth to compensate. Pittsburgh's willingness to play a grinding, physical game can neutralize the Islanders' speed advantage and turn this into the kind of low-possession, board-battle war that Crosby thrives in.

The 6.5 total is set high for a game between two teams that aren't exactly offensive juggernauts, and that number creates some intrigue. If both goaltenders are sharp, this could easily be a 3-2 or 2-1 affair that makes the under look obvious in hindsight. But both defenses have been leaky enough at times to produce crooked numbers, and a single sloppy period from either side could push the scoring into the four-or-five range for one team. The goaltending matchup is going to determine the tempo, and whoever gets the first power play goal might dictate the entire flow of the game from that point forward.

ESPN+

Canucks @ Golden Knights

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas, NV
Puck Line
VGK -1.5 (+110) / VAN +1.5 (-125)
Moneyline
VAN +310 / VGK -360
Total
O/U 6.5

Vegas at -360 is an enormous home favorite, and T-Mobile Arena is going to be a hostile environment for a Canucks team that has struggled mightily on the road this season. The Golden Knights possess one of the deepest forward groups in the entire Western Conference, with elite playmaking vision and offensive creativity running through every line. Jack Eichel has been sensational all season, and the supporting cast around him has created a multi-dimensional attack that is nearly impossible to game-plan against. Vancouver's defensive structure simply isn't built to contain this many weapons for 60 minutes, and the +310 moneyline reflects the enormous gap between these two rosters.

The Canucks' season has been a major disappointment after last year's run to relevance, and road games against elite opponents have exposed every weakness in their lineup. Vancouver needs their goaltending to steal the show and their top forwards to generate something from limited opportunities, because extended time in the defensive zone against Vegas's relentless forecheck is going to lead to breakdowns. The Golden Knights are relentless in transition and their power play has been lethal at home, converting at a rate that punishes any opponent foolish enough to take penalties. If Vancouver can't stay disciplined and keep this a 5-on-5 affair, the scoreboard could get ugly in a hurry.

The 6.5 total feels about right for a game where one team has the offensive firepower to score four or five and the other team's defense has been porous enough to allow it. Vegas at home in a late-night time slot with a raucous crowd behind them is a situation that has produced lopsided results all season. The puck line at VGK -1.5 (+110) is one of the more interesting numbers on the slate, because the Golden Knights don't tend to play tight games against inferior opponents in their own building. They push the pace, overwhelm their opponents with waves of offense, and put games away before the third period even starts.

Game 4
ESPN+

Maple Leafs @ Ducks

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Honda Center, Anaheim, CA
Puck Line
ANA -1.5 (+124) / TOR +1.5 (-140)
Moneyline
TOR +170 / ANA -190
Total
O/U 6.5

If you told anyone three years ago that Anaheim would be -190 home favorites against the Toronto Maple Leafs, they would have laughed you out of the room. But here we are, and the Ducks' remarkable resurgence this season has turned Honda Center into a legitimate home-ice advantage. Anaheim's young core has developed faster than anyone projected, and their blend of speed, skill, and defensive commitment has made them a team that nobody wants to face in a playoff race. Toronto at +170 brings Auston Matthews and his generational goal-scoring ability, but the Maple Leafs' road struggles this season have been a persistent problem that even Matthews can't single-handedly fix.

The Ducks have been one of the best stories in the NHL this season, transforming from a rebuilding project into a genuine contender seemingly overnight. Their defensive structure has tightened considerably, and the goaltending has been reliable enough to give the young forwards confidence to play their game without worrying about making mistakes. Anaheim's transition game is fast and dangerous, and their ability to capitalize on turnovers has been a nightmare for teams that try to force plays through the neutral zone. Toronto's tendency to get careless with the puck at times could play right into the Ducks' hands.

Matthews is always the wild card for Toronto, because his ability to score from anywhere in the offensive zone makes the Leafs a threat regardless of how the rest of the game is going. But the Leafs need more than just Matthews to win in Anaheim tonight, and the secondary scoring has been inconsistent on the road. The 6.5 total suggests both offenses should produce, and that feels right given Toronto's defensive inconsistencies and Anaheim's ability to generate chances in transition. This could be a fun, wide-open game where the young Ducks play with fearless energy and the veteran Leafs try to impose their will through individual skill.

Game 5
ESPN+

Blues @ Sharks

Monday, 10:30 PM ET | SAP Center, San Jose, CA
Puck Line
SJ -1.5 (+220) / STL +1.5 (-250)
Moneyline
STL -110 / SJ -110
Total
O/U 5.5

The late-night closer is a genuine coin flip, with both teams sitting at -110 and the market refusing to separate them. And honestly, that's the right call. San Jose's transformation this season has been powered by the otherworldly talent of Macklin Celebrini, whose pursuit of the 100-point mark at just 19 years old has been one of the most electrifying individual storylines in the NHL. Celebrini has single-handedly changed the identity of this franchise, and SAP Center has gone from a quiet, half-empty building to a rocking arena where the fans show up specifically to watch a generational talent put on a show. St. Louis brings enough experience and grit with their veteran group to make this a legitimate toss-up.

The Blues have been a middle-of-the-pack team all season, never dominant enough to pull away from the pack but never bad enough to fall completely out of the picture. Their veteran leadership and playoff experience give them a steadiness that younger teams sometimes lack, and Robert Thomas has been excellent running the offense. St. Louis plays a physical, structured game that can frustrate skilled opponents by limiting time and space in the offensive zone. If the Blues can successfully contain Celebrini's zone entries and transition opportunities, they have enough depth scoring to steal a road win against a team that leans heavily on one player to generate offense.

But containing Celebrini is easier said than done. The kid has the kind of speed, vision, and finishing ability that makes defensive game plans look foolish, and when he's feeling it at SAP Center with the crowd behind him, there isn't a defensive pairing in the league that can consistently shut him down. His ability to create something out of nothing on every shift puts constant pressure on opposing defenses, and the Blues' blue line will need to be at their absolute best to limit his impact. The 5.5 total is set conservatively, reflecting the possibility that this becomes a tight, grinding affair where both teams play responsible hockey and the goaltenders keep things close into the third period. A single power play conversion or a timely Celebrini highlight-reel goal could be the difference in a game that feels destined to go down to the wire.
