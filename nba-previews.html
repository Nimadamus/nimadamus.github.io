8-Game Monday Slate

NBA Previews Today

Monday's 8-game slate is headlined by a potential NBA Finals preview as the Detroit Pistons (54-20) visit the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder (59-16), the two best records in basketball. Elsewhere, the surging Hawks host Boston on NBCSN, the Spurs look to obliterate Chicago behind Wembanyama and Fox, and Luka Doncic's Lakers welcome the woeful Wizards to close out the night on the West Coast.

76ers @ Heat

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Kaseya Center, Miami, FL
Spread
PHI -2.5
Moneyline
PHI -142 / MIA +120
Total
O/U 245.5

This is a playoff positioning game that both teams desperately need, and the 245.5 total tells you everything about how Vegas expects this one to play out. Philadelphia (41-33) and Miami (39-36) are separated by just two games in the Eastern Conference standings, and with the postseason right around the corner, neither team can afford to sleepwalk through this one. The 76ers come in as 2.5-point road favorites, which speaks volumes about how the market views these two rosters right now.

The Heat are a fundamentally different team than the one that made deep playoff runs with Jimmy Butler. Butler is now in Golden State nursing a torn ACL, and Miami has had to completely reinvent its identity this season. Andrew Wiggins, who came over in the roster shuffle, has given them a solid two-way wing, but Bam Adebayo and Tyler Herro have shouldered the lion's share of the offensive burden.

Philadelphia's story this season revolves around Tyrese Maxey's ascension into a legitimate superstar. Joel Embiid's availability, as always, looms large over any Sixers game. When Embiid is on the floor, Philly's ceiling is conference finals caliber. The spread suggests the market believes Embiid is likely in the lineup tonight.

The 245.5 total is the highest on tonight's board. Both of these teams play at a frenetic pace when their offenses are clicking, and neither defense has been particularly stingy down the stretch. For playoff seeding purposes, this is essentially a must-win for Miami at home.

NBCSN

Celtics @ Hawks

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | State Farm Arena, Atlanta, GA
Spread
ATL -2.5
Moneyline
BOS +114 / ATL -135
Total
O/U 223.5

Atlanta as a 2.5-point home favorite over Boston in late March tells you everything about how these seasons have gone. The Hawks (42-33) have quietly built something real, while the Celtics (50-24) have been gutting their way through without Jayson Tatum, who suffered a devastating Achilles injury that ended his season. Boston is still a quality team, but the loss of Tatum has fundamentally changed their ceiling.

Jaylen Brown has been nothing short of heroic carrying this Celtics team. He has had to shoulder a usage rate that would break most players, and to his credit, he has kept Boston firmly in the upper tier of the East. But there is a noticeable drop-off when Brown has to create everything from scratch.

Atlanta has found its groove with Jalen Johnson taking a massive leap this year, emerging as a legitimate two-way force. The Hawks at State Farm Arena have been a tough out all season, and their offensive rating at home ranks in the top ten league-wide.

The 223.5 total is moderate, suggesting a competitive game rather than a shootout. Boston's defense remains elite even without Tatum. Can Atlanta prove they belong with the true contenders? The national television audience gets a treat tonight.

Bulls @ Spurs

Monday, 8:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX
Spread
SA -18.5
Moneyline
CHI +1000 / SA -1800
Total
O/U 244.5

Eighteen and a half points. San Antonio (56-18) is laying nearly three touchdowns against Chicago (29-45). The Spurs have been an absolute buzzsaw this season, and the pairing of Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox has turned San Antonio from a rebuilding project into a legitimate championship contender in record time. Fox, acquired from Sacramento in February 2025, has injected the backcourt speed and creation that this team was desperately missing.

Wembanyama continues to do things that should not be physically possible. His combination of rim protection, perimeter shooting, and playmaking at 7-foot-4 has broken every analytical model. Fox running the pick-and-roll with Wemby has produced one of the most efficient two-man actions in the entire NBA this year.

Chicago shipped Zach LaVine to the Kings and has fully committed to the rebuild. The talent gap between these two rosters is a canyon. The Bulls rank near the bottom in both offensive and defensive efficiency.

The Frost Bank Center crowd has embraced this team with a fervor not seen since the Duncan dynasty. The real question is not whether the Spurs win, but whether 18.5 points is enough to account for the sheer talent disparity.

Phoenix (41-33) is in a dogfight for playoff positioning, and games like this against Memphis (25-49) are trap spots that can derail a season. The Suns are 12.5-point road favorites, reflecting just how far the Grizzlies have fallen. Memphis has been gutted by injuries and roster turnover, with 14 players on the injury report.

Devin Booker has been magnificent this season carrying a roster that lost Kevin Durant to the Rockets last July. Without KD, the offensive load has fallen squarely on Booker's shoulders, and he has responded with one of the best seasons of his career, averaging 25.5 PPG with 27.9 PPG in March alone.

Memphis is in full developmental mode. Ja Morant's availability has been a question mark throughout the year, and when he is not on the floor, Memphis' offense becomes stagnant and predictable.

For the Suns, this is a business trip. The 12.5-point spread is steep for a road team, but Phoenix has the talent advantage at virtually every position.

Dallas (24-50) is living in a completely different reality than the one Mavericks fans knew a year ago. Trading Luka Doncic to the Lakers in February 2025 ripped the heart out of this franchise. Yes, they landed Cooper Flagg with the number one overall pick, and the kid has shown flashes of brilliance, but building around a rookie while trying to remain competitive is a near-impossible balancing act.

Minnesota (45-29) is everything Dallas wishes it could be: a cohesive, talented, playoff-tested roster with a legitimate superstar. Anthony Edwards has cemented himself as one of the five best players in the NBA this season. Rudy Gobert continues to anchor one of the league's best defenses.

The 7.5-point spread feels right given the talent disparity. Dallas at home can occasionally muster enough energy to keep things closer than expected, as the American Airlines Center crowd has rallied behind Flagg.

Minnesota's defensive identity travels well, and they have been dominant against sub-.500 teams all season. The Timberwolves rank in the top three in defensive efficiency.

Cleveland (46-28) rolls into Salt Lake City as a 16.5-point road favorite. The Cavaliers have been one of the most consistent teams in the Eastern Conference, riding Donovan Mitchell, Darius Garland, and Evan Mobley. Utah (21-54) owns the worst record in the Western Conference and has been a punching bag all year.

Mitchell returning to Utah always adds a layer of intrigue. He spent the first years of his career in Salt Lake City and became a fan favorite before the trade. His scoring average sits in the upper echelon of the league, and his ability to take over games in the fourth quarter makes Cleveland dangerous in every contest.

Evan Mobley's continued development might be the most important storyline in Cleveland this season. His ability to switch onto guards on the perimeter while also protecting the rim gives the Cavaliers a defensive versatility that most teams cannot replicate.

The Jazz are led by Lauri Markkanen, who has been a bright spot on an otherwise bleak roster. The 242.5 total reflects the expectation that Cleveland will push tempo and pile up points against a Utah defense that ranks near the bottom of the league. This one could get out of hand early.

Pistons @ Thunder

OKC -11.5
Moneyline
DET +500 / OKC -700

O/U 218.5

Here it is, the game of the night. The two best records in basketball collide as the defending champion Thunder (59-16) host the resurgent Pistons (54-20). Detroit, a franchise that was the laughingstock of the league just two seasons ago, is 54-20 and has established itself as a legitimate title contender. Oklahoma City is running away with the best record in the league at a historic 31-6 at home.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is the frontrunner for MVP, averaging 31.4 points per game on 55.1% shooting. He just dropped 30 on the Knicks last night and has now scored 20 or more in 135 consecutive games, extending his own NBA record. Jalen Williams has emerged as one of the best second options in the league, and Chet Holmgren provides rim protection and floor spacing that makes OKC's offensive spacing almost impossible to solve.

Detroit is without Cade Cunningham, who suffered a collapsed lung on March 17 and is out until at least April 2. But the Pistons have gone 5-1 without their franchise player, showing incredible depth and resilience. Their 7-1 ATS record as a road underdog is the best mark in the NBA this season, a fascinating trend against a Thunder team that is just 15-20 ATS as home favorites.

The 218.5 total is the lowest on tonight's entire board, reflecting the defensive intensity both teams bring. The 11.5-point spread feels steep for a game between the two best records in basketball, and 92 percent of the public is on Detroit against the spread. This is appointment television and the marquee matchup of a loaded Monday slate.

Wizards @ Lakers

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Spread
LAL -15.5
Moneyline
WAS +800 / LAL -1350
Total
O/U 236.5

The nightcap brings us to Los Angeles, where the Lakers (48-26) host the woeful Washington Wizards (17-57). The Lakers have been transformed since acquiring Luka Doncic from Dallas in February 2025, and the pairing of Doncic with the existing LA roster has turned this team into a genuine championship contender in the loaded Western Conference.

Doncic has been absolutely absurd in a Lakers uniform, averaging approximately 33 points per game while dishing out assists at a rate that makes everyone around him better. His ability to control the tempo of a game, manipulate defenses with his step-back three, and find cutters with impossible passes has given Los Angeles an offensive ceiling they have not had in years. Austin Reaves has thrived as a secondary playmaker.

Washington is, to put it charitably, not good. The Wizards own the worst record in the Eastern Conference at 17-57. Jordan Poole provides some offensive spark, and Bilal Coulibaly has shown flashes, but the roster is thin and outmatched against quality opponents.

The 236.5 total is elevated, reflecting the Lakers' offensive firepower and Washington's inability to get stops. Doncic in particular has shown no mercy against inferior competition, often putting up eye-popping first-half numbers before sitting the fourth quarter. The 15.5-point spread is massive, but the talent gap is even bigger.
