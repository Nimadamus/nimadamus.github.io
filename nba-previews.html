NBA Previews Today

Meanwhile, the San Antonio Spurs roll into Milwaukee against a Bucks squad so shorthanded you almost feel bad laying the number, and Phoenix wraps up the night hosting a Jazz team that barely has a roster to dress.

Game 1 of 6 League Pass

SAS (55-18) @ MIL (29-43)

Saturday, 3:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee
Spread
SAS -18.5
Moneyline
SAS -2222 / MIL +1100
Total
O/U 225.5

Let's be honest about what this game actually is. The Milwaukee Bucks are going to send out a collection of G-League callups and end-of-roster guys against the second-best team in the Western Conference, and San Antonio is supposed to cover 18.5 points. The Bucks are without Giannis Antetokounmpo (knee hyperextension), Damian Lillard (Achilles management), Bobby Portis (wrist, questionable), and Kuzma (Achilles, questionable). That is their four most significant players, gone. Whatever Milwaukee puts on the floor today is not an NBA-caliber lineup in any traditional sense.

The Spurs are riding an 8-game win streak and have locked up second in the West at 55-18. De'Aaron Fox is listed as day-to-day but is expected to suit up, which matters because San Antonio's depth and pace would absolutely destroy a shorthanded Bucks squad even without their star guard. The Spurs have gone 16-8-1 ATS over their last 25 games, showing they don't just win, they cover. They play through the fourth quarter, they don't take nights off, and Victor Wembanyama's competitive instincts don't have a dial-it-back setting.

The number is enormous and yet somehow it doesn't feel crazy given the circumstances. Milwaukee's replacement-level roster is going to struggle to generate offense, turn the ball over frequently, and get torched in transition. San Antonio's defense, ranked fourth in defensive rating on the road this season, will have a field day. The Spurs average 118.9 points per game, and if they push pace against a depleted front line, the points are going to pile up fast.

The total of 225.5 is where the real intrigue lies. On paper, San Antonio scores at will, but Milwaukee's replacement roster may play at a dramatically slower pace and miss enough shots to keep the raw scoring down. The Spurs won't need 65 points to win this one comfortably, and garbage time minutes could compress the offense late. The spread is the story here. This is one of those rare games where the market is essentially asking bettors to decide whether they trust a healthy contender on an 8-game heater against a team that lost its four best players to injury.

DET (53-20) @ MIN (45-28)

Saturday, 5:30 PM ET | Target Center, Minneapolis
Spread
MIN -2.5
Moneyline
DET +118 / MIN -138
Total
O/U 223.5

This is the most fascinating game on the entire Saturday slate. Two elite teams, both gutted by injury, playing a game with genuine playoff seeding implications and a spread that feels almost insultingly small given what Detroit is dealing with. Cade Cunningham, the engine of the Pistons' historic turnaround season, has been out since March 17 with a collapsed lung. Isaiah Stewart (calf) and Marcus Sasser (hip) are also unavailable. The question isn't whether Detroit can win this game, it's whether they have enough functional players to stay competitive against a Wolves team that has been quietly clicking without Anthony Edwards, who remains out with a knee injury and is 1-2 weeks from re-evaluation.

Here's what makes this genuinely compelling rather than just a mismatch on paper: Detroit is 5-0 ATS as an underdog of 2.5 points or more this season. Five for five. This Pistons team has bought into the fight-for-every-game mentality that drove them to the number one seed in the East, and they don't quit when the names on the back of the jersey change. Their depth is real. Their culture is real. Minnesota, by contrast, is 23-30 ATS when favored by 2.5 points or more. That's a number that should make you stop and think.

Minnesota has gone 4-1 since Edwards went down, which is admirable, but those wins came against teams with a combined record that isn't exactly intimidating. Detroit without Cunningham is a different test. The Pistons' second unit has stepped up, and whoever has been starting in Cade's place has been forcing opponents to respect the offense enough to prevent easy layups and transition buckets. The Wolves rank 12th in defensive rating at home over the last 15 games, which isn't elite, and Detroit's ball movement without their star actually generates more open catch-and-shoot opportunities.

The total of 223.5 looks right given both teams are navigating injuries and playing a grind-it-out style in the absence of their best offensive players. Neither team is going to push pace the way they do at full strength. For the spread, the historical ATS data is a blinking light. Detroit has covered in this exact role all year. Minnesota hasn't been reliable as a short home favorite. This is the kind of game where the public hammers the home team on a national broadcast, the line barely moves, and then something unexpected happens in the fourth quarter.

Game 3 of 6 League Pass

PHI (40-33) @ CHA (39-34)

Saturday, 6:00 PM ET | Spectrum Center, Charlotte
Spread
CHA -5.5
Moneyline
PHI +198 / CHA -240
Total
O/U 232.5

The Charlotte Hornets have been one of the best stories in the second half of the NBA season. Five straight wins, seven of their last eight, and a team that has figured out its identity around LaMelo Ball and a three-point barrage that opposing defenses simply cannot contain. Charlotte leads the entire NBA in made threes at 16.3 per game and ranks third in three-point percentage at 38.2%. When they're on, they are impossible to game-plan against.

The 76ers are showing up in an unusual state of flux. Tyrese Maxey is out with a finger injury and Oubre is unavailable due to an elbow problem, which strips Philadelphia of two rotational pieces they need to be functional. But the headline is the opposite of what you'd expect: both Paul George and Joel Embiid are back. George returned from his 25-game suspension and dropped 28 points. Embiid came back from his oblique issue and delivered 35. The sheer offensive firepower those two bring changes the entire calculus of this matchup.

This is a fascinating chess match because of what each team does best. Charlotte's three-point bombardment is most effective when the defense is spread thin chasing shooters, but Embiid in the paint collapses coverage and creates hard decisions. LaMelo Ball is healthy and playing some of the best basketball of his career, and his ability to create in the pick-and-roll is a genuine problem for Philly's bigs on the perimeter. The 232.5 total feels low given how Philadelphia played in their last game, a 157-137 obliteration of Chicago.

The spread of 5.5 favoring Charlotte feels like the market saying something real here. Philly's backcourt depth without Maxey is a concern over 48 minutes, and the Hornets at home on a winning streak are genuinely dangerous. Charlotte's home record this season has been excellent, and their three-point shooting creates the kind of scoring variance that can blow a game open quickly. Whether Philadelphia's star duo can carry a shorthanded roster on the road against a team this hot is the real question.

SAC (19-55) @ ATL (41-33)

Saturday, 7:30 PM ET | State Farm Arena, Atlanta
Spread
ATL -14.5
Moneyline
SAC +700 / ATL -1100
Total
O/U 236.5

The Sacramento Kings have had one of the most catastrophically unlucky seasons in recent memory, and at this point they're just trying to get to the offseason in one piece. Domantas Sabonis, Zach LaVine, De'Andre Hunter, and Drew Eubanks are all done for the year. That is nearly the entire starting lineup, wiped out by injury. Sacramento has been fielding lineups that would struggle in the G-League, and their 19-55 record reflects it.

The Hawks are rolling. Eleven consecutive home wins at State Farm Arena, which is a number that commands respect regardless of opponent quality. Atlanta has been averaging 124.1 points per game over their last 15 games, a genuinely elite offensive pace that reflects how well their pieces have come together in the second half of the season. It's worth noting that Trae Young is no longer in Atlanta, having been traded to Washington, but the Hawks have figured out their identity without him.

Sacramento's injury decimation creates a very specific problem in a game like this. It's not just that Atlanta is better, it's that the Kings don't have the personnel to run a coherent defensive scheme or execute reliable half-court offense against a top-tier Atlanta defense. The Hawks are going to attack the weak links in Sacramento's lineup repeatedly, and the second and third quarters are where these games tend to become genuinely ugly.

The 14.5 spread and the 236.5 total tell the full story of what Vegas thinks is happening here. Atlanta is going to put up 130-plus in a performance that will look clinical rather than spectacular. The question for anyone interested in the number is whether the Kings' young players fight enough to keep the margin reasonable or whether this becomes a national broadcast embarrassment. Sacramento's coaches are presumably using these final weeks to evaluate talent for the offseason.

CHI (29-44) @ MEM (24-49)

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | FedExForum, Memphis
Spread
CHI -3.5
Moneyline
CHI -162 / MEM +144
Total
O/U 245.5

This is the highest total on the entire Saturday board at 245.5, and when you look at what these two rosters are working with right now, it starts to make a strange kind of sense. Memphis is without Ja Morant for the season after his elbow UCL surgery, Zach Edey is done, and Brandon Clarke, Scotty Pippen Jr., and Kentavious Caldwell-Pope are all unavailable. The Grizzlies are on a 5-game losing streak and playing with a patchwork roster.

Chicago is the favorite here at 3.5 points, which reflects the Bulls having a marginally more functional roster rather than any genuine offensive or defensive quality. The Bulls are 29-44, which is exactly what they are, a middle-of-nowhere team finishing out a season without playoff stakes and without a clear identity beyond "we have some professional basketball players." Their motivation to execute in a road game against Memphis is a genuine question.

The offensive environment is where this game lives. Memphis's defensive rating over the last 15 games without their starters has been genuinely alarming, and Chicago's offense, while not explosive, is capable of generating consistent half-court scoring against a defense with this many holes. The problem is Chicago's defense isn't exactly suffocating either, and Memphis's younger players who have been getting increased minutes have shown flashes of offensive creativity.

The total is the real conversation piece here. At 245.5, you need both teams combining for somewhere in the 123-122 range to hit the over, which, given the defensive quality of both these rosters late in a lost season, isn't as absurd as it sounds. Both teams rank in the bottom third of the league in defensive rating over the last 20 games, and FedExForum plays reasonably fast when neither team has the personnel to slow possessions.

UTA (21-53) @ PHX (40-33)

Saturday, 10:00 PM ET | Footprint Center, Phoenix
Spread
PHX -16.5
Moneyline
UTA +810 / PHX -1250
Total
O/U 230.5

The Utah Jazz roster situation has graduated from bad to genuinely unprecedented territory. Jaren Jackson Jr. (knee), Lauri Markkanen (hip), Keyonte George (hamstring), Isaiah Collier (hamstring), and both Jusuf Nurkic and Walker Kessler are out for the season following surgery. Utah is essentially sending a crew of developmental players and end-of-bench contributors into the desert to face a Phoenix team with legitimate playoff stakes. Devin Booker is expected to play for the Suns, and while Phoenix has dropped six of their last seven games, this matchup is the reset button they've been waiting for.

It's worth being crystal clear about one thing given how much the NBA landscape has shifted: Kevin Durant is not on this Suns roster. He's been with the Houston Rockets since the trade last summer. Phoenix has built their identity around Booker as the primary offensive engine, and the supporting cast around him will need to show up against a Jazz defense that literally cannot stop anyone at this point in the season.

The 16.5 spread is large but the context justifies the number. Phoenix at home, Booker available, against a Utah team that cannot field a competitive lineup at any position. The Suns' 40-33 record means they need wins badly to secure their playoff position, and losing a game like this would be an embarrassing result. The motivation angle is entirely in Phoenix's favor.

The 230.5 total reflects the expectation that Phoenix scores at will and Utah posts passable-but-limited numbers with their patchwork roster. The Suns are going to push pace early, score in transition, and build a cushion that removes any fourth-quarter drama. The interesting question is whether Phoenix's recent struggles (6 of 7 losses) bleeds into their performance even in a favorable spot, or whether facing a depleted Jazz team snaps them out of whatever funk they've been in.
