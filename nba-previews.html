Clippers @ Pacers

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Gainbridge Fieldhouse, Indianapolis, IN
Spread
LAC -9.5
Moneyline
LAC -370 / IND +300
Total
O/U 238.5

The Clippers at 37-36 have won three straight and hold a half-game lead over Portland for the 8th seed in the Western Conference. This is a team that started the season 6-21 and has completely reinvented itself since December, turning what looked like a lost season into a genuine playoff push. Darius Garland and John Collins are both questionable for tonight, which could impact the rotation, but the Clippers have shown remarkable resilience all season when dealing with availability issues.

Indiana at 16-57 has the worst record in the Eastern Conference and has been ravaged by injuries all season, deploying 27 different players with 22 different starting lineups. Pascal Siakam has been a bright spot at 23.9 points and 6.6 rebounds per game, while Andrew Nembhard has posted career-highs of 17.1 points and 7.6 assists. Both have played with professionalism despite the circumstances, but this roster simply doesn't have the talent to compete with a motivated Clippers team fighting for its playoff life.

The 9.5-point spread reflects the massive gap between a team with everything to play for and one that's been eliminated from contention for months. LA's defense has tightened significantly during their winning streak, and their half-court offense has found a rhythm that was missing early in the season. The Clippers have been particularly dominant against bottom-five teams when favored by 8 or more.

The 238.5 total suggests both teams will push pace, which makes sense given Indiana's willingness to play fast regardless of personnel. The Pacers have nothing to lose and will fire away from three, which can keep games closer than the talent gap suggests. If Garland sits, that takes a significant playmaker off the floor for LA, but the Clippers' depth should be more than enough to handle the league's worst team on the road.

Hawks @ Celtics

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Spread
BOS -7.5
Moneyline
ATL +235 / BOS -303
Total
O/U 224.5

Boston at 47-24 has been carrying on without Jayson Tatum (Achilles) all season, and Jaylen Brown has been phenomenal in the lead role. Brown is questionable tonight with left calf tightness after scoring 31 points with 8 rebounds and 8 assists against the Thunder on Wednesday. Derrick White is also questionable with a knee bruise. If both sit, this becomes a very different game, but the Celtics' depth has been strong enough to handle most opponents at TD Garden.

Atlanta at 40-32 sits 6th in the Eastern Conference and is fighting for playoff seeding. The Hawks traded Trae Young to Washington earlier this season, which shifted their offensive identity significantly. Without Young's elite playmaking, Atlanta has become more of a balanced, defense-first team that grinds out wins. The transition hasn't been seamless, but at 40-32, the results speak for themselves.

The 7.5-point spread feels generous for a Celtics home game, but Brown's health is the key variable. If he plays, Boston's offensive ceiling is high enough to blow this open. If he sits alongside White, the Celtics would be missing their two best players and the spread would likely be too high. The uncertainty around those two decisions makes this line interesting heading into tip-off.

The 224.5 total is one of the lower numbers on the board, reflecting both teams' defensive capabilities. Boston's home defense has been suffocating all season, and Atlanta's post-Young identity leans heavily on the defensive end. If Brown and White both play, the Celtics should control the pace and pull away. If they sit, this could be a grinder that stays within single digits.

Heat @ Cavaliers

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH
Spread
CLE -4.5
Moneyline
MIA +162 / CLE -194
Total
O/U 241.5

This is a massive play-in positioning game. Cleveland at 45-28 sits 4th in the East, while Miami at 39-34 is clinging to 9th and fighting to make the play-in tournament. Donovan Mitchell has been on an absolute tear, averaging 28.3 points per game (6th in the NBA), recently scoring 42 while playing through a bruised left eye from a practice collision with Evan Mobley. That kind of toughness sets the tone for an entire roster.

The Cavaliers' biggest question is Jarrett Allen, listed questionable with a knee injury after missing the last 10 games. Allen's rim protection and rebounding have been sorely missed, and Cleveland's defensive efficiency has dipped noticeably without him anchoring the paint. Miami has been inconsistent, hovering around .500 all season without a true star since Jimmy Butler was traded to Golden State.

The 241.5 total is the second-highest on the board, suggesting a fast-paced affair. Cleveland's pace has increased since Allen's absence forced smaller lineups. Mitchell's scoring gives the Cavaliers a clear offensive edge, but Miami's defensive toughness has kept them competitive in almost every game this season.

The 4.5-point spread is tight enough that this game could go either way. Miami's physical style can neutralize more talented teams, and they've shown an ability to hang around in games where they shouldn't belong. But Mitchell in a must-win situation at home is a scary proposition for the Heat defense, and the Cavaliers need this win to maintain their position in the top four.

Houston at 43-29 sits 6th in the West but has lost 4 of their last 6, a concerning slide for a team that looked like a contender earlier in the season. Memphis at 24-48 has lost 12 of their last 13 since Ja Morant went down with a UCL sprain in his left elbow on January 21st. Morant played just 20 games this season (19.5 PPG, 8.1 APG) before the injury ended his year.

The 12.5-point spread is massive, but Houston is only 6-6 ATS when favored by 12.5 or more this season, suggesting the Rockets haven't consistently crushed inferior opponents. Road favorites laying double digits in the NBA have a historically tough time covering, and Houston's recent struggles could carry over even against a depleted Memphis squad.

Houston has won all 7 games this season when favored by -800 or more on the moneyline, so the straight-up result seems like a lock. The more interesting angle is the 226.5 total. Memphis plays fast even in losses, and Houston's defensive lapses during their skid could keep this competitive.

The Grizzlies have nothing to play for except pride and draft lottery positioning, but their young players still compete hard every night. The Rockets need to use this game to build momentum heading into the stretch run, and a convincing wire-to-wire win would do wonders for their confidence after the rough recent stretch.

OKC -19.5
Moneyline

Oklahoma City at 57-16 has the best record in the NBA and is on a 7-game home winning streak. The defending champions have been the most dominant force in basketball this season, and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has cemented his status as the best player in the world. SGA has been managing an abdominal issue that caused him to miss 9 games in February, but when healthy, his smooth mid-range game, elite finishing, and defensive intensity make him a nightmare for any guard in the league.

Chicago at 29-43 is going nowhere, with Jaden Ivey and Jalen Smith both ruled out for the remainder of the season. The Bulls lack both star talent and the development pipeline to feel optimistic about the future. A trip to Oklahoma City is about as unfavorable a matchup as exists in the NBA right now.

The 19.5-point spread is the largest on the board. OKC's elite defense has the versatility to shut down Chicago's limited offense. Isaiah Hartenstein is managing a soleus injury but should play, giving the Thunder their full complement of weapons. When OKC is healthy and engaged at home, 30-point blowouts against inferior opponents are routine.

The 237.5 total suggests both offenses will be active. Even in blowouts, the Thunder's pace and firepower push totals higher, and their bench units maintain scoring efficiency in garbage time. Chicago's defense has been among the worst in the league down the stretch, which should allow OKC to score at will from the opening tip.

Spread
LAL -17.5
Moneyline
BKN +1329 / LAL -1567
Total
O/U 235

All eyes are on Luka Doncic, who's listed questionable with left hamstring soreness. Luka has played 61 games this season and needs 4 more to qualify for MVP voting, so there's real motivation to suit up tonight even at less than 100%. Since arriving in Los Angeles via the trade that sent Anthony Davis to Dallas, Doncic has turned the Lakers into title contenders at 47-26, good for the 2nd best record in the West behind Oklahoma City.

Brooklyn at 17-56 is the worst team in the Eastern Conference, ravaged by injuries. Nick Smith Jr. (back), Noah Clowney (wrist), Michael Porter Jr. (hamstring), Da'Ron Sharpe (thumb), and Danny Wolf (ankle) are all dealing with issues. This roster is essentially running a developmental camp with no player capable of matching up against Doncic or the Lakers' supporting cast.

The 17.5-point spread is enormous, and the -1567 moneyline makes this essentially a guaranteed Lakers victory. The only question is whether Doncic plays, because without him the Lakers' offense loses its primary creator. Rui Hachimura (right calf) is also questionable, and Marcus Smart is out with an ankle contusion.

If Doncic plays even 25-30 minutes, this game should be over by halftime. Crypto.com Arena with Luka in MVP form is one of the toughest environments in the league. The Nets don't have the firepower to keep pace, and a 20+ point Lakers victory wouldn't surprise anyone.
