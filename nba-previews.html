Game 1 NBC/Peacock

Denver Nuggets (44-28) @ Phoenix Suns (40-32)

Tuesday, 11:00 PM ET | Footprint Center
Spread
DEN -5.5
Moneyline
DEN -245 / PHX +200
Total
O/U 233.5

This is the headliner of Tuesday's four-game slate and it's easy to see why NBC snagged it for national television. Denver rolls into Footprint Center having absolutely demolished Phoenix in their previous two meetings this season, winning by 22 and 18 points respectively. That kind of dominance doesn't just happen by accident. Nikola Jokic is putting together another historic campaign, averaging 28.0 points, 12.6 rebounds, and 10.6 assists per game, essentially a season-long triple-double that continues to redefine what a center can be in this league. The Nuggets are 5-2 over their last seven and fighting tooth and nail for playoff positioning, currently tied for the 4th/5th seed and sitting just half a game ahead of Houston.

Phoenix's situation is considerably more precarious. The Suns are the 7th seed at 40-32, sitting 3.5 games behind the Rockets for the 6th spot, which means they're staring down a potential play-in tournament appearance if they can't climb. They did manage to snap a brutal five-game losing streak recently and have gone 6-4 over their last 10, but the roster is banged up at exactly the wrong time. Dillon Brooks is out with a hand injury, and Grayson Allen is questionable with a knee issue. That's two key perimeter defenders and rotation shooters who could be unavailable against a Nuggets offense that thrives on Jokic's passing creating open looks for cutters and shooters.

The 233.5 total reflects both teams' ability to score in bunches, but Denver's defensive identity has been the real story of their recent surge. When Jokic is locked in on both ends, the Nuggets' half-court offense becomes nearly impossible to stop because of the constant movement and decision-making flowing through the big man. Phoenix will need Devin Booker and Bradley Beal to be extraordinary just to keep this competitive, and even then, the Suns have struggled to match Denver's physicality on the glass. The Nuggets have owned the rebounding battle in both previous meetings, and without Brooks to provide toughness on the wing, that problem only gets worse.

For Phoenix, this game carries enormous weight beyond the standings. Losing three straight to the Nuggets, with two of those being blowouts, creates a psychological barrier that's hard to shake if these teams meet in the playoffs. The Suns need to prove they can compete with Denver before the postseason arrives. The 5.5-point spread suggests the market respects Denver's superiority in this matchup but acknowledges Phoenix's home court and desperation. Still, the Nuggets have covered in both previous meetings with room to spare, and there's no indication the gap between these teams has closed in any meaningful way.

Game 2 Local

Sacramento Kings (19-53) @ Charlotte Hornets (37-34)

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | Spectrum Center
Spread
CHA -17.5
Moneyline
CHA -1667 / SAC +817
Total
O/U 231.5

A 17.5-point spread in the NBA is genuinely rare, and it tells you everything you need to know about the state of the Sacramento Kings right now. At 19-53, the Kings are in full tank mode with an absurd 11 players on the injury report, including Zach LaVine, Domantas Sabonis, Keegan Murray, De'Andre Hunter, Russell Westbrook, and others all sidelined. This isn't a basketball team at this point, it's a collection of G League call-ups and end-of-bench players logging minutes they'd never see on a healthy roster. Sacramento set a franchise record earlier this season with a 16-game losing streak, and games like this are exactly how streaks like that happen.

Charlotte, on the other hand, is having the kind of breakout season that nobody predicted. The Hornets are 37-34 and firmly in the playoff picture, going 7-3 over their last 10 games with a two-game winning streak heading into tonight. LaMelo Ball has been sensational, averaging 19.7 points and 7.1 assists per game while running one of the league's most entertaining offenses. What's remarkable about Charlotte's season is their consistency against the spread, posting a 43-28 ATS record overall and an excellent 21-12-1 ATS mark at home. That's one of the best cover rates in the entire league, and it suggests this team doesn't take nights off regardless of the opponent.

The 231.5 total is interesting given the talent disparity. Sacramento's depleted roster simply doesn't have the firepower to keep pace with Charlotte's starters, but the Hornets may also pull their key guys early if this gets out of hand, which could suppress scoring in the fourth quarter. Charlotte's bench depth will be tested in terms of maintaining offensive efficiency against Sacramento's scrappy reserves who have nothing to lose. Don't be surprised if this one gets sloppy in the second half as Charlotte's coaching staff manages minutes with the bigger picture in mind.

The real question here isn't who wins, it's whether Charlotte covers this massive number. Nearly 18 points is a lot to lay in any NBA game, even against a team this depleted. The Hornets have shown they can blow out inferior competition, but they've also had a few letdown games where they've taken their foot off the gas. Sacramento's players are fighting for roster spots and contract extensions, which can produce surprisingly competitive stretches even in games that are objectively over. Charlotte's ATS track record at home is elite, but 17.5 is the kind of number that makes you sweat even when the outcome is never in doubt.

New Orleans Pelicans (25-47) @ New York Knicks (47-25)

Tuesday, 7:30 PM ET | Madison Square Garden
Spread
NYK -8.5
Moneyline
NYK -391 / NOP +310
Total
O/U 231.5

The Knicks are absolutely rolling right now and Madison Square Garden has been rocking. New York comes in on a six-game winning streak and an 8-2 record over their last 10, posting an absurd +12.5 point margin during that stretch. That's not just winning, that's dominating. Jalen Brunson continues to be one of the most reliable offensive engines in the league at 26.1 points per game, and Karl-Anthony Towns has been the perfect complement at 20.2 points, 12.0 rebounds, and 2.9 assists. This roster is deep, well-coached, and playing with the kind of confidence that makes them dangerous in the postseason. At 47-25, they're one of the best teams in the Eastern Conference and playing like it.

New Orleans has been officially eliminated from playoff contention, and honestly, that happened in spirit a long time ago. The Pelicans are 25-47 and have spent most of this season dealing with injuries and roster turnover that have made it impossible to build any continuity. That said, there's individual talent worth watching here. Trey Murphy III has blossomed into a legitimate scorer at 21.8 points per game, and Zion Williamson is putting up 21.3 per game when healthy, continuing to flash the talent that made him the number one pick. The problem is that talent in isolation doesn't translate to wins when the supporting cast and team chemistry aren't there.

New York's home dominance has been a defining feature of their season, and the 23-12 ATS record at MSG reflects a team that doesn't just win at home but covers consistently. The energy in that building when the Knicks are clicking is unlike anything else in the NBA, and opponents often look overwhelmed by the intensity of the crowd and the defensive pressure Tom Thibodeau's squad brings every night. The Knicks' defensive versatility, with Anunoby, Bridges, and Hart all capable of switching across multiple positions, creates matchup nightmares that a depleted Pelicans roster simply isn't equipped to solve.

The 8.5-point spread is substantial but feels right given where both teams are mentally and physically. New Orleans has no playoff incentive and is likely managing minutes for their young players with an eye toward the future. The Knicks, meanwhile, are jockeying for seeding and building momentum for a deep playoff run. That motivation gap is worth points by itself. The 231.5 total could be influenced by how quickly New York builds a lead. If they jump out early, which has been a pattern during this winning streak, we could see the starters sit extended stretches in the fourth quarter, potentially capping the scoring output on both sides.

Game 4 NBC/Peacock

Orlando Magic (38-33) @ Cleveland Cavaliers (44-27)

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse
Spread
CLE -10.5
Moneyline
CLE -470 / ORL +360
Total
O/U 229.5

Orlando's season is reaching a critical juncture, and not in a good way. The Magic have dropped five straight games and are watching their playoff hopes slip away, sitting just 1.5 games back of the 8th seed play-in spot with time running out. The injury report is a nightmare. Franz Wagner is out with an ankle injury, robbing Orlando of their most versatile offensive weapon, and Jalen Suggs is questionable with an illness. Without Wagner, the Magic's already inconsistent offense loses its best playmaker and shot creator, putting enormous pressure on Paolo Banchero to carry the load single-handedly against one of the league's best defenses.

Cleveland has quietly been one of the most consistent teams in the NBA all season, and they're surging at exactly the right time with three straight wins and a 7-3 mark over their last 10 games. Donovan Mitchell is playing at an MVP-caliber level, pouring in 28.0 points per game on 47.7% shooting from the field. His ability to score at all three levels makes him nearly impossible to game-plan for, and when he's aggressive attacking the rim, it opens up everything else for the Cavaliers' offense. The one concern for Cleveland is the absence of Jarrett Allen, who's out with a knee injury, which impacts their rim protection and rebounding. However, the Cavaliers' depth has been good enough to absorb that loss during this winning streak.

The season series tells a clear story: Cleveland leads 2-1 and won the previous two meetings by 14 and 16 points. Those weren't close games that broke late, they were wire-to-wire beatdowns where the Cavaliers' superior talent and coaching simply overwhelmed Orlando's defense-first approach. Without Wagner in the lineup, the gap between these rosters widens significantly. Orlando's defensive identity can keep them in games, but you need to score to win in the NBA, and their offensive ceiling without their best perimeter player is severely limited. The Magic are shooting just 44.2% from the field during this five-game skid, and that number could get worse against Cleveland's length and activity.

The 10.5-point spread is hefty, but the market is clearly factoring in Wagner's absence and Orlando's freefall. The 229.5 total is the lowest on tonight's board, reflecting both Cleveland's defensive prowess and Orlando's offensive limitations. This feels like a game where the Cavaliers control the pace, build a comfortable lead by halftime, and manage the clock down the stretch. For Orlando, this isn't just about tonight, it's about whether this losing streak has broken their spirit heading into the final stretch. If they can't compete against a Cleveland team missing its starting center, it's hard to see how they turn things around in time to save their season.
