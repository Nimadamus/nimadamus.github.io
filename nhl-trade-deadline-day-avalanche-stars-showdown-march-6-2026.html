7-Game Friday Slate

NHL March 6, 2026

Friday's seven-game slate arrives on the heels of the NHL trade deadline, and the headliner is a Central Division colossus in Dallas where the 91-point Avalanche visit the Stars on a franchise-record 10-game winning streak. Nathan MacKinnon's 100-point season meets Jake Oettinger's historic run. Carolina (84pts) travels to Edmonton, the defending champion Panthers continue their free fall in Detroit, Minnesota visits Mitch Marner and the Golden Knights, and three more games round out a loaded night of post-deadline hockey.

Game 1
Featured

Avalanche @ Stars

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | American Airlines Center, Dallas, TX | ESPN+
Puck Line
COL -1.5 (+205)
Moneyline
COL -120 / DAL EVEN
Total
O/U 5.5

This is the crown jewel of Friday night hockey and tonight's Featured Game of the Day. The Colorado Avalanche (41-10-9, 91 points) bring the best record in the NHL into American Airlines Center to face a Dallas Stars (38-14-9, 85 points) team riding a franchise-record 10-game winning streak. Nathan MacKinnon has hit the 100-point milestone with 41 goals and 59 assists in just 59 games, making him the runaway Hart Trophy favorite, and Cale Makar's 64 points from the blue line continue to make him a Norris Trophy contender. Colorado's +81 goal differential is the best in the league by a wide margin.

Dallas has been the best team in hockey over the past three weeks, outscoring opponents 42-23 during their historic 10-game run. Jake Oettinger (16-6-2, 2.50 GAA, .910 SV%) has been the backbone of the streak, and Miro Heiskanen's 50 points from the blue line give the Stars a legitimate two-way weapon on the back end. The Stars' forward group has delivered balanced scoring from all four lines during the streak, making them nearly impossible to game-plan against. This is the first meeting between these two Central Division heavyweights where both teams enter in peak form.

The market has Colorado as a slight -120 road favorite, essentially calling this a coin flip with the slightest nod to the Avalanche's superior overall body of work. Dallas at EVEN money on home ice during a franchise-record winning streak is a number that screams respect from the oddsmakers. The 5.5 total with the over juiced to -135 suggests goals, but not a shootout, given the quality of goaltending on both sides. With the trade deadline just passed, both teams could debut newly acquired players, adding an element of unpredictability to what's already the most anticipated regular season game of the month. Hurricanes @ Oilers

Friday, 9:00 PM ET | Rogers Place, Edmonton, AB | ESPN+
Puck Line
CAR -1.5 (+210)
Moneyline
CAR -115 / EDM -105
Total
O/U 6.5

Carolina at 39-16-6 with 84 points is the best team in the Eastern Conference and the second-best team in the NHL behind Colorado. The Hurricanes have been on a tear lately, going 8-1-1 over their last 10, and they bring a relentless, suffocating defensive system to Rogers Place. Carolina's ability to control play at 5-on-5, generate high-quality chances, and limit opponents' time in the offensive zone is the foundation of their success. They don't beat themselves, they play the right way every shift, and they've been remarkably consistent over the full season.

Edmonton at 30-24-8 with 68 points is fighting for a Pacific Division playoff spot, and every game in March carries outsized importance. The Oilers have Connor McDavid and Leon Draisaitl, which means their offensive ceiling is always sky-high, but their defensive consistency has been a question mark all season. Edmonton is 5-5-0 over their last 10, and the kind of inconsistency that produced that split isn't going to cut it against a Carolina team that punishes mistakes with ruthless efficiency. Rogers Place will be rocking, but the Hurricanes have proven they can win in any building this season.

The moneyline at CAR -115 / EDM -105 is essentially a pick'em with the slightest edge to Carolina, and that's a remarkable number when you consider the talent gap in the standings. Carolina is 16 points ahead of Edmonton in the overall standings, but the market is giving the Oilers significant home-ice credit, and for good reason. McDavid at home is a different animal, and the Oilers' offensive explosiveness can overwhelm any defense on a given night. The 6.5 total is the highest-priced game on the board and reflects the offensive talent on both sides. This has the potential to be the most entertaining game of the night outside of the Featured Game in Dallas.

Panthers @ Red Wings

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Puck Line
DET -1.5 (+164)
Moneyline
DET -155 / FLA +130
Total
O/U 5.5

The Florida Panthers' fall from grace is one of the most shocking stories in the NHL this season. The defending back-to-back Stanley Cup champions sit at 30-29-3 with 63 points, dead last in the Atlantic Division, and they've lost four straight heading into Detroit. Let that sink in. A team that won the Cup in 2024 and 2025 is now below .500 and fighting just to stay alive in the playoff picture. The Panthers' 2-8-0 record over their last 10 games is the kind of collapse that gets coaches fired and general managers making desperate phone calls at the trade deadline.

Detroit at 35-20-7 with 77 points has been one of the pleasant surprises in the Eastern Conference. The Red Wings are firmly in playoff position, third in the Atlantic, and they've done it with a balanced roster that competes hard every night. At home against a reeling Florida team, Detroit is a justified -155 favorite. Little Caesars Arena has been a tough building for opponents this season, and the Red Wings' defensive structure has improved significantly under their coaching staff. Florida's inability to generate consistent offense during their losing streak makes them vulnerable against a team that can control play at 5-on-5.

The 5.5 total is the lowest on Friday's board, and it reflects the reality that Florida isn't scoring enough to push games over higher numbers. During their current losing streak, the Panthers have looked like a shell of the championship team that dominated the NHL for two consecutive seasons. The talent is still there on paper, but the execution, the confidence, and the swagger that defined championship Florida have evaporated. Detroit should be able to control this game with their defensive structure and capitalize on a Panthers team that's lost its identity. The DET -155 moneyline looks justified given the current form of both teams.

Game 4

Wild @ Golden Knights

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas, NV | ESPN+
Puck Line
VGK -1.5 (+215)
Moneyline
MIN -108 / VGK -112
Total
O/U 6.5

Minnesota at 36-16-10 with 82 points is third in the Central Division and one of the most consistent teams in the Western Conference. The Wild have been 7-2-1 over their last 10, demonstrating the kind of steady play that makes them a legitimate Cup contender. They've done it with a balanced roster that doesn't rely on a single superstar, instead winning with depth, goaltending, and a commitment to team defense that makes them difficult to play against. Walking into T-Mobile Arena is never easy, but the Wild have the road toughness to compete anywhere.

Vegas at 29-19-14 with 72 points leads the Pacific Division, but the 14 overtime losses tell a story of a team that can't finish games. The Golden Knights, now featuring Mitch Marner acquired from Toronto in July 2025, have the offensive talent to compete with anyone. Marner's playmaking ability has given Vegas a dimension they lacked, and his chemistry with the Knights' forward group has developed throughout the season. But Vegas's inability to close out tight games has been a recurring theme, and they'll need to find that killer instinct if they're going to advance in the playoffs.

The moneyline at MIN -108 / VGK -112 is the closest to a true pick'em you'll find on Friday's board. The market sees these two teams as essentially equal, with the slightest home-ice edge to Vegas. That feels right given both teams' current form and the quality of play they bring to the table. The 6.5 total reflects the offensive talent on both rosters and the possibility of this becoming a high-event game. Minnesota's road form has been solid, and Vegas's home record has been strong enough to justify the slight favorite status. This is a playoff-caliber matchup between two teams that could meet again in the first or second round.

Canucks @ Blackhawks

Friday, 8:30 PM ET | United Center, Chicago, IL | ESPN+
Puck Line
CHI -1.5 (+170)
Moneyline
CHI -142 / VAN +120
Total
O/U 6.5

Vancouver at 18-36-7 with 43 points is having one of the worst seasons in the NHL, and the numbers tell a devastating story. The Canucks have a -73 goal differential, the worst in the league by a significant margin, and they've lost seven straight games. A team that made the playoffs last season has completely unraveled, and the free fall has been painful to watch. Vancouver is allowing 3.77 goals per game and scoring just 2.57, a recipe for the kind of consistent losing that leads to coaching changes and roster overhauls. The trade deadline likely saw the Canucks selling off assets, which further depletes an already thin roster.

Chicago at 23-28-10 with 56 points isn't having a great season either, but the Blackhawks are considerably better than Vancouver, and at home they should be able to take care of business. Chicago has been 2-6-2 over their last 10, which isn't inspiring, but the Canucks' 1-7-2 recent form is significantly worse. The United Center crowd won't be expecting a masterpiece, but they should get a win. The CHI -142 moneyline reflects the gap between these two teams, and the 6.5 total suggests goals will flow freely given both teams' defensive deficiencies.

This is the kind of game that won't draw national headlines, but it's fascinating from a betting perspective because both teams are flawed in ways that create opportunities. Vancouver's seven-game losing streak suggests they've completely given up on the season, while Chicago's inconsistency means they're capable of laying an egg on any given night. The total of 6.5 is tied for the highest on the board, and recent history supports the over. Both defenses are porous, both goaltending situations are inconsistent, and neither team has the defensive structure to limit quality chances. If you're looking for a high-scoring game to close out the first half of your Friday night hockey slate, this is it.

Canadiens @ Ducks

Friday, 9:00 PM ET | Honda Center, Anaheim, CA | ESPN+
Puck Line
ANA -1.5 (+215)
Moneyline
MTL -110 / ANA -110
Total
O/U 6.5

Montreal at 33-18-9 with 75 points has been one of the most surprising teams in the Eastern Conference, and the Canadiens' rebuild has progressed faster than anyone expected. At 75 points, Montreal sits fourth in the Atlantic and is firmly in playoff position, a development that seemed years away at the start of the season. The Canadiens have been competitive in virtually every game, and their young core has shown the kind of growth that suggests this isn't a one-year anomaly but the beginning of a sustained run of competitiveness. Their 5-3-2 record over the last 10 reflects a team that's solid but not dominant on the road.

Anaheim at 34-24-3 with 71 points has been a revelation in the Pacific Division. The Ducks' rebuild has similarly accelerated ahead of schedule, and they're in the thick of the Pacific playoff race with a 7-3-0 record over their last 10 games. Honda Center has been a surprisingly tough place to play this season, and Anaheim's young talent has brought an energy and competitiveness that makes every home game a genuine contest. The market has this as a dead pick'em at -110 on both sides, and that's a testament to how evenly matched these two teams are.

The 6.5 total reflects the offensive capabilities of both teams. Montreal generates 3.55 goals per game but also allows 3.33, while Anaheim's numbers are similar with 3.36 goals for and 3.48 against. Both teams play with enough pace and offensive freedom to push games over higher totals, and neither defense is airtight enough to consistently keep scores down. This is a fascinating matchup between two teams that are ahead of their rebuild timelines, and the even moneyline means the market genuinely doesn't know which way this one will go. That's the kind of game that rewards careful analysis and creates genuine betting value for those who can identify an edge.

Blues @ Sharks

Puck Line
SJ -1.5 (+190)
Moneyline
SJ -135 / STL +114
Total
O/U 6.5

St. Louis at 23-29-9 with 55 points has had a season to forget. The Blues' -51 goal differential is one of the worst in the league, and their inability to compete consistently has made them sellers at the trade deadline. St. Louis is 4-5-1 over their last 10, which is actually an improvement from some of the stretches they've endured earlier in the season, but the overall trajectory of this franchise is clearly pointed toward a rebuild. Walking into San Jose to face a Sharks team that's been surging isn't the kind of late-season assignment that inspires confidence.

San Jose at 30-25-4 with 64 points is one of the more interesting stories in the Pacific Division. The Sharks have won three straight and are 5-4-1 over their last 10, showing the kind of competitive fight that wasn't expected from a franchise that's been rebuilding for several seasons. San Jose's young talent, headlined by their recent draft acquisitions, has started to show real development, and the Sharks are playing with a level of energy and purpose that makes them dangerous at home. The SJ -135 moneyline reflects the gap in current form between these two teams.

The 6.5 total is consistent with the rest of the late-night games, and it reflects the offensive capabilities of both teams. St. Louis's defensive struggles mean they give up a lot of goals, and San Jose has shown enough offensive firepower during their winning streak to take advantage. The Blues' +114 price as an underdog isn't unattractive for bettors who believe in St. Louis's ability to hang in games, but the current form and home-ice advantage favor San Jose. This is the nightcap of Friday's slate, and while it won't generate the same excitement as Colorado-Dallas, it's a genuine competitive matchup between two teams on very different trajectories.
