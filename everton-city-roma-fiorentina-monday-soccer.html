Everton vs Manchester City

Monday, 3:00 PM ET / 8:00 PM Local | Hill Dickinson Stadium, Liverpool, England

Everton host Manchester City at Hill Dickinson Stadium Monday night for the headline Premier League fixture of the spring's quieter midweek window, with the visitors carrying the kind of moneyline favoritism that reflects both Pep Guardiola's structural ceiling and the Toffees' thin attacking margins through the back end of the season. Manchester City are -213 favorites on the moneyline, which translates to roughly a 68 percent implied win probability after juice. Everton are priced at +550 to win, with the draw at +325. The line shape reflects the talent gap between a Manchester City top six built around Erling Haaland's box-presence finishing and an Everton attack that has produced the kind of low-event Premier League games that have defined David Moyes's second spell on the touchline.

The structural read on the match starts with Manchester City's xG profile. Guardiola's side has been the highest-shot-volume team in the Premier League across 2025-26, and the deeper-bracket attacking shape that runs through Phil Foden, Bernardo Silva, and Haaland produces the kind of high-quality chance creation that punishes the kind of low-block defensive structure Everton typically deploys against superior opponents. The matchup against Jordan Pickford in goal is the structural variable - the England No. 1 has been the season-saving piece across multiple Toffees campaigns, and his ability to absorb the City shot volume and force Haaland into the kind of variance-heavy finishing windows is the only realistic path to a draw or upset result. The total has settled at 2.5 goals on most major books with the over juiced to a small favorite read, which reflects the structural matchup of City's attack against Everton's defensive shape.

Everton's path to the upset starts at the back. Moyes has built the spring profile around a defensive-first 4-5-1 base shape, with the midfield-heavy structure designed to absorb pressure and counter-attack through the wide channels. The matchup against Manchester City's high defensive line is the structural piece - the kind of through-ball entries that Beto and Iliman Ndiaye produce on the counter can punish City if Ruben Dias and John Stones step too aggressively. The crowd at Hill Dickinson Stadium provides the home-energy element that has been the structural fuel for the Toffees' upset wins across recent campaigns. The stake on the match has been reduced to mid-table positioning rather than relegation survival, which removes the urgency element but leaves the pride-and-pay-day stakes that make Premier League home games against the league's top side meaningful in their own right.

Roma vs Fiorentina

Monday, 12:45 PM ET / 6:45 PM Local | Stadio Olimpico, Rome, Italy

The Stadio Olimpico hosts Roma and Fiorentina in a Matchday 35 Serie A fixture that closes the round and carries genuine European-qualification stakes for both clubs. Roma are priced at 1.61 in decimal odds (roughly -164 American), with Fiorentina at 5.00 (+400) and the draw at 4.20 (+320). The line shape reflects Roma's home-form profile across the back end of the season - Claudio Ranieri's Giallorossi have produced the kind of late-season Champions League push that has anchored the team's positioning in the European-qualification scrap, and the home crowd at the Olimpico provides the structural fuel that has been the franchise identity for nearly a decade. Fiorentina arrive with the relegation-zone math fully cleared but with the team in the kind of mid-table comfort zone that historically struggles to produce a road result against a top-six Serie A side.

The over/under has settled at 2.5 goals with the under juiced as a small favorite, which reflects the structural matchup of Roma's defensive identity against Fiorentina's modest road-attacking output. The Giallorossi have been the structurally-best defensive team in the bottom half of the Serie A xG-against table - the back four of Gianluca Mancini, Evan Ndicka, and Zeki Celik has produced top-six defensive numbers, and Mile Svilar in goal has absorbed the kind of variance windows that define late-season tight matches. Fiorentina's attacking output on the road has been the structural concern - Moise Kean has been the team's primary scoring outlet, but the supporting cast has not produced the kind of secondary scoring that translates into away results against superior opponents. The matchup against the Roma central midfield duo of Manu Kone and Bryan Cristante defines the half-court geometry of the Fiorentina attack.

The stake on the match is the Champions League seeding for next season. Roma are inside the qualification zone but separated from the Europa League positions by a single result, and a home win against Fiorentina would consolidate the Champions League berth that the club has chased across multiple recent campaigns. Fiorentina are no longer in the European-qualification scrap mathematically and arrive with the kind of low-stakes profile that doesn't always show up in a Roma-Fiorentina match given the historical rivalry component. The home crowd will provide the structural fuel that has been the franchise's late-season identity, and the closeout match of the round at the Olimpico is the kind of Italian-football Sunday-night theater that always produces moments worth tuning in for.

Real Sociedad's road profile has been the structurally-weaker side of the team's overall ledger - the Basque side has not produced consistent away results across the spring, and the matchup against a Sevilla home crowd in a relegation-stakes match is the kind of road environment that historically suppresses the visitors' attacking output.
