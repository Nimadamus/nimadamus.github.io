World Cup Sunday: Germany And The Netherlands Headline Four Group-Stage Matches

The 2026 World Cup serves up a full Sunday across its co-host venues, with Germany meeting Curacao in Houston, the Netherlands facing Japan in Arlington, Ivory Coast taking on Ecuador in Philadelphia, and Sweden closing the night against Tunisia. Kickoff times, venues, and storylines for all four below. Analysis and breakdown only, no picks.

Group E

Germany vs Curacao

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Houston, TX
Stage
Group E
Kickoff
1:00 PM ET
Venue
Houston

The marquee name on the Sunday card is Germany, one of the most decorated programs in the history of the World Cup, opening its Group E business against tournament debutant Curacao in Houston. For Germany, this is the kind of group-stage match where the expectation is total control of possession and a steady accumulation of chances, the test being efficiency in the final third rather than survival. The Germans bring the pedigree, the tactical structure, and the depth that has defined them across generations of major tournaments.

Curacao, by contrast, is writing one of the great underdog stories of the 2026 finals simply by being here, a tiny Caribbean nation stepping onto the sport's biggest stage. The realistic approach for Curacao is a compact, disciplined defensive block and a hope to spring forward on the counter, the classic blueprint for a smaller side facing a heavyweight. The afternoon heat in Houston is its own factor, and how Germany manages the tempo in those conditions against a side with everything to gain and little to lose will shape the rhythm of the match.

Group F

Netherlands vs Japan

Sunday, 4:00 PM ET | Arlington, TX
Stage
Group F
Kickoff
4:00 PM ET
Venue
Arlington

The most intriguing tactical matchup of the day comes in Arlington, where the Netherlands meet Japan in a Group F clash that pits two technically gifted, possession-minded sides against each other. The Dutch carry the European pedigree and the individual quality that makes them a perennial threat to go deep, a program built on positional play and fluid attacking movement. Against a side as organized as Japan, the Netherlands will need to be sharp in transition and clinical when the chances arrive.

Japan has become one of the most respected national teams outside the traditional powers, a side that presses with discipline, moves the ball quickly, and has repeatedly troubled European heavyweights on the biggest stage. The Samurai Blue do not sit back and absorb; they compete for the ball and look to play through the lines, which makes this a genuine contest of styles rather than a one-sided affair. In a domed stadium that takes the elements out of play, this has the makings of an open, technical game between two teams comfortable on the ball.

Group E

Ivory Coast vs Ecuador

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Philadelphia, PA
Stage
Group E
Kickoff
7:00 PM ET
Venue
Philadelphia

Philadelphia hosts a fascinating intercontinental matchup as Ivory Coast meets Ecuador in the other Group E fixture. The Ivorians bring the athleticism and attacking flair that have long defined West African football, a physical, fast, direct side capable of turning a single moment of individual brilliance into a goal. In a group that includes Germany, every point is precious, and Ivory Coast will see this as a match it needs to win to control its own path to the knockout rounds.

Ecuador arrives as a stubborn, well-drilled South American side that has built its identity on defensive organization and a willingness to grind out results. The Ecuadorians are rarely easy to break down, and their counterattacking threat means Ivory Coast cannot simply pour forward without risk. This is the type of evenly matched group-stage game that often hinges on a set piece or a single defensive lapse, and both sides know how much is riding on it with Germany lurking at the top of the group.

Group F

Sweden vs Tunisia

Sunday, 10:00 PM ET | Guadalupe, Mexico
Stage
Group F
Kickoff
10:00 PM ET
Venue
Mexico

The Sunday card closes south of the border, where Sweden takes on Tunisia in a Group F night match at one of the tournament's Mexican co-host venues. Sweden brings the physical, organized, set-piece-oriented style that has long served Scandinavian sides well in major tournaments, a team that defends in numbers and looks to be ruthless from dead-ball situations and on the break. In a group that features the Netherlands and Japan, Sweden cannot afford to drop points against a side it will view as a must-win.

Tunisia represents North Africa with a side that has become a regular presence at the World Cup, built on defensive solidity and the kind of tournament experience that makes them a tricky out. The Carthage Eagles are comfortable sitting deep, frustrating more fancied opponents, and looking to nick a goal on the counter or from a set piece of their own. The late kickoff and the unique atmosphere of a Mexican host crowd add their own flavor to a match both teams will treat as pivotal to their hopes of advancing.
