He now has 909 career goals, sitting atop the all-time list, and he's on pace for his 20th consecutive 30+ goal season. That kind of consistency over two decades is unprecedented.

Ovechkin's recent hat trick shows he still has the killer instinct. He now has 33 career hat tricks, tied with Brett Hull for 4th all-time. San Jose is heading in the opposite direction. The Sharks just got demolished 6-0 by Colorado on December 1st, with Mackenzie Blackwood posting a 26-save shutout against them. That's the kind of demoralizing loss that can linger. San Jose ranks 19th in goals for (2.96 per game) and 22nd in goals against (3.22) - they're a below-average team on both ends of the ice.

The form gap here is massive. Washington is one of the hottest teams in the NHL right now, playing with confidence and riding their superstar's resurgence. San Jose is struggling to find any consistency and just suffered an embarrassing shutout loss. The Capitals should control this game from start to finish.
