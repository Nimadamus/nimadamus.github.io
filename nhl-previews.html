Game 1 TNT

Minnesota Wild @ Tampa Bay Lightning

Tuesday, 7:30 PM ET | Amalie Arena
Puck Line
TB -1.5 (+150)
Moneyline
TB -170 / MIN EVEN
Total
O/U 6.5

This is the marquee matchup of the evening and it's easy to see why TNT snagged it. Minnesota's getting a massive boost with both Kirill Kaprizov and Joel Eriksson Ek returning from injury tonight, injecting life into a Wild team that's been grinding through a tough stretch without its top players. The Wild sit at 92 points and are firmly in the playoff picture, but they can't afford to coast with the Western Conference race tightening by the day. Kaprizov's return alone changes the complexion of this roster, and his chemistry with the top line should be instant.

Tampa Bay has been one of the most consistent teams in the league this season, riding Andrei Vasilevskiy's Vezina-caliber play to 91 points and second place in the Atlantic Division. Vasilevskiy's been a brick wall at home, and the Lightning's 5-on-5 play has been elite, particularly at Amalie Arena where their goals-for-per-game numbers are among the league's best. The depth scoring that Jon Cooper has cultivated this year makes them a nightmare matchup for any team, and they've been capitalizing on opponents who give them even the slightest opening.

The Wild will be without Marcus Foligno for a 12th consecutive game, and that's a real absence in the bottom six. Foligno's physical presence and penalty killing have been sorely missed, and it puts more pressure on the returning players to produce immediately. Filip Gustavsson will need to be sharp between the pipes, because Tampa's transition game can turn defensive zone turnovers into Grade-A chances in a heartbeat. Minnesota's penalty kill will be tested against a Lightning power play that's been clicking at a dangerous rate recently.

The total at 6.5 is fascinating for a game featuring two teams that can score in bunches. With Kaprizov and Eriksson Ek returning, Minnesota's offensive ceiling just got a whole lot higher, and Tampa rarely plays low-event hockey at home. Both teams have the goaltending to steal a period or two, but the talent on the ice tonight suggests fireworks. This feels like a playoff-atmosphere game on a Tuesday night in Tampa, and both teams know they're jockeying for seeding that could matter in April.

Game 2 ESPN+

Colorado Avalanche @ Pittsburgh Penguins

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | PPG Paints Arena
Puck Line
COL -1.5 (+142)
Moneyline
COL -175 / PIT +145
Total
O/U 6.5

Colorado became the first team in the NHL to clinch a 2026 playoff berth, and they're showing zero signs of taking their foot off the gas. At 46-13-10 with 102 points, the Avalanche are the class of the league and they're getting healthier at exactly the right time. Logan O'Connor and Ross Colton are both returning to the lineup tonight, giving Jared Bednar more depth options as the team starts ramping up for the postseason.

Pittsburgh has a storyline all their own tonight. Sidney Crosby, who's been nursing a knee injury sustained at the Olympics, has put up 4 points in his last 3 games and looks like he's rounding back into form. The Penguins sit at 86 points and are making their first real playoff push since 2022. This is a franchise that's been through the wilderness and is finally seeing light. However, they'll be without Evgeni Malkin tonight, which puts more offensive burden on Crosby and the supporting cast.

The goaltending matchup is intriguing, with Scott Wedgewood for Colorado and Arturs Silovs for Pittsburgh. Neither inspires overwhelming confidence, which is why that 6.5 total feels right. Colorado's transition game and power play are lethal, and Pittsburgh has shown a tendency to trade chances rather than lock things down defensively. When the Penguins get into track meets, they can hang with anyone, but against the deepest forward group in the league, that's a dangerous game.

The real question is whether Pittsburgh can match Colorado's intensity level. The Avalanche are playing with purpose even though they've already clinched, because home ice and seeding still matter. Colorado's Corsi and Fenwick numbers are top-five in the NHL, and their ability to control possession makes them a brutal team to play from behind. Pittsburgh will need Crosby to be Superman, their power play to convert early, and Silovs to steal at least one period. It's a tall order against the best team in hockey, but PPG Paints Arena in a meaningful March game will be rocking.

Game 3 ESPN+/Hulu

Edmonton Oilers @ Utah Mammoth

Tuesday, 9:30 PM ET | Delta Center
Puck Line
UTA -1.5 (+180)
Moneyline
UTA -135 / EDM +114
Total
O/U 6.5

This is uncharted territory for the Edmonton Oilers. For the first time since Connor McDavid arrived in 2015, both he and Leon Draisaitl are out of the lineup simultaneously. McDavid went down on March 20 with a lower body injury, and Draisaitl has been ruled out for the remainder of the regular season since March 18. That's roughly 40% of Edmonton's offensive production just gone. The Oilers sit at 77 points and are in genuine danger of missing the playoffs entirely.

Utah is in a completely different headspace. The Mammoth hold the first wild card spot in the West at 80 points, and they've been playing with the urgency of a franchise in its inaugural season trying to make the postseason. Karel Vejmelka has been steady in goal, and Utah's defensive structure has given them a chance in virtually every game. They don't blow teams out often, but they rarely get blown out either, and their ability to play tight hockey at home makes them a tough out at Delta Center.

Tristan Jarry gets the start for Edmonton, and there's a real question about how this Oilers team functions without two generational talents driving the bus. Guys like Zach Hyman, Ryan Nugent-Hopkins, and Evan Bouchard need to shoulder a burden they've never had to carry alone before. Edmonton's power play, which lives and dies with McDavid and Draisaitl, will look fundamentally different tonight.

The 6.5 total feels high given the circumstances. Edmonton's offensive ceiling without their two superstars drops dramatically, and Utah doesn't play the kind of wide-open game that generates a lot of goals. For Utah, this is a golden opportunity to grab two points against a weakened opponent. For Edmonton, it's about pride, resilience, and proving that the supporting cast can keep the ship afloat during the most critical stretch of the season. The hockey world will be watching to see how the Oilers respond to adversity unlike anything they've faced in the McDavid era.

Toronto Maple Leafs @ Boston Bruins

The Original Six rivalry doesn't carry the same weight it usually does with Toronto at 29-29-13 and essentially eliminated without Auston Matthews (Grade 3 MCL). Anthony Stolarz draws the start for a Leafs team playing out the string. Boston is clinging to the first wild card at 86 points and can't afford to drop these two points.

Jeremy Swayman has been solid for the Bruins, and their defensive structure remains one of the league's best. Boston's penalty kill has been particularly effective recently, and they'll look to suffocate a Toronto offense that's been anemic without Matthews driving the top line. The Bruins have gone 5-2-3 in their last 10, which is fine but not great.

Toronto's power play needs William Nylander and John Tavares to step up, but Boston's disciplined play at home won't make that easy. The Leafs have shown a tendency to play loose when the pressure is off, which can lead to track meets, but Swayman vs. Stolarz slightly favors the home side.

Expect the Bruins to control the tempo and grind Toronto into submission. Boston needs this one for playoff positioning, and the urgency gap between these two teams should show up on the ice. The 6.5 total could lean under with both teams' defensive tendencies and Toronto's limited offensive ceiling.

Carolina leads the Metro at 96 points but they've taken a goaltending blow with Pyotr Kochetkov out for the season (hip). Frederik Andersen is the undisputed number one now, and he's been outstanding when healthy. The Hurricanes' Corsi and expected goals numbers rank top-five in the NHL, and Rod Brind'Amour's system continues to generate high-quality chances while limiting opponents.

Montreal has been one of the best stories in hockey this season at 38-21-10 with 86 points. Cole Caufield has 43 goals and is on pace for 50, establishing himself as one of the premier goal scorers in the league. The Bell Centre will be rocking, and Jakub Dobes gets the start for a Canadiens team that's earned its place in the playoff race.

The matchup to watch is Carolina's relentless forecheck against Montreal's breakout game. The Hurricanes pressure puck carriers and force turnovers that lead to extended zone time. Montreal has handled that pressure better than most, but Carolina's pace can wear down anyone over 60 minutes. The Canadiens will look to use their speed in transition and get Caufield the puck in dangerous areas.

That 6.5 total tells you the market expects offense, and both teams have the firepower to deliver. The power play battle could be decisive. This is a fantastic early-evening matchup between a Metro powerhouse and a resurgent Original Six franchise. Caufield chasing 50 makes every shift electric, and Carolina's road record has been stellar all season.

The defending back-to-back Stanley Cup champions are decimated. Florida's home crowd will try to rally a battered roster, but the question is whether there's enough left.

This feels like a game decided by a goal or two. Special teams will loom large, and whichever team's depth players step up will grab the two points. It's been one of the most dramatic falls from grace in recent NHL history for Florida, and tonight is another chapter.
