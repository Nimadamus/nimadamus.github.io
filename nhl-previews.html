Jets @ Rangers

Sunday, 12:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York
Puck Line
NYR -1.5
Moneyline
WPG +103 / NYR -124
Total
O/U 5.5

Here's a matinee between two teams whose seasons haven't gone according to plan, but there's a wrinkle that makes this one worth watching. The Rangers are throwing Dylan Garand into his NHL debut after Jonathan Quick went down with an upper-body injury. That's a massive wildcard. Garand's been solid in Hartford, but making your first NHL start at MSG on a Sunday matinee with the building half-expecting you to fail? That's a tall order for any kid.

Winnipeg's been a study in mediocrity down the stretch, sitting at 5-3-2 over their last ten and firmly out of the playoff picture. The Jets can still score, but their inconsistency on the road has been a theme all year. New York's power play is humming at 24.0%, which gives them a real weapon if the Jets take undisciplined penalties, and the Rangers' 5-4-1 L10 mark shows they've at least been competitive even if the season's been a disappointment.

The total at 5.5 feels about right given these teams' offensive struggles, but a rookie goalie making his debut could push this one either direction. If Garand settles in, the Rangers' slight home-ice edge and power play advantage should give them the nod. If he's shaky early, the Jets will capitalize. It's a coin-flip game, which is exactly what the near-even moneyline suggests.

Here's what makes Garand's debut so fascinating from a betting perspective: MSG crowds can go one of two ways with a rookie. They'll either rally behind the kid and create an electric atmosphere that lifts his game, or they'll turn on him at the first soft goal and make the nerves even worse. Either way, the 5.5 total becomes a real sweat. Both teams have had inconsistent goaltending all season, and throwing an untested kid into the mix adds another layer of uncertainty. If you're playing this game, the total is where the value lives, because neither team's netminding inspires confidence and this one could easily tip over with a shaky period from either end.

Avalanche @ Capitals

Sunday, 12:30 PM ET | Capital One Arena, Washington
Puck Line
COL -1.5
Moneyline
COL -160 / WSH +135
Total
O/U 6

Colorado has already clinched a playoff spot, the first team in the NHL to do so, and they're sitting at a staggering 100 points with a 45-13-10 record. This is the best team in hockey and it's not particularly close. Their 3.69 goals per game lead the entire league, and the offensive depth is absurd. Mackenzie Blackwood gets the start with a 19-8-1 record and .905 save percentage, which isn't elite but more than serviceable behind this kind of firepower.

Washington's in a tricky spot. They're sitting 5th in the Metro with 78 points and desperately need wins to stay in the wild card conversation. Logan Thompson (24-19-5, .915 SV%) has been the better goalie on paper, and the Caps will need him to be spectacular today. The problem is that nobody's been able to consistently slow down Colorado's attack. The Avs just keep rolling through everybody, and they don't take games off even after clinching.

The total at 6 is interesting because Colorado pushes pace and creates volume, but Thompson's been solid enough to keep Washington competitive. Don't be surprised if this one turns into a track meet. The Avalanche are deserved favorites at -160, and laying -1.5 on the puck line isn't crazy given how dominant they've been. Washington will fight, but there's a clear talent gap here.

The pace implications here are significant. Colorado plays at a tempo that very few teams can match, and when opponents try to run with them, it usually ends badly. The Avs' explosive transition game generates high-danger chances in bunches, and Thompson will need to be at his absolute best to keep this within striking distance. For Washington, a win here would be monumental for their wild card push, giving them a signature result against the best team in hockey. For Colorado, it's about the Presidents' Trophy chase, and they don't take their foot off the gas. The total at 6 feels like it should be higher given the pace Colorado dictates, but Thompson's solid season could keep just enough rubber out to sneak under.

Hurricanes @ Penguins

Puck Line
CAR -1.5
Moneyline
CAR -143 / PIT +120

O/U 6.5

This is a massive Metro Division matchup with serious playoff implications. Carolina sits atop the division with 94 points and 3.48 goals per game, playing the kind of relentless hockey that makes them a legitimate Cup contender. Pittsburgh, meanwhile, is an absolute enigma. They've got 86 points, but look at that record: 35-18-16. Sixteen overtime losses. That's the most in the entire league, and it tells you everything about this team. They're good enough to be in every game but can't close the deal in tight ones.

Pittsburgh's power play at 25.4% is elite, and their penalty kill at 84.0% is equally impressive. Those special teams numbers keep them dangerous against anyone, including the Hurricanes. But here's the concern: Jeff Skinner's in net for the Pens with a 20-13-9 record and an .889 save percentage. That .889 number is not what you want from your goaltender in a game of this magnitude. Carolina's high-octane attack with a 22.1% power play will test him constantly.

The 6.5 total is the highest on the board alongside the Sabres-Ducks game, and it makes sense. Both teams can score in bunches, Pittsburgh's goaltending has been leaky, and Carolina doesn't have many quiet nights offensively. This has the feel of a 4-3 or 5-3 type game. Carolina's the better team on paper, but Pittsburgh at +120 at home with those special teams numbers isn't a bad spot if you like an underdog.

The power play battle is what could decide this one. Carolina's 22.1% conversion rate is dangerous, but Pittsburgh's 25.4% is even better, and their 84.0% penalty kill means they're excellent on both sides of special teams. That 6.5 total being the highest involving either of these teams all season tells you the market expects fireworks. Pittsburgh's 16 overtime losses prove they don't get blown out, they stay in every game, which is why the +120 price isn't wider. This result has real Metro Division implications: a Carolina win tightens their grip on the top spot, while a Pittsburgh regulation win could vault them into a more comfortable playoff position. However this shakes out, don't expect either team to go quietly.

Two teams playing out the string with nothing to play for except pride and draft positioning. Nashville's been brutal lately, going 3-6-1 over their last ten, and there's a real question mark in net with Juuse Saros listed as day-to-day after missing three straight games. If Saros can't go, you're looking at a backup goalie in a game that already feels like a toss-up. Chicago's been even worse at 2-6-2 in their last ten, and the United Center hasn't exactly been a fortress this year.

The one thing that stands out for Chicago is their penalty kill, which sits at a surprisingly impressive 83.8%. Nashville's power play converts at 23.3%, so if the Preds get opportunities with the man advantage, it won't be an automatic bucket. Alex Knight (18-19-9, .909) has been competent for Chicago, and in a game between two below-average teams, competent goaltending might be enough to steal one.

The moneyline being essentially dead even tells you everything. Nobody knows what's going to happen here, and honestly, it's hard to care deeply about the outcome. The total at 6 feels like the play to focus on. Both teams' defenses have been suspect all year, and without Saros in net for Nashville, there's a real chance this one opens up. It's an ugly game on paper, but ugly games can still be fun to watch when both sides are leaking goals.

Saros' availability is genuinely the key variable in this entire game. If he's in, Nashville has a slight edge because he's been their best player all season and can steal games on his own. If he's out, you're looking at a backup-versus-backup situation where neither team inspires any defensive confidence, and the 6 total starts looking like a floor instead of a ceiling. Both teams' defensive struggles have been well-documented, and this is essentially a meaningless game for the standings, but there are still spoiler implications. A Nashville win keeps them mathematically alive for a few more days, and Chicago would love nothing more than to play the role of dream-crusher. The total is the angle here, and if Saros sits, the over becomes very attractive.

Blue Jackets @ Islanders

Puck Line
CBJ -1.5
Moneyline
CBJ -118 / NYI -102

O/U 6

Circle this one. Columbus sits at 85 points and the Islanders are at 83, and both teams are fighting for their playoff lives in the Eastern Conference. The Blue Jackets have been one of the best stories in hockey since the coaching change to Rick Bowness, going an absurd 17-2-4 under his leadership. That's a complete transformation from the team that was floundering earlier in the season. Nico Greaves (23-12-8, .908) gets the start and has been rock-solid during this run.

The Islanders aren't going to just roll over, though. They're 7-3-0 over their last ten games and have been playing with desperation you'd expect from a team on the bubble. Ilya Sorokin (24-17-2, .909) is likely to get the call, and when he's dialed in, he can steal games single-handedly. UBS Arena has been a tough building for visitors, and the crowd will be electric for a game with this much on the line.

This feels like a classic "game of the night" situation. Two evenly matched teams, both desperate for points, both playing their best hockey of the season. The moneyline being so tight (CBJ -118, NYI -102) reflects just how close this is. Columbus has the better recent track record under Bowness, but the Islanders have home ice and momentum. Don't be shocked if this one goes to overtime. Every point matters for both sides down the stretch.

The Bowness effect can't be overstated. Going 17-2-4 under a new coach isn't just good, it's borderline historic, and it's turned Columbus from a lottery team into a legitimate playoff threat. But here's the thing: this coin-flip game could genuinely swing the entire Eastern Conference playoff picture. A two-point swing between teams separated by two points in the standings is massive at this stage of the season. The goaltending matchup between Greaves (.908) and Sorokin (.909) is essentially a wash, which means this comes down to five-on-five execution and which team handles the pressure better. UBS Arena with playoff hopes on the line is going to be absolutely rocking, and that atmosphere could be the difference in a one-goal game. This is appointment hockey.

Dallas is an absolute machine right now. They're 8-1-1 in their last ten, sitting at 97 points (second-best in the NHL behind only Colorado), and their power play is converting at a scorching 28.8%. That's elite-level stuff, and it means Vegas has to play a clean, disciplined game to have any chance. The Stars at home in the American Airlines Center have been nearly unbeatable, and the atmosphere for a Sunday night game against a division rival will be intense.

Vegas is clinging to wild card hopes at 76 points, but here's the problem: Adin Hill's been an absolute liability with an .869 save percentage and an 8-8-3 record. That number is brutal. You can't expect to compete against the second-best team in hockey when your goaltender is posting sub-.870 numbers. The Golden Knights are 4-4-2 in their last ten, which is the definition of treading water when they need to be sprinting.

The mismatch in goal is the story of this game. Dallas has the firepower to exploit Hill's weaknesses, and that 28.8% power play means one or two Vegas penalties could blow this thing wide open. The total at 6.5 reflects the expectation that Dallas will pour it on. Vegas at +124 might tempt some bettors, but it's hard to back a team with that kind of goaltending crisis on the road against a juggernaut. Dallas should roll here.

Dallas's 28.8% power play is basically a death sentence for a Vegas team that takes penalties. One undisciplined moment, one stick infraction, and the Stars are converting at nearly a 1-in-3 clip. That's terrifying when you're asking Adin Hill and his .869 save percentage to bail you out. It's an impossible ask against this level of offensive talent. For Vegas, a loss here is devastating for their wild card hopes, potentially putting them six or more points out of the final spot with the season winding down. The Golden Knights need to play the perfect road game, draw zero penalties, and somehow outwork Dallas at even strength. The odds of all three happening? Not great. That's why the -148 moneyline feels fair despite the seemingly tight number.

Puck Line
TBL -1.5
Moneyline
TBL -180 / CGY +160

Tampa Bay is rolling into Calgary on a three-game win streak and coming off a 5-2 demolition of Edmonton where Nikita Kucherov went off for two goals and two assists. When Kucherov's cooking like that, there aren't many teams in the league that can keep up. The Lightning are sitting at 90 points and second in the Atlantic, and their 3.59 goals per game ranks second in the entire NHL. This is a team that's built for a deep playoff run and is playing like it right now.

Calgary, on the other hand, is in the basement. Their 63 points tell the story, and their 2.46 goals per game is dead last in the NHL. Dead. Last. You can't win hockey games if you can't score, and the Flames simply don't have the offensive talent to hang with a team like Tampa Bay. The Saddledome crowd has had very little to cheer about this year, and tonight probably won't be any different.

Tampa at -180 is a heavy price, but it's hard to argue with the talent gap. The Lightning's offensive onslaught against Calgary's toothless attack is a nightmare matchup for the Flames. The total at 6 might actually skew to the over if Tampa gets rolling early, because Calgary doesn't have the defense to shut them down and will need to open things up to have any shot. This one could get ugly fast.

Kucherov's hot streak is what makes Tampa so terrifying right now. When he's cooking like this, the Lightning's depth scoring feeds off his energy, and everyone in the lineup becomes a threat. That's a problem for a Calgary team with the dead-last offense in the NHL at 2.46 goals per game. The Flames simply don't have the firepower to trade punches with Tampa's attack, which means they'd need to play a perfect defensive game and steal one at the Saddledome. The total at 6 is interesting because Tampa's road inconsistency (5-5-0 in their last ten away from home) suggests they're not always dialed in when traveling, and that could make this closer than the talent gap implies. But even a slightly off Tampa team should have enough to handle what Calgary puts out. If the Lightning play anywhere close to their ceiling, the Flames are in for a long night.

Sabres @ Ducks

Puck Line
BUF -1.5
Moneyline
BUF -111 / ANA -108
Total
O/U 6.5

Buffalo is the hottest team in hockey right now and it isn't even close. The Sabres are 9-1-0 in their last ten games, riding a four-game win streak, and sitting at 94 points tied for the most in the Eastern Conference. After years of being the league's punchline, Buffalo's finally playing like a legitimate contender, and the confidence is oozing from this roster. Malcolm Lyon (20-8-3, .914) has been excellent in net, giving them the kind of goaltending stability they haven't had in ages.

Here's the catch, though: Buffalo played in LA last night. Back-to-backs are the great equalizer in hockey, and even the hottest team in the world can look sluggish on the second night. Anaheim's no pushover either, leading the Pacific Division with 80 points and getting solid play from Lukas Dostal (28-15-3, .893). The Ducks score 3.20 goals per game and play a fast, physical style at Honda Center that can wear down tired legs. This is a legitimate test for the Sabres.

The total at 6.5 is the highest on the entire board, and for good reason. Buffalo averages 3.46 goals per game, Anaheim sits at 3.20, and both teams play at a pace that creates chances. Dostal's .893 save percentage suggests he's been beatable, and Lyon on the second half of a back-to-back might not be at his sharpest. This game screams offense. The moneyline being nearly even (BUF -111, ANA -108) shows the market respects the back-to-back factor. Anaheim might be live as a slight dog here.

The big question is whether that 9-1-0 streak survives the fatigue factor. Playing in LA yesterday and then hopping down to Anaheim isn't the worst travel in the world, but back-to-backs have a way of catching up with even the hottest teams, especially late in the season. Buffalo's legs could be heavy, and a fresh Anaheim squad at home knows they can exploit that. The 6.5 total being the highest on the board is no accident. Both of these offenses generate volume, and when you combine two teams that average a combined 6.66 goals per game with a tired goalie on one side and a beatable one on the other, you've got a recipe for fireworks. If Buffalo's energy dips even slightly, the Ducks have the talent to take advantage, and this one could turn into an up-and-down track meet that goes well north of the total.

Utah's fighting for their postseason lives with 78 points and a wild card spot hanging in the balance, and they draw a Kings team that's also on the second night of a back-to-back after losing to Buffalo yesterday. LA's record of 28-25-16 includes a league-tying 16 overtime losses, which means they're perpetually in games but can never quite finish. Darcy Kuemper (17-13-11, .897) has been inconsistent all year, and playing on tired legs won't help.

Karel Vejmelka (31-17-3, .897) gets the nod for Utah and has been a workhorse all season. The Hockey Club has been impressive in recent action, posting a 2-1-0 record with a 13-7 goal differential in a recent stretch against playoff-caliber opponents. The Delta Center crowd has embraced this team, and the energy in that building for a meaningful March game should give Utah a real boost. They know every point matters from here on out.

The Kings at +140 might look tempting as a price, but the back-to-back factor combined with Utah's desperation makes it a tough sell. LA hasn't shown the ability to win games that matter this season, and tonight qualifies as one that matters far more for Utah than for the Kings. The 5.5 total is the lowest on the board, reflecting both teams' goaltending and the likelihood that Utah plays a tight, structured game to protect a lead. Expect Utah to grind this one out at home.

The Kings being on a back-to-back too, after losing to Buffalo last night, is a huge factor that can't be ignored. LA's already struggled to close out games all season with those 16 overtime losses, and asking tired legs to do it on the road against a desperate team is a recipe for disappointment. Utah's wild card desperation means they'll play this like a playoff game. Every loose puck, every board battle, every blocked shot will have maximum effort behind it. The 5.5 total being the lowest on the board tells you everything about the expected pace. Utah won't open this up. They'll play a grinding, physical, low-event game designed to smother LA's already limited attack and protect whatever lead Vejmelka gives them. For bettors, the under looks appealing in a game where one team can't score and the other team doesn't want to.
