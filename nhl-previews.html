NHL Previews Today

A quiet two-game Friday in the NHL, but both matchups carry weight. The surging Buffalo Sabres (44-20-8, 96 pts) host the Detroit Red Wings at KeyBank Center as Buffalo continues to cement its status as one of the league's elite teams, while the New York Rangers try to snap a brutal six-game losing streak when they welcome the Chicago Blackhawks to Madison Square Garden in a battle between two teams whose seasons have gone sideways.

Game 1
NHL Net

Red Wings @ Sabres

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | KeyBank Center, Buffalo, NY
Puck Line
BUF -1.5 (+160)
Moneyline
DET +132 / BUF -158
Total
O/U 6.5

Buffalo has been the story of the NHL this season, and tonight they get a chance to flex that dominance at home against a Detroit team fighting tooth and nail for playoff positioning. The Sabres are rolling at 44-20-8, putting up 3.53 goals per game while allowing just 2.93. That kind of differential doesn't happen by accident. This is a team built from the net out, with Ukko-Pekka Luukkonen turning into one of the league's elite goaltenders. His last six starts tell the whole story: 5-1-0 with a 2.18 GAA and a .925 save percentage. When he's dialed in like this, KeyBank Center becomes a fortress.

Detroit sits at 38-25-8, which is a perfectly respectable record, but the Red Wings have a scoring problem that could haunt them tonight. At 2.92 goals per game, they're middle of the pack offensively, and John Gibson's .907 save percentage isn't going to steal many games against a Buffalo attack this potent. The Red Wings also have a Cam Talbot situation to monitor. He's listed as day-to-day after missing practice, and if he can't go, that puts even more pressure on Gibson to be perfect in a building where the Sabres rarely lose.

Tage Thompson has been an absolute wrecking ball this year with 36 goals and 71 points, and Rasmus Dahlin continues to rewrite what's possible from the blue line with 15 goals and 63 points. Buffalo's power play has been clinical at home, and Detroit's penalty kill has shown cracks on the road this season. If the Red Wings take undisciplined penalties in this one, it could get ugly fast. The Sabres' depth scoring has also been elite, meaning you can't just shut down Thompson and Dahlin and call it a night.

The 6.5 total is intriguing for a game that features one of the league's best offenses against a team that can be leaky defensively. Buffalo's games have been trending toward fireworks at home, and Detroit has enough talent to contribute to a high-scoring affair even if they're outmatched. The puck line at BUF -1.5 (+160) tells you the market respects how close Detroit can keep things, but the Sabres have been covering at an impressive clip when they're rolling like this. This is a marquee matchup between a legitimate Cup contender and a hungry playoff hopeful, and the atmosphere at KeyBank Center should be electric.

Game 2
ESPN+

Blackhawks @ Rangers

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Puck Line
NYR -1.5 (+150)
Moneyline
CHI +140 / NYR -165
Total
O/U 5.5

Welcome to the battle of two teams heading in very different long-term directions, even if the standings don't look pretty for either side right now. The Rangers have been officially eliminated from playoff contention, sitting at 28-35-9 with a brutal 0-6 losing streak and a 9-18-7 home record that would make any MSG ticket holder wince. New York's season has been a disaster by their own standards, and the energy at Madison Square Garden has shifted from frustration to resignation. On the other side, Chicago at 27-32-13 isn't going anywhere either, but they've got a reason to watch every single game: Connor Bedard.

Bedard has been spectacular down the stretch with 29 goals and 66 points in just 57 games this season. His March has been particularly impressive, posting 4 goals and 7 assists in his last 11 games. The kid is special, and he's showing it on a nightly basis. For a rebuilding franchise, this is exactly what you want to see from your cornerstone player. Spencer Knight has been steady in net at 18-19-10 with a 2.64 GAA and .909 save percentage, giving Chicago a reliable foundation even in a losing season.

For the Rangers, Igor Shesterkin remains the one bright spot in this mess. His 22-15-6 record with a 2.55 GAA and .912 save percentage is impressive given the dumpster fire around him. Jonathan Quick is listed as day-to-day, so Shesterkin will likely get the start, and he'll want to put on a show for the home crowd even with nothing left to play for. The problem is that the Rangers' skaters in front of him have completely checked out. A six-game losing streak in late March tells you everything about where this team's head is at.

The 5.5 total reflects the expectation that this could be a lower-scoring, grinding affair. The under has cashed in four of the Rangers' last five home games, and neither team is exactly lighting the lamp at will right now. New York's home struggles are well documented, and Chicago, despite the rebuild, has been competitive on the road. The Rangers are the slight favorite at -165, but laying that kind of juice on a team that's lost six straight at home feels dicey. This game could go either way, and the real entertainment value might just be watching Bedard and Shesterkin go head to head in what should be a tight, low-event contest.
