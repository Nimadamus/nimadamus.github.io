5-Game Monday Slate

NHL Previews Today

Monday's 5-game NHL slate is headlined by a critical playoff-positioning showdown between the Penguins and Islanders at UBS Arena, with Sidney Crosby's squad sitting just one point behind New York in the wild-card race. The 48-win Colorado Avalanche host Calgary as overwhelming favorites, the Golden Knights welcome Vancouver with Mitch Marner leading the charge, and the surprising Anaheim Ducks host Toronto as home favorites in a game that perfectly captures the changing of the guard in hockey.

Game 1
NHL Network

Penguins @ Islanders

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | UBS Arena, Elmont, NY
Puck Line
PIT +1.5 (-238) / NYI -1.5 (+195)
Moneyline
PIT +110 / NYI -130
Total
O/U 5.5

This is the game of the night, and it isn't particularly close. Pittsburgh and New York Island enter Monday separated by a single point in the Eastern Conference wild card race, with the Penguins sitting at 88 points and the Islanders at 89. That's as close to a must-win as it gets for both sides with only a handful of games remaining in the regular season. Every shift, every faceoff, every save carries playoff implications tonight at UBS Arena.

Sidney Crosby continues to defy Father Time, and he's been sensational down the stretch. The captain has posted 7 points in his last 5 games and remains one of the most dangerous playmakers in the league when the stakes are highest. Pittsburgh's power play has been clicking at 24.3% over the last month, and if the Islanders take penalties, Crosby and company will make them pay. The Penguins also get a boost from Tristan Jarry, who has been significantly more consistent since the All-Star break.

For the Islanders, Ilya Sorokin has been the backbone all season long. His 2.41 GAA and .921 save percentage give New York a chance to win every single night, and he's been even better at home where the Islanders have posted a 22-12-4 record. Mathew Barzal has elevated his game with 5 goals and 9 points over the last 8 contests, and Bo Horvat continues to provide the kind of two-way center play that makes this Islanders team tough to break down. Their defensive structure under Patrick Roy has been suffocating at times.

The total sits at 5.5, which feels about right for two teams that play tight, structured hockey. These divisional matchups tend to be lower-scoring affairs, and both goaltenders have the ability to steal a game. Expect an intense, physical battle where the margin of error is razor-thin. The team that wins the special teams battle and limits turnovers in the neutral zone will likely be the one celebrating when the final horn sounds. This is playoff hockey in late March, and it doesn't get much better than this.

Game 2
ESPN+

Flames @ Avalanche

Monday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Puck Line
CGY +1.5 (+120) / COL -1.5 (-142)
Moneyline
CGY +295 / COL -375
Total
O/U 6.5

Colorado is the best team in the NHL, and they play like it every single night. At 48-14-10, the Avalanche have been an absolute buzzsaw all season, and they're rolling into this Monday showdown with the kind of confidence that only comes from knowing you're the class of the league. Nathan MacKinnon is putting together another MVP-caliber campaign, and Cale Makar continues to redefine what a defenseman can be in the modern NHL. Together, they form the most dangerous 1-2 punch in hockey.

The Flames have had a solid season overall but come into Ball Arena as massive underdogs for good reason. Calgary's road record has been pedestrian compared to their home form, and playing at altitude in Denver against the league's deepest roster is a nightmare assignment. The Flames' defensive structure will be tested relentlessly by Colorado's transition game, which generates high-danger chances at an elite rate. Dustin Wolf has been outstanding in net for Calgary, but even the best goaltenders get overwhelmed when the Avs are clicking.

That 6.5 total tells you everything about what the market expects. Colorado averages 3.8 goals per game at home, and they have the ability to put up crooked numbers in a hurry. Their power play is operating at 27.1% and their 5-on-5 expected goals percentage ranks top-3 in the league. MacKinnon, Mikko Rantanen, and the rest of the forward group generate so much offensive zone time that it becomes exhausting for opponents. The Flames will need to be disciplined and hope their goaltending keeps them close.

For Calgary, this is a chance to prove something against the best. Nazem Kadri returns to Colorado where he won a Stanley Cup, and he'll be motivated to show out. Jonathan Huberdeau has quietly found his game in the second half, and his vision in the offensive zone gives Calgary a legitimate playmaking threat. Still, the altitude, the crowd, and the sheer talent gap make this a brutal spot for the visitors. Colorado should control this game from the opening faceoff.

Game 3
ESPN+

Canucks @ Golden Knights

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas, NV
Puck Line
VAN +1.5 (+120) / VGK -1.5 (-142)
Moneyline
VAN +285 / VGK -360
Total
O/U 6.5

The Vegas Golden Knights have been one of the league's elite teams all season, and the addition of Mitch Marner has taken their offense to another level. Marner, acquired from Toronto in July 2025, has been everything Vegas hoped for and more. His playmaking ability combined with Jack Eichel's scoring touch gives the Golden Knights one of the most dynamic top-six forward groups in the NHL. Vegas is firmly locked into a playoff position and looking to build momentum heading into the postseason.

Vancouver, on the other hand, has had a season to forget. At 21-43-8, the Canucks own the worst record on Monday's slate and are already looking ahead to the draft lottery. The magic of their 2024 playoff run feels like a lifetime ago, and the roster has been decimated by injuries, trades, and underperformance. Elias Pettersson hasn't been able to carry this team the way he did last season, and the defensive structure has completely fallen apart. They're allowing over 3.5 goals per game, which is a recipe for disaster against a team as lethal as Vegas.

Eichel has been phenomenal this season, consistently producing at a point-per-game pace while driving play at both ends of the ice. Marner's transition from Toronto to Vegas has been seamless, and the two have developed legitimate chemistry on the top line. The Knights' power play ranks in the top 10 league-wide, and they've been ruthless at home where T-Mobile Arena remains one of the toughest buildings for visiting teams. Vancouver's penalty kill has been among the league's worst, which spells trouble tonight.

The 6.5 total reflects the one-sided nature of this matchup. Vegas should generate plenty of offense against a Canucks defense that has been leaking goals all year. Vancouver might get on the board through some individual skill or a lucky bounce, but containing the Golden Knights' attack for a full 60 minutes seems like a tall order. This is a game where Vegas should control possession, dictate tempo, and cruise to a comfortable victory on home ice.

Game 4
ESPN+

Maple Leafs @ Ducks

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Honda Center, Anaheim, CA
Puck Line
TOR +1.5 (-148) / ANA -1.5 (+124)
Moneyline
TOR +160 / ANA -192
Total
O/U 6.5

This might be the most fascinating storyline of the night. The Anaheim Ducks, who were supposed to be in rebuild mode, have shocked the hockey world with a 41-28-4 record that has them firmly in the playoff picture. Meanwhile, the Maple Leafs are still adjusting to life without Mitch Marner, who was traded to Vegas in July 2025, and the results have been mixed. Toronto has talent, obviously, with Auston Matthews leading the charge, but there's a clear void in their playmaking that hasn't been adequately filled.

Anaheim's emergence has been one of the best stories in the NHL this season. Their young core, led by Trevor Zegras, Leo Carlsson, and Mason McTavish, has matured faster than anyone expected. The Ducks play a fast, aggressive brand of hockey that creates turnovers and generates transition offense in bunches. Their goaltending has been solid, and the defensive pairs have bought into a system that limits high-danger chances. At home, they've been dominant, feeding off an energized fanbase that hasn't had much to cheer about in recent years.

Matthews remains an absolute weapon and is always capable of taking over a game on his own. His release is the best in hockey, and he's scored at least one goal in 8 of his last 12 games. William Nylander has stepped into a larger role since Marner's departure and has been excellent, but Toronto's secondary scoring has been inconsistent. The Leafs need more from their bottom-six forwards if they want to compete in games where the top line gets shut down.

The market has Anaheim as a clear home favorite, which would have been unthinkable at the start of the season. But the Ducks have earned that respect with their play on the ice. Toronto is a live underdog thanks to Matthews' ability to single-handedly swing a game, but the Ducks' depth and home-ice advantage make them the rightful favorites. This is a fascinating clash of a surprise contender against a traditional power that's going through an identity crisis.

Game 5
ESPN+

Blues @ Sharks

Monday, 10:30 PM ET | SAP Center, San Jose, CA
Puck Line
STL +1.5 (-278) / SJ -1.5 (+225)
Moneyline
STL -110 / SJ -110
Total
O/U 5.5

Here's your true coin-flip game of the evening. Both moneylines sitting at -110 tells you everything you need to know about how the market views this one. St. Louis and San Jose are essentially mirror images of each other right now, two teams in the mushy middle of the standings without much to play for beyond pride and player evaluation heading into the offseason. Neither team is making the playoffs, but neither is bad enough to be tanking with any real conviction.

The Blues have some intriguing pieces that could be building blocks for the future. Robert Thomas continues to develop into a legitimate top-line center, and Jordan Kyrou's speed and skill are undeniable when he's engaged. Their power play has been average, and the defensive corps has been a revolving door of inconsistency all season. Jordan Binnington has had his moments in goal but has also been prone to rough stretches that have defined St. Louis' boom-or-bust nature this year.

San Jose's rebuild under Macklin Celebrini has been fascinating to watch. The first overall pick from the 2024 draft has shown flashes of the franchise-altering talent that made him the consensus top selection, though the growing pains have been real. The Sharks aren't going to scare anyone in the standings, but they're developing a competitive culture that wasn't there a year ago. Their young players are playing harder and smarter, and that progression matters more than wins and losses at this stage of the rebuild.

With both teams essentially playing out the string, this game comes down to effort and execution on a given night. The total at 5.5 suggests a relatively tight affair, and these late-season games between non-contenders can go either way depending on which team shows up with more energy. Don't expect a masterpiece of tactical hockey, but do expect two teams with young talent looking to make an impression on their coaching staffs with the offseason right around the corner.
