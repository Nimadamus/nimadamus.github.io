Canadiens @ Sabres

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | KeyBank Center, Buffalo, NY

The Stanley Cup Playoffs Round 2 East series between the Buffalo Sabres and Montreal Canadiens returns to KeyBank Center Friday night with the Sabres carrying a 1-0 series lead and the structural pressure on Montreal to manufacture a road split before the bracket shifts to the Bell Centre. Buffalo opens as the moneyline favorite at -130, an implied probability of 56.5 percent, with the total set at 5.5 goals. Buffalo controlled Game 1 in front of the home crowd at KeyBank Center, the kind of structural read on the matchup that confirms the Sabres' young-and-fast forward group has the playoff identity to stretch a Cup run into the second round.

Buffalo's structural identity is built around the centers Tage Thompson and Dylan Cozens leading the forward group, the kind of size-and-skill combination that produced the regular-season scoring depth that anchored the team across the 82-game grind. The defensive core of Owen Power and Rasmus Dahlin is the franchise piece that has finally translated into playoff results, and the goaltending tandem behind them carried the Round 1 series win that delivered the 2-seed at home-ice advantage. The home crowd at KeyBank Center is a structural variable books typically build into a -130 moneyline, and the Sabres' 1-0 lead reflects the kind of playoff identity that has produced a contender-caliber spring run.

Montreal's Game 2 path runs through Nick Suzuki's primary-creator profile and the goaltending performance behind him. Suzuki is the captain and the franchise scoring engine, and the Canadiens' young core of Lane Hutson, Cole Caufield, and Juraj Slafkovsky is the structural piece that has powered Montreal's playoff identity. The Habs entered the series as the underdog and historically converted at the kind of road-puck-line rate that any team with a competitive young core can extend into multiple games - Montreal had covered the puck line in 31 of 32 games as an underdog before Game 1 ended that streak. The road-split path requires the Caufield-Suzuki line to deliver high-leverage scoring, the defensive shape to neutralize the Sabres' top six, and the goaltending to produce the kind of clutch save sequences that flip momentum. Faceoff is 7:00 PM ET on TNT, truTV, and Max.

West Round 2 - Game 3
TNT/truTV/Max

Golden Knights @ Ducks

Friday, 9:30 PM ET | Honda Center, Anaheim, CA

The Stanley Cup Playoffs Round 2 West series shifts to Honda Center Friday night with the Vegas Golden Knights and Anaheim Ducks tied 1-1 after a road split in Las Vegas. Vegas opens as the slight road favorite at -111, with the Ducks just on the other side of the line at -109, a near-pick-em moneyline that reflects the kind of competitive read books have built into the Game 3 spot. The series shape coming into Anaheim is the kind of two-team-each-grabbed-a-game structural read that produces tightly-priced Game 3 lines, and the Honda Center home-crowd energy is the variable that books have weighted into the small price gap.

Vegas's structural identity is built around the Cup-window roster head coach Bruce Cassidy assembled across the 2023 championship cycle and the trade deadline depth that surrounded Jack Eichel's primary-creator profile. The Mitch Marner addition that arrived in July of 2025 added the kind of high-end skill and playoff scoring profile that has translated into the Round 2 series. Marner's combination with Eichel produced the regular-season scoring ceiling that anchored the Pacific Division standings, and the playoff identity has carried into the postseason at the kind of rate Vegas's championship-window organization expected. The defensive corps and goaltending tandem behind them remain the structural base that has produced consistent Cup contention across multiple seasons.

Anaheim's Game 3 path runs through the home-crowd energy and the kind of playoff variance the second-seeded Pacific bracket usually produces. The Ducks have hit the over in six of their last 10 home games and have earned a +4.75 unit ROI on home overs, the kind of variance trend that points toward a high-event Game 3 if the early possessions produce open ice. The young core forwards anchor the offensive identity, and the goaltending performance behind them is the structural variable that defines whether Anaheim can hold serve at home or whether Vegas's championship-window roster pulls the series back. Faceoff is 9:30 PM ET on TNT, truTV, and Max.
