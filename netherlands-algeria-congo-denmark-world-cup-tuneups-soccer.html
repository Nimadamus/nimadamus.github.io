World Cup Tune-Ups: Netherlands-Algeria Headlines the Card

The final wave of pre-World Cup friendlies brings a first-ever meeting to Rotterdam as Ronald Koeman's unbeaten Netherlands host Algeria at De Kuip. In Liege, Denmark face DR Congo, and in Fort Lauderdale, Haiti meet New Zealand. We break down all three with verified form and context ahead of the June 11 kickoff.

Netherlands vs Algeria

2:45 PM ET | De Kuip, Rotterdam
Favorite
Netherlands (~1.58)
Venue
De Kuip, Rotterdam
Competition
WC 2026 Tune-Up

The marquee international friendly of June 3 sends Ronald Koeman's Netherlands against Algeria at De Kuip in Rotterdam, the final major test for both nations before the 2026 World Cup kicks off on June 11. Remarkably, this is the first senior international meeting between the two countries, which adds a layer of intrigue to what is otherwise a tune-up. The Dutch are clear favorites at home, priced around 1.58 to win, a reflection of both form and setting.

The Netherlands arrive on a genuine roll, unbeaten in their last 14 matches, with Koeman fine-tuning a side built to make noise in North America. Algeria are no pushovers: the Desert Foxes have won three of their last five and bring real attacking talent and pace that can trouble any back line. As a friendly, the value is in the details, how heavily Koeman rotates, which fringe players stake a claim for the World Cup roster, and whether Algeria can use their athleticism to test a confident Dutch defense.

This is analysis only, not a pick. The storyline to watch is whether the Netherlands treat this as a serious dress rehearsal or a chance to look at depth, and whether Algeria's quick forwards can exploit any complacency. A first-ever meeting in a packed Rotterdam atmosphere is exactly the kind of stage both managers wanted for a final marquee warm-up.

DR Congo vs Denmark

2:00 PM ET | Liege, Belgium
Type
International Friendly
Venue
Liege, Belgium
Competition
WC 2026 Tune-Up

In Liege, DR Congo meet Denmark in another pre-World Cup warm-up, a clash of contrasting styles between an athletic, physical African side and a technically polished European outfit. Both camps are using this window to sharpen combinations and settle on their strongest available eleven before the tournament, so expect plenty of in-game tinkering as the managers run through their options.

Denmark's strength has long been their organization and midfield control, and they will want to dictate tempo against a DR Congo team that thrives in transition and on the counter. The Congolese have the individual quality up front to punish any lapse, which makes this a useful test of Denmark's defensive structure away from home in a neutral-feeling environment.

This is analysis only, not a pick. Watch how Denmark build through the middle and whether DR Congo's pace can stretch them, plus which depth players on both sides make a late case for a tournament role. For a friendly, there is real evaluative value here for two staffs finalizing their plans.

Haiti vs New Zealand

Chase Stadium, Fort Lauderdale
Type
International Friendly
Venue
Fort Lauderdale
Competition
WC 2026 Tune-Up

Rounding out the June 3 international slate, Haiti face New Zealand in Fort Lauderdale, a meeting of two sides preparing for their World Cup campaigns on North American soil. For Haiti, playing in Florida is close to a home environment given the large Haitian community in the region, and the Caribbean side will want to build rhythm and confidence in front of a supportive crowd.

New Zealand bring a disciplined, physical approach and will treat the trip as valuable acclimatization to the conditions and travel they will face during the tournament. The All Whites tend to be well-drilled defensively and dangerous from set pieces, while Haiti lean on energy and pace to create chances.

This is analysis only, not a pick. The intrigue is in preparation rather than the scoreline: which players seize their minutes, how each side handles the warm Florida conditions, and whether Haiti can carry momentum from a friendly into the biggest stage in their footballing history.
