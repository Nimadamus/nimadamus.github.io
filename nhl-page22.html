Blues @ Lightning

2025-12-23T00:00Z | Benchmark International Arena | ESPN+, FanDuel SN MW
STL: 14-15-8 | TB: 19-13-3

The Blues are limping into Tampa Bay riding a brutal stretch of hockey. At 14-15-8, St. Louis sits outside the playoff picture in the Western Conference and desperately needs to string together some wins before the holiday break. The problem? They're running into a Lightning team that's been quietly dominant at home, where Tampa Bay is 11-5-1 this season.

Here's what makes this a tough matchup for St. Louis: the Lightning's power play has been absolutely lethal, clicking at over 28% with Nikita Kucherov and Brayden Point running the show. The Blues' penalty kill, meanwhile, ranks in the bottom third of the league. That's a recipe for disaster. Tampa's also getting stellar goaltending from Andrei Vasilevskiy, who's looked much more like his Vezina-winning self lately.

Jordan Binnington will need to be sharp to keep St. Louis in this one. The Blues can compete when they get solid netminding and play a physical, forecheck-heavy style, but the Lightning's speed and skill might be too much to handle on the road.

Canucks @ Flyers

2025-12-23T00:30Z | Xfinity Mobile Arena | NBC Sports Phil, NHL Net
VAN: 15-17-3 | PHI: 17-10-7

This is a fascinating spot for the Canucks. Vancouver's been disappointing this year at 15-17-3 after making serious noise in last year's playoffs. Injuries have played a part, but the bigger issue is their defensive structure has completely collapsed. They're giving up way too many quality chances and Thatcher Demko hasn't been the elite goalie we saw last season.

Philadelphia, on the other hand, has been one of the pleasant surprises of the 2025-26 season. The Flyers are 17-10-7 and playing with swagger at home, where they've been tough to beat at the Wells Fargo Center. Travis Konecny continues to be an elite playmaker, and their young core is growing up fast.

Look for the Flyers to control the pace of this game. They play a tight, defensive system under John Tortorella that limits high-danger chances, and Vancouver's struggling offense might have trouble generating quality looks. The Canucks need a spark, but Philly isn't the place to find one right now.

Kraken @ Ducks

2025-12-23T03:00Z | Honda Center | ESPN+, KONG
Line: ANA -175 | O/U: 6.5 | SEA: 13-14-6 | ANA: 21-13-2

The Ducks are for real. Anaheim's 21-13-2 record is no fluke - this is a team that's found its identity and is playing winning hockey. The -175 moneyline reflects how much respect Vegas is giving the home team here. Mason McTavish has emerged as a legitimate top-line center, and the supporting cast has stepped up across the board.

Seattle comes in scuffling at 13-14-6, a far cry from where they hoped to be at this point. The Kraken's defensive issues persist, and their goaltending tandem hasn't been able to steal games consistently. When you're giving up goals at the rate Seattle is, you need elite netminding to compensate - and they just don't have it.

The total of 6.5 tells you oddsmakers expect some scoring in this one. The Ducks can put the puck in the net, and Seattle's defense might give them plenty of opportunities. Anaheim's home ice advantage has been significant this season, and I'd expect them to control this game from start to finish.

Jackets @ Kings

2025-12-23T03:00Z | crypto.com Arena | ESPN+, FanDuel SN West
Line: LA -162 | O/U: 5.5 | CBJ: 14-15-6 | LA: 15-10-9

The Kings are slight favorites at -162 here, and for good reason. Los Angeles is playing solid, consistent hockey at 15-10-9 and their veteran core knows how to close out games. Anze Kopitar might be 38, but he's still a difference-maker who elevates everyone around him.

Columbus is stuck in the middle at 14-15-6 - not good enough to be a playoff team, not bad enough to get a top draft pick. The Blue Jackets have young talent, but they're still learning how to win consistently in the NHL. Road games against experienced teams like the Kings expose their weaknesses.

The 5.5 total is the lowest of the night for NHL, signaling that oddsmakers expect a tight, defensive battle. Both goalies - David Rittich for the Jackets and Darcy Kuemper for the Kings - are capable of keeping this close. LA's structure and home ice should be the difference, but don't expect a blowout.
