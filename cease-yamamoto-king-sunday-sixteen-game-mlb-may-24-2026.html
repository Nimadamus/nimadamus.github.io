Cease, Yamamoto, King: Sunday's 16-Game MLB Slate

Dylan Cease of the Blue Jays and his league-leading strikeout total host Pittsburgh in Toronto, Yoshinobu Yamamoto looks to steady himself in Milwaukee, and Michael King anchors a Padres staff in San Diego. A Tigers-Orioles doubleheader at Camden Yards and a Dodgers-Brewers showdown between division leaders round out a loaded Sunday board.

Pirates at Blue Jays

12:15 PM ET | Rogers Centre | Keller vs Cease

This is the marquee pitching matchup of the early window. Dylan Cease of the Blue Jays has been the story of Toronto's season since signing in Toronto, carrying a 2.58 ERA with a 66-to-18 strikeout-to-walk ratio across his first 45-plus innings and an MLB-leading strikeout total through the season's first quarter. The swing-and-miss is the calling card, and a 12:15 ET start at the Rogers Centre puts that stuff in front of a home crowd that has watched him anchor the rotation. Mitch Keller counters for Pittsburgh, a durable strike-thrower who eats innings but does not generate Cease's whiff rate, which sets up a clear contrast in styles.

The market reflects the gap: Toronto sits as the home favorite at -168 on the moneyline, with Pittsburgh +139 and the run line at Blue Jays -1.5 (+130). The total opened at 7.5. The under is the cleaner directional read whenever Cease is missing bats, because his strikeout profile limits the kind of traffic that turns into crooked numbers. Both clubs enter at 26-26 and 25-27 respectively, hovering around .500, so the records say this is closer than the price, and Keller's ability to keep the ball on the ground is the structural reason Pittsburgh can hang. The first inning is the watch number, because Cease has the stuff to set an early tone.

Tigers at Orioles (Doubleheader)

12:35 PM & 6:05 PM ET | Camden Yards | Valdez / Melton vs Young / Rogers

A makeup from Saturday created a split doubleheader at Camden Yards. Framber Valdez headlines the opener for Detroit after signing in free agency, one of the best ground-ball pitchers in the sport, a profile that travels well into Baltimore's ballpark by keeping the ball away from the short porches the Orioles' lineup wants to attack. Brandon Young counters in Game 1 for Baltimore at 3-1, a young arm who has held up but does not match Valdez's track record. The Tigers come in scuffling at 20-32 and riding a long losing skid, while Baltimore sits 22-29 and is looking to build on a strong series at home.

The lines tell the doubleheader story. In the opener, Baltimore is the modest favorite at -126 with Detroit +105 and a total of 8.0, close to a pick-em that reflects both the quality of Valdez and Detroit's recent struggles scratching across runs. The nightcap pairs Troy Melton, returning from the injured list to make his 2026 debut for Detroit, against Trevor Rogers for Baltimore, a left-hander who has scuffled to a 2-5 record and a 6.87 ERA. The markets price the second game nearly even (Tigers +100, Orioles -121) on a total of 8.5, a reflection of the uncertainty around a pitcher coming off the injured list and a struggling Rogers. The under is the directional read in the opener whenever Valdez is sinking the ball, while the nightcap is the more volatile game given Melton's rust and Rogers' command issues, with a tired pair of bullpens looming by the late innings.

Guardians at Phillies

1:35 PM ET | Citizens Bank Park | Messick vs Painter

Andrew Painter takes the ball for Philadelphia as the long-awaited rotation centerpiece, and the early returns have been bumpy. The young right-hander is 1-4 with a 6.89 ERA and a 1.71 WHIP across his first six starts, a command profile that has not yet settled into the dominance scouts have long projected. Parker Messick counters for Cleveland, a left-hander the Guardians have leaned on as they sit 31-23 and stay near the top of the AL Central. This is a matchup where the more talented arm on paper is the one still finding his footing.

The market makes Cleveland the road favorite at -131 with Philadelphia +108 and a total of 7.5, a price built largely on Painter's early-season WHIP. The over has appeal if Painter's traffic problems continue, because a deep Cleveland lineup can punish free baserunners before the Phillies' bullpen enters. The under read runs through Painter rediscovering his command and Messick navigating a Philadelphia order that, at 26-26, has been streaky. Watch Painter's first time through the lineup; if the walks pile up early, this opens up in Cleveland's favor.

Twins at Red Sox

1:35 PM ET | Fenway Park | Ober vs Gray

Sonny Gray gives Boston the edge on the mound, carrying a 3.54 ERA into a Fenway Park matinee, a veteran who mixes a deep arsenal and changes speeds rather than overpowering hitters. Bailey Ober counters for Minnesota, a tall right-hander who lives at the top of the zone and relies on deception and command. Boston sits 22-29 and Minnesota 25-27, two clubs hovering below .500 in a game that has more on the line for the standings than the records suggest.

The Red Sox are the home favorite at -157 with Minnesota +130 and a total of 7.5. The under is the directional read with two command-oriented starters who limit hard contact, though Fenway's dimensions always keep the over alive if either pitcher elevates to the Green Monster. Ober's fly-ball tendency is the structural risk at Fenway, where routine fly balls become doubles off the wall, which is the path to the over even on a day with two quality arms. Watch how Gray sequences early, because his ability to steal first-pitch strikes dictates how deep he works.

Rays at Yankees

1:35 PM ET | Yankee Stadium | Rasmussen vs Weathers

The best team in the American League visits the Bronx for a matinee. Drew Rasmussen takes the ball for Tampa Bay, an efficient front-of-rotation arm who has helped the Rays build a 34-15 record and the AL's top mark. Ryan Weathers counters for New York, a serviceable left-hander who will need run support against a Tampa Bay club that has been the most consistent team in the league. The Yankees sit 30-22, the better lineup in the building but the lesser team by the standings.

New York is the home favorite at -136 with Tampa Bay +113 and a total of 7.0. That tight total reflects two starters who can keep games low, while the home-run environment at Yankee Stadium is the swing factor on the over. The short porch in right field favors the left-handed bats in both lineups, which tilts the directional read toward the over if either starter leaves a ball over the plate. The Rays are the better team overall, but the Yankees are home and dangerous, which keeps this near a coin flip despite the 19-game gap in the standings.

Mets at Marlins

1:40 PM ET | loanDepot park | Scott vs Phillips

Christian Scott takes the ball for New York, a young right-hander the Mets have built their near-term rotation hopes around, against Tyler Phillips for Miami, a contact-oriented arm who has bounced between roles. The Mets enter at 22-30, scuffling well below preseason expectations, while the Marlins at 24-29 have played the better recent baseball at home in loanDepot park, a pitcher-friendly environment when the roof is closed.

The market is nearly even, with the Mets slim favorites at -118 and the Marlins +102 on a total of 8.5. That higher number reflects two starters who do not miss bats at an elite rate, which puts balls in play and pressure on both bullpens. The under is alive if the roof is closed and the pitchers locate, while the over has appeal once either starter exits and the relievers take over. Watch the Mets' lineup against Phillips early, because New York's offense has been the team's biggest disappointment and a get-right spot here would change the directional read.

Cardinals at Reds

1:40 PM ET | Great American Ball Park | Mautz vs Singer

Brycen Mautz takes the ball for St. Louis, a young left-hander getting an extended look as the Cardinals stay in the NL Central race at 29-22, against Brady Singer for Cincinnati, a sinker-and-slider right-hander who generates ground balls but can get hit when the ball stays up. The Reds sit 27-25 and play in Great American Ball Park, one of the most homer-friendly venues in baseball, which colors every total in this building.

The line is nearly pick-em on the moneyline, with Cincinnati a slight favorite at -120 and St. Louis -101, and the total sits high at 9.5 for exactly the reason you would expect at GABP. Both lineups swing early, the park rewards fly-ball contact, and Singer's sinker can flatten out in the heat. The over is the structural lean given the venue, while the under requires both starters to keep the ball down and the wind to blow in off the river. Wind direction at first pitch is the single biggest variable on this total.

Dodgers at Brewers

2:10 PM ET | American Family Field | Yamamoto vs Sproat

This is the best matchup of two division leaders on the board, the 32-20 Dodgers visiting the 30-19 Brewers. Yoshinobu Yamamoto takes the ball for Los Angeles working through an uneven stretch; through eight starts he owns a 3-3 record with a 3.60 ERA, a 3.92 FIP and a 1.00 WHIP across 50 innings, and he is coming off an outing in which he allowed a career-worst three home runs to San Francisco. The stuff remains elite, but the home-run suppression has wavered. Brandon Sproat counters for Milwaukee, a young right-hander the Brewers are leaning on as they hold the top spot in the NL Central.

Los Angeles is the road favorite at -175 with Milwaukee +144 and a total of 8.0, with the run line nearly even at Dodgers -1.5 (-104). The over has appeal given Yamamoto's recent home-run trouble and two top-half lineups, while the under is alive if Yamamoto rediscovers the command that made him a postseason force last fall. The watch number is Yamamoto's first time through the Milwaukee order; if the splitter is landing and he keeps the ball in the yard, this is a different game than his recent line suggests. American Family Field with the roof open plays bigger than its reputation, so the roof status is a swing factor on the total.

Mariners at Royals

2:10 PM ET | Kauffman Stadium | Woo vs Lugo

Bryan Woo gives Seattle the edge on the mound, the right-hander who has emerged as the ace of the Mariners staff with a sub-3.00 ERA and elite command that keeps his walk rate low and his innings deep. Seth Lugo counters for Kansas City, a veteran with one of the deepest arsenals in the league who pitches to contact and relies on sequencing rather than power. Seattle sits 25-28 and Kansas City 21-31, with the Mariners the more complete club entering this one.

Seattle is the road favorite at -137 with Kansas City +114 and a total of 8.5. Kauffman Stadium is a spacious, pitcher-friendly park, which keeps the under in play whenever Woo is locating, and his command profile produces strong early innings that make the no-run-first-inning angle worth watching. The path to the over runs through Lugo's contact approach getting punished by Seattle's patient lineup, which can grind a starter into the Kansas City bullpen by the sixth. The Royals' relief corps has been the soft underbelly of the roster, so the middle innings are where this total can tilt up.

Astros at Cubs

2:20 PM ET | Wrigley Field | Lambert vs Imanaga

Shota Imanaga has been one of the best stories in the National League for the Cubs, though his ERA jumped to 3.38 after the Brewers tagged him for eight earned runs in his last outing. The left-hander still misses bats and limits walks, and a bounce-back at Wrigley is the kind of spot he has thrived in this season. Peter Lambert counters for Houston, a right-hander filling rotation innings for an Astros club that has scuffled to 22-31 and badly needs starting pitching. Chicago, at 29-23, has used a strong home record to stay in the NL Central mix.

The Cubs are the home favorite at -179 with Houston +147 and a total of 7.0. The wind at Wrigley is the swing variable on the total, as it always is; blowing out to right opens the over, blowing in tightens both contact profiles and points under. The under read leans on Imanaga rebounding and Lambert keeping the ball on the ground, while the over needs the Houston lineup to get to Imanaga early before he settles in. Watch the flags on the foul poles at first pitch, because that tells you more about this total than either starter does.

White Sox at Giants

4:05 PM ET | Oracle Park | Schultz vs Ray

Robbie Ray gives San Francisco the edge on the mound, the veteran left-hander carrying a 2.76 ERA into a matchup at Oracle Park, where the marine layer and deep dimensions suit his fly-ball tendencies. Noah Schultz takes the ball for Chicago, a highly regarded young left-hander getting a look for a White Sox club that has continued to find wins hard to come by. San Francisco sits 21-31, scuffling more than expected, while Chicago at 26-25 has been the more surprising club to this point in the season.

The market is essentially even, with the Giants -112 and the White Sox -107 on a total of 8.0, a reflection of two clubs that have underwhelmed and a venue that suppresses scoring. The under is the cleaner read at Oracle Park, where the marine layer cuts down on home runs after sunset and Ray's fly balls die at the warning track. The over needs Schultz's youth to show in the form of early traffic, because his command will be tested against a Giants lineup that grinds at-bats. The wind off the bay at first pitch is the swing factor, as it is in every game in this park.

Nationals at Braves

4:10 PM ET | Truist Park | Griffin vs Perez

Atlanta owns the best record in the National League at 36-17, and the Braves send Martin Perez to the mound, a veteran left-hander who pitches to contact and leans on a deep Atlanta defense behind him. Foster Griffin counters for Washington, a journeyman left-hander filling innings for a Nationals club sitting at 26-27, hovering around .500 but overmatched against the class of the division on the road. The talent and lineup gap is significant in this spot.

The Braves are a heavy home favorite at -170 with Washington +140 and a total of 8.5, which puts the run line at Atlanta -1.5 (+124) in play as the alternative to the steep moneyline. The over has appeal given Griffin's contact profile in a hitter-friendly Truist Park, where the Braves' lineup punishes mistakes. The under read leans on Perez working efficiently and the game staying tight, which is the less likely outcome given Atlanta's offensive firepower. Watch the first inning; if the Braves get to Griffin early, this becomes a comfortable home win that the bullpen closes out.

Athletics at Padres

4:10 PM ET | Petco Park | Medina vs King

Michael King anchors the San Diego staff, carrying a 2.31 ERA into Petco Park, one of the most pitcher-friendly venues in baseball and a perfect match for his command-and-mix approach. Luis Medina counters for the Athletics, a hard-throwing right-hander whose stuff is loud but whose command has been inconsistent. San Diego sits 31-20, right at the top of the NL West alongside the Dodgers, while the Athletics at 26-26 have hung around .500 and play a scrappier brand than their roster suggests.

The Padres are the home favorite at -175 with the Athletics +144 and a total of 8.0, with the run line at San Diego -1.5 (+124). The under is the directional read given King on the mound and Petco's run-suppressing conditions, a combination that has produced low-scoring games all season. The over needs Medina's command to wobble into walks and the Athletics' lineup to capitalize before the Padres' bullpen takes over. Watch King's efficiency early, because when he is locating he works deep and hands a lead to one of the better late-inning relief groups in the league.

Rockies at Diamondbacks

4:10 PM ET | Chase Field | Quintana vs Nelson

Jose Quintana takes the ball for Colorado, a crafty veteran left-hander whose command and changeup have kept him useful long past his peak velocity, against Ryne Nelson for Arizona, a right-hander who fills the zone and relies on his defense. The Rockies at 20-33 have been one of the league's weaker clubs, while Arizona at 27-24 hangs around the .500 mark and can do real damage in the thin air of Chase Field.

Arizona is a clear home favorite at -205 with Colorado +168 and a high total of 9.5, with the run line at Diamondbacks -1.5 (+101). The roof at Chase Field is the swing factor on the total; an open roof with warm desert air plays to the over, while a closed roof favors a more controlled game. The over read leans on the Arizona lineup getting to Quintana and the Rockies' offense, which has been potent when healthy, putting up runs of its own. The under requires both veterans to navigate cleanly, which is the tougher ask given the talent gap and the venue. Arizona is the side on talent and lineup depth.

Rangers at Angels

7:20 PM ET | Angel Stadium | Gore vs Detmers

The nightcap of the slate pairs two left-handers with swing-and-miss stuff. MacKenzie Gore takes the ball for Texas carrying a 3.38 ERA, a power lefty whose strikeout rate ranks among the best in the league and who gives the Rangers a legitimate front-of-rotation arm. Reid Detmers counters for the Angels, a left-hander with a high-spin breaking ball who can dominate when he is locating but has battled consistency. Texas sits 24-27 and the Angels 19-34, the latter the weakest record in the AL West.

The line is tight despite the standings gap, with the Angels a slight home favorite at -126 and Texas +105 on a total of 8.0. That close price reflects Detmers' upside at home against a Rangers lineup that has been inconsistent. The under is the directional read with two strikeout lefties on the mound who can keep traffic off the bases, while the over needs either pitcher's command to slip into hittable counts. The late West Coast slot makes this the one to watch for live overs if the day's earlier scoring runs light. Watch how deep Gore works, because his pitch efficiency dictates whether Texas sees the Angels' bullpen early.
