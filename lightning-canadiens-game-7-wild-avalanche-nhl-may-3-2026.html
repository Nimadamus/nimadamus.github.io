TNT

Canadiens @ Lightning

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Amalie Arena, Tampa, FL

The first Game 7 of the 2026 Stanley Cup playoffs goes Sunday at Amalie Arena, the kind of winner-take-all opening act that has defined the early-bracket drama. Tampa Bay is a minus-156 home favorite on the moneyline with the total at 5.5 and the under priced at minus-135. Montreal is plus-130 on the road with the puck-line plus-1.5 priced at minus-185 for bettors who want the Habs but don't want to sweat a one-goal loss. The opening Game 7 number had Tampa at minus-166 and Montreal at plus-140, and the late-week steam has shifted slightly toward the Canadiens side with the spread tightening across major books. The 5.5 total reflects the structural identity of the series - Tampa Bay and Montreal have combined for the under in every meeting outside of Game 1, the kind of low-event tight-checking pattern that defines a deep-bracket Game 7.

Brandon Hagel has found the scoresheet in five of six contests as Tampa Bay's structural goal-scoring trigger and co-leads all skaters in the series with eight points. He also ranks second on Tampa Bay in ice time per game at 25:21, the kind of high-leverage two-way profile that has made him a staple of Jon Cooper's deployment patterns. The Tampa Bay defensive structure has been the foundation of the series - the Lightning's neutral-zone trap and high-press forecheck have produced the kind of low-scoring environment that keeps the under priced as a juiced favorite, and Andrei Vasilevskiy in goal has been the structural anchor across the six games. The Canadiens' road profile in this series has been competitive, but the Tampa Bay home environment in a Game 7 carries a structural premium that the minus-156 reflects.

Montreal's path to Game 7 has been the kind of underdog story that defines a young roster's first deep-playoff run. Cole Caufield, Nick Suzuki, and the Canadiens' top-six forward group have produced enough offensive variance to win three games against a championship-caliber Tampa Bay team, and the structural read on the road Game 7 is the variance environment of a single-night winner-take-all. Sam Montembeault in goal has been the leverage piece - his Round 1 save percentage and his ability to absorb the Tampa Bay shot-volume at Amalie Arena defines the upset chance. The series-price market has Tampa Bay heavily favored to advance, and the second-round opponent is already locked - the Buffalo Sabres are waiting for the winner of this series, the kind of well-rested second-round opponent that creates the structural urgency for a Tampa Bay closeout. The 6 PM ET tip on TNT is the early-Sunday anchor of the NHL slate.

Round 2 Game 1 - Western Conference
TNT / HBO MAX / CBC

Wild @ Avalanche

Sunday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO

The first second-round Game 1 of the 2026 Stanley Cup playoffs is at Ball Arena Sunday night, the kind of fresh-bracket opener that shifts the postseason narrative from the Round 1 grind to the next tactical battle. Colorado is a minus-178 home favorite on the moneyline with FanDuel and minus-185 at other major books, with Minnesota plus-154 on the road and plus-170 at the heaviest underdog books. The total is set at 5.5. The series price market has Colorado favored at minus-205 to advance and Minnesota plus-170 in the upset bid - a 9 PM ET tip on TNT, HBO MAX, SN, SN360, and CBC opens the second round of the West bracket and sets up an Avalanche-Wild matchup that the Northwest Division has been pointing toward for years.

Colorado's path through Round 1 was the kind of dominant sweep that telegraphs a deep run. The Avalanche swept the Los Angeles Kings in four straight games and have been sitting with their feet up for nine days while the Wild were grinding through a tough six-game series with the Dallas Stars. That rest-and-prep gap is the structural variable for the Game 1 spread - Colorado's home-ice cushion at Ball Arena, the team's regular-season offensive profile led by Nathan MacKinnon and Cale Makar, and the well-rested defensive structure under Jared Bednar all give the home side the structural edge. The 5-on-5 expected goals model from the regular season ranked Colorado near the top of the league, and the home-rink advantage at altitude is one of the most measurable structural premiums in playoff hockey.

Minnesota's six-game series win over Dallas was the harder road, and the Wild come into Round 2 with the kind of grind-game identity that head coach John Hynes has built across the season. Kirill Kaprizov has been the structural offensive anchor and Filip Gustavsson in goal has produced the kind of save-percentage profile that anchors the upset chance. The structural read on the Wild road profile is the question of whether the team can absorb the rest-disadvantage and the altitude environment at Ball Arena across the 60-minute window. The puck-line market has Wild plus-1.5 priced at a juiced favorite and the moneyline plus-154 reflects the meaningful gap that Vegas has priced in. Projections call for a final result of Avalanche 4, Wild 2 in the early models, but the Game 1 home-favorite read is the structural piece of the Sunday market.
