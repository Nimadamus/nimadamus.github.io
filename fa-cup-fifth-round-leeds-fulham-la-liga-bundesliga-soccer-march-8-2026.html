Draw

The first FA Cup fifth round match of the day sends Southampton to Craven Cottage to face Fulham in what should be a fascinating contest between two Premier League sides with very different ambitions this season. Fulham are the favorites at around -117 on the moneyline (1.54 decimal), and Marco Silva's side will be desperate to make a deep cup run after securing their fourth-round passage with a 2-1 win at Stoke City. The Cottagers have been solid at home this season, and Craven Cottage's intimate atmosphere creates a unique challenge for visiting teams trying to impose themselves in knockout football.

Southampton arrive having edged past Leicester in extra time in the fourth round, demonstrating the kind of resilience and grit that's defined their season. The Saints' attacking form has been impressive in recent weeks, and they'll see this as a genuine opportunity to reach the quarterfinals of a competition that represents their most realistic path to European football. The head-to-head between these two sides has been remarkably balanced historically, though Fulham are unbeaten in their last four meetings. Southampton's +300 moneyline reflects the away disadvantage, but there's genuine value there if the Saints can bring their best attacking game to West London.

Fulham's defensive inconsistencies have been a recurring theme this season, and that gives Southampton reason for optimism. The Cottagers are capable of dominating possession and creating chances, but they've also shown a tendency to switch off at the back, particularly in the second half of tight matches. Southampton will look to exploit those moments of vulnerability, pressing high and forcing turnovers in dangerous areas. With a quarterfinal spot on the line and both sides capable of producing quality football, this has the ingredients for an entertaining cup tie that could go either way despite Fulham's status as home favorites.

If you want a feel-good FA Cup story, look no further than Port Vale's remarkable run to the fifth round. The Valiants sit bottom of League One, rock bottom of the third tier of English football, yet here they are, hosting Sunderland for a place in the quarterfinals. Port Vale beat Bristol City in extra time in the fourth round to extend their fairytale run, and Vale Park will be absolutely bouncing for the biggest match at the ground in years. The magic of the FA Cup is alive and well in Burslem, and Port Vale's players will be running on adrenaline and emotion that no amount of tactical preparation can replicate.

Sunderland are the clear favorites at -175 on the moneyline, and rightfully so. The Black Cats reached the fifth round with victories over Everton on penalties and a 1-0 win over Oxford United, and this represents their deepest FA Cup run in over a decade. Sunderland's squad is built for promotion from the Championship, and the quality gap between a League One basement club and a Championship promotion contender is substantial. But the FA Cup has a long history of producing exactly this kind of upset, and Sunderland will need to match Port Vale's intensity from the first whistle or risk becoming the latest victims of cup magic.

Port Vale's approach will be simple: make Vale Park a fortress, fight for every ball, and create moments of chaos that Sunderland aren't prepared for. The compact pitch and hostile atmosphere will neutralize some of Sunderland's technical superiority, and if the Valiants can keep the match tight into the second half, the pressure will shift entirely onto the away side. Sunderland's quality should eventually tell, but don't be surprised if Port Vale give them the fight of their lives in front of what will be one of the most emotionally charged crowds in the entire FA Cup this season.

The final FA Cup fifth round match on Sunday pits Leeds United against Norwich City at Elland Road, and this is a fascinating match on multiple levels. Leeds are the favorites at -150 on the moneyline (1.46 decimal), but there's genuine concern around Daniel Farke's side right now. The Whites have lost their last two Premier League home games without scoring, and they sit just six points above the relegation zone in what has been a far more difficult return to the top flight than anyone anticipated. Leeds beat Derby County and Birmingham City to reach this stage, but their recent home form suggests they're vulnerable.

Norwich arrive at Elland Road in surprisingly good form, having won five of their last six matches across all competitions. The Canaries have been punching above their weight in the cup, and they'll fancy their chances against a Leeds side that's clearly lacking confidence at home. However, Norwich carry a heavily depleted squad, and the physical demands of competing on multiple fronts have taken their toll. The injury situation could be the decisive factor, as Norwich may struggle to maintain their recent intensity over 90 minutes at a raucous Elland Road.

This match feels like a genuine 50-50 despite Leeds' home advantage and the odds suggesting otherwise. Leeds need a positive result to build momentum heading into the crucial relegation run-in, and a quarterfinal berth would provide a massive confidence boost for a squad that's been short on self-belief in recent weeks. Norwich's form is undeniable, but away from home against a desperate Leeds side, the Canaries will need to be at their absolute best. The atmosphere at Elland Road will be intense, and Leeds' fans will demand a performance from a team that owes them one after consecutive goalless home defeats in the league.

Villarreal host Elche at the Estadio de la Ceramica in La Liga Matchday 27, and the Yellow Submarine are the clear favorites at approximately -155 on the moneyline (1.65 decimal). Villarreal's season has been a familiar one under their coaching staff, with the club positioning themselves as one of the most consistent mid-to-upper table sides in Spanish football. Their home record at the Ceramica has been strong, and they'll be looking to maintain their push for European qualification with three points against a promoted Elche side.

Elche's return to La Liga has been a survival battle from the start, and every point on the road is precious at this stage of the season. The Ilicitanos will set up to be difficult to break down, compact and disciplined defensively, and try to hit Villarreal on the counter when opportunities present themselves. Villarreal's offensive quality should be the difference maker at home, but Elche's fighting spirit and organization make them more dangerous than their table position suggests. The +400 moneyline on Elche is the longest shot on the La Liga Sunday slate, and while a result would be a surprise, stranger things have happened in Spanish football.

Villarreal's midfield creativity and attacking depth should ultimately prove too much for Elche to contain over 90 minutes. The Yellow Submarine's ability to control possession and create chances from multiple angles gives them a structural advantage that Elche will struggle to negate, particularly as the match progresses and the visitors' energy levels drop. Villarreal should secure all three points at home, but the margin might be tighter than the odds suggest.

Getafe welcome Real Betis to the Coliseum Alfonso Perez in a La Liga Matchday 27 clash that pits two mid-table sides with contrasting styles against each other. Getafe's brand of football remains one of the most physical and defensively organized in La Liga, and they thrive on making matches ugly, disrupting opponents' rhythm, and capitalizing on set pieces and transitions.

Real Betis arrive with their own ambitions of finishing in the European places, and Manuel Pellegrini's side is capable of playing attractive, possession-based football that can unlock even the most well-organized defenses. The Verdiblancos' attacking talent gives them the tools to compete anywhere in Spain, but the Coliseum is one of the toughest away grounds in La Liga specifically because Getafe's style is designed to neutralize technical superiority. Betis will need to maintain their composure and avoid getting dragged into a physical battle that plays directly into Getafe's hands.

This match could go either way, and the relatively tight odds reflect the uncertainty. Getafe's home advantage and physical approach give them a slight edge, but Betis' quality on the ball means they're always capable of producing a moment of brilliance that changes the match. The +250 draw price is interesting given that Getafe matches frequently produce tight, low-scoring affairs, and a 1-1 result wouldn't surprise anyone who has watched either of these teams regularly this season.

Dino Toppmoller's side has been one of the more exciting teams in the Bundesliga this season, with their counter-attacking style producing some spectacular results.

St. Pauli's return to the Bundesliga has been about survival, and every home match is an opportunity to pick up the points they need to stay in the top flight. The Kiezkicker's defensive organization and work rate have been their calling cards, and they'll look to make the Millerntor as hostile as possible for Frankfurt. The tight odds suggest this could be one of the closer matches on the Bundesliga slate, and the draw at +260 is a viable outcome given both teams' tendency to play tight, physical matches against each other.

Union Berlin welcome Wolfsburg to the Stadion An der Alten Forsterei for a Bundesliga Matchday 25 fixture that carries significant implications for both clubs' seasons. Union's iconic stadium in Kopenick is one of the most unique grounds in European football, and the fervent atmosphere created by their standing terraces gives them a genuine home advantage that goes beyond the usual metrics. The odds suggest this is essentially a pick'em match, with Union at +140 and Wolfsburg at +170, and the tight pricing reflects the difficulty of separating these two sides.

Wolfsburg's season has been one of inconsistency, as the Wolves have alternated between impressive performances against top sides and frustrating results against teams they should beat. Their squad depth and financial resources give them the theoretical edge, but translating that into consistent results has been the challenge all season. Away from the Volkswagen Arena, Wolfsburg have been particularly vulnerable, and the trip to Union's atmospheric home ground is the kind of fixture that can derail any team's momentum.

Union Berlin's identity is built on collective effort, defensive discipline, and making their home ground an absolute fortress. The Eisernen won't win any beauty contests, but they're incredibly difficult to beat at the Alte Forsterei, and their ability to grind out results through sheer willpower and organization is something that Wolfsburg will have to match. This has the makings of a tight, physical encounter that's decided by a single goal or a set-piece moment. The +240 draw reflects the genuine possibility that neither side finds a breakthrough, and that wouldn't surprise anyone familiar with the Bundesliga's mid-table dynamics.

Toronto FC's road trip continues as they visit FC Cincinnati at TQL Stadium in an early-season MLS clash. TQL Stadium has developed into one of the toughest away grounds in MLS, and Cincinnati's passionate fanbase creates an atmosphere that can overwhelm visiting teams, particularly early in the season when rosters are still finding their rhythm.

Toronto FC begin their 2026 campaign with an initial three-game road stretch before their home opener on March 14 against Red Bull New York, and starting the season with this kind of challenging away fixture is a stern test of their preseason preparation. The Reds have retooled their roster and will be eager to prove they can compete with the conference's best, but building chemistry away from home is always difficult, particularly against a Cincinnati side that's had time to gel and build on their recent success.

Cincinnati's combination of attacking quality and home-field advantage makes them the rightful favorites, but early-season MLS matches can produce unpredictable results as teams work through new signings, tactical adjustments, and fitness levels. Toronto's +280 moneyline reflects the challenge they face, but the beauty of early-season MLS is that form from previous campaigns means very little once the new season kicks off. Both sides will be motivated to start strong, and this should produce an entertaining contest at one of MLS's best venues.
