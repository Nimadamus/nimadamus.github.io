Bournemouth vs Brentford

Tuesday, 2:30 PM ET | Vitality Stadium | Premier League
Bournemouth ML
+145
Draw

Bournemouth welcome Brentford to the Vitality Stadium riding an eight-match unbeaten run across all competitions, and this is a fascinating clash between two sides that play open, entertaining football that rarely produces dull results. The Cherries sit 10th with 39 points (9W 12D 7L), and while those 12 draws tell you they've struggled to put teams away, the unbeaten streak suggests something is clicking defensively even if the finishing hasn't been clinical. Brentford arrive in 7th with 43 points (13W 4D 11L), a wildly inconsistent side that either wins or loses with very little in between. That four-draw record across 28 matches is genuinely remarkable for how binary their results have been.

The injury situation is significant on both sides. Bournemouth will be without Justin Kluivert (knee), Ben Doak (thigh), Julio Soler (hamstring), and Lewis Cook (hamstring), while Evanilson is listed as doubtful. That's a lot of attacking and creative talent missing from the home side's lineup. Brentford aren't exactly in great shape either, with Aaron Hickey, Vitaly Janelt, Fabio Carvalho (ACL), and Antoni Milambo (ACL) all sidelined. The Bees have leaned heavily on Igor Thiago, who's been sensational with 18 goals in 27 appearances this season, and his presence gives Brentford a genuine focal point that most mid-table sides simply can't match.

The head-to-head numbers scream Brentford here. They've won nine of the last 12 meetings between these two clubs, and that kind of psychological dominance doesn't just evaporate overnight. Even at the Vitality Stadium, where Bournemouth have been respectable, Brentford seem to have the Cherries' number. The moneyline tells an interesting story with both teams priced as slight underdogs (BOU +145, BRE +170 with the draw at +265), suggesting the market sees this as a genuine coin-flip with the draw being a very live outcome. The over/under 2.5 goals line is juiced heavily toward the over at -167, and with both teams committed to attacking football and BTTS priced at -208, the market is pricing in goals from both ends.

Form is where the contrast gets interesting. Bournemouth's recent run reads D-D-W-D-W, which is solid but unspectacular, full of those draws that define their season. Brentford's form reads W-L-L-L-W, and that pattern of winning, then losing three straight, then winning again captures everything you need to know about this side's maddening inconsistency. When Brentford show up, they're capable of beating anyone. When they don't, they lose to sides they have no business losing to. The question Tuesday is which version of the Bees walks through the tunnel at the Vitality Stadium.

On paper, this looks like a straightforward home banker for Everton. The Toffees sit 8th with 40 points (11W 7D 10L), Burnley are stuck down in 19th with just 19 points (4W 7D 17L) and sitting 8 points adrift of safety, and the moneyline reflects that gap with Everton priced at -182. But here's where things get weird, and this is exactly the kind of match that trips people up. Everton's home record is genuinely terrible: 4W 4D 6L at the Hill Dickinson Stadium this season. They're on a six-match home winless streak. That's not a blip. That's a pattern, and it should make anyone nervous about laying -182 on the Toffees in front of their own supporters.

The paradox of David Moyes' Everton side is that they've been significantly better on the road (7W 3D 4L) than at home, which is almost unheard of in the Premier League. Something about playing at the Hill Dickinson Stadium has been toxic for this team, whether it's the pressure of performing in front of their own fans or tactical issues that become more pronounced when they're expected to take the initiative. Regardless of the reason, you can't ignore a six-match home winless run when you're being asked to lay -182 on them. That's a disconnect between the market's perception of Everton and their actual home form that creates uncertainty.

Burnley's situation is dire but not entirely hopeless. They've failed to win 17 of their last 18 matches, and at some point you have to wonder whether the sheer weight of that run starts to become self-fulfilling. With seven senior players injured, including Cullen, Roberts, Broja, and Beyer, they're putting out a squad that's essentially held together with string and hope. Their 32 goals for and 56 goals against tells you everything about a side that's been competitive in patches but simply doesn't have the defensive structure to keep results coming consistently. The 2.5 total is priced at Over -109 / Under -114, essentially a pick'em, and BTTS at -106 suggests the books see both teams finding the net.

Here's the uncomfortable truth for Everton backers. Burnley might be a historically bad Premier League side this season, but the Toffees have been historically bad at home. When an unstoppable force of away mediocrity meets an immovable object of home futility, the result is often closer than anyone wants it to be. Everton should win this match on talent alone, and the -182 price says the market agrees. But that home record is a flashing neon warning sign that this could easily end as another frustrating draw for the Goodison faithful, or whatever they're calling the ground these days.

Two promoted sides who are having very different seasons meet at Elland Road, and both clubs are in desperate need of a result for completely different reasons. Leeds sit 15th with 31 points (7W 10D 11L), uncomfortably close to the relegation conversation despite the optimism that surrounded their return to the top flight. Sunderland occupy 12th with 37 points (9W 10D 9L), a more stable position but one that their supporters would consider underachieving given the quality in their squad. Neither side has been in this division since 2016-17, and the growing pains of Premier League football are evident in the inconsistency that defines both campaigns.

Leeds' recent form is alarming. They've gone 0W 2D 3L in their last five matches, and that kind of slide is how clubs tumble from mid-table obscurity into genuine relegation danger before they realize what's happening. The good news is that Pascal Struijk and Dan James are back in training, which should provide a boost in both defensive solidity and attacking dynamism. Noah Okafor is out with a hamstring issue, but the returning players should offset that loss. The bigger concern for Leeds is whether the confidence has drained out of this squad during the recent winless run, because Elland Road needs to be a fortress if they're going to stay up, and right now it doesn't feel like one.

Sunderland arrive with their own problems. Six players are sidelined, including Cirkin, Mandava, Mundle, Brobbey, Mukiele, and Ta Bi, which is a significant chunk of first-team talent unavailable for a crucial away fixture. Their form reads D-L-L-L-W, which is almost as ugly as Leeds', though they at least snapped a three-match losing streak with their most recent victory. The Black Cats have been remarkably tight defensively, conceding just 34 goals in 28 matches, but their attacking output of 29 goals is among the lowest in the top half of the table. They're built to grind out results rather than blow teams away, and that approach tends to produce a lot of low-scoring affairs.

The head-to-head record is fascinating. These two have drawn four of their last six meetings, which tells you just how evenly matched they've been across recent encounters. The under 2.5 goals is favored at -137 (over at +112), and given that Sunderland's defensive identity and Leeds' recent inability to score consistently both point toward a tight affair, the low-scoring outcome feels like the most probable scenario. Leeds are slight favorites at -119, which feels about right for a home side that's been poor recently but should get a boost from the returning players. The draw at +240 is very much alive based on how often these two have split the points against each other.

The marquee fixture of Tuesday's Premier League slate sends Liverpool to Molineux to face bottom-of-the-table Wolverhampton, and this is the first of two meetings between these sides at this ground this week, with an FA Cup tie following shortly after. Arne Slot's Liverpool sit 5th with 48 points (14W 6D 8L) and are firmly in the hunt for a top-four finish, while Wolves are rooted to the bottom with a miserable 2W 7D 19L record and just 13 points to show for their season. On paper, this couldn't be more one-sided, and the -213 moneyline on Liverpool reflects the enormous gap in quality between these two squads. But Premier League football doesn't always care about what paper says.

Here's the thing about Wolves that the table doesn't fully capture. Under Rob Edwards, this team has shown genuine signs of improvement in recent weeks. They beat Aston Villa 2-0 and drew Arsenal 2-2, two results that suggest this isn't a side that's simply rolled over and accepted their fate. Yes, the record is horrific and they're almost certainly going down, but there's a difference between a team that's already mentally checked out and one that's fighting for every point with nothing to lose. Wolves fall into the latter category right now, and that kind of desperation at home, in front of a Molineux crowd that will be electric for a visit from Liverpool, can produce results that the market doesn't see coming.

Liverpool's injury situation adds intrigue to what would otherwise be a routine away day. Florian Wirtz is out with a back problem, and Alexander Isak remains sidelined following ankle surgery with a return not expected until late March. Jeremie Frimpong is back in contention, which helps on the right side, but missing Wirtz's creativity and Isak's goal threat reduces Liverpool's attacking ceiling significantly. Hugo Ekitike has been carrying the scoring load with 11 league goals this season, and the Reds will need him to be sharp at Molineux because Wolves have proven they can make life uncomfortable for top sides when they defend with structure and intensity.

Liverpool have won the last five head-to-head meetings against Wolves, so the historical dominance is clear and convincing. Their recent form of W-W-W-W-L was near-perfect until their most recent setback, and the overall quality of this squad means they should be expected to win this match more often than not. The over 2.5 goals is juiced at -172 (under at +135), and BTTS at -141 tells you the market expects both teams to find the net. Given Wolves' improved attacking play in recent weeks and Liverpool's missing defensive and midfield pieces, there's logic behind that pricing. This feels like a 2-1 or 3-1 type of match where Liverpool ultimately get the job done but Wolves make them work for every minute of it. The +550 on a Wolves result feels extreme given their recent form, though Liverpool's squad depth and big-game experience should prevail.
