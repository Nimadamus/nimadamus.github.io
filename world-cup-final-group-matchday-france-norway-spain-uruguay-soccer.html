Group I

France vs Norway

Stage
Group I
Kickoff
3:00 PM ET
Venue
Gillette Stadium

The marquee fixture of the final matchday pits two of the strongest sides in Group I against each other at Gillette Stadium, with first place very much on the line. France brings the depth, tournament pedigree, and individual quality that make it a perennial favorite to go deep, a side comfortable controlling possession and striking through pace and movement in the final third. Both teams arrived at the closing day in strong position after the opening two rounds, which raises the stakes on a meeting that could decide who tops the group.

Norway counters with the physical, direct, and increasingly confident profile that has made it one of the more dangerous emerging sides on the international stage, capable of hurting any defense in transition and from set pieces. The midfield battle is the key area, with France looking to dictate tempo and Norway intent on disrupting that rhythm and turning the game into a contest of direct play. With seeding for the knockout rounds at stake, this is the heavyweight occasion of the day and the match that shapes the top of the group.

How each side manages the balance between attack and defensive shape will define the night, because a single goal can swing the final standings for both teams.

The other Group I fixture runs simultaneously at BMO Field, where Senegal and Iraq close out their group campaigns. On the final matchday, the Senegalese will be intent on finishing their group on a positive note and building momentum heading toward the knockout rounds.

Iraq represents a disciplined, organized style built on defensive structure and a willingness to stay compact and frustrate more fancied opposition. The Iraqis are a difficult side to break down when they sit deep and protect their shape, and they will look to spring forward on the counter when the chance arrives. With both Group I matches kicking off at the same time, the result in Toronto is tied directly to events in Foxborough, adding a layer of scoreboard-watching tension to the afternoon.

Group H reaches its conclusion in the evening window, and the headline meeting sends Spain against Uruguay at Estadio Akron in Mexico. Spain brings the possession-based, technically refined style that has defined its golden generations, a side that dominates the ball, moves opponents side to side, and looks to break down defenses through patience and precision. Having looked among the sharper teams in the group through two rounds, the Spanish will aim to secure top spot with a controlled performance.

Uruguay counters with the competitive, physical, and tactically streetwise identity that has long made it one of South America's most respected national teams. The Uruguayans defend with intensity, compete for every second ball, and carry a genuine threat in the final third, which makes them a dangerous final-day opponent for any side. With qualification scenarios still in play, Uruguay will treat this as a match it needs to control, setting up a compelling clash of contrasting football cultures. Because the two Group H matches kick off together, both Spain and Uruguay will be tracking events in Houston in real time, and a late goal in either fixture could reshuffle the entire group. That simultaneous format rewards teams that stay composed and avoid the defensive lapse that turns a manageable afternoon into a nervous one.
