Wolves @ Bulldogs

2025-12-22T19:00Z | Stegeman Coliseum | SECN+
WGA: 6-6 | UGA: 11-1

This is the definition of a buy game for Georgia. The Bulldogs are hosting their in-state D2 program to tune up before SEC play begins, and West Georgia is just looking to get out of Athens in one piece. At 11-1, Georgia has been impressive this season under Mike White, with a balanced attack that can beat you inside and out.

West Georgia is 6-6 against their own level of competition, but the jump to SEC basketball is massive. The Wolves simply don't have the athletes to hang with Georgia's length and depth. Expect the Bulldogs to run them off the floor in transition and control the glass completely.

This game exists so Georgia can get everyone minutes and stay sharp during the holiday break. The starters probably play 20 minutes max, and the Bulldogs cruise to an easy double-digit win. Not much betting value here - it's just a glorified practice session for UGA.

Eagles @ Cavaliers

2025-12-22T23:00Z | John Paul Jones Arena | ACC Network
AMER: 7-6 | UVA: 11-1

Virginia's pack-line defense continues to suffocate opponents, and American University is walking into a buzzsaw. The Cavaliers are 11-1 and playing elite basketball under Tony Bennett, holding teams to under 55 points per game on average. That's a nightmare matchup for a mid-major team that relies on half-court execution.

American comes in at 7-6 and has shown some fight in the Patriot League, but they don't have the size or depth to compete with Virginia's length. The Cavs will clog passing lanes, force tough twos, and make the Eagles work for every single point. It's going to be ugly.

If you've watched Virginia play over the years, you know the deal. They slow the game to a crawl, execute in the half-court, and grind you down. American will hang around early because of the pace, but UVA pulls away in the second half when fatigue sets in and the Eagles' shots stop falling.

Pirates @ Heels

2025-12-23T01:00Z | Dean E. Smith Center | ACC Network
ECU: 5-7 | UNC: 11-1

The Tar Heels host their in-state AAC rival in what should be a comfortable holiday tune-up. North Carolina is 11-1 and has looked like a legitimate Final Four contender this season. RJ Davis is playing the best basketball of his career, and the supporting cast has stepped up big time.

East Carolina is struggling at 5-7 and simply doesn't have the horses to compete with UNC's talent. The Pirates play hard, but they're going to get outclassed physically on both ends of the floor. The Tar Heels' transition game should feast against ECU's limited depth.

Carolina wants to get in and out of this one healthy with ACC play looming. Expect Hubert Davis to play his starters enough to stay sharp, then empty the bench in the second half. The Dean Dome crowd gets an easy win, and the Heels cruise into conference play with momentum.

Wildcats @ Jayhawks

2025-12-23T01:00Z | Allen Fieldhouse | ESPN+
DAV: 8-3 | KU: 9-3

This is an intriguing non-conference matchup. Davidson is a quality mid-major at 8-3 with a history of pulling off upsets - they're not afraid of the big stage. The Wildcats play smart, disciplined basketball and have shooters who can get hot from deep. That's dangerous in Allen Fieldhouse.

Kansas, though, has been finding their footing at 9-3 and playing better as the season progresses. The Jayhawks' talent advantage is clear, but they've shown some vulnerability against well-coached teams that can control tempo and execute in the half-court. That's exactly what Davidson does.

Here's the thing - Allen Fieldhouse is one of the toughest environments in college basketball, and Kansas has an insane home winning streak to protect. Davidson will compete, but the Jayhawks' athleticism and depth should wear them down over 40 minutes. Expect a competitive first half before KU pulls away late.

Tigers @ Illini

2025-12-23T01:00Z | Enterprise Center | FS1
MIZ: 10-2 | ILL: 8-3

The Braggin' Rights game is one of the best rivalry matchups in college basketball, and this year it's at the Enterprise Center in St. Louis. Missouri comes in at 10-2 and has been one of the surprises of the season under Dennis Gates. The Tigers are athletic, deep, and playing with confidence.

Illinois is 8-3 and has the talent to compete with anyone in the Big Ten. The Illini have had some inconsistent moments, but when they're locked in defensively, they're a tough out. This game always brings extra intensity regardless of records.

Neither team will want to lose bragging rights heading into conference play. Expect a physical, emotional game with the split crowd creating an electric atmosphere. Missouri's been the better team so far this year, but Illinois has the individual talent to steal this one. This is a coin-flip game that could go either way.

Wildcats @ Wildcats

2025-12-23T02:00Z | McKale Memorial Center | ESPN+
Line: ARIZ -39.5 | O/U: 154.5 | BCU: 3-8 | ARIZ: 11-0

Arizona is PERFECT at 11-0 and has looked like a legit national title contender. The Wildcats are deep, talented, and playing with an edge under Tommy Lloyd. The -39.5 spread tells you everything you need to know about this mismatch. Bethune-Cookman is walking into McKale Center as a sacrificial lamb.

At 3-8, the SWAC's Bethune-Cookman has struggled against their own level of competition. The jump to a top-5 team like Arizona is astronomical. They don't have the athletes, the depth, or the defensive capability to slow down Arizona's high-powered offense.

This is about Arizona staying sharp and getting everyone minutes before conference play. The starters will play 20-25 minutes, build a big lead, and the bench will finish it off. The only question is whether Arizona covers that massive spread - the Wildcats usually do against overmatched opponents at home.

Eagles @ Cougars

2025-12-23T02:00Z | Marriott Center | ESPN+
Line: BYU -34.5 | O/U: 168.5 | EWU: 2-10 | BYU: 11-1

BYU is rolling at 11-1 and has looked like a Big 12 sleeper this season. The Cougars have offensive firepower, with multiple guys who can get buckets in iso situations and a three-point shooting attack that keeps defenses honest. The -34.5 spread reflects the massive talent gap here.

Eastern Washington is just 2-10 and has been one of the worst teams in the Big Sky this year. The Eagles don't have an answer for BYU's size and skill, and they're going to struggle to score against the Cougars' length. This could get ugly in a hurry.

The Marriott Center will be rocking, and BYU will want to put on a show for their home crowd before the holiday break. Expect the Cougars to come out aggressive, build a big early lead, and coast to an easy win. Eastern Washington just doesn't have the talent to compete at this level.
