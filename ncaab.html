Monday Slate Overview

A quiet Monday with 13 games on the board. No ranked teams in action today as programs wrap up non-conference schedules. TCU hosts Incarnate Word in the marquee matchup, while mid-majors battle across the country.

Incarnate Word @ TCU

8:00 PM ET | Ed & Rae Schollmaier Arena
TCU: 6-3 | UIW: 5-5

TCU (6-3) hosts Incarnate Word (5-5) in a tune-up game before Big 12 conference play begins. The Horned Frogs have been competitive this season with solid guard play and improved depth. This is a get-right spot for TCU after some early season struggles, and the home court advantage at Schollmaier Arena should be decisive.

Incarnate Word has been competitive in the Southland Conference but faces a significant talent gap in this matchup. The Cardinals will look to keep the game in the half-court and limit TCU's transition opportunities. For the Horned Frogs, this is about building momentum and getting bench players meaningful minutes before conference play intensifies.

Wyoming @ South Dakota State

8:00 PM ET | Frost Arena
WYO: 8-2 | SDSU: 7-5

Wyoming (8-2) has been one of the Mountain West's pleasant surprises, and they travel to Frost Arena to face South Dakota State (7-5). The Cowboys have played well above expectations with strong perimeter shooting and tough defensive effort. This non-conference road test will measure how far they've come.

South Dakota State is always competitive at home and brings a physical, grinding style that can slow down opponents. The Jackrabbits' experience playing in hostile environments will help them compete, but Wyoming's hot start suggests they're more than capable of winning on the road. An intriguing mid-major matchup with potential NCAA Tournament implications.

Niagara @ VCU

7:00 PM ET | Stuart C. Siegel Center
VCU: 6-4 | NIAG: 2-8

VCU (6-4) hosts Niagara (2-8) at the Siegel Center in what should be a comfortable win for the Rams. VCU's havoc defense continues to force turnovers and create chaos, though their offensive efficiency has room for improvement. This is a chance to work on execution against an overmatched opponent.

Niagara has struggled through a difficult early season and faces a significant challenge against VCU's pressure defense. The Purple Eagles will need to value possessions and limit live-ball turnovers to keep this game competitive. For VCU, it's about maintaining intensity and building good habits before Atlantic 10 play heats up.

Campbellsville @ Western Kentucky

8:00 PM ET | E.A. Diddle Arena
WKU: 6-3 | CAMP: NAIA

Western Kentucky (6-3) hosts NAIA opponent Campbellsville in a non-conference tune-up at Diddle Arena. The Hilltoppers have been solid this season and should cruise in this one. WKU's depth and athleticism will be overwhelming against lower-division competition, making this a good opportunity for reserves to log significant minutes.

This is strictly a preparation game for Western Kentucky as they gear up for Conference USA play. Expect the Hilltoppers to run their sets, work on defensive principles, and get everyone involved offensively. The only intrigue is whether WKU can maintain focus and execute cleanly against a massive talent mismatch.
