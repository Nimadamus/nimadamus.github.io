@

Minnesota Golden Gophers (4-4) @ #22 Indiana Hoosiers (7-0)

#22 Indiana 7:00 PM ET Assembly Hall BTN Line: IND -8.5 Total: 153.5

Indiana enters this Big Ten matchup as a perfect 7-0 and ranked #22 in the AP Poll. The Hoosiers have been dominant at Assembly Hall, where the atmosphere is one of the best in college basketball. Mackenzie Mgbako leads the offense with 16.2 PPG, and Indiana's defense is holding opponents to just 64.3 PPG.

Minnesota's Struggles: The Golden Gophers are 4-4 with losses to quality opponents. They're shooting just 42% from the field and 31% from three. Dawson Garcia is averaging 17.1 PPG but needs help from a supporting cast that's been inconsistent. Minnesota is 0-2 on the road this season.

Why Indiana Covers: The Hoosiers are 5-2 ATS this season and have covered in 4 of their last 5 home games. Their depth and defensive intensity wear down opponents in the second half. Indiana has won by double digits in 5 of their 7 games. Assembly Hall in December is a nightmare for visiting teams.

Key Stat: Indiana is +8.3 in rebounding margin, 15th nationally. Minnesota is -2.1, ranking 278th.
Sources: ESPN, KenPom, Team Statistics

Utah State Aggies (7-0) @ South Florida Bulls (4-4)

7:00 PM ET Yuengling Center ESPN+ Line: USU -1.5 Total: 162.5

Utah State is 7-0 and receiving votes in the AP Poll, making them one of the best mid-major stories in the country. The Aggies are averaging 81.2 PPG with a balanced attack led by Great Osobor (16.8 PPG, 8.2 RPG). Their offense ranks in the top 30 nationally in efficiency according to KenPom.

Road Test for the Aggies: This is Utah State's first true road game against a Power conference opponent. South Florida is 4-4 but plays in the AAC and has the athletes to match up. The Bulls are 3-1 at home and averaging 74.5 PPG at the Yuengling Center.

Line Movement: This opened at USU -2.5 and has moved toward South Florida, suggesting sharp money likes the home dog. Utah State's perfect record has been built against mid-major competition, and this is their first real test away from home.

Key Stat: Utah State is shooting 49.2% from the field (12th nationally). South Florida allows opponents to shoot 44.8% (213th).
Sources: ESPN, KenPom, Team Statistics

The Citadel Bulldogs (2-6) @ Davidson Wildcats (6-1)

7:00 PM ET John M. Belk Arena ESPN+ Line: DAV -21.5 Total: 139.5

Davidson's 6-1 record includes quality wins and they're one of the best mid-major programs in the country under Bob McKillop's system. The Wildcats shoot 47% from the field and 38% from three, spacing the floor with their motion offense. Reed Bailey leads with 14.2 PPG.

The Citadel's Issues: The Bulldogs are 2-6 and struggling on both ends. They're scoring just 62.4 PPG (312th nationally) and allowing 71.3 PPG. Their best player, Hayden Brown, is averaging 13.1 PPG but the supporting cast is thin. The Citadel is 0-3 on the road.

Spread Concerns: 21.5 points is a massive number, even against a struggling team. Davidson has won by 20+ only once this season. They tend to take their foot off the gas in blowouts, which could keep The Citadel within the number late. The Under 139.5 might be the better play.

Key Stat: Davidson's KenPom offensive efficiency is 112.4 (42nd). The Citadel's defensive efficiency is 108.2 (297th).
Sources: ESPN, KenPom, Team Statistics

Robert Morris Colonials (6-3) @ Green Bay Phoenix (4-5)

7:00 PM ET Resch Center ESPN+ Line: RMU -1.5 Total: 138.5

Robert Morris is 6-3 and has been one of the more consistent mid-major programs this season. The Colonials are averaging 73.4 PPG with Enoch Cheeks leading the way at 15.8 PPG. They're 3-1 on the road, showing they can win in hostile environments.

Green Bay's Home Court: The Phoenix are 4-5 overall but 3-1 at the Resch Center. They're averaging 68.2 PPG and playing solid defense at home, allowing just 65.4 PPG in their four home games. This is a dangerous spot for a road favorite.

Trap Game Potential: Robert Morris is laying points on the road in a mid-major game that gets zero national attention. These are the spots where upsets happen. Green Bay has enough talent to keep this close and potentially steal a win at home.

Key Stat: Robert Morris is 4-5 ATS this season. Green Bay is 5-4 ATS and 3-1 ATS at home.
Sources: ESPN, KenPom, Team Statistics

Long Beach State Beach (2-6) @ UC Santa Barbara Gauchos (6-2)

9:00 PM ET The Thunderdome ESPN+ Line: UCSB -12.5 Total: 144.5

UC Santa Barbara is 6-2 and looks like the class of the Big West Conference. The Gauchos are averaging 76.8 PPG with excellent guard play from Stephan Swenson (14.2 PPG). The Thunderdome is one of the best home court advantages in mid-major basketball.

Long Beach State's Struggles: The Beach are 2-6 and have lost 5 straight. They're scoring just 64.3 PPG (289th nationally) and their offense is stagnant. Devin Askew leads with 12.4 PPG but the team lacks a consistent second scorer.

Big West Rivalry: These teams know each other well, which typically leads to closer games than the spread suggests. Long Beach State has covered 4 of their last 6 against UCSB, even in losing efforts. The 12.5 spread is steep for a conference game.

Key Stat: UC Santa Barbara is 5-1 at home over the last two seasons. Long Beach State is 1-8 on the road in the same span.
Sources: ESPN, KenPom, Team Statistics

Cal State Bakersfield Roadrunners (4-4) @ Cal State Northridge Matadors (3-4)

10:00 PM ET Premier America Credit Union Arena ESPN+ Line: CSUN -4.5 Total: 155.5

Two Big West teams meeting early in the season with similar records. Cal State Northridge is 3-4 and playing at home, where they're 2-1 this season. The Matadors are averaging 71.3 PPG with solid guard play from Keonte Jones (13.8 PPG).

Bakersfield's Balance: The Roadrunners are 4-4 with a balanced attack featuring four players averaging double figures. Kaleb Higgins leads with 14.2 PPG. Bakersfield is 2-2 on the road and has shown they can compete in hostile environments.

Conference Implications: Early Big West games matter for conference positioning. Both teams need this win to stay in the upper half of the standings. Expect a physical, competitive game between two evenly matched teams.

Key Stat: These teams split last season's series. Home team covered in both games.
Sources: ESPN, KenPom, Team Statistics
