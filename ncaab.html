Tonight's College Hoops

Massive Tuesday slate with undefeated Arizona, Duke chasing 100 wins, and a Top-20 showdown in Knoxville

December 16, 2025

#11 Louisville @ #20 Tennessee

7:00 PM ET | Food City Center | ESPN
Line: TENN -2.5 | O/U: 145.5 | LOU: 9-1 | TENN: 7-3

This is your game of the night. Louisville (9-1) comes into Knoxville riding high after beating both Indiana and Kentucky this season, plus a 26-point demolition of Memphis. The Cardinals have a 22.0 BPI (6th overall) and shoot the most threes in the country. Tennessee (7-3) started 7-0 with a win over Houston but has dropped three straight to Syracuse and Illinois.

The Volunteers have a 41-game non-conference home winning streak, just two shy of the program record. Rick Barnes is chasing his 843rd career win, which would tie him for 9th all-time among active coaches. Tennessee's defense ranks in the top 25 in adjusted efficiency. Louisville's only loss came on the road at Arkansas (89-80). Something has to give here.

#1 Arizona vs Abilene Christian

9:00 PM ET | McKale Center | ESPN+
Line: ARIZ -28.5 | O/U: 160.5 | ARIZ: 9-0 | ACU: 7-3

Arizona is the first team in AP Poll history to beat five ranked teams in their first nine games. Let that sink in. The Wildcats are 9-0 and averaging 89.3 points per game with elite efficiency across the roster. Brayden Burries scored a career-high 28 points (20 in the second half) against #12 Alabama, including 15 straight points to pull away.

Jaden Bradley (14.3 PPG), Koa Peat (14.8 PPG), and Burries (13.1 PPG) give Arizona three legitimate scoring options. KenPom gives them a 99.6% win probability here. Abilene Christian (7-3) is on a 3-game winning streak and Bradyn Hubbard leads with 16.4 PPG, but this is a massive step up in competition.

#3 Duke vs Lipscomb

6:00 PM ET | Cameron Indoor | ACC Network
Line: DUKE -33.5 | O/U: 151.5 | DUKE: 10-0 | LIPS: 6-4

Duke's 10-0 start is their best since winning their first 11 games in 2017-18. Cameron Boozer leads the nation with 23.0 PPG and adds 9.9 RPG (18th nationally). The Blue Devils average 86.8 PPG (45th) while allowing just 59.6 (4th nationally). Coach Jon Scheyer is one win away from his 100th career victory (99-22, 53-3 at home).

Duke has won 22 consecutive home games and 35 straight vs unranked non-conference opponents at Cameron Indoor. Lipscomb is the reigning Atlantic Sun champion on a 5-game winning streak, but they're 13th nationally in 3-point attempts (31.6/game) and will need to get hot from deep to have any chance. First-ever meeting between these programs.

#5 UConn vs Butler

8:30 PM ET | Hartford | Peacock
Line: UCONN -15.5 | O/U: 148.5 | UCONN: 10-1 | BUT: 8-2

UConn begins their Big East title defense against Butler in their conference opener. The Huskies (10-1) are 8th in NET rankings with signature wins over BYU, Illinois, Kansas, and Florida. Their only loss came to then-#4 Arizona at home on November 19. Solo Ball (14.5 PPG) and Alex Karaban (13.8 PPG) lead the attack.

Butler (8-2) is coming off a wild 113-110 double-OT win over Providence. Michael Ajayi had 28 points and 15 rebounds in that one, while Finley Bizjack hit the huge 3-pointer in 2OT. UConn is 11-0 all-time against Butler, including the 2011 national title game (53-41). Butler lost starting PG Jalen Jackson to a season-ending ankle injury, which limits their backcourt depth significantly.

#9 Michigan State vs Toledo

6:30 PM ET | Breslin Center | Peacock
Line: MSU -23.5 | O/U: 152.5 | MSU: 9-1 | TOL: 5-5

Michigan State is one of only six teams nationally with four Quad 1 wins (Arkansas, Kentucky, North Carolina, Iowa). The Spartans (9-1, 2-0 Big Ten) allow just 62.1 PPG and have a plus-11.5 rebounding margin. Divine Ugochukwu had a career-high 23 points vs Penn State, and Jaxon Kohler is averaging 10 PPG and 12 RPG with 5 double-doubles.

Toledo (5-5, 161st KenPom) is just 1-3 on the road and coming off a brutal blown 18-point halftime lead against Robert Morris. Sonny Wilson leads at 16.8 PPG, but coach Tod Kowalczyk is 0-5 against Michigan State in his career. The teams last met in 2002 when MSU won 81-76.

#12 North Carolina vs East Tennessee State

8:00 PM ET | Chapel Hill | ACC Network
Line: UNC -18.5 | O/U: 154.5 | UNC: 9-2 | ETSU: 8-3

Freshman Caleb Wilson is having a historic season for UNC, averaging 19.4 PPG and 10.6 RPG with 7 double-doubles (leading the ACC, 4th nationally). He set the UNC freshman record with 4 consecutive double-doubles. Henri Veesaar (Arizona transfer) adds 16.0 PPG and 9.2 RPG. That frontcourt combination is producing 35.4 PPG and 19.8 RPG.

ETSU's 8-3 start is their best since 2019-20. They force 15+ turnovers per game and average 10+ steals per night, but UNC only turns it over 11 times per game (top third nationally). This is ETSU's first major conference opponent of the season, and they have the 330th-ranked strength of schedule per KenPom.
