Providence @ Marquette

Monday, 6:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee | FS1
Spread: PROV +1.5 (-110) / MARQ -1.5 (-110) | ML: PROV +105 / MARQ -125 | O/U: 166.5

This is as close to a pick'em as you'll find in Big East play. Marquette at home as just 1.5-point favorites against Providence tells you everything about where these programs stand right now. The Golden Eagles are 6-13 overall and 1-7 in conference play, while the Friars come in at 9-9 with a 2-5 Big East mark. Neither team has met expectations, and tonight represents a chance for one to salvage their season's trajectory.

Marquette's Home Struggles

Fiserv Forum has been anything but a fortress this season. Marquette's ATS record stands at a brutal 5-14, suggesting the market has consistently overvalued this squad. The Golden Eagles haven't covered against quality opponents, and their offensive inconsistency has frustrated bettors all season. That said, they're still at home in a conference game with tournament implications on the line.

The offense can score in bunches when clicking, which the 166.5 total reflects. Marquette's pace-and-space system creates three-point opportunities, but their shooting percentages have fluctuated wildly. Some nights they look like a Sweet 16 team; others, they struggle to crack 60 points against inferior competition. Providence's defense will test which version shows up tonight.

Providence's Road Credentials

The Friars have been competitive in conference play despite their record. Their 3-0 straight-up record as underdogs in away games shows they don't back down in hostile environments. Providence's physical style travels well because it doesn't rely on shooting to win. They crash the glass, get to the line, and make opponents earn everything in the paint.

Ed Cooley's teams are always prepared for Big East battles. Providence knows how to execute in close games, and their experience in these environments matters. The Friars have played tighter defensively in conference play, and they'll look to slow this game down and limit Marquette's transition opportunities.

The Total

The 166.5 total is notably high for a Big East game featuring two teams below .500 in conference. Providence has hit the over in 20 of their last 31 games, suggesting they either score or allow plenty of points. Marquette's home games have been volatile, with some shootouts and some defensive slugfests. The market expects offense, and both teams have the potential to get into the 80s when pushing tempo.

ATS Trends

The consensus shows 56% of picks on Providence at +1.5, with the over/under split nearly even at 49% over and 51% under. Marquette's 5-14 ATS record is one of the worst in college basketball this season, and backing them to cover hasn't been profitable. Providence, meanwhile, has proven they belong on the court with better teams. This should be a dogfight down to the final possessions.

Columbia @ Yale

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Leede Arena, Hanover | ESPN+
Spread: COL +9.5 (-110) / YALE -9.5 (-110) | ML: COL +390 / YALE -500 | O/U: 151

Ivy League basketball provides an intriguing matchup between two programs heading in different directions this season. Yale enters at 12-3, positioning themselves as favorites for the conference's automatic NCAA Tournament bid. Columbia comes in at 12-4, a respectable record but one built more against weaker competition. The 9.5-point spread reflects Yale's home dominance and their superior efficiency metrics.

The Bulldogs' system creates open looks through ball movement and cutting. Yale's offensive rating ranks among the best in the Ivy League, and their three-point shooting has been lethal from the corners. Columbia's defense will need to close out aggressively while avoiding fouling, a difficult balance to maintain for 40 minutes against a team this patient with the basketball.

The 151 total is modest, suggesting a pace-controlled game that favors Yale's half-court execution. Both teams prefer to play in the mid-60s, using the shot clock and minimizing transition opportunities. Columbia's path to covering involves hanging around, forcing turnovers, and converting in transition before Yale can set their defense. That's easier said than done against a team this disciplined.

Double-digit spreads in Ivy League play always carry risk because these teams know each other so well. Coaches have extensive film, and players understand tendencies. Columbia won't be surprised by anything Yale runs, but execution becomes the question. Can the Lions match Yale's shooting efficiency? History suggests that's a tall order on the road against the league's best team.

George Washington @ George Mason

Monday, 5:00 PM ET | EagleBank Arena | CBSSN
Spread: GW +3.5 (-110) / GMU -3.5 (-110) | ML: GW +145 / GMU -175 | O/U: 145.5

A regional rivalry adds spice to Monday's slate as George Washington visits George Mason in a game that means more to fans than national audiences might realize. These programs recruit the same territory, and bragging rights matter in the DMV. George Mason enters as modest home favorites, reflecting their solid play at EagleBank Arena this season.

The Patriots' defense has been their calling card, limiting opponents to below average efficiency numbers. They pack the paint and force teams to beat them from three, a strategy that's worked more often than not. George Washington's perimeter shooting can be streaky, which plays directly into Mason's game plan. If GW goes cold from deep, this gets ugly quickly.

The 145.5 total is low even by A-10 standards, and it should be. Neither team prioritizes pace, and both prefer grinding out possessions until good shots emerge. This feels like a game decided by a handful of possessions in the final minutes, exactly the type of environment where home court becomes decisive. George Mason knows how to close games at EagleBank.

Conference games in mid-January carry weight for tournament positioning, even if neither team projects as an at-large candidate. The A-10 Tournament remains the path for both programs, and every conference win builds momentum. George Washington needs this road win more than Mason needs the home victory, which could create interesting dynamics late in a tight game.
