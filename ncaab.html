Thursday, November 27, 2025 - Thanksgiving Day

VS

#4 Duke Blue Devils vs #22 Arkansas Razorbacks

CBS Sports Thanksgiving Classic | 8:00 PM ET | CBS | United Center, Chicago | Duke -10.5 | O/U 157.5

This is the marquee game of Thanksgiving and one of the biggest nonconference matchups of the entire college basketball season. The fourth-ranked Duke Blue Devils bring a perfect 7-0 record into the United Center to face the 22nd-ranked Arkansas Razorbacks, who are 5-1 and winners of four straight. This is primetime college hoops on CBS, and both teams are loaded with talent.

Duke's Dominant Start

The Blue Devils have been absolutely suffocating opponents this season. Duke is scoring 93.6 points per game, ranking 16th nationally, while allowing just 57.0 points per contest, which ranks first in all of college basketball. Thats a plus-36.6 scoring differential through seven games. Cameron Boozer has been the centerpiece, averaging over 21 points per game and establishing himself as a potential top-five NBA draft pick. Duke is 5-2 ATS this season and has gone UNDER in six of their last seven games, a testament to that elite defense.

Arkansas Looking to Make a Statement

The Razorbacks would love nothing more than to knock off a blue blood on the national stage. Meleek Thomas leads Arkansas with 18.3 points per game and has been the offensive catalyst in their four-game winning streak. Arkansas is 3-3 ATS this season and has gone 7-3 ATS in their last ten games overall. The UNDER has hit in four of their last five, suggesting this could be a grind-it-out affair despite the offensive firepower on both sides.

The Betting Angle

Duke is laying 10.5 points against a ranked opponent, which is a massive spread in a neutral-site showcase game. The Blue Devils have the defense to keep this low-scoring, and Arkansas has shown they can hang with quality opponents. Duke is 6-1 ATS in their last seven games overall, but double-digit spreads against ranked teams always deserve scrutiny. The total of 157.5 feels high given both teams' recent UNDER trends.

VS

#16 North Carolina Tar Heels vs #11 Michigan State Spartans

Fort Myers Tip-Off Classic | 4:30 PM ET | FOX | Suncoast Credit Union Arena, Fort Myers, FL | MSU -1.5 | O/U 150.5

Two of college basketballs most storied programs clash in what could be an NCAA Tournament preview. Both North Carolina and Michigan State enter this game with perfect 6-0 records, making this one of the best matchups of Feast Week. The Spartans are narrow 1.5-point favorites, and Michigan State won the last meeting between these teams 94-91 in overtime.

The UNC Injury Situation

North Carolina has a massive frontcourt anchored by superstar freshman Caleb Wilson, who is averaging 20.5 points and 10.3 rebounds per game. The 7-foot center Henri Veesar adds 16.3 points and 8.7 boards. However, the Tar Heels lost senior guard Seth Trimble to a broken arm a few weeks ago. Freshman Luka Bogavac has done a commendable job replacing him, but the backcourt depth has been significantly hurt. This is the first time this season UNC is listed as a moneyline underdog.

Michigan State's Balanced Attack

The Spartans already have marquee wins over Arkansas and Kentucky this month, proving they belong among the nations elite. Point guard Jeremy Fears leads the entire nation with 10.2 assists per game while also scoring in double figures. Hes one of four Spartans averaging double digits, with big man Jaxon Kohler putting up 15.3 points and 9.5 rebounds per game. Michigan State is 9-3 SU and ATS against ranked opponents since the start of last season.

The Betting Angle

This line screams coin flip. Michigan State has won three games ATS while failing to cover three times. UNC is 4-2 ATS and has the size advantage inside. The Spartans' experience against ranked opponents gives them an edge, but 1.5 points is essentially a pick'em. The total of 150.5 reflects two teams that can score but also defend. This game has overtime written all over it.

VS

#9 BYU Cougars vs Miami Hurricanes

5:00 PM ET | ESPN | State Farm Field House, Kissimmee, FL | BYU -9.5 | O/U 164.5

The ninth-ranked BYU Cougars are heavy 9.5-point favorites against a Miami team thats been solid at 5-1 this season. BYU enters at 4-1 and looks to bounce back from their only loss while continuing to establish themselves as Big 12 contenders. This game features the highest projected total on the Thanksgiving slate at 164.5 points.

The Spread Question

BYU is 3-2 ATS this season and has a concerning trend: they are just 1-6 ATS in their last seven games played on Thursday when on the road. Miami, meanwhile, is 4-2 ATS and has covered in four of their last five games overall. The Hurricanes havent covered as a 9.5-point underdog yet this season, going 0-1 in that spot, but the sample size is small. The OVER has hit in 8 of Miamis last 11 games, supporting that lofty total.

VS

#10 Florida Gators vs TCU Horned Frogs

3:00 PM ET | FS1 | Jenny Craig Pavilion, San Diego, CA | Florida -13.5 | O/U 155.5

The tenth-ranked Florida Gators are massive 13.5-point favorites against TCU in San Diego. Florida enters at 4-1 with a plus-17.0 scoring differential, while TCU is 3-2 and looking to pull off the upset of the day. The Gators score 86.2 points per game while allowing 69.2, but this spread feels inflated.

The TCU Cover Spot

Here's the thing with this game: Florida is 0-3 ATS when favored by 13.5 points or more this season. Meanwhile, TCU is 5-0 ATS in their last five games played on Thursday when on the road, and the Horned Frogs have covered in their last five overall. Florida is just 1-4 ATS in their last five games. The UNDER has hit in 13 of TCUs last 20 games, and this total of 155.5 might be worth a look.
