Feast Week Continues - 23-Game Slate

Feast Week Feast: Friday's 23-Game College Basketball Slate

Posted: November 28, 2025 | ESPN Events Invitational, Various Tournament Games

Black Friday brings another loaded day of Feast Week college basketball with 23 games on the schedule. The ESPN Events Invitational at Walt Disney World concludes with championship games in all three brackets - Adventure, Imagination, and Magic. Tournament action features teams jockeying for positioning and early-season statement wins that will matter come Selection Sunday.

ESPN EVENTS INVITATIONAL FINALS

State Farm Field House, Kissimmee, FL

The Terry's Chocolate ESPN Events Invitational wraps up with championship games in three brackets. The Adventure bracket features eight teams including Bradley, Liberty, Princeton, Rhode Island, Temple, Towson, UC San Diego, and Vermont - with Liberty, Towson, and Vermont all favored to win their respective conferences. These games provide glimpses at mid-major programs with NCAA Tournament aspirations.

The Imagination bracket includes Charlotte, Furman, Illinois State, and Richmond, while the Magic bracket features BYU among its participants. Each bracket crowns its own champion, and the winners will carry momentum into conference play. These early-season tournament results help shape bracketology discussions as we move toward March.

KEY INDIVIDUAL MATCHUPS

Duke takes on Arkansas in the CBS Sports Thanksgiving Classic at the United Center in Chicago. No. 4 Duke (7-0) brings the best scoring defense in college basketball against No. 22 Arkansas (5-1). Cameron Boozer has been the star for the Blue Devils, projected as a top-three 2026 NBA Draft pick. Arkansas counters with potential lottery picks Meleek Thomas and Darius Acuff Jr. Duke is favored by 10.5 points with the total at 159.5.

The Blue Devils have made a convincing case for the best defense in college basketball, not allowing more than 66 points in any game this season and holding Niagara to just 42. Duke has covered in seven straight games and eight of their last ten. The under has hit in four of Arkansas's six games, suggesting this could be a defensive battle between two talented teams. Both programs are early contenders for deep March runs.
