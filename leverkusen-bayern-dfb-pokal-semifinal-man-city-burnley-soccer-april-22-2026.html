Bayer Leverkusen vs Bayern Munich

Wednesday, 2:45 PM ET | BayArena, Leverkusen, Germany

Bayer Leverkusen hosts Bayern Munich at the BayArena in a DFB-Pokal semifinal with a cup final at the Olympiastadion on May 23 waiting for the winner. Bayern arrive as heavy favorites priced at roughly 1.60 on the outright market with Leverkusen at 5.20 and the draw in between. Vincent Kompany's side is unbeaten in 17 consecutive matches across all competitions, were crowned Bundesliga champions over the weekend, and are now one semifinal and one final away from the first Bayern treble since the 2019-20 Hansi Flick peak. Harry Kane's cup scoring form and Jamal Musiala's return to full fitness give Bayern the kind of attacking depth no German side can match match-for-match.

Leverkusen's path to an upset runs through Lukas Hradecky's goalkeeping and Xhaka's midfield control against Joshua Kimmich and Aleksandar Pavlovic. Xabi Alonso's side has won just one of its last six home matches, a structural slump that has coincided with the post-Florian Wirtz rebuild becoming more difficult than the front office originally expected. Granit Xhaka remains the tactical anchor, Alejandro Grimaldo's set-piece delivery is the best in the Bundesliga, and the Leverkusen home crowd at BayArena is the kind of environment that has produced upsets against Bayern before. But the overall Bayern profile is too complete and too deep to bet against on head-to-head terms.

Kane's goal record against Leverkusen specifically is the kind of matchup history that supports the short price. Michael Olise's direct running at a Leverkusen back line that has aged quickly produces the expected wide-zone mismatch, and Leroy Sane's form down the right is the complementary threat. Konrad Laimer and Pavlovic's box-to-box work rate has replaced what the club lost with the Leon Goretzka departures, and Manuel Neuer's late-career playoff goalkeeping profile remains above replacement even against the best chances Leverkusen can produce.

The match total projects at 3.0-3.5 with Bayern's 3-2 correct-score at 14/1. The over 2.5 is near the implied line. The three-way odds market favors Bayern straight up, but the Asian handicap market for Leverkusen +1 at near-evens is where the informed money has landed. Kickoff 2:45 PM ET on ESPN+ with the return leg in the final. A Bayern win books the May 23 Berlin final appearance and keeps the treble alive.

Burnley vs Manchester City

Wednesday, 3:00 PM ET | Turf Moor, Burnley, England

Newly-promoted Burnley hosts Manchester City at Turf Moor with the visitors priced at a -714 outright favorite, Burnley at +1200, and the draw at +700. City's 2-1 Premier League win at home over Arsenal on April 19 kept the title chase alive, and Pep Guardiola's side arrives in East Lancashire knowing that three points keep the mathematical gap to Liverpool at the front in single digits with three matches remaining. Erling Haaland has scored seven goals in four previous matches against Burnley, an average of a goal every 36 minutes, and Fairplay are offering an enhanced 5/2 boost for Haaland first goalscorer.

Scott Parker's Burnley are fighting against the drop and have the kind of compact mid-block that has frustrated better sides this season at Turf Moor. James Trafford's goalkeeping has been the single most important individual performance keeping Burnley in the league, and his one-on-one positioning against Haaland's left-foot finishes is the game's central micro-matchup. Josh Cullen's midfield screening, Anass Zaroury's set-piece deliveries, and Lyle Foster's lone-striker hold-up play produce the template Burnley need to execute if they're going to pull the kind of 1-0 or 0-0 stagnation result that any home underdog against City fantasy-drafts before a match.

City's probable rotation around the DFB-Pokal week isn't a concern because the Premier League title chase matters more than the Champions League status for this club, and Guardiola historically leans into first-choice lineups when the title is on the line. Rodri's return to full form, Phil Foden's creative output, Bernardo Silva's tactical versatility, and Josko Gvardiol's left-back overlaps all remain. Savinho and Jeremy Doku on the wings give Guardiola the kind of wide threat that has historically broken compact Burnley blocks. The expected-goals line is heavily in City's favor.

The over 2.5 goals is the handicap the betting market has priced highest, and Haaland anytime goalscorer at -244 is the cleanest prop. A narrow margin City win with a 2-0 or 2-1 scoreline projects as the most likely match outcome. The match handicap of City -2 is where the sharper money has moved. Kickoff 3:00 PM ET on USA Network and Peacock.

Bournemouth vs Leeds United

Wednesday, 3:00 PM ET | Vitality Stadium, Bournemouth, England

Bournemouth hosts Leeds United at the Vitality Stadium in one of the two Wednesday Premier League fixtures. The Cherries are favored at -103 with Leeds priced at +132 and the draw in the mid-range, a pick'em market that reflects both sides' mid-table status and Leeds's better-than-expected first season back in the top flight. Andoni Iraola's Bournemouth have been the model of mid-table overachievement for a third consecutive season, and the counter-pressing identity of the Iraola era is still the structural tactical advantage against sides that try to play out from the back.

Justin Kluivert's winger production, Dango Ouattara's direct running, and Evanilson's center-forward physical play produce the Bournemouth attacking template. Antoine Semenyo's form on the right has been the single biggest individual breakout of the season, and the captured England international profile has elevated his ceiling toward a summer transfer-window departure. Leeds's defensive structure under Daniel Farke has been tested at the back in recent matches, and the central-defensive pairing of Joe Rodon and Pascal Struijk will be asked to handle the Bournemouth pace at Vitality Stadium in a way Leeds haven't always been asked to do this season.

Leeds's attacking profile runs through Joel Piroe's finishing, Brenden Aaronson's creativity in the half-spaces, and Willy Gnonto's wing threat. Ethan Ampadu and Ilia Gruev in the midfield provide the structural discipline the Farke system requires, and the Elland Road home form has been the biggest points-banker for the season. Away form has been more mixed, which is the profile reason Bournemouth is favored despite Leeds's overall goal differential being the cleaner number.

The both-teams-to-score market is priced at -172 with over 2.5 goals at roughly -110, reflecting the expectation of an open game between two counter-attacking sides. Independent modeling has the match-winner probabilities at Bournemouth 47.9 percent, draw 24.6 percent, and Leeds 27.5 percent. Kickoff 3:00 PM ET on Peacock with both sides still mathematically in Europa Conference League qualification contention.
