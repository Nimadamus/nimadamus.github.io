Premier League Matchweek 33

Chelsea Hosts Manchester United at Stamford Bridge

Five Premier League fixtures on Saturday. Brentford vs Fulham opens the slate at 7:30 AM ET. Leeds vs Wolves and Newcastle vs Bournemouth share the 10 AM window. Tottenham vs Brighton kicks at 12:30 PM. And the marquee: Chelsea vs Manchester United at Stamford Bridge at 3 PM ET on TNT Sports, with European places on the line for both sides.

Brentford vs Fulham

Saturday, 7:30 AM ET | Gtech Community Stadium, London

The West London derby kicks off the Premier League Saturday slate with both clubs in the mid-table scramble for European places. Brentford under Thomas Frank has once again been one of the most efficient mid-table sides in the league, built around Bryan Mbeumo's goal-scoring form and Mikkel Damsgaard's creative output from the half-spaces. Fulham under Marco Silva has been punching above their weight, with Raul Jimenez providing the central striker profile the club has lacked in recent years and Antonee Robinson giving them one of the most reliable left-backs in the league.

Brentford open as a slight home favorite given the Gtech Stadium advantage and Mbeumo's recent form. Fulham are at a stage of the season where a draw might be the politically correct outcome given both clubs' European ambitions, but both managers will want the three points. The over 2.5 goals has historically been the sharper play in this fixture, with Brentford's pressing style producing open games more often than not.

USA Network

Leeds United vs Wolves

Saturday, 10:00 AM ET | Elland Road, Leeds

Promoted Leeds United host Wolves at Elland Road in a fixture with relegation undertones for both clubs. Leeds under Daniel Farke have been fighting every week to establish themselves in the top-flight, with Daniel James and Willy Gnonto providing the pace that cuts opponents open in transition. Wolves have been in a relegation fight for much of the year, with Matheus Cunha carrying the attack on his own while the rest of the Wolves forward line has struggled for consistency.

Leeds are a narrow home favorite given the Elland Road intensity and Wolves' away form. This is the kind of fixture where home advantage can swing 0.5-1.0 goals of expected value, and Wolves will be happy to take a point and move on. The under 2.5 is the more calculated angle because both defenses have been decent and both attacks have been inconsistent, but Elland Road can produce quick first-goal moments.

Match 3
Peacock

Newcastle vs AFC Bournemouth

Saturday, 10:00 AM ET | St James' Park, Newcastle

Newcastle host AFC Bournemouth at St James' Park with Champions League ambitions still live for Eddie Howe's side. Alexander Isak remains one of the most lethal No. 9s in the league when fit, and Anthony Gordon's form on the left has given Newcastle a wide-attack dimension that stretches defensive lines. Bournemouth under Andoni Iraola have been the quiet success story of the season, with Evanilson leading the line and Dean Huijsen being one of the most valuable center-backs in the league.

Newcastle are expected to be strong home favorites given St James' Park's noise and Isak's recent form. Bournemouth's away record has been solid but not spectacular, and they're going to need to win a lot of second balls in midfield to stay in this. The over 2.5 is the sharper play given both teams' attacking threats, particularly with Newcastle's high defensive line leaving space for Evanilson's runs.

Match 4
Sky Sports

Tottenham vs Brighton

Saturday, 12:30 PM ET | Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, London

Tottenham host Brighton at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in a fixture that has been a fixture of the EPL's most entertaining over-the-top viewing in recent years. Ange Postecoglou's Spurs continue to play the highest defensive line in the league, which produces the highest xG swing variance match by match. Brighton under Fabian Hurzeler have settled into a cohesive tactical unit that punishes high lines through long vertical passes into space, with Joao Pedro and Kaoru Mitoma providing the majority of their goal threat.

Tottenham are the home favorites given the Premier League schedule disparity and Son Heung-Min's late-season form. Brighton are one of the best defensive teams in the league by xGA, and they will be happy to play for counter-attack opportunities. The over 3.0 goals is the statistically cleanest angle because these two teams have been involved in more 3-plus goal fixtures than any other top-10 matchup over the last two seasons.

Match 5 - Featured
TNT Sports

Chelsea vs Manchester United

Saturday, 3:00 PM ET | Stamford Bridge, London

The marquee. Chelsea host Manchester United at Stamford Bridge with European places on the line for both clubs. Enzo Maresca's Chelsea have stabilized into a top-six side after the turbulence of recent years, with Cole Palmer continuing to be one of the most creative attacking midfielders in European football. Manchester United under Ruben Amorim have been the more inconsistent side, but Bruno Fernandes remains the captain-driven force that turns flat matches into 3-2 finals, and Alejandro Garnacho's left-wing play has given United a direct threat they missed last year.

Chelsea are the home favorites given Stamford Bridge's return to form as a fortress, but Manchester United have a strange habit of winning at Chelsea in spite of their broader form. Palmer's performance against United has been a recurring theme of recent seasons, and his underlying xG per 90 remains one of the best numbers in the league. Both teams have defensive questions, so the over 2.5 is a live angle. If you're looking for a sharper line, the total goals market at 2.75 or higher has the best expected value given both clubs' recent away records. Kickoff at 3:00 PM ET on TNT Sports. Winner takes a legitimate step toward European qualification.
