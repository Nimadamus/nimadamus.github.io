Yankees Moneyline -143
3 units | New York Yankees at Athletics | 9:40 p.m. ET

Yankees Moneyline -143 is the headline MLB sheet position for Friday, May 29, 2026. New York opens a three-game set in West Sacramento with Carlos Rodon on the mound against a familiar face in Luis Severino, and the moneyline keeps the bet simple: take the deeper, more talented roster on the road and let the better arm do the work.

Game Setup

  • Away: New York Yankees
  • Home: Athletics
  • Venue: Sutter Health Park, West Sacramento
  • Time: 9:40 p.m. ET

Probable Starters

  • New York Yankees: Carlos Rodon (LHP)
  • Athletics: Luis Severino (RHP)
  • Carlos Rodon: 0-2, 4.15 ERA on the season.
  • Luis Severino: 2-5, 4.23 ERA on the season.

Why The Yankees Travel Well Here

This is a classic talent-gap moneyline. The Athletics are scuffling at home, dragging a multi-game home skid into the series, while the Yankees come in playing their best ball of the stretch. New York's lineup length is the separator. Even on a night when the top of the order goes quiet, the Yankees keep sending professional at-bats to the plate, and that depth is exactly what wears down a thin Athletics staff over nine innings. Laying -143 with the road favorite is buying the more reliable path to four-plus runs.

The Rodon Versus Severino Angle

Rodon's win-loss line does not tell the story; the ERA and his strikeout base are the read here, and a left-hander missing bats plays up against an Athletics group that has been chasing offense at Sutter Health Park. On the other side, Severino faces the organization he came up with, and that revenge-game emotion can cut both ways, often pushing a pitcher to overthrow early. If New York gets to him before he settles in, the moneyline is in great shape long before the bullpens matter.

Risk Checklist

The risk on any road moneyline favorite is a quiet night against a pitcher feeding on adrenaline, plus the bullpen variance that comes with a one-run game. The hedge in the design is the unit weight: three units says this is a confident spot, not a coin flip. As long as Rodon gives the Yankees length and the offense reaches Severino before he locks in, New York controls this game from the middle innings on.
