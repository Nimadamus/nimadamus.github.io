MLB Regular Season - Wednesday Slate

Skubal, Ohtani And deGrom Headline A Sixteen-Game MLB Wednesday

A full sixteen-game MLB Wednesday spans every time slot, from a 12:10 PM ET getaway-day start in Miami to a 9:45 PM nightcap at Oracle Park. Tarik Skubal hosts the Yankees at Comerica. Shohei Ohtani takes the ball for the Dodgers at Target Field. Jacob deGrom starts for Texas in Miami, and the Cubs and Mets play a doubleheader at Citi Field. Game-by-game breakdowns below.

Game 1 - Marquee
YES/BSDET

Yankees @ Tigers

Wednesday, 6:40 PM ET | Comerica Park, Detroit, MI

The best pitching matchup on the board belongs to Detroit. Tarik Skubal takes the ball for the Tigers against Ryan Weathers and the Yankees at Comerica Park, and Skubal is the single biggest reason to circle this game. New York arrives with the best record in the American League at 47-31; Detroit sits at 34-45 and is leaning on its ace to steal one against a contender. That gap in the standings is real, but a front-line left-hander on the mound flattens a lot of it for one night.

Weathers, a left-hander himself, draws the road assignment against a deep Yankee lineup that has carried New York to the top of the AL East. The Tigers' path is simple: Skubal gives them length and keeps the game close enough for their bullpen and a timely swing to matter. Comerica Park's spacious outfield has long suppressed run scoring, which favors the side with the better starter. First pitch 6:40 PM ET.

Game 2
FDSNFL/RSN

Rangers @ Marlins

Wednesday, 12:10 PM ET | loanDepot park, Miami, FL

The earliest first pitch of the day is a getaway-day matinee in Miami, and it carries star power. Jacob deGrom takes the ball for Texas against Eury Perez and the Marlins at loanDepot park. Both teams sit near .500, with Texas at 38-41 and Miami at 41-39, so this is a coin-flip on paper that the starters can tilt. deGrom's velocity and swing-and-miss profile remain among the most electric in the sport when he is healthy and on the mound, and a daytime start under the closed Miami roof is a neutral, controlled environment that suits a power arm.

Perez, the young Marlins right-hander, is the counter, and his own high-end stuff is the reason Miami can match Texas pitch for pitch. loanDepot park's roof removes weather as a variable, so this projects as a pitching-led matinee that turns on which ace blinks first. First pitch 12:10 PM ET.

Game 3 - Doubleheader G1
SNY/MARQUEE

Cubs @ Mets

Wednesday, 1:10 PM ET | Citi Field, New York, NY

The Cubs and Mets play a doubleheader at Citi Field, and the opener sends Javier Assad to the mound for Chicago against Nolan McLean. The Cubs come in at 41-37 and the Mets at 34-44, a notable split between a Chicago club hovering in the NL Central race and a New York team that has slipped under .500. Assad gives the Cubs a strike-throwing starter to set the tone in game one of a long day for both pitching staffs.

McLean represents the Mets' young-rotation depth, and a doubleheader puts a premium on length from both starters since the bullpens have to cover eighteen innings across the day. Citi Field's pitcher-leaning dimensions reward the side that limits free passes. First pitch 1:10 PM ET in the opener.

Game 4
CLEGUARDIANS.TV/CHSN

Guardians @ White Sox

Wednesday, 2:10 PM ET | Rate Field, Chicago, IL

Cleveland sends Tanner Bibee to the mound against Erick Fedde and the White Sox at Rate Field in a daytime divisional matchup. The Guardians sit at 41-39, a game over .500 and in the thick of the AL Central picture, while Chicago at 41-37 has quietly hung around the same neighborhood. Bibee is the steadier of the two profiles, a strike-thrower who gives Cleveland a chance to control tempo against a White Sox lineup that has been streaky.

Fedde counters for Chicago, and his ability to keep the ball on the ground is the key against a Guardians offense built more on contact than power. Divisional familiarity tends to tighten these games, and a midweek matinee with two rotation regulars projects as a measured, lower-scoring affair. First pitch 2:10 PM ET.

Game 5 - Coors Field
NESN/COLR

Red Sox @ Rockies

Wednesday, 3:10 PM ET | Coors Field, Denver, CO

Boston heads to altitude, sending Ranger Suarez to the mound against Kyle Freeland and the Rockies at Coors Field. The Red Sox at 32-45 have had an uneven season, but they are still the road favorite here against a Colorado club that sits at 31-49, one of the worst records in baseball. Suarez, a left-hander who relies on command and ground-ball contact, faces the toughest assignment a pitcher can draw: keeping the ball in the yard a mile above sea level.

Freeland is the rare Coors arm with experience pitching to the park's quirks, which matters when the altitude turns routine fly balls into extra-base damage. Coors Field's run environment is the dominant betting factor in any game here, and totals at 20th and Blake routinely play higher than they would anywhere else. First pitch 3:10 PM ET.

Game 6
MASN2/BSW

Orioles @ Angels

Wednesday, 4:07 PM ET | Angel Stadium, Anaheim, CA

Baltimore visits Anaheim with Trey Gibson on the mound against Jose Soriano and the Angels at Angel Stadium. Both clubs have struggled, with the Orioles at 38-43 and the Angels at 33-48, so this is a matchup of two teams trying to play spoiler more than contender. Gibson is an early-career arm for Baltimore being asked to give length against a Los Angeles lineup that still has power at the top.

Soriano counters for the Angels, and his sinker-heavy approach is built to generate ground balls and keep the Orioles' bats from elevating. Angel Stadium has historically played close to neutral, leaning slightly toward pitchers, which keeps the projected total in a moderate range. First pitch 4:07 PM ET.

Game 7
ROOT/SNP

Mariners @ Pirates

Wednesday, 6:40 PM ET | PNC Park, Pittsburgh, PA

Seattle sends Bryan Woo to the mound against Braxton Ashcraft and the Pirates at PNC Park. The Mariners at 41-39 are the more complete club and arrive as the side with the better rotation depth, while Pittsburgh at 39-40 has stayed within range largely on the strength of its young pitching. Woo's strike-throwing profile travels well to a pitcher's park like PNC, where the deep outfield dimensions reward arms that limit hard contact.

Ashcraft represents the Pirates' developmental pipeline, and his ability to work efficiently into the middle innings is what keeps Pittsburgh in games against better lineups. PNC Park's spacious gaps make this a spot where the under is always in play. First pitch 6:40 PM ET.

Game 8
BSKC/RAYS.TV

Royals @ Rays

Wednesday, 6:40 PM ET | Tropicana Field, St. Petersburg, FL

Kansas City visits Tampa Bay with Noah Cameron taking the ball against Griffin Jax and the Rays at Tropicana Field. The Rays at 43-33 are well above .500 and pushing in the American League race, while the Royals at 34-46 have spent the year as one of the weaker clubs in the league. Cameron has been the steadiest of the Royals' starters, and his job is to keep Kansas City close on the road against a deeper opponent.

Jax draws the home assignment for Tampa Bay, where the Rays' run-prevention identity and a low-scoring dome environment work in their favor. The Trop has long suppressed offense, which tilts a close-on-paper pitching matchup toward the better roster. First pitch 6:40 PM ET.

Game 9
NBCSP/MASN

Phillies @ Nationals

Wednesday, 6:45 PM ET | Nationals Park, Washington, DC

Philadelphia sends veteran Aaron Nola to the mound against Miles Mikolas and the Nationals at Nationals Park in a battle of experienced right-handers. The Phillies at 43-36 are a contender in the National League and arrive as the more dangerous lineup, while Washington at 41-39 has played better than expected and sits right around .500. Nola's command and ability to work deep into games is the Phillies' calling card, and a veteran-versus-veteran matchup tends to move briskly.

Mikolas counters for Washington, leaning on control and pitch sequencing rather than overpowering stuff. Nationals Park plays close to neutral, so the total here projects in a moderate range with two strike-throwers on the mound. First pitch 6:45 PM ET.

Game 10
SNET/SCHN

Astros @ Blue Jays

Wednesday, 7:07 PM ET | Rogers Centre, Toronto, ON

Houston visits Toronto with Mike Burrows on the mound against Trey Yesavage and the Blue Jays at Rogers Centre. Both teams sit just under .500, with the Astros at 38-43 and the Blue Jays at 39-40, making this a tight matchup between two clubs trying to climb back into their respective races. Burrows is being asked to give Houston innings against a Toronto lineup that runs deep through the middle of the order.

Yesavage takes the ball for the Blue Jays at home, where Toronto leans on its lineup depth to grind out at-bats. Rogers Centre's retractable roof and dimensions can play either way depending on conditions, but the matchup of two younger starters keeps the early innings as the swing point. First pitch 7:07 PM ET.

Game 11
FDSNWI/FDSNOH

Brewers @ Reds

Wednesday, 7:10 PM ET | Great American Ball Park, Cincinnati, OH

Milwaukee, owner of the best record in baseball at 48-29, sends Shane Drohan to the mound against Rhett Lowder and the Reds at Great American Ball Park. The Brewers have built their league-leading record on depth and run prevention, while Cincinnati at 37-41 is trying to stay relevant in a crowded NL Central. Drohan, a left-hander, faces the challenge of a hitter-friendly Cincinnati ballpark that can turn modest contact into damage.

Lowder counters for the Reds, and the young right-hander's poise will be tested by a Brewers lineup that has been one of the most consistent in the National League. Great American Ball Park's short porches make the home run the dominant variable, which keeps the total elevated. First pitch 7:10 PM ET.

Game 12 - Marquee
SNLA/BSN

Dodgers @ Twins

Wednesday, 7:40 PM ET | Target Field, Minneapolis, MN

The interleague heavyweight of the night sends Shohei Ohtani to the mound for the Dodgers against Joe Ryan and the Twins at Target Field. Los Angeles owns the best record in the National League at 51-29, and Ohtani has been pitching at a Cy Young level, carrying a 1.47 ERA and a 0.88 WHIP into this start. Minnesota at 38-43 is the underdog, but it counters with one of its own best arms.

Joe Ryan has quietly been excellent for the Twins, sitting at a 2.99 ERA with a 1.00 WHIP and 99 strikeouts across his first sixteen starts. That makes this a genuine pitchers' duel on paper, two top-of-the-rotation arms in a ballpark that plays close to fair. Whichever lineup solves the opposing ace first likely decides it. First pitch 7:40 PM ET.

Game 13
DBACKS.TV/FDSNMW

Diamondbacks @ Cardinals

Wednesday, 7:45 PM ET | Busch Stadium, St. Louis, MO

Arizona visits St. Louis with Mitch Bratt on the mound against Matthew Liberatore and the Cardinals at Busch Stadium. The Cardinals at 42-35 are above .500 and in the NL Central mix, while the Diamondbacks at 40-39 sit right around even. Bratt is an early-career arm being asked to navigate a deep St. Louis lineup on the road, and his command will decide how long he can keep the Cardinals' bats in check.

Liberatore, a left-hander, takes the ball at home for St. Louis, where Busch Stadium's spacious dimensions reward pitchers who keep the ball in the park. The Cardinals' veteran lineup tends to grind out at-bats, which puts a premium on Bratt's pitch efficiency. First pitch 7:45 PM ET.

Game 14
FDSNSO/PADRES.TV

Braves @ Padres

Wednesday, 8:40 PM ET | Petco Park, San Diego, CA

Atlanta visits San Diego with veteran Martin Perez on the mound against JP Sears and the Padres at Petco Park in a matchup of two clubs above .500. The Braves at 48-30 own one of the best records in the National League, and the Padres at 41-37 are firmly in the playoff conversation, so this carries real October implications. Perez, a left-hander, leans on command and changeup sequencing to navigate a deep San Diego lineup.

Sears, also a left-hander, counters for the Padres at home, where Petco Park's pitcher-friendly dimensions have long suppressed run scoring. Two southpaws in a park that favors arms keeps the projected total on the lower side, with the lineups' ability to manufacture runs as the deciding factor. First pitch 8:40 PM ET.

Game 15 - West Coast Nightcap
NBCSCA/NBCSBA

Athletics @ Giants

Wednesday, 9:45 PM ET | Oracle Park, San Francisco, CA

The latest first pitch of the day sends Gage Jump to the mound for the Athletics against Tyler Mahle and the Giants at Oracle Park. The A's at 38-41 and the Giants at 32-46 are both below .500, making this a Bay Area matchup of two clubs looking to close out a long day on a high note. Jump is an early-career arm for the Athletics, and his ability to give length in a pitcher's park is the key to keeping the bullpen fresh.

Mahle counters for San Francisco at home, where Oracle Park's cold-night marine air and deep right-field dimensions are among the most run-suppressing in baseball. That environment favors the under and rewards the starter who throws strikes and lets the park do the work. First pitch 9:45 PM ET.

Game 16 - Doubleheader G2
SNY/MARQUEE

Cubs @ Mets

Wednesday, 7:10 PM ET | Citi Field, New York, NY

The nightcap of the Cubs-Mets doubleheader sends a yet-to-be-named Chicago starter against Sean Manaea and the Mets at Citi Field. With Chicago at 41-37 and New York at 34-44, the Cubs are the more complete club, but a doubleheader rewards the team that gets the most innings from its starters and protects its bullpen. Manaea, a left-hander, gives the Mets a steady veteran presence to anchor the second game of the day.

The Cubs' rotation flexibility in the nightcap will determine how aggressive Chicago can be late, since both bullpens have already worked the opener. Citi Field's pitcher-leaning dimensions again favor the side that avoids the big inning. First pitch 7:10 PM ET in the nightcap.
