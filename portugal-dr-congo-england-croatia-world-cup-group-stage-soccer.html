World Cup Day 7

England Meets Croatia As Portugal And Colombia Open Group K And L Days

Day 7 of the 2026 World Cup pushes Groups K and L forward with four matches across the United States, Canada, and Mexico, from Portugal opening against DR Congo in Houston to England renewing acquaintances with Croatia in Arlington, Ghana facing Panama in Toronto, and Uzbekistan making its World Cup debut against Colombia at the Estadio Azteca. Group L

England vs Croatia

Wednesday, 4:00 PM ET | Arlington, Texas
Group
L
Venue
AT&T Stadium
Broadcast
Fox / Telemundo

The marquee fixture of Day 7 brings England and Croatia together under the roof at AT&T Stadium in Arlington, and the history between these two is impossible to ignore. They met in the 2018 World Cup semifinal in Moscow, where Croatia came from behind to win in extra time and break English hearts, then crossed paths again in the group stage at Euro 2020. England arrives as one of the deepest squads in the tournament, blessed with attacking talent across the front line and the kind of squad depth that lets a manager rotate without a steep drop in quality. Controlling tempo and turning territory into clear chances has long been the English question, and an air-conditioned dome removes the heat variable that hangs over so many of this tournament's daytime matches.

Croatia, as ever, are built around midfield control and tournament savvy, a nation that has punched far above its population size at World Cup after World Cup, reaching the final in 2018 and finishing third in 2022. Luka Modric's generation set a standard of keeping the ball, slowing the game when it suits them, and refusing to be rushed by more athletic opponents. The tactical battle is the one Croatia almost always present: can their midfield dictate the rhythm and frustrate a faster, more direct English side, or does England's depth and pace in the wide areas finally tell over ninety minutes. As an early Group L heavyweight clash in a marquee American venue, it is the match of the day.

Group K

Portugal vs DR Congo

Wednesday, 1:00 PM ET | Houston, Texas
Group
K
Venue
NRG Stadium
Broadcast
Fox / Telemundo

Portugal opens its Group K campaign against DR Congo at NRG Stadium in Houston, and the gap in pedigree is stark. Portugal is one of the most talented squads in the field, a side stacked with attacking quality and European pedigree that lifted the UEFA Nations League again in 2025. The Portuguese have spent years being measured by tournament expectations rather than talent, and a clean, controlled opener against an African debutant in this group is exactly the start they want. The indoor setting in Houston favors a possession-based side that wants to move the ball cleanly and avoid the energy drain of midday heat.

DR Congo reaches the World Cup as one of the tournament's compelling stories, a return to the global stage for a proud football nation that has produced wave after wave of athletic, technically gifted players who populate top European leagues. The Leopards will set up to be organized and hard to break down, leaning on physicality and pace in transition to make Portugal uncomfortable on the counter. The tactical question is whether DR Congo's athleticism and structure can absorb sustained Portuguese pressure and steal a moment the other way, or whether the sheer quality of Portugal's attack proves decisive. For a nation back among the world's best, simply competing on this stage carries real weight.

Group
L

The other half of Group L meets in Toronto, where Ghana faces Panama at BMO Field in a match that looms as a pivotal early swing game in the group. With England and Croatia expected to take the headlines, this is the fixture that often decides who chases the knockout places, and both sides will know how valuable three points are when the heavyweights are lurking. Ghana, a four-time World Cup participant, brings the athleticism and attacking talent that has long defined the Black Stars, a team capable of dangerous, fast-flowing soccer when its front line clicks.

Panama arrives as a CONCACAF side that has steadily grown into a tournament-tested program, organized, physical, and confident on the bigger stage after breaking through to the World Cup. The Panamanians will look to be compact, disciplined, and dangerous on set pieces, the classic blueprint for a smaller nation trying to neutralize a more talented opponent. The tactical battle centers on whether Ghana's pace and individual quality can break down a stubborn Panama block, or whether Panama's organization and physicality can drag the game into the kind of tight, low-event match that suits the underdog. In a soccer-mad Toronto setting, it is a meaningful early test for both.

Uzbekistan vs Colombia

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | Mexico City
Group
K
Venue

The nightcap carries genuine historic weight, as Uzbekistan makes the first World Cup appearance in its history against Colombia at the iconic Estadio Azteca in Mexico City. For Uzbekistan, simply walking out at one of the sport's most storied venues is a milestone decades in the making, the reward for a steady, organized program that finally broke through to the global stage. The White Wolves will lean on structure, work rate, and belief, the tools every debutant needs against more decorated opposition, and the altitude and atmosphere of the Azteca add a layer that can unsettle any opponent.

Colombia represents one of South America's most dangerous sides, a team with the kind of attacking flair and big-match experience that makes it a threat to advance deep into the tournament. Los Cafeteros blend creativity through the middle with pace out wide, and they will expect to control this Group K opener and set the tone for their campaign. The tactical intrigue is whether Uzbekistan's discipline and the unique demands of the Azteca can keep the match tight and turn it into a nervy night for the favorite, or whether Colombia's quality shines through early. For a debutant nation, it is a stage that will be remembered no matter the result.
