World Cup Round of 32 Tuesday: Ivory Coast vs Norway In Dallas, France vs Sweden At MetLife, Mexico vs Ecuador At The Azteca

Three knockout-round ties on Tuesday, June 30. Ivory Coast meet Norway at 1 PM ET at AT&T Stadium in Dallas, France face Sweden at 5 PM ET at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey with the holders a heavy market favorite, and Mexico host Ecuador at 9 PM ET inside the Estadio Azteca. Win or go home, with a Round of 16 place waiting on the other side of ninety minutes.

France vs Sweden

Tuesday, 5:00 PM ET | MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford

The marquee tie of the day sends France into MetLife Stadium as a heavy favorite, with the market pricing Les Bleus around -350 on the moneyline in regulation. That number tells you everything about how the bookmakers read the gap in this matchup. France carry one of the deepest squads in the tournament, a forward line that can win a knockout game in a single moment, and the kind of tournament pedigree that has put them in the late rounds of every recent World Cup. In a one-and-done format, that combination of top-end talent and big-stage experience is exactly what a price that short is built on.

Sweden arrive as the clear underdog, and their route to an upset is the one almost every underdog has to walk in a knockout: defend in a disciplined block, stay compact between the lines, keep the game at zero deep into the second half, and hope a set piece or a transition moment swings it. The Swedes have the physical profile to make that plan credible for stretches, but ninety minutes of holding a French attack at bay is a tall order, and the longer the game stays level the more the pressure tilts toward the favorite. If France score first, the tie can open up quickly; if Sweden reach the hour mark even, the nerves start to matter.

The tactical battle hinges on whether Sweden can take the sting out of France's transition game and force the holders into patient, half-chance buildup against a packed box. France have the individual quality to break a low block, but knockout football has a way of tightening even the best teams, and a single mistake at this stage ends a tournament. Kickoff is 5:00 PM ET at MetLife Stadium, the same venue that will host the final, with the winner advancing to the Round of 16.

Mexico vs Ecuador

Tuesday, 9:00 PM ET | Estadio Azteca, Mexico City

The night cap is a co-host's dream and its biggest pressure test all at once. Mexico walk into the Estadio Azteca for a knockout tie with a nation behind them, and the altitude and noise of Mexico City are a genuine sporting weapon that no neutral venue can replicate. Home advantage in a World Cup is rare and powerful, and El Tri have leaned on the Azteca for decades. The flip side is the weight of expectation: a host nation losing a Round of 32 tie at home is the kind of result that defines a tournament for the wrong reasons, and Mexico will feel every ounce of that.

Ecuador are a difficult, well-organized opponent who have built their identity on defensive structure and a physical, athletic midfield that travels well in South American qualifying and in tournaments. They are comfortable absorbing pressure and springing forward in transition, and they will not be fazed by the occasion the way a less battle-tested side might. La Tri have the profile to frustrate a home crowd, slow the tempo, and turn the tie into the kind of tense, low-event game that favors the underdog the longer it stays close.

For Mexico, the key is patience: use the crowd, move Ecuador's block from side to side, and avoid the frustration that creeps in when a host is expected to win and the goal will not come. For Ecuador, it is discipline and game management, keeping eleven men behind the ball and making Mexico force the issue. Kickoff is 9:00 PM ET at the Estadio Azteca, with a place in the Round of 16 on the line.

Ivory Coast vs Norway

Tuesday, 1:00 PM ET | AT&T Stadium, Arlington

The early kickoff at AT&T Stadium in Dallas is the most evenly matched tie of the three, a meeting of an athletic African side and a Norway team that booked its place in the knockout rounds on the back of a late Erling Haaland goal. Haaland is the headline, and for good reason: a striker of his finishing level changes the math of any knockout game, because Norway do not need to dominate to win when they have a forward who can settle a tie with one chance. The question for the Norwegians is whether they can create enough to feed him against a quick, aggressive Ivorian back line.

Ivory Coast bring pace and power, the kind of athletic profile that can unsettle a European side that prefers a controlled tempo. The Elephants will look to press, win the ball high, and turn the game into an end-to-end contest where their runners can stretch Norway's shape. If they can keep Haaland isolated and force Norway to build patiently, the tie tilts their way; if they leave space in behind, they are handing the most dangerous striker in the field exactly the kind of room he punishes.

This is the classic knockout coin-flip: one side built around a generational goalscorer, the other around collective athleticism and intensity. Expect a cagey opening as both teams respect the stakes, with the game most likely to be decided by a single moment, a set piece, a transition, or a flash of Haaland quality. Kickoff is 1:00 PM ET at AT&T Stadium, the winner moving on to the Round of 16.
