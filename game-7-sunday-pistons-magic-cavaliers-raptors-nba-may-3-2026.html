Game 7 - Series Tied 3-3 - Featured
ABC

Magic @ Pistons

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI

The first of Sunday's two Eastern Conference Game 7s tips at 3:30 PM ET on ABC from Little Caesars Arena. Detroit is a 8.5-point home favorite with the total settled at 202.5, the kind of low-scoring number that reflects a series where both teams have failed to crack 100 points in three of the six games. The Pistons forced this Game 7 with one of the most improbable closing performances in modern playoff history - a road win in Game 6 in Orlando that featured a 24-point comeback, the largest by a road team facing elimination since the 1996-97 season. The structural read on the home spread is the combination of Detroit's regular-season home-court strength and the Pistons' fourth-seed-vs-fifth-seed status, with Orlando coming in as the lower seed that won three games in this series largely on home court.

Cade Cunningham has been the structural piece of the Detroit offense across the six games as the lead-guard creator, and the Pistons' defensive scheme has produced the kind of physical, half-court-grind environment that the 202.5 total reflects. Orlando's offense, anchored by Paolo Banchero and Franz Wagner on the wings, has been at its best when the team gets out in transition - the Magic's pace metrics have been higher than the Pistons' across the series, and Detroit's structural strength has been forcing the game into a half-court, possession-by-possession battle. The Game 6 Pistons defensive shutdown of the Magic offense was the structural template - Detroit held Orlando to a sub-90 score line in the closing minutes and produced the kind of defensive identity that travels into a hostile-environment Game 7. The under has hit in four of six matchups in this series.

The market profile reflects the home-court premium and the Game 6 momentum. Detroit at -8.5 is the larger home spread of the two Sunday Game 7s and reflects a market that priced in both the Pistons' Game 6 closing punch and the Orlando road profile across the regular season. The 202.5 total is the lowest of any Game 7 spread of the entire 2026 first round. The winner advances to face the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference semifinals, the same path that the Celtics-76ers Game 7 winner from Saturday night secured. The 3:30 PM ET tip on ABC is the early-Sunday afternoon anchor of a Game 7 doubleheader that decides the entire bottom half of the East bracket.

Game 7 - Series Tied 3-3 - Featured
NBC / Peacock

Raptors @ Cavaliers

Sunday, 7:30 PM ET | Rocket Arena, Cleveland, OH

The second of Sunday's two Eastern Conference Game 7s tips at 7:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock from Rocket Arena. Cleveland is a 8.5-point home favorite with the moneyline at minus-333, Toronto at plus-260, and the total set at 211.5. The Cavaliers entered the postseason as the East's top seed at 64-18, and the matchup against the seventh-seed Raptors was supposed to be a structural mismatch on paper. Six games in, the series sits at 3-3 with one structural pattern that has defined every result - the home team has won every single game. That trend is the defining piece of the 8.5 number and the structural reason Cleveland is favored despite the back-to-back Toronto wins that brought the series back to even at 3-3.

Donovan Mitchell, Darius Garland, Evan Mobley, and Jarrett Allen anchor the Cleveland core, and the Cavaliers' regular-season offensive rating ranked among the league's top three. The home offensive efficiency at Rocket Arena across the regular season was a meaningful step beyond the road profile, and the Game 7 reset to home court is the structural piece of the Cleveland favorite read. Toronto's offense, anchored by Scottie Barnes (24.2 points, 9.0 assists, 1.8 blocks per game) and RJ Barrett (24.3 points on 50.5 percent shooting), has produced the kind of two-headed scoring profile that has kept the series alive. Barnes has been "playing the best basketball of his career" across the series, and the Game 6 Toronto win at Scotiabank Arena was the structural confirmation that the Raptors can produce a high-output offensive night when the home crowd is on their side.

The injury picture is the structural variable for both teams. Immanuel Quickley is expected to miss Game 7 with a hamstring injury for the Raptors, removing a key ball-handler from the rotation and adding pressure to the Barnes-Barrett creation profile. Brandon Ingram missed Game 6 with a sore right heel and his status for Sunday is uncertain - Ingram returning would give Toronto a third creator and a meaningful boost to the road-environment scoring profile. The Cavaliers enter Game 7 healthy. The 211.5 total reflects two pace-and-shooting offenses meeting in a high-stakes environment, and the Cleveland minus-8.5 home spread is the market's read on the home-team-wins-every-game pattern that has defined the entire series. The winner faces the Boston-Philadelphia Game 7 winner from Saturday in the Eastern Conference semifinals.

Series Context - Western Conference
Off Day

West Semifinals Set - Tip Tuesday

Sunday Off Day | West Bracket Locked

Sunday's NBA slate is exclusively the two Eastern Conference Game 7s. The Western Conference first round is locked - the Lakers closed out the Rockets 4-2 with a 98-78 Game 6 win in Houston on Friday night, advancing to the Western Conference semifinals to face the Oklahoma City Thunder. The Lakers' Game 6 took control in the second quarter, built a 25-point lead in the third, and produced the kind of LeBron James-anchored closeout performance that ensured the series would not become the fifth in NBA history to reach seven games after one team led 3-0. Luka Doncic remained out with a hamstring injury for the entire series, and Houston was without Kevin Durant for Game 6 due to an ankle issue. The closeout-success math for LeBron continues - he has now won the closeout game in every series of his playoff career when his team led the series.

The full Western Conference semifinal bracket is set: Oklahoma City Thunder vs Los Angeles Lakers and Denver Nuggets vs Minnesota Timberwolves. Game 1 of OKC-Lakers is scheduled for Tuesday, May 5, with the 8:30 PM ET tip on NBC and Peacock as the marquee opener of the round. The Lakers enter the second round as the lower seed but with the kind of healthy roster around LeBron James and Anthony Davis that defines the team's championship window. The Thunder enter as the No. 1 overall seed and the defending champions following their 2025 Finals win over the Pacers. The Sunday off day for both Western teams is the rest-and-prep window before Game 1.

The complete first-round bracket also produced the Saturday Boston-Philadelphia Game 7 (3-3 series), making three Game 7s across the 2026 first round - the most of any first-round window since the 2019 playoffs. The four Eastern Conference second-round seeds will be determined by Sunday night, with the Knicks waiting for one Game 7 winner and the surviving second-round opponent waiting for the other. The Pistons-Cavaliers Saturday/Sunday combination has produced the most decisive first-round drama in years, and the Sunday Game 7 doubleheader is the structural anchor of the East semifinal seedings.
