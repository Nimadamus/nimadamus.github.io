Why This Pick Fits The Slate

Atlanta is 31-15 coming into the matchup, while Boston is 19-26. Holmes' surface ERA is not as dominant as the Skenes or Rasmussen profiles, but his .213 opponent average gives the under a usable base when paired with a 3.5 run target. Boston has to create four runs on the road, and the sheet price gives even money rather than forcing a heavy tax.

Market Read

The plus-money tag is important. This is not priced like a certainty; it is a value position where the payout reflects the volatility of a 3.5 team total. The sheet lists 2.5 units, which is appropriately below the 3-unit positions because the pitching profile carries more variance than the premium arms elsewhere on the slate.

The obvious concern is Truist Park run environment and Boston's ability to turn one inning into three or four runs if Holmes walks hitters. The bet still makes sense at +100 because four Red Sox runs is a real threshold, and Holmes has held opponents to a low average through eight starts.
