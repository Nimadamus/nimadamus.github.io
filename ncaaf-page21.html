Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

Two 8-5 teams squaring off in Tampa for the Myrtle Beach Bowl - sounds like a coin flip on paper, right? Not so fast. Memphis and NC State both had up-and-down seasons, but the storylines coming in couldn't be more different. The Tigers wrapped up a solid AAC campaign and head coach Ryan Silverfield has this team playing with an edge. They've got one of the more underrated rushing attacks in the Group of Five, and in bowl season, running the football wins games.

NC State's had the more turbulent year. The Wolfpack started hot but hit a wall in November, dropping some winnable games down the stretch. Dave Doeren's squad has talent, especially on the defensive line, but consistency has been their Achilles heel all season. When they're on, they can compete with anyone. When they're not, they look like a team that forgot how to tackle.

Here's what makes this game interesting: both teams need a win to build momentum into 2026. Memphis wants to prove the AAC can hang with Power Four programs, while NC State needs something positive after a frustrating finish. The neutral site in Tampa favors neither team, so this one comes down to who prepared better during bowl week. Expect a physical, low-scoring affair where turnovers decide the outcome.

Cougars @ Aggies

2025-12-22T19:00Z | Albertsons Stadium | ESPN
WSU: 7-6 | USU: 6-7

Washington State's in a weird spot right now. When Oregon and Washington bolted for the Big Ten, the Cougars got left behind in a hollowed-out Pac-12. But you know what? This team played with a chip on their shoulder all year because of it. Jake Dickert has done an incredible job keeping this program competitive despite all the conference realignment chaos swirling around them. At 7-6, they exceeded expectations.

Utah State comes in at 6-7 - not exactly a banner year in Logan. The Aggies struggled with consistency on both sides of the ball, and their defense gave up way too many big plays. Their rushing attack has been solid when healthy, but injuries hampered them down the stretch. Head coach Blake Anderson has some questions to answer about where this program is headed.

The Famous Idaho Potato Bowl in Boise is basically a home game for Utah State, which gives them a slight edge in terms of familiarity and travel. But Washington State's been road warriors all season - they know how to win in hostile environments. This feels like a game where the team with the better quarterback play wins, and right now that's the Cougars. Wazzu's air raid system should give Utah State's secondary fits if they can protect the quarterback.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

The SWAC and MEAC Championship Game brings together two powerhouse HBCU programs in Atlanta's Mercedes-Benz Stadium. South Carolina State comes in at 10-3 as MEAC champions, and this program has been building toward a moment like this for years. Head coach Buddy Pough has turned the Bulldogs into one of the most consistent FCS programs in the country, and their physical, run-heavy offense is exactly what you want in a championship environment.

Prairie View A&M answered their own bell by winning the SWAC at 10-4. The Panthers have explosive playmakers on offense and a defense that can take the ball away when it matters. This is a program on the rise, and they're looking to prove that the SWAC is the premier HBCU conference in the country. Their quarterback has been electric all season, making plays with both his arm and his legs.

Here's what makes this game special beyond the Xs and Os - it's a celebration of HBCU football on a national stage. The atmosphere in Mercedes-Benz Stadium will be incredible, with bands, tradition, and two proud fan bases bringing the energy. On the field, expect South Carolina State to try to control the clock with their ground game while Prairie View A&M looks to create explosive plays. Whoever wins the turnover battle probably wins the game.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

Now this is a fascinating matchup. Boise State at 9-5 had a solid Mountain West campaign, but the Broncos feel like they left some wins on the table. Spencer Danielson's team has been explosive at times, especially with Ashton Jeanty in the backfield - the kid is a legitimate NFL prospect who can take over games single-handedly. When Jeanty gets going, Boise State is as dangerous as any team in the country.

Washington's transition to the Big Ten at 9-4 had its bumps, but overall this was a successful first year in their new conference. The Huskies lost some key pieces from last year's national championship runner-up team, but Jedd Fisch has done a nice job retooling the roster. Their defense has been the strength of the team, and they've shown they can slow down good offenses when they're locked in.

The LA Bowl at SoFi Stadium is a neutral site that slightly favors Washington in terms of travel and fan base. But here's the thing - Boise State lives for these moments. The blue turf might be their home, but the Broncos have a history of showing up in bowl games against bigger programs. The key matchup to watch is Jeanty versus Washington's front seven. If the Huskies can contain him, they win. If he breaks loose, all bets are off.

Trojans @ Gamecocks

2025-12-17T02:00Z | Cramton Bowl | ESPN
TROY: 8-6 | JVST: 9-5

The Cramton Bowl brings together two Sun Belt rivals in what should be a chippy, physical affair. Troy at 8-6 had a respectable season under Jon Sumrall, and the Trojans always seem to play their best football when the calendar turns to December. They've got a balanced offense that can run the ball and take shots downfield, and their defense has been stingy in the red zone all year.

Jacksonville State comes in at 9-5 after making real noise in just their second FBS season. Rich Rodriguez has this program moving in the right direction, and the Gamecocks have proven they belong at this level. Their quarterback has been making plays all season, and they've got playmakers at receiver who can stretch the field. This is a team that believes they should have won more games.

Here's the spicy subplot - these are geographic rivals, and the Cramton Bowl in Montgomery, Alabama gives Troy a slight home-field edge. The Trojans recruit this area heavily, and their fans will travel. But Jacksonville State's been building their own following, and they won't be intimidated. Expect a tight game that comes down to the fourth quarter. Turnovers and special teams could easily swing this one either way.

Monarchs @ Bulls

2025-12-17T22:00Z | Camping World Stadium | ESPN
ODU: 10-3 | USF: 9-4

Old Dominion at 10-3 is one of the feel-good stories of the college football season. Ricky Rahne has transformed this program into a legitimate Sun Belt contender, and the Monarchs have been beating quality opponents all year. Their defense has been suffocating - they don't give up easy yards, and they force turnovers at a high rate. This is a team that believes it deserves more national respect.

South Florida at 9-4 had a bounce-back year under Alex Golesh. The Bulls' offense has been exciting to watch, with a quarterback who can extend plays and receivers who win 50-50 balls. They've been especially dangerous at home, and while Camping World Stadium is technically neutral, it's close enough to Tampa that USF fans will pack the place.

The Gasparilla Bowl is always a fun atmosphere, and this matchup has the potential to be an offensive shootout. Old Dominion wants to control the clock and play physical defense, while South Florida wants to get into a track meet. Which style wins out probably determines the game. The Monarchs have shown they can win ugly, but the Bulls have the athletes to make plays if this game opens up. Should be entertaining either way.

Cajuns @ Hens

2025-12-18T01:40Z | Hancock Whitney Stadium | ESPN
UL: 6-7 | DEL: 7-6

Louisiana at 6-7 isn't where the Ragin' Cajuns expected to be this season. After years of dominance in the Sun Belt under Billy Napier, the program has been searching for its identity under Michael Desormeaux. The talent is still there - their skill position players can make plays in space - but they haven't put it all together consistently. This bowl game is a chance to end the year on a positive note and build momentum.

Delaware at 7-6 is a CAA program that knows how to win. The Blue Hens have a proud FCS tradition, and they've been competitive all season despite playing a brutal schedule. Their run game is physical and relentless - they wear you down in the second half. This is a team that doesn't beat itself, which is half the battle in bowl games.

This is a unique FBS versus FCS matchup that could go either way. Louisiana has the talent advantage on paper, but Delaware has the coaching and discipline to hang around. The Blue Hens won't be intimidated - they've seen good football all year. If Louisiana comes in flat or unfocused, they're going to be in for a long afternoon. The Cajuns need to assert their physicality early and not let Delaware hang around.

Bears @ Wolves

2025-12-19T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN2
MOST: 7-6 | ARST: 7-6

The Xbox Bowl at the Dallas Cowboys' practice facility brings together two 7-6 teams looking to finish the season on a high note. Missouri State made the jump to FBS this year and went through the growing pains you'd expect. The Bears showed flashes of what they can be, but consistency was elusive. Their defense improved as the season went on, and they've got some young talent that should develop nicely.

Arkansas State at 7-6 had a solid but unspectacular year. The Red Wolves play in a tough Sun Belt conference and held their own, but they couldn't break through against the league's top teams. Their offense can put up points when the passing game is clicking, but they've been boom or bust at times. Butch Jones has this program in a decent spot, but they need a statement win.

Playing at The Star in Frisco gives this game a unique feel - it's NFL-caliber facilities for two mid-major programs. The neutral site should mean even crowds, though Arkansas State probably has the slight edge in terms of travel distance. This is a game where motivation matters more than anything. Whoever wants to be there more, whoever prepared harder during bowl week, that's who wins. Both teams have something to prove.

Tide @ Sooners

2025-12-20T01:00Z | Memorial Stadium (Norman, OK) | ABC, ESPN
ALA: 11-3 | OU: 10-3

This is the heavyweight showdown everyone's been waiting for. Alabama at 11-3 is in the College Football Playoff, and they're heading to Norman to face Oklahoma in the first round. Kalen DeBoer's first year in Tuscaloosa has had its ups and downs - three losses is more than Alabama fans are used to - but this team is still loaded with NFL talent. The offensive line has been inconsistent, but the skill position players are elite. When Alabama plays clean football, they're nearly impossible to stop.

Oklahoma at 10-3 proved a lot of doubters wrong this season. The Sooners struggled in year one of the SEC, but they've grown up over the course of the year. Brent Venables finally has his defense playing the way he wants, and their quarterback has been clutch in big moments. Playing at Memorial Stadium gives Oklahoma a massive advantage - that crowd is going to be deafening, especially for a playoff game.

Here's the elephant in the room: Alabama hasn't played their best football lately. The three losses hurt, and there are questions about whether this team peaked too early. Oklahoma, on the other hand, is playing their best ball right now. The Sooners have momentum, home field, and a chip on their shoulder from being counted out. This has upset potential written all over it. The atmosphere in Norman is going to be absolutely electric.

Owls @ Broncos

2025-12-19T16:00Z | Brooks Stadium (SC) | ESPN
KENN: 10-4 | WMU: 10-4

The Myrtle Beach Bowl's second game features two teams with identical 10-4 records who are both playing with house money. Kennesaw State made the jump from FCS just last year, and getting to 10 wins this quickly is remarkable. Brian Bohannon has built a culture of toughness and physical football, and the Owls don't back down from anyone. Their triple-option offense is unique and can absolutely frustrate opponents who don't prepare for it properly.

Western Michigan at 10-4 is coming off a strong MAC season. The Broncos have playmakers on the perimeter and a defense that's been getting key stops all year. They've won close games, they've won blowouts - this is a versatile team that finds ways to get it done. Lance Taylor has this program believing they can beat anybody on any given Saturday.

The stylistic contrast here is fascinating. Kennesaw State wants to slow the game down, control the clock, and pound the ball with their option attack. Western Michigan wants to spread you out and use their speed in space. Whoever dictates the tempo probably wins this game. If KSU can keep the chains moving on the ground, Western Michigan's athletes are standing on the sideline. But if the Broncos can force three-and-outs and get into a rhythm, their talent should shine through.

Dukes @ Ducks

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Autzen Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
JMU: 12-2 | ORE: 12-1

James Madison at 12-2 has been the darling of the college football world all season. The Dukes made the leap from FCS to FBS and immediately became one of the best stories in the sport. They've beaten good teams, they've dominated bad teams, and they've done it with a swagger that's impossible not to love. Curt Cignetti had this program humming, and even after he left for Indiana, they haven't skipped a beat. This is a team that genuinely believes they belong on any field.

Oregon at 12-1 is a legitimate national championship contender. Dan Lanning has the Ducks playing elite football on both sides of the ball, and Autzen Stadium is one of the toughest environments in the country. Their offense is explosive and their defense has been championship-caliber. This is a team with dreams of winning it all, and they're not about to overlook anyone in the playoff.

Here's where it gets interesting: James Madison isn't intimidated by this stage. They've been winning big games all year, and their players genuinely believe they can hang with anyone. Oregon is more talented, sure, but the Dukes have something to prove on the national stage. Autzen will be rocking, but JMU's been in hostile environments before. Don't be shocked if this one is closer than expected early - though Oregon's depth and talent should eventually take over.

Wave @ Rebels

2025-12-20T20:30Z | Vaught-Hemingway Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
TULN: 11-3 | MISS: 12-1

Tulane at 11-3 has quietly become one of the best Group of Five programs in the country. Jon Sumrall took over for Willie Fritz and hasn't missed a beat - the Green Wave won the AAC and earned a playoff spot. Their offense is balanced and efficient, and their defense has been making plays all season. This is a program that's built something real in New Orleans, and they're hungry to prove they belong on the big stage.

Ole Miss at 12-1 is a juggernaut. Lane Kiffin has the Rebels rolling, and Vaught-Hemingway Stadium is going to be absolutely insane for this playoff game. Their offense is explosive - they can score from anywhere on the field at any time. The defense has been stout against the run, though they've been vulnerable to tempo at times. This is a team that expects to win the national championship, and anything less would be a disappointment.

Here's the brutal truth: Tulane is a very good football team, but Ole Miss is on another level. The Rebels have more talent at every position group, and Kiffin has had weeks to game plan for this matchup. Tulane's best chance is to control the clock, limit possessions, and hope for some turnovers. But that's a lot of things that need to go right against a team that's been dominant all year. The Green Wave will compete, but this should be Ole Miss's game to lose.

Hurricanes @ Aggies

2025-12-20T17:00Z | Kyle Field | ABC, ESPN
MIA: 11-2 | TA&M: 11-2

Miami at 11-2 is back, and The U is playing meaningful football in December again. Mario Cristobal took some heat early in his tenure, but this team has come together at the right time. Cam Ward has been electric at quarterback - he's a Heisman finalist for a reason. The offense can score with anyone, and when the defense shows up, the Hurricanes are dangerous. They've waited a long time for a game like this.

Texas A&M at 11-2 has had their own remarkable turnaround. Mike Elko's first year in College Station exceeded all expectations, and Kyle Field is going to be absolutely rocking for this playoff game. The Aggies' defense has been the story all year - they're physical, they fly to the ball, and they don't give up easy points. Their offense has been good enough, and that's all you need when your defense plays like this.

This is a clash of styles that should be incredible to watch. Miami wants to spread you out and let Cam Ward make plays. Texas A&M wants to play physical, win the line of scrimmage, and control the tempo. Kyle Field's 12th Man is a legitimate factor - that crowd is going to be as loud as any stadium in the country. The Hurricanes will need to handle the road environment and find ways to create explosive plays. If A&M turns this into a rock fight, that favors the home team.

Grizzlies @ Bobcats

2025-12-20T21:00Z | Bobcat Stadium (MT) | ABC
MONT: 13-2 | MTST: 13-2

This is the FCS semifinal matchup everyone wanted to see. Montana at 13-2 and Montana State at 13-2 - two bitter rivals with identical records meeting with a trip to the national championship on the line. The Brawl of the Wild has been one of the best rivalries in college football for decades, and this year it reaches a new level. Montana has a proud tradition and a fan base that travels incredibly well. They've been battle-tested all year and play physical, old-school football.

Montana State has home field advantage at Bobcat Stadium, and they're going to need every bit of it. The Bobcats have been dominant at home all season, and their defense has been absolutely suffocating. They've got playmakers on both sides of the ball and a coaching staff that's prepared for everything Montana might throw at them. This is the biggest game in program history, and they know it.

Here's what makes this so special - these teams know each other intimately. They recruit the same players, they play every year, and there's genuine bad blood between the programs. Nothing in this game will be a surprise. It's going to come down to execution, turnovers, and which team handles the pressure better. Bobcat Stadium in December is brutal conditions, and the team that's more physical in the trenches is going to advance. This is what FCS football is all about.

Redbirds @ Wildcats

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Villanova Stadium | ESPN2
ILST: 12-4 | VILL: 12-3

The other FCS semifinal brings Illinois State at 12-4 to Villanova at 12-3. The Redbirds have had a remarkable season under Brock Spack, who's built this program into a consistent contender. Illinois State plays physical, run-first football and they don't make mistakes. They've won playoff games before, and this team knows what it takes to advance in the bracket. Their defense has been flying around making plays all postseason.

Villanova at 12-3 is playing their best football of the year at the right time. The Wildcats have the athleticism and speed to give opponents problems, and their quarterback has been making clutch throws when it matters. Playing at home gives them a significant advantage - Villanova Stadium gets loud, and the Wildcats feed off that energy. This is a program with championship aspirations, and they're playing like it.

This matchup is a clash of cultures. Illinois State wants to line up, run the football, and impose their will. Villanova wants to spread the field and use their speed in space. The weather in late December could favor the Redbirds' physical style, but Villanova has the athletes to make plays regardless of conditions. Home field is a real factor here - Villanova hasn't lost at home all year. Expect a tight, competitive game that could come down to the final possession.
