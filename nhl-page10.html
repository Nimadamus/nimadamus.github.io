Panthers @ Lightning

7:00 PM ET | Benchmark International Arena | Scripps Sports
ML: TB -130 | O/U: 5.5 | FLA: 16-13-2 | TB: 18-11-3

The Florida Panthers (16-13-2) travel across the state for a rivalry showdown with the Tampa Bay Lightning (18-11-3). This I-4 battle has intensified over the years, with Florida winning back-to-back Stanley Cups in 2024 and 2025. The Panthers have proven themselves as the class of the Atlantic Division when healthy, though their record doesn't fully reflect their talent level this season.

Tampa Bay has been solid at home and enters with a slight edge in the standings. The Lightning still possess dangerous offensive weapons and championship pedigree. Steven Stamkos remains a threat, and their power play can change games quickly. This is always a physical, emotional affair when these two Florida clubs meet.

Ducks @ Rangers

7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden | ESPN+
ML: NYR -120 | O/U: 6.5 | ANA: 19-12-1 | NYR: 16-13-4

Anaheim (19-12-1) has been one of the season's surprise teams, and they bring their strong road record into Madison Square Garden to face the Rangers (16-13-4). The Ducks' rebuild has accelerated faster than expected, with their young core playing beyond their years. Their goaltending has been solid, and the defensive structure under their coaching staff has been impressive.

New York has underperformed expectations but remains dangerous at MSG. The Rangers have elite goaltending with Igor Shesterkin and skilled forwards who can fill the net when clicking. The high total suggests an open, offensive game where both teams will trade chances. Anaheim's confidence is high, but the Rangers need a statement win at home.

Senators @ Jets

8:00 PM ET | Canada Life Centre | ESPN+
ML: WPG -130 | O/U: 5.5 | OTT: 14-13-4 | WPG: 15-15-1

Ottawa (14-13-4) visits Winnipeg (15-15-1) in a matchup between two Canadian teams looking to find consistency. The Senators have talented young players like Tim Stutzle and Brady Tkachuk, but putting together complete games has been a challenge. Ottawa's offense can be explosive but their defensive coverage remains a work in progress.

Winnipeg has dealt with roster turnover and injuries but remains competitive in the Central Division. The Jets' home ice advantage at Canada Life Centre is significant, and their physical style of play can wear down opponents. Kyle Connor continues to be one of the league's most underrated scorers. Expect a tightly contested affair with both teams fighting for crucial points.

Predators @ Blues

8:00 PM ET | Enterprise Center | ESPN+
ML: STL -115 | O/U: 5.5 | NSH: 12-15-4 | STL: 12-14-7

Nashville (12-15-4) faces St. Louis (12-14-7) in a Central Division matchup between two struggling teams. The Predators have been disappointing this season despite offseason additions. Their offense has been inconsistent, and the defense that used to be their calling card has shown cracks. Filip Forsberg needs more support to shoulder the scoring burden.

The Blues have been in a similar boat, hovering around .500 all season with no clear identity. St. Louis has veteran leadership and championship experience from their 2019 Cup run, but the roster is in transition. This is a game where neither team can afford to lose ground in the playoff race. The low total reflects both teams' scoring struggles.

Kings @ Stars

8:30 PM ET | American Airlines Center | ESPN+
ML: DAL -130 | O/U: 5.5 | LA: 14-8-9 | DAL: 21-7-5

The Los Angeles Kings (14-8-9) head to Dallas to face the Western Conference-leading Stars (21-7-5). LA has been competitive all season with a blend of veterans and emerging young talent. Their overtime record speaks to their ability to hang in tight games, though converting more regulation wins would boost their playoff positioning. Anze Kopitar continues to defy age with his two-way excellence.

Dallas has established themselves as the team to beat in the West with their dominant start. The Stars have elite goaltending, a deep defensive corps, and enough scoring to win in different ways. Jason Robertson and Roope Hintz lead a dangerous forward group, while Jake Oettinger has been sensational between the pipes. This is a potential playoff preview between two contenders.
