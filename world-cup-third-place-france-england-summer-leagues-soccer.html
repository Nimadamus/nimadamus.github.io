A World Cup Bronze Final In Miami Headlines A Global Soccer Saturday

World Cup Third Place, Liga MX, Eliteserien and the NWSL
Published July 18, 2026

The biggest game on the planet this weekend is the one nobody wanted to be in. France and England meet in Miami on Saturday to decide third place at the World Cup, one day before the final, and around that bronze match sits a full global Saturday: two of Mexico's giants opening their Apertura in Guadalajara, a Norwegian heavyweight hosting a side that cannot stop trading goals, and a rebuilt Gotham defence putting its numbers on the line in New York. FIFA World Cup - Third-Place Play-off
World Cup

England @ France

Saturday, 5:00 PM ET | Hard Rock Stadium, Miami Gardens, FL

Nobody grows up dreaming about the third-place game. France and England both arrived at this World Cup believing they would still be playing on Sunday, and instead they meet on Saturday afternoon in Miami to settle bronze, one day before the final kicks off up the coast at MetLife Stadium. It is the match that exists because the tournament needs somewhere to put two teams that came within a game of the summit and fell.

The names carry it. Kylian Mbappe leads a France side that has spent the last decade treating deep World Cup runs as the baseline, and Jude Bellingham anchors an England group that keeps knocking on this door. That is the strange tension of a bronze final: the talent on the pitch is worth a knockout tie, but the stakes are a medal that neither side circled in January. How much either manager empties the tank after a semifinal defeat is the whole question.

Hard Rock Stadium has been one of the loud venues of this tournament, and a neutral crowd in Miami will treat this like an event regardless of the trophy that is not on offer. Expect two teams that can still hurt anyone if they choose to, and a genuine unknown about how much either wants to. The final at MetLife the next day is the headline. This is the last dance for two sides that will spend four years thinking about the game before this one.

Liga MX Apertura 2026 - Matchday Early
Liga MX

Toluca @ Guadalajara

Saturday, 9:07 PM ET | Estadio Akron, Guadalajara

Liga MX only started its Apertura on July 14, so this is early-season football with the table still forming, but the fixture list did the neutrals a favour by pairing two of the country's biggest names in the first week. Chivas open at home at Estadio Akron against a Toluca side that has spent recent seasons among the league's benchmark clubs.

Guadalajara carry a weight that has nothing to do with the standings. They are the all-Mexican club, the one that refuses to sign foreign players, and the Akron is one of the loudest buildings in the league on a Saturday night. When they start a tournament well the whole city leans in, and an opening home date against a heavyweight is exactly the kind of night that sets a tone.

Toluca are the more settled proposition on paper, the kind of side that treats the early rounds as a chance to bank points before the schedule tightens. Two clubs this size meeting before the table has any shape is a genuine test of who is further along in preseason, and the away side walking into the Akron for the opener is the version of this fixture that carries the most edge.

Eliteserien - Norway
Eliteserien

SK Brann @ Molde

Saturday | Aker Stadion, Molde

The Eliteserien plays right through the Norwegian summer, and this is a proper one. Molde sit fifth on 20 points at 6-2-4, with 20 goals scored and 15 conceded, the tidy home-heavy profile that has kept them near the top of this league for a decade. Aker Stadion has long been one of the harder trips in the country.

SK Brann arrive as the more chaotic watch. They are ninth on 16 points at 5-1-7, but the eye-catching number is at the other end: 26 goals scored, more than Molde, against 21 conceded. Brann have drawn once all season, which tells you how they play, every match tipping decisively one way or the other rather than settling into a stalemate.

That contrast is the match. Molde defend and control at home; Brann trade blows and rarely leave with a point split. A side that scores freely but leaks walking into one of the league's stingier home teams is the sort of tactical mismatch that usually produces goals at one end or the other, and Brann's complete absence of draws suggests this will not be a cagey afternoon in Molde.

NWSL - League Table
NWSL

Seattle Reign @ Gotham FC

Saturday | Icahn Stadium, New York, NY

Gotham FC have quietly built the most convincing defence in the NWSL this season. They sit fourth on 27 points at 8-3-3, and the number that matters is eight goals conceded across fourteen matches. Sixteen scored, eight allowed, a plus-eight difference built almost entirely on not giving anything away, and they host this one in New York.

Seattle Reign are the opposite story. They are tenth on 17 points at 5-2-6, with 13 goals scored and 16 conceded, a team that has struggled to string results together and now travels cross-country to face the league's meanest back line. A minus-three difference against a side that has conceded eight all year is a difficult brief.

The whole match is legible in the defensive numbers. Seattle have to find a way through a Gotham unit that simply does not concede, and they have not scored freely enough this season to suggest they will crack it easily on the road. Gotham do not need to be spectacular to win these; they need to keep doing the one thing they have done better than anyone, and the Reign are exactly the kind of visitor that plays into it.
