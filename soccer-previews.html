World Cup Final Matchday: The United States Closes Group Play In Los Angeles

The 2026 World Cup reaches the decisive final matchday of group play, and the headline belongs to the host nation, with the United States facing Turkiye at SoFi Stadium. Germany meets Ecuador in New Jersey, the Netherlands takes on Tunisia in Kansas City, Japan plays Sweden in Arlington, and Curacao faces Ivory Coast in Philadelphia. Kickoff times, venues, and storylines for all five below. Analysis and breakdown only, no picks.

United States vs Turkiye

Thursday, 10:00 PM ET | Inglewood, CA
Stage
Group D
Kickoff
10:00 PM ET
Venue
SoFi Stadium

Center stage on the final group matchday belongs to the match that matters most to the home crowd, as the United States closes its group at SoFi Stadium against Turkiye. For a host nation, the final group game is where tournaments are made or unmade, and the Americans will be playing in front of a partisan Los Angeles crowd that expects a result to carry into the knockout rounds. The blueprint for the United States is the one that has defined its best performances: high energy, aggressive pressing, and using the pace of its attacking players to stretch the game in transition.

Turkiye arrives as a technically gifted, possession-comfortable European side that can punish any opponent that leaves space between the lines. The Turkish midfield is the key battleground, and how the United States manages tempo and protects its back line against a side that likes to build patiently will define the night. With a late kickoff under the SoFi roof and the home crowd at full voice, this is the signature occasion of the day and the match every American fan will be watching.

Group E

Germany vs Ecuador

Thursday, 4:00 PM ET | East Rutherford, NJ
Stage
Group E
Kickoff
4:00 PM ET
Venue
MetLife Stadium

One of the most decorated programs in World Cup history closes Group E at MetLife Stadium, where Germany faces a stubborn Ecuador side. The Germans bring the tactical structure, depth, and tournament pedigree that have defined them across generations, and on a final matchday they will be intent on controlling possession and dictating the rhythm to secure their path into the knockout rounds. The test for Germany is the familiar one: efficiency in the final third against a side that defends in numbers.

Ecuador is exactly that kind of opponent, a well-drilled South American team built on defensive organization and a willingness to grind out results. The Ecuadorians are rarely easy to break down and carry a genuine counterattacking threat, which means Germany cannot pour forward without managing the risk behind the ball. With both teams aware of the stakes on the closing day of group play, this profiles as a patient, tactical contest where a single set piece or defensive lapse could decide which direction the group breaks.

Group F

Netherlands vs Tunisia

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Kansas City, MO
Stage
Group F
Kickoff
7:00 PM ET
Venue
Arrowhead Stadium

In Kansas City, the Netherlands wraps up Group F at Arrowhead Stadium against Tunisia in a match that carries real weight for the Dutch. The Netherlands carry the European pedigree and individual quality that make them a perennial threat to go deep, a program built on positional play and fluid attacking movement. On the final matchday, the Dutch will look to be clinical and assert control early against a side that specializes in frustrating more fancied opponents.

Tunisia represents North Africa with a team built on defensive solidity and tournament experience, the kind of side that sits deep, stays compact, and looks to nick a goal on the counter or from a set piece. The Carthage Eagles are a tricky out, and against a Dutch team that needs a positive result, their discipline and organization could make for a tense evening in Kansas City. The subplot is whether the Netherlands can break down a stubborn block before the game tightens late.

Group F

Japan vs Sweden

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Arlington, TX
Stage
Group F
Kickoff
7:00 PM ET
Venue
AT&T Stadium

The other Group F fixture runs simultaneously at AT&T Stadium, where Japan meets Sweden in a clash of contrasting styles. Japan has become one of the most respected national teams outside the traditional powers, a side that presses with discipline, moves the ball quickly, and has repeatedly troubled European heavyweights on the biggest stage. The Samurai Blue do not sit back and absorb; they compete for the ball and play through the lines, which makes them dangerous on a decisive matchday.

Sweden brings the physical, organized, set-piece-oriented style that has long served Scandinavian sides well in major tournaments, a team that defends in numbers and looks to be ruthless from dead-ball situations and on the break. In a domed stadium that removes the elements, this becomes a test of Japan's quick passing against Sweden's structure and aerial threat. With both groups settling on the same night, the result here is tied to what happens in Kansas City, and both teams know every goal could matter for the final standings.

Group E

Curacao vs Ivory Coast

Thursday, 4:00 PM ET | Philadelphia, PA
Stage
Group E
Kickoff
4:00 PM ET
Venue

Philadelphia hosts the other Group E fixture as Curacao closes one of the great underdog stories of the 2026 finals against Ivory Coast. Simply reaching the World Cup is a landmark for the tiny Caribbean nation, and the final matchday gives Curacao a chance to leave its mark with a compact, disciplined defensive block and the hope of springing forward on the counter. For a smaller side facing more established opposition, that resilient blueprint is the path to a memorable result.

Ivory Coast brings the athleticism and attacking flair that have long defined West African football, a physical, fast, direct team capable of turning a single moment of individual brilliance into a goal. In a group anchored by Germany, the Ivorians will treat this as a match they need to control to shape their own path through the closing day. With both Group E games kicking off at the same time, the result in Philadelphia is directly linked to events at MetLife Stadium, adding a layer of scoreboard-watching tension to the afternoon.
