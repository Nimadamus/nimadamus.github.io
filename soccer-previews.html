This is genuinely historic. The first-ever London derby in the UEFA Women's Champions League, and it arrives at the quarterfinal stage with both sides playing some of the best football in Europe. Arsenal are the defending champions after their extraordinary run last season, and they've been absolutely relentless in the group stage this time around, winning five of six matches and topping their group with an air of inevitability that only champions carry.

Chelsea, though, are no strangers to the biggest occasions. Sonia Bompastor's side have been building toward exactly this kind of fixture since their squad overhaul in the summer, and their away form in Europe has been outstanding. They dismantled Real Madrid in the Round of 16 with a level of tactical discipline that suggested a team peaking at precisely the right time. Arsenal's home record in Europe is formidable, losing just once in their last 14 home Champions League matches. But Chelsea's counter-attacking threat, particularly through Lauren James and Catarina Macario, is tailor-made for exploiting the high defensive line that Jonas Eidevall's side favor.

With the second leg still to come at Stamford Bridge, the tactical chess match here will be fascinating. Neither side can afford to be overly aggressive, but neither wants to go into the return fixture at a disadvantage. Expect a cagey opening 20 minutes before the quality on both sides forces the game open. This has all the makings of a classic.
