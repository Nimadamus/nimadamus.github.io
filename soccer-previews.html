World Cup - Friday, June 19

The USMNT Chases A Knockout Berth Against Australia, And Brazil Meets Haiti In Group C

A four-match Friday at the 2026 World Cup is headlined by the United States facing Australia for control of Group D, Scotland and Morocco meeting in a tense Group C clash, Brazil taking on Haiti, and Turkey battling Paraguay. Group standings, betting lines and storylines below, analysis only.

Marquee Matchup
FOX

USA vs Australia

Friday, 3:00 PM ET | Lumen Field, Seattle | Group D

The marquee on the Friday slate is the one every American is watching, as the United States meets Australia at Lumen Field with a place in the knockout rounds on the line. The USMNT opened its home World Cup in style, thumping Paraguay 4-1 on Matchday 1 to seize early control of Group D, and a result here would all but guarantee a spot in the round of 32. Playing in front of a raucous Seattle crowd, the hosts are firmly in the driver's seat.

Australia arrives with belief after a stunning result of its own. The Socceroos shocked Turkey 2-0 in their opener, a defensively disciplined performance that left the group wide open behind the United States. That win means Australia controls its own destiny too, and a point here would keep them in strong position to advance. Their organized, physical approach is exactly the kind of stubborn opponent that can frustrate a favorite.

The betting market sees the United States as a clear favorite, with the hosts priced around -160 on the 90-minute line, Australia near +410, and the draw around +320. The total sits at 2.5 goals, a reflection of the quality on the American side and the defensive resilience the Socceroos showed against Turkey. The story is straightforward: the USMNT can clinch on home soil, while Australia aims to spoil the party and book its own ticket to the knockouts.

Group C Battle
FS1

Scotland vs Morocco

Friday, 6:00 PM ET | Gillette Stadium, Foxborough | Group C

One of the more intriguing Group C matchups sends Scotland against Morocco at Gillette Stadium, with both sides eyeing a crucial three points. Scotland enters in a strong position after a 1-0 win over Haiti on Matchday 1 lifted them to the top of the group, an outstanding start for a side that landed in a brutal draw alongside Brazil and Morocco. The Tartan Army will travel well to Foxborough, and a positive result here would put Scotland on the brink of a historic advancement.

Morocco, one of the tournament's dark horses, opened with a tense 1-1 draw against Brazil, a result that announced their quality against the group's heavyweight. The Atlas Lions are organized, technically gifted, and dangerous on the counter, and they will view this as a must-win to keep pace at the top of the group. With Brazil expected to be the favorite to advance, the race for the second knockout spot may well run through this fixture.

The market reflects the tightness, with the betting odds to win Group C showing Brazil at -165, Morocco at +210, and Scotland at +700, a sign that this head-to-head could decide which of the two challengers pushes Brazil. Both teams play with discipline and intensity, and with so much at stake so early, expect a cagey, physical contest where a single moment may settle it.

Tournament Favorite
FOX

Brazil vs Haiti

Friday, 8:30 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field, Philadelphia | Group C

One of the tournament's heavyweights takes the stage in Philadelphia as Brazil, the +900 co-favorite to lift the trophy, faces Haiti at Lincoln Financial Field. The Selecao were held to a 1-1 draw by Morocco in their opener, an uncharacteristic result that leaves them needing a convincing performance to reassert their status atop Group C. With the talent at their disposal, Brazil will be expected to control this match from start to finish.

Haiti arrive as the group's clear outsider, listed at extreme odds of +25000 to win Group C, and they fell 1-0 to Scotland in their first match. Reaching the World Cup is an achievement in itself for the Caribbean side, and they will look to defend deep, stay compact, and frustrate one of the most gifted attacking nations on the planet. For Haiti, a strong defensive showing against Brazil would be a moral victory in a daunting group.

The contrast in quality is stark, and Brazil's blend of pace, flair, and individual brilliance makes them heavy favorites to bounce back from the Morocco draw. The story here is whether the Selecao can find the fluency that was missing on Matchday 1, and whether Haiti's resolute defending can keep the scoreline respectable against a side desperate to make a statement.

Group D Survival
FS1

Turkey vs Paraguay

Friday, 11:00 PM ET | Levi's Stadium, Santa Clara | Group D

The Friday nightcap carries genuine desperation, as Turkey and Paraguay meet at Levi's Stadium with both sides needing a result to keep their knockout hopes alive. Both opened Group D with defeats, Turkey falling 2-0 to Australia and Paraguay losing 4-1 to the United States, leaving each side staring at elimination if they slip up again. A loss for either could end their tournament before the final round of group matches.

Turkey, listed at +800 to win Group D, will look to rediscover the attacking quality that earned them their place at the tournament after a flat opening performance against the Socceroos. Paraguay, the longest shot in the group at +3000 to win it, were undone by a sharp United States side but will fancy their chances against an opponent who also struggled on Matchday 1. The margins in this match are razor thin.

With both teams in must-win territory, expect an open and urgent contest. Turkey has the more talented individual roster on paper, but Paraguay's physical, combative style can drag opponents into a scrap. This is the definition of a six-pointer in the group context, and the loser will almost certainly be heading home early from a World Cup on North American soil.
