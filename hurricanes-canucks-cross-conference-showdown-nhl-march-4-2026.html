5-Game Wednesday Slate

NHL March 4, 2026

Wednesday brings a compact 5-game NHL slate with some genuinely compelling storylines. Vegas visits Detroit dealing with a rash of injuries while the Red Wings keep rolling at Little Caesars Arena. Toronto desperately tries to snap a 4-game losing skid in New Jersey. Carolina, one of the league's elite at 82 points, takes on a Vancouver team that's been one of hockey's biggest disappointments. The Islanders and Ducks, both on scorching 5-game win streaks, collide at Honda Center. And St. Louis travels to Seattle, where the Kraken have won 5 straight at home.

Golden Knights @ Red Wings

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Puck Line
DET -1.5 (+195)
Moneyline
VGK +114 / DET -135
Total
O/U 5.5

Detroit at 35-20-6 with 76 points has been one of the best stories in the Eastern Conference this season, and Little Caesars Arena has become a legitimately difficult building to play in. The Red Wings have won 6 of their last 10 home games, and their overall body of work puts them firmly in the playoff picture with some breathing room. John Gibson has been outstanding between the pipes, posting a 23-12-2 record with a 2.60 GAA and a .906 save percentage that gives Detroit a reliable foundation every single night. When your goaltending is that steady, you can weather just about any storm that rolls into your building.

Vegas at 28-19-14 with 70 points continues to be one of the NHL's most fascinating and frustrating teams simultaneously. Those 14 overtime losses are an eye-popping number, telling you that the Golden Knights are competitive in practically every game they play but simply cannot close out tight contests. The injury situation heading into Detroit is rough. Mark Stone is day-to-day with a left arm issue, Seth Jones has been out week-to-week with an upper-body injury and has already missed 21 games, and Shea Theodore's status is uncertain after dealing with an illness. That's a massive chunk of their defensive infrastructure either gone or limited, and it puts an enormous amount of pressure on the healthy bodies to carry the load.

Adin Hill gets the call in net for Vegas, and his numbers tell you everything you need to know about the Golden Knights' struggles. Hill is 5-4-3 with a 3.50 GAA and an .858 save percentage, which is the kind of stat line that makes you wince. That .858 number is brutal, well below the league average, and when you're getting that level of goaltending on the road against a team as sharp as Detroit, you're already playing from behind before the puck drops. Mitch Marner and Jack Eichel can generate offense, no question about it, but the Golden Knights need Hill to be significantly better than those numbers suggest if they're going to steal one in Detroit.

The DET -135 moneyline feels about right given the disparity in goaltending and the injury situation for Vegas. Detroit at home against a short-handed opponent that's lost 4 of its last 5 road games is a spot where the Red Wings should feel comfortable. The 5.5 total is interesting, sitting below 6 for a game involving two teams capable of putting the puck in the net. The over at -130 suggests the market leans toward more goals, and given Hill's .858 save percentage, it's hard to argue against the idea that Detroit's offense is going to find the back of the net. The puck line at DET -1.5 (+195) is a bigger number that reflects how close these games can be, but if Vegas's goaltending collapses the way those Hill numbers suggest it might, a multi-goal Detroit win is very much on the table.

ESPN+/Hulu

Maple Leafs @ Devils

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Prudential Center, Newark, NJ
Puck Line
NJ -1.5 (+215)
Moneyline
TOR +102 / NJ -122
Total
O/U 5.5

Toronto at 27-24-10 walks into the Prudential Center in full crisis mode, and that's not an exaggeration. Four consecutive losses will do that to any team, but when you're the Maple Leafs and every single game gets dissected under a microscope by the most intense hockey media market in the world, a 4-game skid feels like an absolute catastrophe. The record itself tells a story of a team that hasn't been able to figure out consistency this season, and the 10 overtime losses suggest that even when Toronto is competitive, they can't finish the job. Joseph Woll gets the start in net at 13-9-4 with a 3.10 GAA and .906 save percentage, numbers that are decent but not the kind that inspire confidence when your team is spiraling.

New Jersey at 30-29-2 has had a thoroughly disappointing season by the standards they set for themselves, but they snapped out of a funk in their last outing with a convincing 5-1 victory. That kind of dominant performance can be a springboard, especially at home where the Prudential Center crowd gets behind this team. Jack Hughes has dealt with injury issues this season that have limited his ability to carry this team the way he's capable of, and when Hughes isn't at full strength, the Devils' ceiling drops considerably. Jake Allen is expected to start in goal at 12-15-1 with a 2.70 GAA and .906 save percentage, and those numbers are actually quite solid despite the losing record, suggesting he hasn't been the problem for New Jersey this season.

The NJ -122 moneyline making the Devils slight home favorites is a reflection of the venue advantage and Toronto's current freefall more than any kind of statement about New Jersey being a superior team. Over a full season, these two clubs have been remarkably similar in terms of overall quality, and the near-even pricing tells you the market sees this as a toss-up that tilts slightly toward the home side. The 5.5 total is a number that could go either way. Both goalies have .906 save percentages, which is an almost eerie mirror image, and both teams have the offensive talent to generate chances when they're engaged.

What makes this game compelling is the desperation factor. Toronto absolutely needs this win to stop the bleeding, and desperate teams can be dangerous because they play with an urgency that overcomes their recent struggles. On the other hand, desperate teams can also press too hard, take bad penalties, and make mental mistakes born from the anxiety of a losing streak. New Jersey is the more relaxed team in this spot, playing at home off a confidence-boosting 5-1 win, and that mental edge matters in a game between two evenly matched opponents. The TOR +102 underdog line is practically a coin flip, and this is the kind of game that could genuinely go either direction depending on which team gets the first goal and sets the tone early.

ESPN+

Hurricanes @ Canucks

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | Rogers Arena, Vancouver, BC
Puck Line
CAR -1.5 (-108)
Moneyline
CAR -278 / VAN +225
Total
O/U 6.5

This is the biggest mismatch on the Wednesday slate, and the betting lines make that painfully clear. Carolina at 38-16-6 with 82 points is one of the best teams in the entire NHL, sitting comfortably in the upper echelon of the Eastern Conference with a 15-8-4 road record that proves they can dominate away from home just as effectively as they do at PNC Arena. The Hurricanes average 3.40 goals per game while allowing just 2.80, and that kind of differential is the hallmark of a team that's built to win in every phase of the game. Even the loss of Pyotr Kochetkov for the season hasn't derailed them, because Dustin Bussi has stepped in and posted a phenomenal 24-win season with a 2.23 GAA and .906 save percentage. That's not a drop-off; that's a seamless transition.

Vancouver at 18-35-7 with 43 points is living through one of the most brutal seasons in recent franchise history, and it's especially jarring given the expectations coming into this year. A 6-18-5 home record is the kind of number that empties buildings and gets people fired, and the Canucks have been outscored badly all season long, averaging just 2.48 goals per game while surrendering a gruesome 3.70 per outing. The injury list has been devastating, with Thatcher Demko on long-term IR and several other key pieces either missing or playing through issues. Kevin Lankinen has been left to handle the bulk of the goaltending burden at 7-20-4 with a 3.69 GAA and an .874 save percentage, which are the kind of numbers that make you wonder how any team wins a single game.

The CAR -278 moneyline is enormous, and it reflects a talent gap that's borderline unfair. Carolina is the superior team in virtually every measurable category: they score more, they defend better, their goaltending is dramatically superior, and their road record alone is better than Vancouver's overall record. The puck line at CAR -1.5 (-108) is telling because the market is essentially saying a multi-goal Carolina win is the most likely outcome, pricing it at near even money. That's a powerful statement. When the puck line favorite is practically a coin flip, you know the disparity between these two teams is massive.

The 6.5 total is the highest number on the slate, and it makes sense when you consider the matchup dynamics. Carolina has the offensive firepower to put up 3 or 4 goals against anyone, and Vancouver's defensive structure, particularly in front of Lankinen, has been porous all season. The over at +114 is the underdog in this total, but it wouldn't be surprising at all to see this game clear 6.5 given that the Canucks have been consistently involved in high-scoring affairs simply because they can't keep the puck out of their net. Carolina doesn't need to play a perfect game here; they just need to play their game, and that should be more than enough to handle a Vancouver team that's been one of the NHL's biggest disappointments from start to finish.

ESPN+

Islanders @ Ducks

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | Honda Center, Anaheim, CA
Puck Line
ANA -1.5 (+225)
Moneyline
NYI -110 / ANA -110
Total
O/U 6.5

This is the most intriguing game on the entire Wednesday slate, and it's not even close. Both the Islanders and the Ducks are riding 5-game winning streaks, which means one of those streaks is dying tonight at Honda Center. Something has to give, and the market knows it, pricing this as a perfect pick'em at -110 on both sides. New York at 35-21-5 with 75 points has been one of the most consistent teams in the Eastern Conference, and their 18-11-3 road record tells you this is a team that travels well and doesn't wilt when the home crowd is against them. David Rittich has been excellent in net at 13-8-3 with a 2.63 GAA and .899 save percentage, and he showed exactly what he's capable of in the previous meeting between these teams, stopping 31 of 33 shots in a 5-2 Islanders victory.

Anaheim at 33-24-3 with 69 points has been one of the NHL's best stories this season, and their home record of 20-8-1 is absolutely elite. The Honda Center has become a legitimate fortress, and the Ducks are playing with the kind of confidence that comes from knowing they can beat anyone in their own building. But they're dealing with some key absences right now. Troy Terry is out for the third straight game with an upper-body injury, and Mikael Granlund has missed four consecutive games with his own upper-body issue. Those are two significant pieces of the Ducks' offensive puzzle, and losing them simultaneously takes a real bite out of Anaheim's scoring punch at the worst possible time.

The pick'em moneyline is fascinating because it creates a genuine debate about which factor matters more: Anaheim's home dominance or their injury concerns. A 20-8-1 home record is spectacular, the kind of number that makes you want to blindly back the home side regardless of the opponent. But losing Terry and Granlund strips away firepower that the Ducks badly need against an Islanders team that's been stingy defensively during their own 5-game heater. The previous meeting was a decisive 5-2 Islanders win with Rittich making 31 saves on 33 shots, and that kind of performance sticks in a team's memory. New York knows they can beat this team, and that confidence matters.

The 6.5 total is tied with the Carolina-Vancouver game for the highest number on the slate, and it speaks to the offensive potential of both clubs even with Anaheim's absences. The Ducks had won 12 of 14 before a loss to Colorado snapped that ridiculous run, and even shorthanded, they still have enough talent to put pucks in the net. The Islanders' offense has been clicking during their streak as well, and when two hot teams collide, the energy tends to produce goals. This is one of those late-night West Coast games that could genuinely be the best game of the entire NHL slate, a clash of momentum between two teams that refuse to lose right now. The puck line at ANA -1.5 (+225) tells you the market doesn't expect a blowout either way, reinforcing just how tight this matchup is.

Game 5
ESPN+/Hulu

Blues @ Kraken

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | Climate Pledge Arena, Seattle, WA
Puck Line
SEA -1.5 (+170)
Moneyline
STL +124 / SEA -148
Total
O/U 5.5

Seattle at 29-22-9 with 67 points has quietly turned Climate Pledge Arena into one of the more difficult buildings to play in across the Western Conference, and their current 5-game home winning streak is proof that the Kraken are a completely different animal when they've got their own crowd behind them. The goaltending tandem of Philipp Grubauer (11-7-3, 2.50 GAA, .914 save percentage) and Joey Daccord (2.00 GAA, .934 save percentage) has been one of the most underrated duos in the NHL, and regardless of which one starts, the Blues are going to be facing a netminder who's been playing at a very high level. Daccord in particular has been sensational, and if he gets the call, St. Louis is in for a long night trying to generate quality scoring chances.

St. Louis at 22-29-9 with 53 points is having the kind of season that leads to front office changes and trade deadline fire sales. The Blues are 14 points out of a wild card spot, and at this point, they're playing for pride and individual auditions rather than anything meaningful in the standings. Jordan Binnington has been hung out to dry far too often this year, posting an 8-18-6 record with a 3.60 GAA and an .867 save percentage. That .867 number is genuinely alarming, ranking among the worst in the league for any goaltender with significant playing time, and while some of that falls on the defense in front of him, Binnington hasn't been sharp enough to steal games the way he has in past seasons.

The SEA -148 moneyline makes the Kraken clear favorites, and the number feels justified when you compare the trajectories of these two teams. Seattle is trending in the right direction with those 5 consecutive home wins, and their goaltending gives them a massive edge in this matchup. The contrast between Daccord's .934 save percentage (or even Grubauer's .914) and Binnington's .867 is staggering. That's a difference of nearly 70 points in save percentage, which translates to roughly 2-3 extra goals allowed per game. You simply cannot overcome that kind of goaltending disparity over 60 minutes, especially on the road in a hostile building.

The head-to-head history favors Seattle as well, with the Kraken winning 6 of the last 10 meetings between these franchises. Climate Pledge Arena has not been kind to St. Louis, and there's no reason to expect that to change tonight given the disparity in team quality and goaltending. The 5.5 total is the lowest number on the late slate, which makes sense because Seattle's goaltending has been elite at keeping scores low, and this is more likely to be a 3-1 or 4-2 type of game rather than a wide-open affair. The puck line at SEA -1.5 (+170) offers some value if you believe the Kraken are going to control this game from start to finish, and given Binnington's struggles and Seattle's home dominance, a multi-goal Kraken win is a very realistic outcome.
