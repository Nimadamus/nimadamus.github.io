The magic of the FA Cup returns with Crystal Palace defending their title at non-league Macclesfield, plus Premier League heavyweight clashes

Macclesfield FC vs Crystal Palace

Saturday 12:30 PM ET | Moss Rose | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | Defending Champions Begin Title Defence

This is what the FA Cup is all about. Crystal Palace, the reigning FA Cup champions after their stunning run last May, travel to Moss Rose to face Macclesfield FC - a phoenix club playing in the sixth tier of English football. The gap between these sides is astronomical on paper, but that's exactly why we watch.

Macclesfield's journey to this point has been nothing short of remarkable. After the original club's dissolution in 2020, the community rallied to form this new entity, and they've climbed through the non-league pyramid with genuine purpose. Their reward? A sold-out crowd and a date with Premier League royalty. The atmosphere at Moss Rose will be absolutely electric - 4,000 fans creating the kind of noise that makes professionals uncomfortable.

Palace manager Oliver Glasner faces the classic dilemma: rotate too heavily and risk an embarrassing upset, or play the stars and potentially burn them out. Expect a mixed XI, but Palace's depth alone should handle this. The Eagles' pressing game could overwhelm Macclesfield's part-time players, and the technical gap will tell eventually. But don't be surprised if it takes 30 minutes for Palace to settle - these early FA Cup ties have a way of staying tight longer than anyone expects.

Tottenham vs Aston Villa

Saturday 3:00 PM ET | Tottenham Hotspur Stadium | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | Premier League Heavyweight Clash

The standout fixture of the Third Round pits two clubs who've been knocking on the door of silverware without breaking through. Tottenham's trophy drought extends past 15 years now, while Unai Emery has transformed Villa into genuine contenders but the cabinet remains bare. Something's gotta give.

Ange Postecoglou's attacking philosophy makes Spurs compulsively watchable but defensively suspect. They've been involved in more high-scoring affairs than anyone in the Premier League this season, and Villa under Emery are more than capable of exploiting the spaces Spurs leave behind their adventurous fullbacks. Ollie Watkins has been in lethal form, and the service from the Villa midfield has been consistently excellent.

At the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, Spurs have the home advantage and their attacking trio of Son Heung-min, Brennan Johnson, and Dominic Solanke can hurt anyone. But Villa's organization and counter-attacking threat make this genuinely unpredictable. Expect goals, expect drama, and expect neither manager to hold back despite the fixture congestion ahead. Both clubs desperately want this trophy.

Manchester City vs Exeter City

Saturday 10:00 AM ET | Etihad Stadium | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | League One Side at the Etihad

Pep Guardiola's relationship with the FA Cup has been complicated - City have won it twice under him but often seemed to treat it as a lower priority than the league and Champions League. That won't change this year, but the Etihad will still be too hostile an environment for League One Exeter to mount any serious challenge.

Exeter City have had a solid season in League One and their manager Gary Caldwell has instilled a disciplined defensive shape. They'll arrive hoping to frustrate, stay in the game, and pray for a moment of magic. The Grecians' fans will make noise in their corner of the Etihad, and there's always that romantic hope that comes with FA Cup third round weekend.

But let's be realistic - City's B-team is better than most Premier League starting XIs. Even with Guardiola resting his stars, the quality difference is vast. Expect City to control possession north of 75%, create chances at will, and eventually break down Exeter's resistance. The only question is whether it stays respectable or becomes a rout. City's finishing has been inconsistent this season, so Exeter might actually escape with some dignity intact.

Charlton Athletic vs Chelsea

Saturday 12:30 PM ET | The Valley | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | South London Derby

The South London derby adds extra spice to what's already a compelling Cup tie. Charlton Athletic, languishing in League One, get to welcome their wealthy neighbors to The Valley - a ground that's seen better days but will be absolutely bouncing for this occasion. The Addicks haven't beaten Chelsea since 2006, but cup football doesn't care about form guides.

Chelsea under Enzo Maresca have found a rhythm in the Premier League, playing possession-based football with a clear identity. Cole Palmer has been sensational this season, but the question is whether he'll even feature in this one. Maresca has options and might use this as an opportunity to integrate some of Chelsea's expensive bench pieces.

The Valley will generate an atmosphere that Charlton fans haven't experienced in years. The noise, the stakes, the chance to embarrass the big boys from across South London - this is why the FA Cup matters. Chelsea should have far too much quality, but if they come out flat and Charlton score early, suddenly the nerves creep in. Don't count the Addicks out completely.

Arsenal vs Manchester United

Saturday 5:30 PM ET | Emirates Stadium | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | Historic Rivalry Renewed

This is a Third Round blockbuster that feels like a quarterfinal. Arsenal and Manchester United - two clubs who've contested some of the most memorable FA Cup ties in history - meet at the Emirates with both sides in the midst of challenging seasons. The Gunners are chasing Liverpool in the title race; United are trying to rebuild under Ruben Amorim.

Mikel Arteta's Arsenal are a well-oiled machine at home, where the Emirates has become a genuine fortress. Bukayo Saka continues to be their talisman, while the midfield axis of Rice and Odegaard controls games with ease. United have shown flashes under Amorim, but consistency has been elusive. The new manager bounce has faded, and this is exactly the kind of hostile environment where United's fragile confidence could crumble.

United's FA Cup pedigree is unmatched - no club has won it more times - but this Arsenal side is built differently than recent Gunners teams. They're physical, organized, and ruthless. Expect Arteta to take this seriously despite the league pressures, because the FA Cup represents a genuine chance at silverware. Arsenal should win this at home, but United in cup competitions always carry a threat.

Liverpool vs Accrington Stanley

Saturday 10:00 AM ET | Anfield | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | League Two Minnows at Anfield

Accrington Stanley - exactly. The famous milk advert reference will echo around Anfield as League Two's plucky visitors arrive at one of football's most iconic stadiums. Liverpool sit atop the Premier League under Arne Slot and have looked utterly dominant, which makes this about as daunting a draw as Accrington could have received.

The Lancashire club, nicknamed 'Owd Reds', have never played at Anfield in a competitive fixture. This will be a day their fans remember forever regardless of the scoreline. Manager John Coleman has done remarkable work keeping Accrington competitive on a shoestring budget - they're the definition of a proper football club, run sustainably and punching above their weight.

Slot will rotate heavily, but Liverpool's depth is frightening. The likes of Darwin Nunez, Diogo Jota, and Curtis Jones should all feature, and that's more than enough firepower to handle League Two opposition. The only question is how professional Liverpool remain - Accrington will defend deep and hope to nick something on the counter. It won't work, but they'll give everything trying.

West Ham vs Aston Villa

Saturday 3:00 PM ET | London Stadium | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | London Derby at the London Stadium

West Ham's FA Cup history is legendary - they've won it three times and produced some of the tournament's most iconic moments. Under Julen Lopetegui, the Hammers have had a turbulent season in the league, but the Cup offers a reset. The London Stadium will be hungry for a run.

Fulham under Marco Silva have been one of the Premier League's surprise packages, playing attractive football and competing with anyone. Joao Palhinha's departure to Bayern was supposed to derail them, but they've adapted well. Andreas Pereira continues to pull strings, and their pressing game can suffocate opponents.

This is a genuine 50-50 tie. Both managers will balance cup ambition against league survival, meaning rotated XIs and opportunities for squad players to stake their claims. West Ham's home support will be a factor, but Fulham's quality shouldn't be underestimated. Expect a tight, tactical affair that could easily require extra time to separate them.

Newcastle vs Bromley

Saturday 12:30 PM ET | St James' Park | ESPN+
FA Cup Third Round | Non-League History at St James' Park

Bromley made history this season by earning promotion to the Football League for the first time in their 132-year existence. Now the Ravens get to experience St James' Park - one of English football's great cathedrals - and take on a Newcastle side with serious ambitions under Eddie Howe.

The Magpies have been excellent at home this season, with Alexander Isak in particularly lethal form. Even with rotation, Newcastle will field a team worth hundreds of millions more than Bromley's entire club valuation. The quality gap is enormous, but Bromley didn't get here by accident - they're well organized and will relish every moment.

For Bromley's fans, this is the reward for years of supporting their local club through the non-league wilderness. They'll make noise in the away section and dream of an upset that would rank among the greatest in FA Cup history. It won't happen - Newcastle are simply too good - but the journey matters more than the destination for the Ravens today.
