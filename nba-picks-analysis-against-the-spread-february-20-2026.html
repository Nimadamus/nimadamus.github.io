The NBA is back. A nine-game Friday night slate marks the first action since the All-Star break, and the league returns with a loaded card highlighted by Dallas at Minnesota on ESPN and the Hallway Series between the LA Clippers and LA Lakers on ESPN. From tank battles in the East to a defending champion flex in Oklahoma City, every game on the board gets the full breakdown below.

GAME 1

Indiana Pacers @ Washington Wizards

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena
Spread
IND -1.5
Moneyline
IND -125 / WSH +105
O/U
231.5

Welcome back from the All-Star break, folks, and what better way to kick things off than a showdown between the two worst teams in the Eastern Conference. Indiana (15-40) and Washington (14-39) are both deep in the tank, and this game has the feel of a contest where neither side particularly wants to win. The Pacers are scoring 111.1 points per game but hemorrhaging 118.6, a net rating that screams "we're playing for ping pong balls." Meanwhile, Washington's defense is allowing a staggering 123.1 points per game, the kind of number that makes you wonder if they've considered playing five guys on offense and just hoping for the best.

The elephant in the room for Indiana is the season-ending Achilles injury to Tyrese Haliburton, which effectively ended any competitive aspirations. This roster is in full rebuild mode, and the Pacers have looked rudderless without their franchise point guard orchestrating the offense. The rotation has been a revolving door of young players and fringe contributors logging heavy minutes, and there's no singular go-to option who can seize control of a game. That said, they're still putting up 111 a night, which tells you the pace is frenetic and the defensive effort is, well, optional.

Washington isn't in much better shape. Trae Young, who was acquired from Atlanta in January, is out with a knee injury, and that's a devastating blow to a team that desperately needs a primary creator. Alexandre Sarr is also sidelined with a hamstring issue, and Khris Middleton remains out. The Wizards are putting up 112.2 points per game despite all the absences, but that 123.1 allowed number is an absolute sieve. Nobody is playing defense in this building tonight.

The 231.5 total tells you everything you need to know about what oddsmakers expect here. Both teams play at a breakneck pace, neither has any incentive to tighten up defensively, and the injury reports have stripped away any semblance of structure on either side. Indiana being a slim 1.5-point road favorite tells you how little separates these two rosters, and the market sees this as essentially a coin flip. This is a game that could genuinely go either way, with the final score likely looking something like 119-115. It's ugly basketball, but it's basketball.

GAME 2

Utah Jazz @ Memphis Grizzlies

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | FedExForum
Spread
MEM -2.5
Moneyline
MEM -140 / UTA +118
O/U
237.5

This is a fascinating game because both rosters look completely different than they did a month ago. The February 3 trade that sent Jaren Jackson Jr. from Memphis to Utah fundamentally reshaped both franchises, and now these teams meet for the first time since the deal. Lauri Markkanen has been phenomenal for Utah, pouring in 26.7 points per game on 47.8% shooting and 36.3% from deep, and now he's got JJJ as a frontcourt partner. The Jazz have been quietly frisky, going 6-1 against the spread in their last seven games, suggesting this 18-38 record doesn't fully capture how competitive they've been recently.

Memphis, on the other hand, is in a full-blown tailspin. The Grizzlies have lost four straight and are 2-10 in their last 12 games, a stretch that coincides with Ja Morant's absence due to an elbow UCL sprain. Morant was reevaluated on February 18 and is expected to miss at least two more weeks. Beyond that, the Grizzlies shipped Desmond Bane to Orlando and JJJ to Utah, so the roster around Morant has been gutted. This isn't the same Memphis team that was supposed to contend this year. It's a skeleton crew trying to figure out who they are.

That 241.5 total is eye-popping, and the over has cashed in six of Memphis's last seven games. Neither team has any interest in slowing the game down, and with both rosters featuring new pieces still figuring out their roles, there are going to be transition opportunities galore. The defensive communication isn't going to be there for either side, which means easy baskets and a lot of points. Markkanen should feast against a Memphis frontcourt that just lost its anchor in JJJ, and the Jazz's recent ATS surge suggests they're playing with a confidence that their record doesn't reflect.

Memphis being a 2.5-point home favorite feels like the market respecting the home court more than the current state of the roster. The Grizzlies are 20-33 and spiraling, while Utah has been covering at an absurd rate lately. This is a game where the 237.5 total feels like the most interesting angle, given the combined pace and defensive deficiencies on both sides. Don't be surprised if this one pushes toward the 250s.

GAME 3

Cleveland Cavaliers @ Charlotte Hornets

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Spectrum Center
Spread
CLE -5.5
Moneyline
CLE -225 / CHA +185
O/U
233.5

This might be the most intriguing game on the entire Friday slate. Cleveland (34-21) just pulled off one of the biggest mid-season acquisitions in years, landing James Harden from the Clippers on February 4, and the early returns have been electric. Harden's been dropping 19.3 points and 8.7 assists per game since the trade, and pairing him with Donovan Mitchell (29.0 PPG) gives the Cavaliers one of the most dangerous backcourts in the NBA. Cleveland is already the second-highest scoring team in the league at 120.0 points per game while shooting 47.6% from the field, and adding Harden's playmaking to that mix has made them look like legitimate Eastern Conference contenders.

There's a massive potential boost for Cleveland tonight as well. Evan Mobley missed the last seven games before the break with a calf injury, but he's expected to return after the All-Star rest period. Getting Mobley back alongside the new-look backcourt could take this offense to another level, as his rim protection and ability to finish lobs creates a completely different dynamic. The Cavaliers have been rolling without him, so imagine what they look like at full strength with Harden, Mitchell, and Mobley all on the floor together for the first time.

Charlotte (26-29) isn't a pushover, though. The Hornets had an incredible nine-game winning streak earlier this season, their longest since 1999, and Brandon Miller has been scorching the nets with 24.6 points per game over his last nine contests. LaMelo Ball is contributing 19.3 points and 7.4 assists per game, and when this Charlotte offense is clicking, they can hang with just about anyone. The big loss here is Miles Bridges, who's suspended through February 24, removing a key piece from the rotation.

Cleveland is a 5.5-point road favorite, and the -225 moneyline tells you the Cavaliers are the clear pick here. The question is whether Charlotte's home court and youthful energy can keep this competitive against a Cavaliers team that's been scoring at an elite clip all season. Miller and Ball are going to get their looks, but can they match the firepower of Mitchell and Harden? That's a tall order. The 233.5 total reflects the offensive firepower on both sides, and this has the makings of a high-scoring, entertaining game with Cleveland's new superstar pairing getting a real test against a Charlotte team with something to prove.

GAME 4

Miami Heat @ Atlanta Hawks

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | State Farm Arena
Spread
MIA -3.5
Moneyline
MIA -165 / ATL +138
O/U
242.5

Both of these teams look radically different than they did at the start of the season, and this game is essentially a meeting of two franchises in transition. Miami (29-27) is no longer the Jimmy Butler-led grit-and-grind squad. Butler was traded to the Warriors, and in his absence, Norman Powell has absolutely blossomed into a legitimate number-one scoring option. Powell is averaging 24.9 points per game on a scorching 49.1% from the field and a career-best 45.6% from three-point range. That kind of efficiency from your top scorer is rare, and it's kept Miami in the playoff conversation with a 32-23-1 record against the spread this season.

The Heat have averaged 122.4 points per game over their last 10 contests, going 6-4 in that stretch, which shows they've fully embraced a more uptempo identity. Bam Adebayo continues to be the engine on both ends at 18.4 points and 9.9 rebounds per game, doing all the little things that don't always show up in the box score. The concern tonight is Tyler Herro being out with a rib injury, which takes another scoring punch out of the rotation. Miami's depth will be tested, and Powell is going to need to carry a heavy load.

Atlanta (26-30) is also in a completely different era. Trae Young is gone, traded to Washington in January, and the Hawks have rebuilt around Jalen Johnson, who has turned into one of the most versatile young players in the league. Johnson is putting up a monster stat line of 23.3 points, 10.6 rebounds, and 8.2 assists per game on 50.2% shooting. Those are borderline All-Star numbers, and he's doing it as the primary option. The Hawks added Jonathan Kuminga and Buddy Hield from the Warriors in exchange for Kristaps Porzingis on February 5, giving them young athleticism and perimeter shooting to complement Johnson's all-around game. But they've been inconsistent, going just 4-6 in their last 10 and scoring only 112.6 points per game.

The 242.5 total suggests oddsmakers expect a track meet, and with Miami's recent offensive surge and Atlanta's defensive inconsistencies, that feels about right. The 3.5-point spread makes Miami a solid road favorite, reflecting how much more stable the Heat have been with Powell running the show compared to Atlanta's ongoing transition. The key matchup to watch is Powell versus Atlanta's perimeter defense, and whether Jalen Johnson's all-around game can outshine Adebayo on the interior. Both teams have new identities, and both are still figuring out exactly who they are. This should be a fun, competitive game from wire to wire.

Dallas Mavericks @ Minnesota Timberwolves

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Target Center | ESPN
Spread
MIN -12.5
Moneyline
MIN -699 / DAL +500
O/U
236.5

This is a mismatch of historic proportions. Dallas (19-35) has been absolutely decimated by roster upheaval, and the franchise received the final blow on February 18 when Kyrie Irving was officially ruled out for the season with a torn ACL. After also losing their franchise guard to a blockbuster deal with the Lakers earlier this season, Dallas has been stripped of its entire star backcourt and is now running out a roster led by Naji Marshall (15.1 PPG) and PJ Washington (14.1 PPG). These are solid NBA players, but they're not guys who can carry a team against elite competition. Dallas has gone 1-9 in their last 10 games with a four-game road losing streak, and things aren't going to get easier in Minneapolis.

Minnesota (34-22) is rolling. The Timberwolves are 7-3 in their last 10 and scoring 119.7 points per game, good for third in the entire NBA. Anthony Edwards just put the world on notice at the All-Star Game, earning MVP honors on February 16, and he's been absolutely ridiculous all season at 29.3 points per game on 49.3% from the field and 40.2% from three. When Ant-Man is shooting like that, Minnesota's offense becomes nearly impossible to contain. Julius Randle has been a perfect complement at 22.3 points and 7.0 rebounds, while Rudy Gobert anchors the defense with 11.0 points and 11.1 rebounds per game.

The 12.5-point spread is massive, but honestly, it might not be enough. Minnesota's combination of Edwards, Randle, and Gobert creates problems on every level, and Dallas simply doesn't have the personnel to compete. The Mavericks can't match Minnesota's offensive firepower, can't contain Edwards on the perimeter, and can't deal with Gobert's presence at the rim. This is a team that was built around Luka and Kyrie, and both of those guys are gone. What's left is a rebuilding project facing a legitimate title contender.

The only real question here is whether Minnesota comes out flat after the All-Star break. Teams sometimes take a game or two to find their rhythm, and the Timberwolves have a tendency to play down to their competition, as evidenced by their 7-3 record rather than a perfect 10-0 in the last stretch. If Dallas catches Minnesota sleepwalking early, they could make this interesting for a half. But over 48 minutes, the talent gap is simply too large. Edwards is going to be energized coming off his All-Star MVP performance, and that's a scary proposition for a team that has nobody who can guard him.

GAME 6

Milwaukee Bucks @ New Orleans Pelicans

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Smoothie King Center
Spread
NOP -3.5
Moneyline
MIL +142 / NOP -170
O/U
222.5

Here's a sentence nobody expected to type this season: the New Orleans Pelicans are favored by 3.5 points over the Milwaukee Bucks. But that's where we are in this wildly unpredictable NBA season. Milwaukee (23-30) has been one of the biggest disappointments in the league, sitting well below .500 and looking nothing like the championship contender everyone projected them to be. The Bucks are scoring 116.9 points per game, which sounds fine until you realize they're allowing 119.8, a negative net rating that has plagued them all year. The biggest concern tonight is the status of Giannis Antetokounmpo, who's listed as questionable with a right calf strain that's kept him out since January 23.

When Giannis plays, he's still a force of nature, averaging 28.0 points, 10.0 rebounds, and 5.6 assists on a ridiculous 61.2% from the field. But the calf issue has lingered, and if he sits tonight, Milwaukee becomes a fundamentally different team. Ryan Rollins has stepped up admirably with 16.9 points per game in extended minutes, but there's a universe of difference between "nice young player getting run" and "Giannis Antetokounmpo." The Bucks have historically dominated this matchup, going 7-1 in their last eight meetings with New Orleans, but that was a different version of Milwaukee.

New Orleans (15-41) doesn't exactly inspire confidence either, but there are genuine bright spots. Zion Williamson has been healthy and engaged, averaging 21.6 points on 58.9% shooting over his last 10 games, and he hasn't missed a game since December 11. That's the kind of consistency and durability that Pelicans fans have been begging for. Trey Murphy III has blossomed into a reliable scorer at 22.1 points per game, giving New Orleans a legitimate one-two punch. The dark cloud remains Dejounte Murray's ruptured Achilles, as he hasn't played a single game this season, robbing the Pelicans of their secondary playmaker.

The 222.5 total is the lowest on tonight's slate, and that makes sense given Milwaukee's defensive struggles and New Orleans's overall pace. The Pelicans are 30-25-1 against the spread on the season and have covered in 17 of 29 home games, showing they've been competitive despite the ugly record. If Giannis sits, this spread could move even further toward New Orleans, and the Pelicans would become a significant favorite. If Giannis plays, this becomes a much different proposition. Keep an eye on the injury report as tip-off approaches, because Giannis's status is the single biggest variable on the entire nine-game slate tonight.

GAME 7

Brooklyn Nets @ Oklahoma City Thunder

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Paycom Center
Spread
OKC -16.5
Moneyline
OKC -1500 / BKN +800
O/U
211.5

If you're looking for competitive basketball, this probably isn't your game. Oklahoma City (42-14) owns the best record in the NBA and they're the defending champions, while Brooklyn (15-38) is in the deepest rebuild imaginable. The moneyline tells the entire story: the Thunder are -1500 favorites, meaning you'd need to wager $1,500 to win $100. That's about as lopsided as NBA odds get. Brooklyn waived Cam Thomas on February 5, and the Nets' roster reads like a G-League call-up sheet. There's simply nobody on this team who can create offense at an NBA-caliber level against OKC's elite defense.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander continues to be the frontrunner for MVP, and his numbers are staggering: 31.8 points and 6.4 assists per game on 66.7% True Shooting. SGA is one of the most efficient high-volume scorers in NBA history, and he's doing it while leading the league's best team. He doesn't need to dominate this game for OKC to win by 20, but he very well might anyway because that's just what he does. Chet Holmgren has been excellent since returning from injury, averaging 16.4 points, 9.8 rebounds, and 2.7 blocks per game over his last 18 contests, giving the Thunder a legitimate two-way force alongside SGA.

Brooklyn's only hope here is that OKC coasts after the All-Star break. The Thunder are 6-4 in their last 10, which sounds pedestrian until you realize they've been resting guys and managing workloads with the one seed essentially locked up. If Oklahoma City decides to treat this like a glorified scrimmage and pulls their starters early in the fourth quarter, the final margin might look somewhat respectable. Otherwise, this has the potential to get genuinely ugly.

The 16.5-point spread is massive, but given the talent disparity, it might actually be a fair number. The 211.5 total is the lowest on the slate, reflecting OKC's elite defense and the likelihood that the Thunder will be coasting in the fourth quarter with a massive lead. The Nets don't have a single player who would start for the Thunder, and OKC's depth alone could probably beat Brooklyn on most nights. This is a game for Thunder fans to enjoy watching SGA and Holmgren work their magic, and for Nets fans to look ahead to the draft lottery. Not every game on the schedule is a must-watch, and this one falls squarely in the "check the box score in the morning" category.

LA Clippers @ LA Lakers

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena | ESPN
Spread
LAL -6.5
Moneyline
LAL -250 / LAC +210
O/U
223.5

The Hallway Series returns with all the drama of a blockbuster movie, and this edition might be the most lopsided it's been in years, but not in the direction most people expected. The Lakers (33-21) are the clear favorites tonight, and the storyline everyone's watching is Luka Doncic. Since being traded from Dallas, Luka has been absolutely spectacular, posting 32.8 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 8.6 assists per game. He missed four games before the break with a hamstring strain, but he played five minutes in the All-Star Game and said afterward that he "felt good." If Luka is back to full strength, pairing him with LeBron James (22.0 PPG, 7.1 APG) gives the Lakers one of the most lethal offensive duos in the entire league.

And then there's Austin Reaves, who made a thunderous return from his calf injury before the break, erupting for 35 points in his first game back. That kind of performance from a third option is what separates good teams from great ones, and the Lakers have legitimate three-level scoring with Luka, LeBron, and Reaves all capable of taking over a game. The Lakers are 6-4 in their last 10, a stretch where they dealt with injuries to both Doncic and Reaves, so there's reason to believe this team is even better than their recent record suggests when healthy.

The Clippers (26-28) are in rough shape. James Harden was traded to Cleveland, Bradley Beal is done for the season after hip surgery, and Derrick Jones Jr. is out with a knee injury. The roster has been gutted. Kawhi Leonard is still producing at an elite level with 27.9 points per game, but he's managing his chronic knee issues and carrying a much heavier burden with so few options around him. LA is shooting 49.6% from the field and scoring 110.0 points per game, but those numbers were propped up by Harden's playmaking, which is now gone. The Clippers are going to struggle to create easy offense tonight.

The Lakers -250 moneyline and 6.5-point spread tell you everything about the talent gap in this one. The real intrigue here is the emotional angle: Kawhi versus LeBron in a battle of LA, with the Clippers playing spoiler against a Lakers team that's building genuine championship momentum. But emotions don't win basketball games, talent does, and the talent gap between these two rosters is wider than it's been in years. Luka's health is the key variable. If he's anywhere close to 100%, this Lakers team is going to be a problem for everyone in the Western Conference, starting tonight.

GAME 9

Denver Nuggets @ Portland Trail Blazers

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | Moda Center
Spread
DEN -2.5
Moneyline
DEN -155 / POR +130
O/U
239.5

Close your eyes and imagine telling someone a year ago that a Denver-Portland game would be this tight. And yet, here we are. The moneyline has Denver at just -155 and Portland at +130, with a slim 2.5-point spread making this one of the closest games on the entire Friday night slate. Part of that is the Nuggets (35-20) dealing with key injuries, as Aaron Gordon is out for four to six weeks with a hamstring issue and Peyton Watson is also sidelined. But the bigger factor is Nikola Jokic, who is putting up one of the most absurd statistical seasons in NBA history: 28.8 points, 12.2 rebounds, and 10.7 assists per game on 59.3% from the field. That's a triple-double average. Every single night. It's inhuman.

Jamal Murray has also been playing the best basketball of his career, posting a career-high 25.8 points per game, and the Jokic-Murray two-man game remains one of the most dangerous actions in basketball. Denver is scoring 117.2 points per game but has gone just 5-5 in their last 10, a stretch that's been marred by Gordon's absence and an occasional lack of defensive intensity. Without Gordon's two-way presence, the Nuggets lose a crucial piece of their defensive identity, and they've been leaking points on the wing as a result.

Portland (27-29) has hit a wall, losing six straight games coming into the break. Shaedon Sharpe is out with a calf injury and Deni Avdija is questionable with a back issue, which further thins out a rotation that's been running on fumes. Scoot Henderson has shown flashes with 13.7 points and 6.0 assists per game, but he's still developing and isn't yet the kind of player who can carry a team through a rough stretch. The Trail Blazers' slide from above .500 to below it has been swift, and the vibes in Portland are not great heading into this one.

The tight 2.5-point line is a reflection of Denver's injuries more than Portland's quality, and that's an important distinction. Even without Gordon and Watson, the Nuggets have Nikola Jokic, and that alone should make them favorites in most games. Jokic has historically dominated in Portland, and his ability to pick apart defenses with his passing makes him virtually unguardable regardless of who's around him. Murray's career-best scoring adds another dimension that Portland simply can't match. The Trail Blazers' six-game losing streak suggests a team low on confidence, and running into a motivated Jokic fresh off the All-Star break is not the cure for what ails them. Denver should win this game, but the margin might be tighter than the talent gap would suggest.
