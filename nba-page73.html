4-Game Tuesday Slate

NBA Analysis

The NBA takes a backseat to the Winter Olympics on NBC tonight, delivering a compact but compelling 4-game Tuesday slate. The headliner is in Los Angeles, where Victor Wembanyama and the league-leading Spurs visit a Lakers squad missing Luka Doncic to a hamstring injury, with San Antonio laying 7.5 on the road. Earlier, the Knicks host a devastated Pacers team at MSG, Kawhi Leonard and the surging Clippers head to Houston to face KD and the Rockets, and Cooper Flagg continues his jaw-dropping rookie campaign as the rebuilding Mavs visit Phoenix. Four games, four distinct storylines, and a late-night marquee that could be a Western Conference Finals preview.

Game 1

Pacers @ Knicks

Tuesday, 7:30 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Spread
NYK -11.5 (-112)
Moneyline
NYK -600 / IND +440
Total
O/U 223.5

This is about as close to a scheduled beatdown as you'll find on an NBA slate. The Knicks (34-19) have been absolutely rolling, winning 9 of their last 10 after a brief hiccup in that 118-80 loss to Detroit on February 6. That loss was an anomaly, not a trend. New York's two-headed monster of Jalen Brunson (27.1 PPG, 6.1 APG) and Karl-Anthony Towns (19.7 PPG, 11.9 RPG) has been the most devastating 1-2 punch in the Eastern Conference. Brunson just dropped 42 points in a double overtime thriller against Denver, and Towns leads the entire NBA in rebounds per game while piling up a league-best 34 double-doubles. When those two are cooking inside the Garden, there aren't many teams in the league that can hang.

Indiana (13-40) is a franchise in full crisis mode. The Tyrese Haliburton torn Achilles from the 2025 Finals continues to cast a long shadow over everything this organization does, and the results on the court are brutal. The Pacers are on a 4-game losing streak and just shipped Bennedict Mathurin and Isaiah Jackson to the Clippers at the trade deadline in exchange for Ivica Zubac and Kobe Brown. That's a move designed for the future, not the present. Zubac is questionable tonight with an ankle injury, and T.J. McConnell is also questionable with a hamstring problem, which could leave Indiana even more shorthanded than their already thin roster.

The 11.5-point spread is massive, but the injury dynamics on both sides are worth monitoring. OG Anunoby is questionable for the Knicks with a toe issue, and Mitchell Robinson remains out with an ankle problem. If Anunoby sits, that takes away some of New York's defensive versatility, though the Knicks have shown they can win by 20 without him this season. The bigger story is whether Indiana can even field a competitive lineup. Without Haliburton, with Zubac potentially out, and with a roster that's been reshuffled at the deadline, the Pacers are essentially asking role players to keep this within double digits at Madison Square Garden.

The 223.5 total is relatively modest for a game involving the Knicks, who have been pushing the pace under Tom Thibodeau this year. But Indiana's offense without Haliburton has been one of the most anemic units in the league, and that drags the total down considerably. This has the feel of a game where New York jumps out to a 20-point first-half lead and then coasts, with the starters sitting by the midway point of the fourth quarter. The Garden crowd should be in a good mood tonight.

Clippers @ Rockets

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -7.5 (-102)
Moneyline
HOU -278 / LAC +225
Total
O/U 209.5

Here's a fascinating clash of styles on NBA TV. The Clippers (25-27) have quietly been one of the hottest teams in basketball over the past three weeks, winning 8 of their last 10 games, including a 114-111 victory over Sacramento on February 6. And the reason is simple: Kawhi Leonard is healthy. When Leonard is on the court and engaged, he's still one of the five best players on the planet, and the numbers prove it. He's averaging 28.0 points, 6.3 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game, and he recently exploded for a 55-point performance that reminded everyone of his ceiling. This version of Kawhi, the one that actually plays, changes everything about how you evaluate the Clippers.

But Houston (32-19) is a different kind of monster. The Rockets sit second in the Western Conference and have built their identity around suffocating defense, allowing just 110 points per game (fourth in the NBA) while dominating the glass with a league-leading 48.8 rebounds per game. Alperen Sengun has made the All-Star team and earned it, posting 20.8 points, 9.4 rebounds, and 6.3 assists per game, including a breathtaking 39-point, 16-rebound performance against the Pacers. And then there's Kevin Durant, who's averaging 26.2 points on 51.0% from the field and 40.5% from three since returning from his ankle sprain. That efficiency at his age and size is absurd.

The Clippers are dealing with significant roster limitations. Bradley Beal is out for the year after hip surgery, and Darius Garland is sidelined with a toe injury. That leaves Leonard as the clear alpha with a supporting cast that has to work twice as hard to generate offense. On the Houston side, Fred VanVleet's torn ACL and Steven Adams' ankle surgery have been season-long absences, but the Rockets have built enough depth around their stars that those losses haven't derailed the season. The 209.5 total is the lowest on tonight's slate and reflects Houston's defensive identity perfectly.

Here's what makes this intriguing despite the 7.5-point spread: Kawhi Leonard in the playoffs or in a nationally televised spotlight game is a different animal. He elevates for these moments. The question is whether the rest of the Clippers roster can keep up with Houston's depth and rebounding dominance. The Rockets' ability to control the boards and limit second-chance points could be the deciding factor, because the Clippers simply don't have the size to match Houston's frontline when Sengun and Durant are both crashing the glass. This is a test of whether one superstar performance can overcome a deeper, more balanced opponent.

Game 3

Mavericks @ Suns

Tuesday, 9:00 PM ET | Footprint Center, Phoenix, AZ
Spread
PHX -7.5 (-115)
Moneyline
PHX -305 / DAL +245
Total
O/U 227.5

Dallas (19-33) is in full teardown mode, and the results are about as ugly as you'd expect. A 7-game losing streak, a trade deadline that saw Anthony Davis shipped to Washington, and a roster that's now built around Cooper Flagg and prayer. But here's the thing: Flagg has been absolutely sensational. The number one overall pick just ripped off three consecutive 30-point games, including 36 against Boston and 32 against the Spurs, becoming the first teenager in NBA history to accomplish that feat. He's not just a future star. He's already a must-watch player, and his presence alone makes every Mavericks game appointment viewing for basketball purists.

Phoenix (31-22) has steadied the ship after a rocky stretch earlier in the season, winning 6 of their last 10 and sitting fifth in the Western Conference. The massive news here is Devin Booker's return from a 7-game absence due to an ankle injury. Booker is officially off the injury report and should be fully available, and when he's on the court averaging 25.3 points and 6.3 assists per game, the Suns' offense transforms into one of the most potent units in the league. Jalen Green, acquired from Houston in a trade, has been finding his footing, averaging 11.0 points per game through his first 6 contests in a Phoenix uniform. He's a work in progress in Mike Budenholzer's system, but the talent is undeniable.

The injury situations make this spread look about right. Dallas is missing Kyrie Irving (knee, expected to debut after the All-Star break), Dereck Lively II (foot), and Klay Thompson is day-to-day. That means Flagg is essentially being asked to carry this team by himself, and while he's shown he can do it in stretches, asking a teenage rookie to do it on a nightly basis against quality competition is a tall order. Phoenix is healthier, with Grayson Allen (knee) and Isaiah Livers (shoulder) the only notable absences, neither of whom is a core rotation piece.

The 227.5 total is the highest on tonight's board, which reflects the likelihood that Dallas' rebuilding defense gives up buckets in bunches while Flagg and the Mavs try to trade baskets on the other end. This is a game where the outcome probably isn't in much doubt, but the entertainment value is sky high. Every time Flagg touches the ball, something spectacular could happen. The kid is averaging numbers that have people drawing comparisons to the all-time greats, and a matchup against Booker in Phoenix gives him another stage to prove this isn't just a hot streak. Watch Flagg. Ignore the final score. That's the best way to consume this one.

Spurs @ Lakers

Tuesday, 10:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Spread
SAS -7.5 (-115)
Moneyline
SAS -310 / LAL +250
Total
O/U 230.5

Let that number sink in for a moment. The San Antonio Spurs are 7.5-point road favorites at Crypto.com Arena against the Los Angeles Lakers. Road. Favorites. By a touchdown and a half. If you told someone two years ago that the Spurs would be laying that kind of lumber in Los Angeles, they'd have laughed you out of the room. But that's where we are, and honestly, it might not be enough. San Antonio (36-16) has the best record in the Western Conference, they're a perfect 4-0 in February with wins over Oklahoma City and Dallas (twice), and Victor Wembanyama is playing like the generational talent everyone promised he'd be. The 7-foot-4 alien is averaging 23.9 points, 11.1 rebounds, and a league-leading 2.6 blocks per game with 24 double-doubles on the season. He alters every shot at the rim and has developed an outside game that makes him virtually unguardable.

The De'Aaron Fox acquisition in February 2025 was the move that turned San Antonio from "promising rebuilding team" into legitimate championship contender. Fox has been outstanding in February, posting 15.3 points and 9.0 assists per game while running the Spurs' offense with the kind of precision and pace that perfectly complements Wembanyama's rim protection and lob-finishing ability. The Fox-Wembanyama pick-and-roll is one of the most devastating actions in the entire NBA, and teams simply haven't figured out how to defend it. You either help on the roll and give Fox an open floater, or you stay home and watch Wemby catch a lob over your head. Pick your poison.

The Lakers (32-19) are in a tough spot tonight. Luka Doncic is out with a hamstring strain, and that's devastating for a team that has leaned heavily on his 32.8 points per game (league-leading). Without Doncic, the offensive burden shifts entirely to LeBron James, who's been excellent in 2026, averaging 27.0 points per game in the calendar year. But LeBron is also on the second night of a back-to-back after losing to OKC 119-110 on Monday, and asking a 41-year-old to carry an offense against the best defense in the West on tired legs is a massive ask. The Lakers are 5-4 without Doncic this season, which is respectable, but none of those games came against a team as dominant as San Antonio.

Here's what scares me about the Lakers' chances tonight: the Spurs' defense is elite, and without Doncic's ability to create something out of nothing in isolation, Los Angeles becomes a much more predictable offensive team. LeBron driving to the rim is going to meet Wembanyama's 7-foot-10 wingspan, and that's a matchup that even the greatest player in basketball history has to respect. The 230.5 total is the highest on the board, suggesting the market expects both teams to score, but the spread tells you the market believes San Antonio's two-way dominance will be the difference. This is a potential Western Conference Finals preview, and even without Doncic, the atmosphere at Crypto.com Arena on a Tuesday night is going to be electric. The question is whether LeBron has one more heroic effort left in the tank on a back-to-back, or if the Spurs' depth, defense, and Wembanyama's otherworldly presence simply overwhelm a shorthanded Los Angeles squad.
