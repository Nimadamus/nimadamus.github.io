Liga MX Clausura Final: Cruz Azul vs Pumas UNAM

Leg 1 played May 21 | Leg 2 May 24, 8:00 PM ET | Estadio Olimpico Universitario

Liga MXClausura FinalReturn Leg Sunday

The headline story across CONCACAF club soccer this Friday is the Liga MX Clausura final between Cruz Azul and Pumas, with the first leg having been played Thursday at Estadio Ciudad de los Deportes and the return leg scheduled for Sunday, May 24 at Estadio Olimpico Universitario. Cruz Azul, the regular-season points leader, faces a Pumas side that advanced past Pachuca on regular-season seeding after a 1-1 aggregate semifinal draw. The aggregate scoreline coming out of leg 1 will set the price for the return leg; the Pumas home crowd at Estadio Olimpico is one of the strongest in CONCACAF club soccer and historically pushes Liga MX home favorites by half a goal of betting value.

The tactical question for the return leg is how Cruz Azul protects whatever lead they take to Mexico City. Manager Vicente Sanchez has been the most consistent in-game manager in the league this season, and his back-five-when-leading shape has been the structural reason Cruz Azul has been the league's stingiest defense across the spring. Pumas under Efrain Juarez has been the inverse: an aggressive 4-3-3 that presses high and concedes counterattacking chances. The final will be decided by whichever manager flinches first in the second half of the second leg.

From a betting perspective, the move on the return leg total has been steady downward since the Thursday leg-1 result; books are pricing the second 90 minutes as a tighter, lower-scoring affair than the first. Pumas to advance is currently around plus money in the long-form draw-no-bet market, but the aggregate adjustment after leg 1 will tighten that price quickly. The Liga MX final is also the highest-rated club soccer broadcast in North America this weekend; the public side will be on Pumas at home if leg 1 ended without a Cruz Azul lead.

Bundesliga Relegation Aftermath: Heidenheim vs Elversberg

Leg 1 Thursday: Heidenheim 2-2 Elversberg | Leg 2 Monday, May 26

The Bundesliga relegation playoff first leg was played Thursday at the Voith-Arena, with Heidenheim scoring twice in three minutes in the second half to claw back from two down and earn a 2-2 home draw. The second leg is set for Monday, May 26 at the Waldstadion Kaiserlinde. Heidenheim, the Bundesliga's 16th-place finisher this season, hosts the German second tier's third-place finisher Elversberg for a chance to stay in the top flight for 2026-27.

The math for the return leg is straightforward: Elversberg holds the two away goals on the aggregate scoreline, which means any 1-1 or higher draw at the Waldstadion sends them up. Heidenheim need a win on the road, or a 0-0 draw under the modified Bundesliga playoff rules. Frank Schmidt's side has held up better in home matches than away this season; the road profile is the bigger question. The market is currently pricing the Monday return leg as a near coin-flip with a slight lean to Elversberg given the away-goals tiebreak position.

The wider Bundesliga relegation context is that St. Pauli and 16th-place Heidenheim were already in the playoff conversation before Saturday's final matchday; St. Pauli was officially relegated. Heidenheim's two-goal comeback Thursday is now the most-watched club soccer storyline in Germany heading into the weekend. For the soccer-betting market, the second leg total is the cleanest read; Elversberg has been a low-event home team this season and Heidenheim's away record is the worst in Bundesliga 1 for the year.

Weekend Action: Premier League Final Day, La Liga Final Round, MLS Friday Slate

Multiple kickoffs Friday-Sunday

The European league season is in its last competitive weekend with the Premier League final matchday on Sunday, May 24, and the La Liga final round of matches played across Friday through Sunday. The European top-of-table picture is largely settled; the Premier League European-qualification race and the La Liga relegation table are the storylines that move the markets across the weekend. Italian Serie A and Bundesliga have both wrapped their regular-season calendars; Coppa Italia and DFB-Pokal finals already played earlier in the spring.

MLS Friday-night action features three kickoffs on the regular-season schedule, all at 7:30 PM ET local time across the Eastern Conference, with attention focused on Inter Miami, the Eastern Conference points leader, in their road match. The Friday-night MLS market is thinner than weekend slates; volume is heaviest on totals and player-prop markets across this slate.

The single biggest soccer-market storyline for the day is the aggregate adjustment on the Liga MX final after Thursday's first leg. Books re-priced the Sunday return leg overnight, and the early-Friday volume has been heavy on Pumas at home if leg 1 ended without a Cruz Azul advantage. Any of the European final-round matches with relegation, European qualification, or top-six placement still in play also command betting volume across Saturday and Sunday.
