2-Game Friday Slate

NHL March 13, 2026

A light two-game Friday slate, but it features Connor McDavid and the Edmonton Oilers visiting St. Louis, plus the Kings traveling to face the Islanders. McDavid has 36 goals and 110 points in 65 games this season. Both games carry significant playoff implications with all four teams in the thick of the race.

Game 1

Oilers @ Blues

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Enterprise Center, St. Louis, MO
Puck Line
EDM -1.5 (+165)
Moneyline
EDM -162 / STL +136
Total
O/U 6.5

Connor McDavid is doing Connor McDavid things, and the numbers are absurd even by his standards. 36 goals and 110 points in 65 games puts him on pace for a 139-point season, which would be his best yet. The Edmonton Oilers (32-26-8, 72 points) aren't the most consistent team in the Western Conference, but when McDavid is cooking alongside Leon Draisaitl, the offensive ceiling in any given game is higher than just about any team in the league. Edmonton sits right on the playoff bubble in the Pacific Division and can't afford to drop points against inferior opponents, which makes this trip to St. Louis a must-win situation dressed up as a routine Friday night game.

The Blues (26-29-10, 62 points) are 10 points back of the final playoff spot and, realistically, their season is winding down toward a lottery position. St. Louis has been struggling defensively for weeks, allowing 3.5 or more goals per game during their recent rough stretch, and that's the last thing you want to see when McDavid and Draisaitl are walking into your building. The Blues likely shed some assets at the trade deadline, which means the roster skating out tonight could look thinner than it did a week ago. Enterprise Center will still be loud, and the Blues have the kind of proud hockey culture that doesn't quit, but the talent gap here is real and significant.

Edmonton's -162 moneyline tells you everything you need to know about how the market views this matchup. The Oilers are the clearly superior team, and the Blues' defensive struggles make them particularly vulnerable against Edmonton's top-end offensive firepower. Draisaitl's ability to operate as a lethal secondary scoring threat takes so much pressure off McDavid and creates a situation where opposing defenses are essentially picking their poison every shift. The 6.5 total is juiced for a reason, too. When you combine Edmonton's explosive attack with St. Louis's leaky defense, goals feel inevitable. The Blues' goaltending will need to be spectacular to keep this one close, and that's been an inconsistent proposition all season.

Here's the thing about this game that makes it compelling despite the lopsided matchup: Edmonton's away record hasn't been nearly as dominant as their overall numbers suggest. The Oilers have been streaky on the road, and they've shown a tendency to come out flat in buildings where they're heavy favorites. St. Louis at +136 isn't the worst price if you believe in the Blues' ability to play spoiler and catch Edmonton on a night when their intensity doesn't match the moment. But banking against McDavid in a game with genuine playoff implications for his team feels like a losing proposition more often than not. The puck line at EDM -1.5 (+165) is the interesting play here, because when Edmonton gets rolling against weaker defensive teams, they don't just win, they bury them.

Game 2
NHL TV

Kings @ Islanders

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | UBS Arena, Elmont, NY
Puck Line
NYI -1.5 (+185)
Moneyline
NYI -130 / LAK +110
Total
O/U 5.5

The Los Angeles Kings (26-32-7, 59 points) are having the kind of season that gets general managers fired and rosters blown up. At 59 points through 65 games, the Kings are firmly out of playoff contention and likely played the role of sellers at the trade deadline. This is a franchise that's been trying to bridge the gap between the Kopitar/Doughty era and whatever comes next, and the bridge has collapsed. LA's struggles have been comprehensive, too. They haven't been able to score consistently, their defensive structure has been inconsistent, and the goaltending hasn't been reliable enough to steal games they don't deserve to win. It's been a long season in Los Angeles, and traveling across the country to play a desperate Islanders team on a Friday night isn't exactly the kind of game that inspires confidence.

New York (32-31-2, 66 points) is in a much more interesting position. The Islanders sit at 66 points and are clinging to the fringes of the Eastern Conference playoff picture, which makes every remaining game feel like a do-or-die situation. UBS Arena has been a factor for the Isles this season, and the home crowd will bring energy for a game that carries legitimate postseason implications. The Islanders' identity has always been built around structured defensive play and goaltending, and if they can control the pace of this game and limit LA's transition opportunities, they should be able to grind out a result. The -130 moneyline reflects their home-ice advantage and the desperation factor that comes with fighting for your playoff life.

The 5.5 total is the lowest number on Friday's board, and it paints a picture of a tight, low-event game between two teams that aren't going to overwhelm you with offense. The Islanders play a grinding, physical style that slows games down, and the Kings don't have the offensive firepower to push the pace even if they wanted to. When you add in the possibility of Islanders goaltending standing on its head in a game with playoff implications, the under looks tempting. This is the kind of game where a 3-1 or 2-1 final score wouldn't surprise anyone. It's not going to be the most exciting hockey you've ever watched, but for the Islanders, it might be the most important game they've played all month.

The value question here centers on whether the Kings, playing out the string with nothing to play for, will even show up with the kind of effort required to compete in a meaningful game. LA at +110 isn't a terrible price if you think the Kings have enough pride to play hard regardless of the standings, but motivation matters in sports, and the Islanders have all of it while the Kings have none. That asymmetry in urgency is exactly the kind of edge that shows up in the final score. The Islanders need this one, and they're at home, and they're facing a team that's essentially already on vacation. That's a dangerous combination for anyone betting the underdog.
