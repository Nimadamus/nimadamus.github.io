11-Game Tuesday Slate

NBA March 10, 2026

Tuesday's massive 11-game slate is headlined by two nationally televised showdowns on NBC Peacock. The 47-17 Spurs host the 43-21 Celtics at 8 PM with Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox taking on a rejuvenated Boston squad that just got Jayson Tatum back from his Achilles injury. Then at 11 PM, Anthony Edwards and the 40-24 Timberwolves visit Luka Doncic and the 39-25 Lakers in a Western Conference clash with major playoff seeding implications. The league-best 45-18 Pistons visit the Nets, the Rockets host the Raptors, Phoenix visits the struggling Bucks, and five more games fill out the board.

Game 1

Grizzlies @ 76ers

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center, Philadelphia, PA
Spread
PHI -5.5
Moneyline
MEM +180 / PHI -218
Total
O/U 229.5

Memphis rolls into Philadelphia in the middle of what's been a deeply frustrating season for a franchise that had legitimate playoff aspirations before injuries derailed everything. The Grizzlies at 23-39 are a shell of what they could be at full strength, and facing a 34-29 Sixers team that's found some footing in the second half of the season, this is a tough spot on the road. Philadelphia has the home-court advantage at Wells Fargo Center, and their crowd has been electric during this push for playoff positioning. Joel Embiid's health, as always, is the variable that determines how high this team's ceiling is on any given night.

The 5.5-point spread suggests the market views this as a comfortable Philly win without it being a blowout. Memphis has young talent that can keep games competitive, Ja Morant remains one of the most explosive guards in the league when he's on the floor, but the Grizzlies don't have the roster depth to hang with a motivated 76ers team over 48 minutes. Philadelphia's defense at home has been stingy this season, and they're playing with the urgency of a team that knows every win matters in a crowded Eastern Conference playoff race. The 229.5 total is moderate, reflecting both the Sixers' defensive capability at home and the Grizzlies' offensive inconsistency on the road.

What makes this game interesting from an analytical standpoint is Memphis's approach. The Grizzlies are in development mode, giving extended minutes to young players and trying to figure out what their core looks like moving forward. That can lead to streaky offense and lapses on defense, which is exactly what a team like Philadelphia capitalizes on. Tyrese Maxey has been sensational this season, and his speed in transition can punish the kind of defensive breakdowns that young teams are prone to. If Embiid is right and Maxey is cooking, this could get out of hand early.

Philadelphia's positioning in the Eastern Conference standings adds urgency to this matchup. At 34-29, they're firmly in the playoff picture but not comfortably seated. Every home game against a team below .500 is essentially a must-win if the Sixers want to avoid the play-in tournament. Memphis doesn't have the same kind of pressure, which can cut both ways. Sometimes the lack of stakes frees a team to play loose and competitive, and other times it leads to a flat effort on the road against a desperate opponent. The 5.5-point line feels like it's accounting for both possibilities.

Game 2

Pistons @ Nets

Tuesday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center, Brooklyn, NY
Spread
DET -14.5
Moneyline
DET -1000 / BKN +650
Total
O/U 216.5

Fourteen and a half points. That's where the market has set this one, and honestly, it might not be enough. The Pistons at 45-18 own the best record in the entire NBA, a sentence that still feels surreal given where this franchise was just two seasons ago. Detroit's transformation has been the story of the 2025-26 season, and Cade Cunningham's development into a legitimate MVP candidate has been the engine driving everything. Walking into Barclays Center to face a 16-47 Nets team is about as soft a spot as exists on the NBA schedule, and the -1000 moneyline tells you this game is essentially a formality on the betting board.

Brooklyn is in full tank mode. The Nets have been one of the worst teams in basketball this season, and their 16-47 record reflects a roster that was intentionally stripped for draft capital. There's no star power here, no go-to scorer who can keep the team in games against elite competition, and no defensive identity that could slow down a juggernaut like Detroit. The Nets are playing for ping-pong balls and development reps, and when that's your motivation, you're not going to match the intensity of a team that's chasing the best record in the league and a potential first seed in the playoffs.

The 216.5 total is one of the lower numbers on Tuesday's board, and it reflects the likelihood that Detroit builds a huge lead and then coasts through the fourth quarter with their bench unit. When the starters sit, the scoring dries up, and that drags the total down. It's a common pattern in these massive mismatches. The first three quarters might play at a 230-pace, but the fourth quarter becomes a glorified scrimmage with players on both sides who aren't getting minutes in meaningful games.

For Detroit, this is a maintenance game. Keep the engine running, get the starters their reps, and then shut it down early. For Brooklyn, it's another step in the process, another game closer to a top draft pick, and another opportunity for their young players to get experience against the league's best. The 14.5-point spread is massive, but when you look at the talent disparity and the motivation gap, it's hard to argue it should be smaller. The Pistons are a legitimate contender; the Nets are a legitimate rebuilding project. The outcome isn't in question, only the margin.

Moneyline

O/U 242.5

Washington continues its tour of misery through the NBA schedule, and Tuesday night's trip to South Beach promises more of the same. The Wizards at 16-47 are tied for the worst record in the league, and heading into Kaseya Center to face a 36-29 Heat team with serious playoff ambitions is about as unfavorable a matchup as it gets. Miami is laying 14.5 points, and the -1050 moneyline essentially prices Washington out of any serious consideration. This is the kind of game where Erik Spoelstra's squad should be able to name their score.

The 242.5 total is the highest on Tuesday's entire board, and it tells you the market expects points in bunches from both sides. Miami's offense at home can be explosive, and Washington's defense has been among the worst in the NBA in terms of points allowed per game. The Wizards simply don't have the personnel to slow down a Heat team that moves the ball well, shoots effectively from three, and plays with the kind of competitive fire that Spoelstra demands every night. On the other end, Washington's young players will get their scoring opportunities because Miami can afford to be aggressive on defense without worrying about falling behind.

Miami's playoff positioning makes this a trap-free spot. At 36-29, the Heat need these wins against inferior opponents to solidify their place in the Eastern Conference playoff picture. Spoelstra won't let his team sleepwalk through this one, and the culture of accountability in Miami means that even against the worst team in the league, the intensity level stays consistent. The Heat's home-court advantage at Kaseya Center has been one of their best weapons this season, and the crowd provides an energy boost that makes it even harder for road teams to compete.

For the Wizards, there's nothing to play for here except individual development and draft lottery positioning. The 14.5-point spread is enormous, but it's a reflection of the reality that these two teams are in completely different stratospheres. Washington's roster is built for the future, not the present, and the present involves getting hammered in buildings like Kaseya Center against teams with veteran leadership and championship aspirations. The only suspense is whether the Heat cover or whether they pull their starters early enough for the Wizards to make the final score look respectable.

Spread

Moneyline

O/U 239.5

Dallas has become the league's punching bag, and there's no relief in sight. The Mavericks at 21-43 are paying the price for the Luka Doncic trade to the Lakers, and the roster that remains is a collection of developmental pieces trying to survive the rest of the season with some dignity intact. Atlanta at 33-31 isn't an elite team, but they're a squad with legitimate playoff aspirations and enough talent to dominate a depleted opponent. Trae Young's playmaking and scoring give the Hawks a reliable offensive engine, and at State Farm Arena, the home crowd provides an energy advantage that Dallas simply can't match in their current state.

The 9.5-point spread is substantial, and it speaks to the gap between these rosters. Atlanta's depth, their ability to score from multiple positions, and their home-court advantage all point toward a comfortable victory. Dallas doesn't have a consistent 20-point scorer right now, and without that kind of shot creation, keeping pace with an Atlanta offense that can put up 120 in their building is nearly impossible. The Mavericks' defense has been porous all season, ranking among the worst in the league in defensive efficiency, and the Hawks have the shooters and creators to exploit those weaknesses all night long.

The 239.5 total is one of the higher numbers on the board, and it reflects the expectation that this game will be played at a fast pace with minimal defensive resistance from Dallas. Atlanta likes to push the tempo, and the Mavericks' transition defense has been a weak point all year. When you combine a team that runs with a team that can't get back, you get high-scoring affairs where the over is very much in play. Young's ability to find open shooters in transition and in the half-court creates easy scoring opportunities that keep the points flowing.

There's a part of this game that matters for Atlanta's playoff seeding. At 33-31, the Hawks are right in the thick of the Eastern Conference race, and every home win against a weak opponent keeps their positioning intact. Dallas is essentially playing exhibition basketball at this point, trying to evaluate young talent and figure out which pieces fit the next iteration of this franchise. The contrast in motivation alone makes this a tough spot for the Mavericks to compete, and the 9.5-point spread feels reasonable given the circumstances.

Game 5

Raptors @ Rockets

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -4.5
Moneyline
TOR +160 / HOU -192
Total
O/U 216.5

This is a fascinating cross-conference matchup between two teams that have been among the more pleasant surprises this season. Toronto at 36-27 has exceeded expectations thanks to Scottie Barnes's continued development and the addition of Brandon Ingram, who was acquired from New Orleans. Ingram's ability to create his own shot, score from the midrange, and operate as a secondary playmaker has given the Raptors an offensive dimension they were missing. Houston at 39-24 sits fourth in the loaded Western Conference, and Kevin Durant has been everything the franchise hoped when they acquired him last July. Durant's scoring versatility makes Houston dangerous in any matchup.

The 4.5-point spread in Houston's favor reflects their home-court advantage at Toyota Center and the slight edge in overall roster quality. The Rockets have built a deep, balanced team around Durant, but they're still dealing with the massive loss of Fred VanVleet, who's out for the season with a torn ACL. That absence has left Houston's backcourt thinner than they'd like, and it's a vulnerability that Toronto can exploit with their ball-handling and perimeter creation. The Raptors aren't pushovers by any stretch, and their 36-27 record proves they belong in the conversation with the league's better teams.

The 216.5 total is the lowest number tied on the board, and it suggests a defensively oriented game where both teams prioritize execution over pace. Houston's defense has been solid this season, using their length and athleticism to contest shots at the rim and on the perimeter. Toronto similarly plays a structured defensive style under their system, and when both teams are locked in on that end, the scoring opportunities become harder to create. This won't be a track meet. It'll be a chess match where the team that executes better in the half-court earns the win.

Durant against Ingram is the individual matchup to watch. Both are long, skilled scorers who can get buckets from anywhere on the floor, and both can impact the game on the defensive end when engaged. Durant has the edge in experience and overall scoring gravity, but Ingram's shot-creating ability can keep Toronto within striking distance. This is the kind of game where the fourth quarter comes down to which team's best player makes the biggest plays, and in those situations, Durant's resume gives Houston the edge.

Game 6

Suns @ Bucks

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee, WI
Spread
PHX -1.5
Moneyline
PHX -142 / MIL +120
Total
O/U 216.5

Two franchises heading in opposite directions meet in Milwaukee, and the fact that Phoenix is a road favorite at Fiserv Forum tells you everything you need to know about where the Bucks have fallen. Milwaukee at 27-36 is in the midst of one of the most disappointing seasons in recent NBA history. A franchise that won a championship just a few years ago is now below .500, and the Giannis Antetokounmpo calf strain that sidelined the Greek Freak for 15 consecutive games only deepened the pain. The good news for Bucks fans: Giannis is expected to return Tuesday night, though he'll likely be on a minutes restriction. In his three limited March appearances, he's averaged just 26.0 minutes per game as the team eases him back. When healthy, Giannis is still a force at 30.6 points, 10.7 rebounds, and 6.5 assists per game, but whether he can produce at that level in a restricted role is the biggest question hanging over this matchup.

Phoenix at 37-27 has quietly put together a solid season since reshaping the roster last summer. After trading KD to Houston, the club pivoted to a younger, deeper roster, and the results have been encouraging. Devin Booker remains the centerpiece at 24.8 points and 6.0 assists per game, and the Suns have surrounded him with enough shooting, defense, and playmaking to compete in the Western Conference. Being a 1.5-point road favorite in Milwaukee is a testament to how far Phoenix has come and how far the Bucks have fallen. A year ago, this line would've been unthinkable.

The 216.5 total suggests a grind-it-out affair, and both teams' defensive tendencies support that number. Milwaukee's defense can still be effective when Giannis is locked in as a rim protector, even in limited minutes, and the Bucks' length on the perimeter creates challenges for perimeter-oriented offenses like Phoenix. On the other end, the Suns' defense has been improved this season, with better effort and more consistent rotations than they showed in previous years. Neither team is going to push the pace, and the half-court execution will determine the outcome.

Giannis will be the best player on the floor, and on any given night, that's enough to swing the result. But the minutes restriction creates uncertainty that the market has clearly baked into this line. If Giannis plays 30+ minutes and looks like himself, Milwaukee could steal this at home. If he's limited to the mid-20s and sits for extended stretches, Phoenix's depth and Booker's scoring should be enough to pull away. The 1.5-point spread screams a coin-flip, and that's exactly what this should be, with Giannis's workload as the variable that tips the scales.

Celtics @ Spurs

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX
Spread
SA -3.5
Moneyline
BOS +130 / SA -155
Total
O/U 222.5

This is the game of the night, and NBC Peacock subscribers are getting a genuine treat. The Spurs at 47-17 have the second-best record in the entire NBA, and the transformation of this franchise from lottery dweller to legitimate title contender has been one of the great stories in recent league history. Victor Wembanyama is averaging 23.8 points, 11.2 rebounds, and 2.9 assists per game while being the most disruptive defensive presence in basketball. His 7-foot-4 frame, combined with his skill level and basketball IQ, creates problems that no opponent has been able to consistently solve. And then there's De'Aaron Fox, acquired from Sacramento in February 2025, who's averaging 18.9 points and 6.3 assists and has given San Antonio the dynamic lead guard they needed to complement Wembanyama's inside-out dominance.

Boston at 43-21 just got a massive boost with the return of Jayson Tatum from his Achilles injury. Tatum made his long-awaited comeback on March 6 against Dallas, posting 15 points, 12 rebounds, and 7 assists in 27 minutes on a restriction. He followed that up with 20 points against Cleveland, clearly shaking off the rust. His presence completely changes the Celtics' ceiling, giving them the go-to scorer and two-way wing that Jaylen Brown (29.2 PPG, 7.2 RPG, 5.1 APG) shouldered alone for months. The question is how many minutes Tatum gets Tuesday and how quickly he can integrate back into the flow alongside Brown, Derrick White, and Jrue Holiday.

The 3.5-point spread in San Antonio's favor is rooted in their home-court dominance at Frost Bank Center, which has been one of the toughest places to play in the NBA this season. The Spurs feed off their crowd's energy, and Wembanyama at home is a different animal than on the road. His shot-blocking, his scoring in the paint, and his passing from the high post all reach another level when the building is rocking. Boston's ball movement will test San Antonio's defensive rotations, and the Celtics' three-point shooting can create problems if the Spurs' perimeter defenders get lost in switches. But Wembanyama's length at the rim provides a safety net that most teams simply don't have.

The 222.5 total is modest, and it reflects the defensive excellence of both teams. San Antonio's defense, anchored by Wembanyama's rim protection and Fox's on-ball pressure, has been suffocating this season. Boston's defense has been elite all year thanks to their switching versatility and the defensive intelligence of Holiday and White, and Tatum's return only adds another long, switchable defender to the mix. The wrinkle is Tatum's minutes restriction. If he's limited to 25-28 minutes again, there will be stretches where Boston is operating without their best player, and San Antonio is absolutely the type of team that punishes those windows. Fox against Holiday is a premier point guard matchup, and Wembanyama against whatever Boston throws at him inside is the kind of chess match that defines high-level basketball. This one is worth staying up for.

O/U 229.5

Golden State's season has been defined by what could have been. The Warriors acquired Jimmy Butler in a February blockbuster, but his torn ACL on January 19 ended his campaign before the partnership with Stephen Curry ever had a chance to fully develop. Since losing Butler, the Warriors at 32-31 have been grinding it out, leaning on Curry's brilliance and the development of Jonathan Kuminga as a secondary scoring option. At Chase Center, they've managed to stay competitive enough to keep their playoff hopes alive, and these home games against below-.500 opponents are the ones they absolutely have to have. Chicago at 26-38 is in the midst of a transition year, and walking into San Francisco as 6.5-point underdogs is the consequence of a roster that's still figuring out its identity.

The Bulls traded Zach LaVine to the Kings, and that move fundamentally changed the direction of the franchise. Without LaVine's scoring punch, Chicago's offense has leaned heavily on young players who are still developing their games. There's talent on this roster, but it's raw, inconsistent, and not yet ready to compete consistently against the league's better teams. Golden State's home-court advantage at Chase Center has been significant this season, and the Warriors' experience in high-leverage games gives them an edge in close situations that young teams simply can't replicate.

Curry remains the engine that drives everything for Golden State, and when he's healthy and in rhythm, there's still no shooter in basketball who commands more defensive attention. His 27.2 points per game when on the floor speaks to his continued elite production, though knee issues have caused him to miss time this season. Kuminga has stepped into a larger role since Butler went down, and his athleticism and improving offensive repertoire have been pleasant surprises. The 229.5 total suggests a game that's played at a moderate pace, and Golden State's defensive effort at home has been solid enough to support that number.

The 6.5-point spread feels right for this matchup. Golden State has the talent advantage with Curry, the home-court advantage, and the motivation advantage. Chicago might keep it competitive for stretches, especially if their young guards get hot from three, but over 48 minutes, the Warriors' experience and firepower should assert itself. This is a game the Warriors need to take care of business in if they want to avoid the play-in tournament, and with Curry leading the charge at Chase Center, they should handle it.

Charlotte being a 3.5-point road favorite in Portland speaks to the Hornets' resurgence this season. At 32-33, Charlotte isn't going to wow anyone with their record, but they've been competitive all year and have the kind of young, athletic roster that can beat anyone on a given night. LaMelo Ball's playmaking remains one of the most exciting shows in the NBA, and his ability to create scoring opportunities for himself and his teammates gives the Hornets a reliable offensive engine. Portland at 31-34 is in a similar spot record-wise, but the market sees enough of a talent gap to make Charlotte the favorite on the road.

The Trail Blazers have been a fun watch this season despite the underwhelming record. Portland plays with pace and energy, their young core has shown flashes of real potential, and Moda Center remains one of the more underrated home-court advantages in the Western Conference. But Portland's defense has been inconsistent, particularly in the half-court, and Charlotte's ball movement and three-point shooting can exploit those lapses. The Hornets' perimeter creation, led by Ball and supplemented by their athletic wings, poses matchup problems that Portland's defensive personnel struggle to contain.

The 227.5 total is moderate, suggesting a game that's competitive but not a shootout. Both teams play at a reasonable pace, and neither has the kind of suffocating defense that holds opponents to the low 100s. This should be a game where both offenses get their looks, the lead changes multiple times, and the fourth quarter determines the outcome. Charlotte's edge in perimeter creation and overall talent makes them the rightful favorite, but Portland's home-court energy and youthful competitiveness should keep this close throughout.

This is a classic late-night West Coast game between two teams that are right on the bubble of the playoff picture. Neither can afford to lose games they're supposed to win, and the urgency on both sides should produce a competitive, entertaining game. Charlotte's slight edge in star power and offensive creation should be the difference, but don't be surprised if Portland makes this one sweat down the stretch. Road favorites of 3.5 in the NBA always carry risk, especially in buildings like Moda Center where the crowd can flip the energy in a heartbeat.

This is the battle of the league's two worst records, and it's the kind of game that only draft lottery enthusiasts and diehard fans of these franchises will circle on their calendars. Indiana at 15-49 has been decimated by the loss of Tyrese Haliburton, who tore his Achilles during the 2025 NBA Finals against the Thunder. Without their franchise point guard, the Pacers have been in free fall, and the supporting cast hasn't been able to compensate for the void he left. Sacramento at 15-50 has had an equally disastrous season, losing Zach LaVine (acquired from the Bulls) to a season-ending hand injury, Domantas Sabonis to a torn meniscus, and Dejounte Murray to injury as well.

The 3.5-point spread favoring Sacramento is based purely on home-court advantage, because there's precious little else separating these two rosters. The Kings at Golden 1 Center at least have the comfort of their own building and whatever energy the home crowd can muster for a game between two teams with a combined 30-99 record. Indiana on the road has been dreadful this season, and the travel to the West Coast for a late-night tipoff doesn't do them any favors. But the talent gap between these two depleted rosters is negligible, and either team could win this game on any given night.

The 236.5 total is one of the highest on the board, and it reflects two teams that simply don't play defense. When you remove the best players from both rosters and fill the floor with young, developing talent and end-of-bench guys getting extended minutes, defense becomes an afterthought. Both teams will run, shoot, and trade buckets with the freedom that comes from having nothing at stake. This is a game where the over looks inviting because neither team has any incentive to grind out possessions or play shutdown defense.

There's a certain entertainment value in watching two teams this bad go at it. The pressure is off, the young players get to show what they can do, and occasionally you see flashes of talent that make you understand why a franchise invested in someone. For Sacramento, LaVine's absence has been particularly painful because he was supposed to be the centerpiece of their competitive push this season. For Indiana, the entire campaign has been defined by Haliburton's injury, and everything since has been about survival mode. This one's for the die-hards and the tank watchers.

The nightcap on NBC Peacock is a legitimate Western Conference heavyweight fight, and the playoff seeding implications are massive. Minnesota at 40-24 sits third in the West, just one game ahead of the 39-25 Lakers in fifth. Anthony Edwards has been on an absolute tear over his last 10 games, averaging 29.5 points per game with the kind of explosive athleticism and shot-making ability that puts him in the conversation with the league's very best players. On the other side, Luka Doncic continues to be the NBA's leading scorer at 32.5 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 8.4 assists per game, operating at a level that's made the Lakers a legitimate contender since he arrived from Dallas in February 2025.

The 1.5-point spread favoring Minnesota is fascinating because the Lakers own a 2-0 series lead this season. Los Angeles has found a way to solve the Timberwolves in both meetings, which makes the market's lean toward Minnesota feel like a statement about recent form. The Wolves are 8-2 over their last 10 games, one of the hottest stretches in the NBA, while the Lakers are 6-4 in that span. Minnesota's defense has been elite during this run, and their 37.2% three-point shooting (second in the West) has given them the offensive floor spacing to maximize Edwards's driving and finishing ability. The Wolves are also a respectable 18-12 on the road, proving they can win away from Target Center.

The big question mark for Los Angeles is LeBron James, who's listed as day-to-day with an elbow issue. Even at 41 years old, LeBron's presence changes everything for the Lakers. He's the facilitator, the defensive communicator, and the clutch performer who elevates everyone around him. Without LeBron, the Lakers become the Luka Doncic show, and while Doncic's 32.5 PPG on 47.4% shooting is spectacular, Minnesota's defense is built to make life difficult for elite individual scorers. Julius Randle at 21.4 points, 7.0 rebounds, and 5.3 assists provides a secondary scoring punch, but the Lakers need LeBron to compete at the highest level against a team this talented.

The 233.5 total is elevated, and both offenses have the firepower to push past it. The Lakers are averaging 115.1 points per game, and Minnesota's 116.0 points per game shows their offense has been humming. Edwards versus Doncic is one of the most compelling individual matchups in basketball right now, two young superstars at the peak of their powers going head-to-head with playoff positioning on the line. The atmosphere at Crypto.com Arena should be electric for the late-night window, and this has all the ingredients of a game that goes down to the final possession. The Western Conference standings are so tight that every game between contenders feels like a playoff preview, and this one might be the best game on the NBA calendar tonight.
