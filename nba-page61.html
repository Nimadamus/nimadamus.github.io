Lakers @ Cavaliers

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH
Spread
CLE -3.5 (-110)
Moneyline
LAL +124 / CLE -148
Total
O/U 234.5

This is your ESPN headliner for Wednesday night, and it's a fascinating matchup. The Lakers come in at 28-17, powered by the Luka Doncic-LeBron James combination that has transformed the Western Conference landscape, though they'll be without Austin Reaves (calf strain) for this one. Doncic is averaging a league-best 33.8 points per game and will attack a Cleveland defense that's dealing with the loss of Evan Mobley (calf, out 1-3 weeks). That frontcourt absence is massive for the Cavs.

Cleveland sits at 28-20 and is laying 3.5 points at home. Donovan Mitchell has been spectacular this season, but the Cavaliers' defensive identity takes a hit without Mobley anchoring the interior. The 234.5 total reflects the offensive talent on both sides, and with neither team at full strength defensively, expect a high-scoring, back-and-forth contest worthy of the national TV spotlight. LeBron facing his former team always adds an extra layer of intensity.

Atlanta visits Boston as 6.5-point underdogs in a matchup that looks much different than it would have a few weeks ago. The 230.5 total suggests a moderately paced game, and Atlanta's transition period makes them vulnerable against a Boston defense that knows how to exploit uncertainty. This is a lot of points, but the talent gap is real, and the Celtics' home-court advantage gives them a significant edge in this spot.
