Steelers @ Eagles

Saturday, 4:30 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field | FOX

Pennsylvania pride is on the line as the Steelers travel to Philadelphia for a rare cross-state showdown. The Eagles have been dominant at home all season, with Saquon Barkley running wild behind a dominant offensive line. Philadelphia's rushing attack has been nearly impossible to stop, and Pittsburgh's defense faces its toughest test of the season.

The Steelers are fighting for playoff positioning in a loaded AFC. Russell Wilson has been solid if unspectacular, doing just enough to win games while the defense carries the load. But this is a different level of competition. The Eagles are playing at an elite level, and Lincoln Financial Field will be rocking for this rare matchup.

Keys to Victory

Pittsburgh needs to contain Barkley early and force Hurts into uncomfortable situations. If the Eagles can establish the run, they'll control clock and wear down Pittsburgh's defense. The Steelers' best chance is to create turnovers and keep this game close into the fourth quarter.

Texans @ Dolphins

Saturday, 8:15 PM ET | Hard Rock Stadium | NFL Network

The Saturday night showcase brings Houston to South Florida in a battle between two teams with playoff aspirations. C.J. Stroud has been excellent in his second season, leading the Texans with poise and precision. Houston's defense has been opportunistic, creating turnovers at a high rate.

Miami's speed-based offense can be explosive when Tua Tagovailoa is healthy and in rhythm. Tyreek Hill and Jaylen Waddle are impossible to cover consistently, and the Dolphins can score in bunches when firing on all cylinders. The weather in Miami favors their style of play.

Matchup to Watch

Stroud against Miami's secondary is the key battle. Houston's passing game has been efficient, but the Dolphins can take chances with their speed at cornerback. Expect a shootout in the warm South Florida night as both offenses have the weapons to put up points.
