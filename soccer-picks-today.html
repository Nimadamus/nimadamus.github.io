Liverpool's midfield has struggled to control games on the road in recent weeks, and Brighton's ability to win the ball high and transition quickly could exploit that vulnerability. If Liverpool come in with any kind of fatigue from their Champions League exertions, this could get ugly. The total market reflects both teams' ability to score, and the Both Teams to Score line should see plenty of action. Liverpool's defensive frailties away from Anfield are well-documented this season, and Brighton won't sit back and absorb pressure. This has goals written all over it.
