NBA Playoffs First Round - Tuesday

Durant Returns, Wemby Rolls, Celtics Push For 2-0 Across Three NBA Game 2s

Three NBA playoff Game 2s close out the Tuesday night slate. Boston took Game 1 over Philadelphia 123-91 and opens as a 13.5-point favorite at TD Garden at 7 PM ET on Peacock and NBC. Victor Wembanyama scored 35 on 6-of-6 from three in a Spurs Game 1 rout of Portland and the Blazers return to the Frost Bank Center at 8 PM on NBC down 11.5. The closer features Kevin Durant's game-time decision for the Rockets at Crypto.com Arena against a short-handed Lakers team that took Game 1 107-98 behind LeBron's 19 and 13.

Game 2 - Featured
NBC

Rockets @ Lakers

Tuesday, 10:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA

The 2-seed Houston Rockets visit the 7-seed Los Angeles Lakers for Game 2 after Los Angeles took Game 1 by a 107-98 final at Crypto.com Arena on Sunday. Houston opens as a 4.5-point road favorite at -175 on the moneyline with the Lakers at +145. Total 205.5. The Game 1 result came with Kevin Durant out (right knee contusion), Luka Doncic out (left ankle), and Austin Reaves out (lower-body issue), and the market expects at least Durant to return for Tuesday night. The 4.5-point road favorite price despite a 0-1 series deficit is entirely about the Durant announcement.

LeBron James at 41 years old played 38 minutes in Game 1 and controlled the Lakers' offense with 19 points on 60 percent shooting and 13 assists. Luke Kennard provided bench-scoring depth that the Rockets' Durant-less rotation could not match. Bronny James checked in during the fourth quarter and became part of the first father-son duo to share the floor in an NBA playoff game. The historical footnote is real. The on-court implication is that the Lakers have a playable depth rotation even when the starting core is injury-compromised, and Los Angeles is going to keep running the LeBron-led facilitation offense until Houston makes them stop.

Kevin Durant's right knee contusion was suffered in practice on April 15, and the Rockets have been publicly optimistic about his Game 2 availability. A fully healthy Durant in Game 2 produces the Houston offensive baseline that finished second in the Western Conference during the regular season. Alperen Sengun's low-post scoring becomes secondary rather than primary. Amen Thompson's transition offense and Jabari Smith Jr.'s corner threes open up. Fred VanVleet's late-clock veteran navigation becomes a lever rather than an obligation. Houston without Durant produced 98 points on sub-40 percent shooting. Houston with Durant produces closer to 115-to-120 in the same venue.

If Luka Doncic and Austin Reaves are cleared for Game 2, the Lakers become a legitimate first-round threat that pushes the Rockets to a 2-0 series deficit. If either or both are out again, Los Angeles has to reproduce the LeBron-carried Game 1 template, which is possible but requires another 38-minute, 19-on-12-shot, 13-assist performance that was already elite at age 41. The 4.5-point spread is a market prediction about the injury news. The expected Game 2 point environment tracks with Durant's status. Tipoff 10:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. Game 2
Peacock

76ers @ Celtics

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA

The Boston Celtics host the Philadelphia 76ers for Game 2 at TD Garden at 7 PM ET on Peacock and NBC Sports after Boston took Game 1 by a 123-91 final on Sunday afternoon. The Celtics open as 13.5-point home favorites with the total sitting at 216.5. The 13.5 number is the highest spread on the Tuesday NBA slate and reflects the Game 1 margin and Boston's season-long home-court dominance. When the Celtics have played as 13.5-plus-point favorites this season, they've gone 5-3 against the spread. The 76ers are 0-5 against the spread when playing as 13.5-plus-point underdogs, which is the cleanest negative ATS indicator any team carried into the playoffs.

Jayson Tatum's recovery from the 2025 Achilles injury has been the central storyline of the Celtics' 2026 season. His Game 1 return to playoff basketball was the kind of controlled, volume-restricted minute load that reflects Joe Mazzulla's season-long management plan. Tatum produced efficient scoring in 30 minutes. Jaylen Brown anchored the perimeter offense. Derrick White's point-of-attack defense generated the live-ball turnovers that fueled Boston's transition offense in the third quarter. The 32-point Game 1 margin is the cleanest evidence Boston has offered all season that a fully healthy Tatum is a first-team All-NBA caliber playoff scorer, and the 76ers' injury-depleted rotation did not have a counter.

Philadelphia's Game 2 path requires Joel Embiid to produce a dominant scoring game against Kristaps Porzingis' perimeter defense and against any trap or double-team looks the Celtics throw at him. Embiid's Game 1 scoring was efficient in possessions but the volume couldn't match Boston's five-man scoring distribution. Tyrese Maxey needs to hit threes at a clip that actually puts pressure on Derrick White's defensive assignment. Paul George has to find his offensive rhythm against Jrue Holiday's coverage. None of those three carried the kind of scoring impact in Game 1 that a 13.5-point underdog needs to cover on the road.

The 216.5 total reflects both teams' average playoff scoring environment but underestimates Boston's tendency to hit the three-ball in bunches in Game 2 after a dominant Game 1. Payton Pritchard off the bench, Sam Hauser in garbage-time minutes, and Luke Kornet's screen-setting all produce the kind of Celtics offensive efficiency that can push the total toward 220. Philadelphia's defensive intensity in a must-win Game 2 could keep the game closer in the fourth quarter, but the Boston roster depth means the total is more likely to push toward the over than to settle under 216.5. Tipoff 7:00 PM ET on Peacock.

Game 2
NBC

Trail Blazers @ Spurs

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX

The San Antonio Spurs host the Portland Trail Blazers for Game 2 at the Frost Bank Center at 8 PM ET on NBC and Peacock after San Antonio took Game 1 by a 111-98 final on Sunday. The Spurs open as 11.5-point home favorites with moneyline at -650 for San Antonio and +470 for Portland. Total sits at approximately 220.5. Victor Wembanyama produced the kind of Game 1 performance that defined him as the 2026 playoff breakout star, scoring 35 points and shooting 6-of-6 from three-point range with 11 rebounds and three assists. His shooting splits in Game 1 were structurally impossible to game-plan against in real time, and the Trail Blazers' defensive coverage was exposed as insufficient at every level of contest.

Wembanyama's regular-season average of 25 points, 11.5 rebounds, and 3.1 assists on 51.2 percent field-goal shooting and 34.9 percent from three was the baseline for a second-team All-NBA selection. The 2026 playoffs are where Wembanyama's ceiling starts to project into the kind of franchise-carrying postseason scoring that is typically reserved for top-three players in the league. His 6-of-6 from three in Game 1 was not a sustainable shooting line, but it was a demonstration of the range and confidence he has built across his third NBA season. Game 2 is going to feature Portland throwing different defensive looks at him, which is a more productive strategy than the Game 1 one-on-one coverage.

Portland's Game 2 adjustments start with Deni Avdija taking primary defensive responsibility on Wembanyama rather than letting Toumani Camara handle the matchup. Avdija has the length, strength, and foot speed to at least contest Wembanyama's perimeter shots without giving up rim-protection positioning. Scoot Henderson needs to produce aggressive rim pressure that forces Wembanyama to commit to weak-side rotations rather than sitting comfortably in drop coverage. Shaedon Sharpe has to hit enough threes to stretch San Antonio's defense and create the kind of driving lanes that Portland's secondary scorers require.

The 11.5 spread is fat by playoff standards and reflects Wembanyama's gravity rather than a full accounting of Portland's capability. The Trail Blazers at +11.5 have a legitimate cover path if Avdija contests Wembanyama's threes at even a replacement-level rate and if Sharpe produces 18-plus points. The outright upset requires Wembanyama to either foul out or shoot sub-40 percent from the floor, which are low-probability outcomes. The 220.5 total is shapeable in either direction depending on pace. If San Antonio pushes the ball and Portland tries to keep up, the over has value. If the game settles into a halfcourt grind, the under cashes. Tipoff 8:00 PM ET on NBC.
