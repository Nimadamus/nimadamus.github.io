Here's the thing about James Madison: this program has completely transformed into a Group of Five juggernaut, and the numbers back it up in every conceivable way. The Dukes rank 21st nationally in total offense at 451.4 yards per game while simultaneously holding opponents to just 253.5 yards per game (5th nationally). That's the kind of two-way dominance that makes 22.5-point spreads look reasonable.

The efficiency metrics tell an even more compelling story. JMU sits 10th in scoring offense (37.8 PPG), 10th in scoring defense (16.0 PPG allowed), and converts third downs at an elite 50.3% clip (9th in FBS). They've won seven of eight conference games by double digits, with four victories coming by 30-plus points. This isn't a team that plays close games against inferior opponents.

Troy has been scrappy all season, covering the spread eight times in twelve games despite entering as underdogs seven times. But the Trojans' offense ranks 22nd-worst in the FBS at just 325.6 yards per game, and they're averaging only 25.2 PPG (86th nationally). Their time of possession disadvantage is glaring: Troy averages 30:01 compared to JMU's nation-leading 34:20. The Dukes will control the ball, control the game, and control their CFP destiny.

Betting Trends

James Madison is 11-0 when favored this season and has not lost a game as the moneyline favorite all year. Troy is 4-3 SU as underdogs but hasn't faced a spread this large against competition this elite.

This game features the most explosive offense in all of college football traveling to an undefeated home team that's playing its final game before its coach leaves for Florida. The storylines are incredible, but the numbers are even better. North Texas leads the entire FBS in scoring (46.8 PPG) and total yards (511.8 YPG), and they've done it with a quarterback leading the nation in passing yards.

Drew Mestemaker's 3,835 passing yards top all FBS signal-callers, and the Mean Green complement that air attack with Caleb Hawkins' 1,229 rushing yards and 23 touchdowns. North Texas has scored 50-plus points in five of their last six games. This offense isn't just good; it's historically productive. They've covered the spread in 10 of 12 games when favored by at least 2.5 points, and nine of their twelve games have sailed over the total.

Tulane's home-field advantage is significant. The Green Wave are 6-0 at Yulman Stadium with the first undefeated home record in stadium history. But their pass defense allows 251.9 yards per game, which is exactly what North Texas wants to exploit. The 66.5 total is the highest of championship weekend for a reason. Tulane owns the head-to-head series 2-0 in recent matchups, but they haven't faced an offense operating at this level. The winner of this game locks up a spot in the College Football Playoff.

Betting Trends

The OVER has cashed in 9 of 12 North Texas games (75%). Both coaches are leaving for Power Four jobs (Jon Sumrall to Florida, Eric Morris to Oklahoma State), creating a "last dance" mentality.

Ashton Jeanty is having one of the greatest rushing seasons in college football history. With 2,288 yards on the ground, the Heisman Trophy contender has single-handedly carried Boise State to an 11-1 record and the cusp of the College Football Playoff. When these teams met in October, Jeanty and the Broncos torched UNLV for 558 total yards and 56 points. That blowout result explains why Boise State opened as favorites despite UNLV initially being lined as road favorites before the October meeting.

The Broncos' defense is generating pressure at an elite rate, ranking 2nd nationally with 45 sacks. That pass rush creates chaos, and UNLV quarterback Matthew Sluka will have to navigate constant pressure if the Rebels want revenge. Boise State's only loss came by three points at Oregon, which tells you everything about how close this team is to being undefeated.

UNLV enters on a four-game winning streak with victories over Houston and Kansas against Power Four competition. The Rebels are 10-2 with a 6-1 conference mark and have proven they belong in this conversation. But the history is damning: Boise State has won 10 straight against UNLV dating back to 1977. This is also Boise State's final game as a Mountain West member before moving to the new-look Pac-12. The motivation to send the program out with a championship is palpable.

Key Injury Note

Boise State starting QB Maddux Madsen has missed the last three games with a lower leg injury but could return for this matchup. His availability will significantly impact the offensive game plan.

Texas Tech's defense is historically good. The Red Raiders allow just 11.2 points per game, the best mark in all of college football. Let that sink in. When you combine that with the nation's third-best scoring offense at 43.2 PPG, you get a team that creates massive point differentials week after week. Texas Tech has covered the spread 11 times in 12 games, including a perfect 7-0 ATS record when favored by double digits against Power Four competition.

The previous meeting was a massacre. Texas Tech demolished BYU 29-7, holding the Cougars to just 255 total yards. BYU didn't even score until the fourth quarter. The Red Raiders' defensive success rate leads the nation, and their ability to suffocate opposing rushing attacks (68.9 yards per game allowed) directly attacks BYU's strength. The Cougars average 194.5 rushing yards per game, but that number means nothing against this defensive front.

BYU's path to the CFP likely requires a victory here, but the matchup couldn't be worse. Bear Bachmeier has thrown 14 touchdowns against 4 interceptions, but he'll face a defense that hasn't allowed anyone to move the ball consistently all season. Texas Tech is seeking their first-ever Big 12 title, and with Behren Morton throwing for 2,428 yards and 20 touchdowns, they have the offensive firepower to make a statement.

Texas Tech opened at -11.5 and has been bet up to -13.5 with heavy public action. BYU is 1-9 SU and 4-6 ATS as underdogs of 13+ points in their last 10 such games. The Red Raiders are 10-2 ATS overall.

Alabama already beat Georgia this season. That's the elephant in the room. On September 27th in Athens, the Crimson Tide walked into Sanford Stadium and left with a 24-21 victory. Now they meet again on neutral turf in Atlanta, and the betting market has Georgia as only 2.5-point favorites. That's a statement about how close these programs are right now.

The ATS splits tell an interesting story. Georgia is just 5-7 against the spread this season, consistently failing to cover as favorites. Alabama, meanwhile, is 8-4 ATS and 1-0 when lined as underdogs (they won that game outright). Under Kalen DeBoer, the Tide have embraced the underdog mentality. Last year's 41-34 victory over Georgia came as underdogs. In fact, Alabama has been an underdog in six of their last 10 matchups with Georgia dating back to 2012, winning five of those games.

The defensive matchup favors Alabama. The Crimson Tide rank 8th nationally in passing yards allowed (158.3 per game) and 12th in total defense (282.6 yards per game). They're also 13th in turnover margin while sitting 11th in giveaways. Georgia's offense, while talented, hasn't dominated opponents the way previous Bulldog teams have. The Bulldogs rank 41st in total offense (415.7 YPG) and have been surprisingly vulnerable to turnovers (105th in takeaways). This is Alabama's fifth-straight SEC Championship Game appearance, and history suggests the underdog has real value.

Series History

Alabama leads the all-time series 45-26-4 and is 4-0 against Georgia in SEC Championship Games. The Bulldogs have won just one of the last 11 meetings. Ty Simpson has thrown for 3,056 yards with 25 TD and only 4 INT.

Virginia has owned this series recently, winning nine of the last 10 head-to-head matchups with an average point differential of +14.2. Just three weeks ago, the Cavaliers went to Durham and dominated Duke 34-17, limiting the Blue Devils to just 255 total yards and 11 first downs. The sample size is overwhelming, and Virginia's defensive prowess is the key.

The Cavaliers' defense ranks in the top 20 nationally in EPA per pass allowed and functions as a top-15 overall unit. Duke quarterback Darian Mensah has been sensational over the last nine games with a 20-to-2 touchdown-to-interception ratio, but he hasn't faced a defense of this caliber with this much film on him. The Blue Devils rank in the bottom half of the ACC in both total defense (409.8 YPG allowed) and scoring defense (33.3 PPG allowed), which creates obvious problems against a Virginia offense that can exploit those weaknesses.

This is Duke's first ACC Championship Game appearance since 2013, and they're seeking their first ACC title since 1989. The Blue Devils defied the odds just to get here, winning a tiebreaker with four other teams to clinch their spot. But Virginia's 8-4 ATS record and 5-3 mark as favorites of 3.5 or more suggests the Cavaliers know how to handle expectations. Neither program has ever won the ACC Championship, adding historical weight to every snap.

Historical Context

Virginia posted the best record in ACC play at 7-1. Duke went 6-2 in conference and won a multi-team tiebreaker. Neither program has won an ACC Championship, making this a legacy game for both coaches.

This is historic. For the first time in Big Ten Championship Game history, both participants enter undefeated. The winner becomes the No. 1 seed in the College Football Playoff and claims the Big Ten title. The stakes could not be higher. Ohio State leads the country in SP+ with the most complete overall profile on both sides of the ball, posting a staggering +29.3 scoring margin.

The Buckeyes' defense is operating at a level we rarely see in college football. They lead the nation in yards allowed (206.6 per game), passing yards allowed (126.6 per game), and rushing yards allowed (80.0 per game). No other team comes under 240 total yards allowed. Ohio State has held opponents to fewer than 10 combined touchdowns all season. Let that sink in. They're allowing just 7.8 points per game, which is absurd at the FBS level.

Indiana has been equally dominant, just in different ways. The Hoosiers lead the Big Ten in scoring (44.3 PPG) and total offense (483.8 YPG) while ranking 4th nationally in yards allowed (251.8 per game) and 2nd in points allowed (10.9 per game). Their +17 turnover margin leads all of college football. Fernando Mendoza is the Heisman betting favorite, throwing for 2,758 yards and 32 touchdowns against just 5 interceptions. Ohio State's ATS record of 10-1-1 is the best in college football, but Indiana's ability to protect the football and create takeaways makes them a legitimate threat to end the Buckeyes' perfect season.

Series Dominance

Ohio State has won 31 straight games against Indiana dating back to 1989, including last year's 38-15 victory that ended Indiana's perfect season bid. The Buckeyes are 5-1 all-time in Big Ten Championship Games while Indiana is making their first-ever appearance.
