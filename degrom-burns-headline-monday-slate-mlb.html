deGrom and Burns Headline a Nine-Game Monday

A lighter Monday card still packs star power. Jacob deGrom takes the ball in St. Louis, Cincinnati hands it to breakout right-hander Chase Burns, Jose Soriano headlines in Anaheim, and the Dodgers head to the desert to face the Diamondbacks. We break down all nine games with verified pitching, records, and market numbers.

Rangers at Cardinals

7:45 PM ET | Busch Stadium
Pitching
deGrom vs McGreevy
Moneyline
TEX -124 / STL +106
Venue
Busch Stadium

The headline arm of the night belongs to Texas, where Jacob deGrom takes the ball at Busch Stadium carrying a 3-4 record and a 3.77 ERA across eleven starts. The strikeout stuff is still there, with deGrom averaging better than six punchouts per outing, and even at this stage of his career he remains the kind of name that moves a market on his own. The Rangers being laid as minus-124 road favorites despite traveling tells you everything about how the betting market still values him on the mound.

Michael McGreevy counters for the Cardinals, and the matchup is a genuine contrast in profile and reputation. McGreevy sits at 3-4 with a sparkling 2.98 ERA, a contact-managing right-hander who has quietly outperformed his more famous opponent on a rate basis this season. St. Louis at plus-106 at home is no fluke; the Cardinals have the steadier recent run-prevention in this specific matchup, and their path is to make deGrom work deep counts, push his pitch count, and lean on McGreevy's command to keep the game low-scoring. The Rangers' edge is the upside of an ace who can take over a game when his stuff is sharp.

Royals at Reds

7:10 PM ET | Great American Ball Park
Pitching
Avila vs Burns
Moneyline
CIN -198 / KC +166
Venue
Great American Ball Park

This is the spot where Cincinnati's young ace gets the spotlight. Chase Burns has been one of the best stories in the National League, sitting at 4-1 with a 2.11 ERA that ranks among the league's elite, backed by a 1.04 WHIP and 48 strikeouts against 16 walks across 47 innings. His strikeout rate sits around 28 percent, and while that is a step down from his absurd rookie-year mark, it still gives the Reds a true difference-maker in a ballpark that can punish anyone who leaves the ball up. Among qualified NL starters, only Cristopher Sanchez has carried a lower ERA this season.

Luinder Avila draws the assignment for Kansas City, and the gap in profile is the reason the Reds are sitting as heavy minus-198 home favorites. Avila has been a strike-throwing, contact-oriented arm whose modest strikeout numbers leave little margin in a homer-friendly park, and the Royals will need their lineup to do the heavy lifting against Burns. Kansas City's only realistic path is to grind at-bats, run up Burns' pitch count, and try to get into the Cincinnati bullpen before the game gets away. The Reds' formula is simple: let their ace pile up strikeouts and protect a lead in front of him.

Rockies at Angels

9:38 PM ET | Angel Stadium
Pitching
Freeland vs Soriano
Moneyline
LAA -215 / COL +180
Venue
Angel Stadium

Jose Soriano headlines this one for the Angels, and his season has been one of the more remarkable arcs in baseball. Soriano opened the year with a historically dominant run, lowering his ERA to 0.24 through his first six starts, the best mark by any pitcher in MLB history over that span with a minimum of 30 innings. The right-hander's strikeout rate has hovered around 30 percent, and even after allowing 12 runs across 17 innings during a rougher May that pulled his numbers back toward normal, he remains the clear class of this pitching matchup. Los Angeles being laid as a steep minus-215 favorite reflects exactly that.

Kyle Freeland takes the ball for Colorado, the veteran left-hander leaning on guile and command rather than swing-and-miss, with a strikeout profile in the mid-single digits per start. The Rockies' challenge away from the thin air of Coors is always the same: they need length from their starter and timely contact, because they do not have the kind of bullpen depth to win a bullpen game on the road. Colorado at plus-180 is a price that respects Soriano's pedigree more than Freeland's, and the Angels' route is to let their best arm set the tone early and lean on their lineup to break a contact pitcher.

Dodgers at Diamondbacks

9:40 PM ET | Chase Field
Pitching
Sheehan vs E. Rodriguez
Moneyline
LAD -158 / ARI +134
Total
O/U 9

The marquee name-brand matchup of the late window sends the Dodgers into the desert to open a series with the Diamondbacks. Los Angeles hands the ball to Emmet Sheehan, a power arm whose upside has always been about missing bats, while Arizona counters with veteran left-hander Eduardo Rodriguez, who has averaged around four and a half strikeouts per start across eleven outings. The Dodgers open as minus-158 road favorites with the Diamondbacks at plus-134 and a total of nine, a number that reflects two dangerous lineups and a hitter-friendly environment at Chase Field.

The story here is the offenses as much as the arms. The Dodgers carry the deeper, more relentless lineup, and their edge is the ability to wear down a starter and feast on middle relief. Arizona's path is to get to Sheehan early before he settles into a rhythm, push the total over with their own bats, and turn this into the kind of slugfest that gives the home underdog a puncher's chance. With the number sitting at nine and the roof potentially open in the desert heat, this profiles as one of the higher-scoring environments on the board, and the team that wins the late-inning bullpen battle likely takes it.

Mets at Mariners

9:40 PM ET | T-Mobile Park
Pitching
Hancock for Seattle
Moneyline
SEA -142 / NYM +120
Venue
T-Mobile Park

Seattle's quiet breakout starter gets a primetime cross-country opponent here. Emerson Hancock has been one of the Mariners' best stories in 2026, carrying a 3.21 ERA with a 1.01 WHIP and 50 strikeouts, including a recent career-high 14-strikeout outburst on 21 swinging strikes, the most punchouts by any pitcher in a single game this season. The pitcher-friendly confines of T-Mobile Park play right into his strengths, and the Mariners being laid as minus-142 home favorites reflects both Hancock's form and the venue's tendency to suppress runs.

The Mets arrive as a plus-120 road underdog in a tough environment for visiting bats. New York's lineup has the talent to do damage against any starter, but T-Mobile Park is one of the harder places in the league to slug, and the formula for the Mets is to manufacture runs, work counts, and get into a Seattle bullpen rather than try to overpower Hancock in a park that rewards his swing-and-miss arsenal. Seattle's path is straightforward: let Hancock pile up strikeouts at home and lean on the spacious outfield to turn fly balls into outs.

Giants at Brewers

7:40 PM ET | American Family Field
Pitching
Roupp vs Drohan
Moneyline
MIL -158 / SF +134
Venue
American Family Field

San Francisco sends Landen Roupp to the mound in Milwaukee, and the right-hander has been a reliable source of strikeouts, averaging better than six per appearance across eleven outings. Roupp's swing-and-miss profile gives the Giants a chance to control the game if he can navigate a Brewers lineup that does damage at home, and San Francisco at plus-134 on the road is a price that suggests the market sees this as closer than the favorite tag implies.

Shane Drohan counters for Milwaukee, a lefty with a more modest strikeout profile sitting around two and a half per outing, which puts more pressure on the Brewers' lineup and bullpen to carry the night. Milwaukee being installed as a minus-158 home favorite leans heavily on home-field comfort and roster depth rather than a starting-pitching edge. The Brewers' path is to get traffic on the bases against Roupp and force the Giants into their middle relief; San Francisco's is to let Roupp miss bats and keep the game in reach for a lineup that can scratch out runs late.

White Sox at Twins

7:40 PM ET | Target Field
Pitching
Sandlin vs Joe Ryan
Moneyline
MIN -172 / CHW +144
Venue
Target Field

Minnesota holds the clear pitching edge in this AL Central matchup, with Joe Ryan taking the ball at Target Field. Ryan has been the steady anchor of the Twins' rotation, a strike-throwing right-hander whose fastball plays up in the zone and who has carried a strong stretch into this start. The Twins being laid as minus-172 home favorites is built almost entirely on the gap between the two starters and Minnesota's edge in roster quality over a rebuilding Chicago club.

David Sandlin draws the assignment for the White Sox, a young arm still establishing himself at the big-league level against a Twins lineup that can punish mistakes. Chicago at plus-144 is the kind of price that reflects a team in transition, and the formula for the visitors is to keep the game close early, lean on their bullpen, and hope Sandlin can give them length. For Minnesota, the path is to let Ryan set the tone, take advantage of any traffic against Sandlin, and protect a lead at home in a series they are expected to control.

Tigers at Rays

6:40 PM ET | George M. Steinbrenner Field
Matchup
Tigers at Rays
Moneyline
TB -178 / DET +150
Game Time
6:40 PM ET

The early window brings Detroit to Tampa Bay, where the Rays are installed as solid minus-178 home favorites against a Tigers club sitting at plus-150 on the road. Tampa Bay's edge has been its trademark all season: depth, matchup-driven bullpen usage, and the ability to squeeze value out of every roster spot, which is exactly the profile that earns a favorite price even on a quieter Monday.

Detroit's path as a road underdog is to get on the board early, lean on its own bats to set an aggressive tone, and avoid letting the Rays dictate the late-inning matchups where Tampa Bay so often wins close games. The plus-150 price gives the Tigers a clear puncher's-chance return if their offense shows up, but the Rays' structural advantages in run prevention and bullpen management are why the market leans firmly in their direction at home.

Marlins at Nationals

6:45 PM ET | Nationals Park
Pitching
Alcantara vs Cavalli
Moneyline
WSH -142 / MIA +120
Venue
Nationals Park

The most intriguing arm in the early window is Sandy Alcantara, who takes the ball for Miami in Washington. The former Cy Young winner has averaged around four strikeouts per start across twelve outings, a reminder that his game has always been more about heavy sinkers and ground-ball contact than pure swing-and-miss. When Alcantara is locating his sinker and changeup, he can pitch deep into games and neutralize a lineup, which is the version Miami needs against a Nationals club playing at home.

Cade Cavalli counters for Washington, and the Nationals being installed as minus-142 home favorites despite Alcantara's pedigree says plenty about how the market views Miami's overall roster. The Nationals' route is to be patient against Alcantara's sinker, avoid chasing early, and capitalize once they get into the Marlins' bullpen. Miami's blueprint is the one it always leans on with its ace on the mound: let Alcantara eat innings, keep the ball on the ground, and hope a modest amount of offense is enough behind a quality start.
