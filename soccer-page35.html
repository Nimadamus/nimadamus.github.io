Man City vs Brighton

Wednesday 2:30 PM ET | Etihad Stadium | Peacock
Man City: -225 | Draw: +380 | Brighton: +550 | O/U: 3.5

Manchester City's pursuit of another Premier League title continues at the Etihad. Pep Guardiola's machine has been relentless as always, and their home form is essentially unbeatable. Erling Haaland continues to score at a historic rate, and the supporting cast knows exactly how to get him the ball.

Brighton have been the Premier League's darlings - playing attractive football and developing talent at an impressive rate. But there's a difference between being plucky and actually beating City at the Etihad. The Seagulls will have the ball at times but struggle to create clear chances against City's high press.

The -225 moneyline is steep, but this is City at home. The over at 3.5 is the play - City will score multiple goals, and Brighton's attacking style means they won't park the bus. Expect a 3-1 or 4-1 City victory.

Fulham vs Chelsea

Wednesday 2:30 PM ET | Craven Cottage | Peacock
Fulham: +280 | Draw: +250 | Chelsea: +100 | O/U: 2.5

The West London derby at Craven Cottage is always spicy. Fulham have been excellent this season - Marco Silva has this team playing above their talent level, and their home form has been particularly impressive. The Cottage atmosphere for a derby is electric.

Chelsea continue to be the Premier League's enigma. Massive spending, questionable returns. On their day, they have the talent to beat anyone, but consistency has been their Achilles heel. The Blues have struggled in tight atmospheres against organized opponents.

This screams draw. Chelsea +100 is tempting given their talent, but Fulham at home in a derby are dangerous. The under at 2.5 is the safer play - both teams will be cagey in a match where neither wants to lose.

Bournemouth vs Tottenham

Wednesday 2:30 PM ET | Vitality Stadium | Peacock
Bournemouth: +320 | Draw: +280 | Tottenham: +085 | O/U: 3.0

Bournemouth continue to punch above their weight under Andoni Iraola. The Cherries play fearless football at the Vitality, pressing high and taking the game to anyone. Their home form has been impressive against sides expected to roll over them.

Tottenham are... well, they're Spurs. Talented but frustrating, capable of brilliance and collapse in the same 90 minutes. Ange Postecoglou's attacking style is entertaining but leaves them vulnerable on the counter. This is exactly the type of match where Spurs find a way to disappoint.

Bournemouth +320 has value for a dart. The Cherries will make this uncomfortable, and Spurs' defensive issues are well-documented. The over at 3.0 is the safer play - both teams will attack and leave space.

Crystal Palace vs Aston Villa

Wednesday 2:30 PM ET | Selhurst Park | Peacock
Palace: +240 | Draw: +240 | Villa: +115 | O/U: 2.5

Selhurst Park under the lights is one of the Premier League's great atmospheres. Crystal Palace have the fans, the energy, and enough quality to cause problems for anyone at home. Oliver Glasner has them organized and dangerous on the counter.

Aston Villa continue their push for Champions League qualification. Unai Emery's side are tactically excellent and can adapt to any opponent. Their away form has been solid, and they have the quality to control matches in difficult environments.

Villa +115 is fair value. They're the better team and should handle the Selhurst atmosphere. The under at 2.5 is appealing - Emery will control tempo, and Palace will be compact. Expect a tight 1-0 or 2-1 affair.

Everton vs Wolves

Wednesday 2:30 PM ET | Goodison Park | Peacock
Everton: +140 | Draw: +230 | Wolves: +200 | O/U: 2.0

Goodison Park's final season continues with Everton fighting for survival. The old ground is hostile and emotional, and the Toffees have found some form at home. Their defensive organization under Sean Dyche has improved, even if the attacking play leaves something to be desired.

Wolves are also in a relegation battle. Gary O'Neil's side have talent but lack the consistency to pull away from danger. Their away form has been concerning, and they struggle to impose themselves in hostile environments.

The under at 2.0 is the play. Two teams fighting relegation will be desperate not to lose. Expect a cagey, low-scoring affair that could easily end 0-0 or 1-0. Neither side has the confidence to open up.

Brentford vs Sunderland

Wednesday 2:30 PM ET | Gtech Community Stadium | Peacock
Brentford: -180 | Draw: +340 | Sunderland: +450 | O/U: 2.5

Brentford continue to defy expectations. Thomas Frank has built something special in West London - the Bees play attacking football and have the quality to compete with anyone. Ivan Toney's departure was supposed to hurt them, but they've adapted impressively.

Sunderland's return to the top flight has been about survival. The Black Cats have the atmosphere and the passion, but the jump in quality is significant. Their away form has been what you'd expect from a newly promoted side.

Brentford -180 is fair. They're clearly the better side and should handle this at home. The over at 2.5 is the secondary play - the Bees attack relentlessly and will create enough chances to score multiple goals.

Burnley vs Man United

Wednesday 3:15 PM ET | Turf Moor | USA Network
Burnley: +360 | Draw: +290 | Man United: -110 | O/U: 2.5

Turf Moor is one of the Premier League's most hostile environments, and Burnley will make this uncomfortable. The Clarets have been scrappy and organized, making life difficult for bigger clubs. Their physicality could disrupt United's rhythm.

Manchester United continue their search for consistency. The talent is there, but the execution varies wildly from week to week. They've struggled in atmospheres like Turf Moor before, where the crowd becomes a factor and the pitch feels smaller.

United -110 should be the call, but this has upset potential written all over it. Burnley +360 is a live underdog bet. The under at 2.5 is safer - Burnley will make this ugly, and United often struggle to break down compact defenses.

Newcastle vs Leeds

Wednesday 3:15 PM ET | St. James' Park | Peacock
Newcastle: -165 | Draw: +320 | Leeds: +400 | O/U: 2.5

St. James' Park is absolutely bouncing right now. Newcastle's project is in full swing, and the Toon Army expects results. Eddie Howe has them playing excellent football, and their home form has been formidable. Alexander Isak leads the line brilliantly.

Leeds are back in the top flight and adjusting to the increased quality. Daniel Farke's side are organized and will compete, but the jump from the Championship is significant. Their away record at elite venues has been poor.

Newcastle -165 is the play. The Magpies at home against a newly promoted side should cruise. The over at 2.5 has value - Newcastle will attack relentlessly, and Leeds' defensive organization can only hold for so long.
