Celtics @ Pacers

Saturday, 7:00 PM ET | Gainbridge Fieldhouse | NBCSB
Line: BOS -8.5 | O/U: 225.0 | BOS: 18-11 | IND: 6-24

This isn't even a fair fight. The Pacers are running on fumes without Tyrese Haliburton, who's done for the season with that Achilles injury he suffered during the Finals. Add in Aaron Nesmith (knee), Obi Toppin (foot), Isaiah Jackson (concussion), and Ben Sheppard (calf) all out, and you're looking at an Indiana roster that belongs in the G-League.

Jaylen Brown is absolutely cooking in December - 32.4 points per game on 52% shooting and 41.7% from deep. The Celtics dominated the first meeting 103-95, and that was before the Pacers lost half their rotation. Boston's won the first quarter in 6 of their last 7 road games, and they're 8-2 in their last 10 overall.

Indiana ranks dead last in the league - 30th in effective field goal percentage, 30th at the rim, 30th from three. They're literally the worst offensive team in basketball right now. The Celtics should cruise by double digits, and 8.5 feels like a gift given how broken this Pacers team is. Take Boston and don't think twice.

Lean: Celtics -8.5

Suns @ Pelicans

Saturday, 7:00 PM ET | Smoothie King Center | NBATV
Line: PHX -5.5 | O/U: 238.5 | PHX: 17-13 | NO: 8-24

Here's something the betting public needs to understand: Devin Booker absolutely owns the Smoothie King Center. His last three games there? 44, 52, and 52 points. That's not a typo. The man averages 44+ points per game in New Orleans over his recent visits, and this is a back-to-back after the Suns already beat the Pelicans 115-108 on Friday.

The Pelicans are reeling - they've lost three straight after a five-game win streak, and their defense is giving up 120+ points per game. Dejounte Murray and Herbert Jones are still out, leaving Zion Williamson to carry an undermanned squad. Derik Queen struggled against the Suns on Friday after a hot month, and there's no reason to expect a different result tonight.

Phoenix is 3-1 in their last four and 2-0 against New Orleans this season with a 2-0 ATS edge. The Pelicans rank 4th in paint scoring, but they can't stop anybody on the other end. Booker's revenge tour continues at a venue where he's been historically unstoppable. Lay the points.

Lean: Suns -5.5

Hornets @ Magic

Saturday, 7:00 PM ET | Kia Center | Bally Sports
Line: ORL -6.5 | O/U: 230.5 | ORL: 17-14 | CHA: 10-20

The Magic have absolutely dominated this series - they've won 10 of the last 11 meetings against Charlotte, and the Hornets are 0-4 in their last four trips to the Kia Center. That's a trend you don't ignore, especially when Orlando's at home where they've been solid despite some recent inconsistency.

Charlotte has some weapons - LaMelo Ball is averaging 23 points lately, Brandon Miller's contributing 20, and rookie Kon Knueppel is shooting a blistering 41.9% from three on 8.7 attempts per game. But the Magic rank 27th in three-point defense, so that shooting might keep this closer than the line suggests.

Franz Wagner is questionable with an ankle issue, and Jalen Suggs is dealing with a hip problem. If either sits, this becomes a much more interesting spot for Charlotte. The Hornets are 3-2 in their last five, showing some life. This is a lean toward Orlando, but 6.5 is a lot to lay against a team that can shoot the lights out.

Lean: Magic -6.5 (with hesitation)

Bucks @ Grizzlies

Saturday, 7:00 PM ET | FedExForum | Bally Sports
Line: MEM -5.5 | O/U: 225.0 | MIL: 12-18 | MEM: 14-16

Milwaukee without Giannis is like ordering a cheeseburger without the meat. The Bucks are 0-8 in their last 9 road games, and they're walking into FedExForum against a team that's beaten them seven straight times. Memphis hasn't lost to Milwaukee since January 2022. Let that sink in.

The Grizzlies are banged up too - Brandon Clarke, Zach Edey, Ty Jerome, Scotty Pippen Jr., and Vince Williams Jr. are all out, and Ja Morant is questionable. But Santi Aldama has been a Bucks killer, shooting 55.1% against them in his career. He just dropped 37-7-5 against Utah. Bobby Portis and Ryan Rollins can only do so much to replace what Giannis brings.

This is two injury-ravaged teams, but Memphis is at home where they've been much better, and Milwaukee simply cannot win on the road this season. The Bucks' dysfunction is well-documented, and the Grizzlies have owned this matchup for years. Memphis should cover, but the injuries make the under interesting too.

Lean: Grizzlies -5.5

Mavericks @ Kings

Saturday, 5:00 PM ET | Golden 1 Center | NBATV
Line: DAL -3.0 | O/U: 229.5 | DAL: 12-20 | SAC: 7-23

This is a hospital game. Dallas is missing Kyrie Irving (knee), Dereck Lively II (foot), Dante Exum (knee), and Anthony Davis is questionable with a groin issue. Sacramento's without Zach LaVine (ankle), Domantas Sabonis (MCL), Keegan Murray (calf), and Drew Eubanks (thumb). These aren't basketball teams anymore - they're skeleton crews.

Cooper Flagg has been Dallas's revelation, averaging 19.4 points per game and exploding for 30.0 in his last two outings. P.J. Washington has stepped up too with 15.6 points and 8.0 rebounds. For Sacramento, DeMar DeRozan is shouldering the load with 19.0 per game, and Russell Westbrook is averaging 14.4 points with 7.2 assists.

Dallas is on a five-game road losing streak, but Sacramento's been allowing 122+ points while only scoring 112. The Kings are 3-1 in the last four head-to-head meetings, but both teams are in such disarray that this feels like a coin flip. The under might be the play here - neither team has the personnel to put up points consistently.

Lean: Under 229.5

Cavaliers @ Rockets

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center | SCHN
Line: HOU -5.5 | O/U: 232.0 | HOU: 18-10 | CLE: 17-15

Houston's been a monster at home - they're 8-2 at Toyota Center, and Kevin Durant has found his groove with the Rockets, averaging 25.2 points per game. Alperen Sengun is questionable with a calf issue, but when healthy he's been dominant (22.7 PPG, 9.4 RPG, 6.8 APG). Amen Thompson has been the surprise, leading scorers with 26 points against the Lakers.

Cleveland's dealing with injuries of their own. Max Strus and Larry Nance Jr. are out, and Evan Mobley just returned from missing six games. He might not be 100%, which is a problem against Houston's frontcourt. Donovan Mitchell has been carrying the load (30.7 PPG), and Darius Garland is averaging 20.0 over the last ten, but the Cavaliers are just 4-6 in that stretch.

The Rockets won the first meeting 114-104 on November 20th behind Sengun's 28-point performance. Fred VanVleet is out for the season with an ACL tear, but Houston has adapted well. Cleveland is 6-7 on the road, and they're walking into a hostile Toyota Center environment. Houston's trying to extend a three-game home win streak.

Lean: Rockets -5.5
