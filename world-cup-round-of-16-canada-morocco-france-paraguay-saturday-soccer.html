World Cup Round of 16: Co-Hosts Canada Host Morocco And France Meet Paraguay

The last 16 begins on the Fourth of July with two very different knockout ties. Co-hosts Canada face a Morocco side that has not lost in ages at NRG Stadium in Houston, and tournament favorites France take on a resilient Paraguay in Philadelphia. Team form, paths to this stage, venues and market context for each below. Analysis only, no picks.

The Round of 16 opens with a genuine story game. Canada, one of the three co-hosts of this World Cup, kept their home tournament alive with a nervy 1-0 win over South Africa in the Round of 32, a stoppage-time goal from Stephen Eustaquio delivering the country's first-ever knockout victory at a World Cup. That result matters beyond the scoreline. A young Canadian side that has spent years building toward this moment now gets to play a last-16 tie on home soil in Houston, and the emotional charge of a co-host chasing history is exactly the kind of energy that can lift a team past a more experienced opponent.

Morocco arrive as the tougher out on paper. The 2022 semifinalists carry the pedigree of a nation that has become a fixture in the latter stages of major tournaments, and they punched their ticket to the last 16 by outlasting the Netherlands on penalties after 120 grinding minutes. Morocco's identity is built on defensive organization, discipline out of possession and the ability to strangle the life out of open games, which is why the market frames them as modest favorites at around -130 while pricing Canada as a live home underdog near +380 and the draw around +245. The total sits at 2.5 goals, and Morocco's recent knockout profile has leaned low-scoring.

The tactical question is straightforward. Canada will lean on the pace and directness of Alphonso Davies down the flank and the finishing of Jonathan David, the country's all-time leading scorer who has already left his mark on this tournament, and they will want a loud NRG Stadium crowd to turn the tie into an emotional, transition-heavy contest. Morocco will try to do the opposite: slow the tempo, compress the space between the lines, and make Canada break down a compact block over ninety-plus minutes. Whichever side imposes its preferred rhythm is likely to advance, and with a co-host's dream on the line, the atmosphere in Houston should be the loudest of the day.

The evening tie in Philadelphia carries a very different shape. France have been the most fearsome attacking team of the tournament so far, rolling through their opening matches with a goal difference that reads like a mismatch, roughly 13 goals scored against just 2 conceded across four games that included wins over Sweden, Norway, Iraq and Senegal. Kylian Mbappe has been the headline act with a tournament-leading haul, Ousmane Dembele has chipped in from the other flank, and Michael Olise has been creating from midfield, all in front of a back line marshaled by Mike Maignan, Dayot Upamecano and William Saliba. It is a squad with few obvious weaknesses, which is why the market treats them as overwhelming favorites at around -550, with Paraguay a long shot near +1500 and the draw around +600.

Paraguay's route here has been the polar opposite of France's, and that contrast is the entire intrigue. La Albirroja have scored only twice across four matches and advanced not on flair but on grit, upsetting Germany on penalties in the Round of 32 with goalkeeper Orlando Gill emerging as the penalty-shootout hero. This is a team that has embraced a defend-first identity, absorbing pressure, staying compact and trusting that a low-event match and a moment on a set piece or a shootout can carry them further than their attacking numbers suggest they belong.

The blueprint for an upset is obvious but extremely difficult to execute against this French side. Paraguay must keep the game at zero for as long as possible, deny Mbappe and Dembele room to run in behind, and try to drag the tie toward the kind of penalty lottery that already claimed Germany. France's counter is simply their quality and depth: score first, force Paraguay to chase, and let the tournament's most dangerous forward line pull a stubborn block apart. With every France match so far sailing over the 2.5-goal line, the pregame market leans toward the favorites finding the net, but knockout football has a way of tightening even the most lopsided ties. Kickoff is 5:00 PM ET at Lincoln Financial Field.
