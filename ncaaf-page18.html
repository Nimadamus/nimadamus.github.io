Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

Look, I love a good bowl game between two evenly matched teams, and this Gasparilla Bowl has all the makings of a fun one. Memphis comes in at 8-5 with one of the more underrated offenses in the Group of Five. The Tigers averaged over 31 points per game this season and quarterback Seth Henigan has been slinging it all year with over 3,400 passing yards. The kid can play, and he's got weapons to work with.

NC State enters with the same 8-5 record but they've been more of a grind-it-out team. The Wolfpack's defense has been solid, allowing under 22 points per game, but they're going to have their hands full with Memphis's tempo. Here's the thing though - NC State's run defense has been suspect at times, and if Memphis can establish the ground game early, it opens up everything for Henigan downfield.

The key matchup here is Memphis's offensive line against NC State's front seven. If the Tigers can protect Henigan and give him time, they're going to move the ball. But if NC State can generate pressure and force quick throws, this becomes a different game entirely. I'm expecting a shootout in Tampa - both teams have too much offensive firepower to keep this low-scoring.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

The Celebration Bowl is always one of my favorite games of bowl season. HBCU football at its finest, and this year we've got two powerhouse programs squaring off. South Carolina State comes in at 10-3 as MEAC champions, while Prairie View A&M sits at 10-4 representing the SWAC. This is going to be physical, passionate, and incredibly entertaining.

SC State's defense has been their calling card all season. They've been suffocating opponents, and their ability to generate turnovers has been a huge factor in their success. On offense, they've got a balanced attack that doesn't make many mistakes - they protect the football and move the chains efficiently. It's not flashy, but it wins games.

Prairie View brings a different style. They're more explosive offensively and they're not afraid to take shots downfield. The Panthers have playmakers at the skill positions who can break games open with one play. The atmosphere at Mercedes-Benz Stadium is going to be electric, and whoever can handle the big stage better is going to come out on top. This one's going to be closer than people think.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

This is a fun bowl matchup at SoFi Stadium between two programs with rich histories. Boise State at 9-5 isn't having their best season by Bronco standards, but they're still a dangerous team. The blue turf magic might be at home, but this program always shows up for bowl games. Washington at 9-4 is looking to build momentum heading into next season after a solid campaign.

The Broncos live and die by their running game, and Ashton Jeanty has been an absolute monster this season. The Heisman finalist has rushed for over 2,400 yards and is a nightmare to tackle in the open field. If Washington can't find a way to slow him down, they're in serious trouble. But here's the thing - the Huskies have faced good rushing attacks all year in the Big Ten and they've held their own.

Washington's balanced offense is going to test Boise State's secondary. The Huskies can spread you out and attack in multiple ways, and their quarterback has been making good decisions all season. The key for Washington is staying patient and not forcing things early. If they can establish the run and keep Jeanty off the field, they control the tempo. This one could go either way, but I'm expecting a hard-fought game that comes down to the fourth quarter.
