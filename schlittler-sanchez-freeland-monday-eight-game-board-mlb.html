MLB Slate - Monday July 6

MLB Preview: Schlittler, Sanchez and Freeland Anchor A Full 8-Game Monday Board

Eight games open the week, from a 2:10 PM ET matinee in Kansas City to a 10:10 PM ET nightcap at Dodger Stadium. Cam Schlittler brings the AL's best ERA to Tropicana Field, Cristopher Sanchez carries a 2.00 ERA into Kauffman Stadium, and baseball's best and worst records collide in Los Angeles. Verified starters, records and matchup context below. Analysis only, no picks.

Afternoon Opener
MLB

Phillies at Royals

Monday, 2:10 PM ET | Kauffman Stadium, Kansas City, MO
Phillies 50-39 at Royals 35-54 | Cristopher Sanchez (10-3, 2.00 ERA) vs Noah Cameron (4-6, 4.95 ERA)

Philadelphia opens the week with its best arm on the mound. Cristopher Sanchez has been the National League's most dominant starter this season at 10-3 with a 2.00 ERA, and he draws a Kansas City club that has dropped four of its last five and sits toward the bottom of the American League Central at 35-54. Noah Cameron counters for the Royals at 4-6 with a 4.95 ERA, a gap of nearly three earned runs a game between the starters. Kauffman Stadium's spacious outfield has bailed out shaky Royals pitching before, but a lineup that has scuffled all series long faces one of the toughest assignments on the entire Monday board.

Evening Marquee
MLB

Yankees at Rays

Monday, 6:40 PM ET | Tropicana Field, St. Petersburg, FL
Rays lead the AL East | Cam Schlittler (8-5, 2.08 ERA, 123 K) vs Griffin Jax (4-5, 3.45 ERA)

Cam Schlittler has been the best pitcher in the American League this season, sitting at 8-5 with a 2.08 ERA and 123 strikeouts across 104.2 innings, numbers that lead AL starters in ERA and ERA-plus. New York has scuffled as a team lately, but Schlittler has been dominant every time out regardless of the offense or bullpen around him. Tampa Bay, which has held the division lead for weeks, counters with Griffin Jax at 4-5 with a 3.45 ERA and nearly a strikeout an inning of his own, giving Monday's evening window two legitimately above-average arms in a dome that has played as one of the sport's more pitcher-friendly environments for a decade.

Evening Window
MLB

Astros at Nationals

Monday, 6:45 PM ET | Nationals Park, Washington, DC
Astros 44-47 at Nationals 46-44 | Mike Burrows vs Miles Mikolas

Washington hosts Houston in a matchup of two clubs hovering right around .500, the Nationals a game over at 46-44 and the Astros a game under at 44-47. Mike Burrows takes the ball for Houston against Miles Mikolas, with the Nationals holding a modest edge as home favorites in a game that projects close on paper.

Evening Window
MLB

Mets at Braves

Monday, 7:15 PM ET | Truist Park, Atlanta, GA
Mets 36-53 at Braves 52-35 | Reynaldo Lopez vs Freddy Peralta

In Atlanta, the Braves at 52-35 look to keep pace in a stacked National League by beating a Mets club that has completely lost its footing at 36-53. Freddy Peralta anchors the Atlanta staff against New York's Reynaldo Lopez in a game where the standings gap is as wide as almost anything else on the board.

Evening Window
MLB

Brewers at Cardinals

Monday, 7:45 PM ET | Busch Stadium, St. Louis, MO
Brewers 55-33 (NL Central lead) | Shane Drohan (3-2, 3.12 ERA) vs Dustin May (5-6, 4.80 ERA)

Milwaukee, owners of the best record in the National League at 55-33, open a series at St. Louis behind Shane Drohan, who has been sharp in a smaller sample at 3-2 with a 3.12 ERA. The Cardinals counter with Dustin May at 5-6 with a 4.80 ERA, a pitcher who has allowed more traffic than damage this year. Milwaukee's lineup is dangerous enough on its own to make this one of the more competitive games on the board regardless of the pitching matchup, and St. Louis will need a strong start from May to keep pace with the league's most complete team.

Late Window
MLB

Diamondbacks at Padres

Monday, 9:40 PM ET | Petco Park, San Diego, CA
Both clubs 44-45 | Brandon Pfaadt (1-1, 5.40 ERA) vs Walker Buehler (5-4, 4.61 ERA)

Two clubs deadlocked at 44-45 close out the night at one of the most pitcher-friendly parks in baseball. Arizona's Brandon Pfaadt, just 1-1 with a 5.40 ERA in a small sample, faces San Diego's Walker Buehler at 5-4 with a 4.61 ERA. Neither offense has hit much this season, and Petco's marine layer tends to keep even mediocre pitching lines respectable.

Late Window
MLB

Blue Jays at Giants

Monday, 9:45 PM ET | Oracle Park, San Francisco, CA
Giants 37-52 | Kevin Gausman (4-7, 4.19 ERA) vs Landen Roupp (5-8, 4.55 ERA)

Toronto's veteran right-hander Kevin Gausman, 4-7 with a 4.19 ERA that undersells how well he has generally pitched, faces a Giants club at 37-52 that has struggled to score all year behind Landen Roupp, who sits at 5-8 with a 4.55 ERA. The cold night air off the Bay has suppressed offense at Oracle for a generation, and neither lineup Monday night figures to change that trend.

Nightcap
MLB

Rockies at Dodgers

Monday, 10:10 PM ET | Dodger Stadium, Los Angeles, CA
Rockies 36-54 (majors-worst) at Dodgers 59-31 (majors-best) | Kyle Freeland (2-7, 7.25 ERA) vs Eric Lauer (4-5, 4.84 ERA)

The night closes with the widest gap in the standings on the entire board. Colorado carries the worst record in the major leagues at 36-54 into a series opener against the 59-31 Dodgers, the best team in baseball, a 23-game difference. Kyle Freeland takes the ball for the Rockies at 2-7 with a 7.25 ERA, and Los Angeles counters with Eric Lauer, who has settled in at 4-5 with a 4.84 ERA since joining the rotation. The full breakdown, including verified betting lines and keys to victory for both sides, lives on the Featured Game page.
