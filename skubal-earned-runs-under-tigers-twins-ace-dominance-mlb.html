Tarik Skubal Under 1.5 Earned Runs (-160) | Tigers at Twins | Monday April 7 | 7:40 PM ET

Posted: 6:00 PM ET, April 7, 2026 | MLB Regular Season | Player Prop

There are certain spots in baseball where the prop market hands you a gift, and tonight's Tarik Skubal earned runs line is one of them. The back-to-back AL Cy Young winner takes the mound for Detroit in Minnesota with his ER set at 1.5, and the under is sitting at -160. That juice tells you the books know what's coming, but I still think there's value here because everything about this matchup screams shutdown performance. Skubal has been historically dominant to open 2026, the Twins are one of the worst offenses in baseball against left-handed pitching, and the data from our edge scanner confirms that the under trends in this game are overwhelming. Let me walk you through all of it.

Skubal's 2026 Start Is Historically Elite

Let's just lay out the numbers because they speak for themselves. Through his first three starts of the 2026 season, Tarik Skubal has pitched 13 innings and allowed exactly one earned run. That's a 0.69 ERA. One. Earned. Run. In thirteen innings of work. He opened the year by shutting down San Diego, then followed that up by carving through Arizona, and the pattern is clear: this is not a pitcher who is finding his groove. He arrived in midseason form on Opening Day and hasn't looked back.

Now, I'll be the first to acknowledge that the xERA sits at 4.06, which suggests some regression is lurking. And that's fair. Skubal has gotten some bounces, and nobody maintains a 0.69 ERA over a full season. But here's the thing: regression doesn't happen on your schedule. It doesn't happen because you read about it on a stat sheet. It happens gradually, over weeks and months, and tonight Skubal is still riding the wave of a pitcher who feels invincible on the mound. When you've given up one earned run in 13 innings, you're locked in. Your mechanics are synced, your confidence is sky high, and hitters are facing a guy who genuinely believes he can't be beaten. That mental edge matters enormously in a sport built on small margins.

Minnesota Can't Hit Left-Handed Pitching, Period

Here's where this play goes from good to great. The Minnesota Twins are hitting .169 against left-handed pitching this season. That ranks 28th in Major League Baseball. Let that sink in. Twenty-eighth out of thirty teams against lefties, and they're about to face the best lefty in the American League. This isn't a matchup where you look at the numbers and think, "Well, maybe they'll figure it out tonight." This is a lineup that has been structurally incapable of producing offense against southpaws, and the pitcher they're facing tonight has a 0.69 ERA and a Cy Young trophy in each hand.

The Twins' struggles against left-handed pitching aren't a fluke or a small sample quirk. Their lineup is built with right-handed hitters who have pronounced platoon splits, and when a lefty with Skubal's velocity and slider comes in throwing 96-97 mph with late break to both sides of the plate, those split gaps get exploited ruthlessly. Minnesota doesn't have the kind of lefty-mashing bat in the middle of the lineup that can anchor an approach against an elite southpaw. They're going to swing at sliders off the plate, chase elevated fastballs, and generally look like they're hacking at ghosts for five or six innings.

The First Five Innings Profile Is Suffocating

One of the most interesting data points for this game is Minnesota's first-five-innings scoring. The Twins are averaging just 1.67 runs through the first five innings of their games this season, which ranks as the 6th lowest mark in all of baseball. That number tells you two things. First, Minnesota's lineup is a slow starter that doesn't typically jump on pitchers early. Second, opposing starting pitchers are getting through five innings against this team with minimal damage on a regular basis.

For a prop like earned runs under 1.5, the first-five profile is everything. Skubal is going to pitch at least five innings tonight barring something unusual, and most of the earned run damage that would kill this prop happens during a starter's time on the mound. If the Twins can barely score 1.67 runs in the first five against the average pitcher, what are they going to do against a guy with a 0.69 ERA and back-to-back Cy Youngs? The answer, in all likelihood, is next to nothing. Maybe a solo shot off a mistake pitch, maybe a bloop and a seeing-eye single that manufactures something. But the ceiling for Minnesota's offense in this matchup is extremely low, and we only need Skubal to hold them to one earned run or fewer to cash this ticket.

All of those factors stack in Skubal's favor tonight.

The Cy Young Pedigree Matters in Spots Like This

Look, there are 15 or 20 quality starting pitchers in the American League on any given night. But there's exactly one guy who has won back-to-back Cy Young awards and is pitching with a 0.69 ERA. Skubal isn't just good. He's operating on a different level than every other pitcher in this league right now. That pedigree matters in prop markets because it represents a floor. Even on a night where Skubal doesn't have his best stuff, his "bad" start is still going to look like most pitchers' average start. He has the command, the secondary stuff, and the composure to limit damage even when he's not dealing at his peak.

Against a Minnesota lineup that can barely touch lefties, Skubal's floor tonight is probably something like six innings, two or three hits, and zero to one earned runs. His ceiling is a complete game shutout. Anywhere in that range cashes the under 1.5 ER prop, and the probability of him landing in that window is much higher than the -160 price implies. I'm comfortable laying the juice here because the matchup, the data, and the pitcher's form all point in the same direction.

The Bottom Line

This is as clean a pitcher prop as you'll find all week. Tarik Skubal, the reigning back-to-back AL Cy Young winner, has a 0.69 ERA with just one earned run in 13 innings this season. He faces a Minnesota lineup hitting .169 against left-handed pitching, ranking 28th in baseball. The Twins average just 1.67 runs in the first five innings, the 6th lowest in MLB. The BetLegend edge scanner shows overwhelming under signals in this matchup with only a 9.1% over rate in comparable scenarios. Everything converges on one outcome: Skubal dominates, Minnesota barely scratches, and the earned runs stay well under the number.
