Rams @ Seahawks

Thursday, 8:15 PM ET | Lumen Field | Prime Video
Line: SEA -1.5 | O/U: 42.5 | LAR: 11-3 (10-4 ATS) | SEA: 11-3 (10-4 ATS)

Historic Matchup

This is not hyperbole: Thursday night's game is arguably the biggest regular season game of the NFL season. The Los Angeles Rams (11-3) travel to Seattle to face the Seahawks (11-3) in the first-ever Thursday Night Football game featuring two teams with 11 or more wins. The winner takes control of the NFC West with two games remaining and positions themselves for the coveted first-round bye. The Rams currently hold the No. 1 seed in the NFC, but a loss would hand that advantage to Seattle.

These two teams met in Week 11, with the Rams winning 21-19 at home. That game was defined by turnovers: Los Angeles intercepted Sam Darnold four times, turning a close game into a comfortable victory. Matthew Stafford threw for 130 yards and two touchdowns, while Kyren Williams rushed for 91 yards on 12 carries. Seattle will be looking for revenge in front of their home crowd at Lumen Field.

Quarterback Battle: Stafford vs. Darnold

Matthew Stafford is having one of the best seasons of his career. He leads the NFL with 37 passing touchdowns and ranks second with 3,722 passing yards. His 7.9 yards per attempt is seventh in the league, and he's thrown multiple touchdown passes in a league-high 12 games this season. At 36 years old, Stafford is playing like an MVP candidate and has the Rams positioned as legitimate Super Bowl contenders.

Sam Darnold has been solid for Seattle with 3,433 passing yards, 22 touchdowns, and 11 interceptions. But here's the concern: Darnold is 1-4 in his career against the Rams, losing his last four outings against them. The four interceptions in Week 11 weren't flukes. Los Angeles seems to have his number, and he'll need to break that trend on Thursday to keep Seattle's NFC West hopes alive.

Elite Receivers Headline the Matchup

This game features the two leading receivers in the NFL going head-to-head. Jaxon Smith-Njigba leads the league with 1,541 receiving yards for Seattle, establishing himself as one of the game's elite young talents. Right behind him is Puka Nacua with 1,367 yards for the Rams. Both receivers have been the primary weapons for their respective offenses, and the secondary that can contain its opponent's star will have a significant advantage.

However, the Rams may be without Davante Adams, who is doubtful with a hamstring injury after missing practice all week. Adams leads the NFL in receiving touchdowns, and his absence would be a significant blow to Los Angeles's red zone attack. Nacua would need to shoulder an even heavier load if Adams can't go.

ATS Trends Favor the Road Team

The betting trends heavily favor the Rams in this matchup. Los Angeles is 10-4 against the spread this season and has covered in 11 of the last 14 meetings against Seattle, including five straight games at Lumen Field. Even more impressive: the Rams are 17-3 ATS in their last 20 December games. They simply don't lose to the number in late-season matchups.

Turnover differential tells another story that favors LA. The Rams have a +9 turnover margin that ranks in the top five of the NFL, while Seattle sits at -1 with the second-most turnovers (23) in the league. If Darnold continues his turnover-prone ways against the Rams, Los Angeles will capitalize like they did in Week 11.

What To Watch

Seattle's defensive front will need to pressure Stafford without giving up big plays. Ernest Jones leads the Seahawks with 104 tackles and five interceptions, while Leonard Williams has 7.0 sacks. They'll need to get home early and often to disrupt the Rams' timing. For Los Angeles, containing Smith-Njigba will be priority number one. The young receiver has been unstoppable, and the Rams secondary will be tested throughout the night.

Weather could also be a factor in Seattle. December games at Lumen Field can get dicey, and both teams will need to adjust their game plans accordingly. The crowd noise advantage for Seattle is real, but the Rams have proven they can win in hostile environments all season. This is appointment television and could determine the trajectory of the NFC playoff picture.
