England vs Costa Rica

Wednesday, 4:00 PM ET | Inter&Co Stadium, Orlando

This is the headline friendly of the date, and it is the last test on American soil before England open their World Cup. Thomas Tuchel's side arrive as one of the favorites to lift the trophy, and the books treat this tuneup accordingly: England sit around 1-7 to win with the draw out near 7-1 and Costa Rica a distant 10-1, which is about as lopsided as a senior international fixture gets. The match is staged at the Inter&Co Stadium in Orlando, a deliberate choice to acclimate the squad to the heat and the surfaces they will live with deep into the summer. For Tuchel, the priority is not the scoreline but the shape: this is the final chance to settle on a first-choice eleven before the group stage begins.

The expected England side reads like a tournament team that knows its spine. Jordan Pickford is in goal behind a back line of O'Reilly, Ezri Konsa, Marc Guehi, and Reece James, with Declan Rice and a midfield partner screening in front of them. The attacking quartet is where the intrigue sits: Marcus Rashford, Jude Bellingham, and Bukayo Saka feeding Harry Kane at the point, the kind of front four that should overwhelm a side ranked far below them. Tuchel has spent this camp auditioning combinations, and a clean, controlled performance here lets him lock in the partnerships rather than keep guessing into a competitive opener.

Costa Rica come in as the clear underdog and will treat the occasion as a measuring stick. Their predicted lineup leans on Manfred Ugalde up top with the experienced spine around him, and the game plan almost certainly involves sitting deep, staying compact, and trying to frustrate an England side that can grow impatient if the early chances do not fall. Los Ticos are not in England's World Cup group, so there is no tactical secrecy at stake, just a chance to test themselves against elite movement and pressing. The value of the night for Tuchel is reps and rhythm, getting Kane and Bellingham on the same wavelength and confirming that his strongest attacking unit is humming before the tournament that matters. Kickoff is 4:00 PM ET in Orlando.

What sharpens the stakes is the calendar. England are drawn into a group alongside Croatia, Ghana, and Panama, with the opener against Croatia looming as the most demanding fixture of the three. Croatia's midfield experience makes them a stern first opponent, which means England cannot afford to walk into the tournament still searching for their best eleven. A decisive, organized display against a disciplined Costa Rica side would let Tuchel enter the group stage with his front four settled and his back line drilled, exactly the clarity a manager wants eight days out. The goal in Orlando is simple: look like the favorite England are supposed to be, and carry that conviction into a group that offers no easy nights.

Portugal vs Nigeria

Wednesday, 3:45 PM ET | Estadio Dr. Magalhaes Pessoa, Leiria

The second tuneup carries its own weight because of who is on the field. Portugal host Nigeria in Leiria for what is shaping up as a final pre-tournament showcase for Cristiano Ronaldo, and the emotional undertone is hard to ignore given where he is in his career. Roberto Martinez's side are heavy favorites here, priced around 1-4 to win with Nigeria out near 17-2 and the draw at 9-2, a reflection of both Portugal's depth and the home setting. For Martinez, this is the last chance to fine-tune a lineup and confirm that his best players are peaking at the right moment rather than still building toward form.

Portugal's appeal as such strong favorites is the talent that surrounds Ronaldo. Martinez can call on a generation of elite attacking and midfield options, and the question in Leiria is less about whether Portugal win and more about how cleanly the pieces fit together. Ronaldo himself remains the focal point and the penalty taker, and bookmakers have him as a live option to open the scoring precisely because Portugal expect to dominate possession and territory against a Nigeria side that will spend long stretches defending. A composed, efficient performance lets Martinez bank confidence and rotate minutes intelligently before the tournament opens.

Nigeria arrive off a busy run of warmup matches and will use this fixture to gauge their own readiness against top-tier opposition. The Super Eagles carry genuine attacking threat and the kind of athleticism that can trouble any defense in transition, so this is not a night Portugal can sleepwalk through even as favorites. For Nigeria, the value is in measuring themselves against a contender on the road and seeing whether their structure holds up under sustained pressure. Expect them to stay organized, look to spring quick counters, and treat the result as secondary to the lessons. Kickoff is 3:45 PM ET in Leiria.

The broader picture is that both of these sides are putting the finishing touches on tournament preparation, and the friendly format means the scoreline matters less than the sharpness. Portugal want Ronaldo and his supporting cast in rhythm and their defensive shape rehearsed; Nigeria want to leave with a clear read on where they stand. With the World Cup opening the very next day, this is the last controlled environment either manager gets before the real thing, and the reps banked in Leiria are the kind that quietly shape how a team starts its tournament.
