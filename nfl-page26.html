Bears @ 49ers

2025-12-29T01:20Z | Levi's Stadium | NBC, Peacock
CHI: 11-4 | SF: 11-4

This is a massive Sunday Night Football showdown between two of the NFC's best. The Bears' turnaround under Ben Johnson has been remarkable - Chicago's 11-4 record is built on an elite offense that's averaging 28.3 points per game. Caleb Williams has looked like a franchise quarterback, and the weapons around him have flourished in Johnson's scheme.

San Francisco is also 11-4 but has faced injury concerns all season. The 49ers' run game remains potent with Christian McCaffrey, and Brock Purdy continues to be one of the most efficient passers in football. At Levi's Stadium, the Niners are 6-2 and this crowd will be rocking for a potential playoff preview.

Both teams are fighting for seeding and potentially a first-round bye. The Bears' pass rush has improved significantly, which could pose problems for Purdy. This should be a tight, high-quality game that comes down to the fourth quarter. Expect points and drama.

Cardinals @ Bengals

2025-12-28T18:00Z | Paycor Stadium | FOX
ARI: 3-13 | CIN: 6-10

Arizona's season has been a complete disaster. The Cardinals are 3-13 and playing out the string, with Kyler Murray's inconsistency continuing to frustrate. The defense has been among the worst in the league, allowing 27.6 points per game. On the road, they're just 1-7 and have shown no fight in recent weeks.

Cincinnati's season hasn't gone as planned either, but at 6-10 they're still competitive. Joe Burrow has been brilliant individually - his arm talent is undeniable - but the pieces around him haven't been enough. Ja'Marr Chase remains one of the best receivers in football, and this home game should give them a chance to close strong.

The Bengals should handle business at Paycor Stadium. Arizona offers little resistance on either side of the ball, and Cincinnati's passing attack should have a field day against this secondary. Look for a comfortable Bengals win.

Steelers @ Browns

2025-12-28T18:00Z | Huntington Bank Field | CBS
PIT: 9-7 | CLE: 4-12

The AFC North rivalry continues with Pittsburgh heading to Cleveland. The Steelers' 9-7 record represents a solid season, with the defense carrying the load as usual. T.J. Watt continues to wreak havoc, and Pittsburgh's ability to control games with their ground attack has made them tough to beat. They're 4-4 on the road but historically play well in these divisional matchups.

Cleveland's 4-12 record tells the story of a team in transition. The quarterback situation has been a mess all season, and the defense that was once the strength of this team has taken a step back. At home, the Browns are just 2-6, and the atmosphere at Huntington Bank Field has been flat with fans losing patience.

This is a classic AFC North grind-it-out affair. Pittsburgh's run game should control the clock against a depleted Browns defense. Cleveland might keep it close for a while, but the Steelers' experience in these divisional games gives them the edge.

Saints @ Titans

2025-12-28T18:00Z | Nissan Stadium | CBS
NO: 6-10 | TEN: 3-13

Two disappointing teams meet in Nashville. New Orleans' 6-10 record represents a step back from expectations, with the offense never finding its rhythm after early-season injuries. Derek Carr has been serviceable but not spectacular, and the running game that used to define this team has sputtered. On the road, they're 2-6.

Tennessee has been one of the worst teams in football at 3-13. The Titans are clearly in tank mode, and it shows on both sides of the ball. Will Levis has shown flashes but remains raw, and the defense has no answer for competent passing attacks. Home field has meant nothing - they're 2-6 at Nissan Stadium.

This is a battle of underachievers, but New Orleans has more talent. The Saints should be able to move the ball against Tennessee's porous defense. Neither team inspires confidence, but the Saints are the better side and should leave Nashville with a win.

Jaguars @ Colts

2025-12-28T18:00Z | Lucas Oil Stadium | FOX
JAX: 12-4 | IND: 8-8

Jacksonville's remarkable 12-4 record has them as one of the top teams in the AFC. Trevor Lawrence has made the leap to elite status, and the weapons around him have elevated the entire offense. The defense has been opportunistic with turnovers, and the Jaguars look like legitimate Super Bowl contenders. They're 6-2 on the road and dangerous everywhere they go.

Indianapolis is fighting for a wild card spot at 8-8 with Philip Rivers' comeback story captivating the league. Rivers has brought leadership and experience to a young roster, and the running game with Jonathan Taylor has been rejuvenated. At home, the Colts are 5-3 and Lucas Oil Stadium will be loud for this AFC South showdown.

This is a huge game for both teams' playoff aspirations. Jacksonville is the better team on paper, but the Colts match up well defensively and Rivers won't be intimidated. Expect a tight, physical game that comes down to late-game execution.

Buccaneers @ Dolphins

2025-12-28T18:00Z | Hard Rock Stadium | FOX
TB: 7-9 | MIA: 7-9

Two Florida teams meeting with identical 7-9 records in a battle for pride. Tampa Bay's season has been up and down, with Baker Mayfield showing his usual inconsistency. The running game has helped stabilize things, but the defense has been too inconsistent to compete with top teams. On the road, the Bucs are 3-5.

Miami's season has been a disappointment given preseason expectations. Tua Tagovailoa has battled injuries again, and when healthy the offense hasn't been explosive enough. Tyreek Hill and Jaylen Waddle are still dangerous, but the lack of a consistent ground game makes them one-dimensional. At home, they're 4-4.

This is essentially a coin flip. Both teams are evenly matched with similar strengths and weaknesses. Miami's home field advantage gives them a slight edge, but don't be surprised if Tampa pulls the upset. The team that wins the turnover battle likely wins the game.

Patriots @ Jets

2025-12-28T18:00Z | MetLife Stadium | FOX
NE: 13-3 | NYJ: 3-13

New England's resurgence has been the story of the AFC East. The Patriots are 13-3 and have completely rebuilt their identity on the fly. The defense is elite once again, and the young quarterback has developed faster than anyone expected. On the road, they're a ridiculous 7-1 and have handled their business in every environment.

The Jets are an absolute disaster at 3-13. The Aaron Rodgers experiment has officially failed, and the franchise is in complete disarray. The defense that was supposed to be their strength has regressed badly, and there's no hope left this season. At MetLife Stadium, they're 1-7 and the boos have been deafening.

This should be a comfortable Patriots win. New England is playing for seeding while the Jets are playing out the string. The Patriots' defense will have a field day against a broken Jets offense. Expect a dominant performance from the visitors.

Seahawks @ Panthers

2025-12-28T18:00Z | Bank of America Stadium | CBS
SEA: 13-3 | CAR: 8-8

Seattle has been one of the best stories in the NFL at 13-3. Geno Smith's career resurrection continues, and the Seahawks' offensive attack has been difficult to stop. The receiving corps led by DK Metcalf and Tyler Lockett remains one of the best in the league. On the road, they're 6-2 and have handled business against quality opponents.

Carolina's 8-8 record represents genuine progress for a franchise that was in the basement last year. Bryce Young has shown improvement in his second season, and the young talent on both sides of the ball is developing. At home, the Panthers are 5-3 and Bank of America Stadium has been energized.

Seattle is clearly the better team, but Carolina has been competitive at home. The Seahawks should control this game with their passing attack, but the Panthers have the young pieces to keep it interesting. Expect Seattle to pull away in the second half for a comfortable win.

Giants @ Raiders

2025-12-28T21:05Z | Allegiant Stadium | CBS
NYG: 3-13 | LV: 2-14

This might be the ugliest game of the week. The Giants are 3-13 and have looked completely lost all season. The quarterback situation has been a revolving door, and the offensive line gives whoever is back there no chance. On the road, they're 1-7 and haven't shown any competitive fire.

Las Vegas is even worse at 2-14, the worst record in football. The Raiders have been atrocious on both sides of the ball, and the franchise is in total rebuild mode. At Allegiant Stadium, they're 1-7 and the building has been empty. There's simply nothing to play for except draft position.

Someone has to win this game, unfortunately. Both teams are terrible, but the Raiders are slightly worse. The Giants should be able to grind out a win with their running game, but don't expect anything pretty. This is a game to avoid entirely.

Eagles @ Bills

2025-12-28T21:25Z | Highmark Stadium | FOX
PHI: 11-5 | BUF: 11-5

This is the game of the week, period. The Eagles and Bills are both 11-5 and represent the best of each conference. Philadelphia's rushing attack led by Saquon Barkley has been devastating, and Jalen Hurts continues to be one of the most dynamic quarterbacks in football. On the road, they're 5-3 and have proven they can win anywhere.

Buffalo's quest for a Super Bowl continues with Josh Allen playing at an MVP level. Allen has been sensational this season, and the Bills' explosive offense is averaging 29.1 points per game. At Highmark Stadium in late December, the cold weather and raucous crowd give them a significant edge. They're 6-2 at home.

This has Super Bowl preview written all over it. Both teams are physical, well-coached, and loaded with playmakers. The Eagles' ground game versus the Bills' aerial attack creates a fascinating contrast. Buffalo has the home field edge, but this should be an instant classic regardless of outcome.
