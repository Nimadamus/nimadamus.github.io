Nebraska Cornhuskers vs Cincinnati Bearcats

6:00 PM ET GEHA Field at Arrowhead Stadium Nebraska -6.5 O/U 51.5

Neutral Site Showdown

This neutral site matchup in Kansas City features two programs looking to make statements in their respective conferences. Nebraska enters under Matt Rhule with renewed optimism after significant roster improvements through the transfer portal. The Cornhuskers return experienced players at key positions and have addressed previous weaknesses on both sides of the ball.

Cincinnati comes in as the underdog despite recent success, including their College Football Playoff appearance just a few seasons ago. The Bearcats have dealt with coaching changes and roster turnover but retain talented skill position players who can create explosive plays. Their defensive secondary has experience that could challenge Nebraska's passing attack.

The neutral site setting at Arrowhead Stadium eliminates home field advantage and should create an even playing field. Both programs are motivated to start strong in what they view as winnable season openers. Nebraska is favored by 6.5 points with a total of 51.5, suggesting oddsmakers expect a relatively low-scoring affair with the Cornhuskers controlling the game.

@

Boise State Broncos @ South Florida Bulls

2:30 PM ET Raymond James Stadium Boise State -5.5 O/U 61.5

Mountain West vs AAC Clash

Boise State travels to Tampa as 5.5-point favorites in what should be an intriguing cross-conference matchup. The Broncos under Spencer Danielson are looking to maintain their program's standard of excellence with a veteran squad that returns key contributors on both sides of the ball. Their rushing attack and disciplined defensive approach have been program hallmarks.

South Florida enters with something to prove after recent struggles, but they're playing at Raymond James Stadium where they've shown flashes of competitive football. The Bulls have athletic talent that could create problems for Boise State if they can establish rhythm early. Their home field advantage in the Tampa heat could be a factor for the visiting Mountain West team.

The total sits at 61.5, the highest on today's slate, indicating both teams possess offensive capabilities. Boise State's balanced attack against USF's potentially explosive offense suggests this could develop into a higher-scoring affair than some of the other Week 1 contests. The spread of 5.5 points reflects Boise State's program pedigree while respecting USF's home field advantage.

@

Buffalo Bulls @ Minnesota Golden Gophers

5:00 PM ET Huntington Bank Stadium Minnesota -17.5 O/U 43.5

Big Ten vs MAC Opener

Minnesota hosts Buffalo in what represents a significant step up in competition for the MAC school. The Golden Gophers under P.J. Fleck have established a physical, ground-based identity that typically serves them well in season openers against overmatched opponents. Their offensive line and running game should control the line of scrimmage.

Buffalo brings a MAC championship pedigree but faces the challenge of competing against Big Ten size and depth. The Bulls have shown ability to compete with Power 5 opponents in specific situations, but sustained drives against Minnesota's defense could prove challenging. Their best chance lies in creating turnovers and short field situations.

The 17.5-point spread reflects the talent and depth differential between the conferences. Minnesota is favored heavily with a low total of 43.5, suggesting the Gophers' ground game could dominate time of possession. This type of matchup often sees the favorite control tempo and gradually pull away as depth becomes a factor in the second half.

@

Wyoming Cowboys @ Akron Zips

4:00 PM ET InfoCision Stadium Wyoming -5.5 O/U 47.5

Mountain West Road Test

Wyoming makes the trip to Ohio as 5.5-point favorites in what appears to be one of the more evenly matched contests on the Week 1 slate. The Cowboys from the Mountain West bring a physical style of play that has served them well in conference competition, but road games against MAC opponents can present unique challenges.

Akron hosts this game looking for a signature victory to start their season. The Zips have struggled in recent years but possess home field advantage and the motivation of facing a perceived stronger opponent. Their familiarity with their home turf and crowd support could help level the playing field against Wyoming's traveling squad.

The relatively small spread of 5.5 points and low total of 47.5 suggests this projects as a defensive struggle where field position and turnovers could determine the outcome. Both programs are looking to establish early season momentum in what appears to be a game that could go either way based on execution rather than pure talent differential.
