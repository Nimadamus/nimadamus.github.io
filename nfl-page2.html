Browns @ Bears

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Soldier Field

Chicago's turnaround under their new coaching staff has been one of the season's best stories. The Bears are firmly in the playoff picture, with Caleb Williams looking like the franchise quarterback they hoped he'd be. The rookie has matured rapidly, making fewer mistakes while the offensive line has given him time to find his talented receivers.

Cleveland is in full rebuild mode, whether they admit it or not. The quarterback carousel has been a disaster, the offensive line can't protect anyone, and the defense that was supposed to carry this team has regressed significantly. This is a team playing for pride and draft position.

What to Watch

Chicago's pass rush against Cleveland's struggling offensive line is a mismatch. Expect the Bears to bring pressure early and force quick decisions. For the Browns, it's about ball security and keeping this game close enough to have a chance late.

Ravens @ Bengals

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Paycor Stadium

The AFC North rivalry gets another chapter as Lamar Jackson brings his MVP-caliber campaign to Cincinnati. The Ravens' offense is clicking on all cylinders, combining Jackson's otherworldly running ability with a passing game that's become increasingly sophisticated. Derrick Henry has been the perfect complement, wearing down defenses and keeping drives alive.

Cincinnati's season has been a rollercoaster. Joe Burrow has been brilliant when healthy, but the Bengals have been inconsistent defensively. Ja'Marr Chase continues to be one of the most explosive receivers in football, capable of taking over any game with a few catches. This division matchup has playoff implications for both teams.

What to Watch

The Jackson vs. Burrow quarterback duel is the main event. Both can make magic happen when the pocket breaks down. Baltimore's rushing attack against Cincinnati's run defense will set the tone. If the Ravens establish Henry early, it opens everything up for Lamar.

Chargers @ Chiefs

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | GEHA Field at Arrowhead Stadium

Jim Harbaugh has transformed the Chargers in his first season. Los Angeles is playing disciplined, physical football that's been missing for years. Justin Herbert finally has a coach who maximizes his talents, and the defense has become one of the league's stingiest units. This is a team built to compete in January.

Kansas City remains the class of the AFC despite some bumps along the way. Patrick Mahomes continues to do Patrick Mahomes things, and Andy Reid always finds ways to scheme players open. Arrowhead Stadium is one of the toughest environments in football, especially for division rivals.

What to Watch

Herbert vs. Mahomes is always appointment television. The Chargers need to pressure Mahomes and force quick decisions. For Kansas City, it's about exploiting LA's secondary when they bring extra rushers. The team that wins the turnover battle will likely win this game.

Bills @ Patriots

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Gillette Stadium

Buffalo is on a mission this season. Josh Allen has been playing at an MVP level, making impossible throws look routine while leading one of the league's most explosive offenses. The Bills have Super Bowl aspirations and are stacking wins heading into the playoffs. This is a team that believes they can beat anyone.

New England is in full rebuild mode under new leadership. Drake Maye has shown flashes of the franchise quarterback they hope he becomes, but the supporting cast is thin and the defense has been exploited. The Patriots are playing for development and draft positioning at this point.

What to Watch

Can New England's young defense slow down Allen at all? The Bills' offensive line has given him time, and when Josh Allen has time, he's virtually unstoppable. For the Patriots, it's about Maye gaining experience against an elite opponent and showing progress.

Commanders @ Giants

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | MetLife Stadium

Washington's resurgence under Jayden Daniels has been one of the season's best stories. The rookie quarterback has exceeded all expectations, playing with poise and making plays with both his arm and legs. The Commanders are in the playoff hunt and have legitimate aspirations after years of disappointment.

The Giants are having another lost season. The quarterback situation remains unsettled, the offense has been anemic, and there's little reason for optimism. This is a franchise desperately searching for answers that won't come until significant changes are made.

What to Watch

Daniels against a demoralized Giants defense should be able to make plays. Washington's rushing attack has been effective, and the Giants can't stop anyone on the ground. For New York, pride is about all they're playing for at this point.

Raiders @ Eagles

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field

Philadelphia is rolling with one of the most complete rosters in football. Jalen Hurts is playing at an MVP level, Saquon Barkley has revitalized the running game, and the defense is creating havoc on a weekly basis. The Eagles look like legitimate Super Bowl contenders and are stacking wins at home.

Las Vegas is playing out the string on a lost season. The Raiders have dealt with injuries, inconsistent quarterback play, and a defense that can't get stops. Antonio Pierce is trying to build something for the future, but this roster has significant holes that need addressing.

What to Watch

Can the Raiders slow down Philadelphia's rushing attack at all? Barkley has been dominant, and when the Eagles establish the run, they're nearly unstoppable. Lincoln Financial Field will be rocking, and Las Vegas will need to weather the early storm just to stay competitive.

Jets @ Jaguars

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | EverBank Stadium

The Jets' season has been a disaster despite high expectations. Aaron Rodgers hasn't been the savior they hoped for, and the supporting cast has underperformed. New York is playing out the string with major questions about the direction of the franchise heading into the offseason.

Jacksonville has been equally disappointing after a playoff run last year. Trevor Lawrence hasn't taken the leap many expected, and the team has regressed in virtually every area. Doug Pederson is on the hot seat, and this is a franchise in flux.

What to Watch

Two struggling teams meeting in a game nobody outside the locker rooms cares about. Watch for effort and execution - which team still has pride and fight? The quarterback play from Rodgers and Lawrence will determine whether this is watchable or a slog.

Cardinals @ Texans

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | NRG Stadium

Houston's emergence under CJ Stroud has been one of the league's best stories. The second-year quarterback has poise beyond his experience, making plays in the pocket and connecting with his receivers downfield. The Texans have legitimate playoff aspirations and control of the AFC South.

Arizona has been frisky this season with Kyler Murray back healthy. The Cardinals have upset potential every week with Murray's ability to create chaos. Marvin Harrison Jr. has been everything they hoped in his rookie season, giving Murray a true number one target.

What to Watch

Stroud vs. Murray is a fascinating stylistic contrast - Stroud the composed pocket passer, Murray the improviser. Houston's defense needs to contain Murray's scrambling, while Arizona has to find a way to pressure Stroud without leaving receivers open downfield.

Panthers @ Saints

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Caesars Superdome

Carolina continues their rebuild with Bryce Young showing improvement. The second-year quarterback has looked more comfortable, though the supporting cast remains thin. The Panthers are playing hard but lack the talent to compete consistently. This is about development.

New Orleans has dealt with injuries and inconsistency all season. The Saints have flashes of competitiveness but can't sustain it. Derek Carr has been solid when healthy, but the team around him has too many holes. The Superdome provides a home advantage, but this isn't a vintage Saints team.

What to Watch

Can Young build on recent progress against a Saints defense that can be exploited? New Orleans needs to establish the run and keep Carolina's offense off the field. Division games are unpredictable, and the Superdome crowd could be a factor.

Vikings @ Cowboys

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | AT&T Stadium

Minnesota has been one of the league's biggest surprises with Sam Darnold playing at a Pro Bowl level. The career journeyman has found a home with Kevin O'Connell's system, and the Vikings are legitimate playoff contenders. Justin Jefferson remains one of the best receivers in football, and this offense can score with anyone.

Dallas has been a massive disappointment after entering with Super Bowl expectations. Injuries have decimated the offensive line, Dak Prescott has dealt with his own issues, and the defense that was supposed to be elite has been merely average. Jerry Jones is facing hard questions about this franchise's direction.

What to Watch

Jefferson against Dallas's secondary is the marquee matchup. The Vikings will try to get him the ball in space and let him create. For the Cowboys, it's about Dak finding rhythm early and keeping Minnesota's offense off the field with sustained drives.

Titans @ 49ers

Sunday, 4:05 PM ET | Levi's Stadium

San Francisco has dealt with injuries all season but remains dangerous when healthy. Kyle Shanahan's offense can still move the ball, and the defense has playmakers at every level. The 49ers are fighting for playoff positioning and need wins down the stretch.

Tennessee is rebuilding under new quarterback Will Levis. The results have been mixed, but there are signs of progress. The Titans are playing for the future, evaluating young talent and trying to establish an identity for next season.

What to Watch

San Francisco's offensive weapons against Tennessee's young secondary. The 49ers will try to create mismatches and exploit inexperience. For the Titans, it's about Levis gaining valuable experience against a quality opponent.

Colts @ Seahawks

Sunday, 4:05 PM ET | Lumen Field

Seattle has been inconsistent this season, with flashes of brilliance followed by head-scratching losses. Geno Smith has been solid, but the team lacks the explosive playmakers to keep up in shootouts. The defense has been the bigger concern, giving up too many big plays.

Indianapolis is in a weird spot - competitive enough to win games but not quite good enough to make a playoff run. Anthony Richardson has the tools but is still learning to be an NFL quarterback. Jonathan Taylor remains one of the league's best backs when healthy.

What to Watch

Lumen Field is a difficult environment for any visitor. The 12th Man is real, and Seattle feeds off the crowd energy. Richardson's ability to handle the noise and execute will be tested. Taylor's workload could determine whether the Colts stay in this game.

Packers @ Broncos

Sunday, 4:25 PM ET | Empower Field at Mile High

Green Bay's young core has matured rapidly. Jordan Love has taken the next step, looking like a franchise quarterback capable of leading this team deep into January. The receiver room is loaded with talent, and the defense has found its identity late in the season.

Denver has been a pleasant surprise with Bo Nix developing nicely at quarterback. Sean Payton has this team playing competitive football, and the defense has been solid. The Broncos are in the playoff hunt and Mile High remains a tough place to play.

What to Watch

Love against Denver's defense at altitude is a challenge. The thin air affects passing games, and Payton will try to confuse Love with pre-snap looks. For the Broncos, it's about Nix limiting mistakes and letting the defense keep them in the game.

Lions @ Rams

Sunday, 4:25 PM ET | SoFi Stadium

Detroit has embraced the aggressive mentality that makes them dangerous. Dan Campbell goes for it on fourth down, calls aggressive plays in big moments, and has this team believing they can beat anyone. The Lions are one of the NFC's best teams and have legitimate Super Bowl aspirations.

Los Angeles is always competitive under Sean McVay. Matthew Stafford still has plenty left in the tank, and the Rams' offensive scheme creates mismatches. Cooper Kupp and Puka Nacua form one of the league's best receiver duos when healthy. This is a dangerous opponent.

What to Watch

Stafford against his former team adds extra juice. He knows these coaches, these players, and he'll be motivated to show out. Detroit's offense is explosive, but the Rams can score with anyone. Expect points in this one.

Dolphins @ Steelers

Monday, 8:15 PM ET | Acrisure Stadium

Monday Night Football brings Miami to Pittsburgh in a potential playoff preview. The Dolphins' speed-based offense can be lethal when Tua Tagovailoa is healthy and in rhythm. Tyreek Hill and Jaylen Waddle are impossible to cover consistently, and Miami can score in bunches.

Pittsburgh has been solid if unspectacular, grinding out wins with their defense and opportunistic offense. Russell Wilson has been competent, and the Steelers play their brand of physical football regardless of opponent. This is a team that won't beat itself.

What to Watch

Miami's speed against Pittsburgh's physicality is the stylistic clash. The Steelers will try to slow this game down and make it a street fight. If Miami can break loose for big plays, they have the explosiveness to pull away. The weather could also be a factor - cold Pittsburgh nights don't favor South Florida speed.
