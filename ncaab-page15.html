Grizzlies @ Spartans

2025-12-20T17:00Z | Little Caesars Arena | BTN
OAK: 6-6 | MSU: 10-1

Michigan State hosts Oakland at Little Caesars Arena in what should be a comfortable tune-up for the Spartans. Tom Izzo's squad is 10-1 and ranked in the top 15 nationally, featuring one of the most balanced attacks in the Big Ten. Oakland comes in at 6-6, but the Horizon League squad has shown flashes of competitiveness against mid-major competition.

The Spartans' defense has been suffocating this season, allowing opponents to shoot just 41% from the field. Jaden Akins continues to lead this team with his two-way play, and the frontcourt depth gives MSU a significant size advantage. Oakland will need to shoot lights out from three to keep this competitive, but on a neutral floor in Detroit, expect Michigan State to control this one from start to finish.

Grizzlies @ Cardinals

2025-12-20T17:00Z | KFC Yum! Center | ACC Extra
MONT: 6-6 | LOU: 9-2

Louisville welcomes Montana to the KFC Yum! Center in what should be a showcase for Pat Kelsey's revamped Cardinals program. At 9-2, Louisville has exceeded expectations in Kelsey's first season, and the home crowd will be electric for this Saturday matinee. Montana enters at 6-6, having struggled against higher-level competition this season.

The Cardinals have been getting contributions across the roster, with improved defense being the biggest storyline of their early-season success. Montana will need to limit turnovers and control tempo to have any chance here. Louisville's depth and athleticism should prove to be too much for the Grizzlies to handle, especially in a hostile road environment.

Storm @ Wildcats

2025-12-20T17:30Z | State Farm Arena | CBS
SJU: 7-3 | UK: 7-4

The CBS Sports Classic tips off at State Farm Arena in Atlanta with St. John's facing Kentucky in the first game of the doubleheader. St. John's replaced UCLA in the event and comes in at 7-3, while Kentucky has been up and down at 7-4 under Mark Pope's new regime. This is a massive opportunity for both programs to grab a marquee non-conference win.

Kentucky's inconsistency has been concerning, but they have the talent to compete with anyone when clicking. St. John's has been one of the pleasant surprises in the Big East, playing tough defense and grinding out wins. Expect a physical, low-possession game as both teams look to establish tempo. The Wildcats' home-state advantage in Atlanta should give them a slight edge in front of what will be a pro-Kentucky crowd.

Buckeyes @ Heels

2025-12-20T20:00Z | State Farm Arena | CBS, Paramount+
Line: UNC -3.5 | O/U: 158.5 | OSU: 8-2 | UNC: 10-1

The marquee matchup of the CBS Sports Classic features North Carolina against Ohio State in Atlanta. The Tar Heels are rolling at 10-1, their best start since 2008-09, with their only loss coming to Michigan State. Ohio State is 8-2 but has dropped two of their last four, including losses to Pitt and Illinois. UNC holds a commanding 13-3 advantage in the all-time series.

Seth Trimble is expected to return for UNC after missing nine games with a forearm injury, which would be a massive boost for the Tar Heels. Ohio State's Bruce Thornton has been excellent, coming off a 23-point performance against West Virginia. Carolina's depth and experience should be the difference here. With Trimble back and playing in front of a crowd that will skew heavily blue, UNC is the pick to extend their CBS Sports Classic winning streak against the Buckeyes to four straight.

Cougars @ Razorbacks

2025-12-20T22:30Z | Prudential Center | CBS
Line: HOU -5.5 | O/U: 145.5 | HOU: 10-1 | ARK: 9-2

Houston travels to Fayetteville for a rematch of last year's Sweet 16 thriller, which the Cougars won 81-77. This time the Razorbacks get the Cougars on their home floor, and Bud Walton Arena will be rocking. Houston is 10-1 and ranked in the top 10, while Arkansas has impressed at 9-2 under John Calipari in his first season in Fayetteville.

The Cougars' trademark suffocating defense remains elite under Kelvin Sampson, forcing turnovers at one of the highest rates in the country. Arkansas has the athletes to match up physically, but Houston's depth and discipline make them tough to beat. Calipari has reinvigorated this program, and the crowd factor will be significant. This should be a dogfight, but Houston's experience in these situations gives them the edge in a low-scoring grinder.

Terrapins @ Cavaliers

2025-12-20T23:00Z | John Paul Jones Arena | ESPN
Line: UVA -15.5 | O/U: 153.5 | MD: 6-5 | UVA: 9-1

The ACC rivals meet in Charlottesville with Virginia looking to prove they're back among the conference elite. The Cavaliers are 9-1 and playing the Pack-Line defense as well as they have in years. Maryland has struggled at 6-5, dealing with inconsistent shooting and defensive lapses throughout the early season.

Tony Bennett's squad controls tempo better than almost anyone in the country, and John Paul Jones Arena is one of the toughest road environments in college basketball. Maryland will need to be patient on offense and avoid the turnovers that Virginia's defense creates. The Terrapins have the talent to compete, but Virginia's discipline and home-court advantage should carry them to a comfortable win in this one.

Tigers @ Boilermakers

2025-12-20T23:30Z | Gainbridge Fieldhouse | Peacock
Line: PUR -7.5 | O/U: 153.5 | AUB: 8-3 | PUR: 10-1

The Indy Classic features a marquee matchup between No. 6 Purdue and No. 21 Auburn at Gainbridge Fieldhouse. Purdue is 10-1 and led by Big Ten Preseason Player of the Year Braden Smith, who's averaging 9.2 assists per game (2nd nationally). Auburn comes in at 8-3 with Keyshawn Hall leading the way at 20.9 points and 8.5 rebounds per game.

The Boilermakers are shooting 40.2% from three (10th nationally) while Auburn's perimeter defense ranks just 149th at 32.0%. That's a massive mismatch. Purdue's 20.2 assists per game (8th) compared to Auburn's 13.3 (250th) tells the story of two vastly different offensive philosophies. The Tigers beat Purdue 87-69 last year in Birmingham, but this neutral site tilts toward the Boilermakers. Purdue's defense (67.1 PPG allowed) should stifle Auburn's pace and control this game throughout.

Devils @ Raiders

2025-12-21T01:00Z | Madison Square Garden | ESPN
Line: DUKE -8.5 | O/U: 153.5 | DUKE: 11-0 | TTU: 8-3

The SentinelOne Classic at Madison Square Garden features undefeated No. 3 Duke against No. 19 Texas Tech. The Blue Devils are 11-0 and own four wins against ranked opponents. Texas Tech is 8-3 but has lost all three games against ranked competition. This is a showcase for two of the nation's best forwards: Cameron Boozer (23.3 PPG, 10.2 RPG, nation's leading scorer) and JT Toppin (21.9 PPG, 10.6 RPG).

Duke ranks 3rd in adjusted defense per KenPom, holding opponents to 27% from three (10th) and 40.4% from two (2nd). Texas Tech has allowed 90+ points in consecutive games and 86.7 PPG against top-25 KenPom competition. The Red Raiders are just 3-8 ATS this season while the under has cashed in 9 of 11 Duke games. Under the bright lights of MSG, expect Jon Scheyer's Blue Devils to put on a clinic and cover this spread comfortably.

Aztecs @ Wildcats

2025-12-21T03:30Z | Mortgage Matchup Center | ESPN2
Line: ARIZ -13.5 | O/U: 152.5 | SDSU: 6-3 | ARIZ: 10-0

Undefeated Arizona hosts San Diego State in a late-night West Coast showdown at McKale Center. The Wildcats are 10-0 and have steamrolled their competition, establishing themselves as one of the favorites to win the national championship. San Diego State is 6-3, a respectable start but nowhere near the level of competition they'll face tonight.

Tommy Lloyd's Arizona squad is playing elite basketball on both ends, with one of the most efficient offenses in the country. McKale Center is one of the toughest places to play in college basketball, especially for a late tipoff when the Zona Zoo is at full volume. The Aztecs have the defensive identity to keep this from becoming a blowout, but Arizona's talent advantage is simply too much. Expect the Wildcats to win big and stay perfect heading into Pac-12 play.
