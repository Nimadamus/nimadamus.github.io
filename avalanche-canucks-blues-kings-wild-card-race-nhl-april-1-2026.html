3-Game Wednesday Slate

NHL Previews Today

Wednesday's compact three-game slate is loaded with storylines. Nathan MacKinnon continues his Art Ross Trophy pursuit as the Avalanche host the hapless Canucks, the Blues and Kings meet in a do-or-die Wild Card showdown on TNT, and Macklin Celebrini's history-making 100-point Sharks welcome the Pacific Division-leading Ducks to San Jose. Three games, three completely different narratives, all of them fascinating.

Game 1 - Marquee
Art Ross Chase

Canucks @ Avalanche

Wednesday, 8:30 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Puck Line
COL -1.5 (-177) / VAN +1.5 (-110)
Moneyline
COL -435 / VAN +350
Total
O/U 6.5

Nathan MacKinnon is on a mission. With 120 points on the season (49 goals, 71 assists) and sitting just four points behind Connor McDavid in the Art Ross race, every single game from here on out carries enormous individual significance for Colorado's superstar center. The Avalanche (49-14-10, 108 points) have already clinched a playoff berth and sit comfortably atop the Central Division, but don't mistake that security for complacency. MacKinnon has been playing with an intensity that suggests the Art Ross Trophy is very much his primary objective down the stretch, and tonight's matchup against the league's worst team is exactly the kind of game where he can close the gap in a hurry. He's been on a tear lately, with Colorado winning five of their last six and coming off a 9-2 demolition of Calgary that felt like a statement of intent.

Vancouver (21-44-8, 50 points) is living through the worst season in franchise history, and there's no sugarcoating how bad things have gotten on the West Coast. The Canucks have lost six straight, their 50 points through 73 games is a historically awful pace, and the vibes around this organization have cratered to a level that makes next season's rebuild feel like a welcome relief. Kevin Lankinen has been hung out to dry behind a porous defense all year, posting an 8-23-5 record with a 3.62 GAA and an .876 save percentage that tells you everything about what the poor guy has been dealing with. Vancouver isn't coming into Ball Arena with any realistic expectation of winning this game, and the -435 moneyline confirms the market feels the same way.

Colorado will be without Cale Makar, who's dealing with an upper-body injury, and that's the one wrinkle that keeps this game from being a total afterthought. Makar is one of the best defensemen on the planet, and his absence from the lineup changes the complexion of the Avalanche's transition game and power play significantly. The good news for Colorado is that Scott Wedgewood has been absolutely magnificent this season, posting a 16-1-4 record with a 2.01 GAA and a .924 save percentage that makes him one of the most efficient goalies in the league. Wedgewood, or potentially Mackenzie Blackwood, will be between the pipes tonight, and either option gives the Avs a massive advantage in net over whatever Vancouver throws out there.

The 6.5 total is worth watching closely. Colorado has the offensive firepower to push this game well over by themselves, especially with MacKinnon hunting points and the supporting cast riding a wave of confidence after that Calgary blowout. Vancouver's defense has been a sieve all season, allowing goals at a rate that would make most coaches lose sleep, and the altitude factor at Ball Arena only compounds the problem for visiting teams. The Canucks' legs tend to get heavy in the third period in Denver, and that's when the Avalanche's depth really takes over. The puck line at COL -1.5 (-177) is the most interesting number here, because Colorado hasn't just been winning games lately, they've been winning them emphatically. Against the league's worst team, a multi-goal victory feels more like an expectation than a stretch.

Game 2 - Wild Card
TNT

Blues @ Kings

Wednesday, 9:00 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Puck Line
LAK -1.5 (+170) / STL +1.5 (-200)
Moneyline
LAK -143 / STL +125
Total
O/U 5.5

This is the game of the night, and it isn't even close. Two teams fighting for their playoff lives, national television spotlight on TNT, and every single point feeling like it could be the difference between postseason hockey and a long summer of regret. The Los Angeles Kings (27-24-15, 69 points) sit just one point behind Nashville for the second Wild Card spot in the Western Conference, while the St. Louis Blues (31-31-11, 73 points) are four points outside looking in. Both teams know what's at stake, and the desperation level in this building is going to be off the charts. This is the kind of game that defines seasons, and one of these teams is going to walk out of Crypto.com Arena with renewed hope while the other takes a devastating blow to their playoff aspirations.

Here's the thing about St. Louis that the market might be undervaluing: the Blues have been one of the hottest teams in hockey over the past few weeks. They've collected points in 12 of their last 14 games and won four of their last five, which is the kind of surge that transforms a team's identity in the final weeks of the season. And the biggest reason for that surge has a name: Joel Hofer. The young goaltender has been absolutely absurd since the Olympic break, going 7-0-2 with a 1.29 GAA and a .959 save percentage that is the best in the entire league during that stretch. When your goalie is stopping everything in sight, you've got a chance to beat anyone, and Hofer has given the Blues the kind of backstop performance that turns fringe playoff teams into legitimate threats. Getting +125 on the road with a goalie playing at that level is the kind of number that makes you sit up and take notice.

Los Angeles has the home-ice advantage and the -143 price tag, but let's be honest about what this Kings team actually is: they've been terrible at home. A 10-17-8 record at Crypto.com Arena is one of the worst home marks in the Western Conference, and that's not the kind of foundation you want to build a playoff push on. Kevin Fiala's absence on injured reserve takes a significant piece of the Kings' offensive puzzle off the board, and while Anze Kopitar continues to will this team forward in what is expected to be his final NHL season, there's only so much one player can do when the supporting cast isn't holding up its end. The February acquisition of Artemi Panarin from the Rangers was supposed to be the move that pushed LA over the top, but the results at home haven't matched the talent on paper.

The 5.5 total is set low, and it should be. Both of these teams are going to play like their seasons depend on the outcome, because they do. Expect tight checking, conservative neutral zone play, and every loose puck being contested like it's Game 7 of the Stanley Cup Finals. Hofer's recent form suggests the Blues will keep this close regardless of how the game flows, and the Kings don't have the offensive depth to blow the doors off anyone right now, especially without Fiala in the lineup. This has the feel of a 2-1 or 3-2 game that comes down to the final five minutes, and in a contest that tight, the goaltender who blinks first is going to send his team home with a loss that stings for the rest of the offseason. The Wild Card race is absolutely brutal right now, and this is the epicenter of it.

Game 3
ESPN+

Ducks @ Sharks

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | SAP Center, San Jose, CA
Puck Line
ANA -1.5 / SJ +1.5
Moneyline
ANA -110 / SJ -105
Total
O/U 6.5

The late-night window delivers a California showdown between two franchises that are both having seasons nobody expected, albeit for very different reasons. The Anaheim Ducks (41-28-5, 87 points) are sitting first in the Pacific Division with 87 points, a sentence that would have gotten you laughed out of any preseason prediction panel. This Ducks team has been a revelation, playing fast, physical, and fearless hockey all season long. But they're coming into SAP Center tonight without their most important player: Cutter Gauthier is out with a lower-body injury, and losing a guy who has 38 goals and 65 points as the team's leading scorer is a massive blow. Gauthier has been the engine that drives Anaheim's offense, and his absence creates a hole in the lineup that won't be easy to fill.

San Jose (34-31-7, 75 points) is having its own remarkable story, and it all centers around one name: Macklin Celebrini. The 19-year-old has reached 101 points this season (38 goals, 63 assists), making him just the seventh teenager in NHL history to crack the 100-point barrier. That's a list that includes Wayne Gretzky, Mario Lemieux, Sidney Crosby, and a handful of other all-time legends. Let that sink in for a moment. Celebrini isn't just having a good rookie season; he's having a historically great one, the kind of campaign that defines a franchise for decades. The Sharks are two points behind Nashville for the final Wild Card spot with two games in hand, and Celebrini's brilliance has turned what was supposed to be another rebuilding year into a legitimate playoff chase. That's the transformative power of a generational talent.

The moneyline on this game is essentially a pick'em, with Anaheim hovering around -110 to -114 and San Jose sitting at -105. That pricing tells you the market respects what both teams bring to the table, and Gauthier's absence is clearly being factored into the number. Without their top scorer, the Ducks are going to need their depth to step up in a big way, and that's always a risky proposition on the road against a team fighting for its postseason life. Lukas Dostal (3.02 GAA, .899 save percentage) gets the call in net for Anaheim, and while he's been solid if unspectacular, tonight's matchup against a team with a 100-point scorer demands his best performance. On the other end, Yaroslav Askarov (19-17-3, 3.52 GAA, .887 save percentage) hasn't been dominant by any stretch, but the Sharks don't need him to be a Vezina candidate. They need him to make timely saves while Celebrini and company take care of the rest.

The 6.5 total is set high for a reason: both of these teams can score. Anaheim's offense doesn't disappear just because Gauthier is out, and San Jose's attack with Celebrini running the show is capable of lighting up any goaltender on any given night. This has the feel of an entertaining, wide-open affair where neither team is going to sit back and play conservatively. The Sharks need every point they can get in the Wild Card race, and the Ducks need to prove they can win meaningful games without their best player if they want to be taken seriously as a Pacific Division title contender. Expect goals, expect pace, and expect Celebrini to put on a show for the home crowd at SAP Center. When a teenager is chasing NHL history while simultaneously dragging his team into the playoffs, you don't want to miss it.
