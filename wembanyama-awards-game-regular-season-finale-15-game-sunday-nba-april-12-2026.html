15-Game Sunday Slate

NBA Previews Today

The final day of the 2025-26 NBA regular season. All 30 teams take the floor in a 15-game Sunday spectacular with everything on the line. Victor Wembanyama needs 20 minutes against Denver to lock in his DPOY and MVP eligibility. The Lakers can steal the 3 seed with a win and a Nuggets loss. Play-in positioning across both conferences remains fluid, with Charlotte, Miami, Portland, Golden State, and the Clippers all fighting for their postseason lives. This is the last chance to separate contenders from pretenders before the real games begin.

Game 1
ESPN

Magic @ Celtics

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA

This might be the most fascinating rest-versus-desperation dynamic on the entire board. Boston has shut it down. Jayson Tatum remains out with his Achilles injury that has defined the second half of the Celtics' season, Jaylen Brown is sitting, and Derrick White is out as well. The Celtics are locked into the 2 seed at 55-26 and have zero incentive to risk anyone meaningful in a game that cannot improve their positioning. What you'll see from Boston is a G League audition, deep bench guys fighting for playoff roster spots in front of a TD Garden crowd that might be half empty by the third quarter.

Orlando, on the other hand, is playing for its life. The Magic sit at 45-36 and need a win to secure the 7th seed and home court for the play-in tournament. That distinction matters enormously, because hosting the play-in game gives you two shots at making the playoffs instead of one. Paolo Banchero and Franz Wagner will be locked in from the opening tip, and Orlando's defensive identity, which has been one of the best in the East all season, should be more than enough to handle whatever Boston's reserves throw at them.

The 11.5-point spread in favor of the Magic as a road team tells you everything about this game. Orlando is essentially playing against a team that has forfeited the competitive aspect of the evening. The -575 moneyline is one of the most lopsided on the board, and the market is essentially pricing this as a scrimmage for the Celtics and a must-win for the Magic. The 217.5 total is suppressed by the expectation that Boston's offense will be putrid without any of their playmakers, and Orlando's defense should clamp down from the start.

The only real question is how quickly Orlando builds a lead and whether the Magic pull their own starters in the fourth quarter. If Banchero and Wagner play 30-plus minutes and the game stays close into the second half, something has gone terribly wrong for Orlando. This should be a professional dismantling, and the Magic need to treat it that way. You don't want to be the team that dropped a game to a team of benchwarmers on the last day of the regular season.

Game 2

Wizards @ Cavaliers

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH

Cleveland is locked into the 4 seed at 51-30, and the question is how many minutes Donovan Mitchell and the rest of the core rotation actually play in this one. The Cavaliers have nothing to gain or lose in the standings, which makes this a classic load management game where the starters might play the first half and then head to the locker room for good. When Cleveland is fully engaged, they're one of the most dangerous teams in the East, but this is not that kind of night.

Washington brings the worst record in the Eastern Conference at 17-64, and the Wizards have been running out developmental lineups for months. This is a team focused entirely on the draft lottery, and every game at this point is about evaluating young talent and building habits. The Wizards play hard because that's what rebuilding teams are supposed to do, but the talent gap even against Cleveland's second unit is enormous.

The 10.5-point spread suggests the market expects Cleveland to send out a competitive enough lineup to handle the league's worst team without much drama. The 235.5 total is elevated because the Wizards play fast and don't defend, which inflates scoring numbers even in blowouts. If Cleveland's bench guys are looking to make a statement heading into the playoffs, they'll push the pace and try to put up numbers that get them in the coaching staff's good graces.

This game is primarily about Cleveland's depth pieces getting reps in a low-stakes environment. The Cavaliers' playoff rotation is set, but the 8th through 12th men could use live game action to stay sharp. Washington will be competitive for stretches because they have nothing to lose and no pressure, but the final result should be a comfortable Cleveland win regardless of who's on the floor.

Game 3

Pistons @ Pacers

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Gainbridge Fieldhouse, Indianapolis, IN

Detroit has been the story of the NBA this season. The Pistons went from laughingstock to 59-22 and the best record in the Eastern Conference, clinching the 1 seed with the kind of turnaround that will be studied by front offices for years. Cade Cunningham has emerged as a legitimate MVP-caliber player, and the roster around him has bought into a defensive identity that suffocates opponents night after night. With the 1 seed locked up, the question is purely about rest and preparation for the playoff opener.

Indiana has endured a miserable season at 19-62, still reeling from the Tyrese Haliburton Achilles injury suffered during the 2025 Finals that derailed everything. The Pacers have been in full rebuild mode, cycling through lineups and giving young players extended run to evaluate what they have going forward. There are some interesting pieces on this roster, but none of them are ready to compete with a juggernaut like Detroit, even if the Pistons rest their core.

The 13.5-point spread is massive, and the -900 moneyline on Detroit is the most lopsided on the entire board. Even with potential rest for Cunningham and other starters, the Pistons' depth is elite. Their bench has outperformed most teams' starting fives in net rating this season, which is a testament to the organizational culture they've built. The 228.5 total could go either way depending on how much Detroit dials up the defensive intensity with their backups.

This is a victory lap game for Detroit. The franchise has gone from the worst team in the league two years ago to the best record in the East, and the Pistons' development program deserves enormous credit. Indiana will compete because that's what young teams do, and the Pacers' up-tempo style ensures the game won't be boring. But the outcome was decided before tipoff, and Detroit could win this game playing their third-stringers for 48 minutes.

Game 4

Hawks @ Heat

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Kaseya Center, Miami, FL

This is one of the most consequential games on the board. Atlanta sits at 46-35 and can clinch the 5th seed with a win, which would give them home court in the first round and avoid the play-in entirely. The Hawks have been one of the most impressive second-half teams in the league, and Trae Young has been operating at an All-NBA level that has silenced every critic who questioned whether he could be the engine of a contender. A win here and Atlanta goes into the playoffs with serious momentum and positioning.

Miami is clinging to life at 42-39, fighting for the 9th or 10th seed and a spot in the play-in tournament. The Heat's season has been defined by the absence of a true second star after trading away their franchise wing to Golden State last offseason. Without that elite two-way presence, Miami has relied on Bam Adebayo to carry an enormous load, and while he's been excellent, the roster simply doesn't have the firepower to compete with the East's upper tier. A loss here could push the Heat to the brink of missing the postseason entirely.

The 3.5-point spread in Miami's favor might surprise some people given the stakes, but home court matters and the Heat have been tough at the Kaseya Center all season. The +136/-162 moneyline tells you this is a genuine toss-up with a slight home adjustment. Atlanta's offense has been elite, averaging well over 115 per game since the All-Star break, and their pace of play has been among the fastest in the league. Miami's defense will try to slow things down, but containing Trae Young in the pick-and-roll has been a problem no team has consistently solved.

The 242.5 total is the third-highest on the board, reflecting the offensive firepower Atlanta brings and Miami's inability to consistently lock teams down without their former franchise wing. Both teams have everything to play for, which means maximum effort, maximum intensity, and the kind of regular-season game that feels like a playoff atmosphere. Young versus Adebayo, pace versus grind, 5th seed versus survival. This is what the final day of the regular season is supposed to look like.

Game 5

Hornets @ Knicks

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY

New York has pulled the plug. Jalen Brunson is sitting, Karl-Anthony Towns is out, OG Anunoby is resting, and Josh Hart is done for the regular season. The Knicks are locked into the 3 seed at 53-28 and have made the decision to prioritize health over momentum heading into the playoffs. Tom Thibodeau resting players is about as rare as a Knicks fan being quiet at MSG, so you know the coaching staff is taking the postseason preparation seriously.

Charlotte needs this game desperately. The Hornets sit at 43-38 and need a win to secure the 9th seed, which gives them a spot in the play-in tournament. LaMelo Ball has been sensational this season when healthy, and the Hornets' offense runs through him in a way that makes them genuinely dangerous when he's on the floor. Getting a shot at the Knicks' practice squad is the best possible scenario for Charlotte on the final day of the regular season.

The 13.5-point spread in Charlotte's favor as a road team at Madison Square Garden is surreal, but the math checks out. The Hornets are essentially a full-strength team playing against the Knicks' summer league roster. The -750 moneyline is the second-most lopsided on the board, and the market is giving Charlotte a near-certainty of winning. The 217.5 total is depressed because the Knicks' offense without Brunson, Towns, Anunoby, and Hart will be virtually non-existent.

Charlotte cannot afford to sleepwalk into this one. Stranger things have happened in the NBA than a motivated bench beating a team that expected an easy night. The Hornets need to come out aggressive from the opening tip, build a lead early, and treat this like a playoff game. If LaMelo Ball attacks the paint from the start and the Hornets' defense doesn't get complacent, this should be a comfortable win. But if Charlotte plays down to the competition, the Knicks' young guys could make this interesting in front of whatever's left of the MSG crowd.

Game 6

Bucks @ 76ers

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center, Philadelphia, PA

Philadelphia is fighting for the 8th seed and home court in the play-in tournament at 44-37, and a win here would lock that up. The 76ers have been on a mission down the stretch, and Joel Embiid has been dominant in the second half of the season after managing his workload carefully. Philly's two-way play has been elite when healthy, and tonight they get a Milwaukee team that is barely recognizable from the powerhouse we've come to expect.

The Bucks are a shell of themselves. Giannis Antetokounmpo is out, Kuzma is sitting, and Myles Turner is sidelined, leaving Milwaukee with a roster that would struggle in the G League on most nights. At 32-49, this has been one of the most disappointing seasons in recent memory for a franchise that was supposed to be contending. The injuries have been catastrophic, and there's simply no fight left in this group heading into the offseason.

The 15.5-point spread is the second-largest on the board, and the -1100 moneyline on Philadelphia tells you everything about how one-sided this is expected to be. Milwaukee's depleted roster has no answer for Embiid in the post, no wing to slow down Tyrese Maxey in transition, and no defensive anchor to organize their rotations. This is a game where Philadelphia could lead by 30 at halftime if they come out focused.

The 226.5 total is moderate, and the under might be live if Philadelphia's defense clamps down and Milwaukee simply cannot generate enough offense to keep pace. The Bucks' reserves will push the ball and take their shots, which could inflate the scoring slightly, but the efficiency on their end will be terrible. Philly should treat this as a playoff rehearsal, run their sets, get their rhythm, and head into the play-in tournament with confidence and home court.

Game 7

Nets @ Raptors

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena, Toronto, ON

The 22.5-point spread is the largest on the entire 15-game board, and it might not be enough. Toronto sits at 45-36 and needs a win to clinch a top-6 seed and avoid the play-in tournament entirely. That's enormous for a franchise that has rebuilt around Scottie Barnes and Brandon Ingram, who was traded to the Raptors in February 2025. The Raptors have been one of the league's most pleasant surprises, playing cohesive basketball that blends Barnes' versatility with Ingram's shot creation and a supporting cast that plays hard every night.

Brooklyn at 20-61 is one of the worst teams in the league and has been playing out the string since before the All-Star break. The Nets are focused entirely on the draft lottery, and the roster that takes the floor tonight will be heavy on end-of-bench guys and two-way contract players. There is nothing for Brooklyn to play for except professional pride, and against a Toronto team with everything on the line, that professional pride won't count for much.

The -8000 moneyline on Toronto is staggering but appropriate. The Raptors cannot afford any slipups, and the expectation is that Scottie Barnes and company will come out firing from the opening tip and never let the foot off the gas. The 218.5 total is suppressed by the likelihood that Toronto's defense suffocates Brooklyn's reserves into an offensive nightmare. The Nets might struggle to crack 90 in this one, which would require Toronto to score in the 120s to push the over.

This is a coronation game for Toronto. The Raptors have earned their spot in the top six through consistent, competitive basketball all season, and closing it out with a win at Scotiabank Arena would be the perfect way to head into the postseason. Barnes has been an All-Star-caliber player this year, and Ingram has fit seamlessly into the system since arriving. Brooklyn is just here to fill the schedule. The Raptors win this one going away.

Game 8

Bulls @ Mavericks

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | American Airlines Center, Dallas, TX

Welcome to the highest total on the entire board at 246.5, and the reason is simple: neither of these teams can defend anything. Chicago at 31-50 and Dallas at 25-56 are both playing out meaningless seasons, and the pace of play in this one should be blistering. Both teams have embraced the youth movement and let their young guards run free, which means transition baskets, early three-pointers, and very little interest in getting stops on the other end.

Dallas has been one of the most depressing stories in the NBA this season. The post-Luka Doncic era, after the superstar was traded to the Lakers, has been painful. The Mavericks have the worst record in the Western Conference, and the roster is a collection of spare parts and development pieces that doesn't have a clear identity or direction. They play hard in spurts at home, but the lack of star power is glaring and the losses have piled up relentlessly.

Chicago is in a similar spot, having committed to a rebuild that has produced more questions than answers. The Bulls' young players show flashes, but consistency has been non-existent, and the defensive effort has been among the worst in the league. The 6.5-point spread in Chicago's favor reflects the fact that the Bulls are slightly less terrible than the Mavericks, which is a sentence nobody wanted to write.

This is the kind of late-season game between eliminated teams that either gives you an entertaining shootout or a sloppy mess with 40 turnovers. Given the 246.5 total, the market is leaning toward the shootout scenario, and that makes sense. Both teams will push pace, both teams will let their young guards hunt, and neither team will exert much energy on the defensive end. If you're looking for elite basketball, look elsewhere. If you're looking for sheer volume of scoring and a fast-paced exhibition, this is your game.

Game 9

Grizzlies @ Rockets

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX

Houston is locked into the 5 seed at 51-30, and Kevin Durant has had the kind of season in his first year with the Rockets that justifies the massive trade from the Suns in July 2025. Durant has given Houston a proven playoff anchor, and the Rockets' young core has grown up around his presence in a way that makes them a legitimate dark horse contender in the West. Whether the coaching staff rests Durant tonight or gives him a light workload is the biggest variable in this game.

Memphis at 25-56 has been going through the motions for months. Ja Morant's availability has been inconsistent all season, and the Grizzlies have never been able to sustain the competitive stretches that made them so dangerous in previous years. The roster around Morant needs significant upgrades, and this offseason will be critical for the franchise's direction. Walking into Houston against a team with nothing to lose but potentially resting its stars creates an interesting dynamic.

The 12.5-point spread is significant, and the -800 moneyline on Houston suggests the Rockets will be competitive regardless of who plays. Houston's depth has been a strength all season, and their bench units have been among the best in the West. The 224.5 total is moderate, reflecting the Rockets' strong defensive identity that has been a hallmark of their season. Even without Durant, Houston's perimeter defense and rim protection should limit what Memphis can generate offensively.

This is a get-right game for Houston heading into the playoffs. The Rockets want to build momentum and keep their rotation guys sharp without pushing them into injury territory. Memphis will come out and compete because they have nothing else to do, but the talent differential is massive. Houston should handle this comfortably and head into the postseason feeling good about where they are.

Game 10

Pelicans @ Timberwolves

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Target Center, Minneapolis, MN

Minnesota is locked into the 6 seed at 48-33, and the Timberwolves face the classic final-day dilemma of whether to keep the engine running or shut it down for the playoffs. Anthony Edwards has had a monster season, and Minnesota's defensive ceiling when everyone is engaged is among the best in the Western Conference. The coaching staff will likely give Edwards and the starters a half of work before pulling the plug, which could keep this interesting late.

New Orleans at 26-55 has had another injury-plagued season that has prevented the Pelicans from ever finding any rhythm. Zion Williamson's availability has been a constant question mark, and without consistent star production, the Pelicans have been overmatched against the league's better teams all year. Walking into Target Center against a Minnesota team that might not be fully engaged is about the best New Orleans could hope for on the final day.

The 6.5-point spread is tighter than you might expect for a 48-win team hosting a 26-win team, and that reflects the rest factor. If Edwards and the starters play limited minutes, the margin could narrow significantly against a Pelicans team with nothing to lose. The +220/-270 moneyline and 235.5 total suggest a moderately competitive game that Minnesota wins comfortably but perhaps not by double digits.

For Minnesota, this is about finishing the regular season on a positive note and heading into the playoffs with confidence. The Timberwolves have been one of the more consistent teams in the West, and their defensive identity gives them a real shot at making a deep run. New Orleans will push the pace and try to make this fun, but the Pelicans simply don't have the horses to hang with Minnesota's top-end talent, even in a reduced-minutes scenario.

Game 11

Suns @ Thunder

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK

Oklahoma City has put together one of the greatest regular seasons in NBA history at 64-17, and the defending champions have been the class of the league from wire to wire. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been the best player in basketball for long stretches of this season, and the Thunder's combination of elite defense and efficient offense has made them the team everyone is trying to catch in the Western Conference. With the 1 seed locked up, the question is whether SGA and the core even take the floor tonight.

Phoenix is locked into the 7 seed at 44-37 and headed for the play-in tournament regardless of tonight's result. The Suns have been a solid team all season, but the ceiling without a true second star next to Devin Booker has limited their ability to compete with the West's elite. Bradley Beal has been serviceable but inconsistent, and Phoenix has had to rely on Booker's brilliance more than any team should rely on a single player. Heading to OKC to face the league's best team isn't ideal preparation for the play-in.

The 5.5-point spread in OKC's favor is surprisingly modest and reflects the possibility that the Thunder rest their starters. The -238 moneyline still favors OKC, which tells you how deep this roster is. Even without Gilgeous-Alexander, Chet Holmgren, and Jalen Williams, the Thunder have enough talent on the bench to compete with most teams. The 213.5 total is the lowest on the entire board, suggesting a defensive grind regardless of who plays.

This game is about OKC's depth getting a final tune-up before the playoff machine kicks into gear. The Thunder's organizational depth is arguably the best in basketball, and their young reserves play with the same intensity and defensive principles as the starters. Phoenix will compete because Booker doesn't have a gear lower than maximum effort, but the Suns would probably prefer to conserve energy for the play-in games that actually matter.

Game 12
ESPN

Nuggets @ Spurs

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX

This is the game of the night, and it has everything. Victor Wembanyama needs just 20 minutes on the floor to clinch his eligibility for Defensive Player of the Year and MVP voting, and the Spurs are going to make sure their generational talent gets those minutes in front of a Frost Bank Center crowd that will be absolutely electric. San Antonio at 62-19 has been the NBA's best story behind Detroit, with De'Aaron Fox and Wembanyama forming the most devastating young partnership in the league. The 2 seed is locked, but this game has awards-season implications that make it must-watch television on ESPN.

Denver comes in at 53-28 and currently holds the 3 seed, but a loss combined with a Lakers win would drop the Nuggets to the 4 seed. That's meaningful because the 3 seed gets a more favorable first-round matchup, and Nikola Jokic's team has worked too hard all season to stumble at the finish line. Jokic himself is listed as Questionable with a wrist issue, and his status could swing this game dramatically. If Jokic plays, Denver becomes a much tougher opponent. If he sits, the Nuggets may not have enough firepower to hang with San Antonio's buzzsaw defense.

The 11.5-point spread reflects the gap between these two teams at full strength, with San Antonio's home dominance being one of the defining features of their season. The Spurs have been nearly unbeatable at Frost Bank Center, and the crowd energy for Wembanyama's awards-eligibility game will be off the charts. The -520 moneyline is steep but justified. The 232.5 total suggests a game with plenty of offense on both sides, driven by the Spurs' transition attack and Denver's half-court precision when Jokic is orchestrating.

The Wembanyama factor makes this appointment viewing. He's been the most impactful defender in the NBA this season, and his offensive game has taken a leap that has put him squarely in the MVP conversation alongside Gilgeous-Alexander and a handful of others. Fox has been the perfect running mate, pushing the pace and creating easy looks in transition while Wembanyama anchors the defense and stretches the floor. If Denver comes in without Jokic, the Spurs could turn this into a coronation. If Jokic plays, this becomes a chess match between two of the most fascinating teams in basketball. Either way, this is the game you circle on the final day of the regular season.

Game 13

Jazz @ Lakers

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA

The Lakers are in a fascinating position at 52-29. They hold the 4 seed, but a win combined with a Denver loss in San Antonio would vault them to the 3 seed and a significantly better first-round matchup. That makes this game critical for Los Angeles, even against a Jazz team that has been out of the picture for months. Luka Doncic, who was traded to the Lakers in February 2025, has been the driving force of this team's offense, and his presence alongside Anthony Davis gives LA two of the most talented players in basketball. LeBron James is listed Questionable with a foot issue, which adds a wrinkle to the rotation.

Utah at 22-59 is one of the worst teams in the league and has been focused on the draft lottery since January. The Jazz have some young pieces that show promise, but the roster as constructed cannot compete with playoff-caliber teams. Lauri Markkanen has been solid when healthy, but the supporting cast around him lacks the defensive capability and shot creation needed to win in the NBA. Walking into Crypto.com Arena against a motivated Lakers team with seeding on the line is about the worst draw Utah could get.

The 14.5-point spread is massive, and the -1200 moneyline on the Lakers is the most extreme on the board. The market is essentially treating this as a foregone conclusion, and it's hard to argue. Even if LeBron sits, the combination of Doncic and Davis should be more than enough to handle the Jazz. The 236.5 total is elevated because the Lakers' offense has been prolific all season, and Utah's defense has been one of the worst in the league, creating the conditions for a high-scoring blowout.

This is a seeding game for the Lakers, and you can expect maximum effort from the coaching staff and the top of the rotation. Doncic will be orchestrating the offense with the kind of precision that has made him one of the best playmakers in NBA history, and Davis will be patrolling the paint looking to establish his dominance heading into the playoffs. Utah will push pace and try to keep the game entertaining, but the talent gap is enormous. The Lakers need to take care of business and then watch the San Antonio scoreboard.

Game 14

Warriors @ Clippers

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Intuit Dome, Inglewood, CA

This is a play-in elimination game disguised as a regular season finale. The Warriors at 37-44 and the Clippers at 41-40 are the 10 and 9 seeds respectively, and tonight's result directly impacts who hosts the first play-in game and who goes on the road. The Clippers need a win to clinch the 9th seed, while the Warriors need a win combined with a Clippers loss (which can't happen since they're playing each other) to climb. In reality, the Clippers control their own destiny here, and a win sends them into the play-in with home court advantage for the 9-10 game.

Golden State's season has been a slow decline from championship contention to play-in uncertainty. Stephen Curry has been brilliant as always, but the roster around him has thinned out, and the Jimmy Butler torn ACL that ended his season with the Warriors after his offseason move has been the single biggest blow. Without Butler's two-way presence, Golden State lacks the secondary star power needed to compete with the West's elite. Curry can still take over any game on any given night, but asking him to carry this group through a play-in gauntlet is a tall order.

The Clippers are a 5.5-point home favorite, and the -245 moneyline reflects the combination of home court at the new Intuit Dome and a roster that, while not spectacular, is deep and well-coached. Kawhi Leonard is listed Questionable with an ankle issue, and his status is the swing factor. If Leonard plays, the Clippers become a legitimate threat to steal a playoff spot. If he sits, this becomes much more of a coin flip. The 224.5 total is moderate and could go either way depending on pace and defensive intensity.

This game has a playoff atmosphere baked in. Both teams know that a loss could effectively end their season within the next week, and the stakes produce the kind of intensity that the final day of the regular season rarely delivers. Curry versus the Clippers at their new arena, with the play-in tournament hanging in the balance, is the kind of narrative that makes the NBA regular season finale worth watching. Expect maximum effort from both sides, a hostile crowd, and a game that comes down to execution in the final five minutes.

Game 15

Kings @ Trail Blazers

Sunday, 8:30 PM ET | Moda Center, Portland, OR

Portland is fighting for the 8th seed and home court in the play-in tournament at 41-40, and a win here clinches it. The Trail Blazers have been one of the league's pleasant surprises this season, playing competitive basketball that has exceeded almost every preseason projection. Anfernee Simons has been electric, and Portland's offensive identity has been built around pace, spacing, and three-point volume. At the Moda Center, the Trail Blazers have been a tough out all year, and tonight's crowd will be fully invested in what could be a season-defining game.

Sacramento at 22-59 has had a brutal season since trading De'Aaron Fox to the Spurs in February 2025. Zach LaVine, who came over from the Bulls, has provided scoring punch but hasn't been able to replicate the engine that Fox was for this franchise. The Kings are focused entirely on the draft lottery, and every game at this point is about evaluating young talent and figuring out the path forward. Walking into Portland to face a desperate team with playoff aspirations is about the worst possible assignment for a rebuilding roster.

The 16.5-point spread is enormous, and the -1450 moneyline on Portland is among the most extreme on the board. The Trail Blazers cannot afford to take this lightly, but the talent gap is so wide that even a casual effort should produce a comfortable win. The 228.5 total could go over if Portland's offense gets rolling and Sacramento's defense, one of the worst in the league, fails to slow them down. The Kings play fast and take a lot of threes, which can inflate scoring totals even in blowouts.

For Portland, this is about clinching the 8th seed and heading into the play-in with the confidence that comes from a strong regular-season finish. The Trail Blazers have earned their spot through consistent effort and competitive basketball, and closing it out at home against a team that can't hurt them would be the perfect cap to the regular season. Sacramento will compete in stretches because LaVine can score on anyone, but the Kings don't have the defensive capability or depth to sustain anything against a motivated Portland team. The Moda Center should be rocking.
