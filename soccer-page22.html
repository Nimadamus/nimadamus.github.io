Bournemouth @ Manchester United

December 15, 2025 | 3:00 PM ET | Old Trafford | USA Network
Bournemouth: 5W-6D-5L | Man United: 7W-5D-4L

Old Trafford hosts a Premier League clash that could go either way. Manchester United's 7-5-4 record looks decent on paper, but anyone who's watched the Red Devils this season knows it's been a rollercoaster. Ruben Amorim's side has shown flashes of brilliance but remains maddeningly inconsistent. They've been vulnerable at home, and the Theatre of Dreams hasn't exactly been a fortress this campaign.

Bournemouth come in at 5-6-5, which doesn't scream title contenders, but Andoni Iraola has this squad punching above their weight. The Cherries are dangerous travelers - they don't fear anyone and they'll come to Old Trafford looking to play their game. Dominic Solanke has been clinical in front of goal, and their pressing has caused problems for bigger sides all season.

Here's the thing about this match: United need the points desperately to stay in the top-four race, but needing points and getting them are two different things. Bournemouth will make this uncomfortable. They won't sit back and invite pressure. Expect an open, end-to-end affair where both teams create chances. Don't be shocked if the visitors nick something here.

Real Betis @ Rayo Vallecano

December 15, 2025 | 3:00 PM ET | Estadio de Vallecas | ESPN+
Betis: 6W-7D-3L | Rayo: 4W-6D-6L

Vallecas is one of the most underrated atmospheres in European football. Rayo's supporters are absolutely rabid, and they turn this compact stadium into a cauldron. At 4-6-6, Rayo have struggled for consistency this season, but at home they're a different animal entirely. Inigo Perez's side is disciplined, hard-working, and they make life miserable for visiting teams.

Real Betis arrive with a 6-7-3 record that suggests they're solid but unspectacular. Manuel Pellegrini's squad has the technical quality to control possession, and they've been strong on the road. Isco continues to pull the strings in midfield when healthy, and they've got goal threats across the front line. The question is whether they can handle Rayo's intense pressing and the hostile environment.

This has draw written all over it. Rayo at home are hard to beat, but Betis have enough quality to avoid defeat. Both managers will set up pragmatically - neither can afford to lose. Look for a cagey tactical battle where the first goal will be absolutely crucial. Under 2.5 goals wouldn't surprise anyone who knows these two sides.

Como @ AS Roma

December 15, 2025 | 2:45 PM ET | Stadio Olimpico | Paramount+
Como: 6W-6D-3L | Roma: 10W-0D-5L (no draws!)

One of the wildest stats in Serie A this season: Roma have played 15 league matches and haven't drawn a single one. Ten wins, five losses, zero draws. That tells you everything about Claudio Ranieri's side - they either dominate or collapse, there's no middle ground. At the Olimpico, they've been formidable, and the fans are starting to believe again after a turbulent start to the campaign.

Como's return to Serie A has been remarkable. At 6-6-3, Cesc Fabregas is proving to be a managerial prodigy. The promoted side plays attractive, possession-based football that's won them admirers across Europe. They don't park the bus and hope for the best - they come to play. Patrick Cutrone has been excellent up front, and they've got technical quality throughout the squad.

This is a fascinating tactical clash. Roma's all-or-nothing approach means there will be goals and drama - that's guaranteed. Como won't be intimidated by the Olimpico atmosphere; they'll try to impose their style and make Roma uncomfortable. The hosts should have too much firepower at home, but don't expect Como to go down without a fight. This one has entertainment value written all over it.
