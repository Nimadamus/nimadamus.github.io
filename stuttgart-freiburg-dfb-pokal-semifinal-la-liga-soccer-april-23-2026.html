ESPN+ / ESPN Deportes

Stuttgart vs Freiburg

Thursday, 2:45 PM ET | MHP Arena, Stuttgart, Germany

The all-Baden-Wurttemberg DFB-Pokal semifinal at MHP Arena is the biggest single-match storyline on the global soccer slate. Defending Pokal holders VfB Stuttgart host SC Freiburg in a single-leg knockout with a Berlin final berth at stake. Stuttgart sit third in the Bundesliga at 56 points with a plus-22 goal difference through 29 matches, while Freiburg occupy eighth at 40 points with a minus-5 goal difference. Market prices reflect home dominance, with Stuttgart implied at 59.5 percent to win the 90 minutes, Freiburg at 18.5 percent, and the draw at 22.5 percent.

Freiburg's Cinderella season adds a layer of tension. Sporting Freiburg just thrashed Celta Vigo 6-1 on aggregate to reach the Europa League semifinal, which means Thursday's Pokal semi is the first half of a two-semifinal week with a Bayer Leverkusen trip looming in the Europa League on April 30. The Freiburg form line is four straight wins across competitions, and Julian Schuster's squad has been producing chance-creation numbers that outrank their league position. Max Rosenfelder and Patrick Osterhage are both out for the trip to Stuttgart, which weakens the midfield spine in a match where the Swabian pressure is going to be relentless.

Stuttgart's own form is mixed. A recent 4-2 loss to Bayern Munich exposed defensive vulnerabilities against elite ball-progression teams, and defender Finn Jeltsch is out for the semifinal. Sebastian Hoeness has been able to rest rotation players during the Bundesliga run-in, and the Pokal holders' squad depth at forward and creative midfield positions gives VfB the structural edge. Head-to-head history favors goal-heavy matches, with six of the last seven direct meetings clearing 2.5 goals at an average of 3.6 goals per game, and the BTTS market is priced around 56 percent for that reason.

The winner heads to the Berlin Olympiastadion for the DFB-Pokal final on May 23. The loser of this semifinal is either a Europa League semi-dependent Freiburg or a Pokal-defending Stuttgart that has to refocus on a top-three Bundesliga finish. The market reads Stuttgart as the heavy favorite because of home advantage, the Jeltsch-free defense notwithstanding, and the over on 2.5 total goals at roughly 1.60 decimal odds has been the sharpest angle highlighted by early German betting publications.

ESPN+

Levante vs Sevilla

Thursday, 1:00 PM ET | Estadi Ciutat de Valencia, Valencia, Spain

La Liga midweek matchday 33 opens with Levante hosting Sevilla at the Estadi Ciutat de Valencia. The market has this priced as a genuine three-way coin flip, with Levante at plus-150, the draw at plus-225, and Sevilla at plus-188 on the three-way moneyline. Implied probabilities land at Sevilla 39 percent, Levante 33 percent, and draw 28 percent, with the over 2.5 goals market priced around 1.58 given both sides' defensive struggles across recent matches.

Sevilla arrive on a two-match La Liga road losing streak, a run that has pushed them out of the European-qualifying discussion and into the mid-table defensive scramble. Jose Luis Mendilibar's squad has underperformed its expected goals numbers at home but has collapsed on the road, with set-piece concessions and pressing lapses being the recurring issues. Levante won their most recent home match 1-0 against Getafe and have been steady at the Ciutat de Valencia across the season, collecting points against teams above them on the table in the second half of the campaign.

The combination of Sevilla's shaky road form and Levante's recent home defensive solidity is the reason the draw is priced as long as it is. If Sevilla fail to concede first, they can slow the game into a possession grind, but any early Levante goal shifts the match's control toward the home side and produces a result that would keep the Andalusians in the relegation-adjacent conversation through the final five fixtures. The over 2.5 is the market's lean because both sides concede, but the under has value if Sevilla's defensive structure holds in the first 30 minutes.

Rayo Vallecano vs Espanyol

Thursday, 2:00 PM ET | Campo de Futbol de Vallecas, Madrid, Spain

Rayo Vallecano host Espanyol at the Campo de Futbol de Vallecas with the home side pushing for European football qualification. The market prices Rayo as slight favorites at plus-106 with Espanyol catching plus-128 as the visiting underdog. The draw market sits around plus-225. Model probabilities have Rayo in the 43 to 48 percent range, Espanyol between 26 and 29 percent, and the draw consensus around 26 percent.

The Vallecas atmosphere is one of La Liga's most difficult for away sides. Rayo's home record has produced the second-highest home points total in the bottom half of the table, and Inigo Perez has built a press-and-counter template that the Ciutat-type visiting sides struggle to handle. Espanyol arrive fighting for survival, and Manolo Gonzalez has leaned defensive across the last three matches, producing low-xG games that the Barcelona-based side has either drawn or lost by a single goal.

The under 2.5 total is the sharper lean in this match. Rayo's home matches have produced a league-leading defensive profile on expected goals against, and Espanyol's current approach is to sit deep and concede possession. If the match stays scoreless through 60 minutes, a late Rayo goal produces a 1-0 home win and the under cashes. If Espanyol find an away goal and Rayo chase a full three points, the total climbs and the over value emerges late.

Real Oviedo vs Villarreal

Thursday, 3:30 PM ET | Estadio Carlos Tartiere, Oviedo, Spain

Real Oviedo host Villarreal at the Estadio Carlos Tartiere with the Yellow Submarine pushing for a Champions League place. Villarreal are priced at plus-110 with Oviedo catching plus-270 as the home underdog, a price that reflects Villarreal's positional edge and Oviedo's promotion-year struggle in the Primera. Model probabilities have Villarreal at 46.2 percent, the draw at 25.5 percent, and Oviedo at 28.3 percent, though the market on some books moves Oviedo to +125 and Villarreal to -145 on the two-way equivalent.

Villarreal's season has been built on expected-goal dominance. Marcelino Garcia Toral's squad has underperformed across the run-in, but the underlying numbers still point to a top-four Champions League qualifying profile. Gerard Moreno and Alex Baena are the primary attacking threats, and the Yellow Submarine's midfield ball-progression is the structural edge against a newly-promoted Oviedo side that has been overmatched against top-half La Liga opposition.

Oviedo's home form has kept them in the relegation fight. The Carlos Tartiere is a difficult venue for away sides, particularly in midweek fixtures, and Oviedo's defensive structure at home has been more solid than their table position suggests. The total sits around 2.5 goals with both the over and under priced near even money. Villarreal's away xG across the last five matches suggests an over lean, but Oviedo's defensive home profile complicates the projection.

PSV Eindhoven vs PEC Zwolle

Thursday, 3:00 PM ET | Philips Stadion, Eindhoven, Netherlands

Newly-crowned Eredivisie champions PSV Eindhoven host PEC Zwolle at Philips Stadion in a match with no league table implications for the home side but plenty of goals potential. PSV sit first on 74 points with the title already secured, and Zwolle sit 13th at 40 points back. The home moneyline price is around 1.17 implied at roughly 85 percent, with Zwolle catching 12.50 as the visiting underdog. The draw price was not cleanly surfaced in early markets.

The PSV goals-per-game average at home exceeds three across the season. Peter Bosz's squad has continued pressing and finishing even after clinching the title, and Zwolle's road form is abysmal across the campaign with a 14-match winless streak and 2.73 goals conceded per game on the road. The two-goal Asian handicap on PSV is the market's primary angle, with the over on total goals priced at a short number reflecting the talent gap.

Luuk de Jong's continued production in front of goal and Joey Veerman's creative midfield work will be the primary attacking threats. Zwolle's 60 goals conceded across the campaign is the league-worst mark, and any PSV press triggers off goal kicks or short-goal-kick build-ups produces the kind of quick transitions the Eindhoven attack thrives on. The over 3.5 goals market priced near 2.29 decimal is the value angle, with PSV at home against the league's worst defensive side producing a result that trends toward 4-0 or 5-1 as the median outcome.

Go Ahead Eagles vs AZ Alkmaar

Thursday, 12:45 PM ET | De Adelaarshorst, Deventer, Netherlands

Go Ahead Eagles host AZ Alkmaar at De Adelaarshorst in a match with European qualification implications for both sides. AZ is priced as the 2.17 to 2.23 decimal favorite at roughly 45 percent implied probability, with Go Ahead Eagles catching 2.88 as the home underdog and the draw unclear in early markets. Both teams are in the Conference League and Europa League qualifying conversation, which raises the stakes beyond the final table positions.

The match trends heavily toward goals. The BTTS market prices Yes at 1.37 decimal, an extremely short number that reflects both sides' attacking tendencies and defensive vulnerabilities. The over 3.5 goals market sits around 2.29 decimal, and the 2-2 or 3-2 scoreline profile is the market's median outcome. De Adelaarshorst is one of the Eredivisie's most intimate venues and produces a home-crowd factor that helps Go Ahead Eagles keep pace with larger clubs.

AZ's season has been built on attacking depth. Troy Parrott and Ruben van Bommel have produced consistent goal output. Go Ahead Eagles counter with Mats Deijl's runs from fullback and the central midfield pairing that has been the backbone of the club's overachievement. The match is expected to be open, goal-filled, and carry late-swing variance, and the fan-expectation for a Conference League-qualification statement match produces the kind of high-energy opening 30 minutes that sets up the chance-heavy contest the market is already pricing.

Casa Pia vs SC Braga

Thursday, 3:15 PM ET | Estadio Municipal de Rio Maior, Rio Maior, Portugal

SC Braga travel to the Estadio Municipal de Rio Maior to face Casa Pia in a match with Primeira Liga top-three implications for the Arsenalistas. Braga are priced at minus-192 with Casa Pia at plus-500 and the draw at plus-128. The decimal prices of 1.60 to 1.65 on Braga, 4.00 on the draw, and 5.25 on Casa Pia reflect a heavy market read on the talent gap and the Casa Pia venue uncertainty.

Casa Pia are playing at their backup venue in Rio Maior rather than the home Estadio Pina Manique, a factor that historically costs them home-field advantage. Their relegation battle is the defining storyline of the season, and the defensive structure at the Rio Maior venue has been porous against attacking Primeira Liga opponents. Braga's rotation risk is real, given the Europa League semifinal first leg against Freiburg on April 30, but Daniel Sousa has prioritized the top-three league position across recent weeks.

The BTTS No market priced at 1.86 decimal is the value angle for those projecting a controlled Braga win. The Arsenalistas' defensive structure under Sousa has been solid on the road, and Casa Pia's finishing rate against top-half Primeira Liga sides has been below 0.8 expected goals per match. A 1-0 or 2-0 Braga win is the median outcome, with the Casa Pia goal threat limited to set pieces and counter-attack moments that produce sub-0.1 expected goals per shot.
