9-Game Slate - Wednesday, December 03, 2025

Wednesday, December 03, 2025

@

Detroit Pistons (17-4) @ Milwaukee Bucks (9-13)

8:00 PM ET Fiserv Forum NBA TV

Listen, if you told me before the season that the Pistons would have the best record in the Eastern Conference in December, I'd have asked what you were smoking. But here we are, and Detroit is absolutely rolling at 17-4. This isn't a fluke, folks. Cade Cunningham has transformed into a legitimate MVP candidate, averaging 28.2 points, 6.5 rebounds, and 9.3 assists per game. The kid just won Eastern Conference Player of the Month, and he's playing with a swagger we haven't seen from a Piston since the Bad Boys era.

The Pistons matched a franchise-best 13-game winning streak earlier this season, tying the legendary 1989-90 and 2003-04 championship teams. That's not coincidence, that's culture. J.B. Bickerstaff has this team playing connected basketball, defending at a high level, and finding ways to win close games. Detroit won 15 of their last 17 heading into this matchup, including a nail-biter 99-98 victory over Atlanta where Cade dropped 18 points with five steals and three blocks.

Now let's talk about the Bucks. Milwaukee is 9-13 and struggling to find consistency. Giannis Antetokounmpo is probable tonight with an adductor issue, averaging 30.6 points and 10.7 rebounds when healthy, but this team is 1-5 in games he's missed. Even with Giannis in the lineup, they're just 8-8. That's not championship-level basketball, and the Bucks know it.

Here's what makes this matchup fascinating: the Pistons are playing like they belong among the elite, while the Bucks are searching for their identity. Milwaukee's home court advantage at Fiserv Forum is real, but Detroit has won seven straight road games and isn't intimidated by anyone right now. Cunningham versus Giannis is a marquee individual matchup, but the Pistons have the better supporting cast and the better team chemistry.

I'm taking Detroit here. The Pistons have earned every bit of respect they're getting, and the Bucks haven't shown me they can stop a team playing this well. Cunningham is going to get his buckets, and Detroit's defense is good enough to make Giannis work for everything. Give me the Pistons to cover, and don't be shocked if they win outright in Milwaukee.

@

Denver Nuggets (14-6) @ Indiana Pacers (4-17)

7:00 PM ET Gainbridge Fieldhouse

Nikola Jokic is doing things we haven't seen since Wilt Chamberlain, and that's not hyperbole. The big man is leading the NBA in both rebounds per game and assists per game, putting him on pace to become the first player to lead both categories in the same season since Chamberlain did it in 1967-68. He's averaging 29 points, 12.8 rebounds, and 11.1 assists while shooting 62.1 percent from the field. Jokic is listed as probable with a minor wrist issue, but there's no way he's missing this one.

The Nuggets are 14-6 and riding a seven-game road winning streak. That's absurd. Denver's been inconsistent at home, dropping four straight at Ball Arena to teams with losing records, but on the road they're absolutely locked in. When they played the Pacers earlier this season on November 8th, Jokic had 32 points, 14 rebounds, and 14 assists in a dominant 117-100 victory. Expect more of the same tonight.

Indiana's season has been a complete disaster. At 4-17, they're one of the worst teams in basketball, and it all traces back to one devastating injury. Tyrese Haliburton tore his Achilles in Game 7 of the NBA Finals back in June, and he's out for the entire season. That's their point guard, their engine, their everything. The Pacers are now leaning on Pascal Siakam (24.1 PPG), Bennedict Mathurin (21.1 PPG), and Andrew Nembhard (17.5 PPG), but they simply don't have the playmaking to compete with elite teams.

This is a mismatch in every sense of the word. Jokic against Indiana's interior defense is going to be a clinic. The Pacers don't have anyone who can slow him down, contain his passing, or match his basketball IQ. Denver's going to run their offense through Jokic, and he's going to pick them apart possession by possession.

Take the Nuggets and whatever spread Vegas gives you. This is Jokic against a team missing its best player, and the result shouldn't be close. Denver covers comfortably.

@

San Antonio Spurs (13-6) @ Orlando Magic (12-8)

7:00 PM ET Kia Center

This game is fascinating because both teams are dealing with significant injury situations. Victor Wembanyama is out for the Spurs with a strained left calf, and Paolo Banchero has missed eight straight games for the Magic with a groin injury. When these two franchises had their stars healthy, they were among the most exciting young teams in basketball. Tonight, it's about who can step up in their absence.

San Antonio started the season on an absolute tear, going 5-0 for the best start in franchise history. Wembanyama was otherworldly, putting up 40 points in his season debut and posting historic stat lines night after night. Even without him, the Spurs are 13-6 and playing disciplined basketball. They've got enough depth to compete, but losing a generational talent like Wemby changes everything about how they play.

The Magic are 12-8 and sitting seventh in the East, but here's the interesting part: they're actually 5-2 without Banchero this season compared to 6-6 with him. That's not a knock on Paolo, it's a testament to Franz Wagner stepping up as the lead guy. Wagner's been exceptional, and his EuroBasket-style team basketball fits perfectly with Orlando's system. The Magic's defense remains elite, and they've got the depth to weather injuries better than most.

This is a coin-flip game that comes down to coaching and execution. Both teams play the right way, both teams defend, and both teams have young cores that believe in what they're building. The Magic have home court advantage at the Kia Center, and that might be the deciding factor.

I'm leaning Orlando here. The Magic's defensive identity translates regardless of who's on the floor, and they're more comfortable playing without their star than San Antonio is. Take the Magic at home.

@

Portland Trail Blazers @ Cleveland Cavaliers (13-9)

7:00 PM ET Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse

Donovan Mitchell is absolutely scorching right now. The Cavaliers' guard is averaging 30.6 points per game this season, and he just went off for 43 points in a 135-119 demolition of the Pacers to snap Cleveland's three-game losing streak. This is the third time this season Mitchell has scored 40-plus, and when he's in this kind of zone, he's as dangerous as any guard in the league.

Cleveland's at 13-9 and sitting in a strong position in the Eastern Conference. They've got the firepower to beat anyone when Mitchell's cooking, and Jaylon Tyson has emerged as a legitimate second option, dropping 27 points with 11 rebounds against Indiana. The Cavaliers are building chemistry, and this feels like a team that's just getting started.

Portland's in a tough spot. They traded Anfernee Simons to Boston in July, and this team is still figuring out who they are without their best perimeter scorer. The Trail Blazers have struggled on the road all season, and walking into Cleveland against a motivated Mitchell is a brutal draw. Portland's young guys are talented, but they're not ready to compete with elite guards in hostile environments.

This should be a comfortable Cleveland victory. Mitchell's going to get his points, the Cavaliers' supporting cast is playing with confidence, and Portland doesn't have the defensive personnel to slow them down. Take the Cavaliers to cover at home.

@

LA Clippers (5-16) @ Atlanta Hawks (13-9)

7:30 PM ET State Farm Arena

The Clippers are a complete mess. At 5-16, they're tied for the worst start since 2010-11, and the problems are everywhere. James Harden is still putting up numbers, averaging 26.9 points and 8.7 assists, and he even dropped a franchise-record 55 points against the Hornets. But one game doesn't fix systemic issues. Kawhi Leonard is back from his ankle injury, but the team was outscored by 39 points with Harden on the floor in their recent loss to Miami. That's not a typo. Minus-39.

Atlanta's in a much better place despite losing Trae Young to an MCL sprain back in late October. The Hawks are 13-9 and have responded beautifully to Young's absence, with Jalen Johnson emerging as one of the most complete young forwards in basketball. Johnson's production has been wild, and the supporting cast of Dyson Daniels and Nickeil Alexander-Walker has stepped up in the backcourt. Young won't be re-evaluated until mid-December, but this team has learned to win without him.

Here's the thing about the Clippers: they have no chemistry. Harden and Kawhi haven't figured out how to co-exist, the defense is inconsistent, and the supporting cast changes night to night. Meanwhile, Atlanta plays with energy, defends with effort, and has a clear identity even without their All-Star point guard.

I'm taking the Hawks at home. They're playing motivated basketball, they're healthier than they've been, and the Clippers have given me no reason to trust them. Atlanta covers.

@

Charlotte Hornets @ New York Knicks (13-7)

7:30 PM ET Madison Square Garden

The Hornets are in freefall. They're on a season-long seven-game losing streak, LaMelo Ball is struggling badly since returning from his ankle impingement injury, and there are serious trade rumors swirling around the franchise point guard. Ball is averaging just 16.6 points on 32.5 percent shooting in his five games back, including a brutal 8-for-38 from three-point range. He's probable tonight with a wrist issue, but even when he plays, he hasn't been himself.

Charlotte is also without Brandon Miller and Tre Mann tonight, which further depletes their already thin roster. This is a team in crisis, and walking into Madison Square Garden against a surging Knicks squad is about the worst possible matchup they could face.

New York is 13-7 and Jalen Brunson is playing like a top-ten player in the league. He's averaging 28.5 points per game, just won Eastern Conference Player of the Week, and dropped 47 points in an NBA Cup-clinching win over the Bucks. Brunson's consistency is remarkable, and when he's at MSG, the energy is electric. The Knicks play their best basketball at home, and they're going to feast on a struggling Hornets team.

This is one of the easier calls on the board. The Knicks are rolling, the Hornets are falling apart, and MSG is going to be rocking. Take New York to cover by double digits.

Brooklyn Nets @ Chicago Bulls (9-12)

8:00 PM ET United Center

This is the definition of a tank battle. The Nets are sitting around 3-14 and have been absolutely brutal without Cam Thomas, who's missed 12 straight games with a hamstring injury. Brooklyn's defense has been historically bad at times, and without their primary scorer, they can't generate consistent offense. Thomas is getting an MRI later this week, so there's no relief coming anytime soon.

Chicago's at 9-12 and going through their own identity crisis. They traded Zach LaVine to Sacramento back in February, and this roster is still figuring out what it's supposed to be. The Bulls have some young pieces, but they lack a go-to scorer who can take over games. This is a team that plays hard but doesn't have enough talent to compete with playoff-caliber squads.

Here's the angle: the Bulls are at home, they're slightly better than the Nets right now, and they need wins to maintain any playoff hopes. Brooklyn has no motivation to win games, they're playing without their best player, and they've been getting blown out regularly. Chicago should be able to take care of business at the United Center.

I'm taking the Bulls at home. Neither team is good, but Chicago's marginally better and has home court. Bulls cover.

@

Sacramento Kings @ Houston Rockets

8:00 PM ET Toyota Center

The Kings have had a nightmare season. They won just three of their first 16 games, De'Aaron Fox was traded to San Antonio, and now Domantas Sabonis is out with a partially torn meniscus until mid-December. They've added Zach LaVine and DeMar DeRozan, but the chemistry isn't there yet. LaVine's been scoring well, averaging 20.7 points, but Sacramento simply cannot figure out how to win games.

Houston is a completely different story. Kevin Durant came over in that massive seven-team trade back in July, and he's been exactly what the Rockets needed. KD is averaging 25.1 points per game, has scored 20-plus in eight of his last nine, and just passed Elvin Hayes for 11th place on the all-time scoring list. When Durant is on the floor, the Rockets have a legitimate go-to option in crunch time.

This is a massive mismatch. Houston's at home, they've got Kevin Durant cooking, and the Kings are without their best player in Sabonis. Sacramento doesn't have the interior presence to compete with Houston's frontcourt, and their guards are going to struggle against the Rockets' length. This should be a comfortable Houston win.

Take the Rockets at home. Durant's going to dominate, and Sacramento doesn't have the personnel to stop him.

@

Miami Heat @ Dallas Mavericks (7-15)

8:30 PM ET American Airlines Center NBA TV

The Mavericks' season has been a rollercoaster. They traded Luka Doncic to the Lakers back in February for Anthony Davis, and the transition hasn't been smooth. Dallas is 7-15, Kyrie Irving is still recovering from a torn ACL and won't return until January, and the team is searching for consistency without their two All-Stars in the lineup together. Davis has been solid when healthy, playing with a minutes restriction, but it's not enough to carry this team.

Miami's got Bam Adebayo leading the way, and he's been phenomenal. Bam just put up 27 points with five threes, 14 rebounds, four steals, and four blocks against the Clippers. Tyler Herro is providing consistent scoring, Andrew Wiggins has fit in nicely since joining the team, and the Heat are playing their typical brand of tough, physical basketball.

Here's the reality: Dallas is undermanned and struggling. They're relying on role players to carry offensive load, and while Anthony Davis can dominate when he's engaged, he's on a minutes restriction. Miami's depth, defense, and experience give them a significant edge in this matchup.

I'm taking Miami on the road. The Heat are the more complete team, they've got the better coaching, and Dallas doesn't have enough firepower to hang for forty-eight minutes. Heat cover.
