Pelicans @ Pacers

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Gainbridge Fieldhouse
Spread: IND -4.5 | ML: NO +155 / IND -185 | O/U: 238.5

Indiana continues to be one of the league's most entertaining teams, playing at a breakneck pace that creates high-scoring affairs. The Pacers' offensive system under Rick Carlisle is a thing of beauty when it's clicking, and they've got the personnel to push the tempo against anyone. Tyrese Haliburton missed the 2025 Finals with an Achilles injury but has been healthy this season, orchestrating one of the league's most dynamic offenses.

New Orleans has dealt with significant injury issues this season, which has hampered their ability to find consistency. When healthy, the Pelicans have All-Star caliber talent, but staying on the court has been the challenge. Their pace-and-space offense should match up well with Indiana's style, but the Pacers' home-court advantage in high-scoring games is significant.

The 238.5 total is one of the highest on the board tonight, and for good reason. Indiana's offensive rating at home has been elite, and they don't slow down for anyone. New Orleans will try to keep pace, but the Pacers' conditioning and depth should wear them down over 48 minutes. The model shows value on Indiana at -155, supported by a 2.8% edge in win probability.

Advanced Trends: The under has been the play in Pelicans road games, hitting 54.9% of the time against this total. Indiana is 12-5 at home this season. The Pacers cover the spread 58% of the time as home favorites of 4 or more points. The over has hit in 6 of Indiana's last 8 home games with a total of 235 or higher.

Cavaliers @ 76ers

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Xfinity Mobile Arena | ESPN
Spread: PHI -2 | ML: CLE +110 / PHI -130 | O/U: 234.5

This is the rematch everybody's been waiting for. Two days ago, Donovan Mitchell dropped 35 points on the 76ers' heads in a dominant 133-107 Cleveland victory. Twenty of Mitchell's points came in the third quarter alone, a stretch where he completely took over the game. Mitchell was named Eastern Conference Player of the Week on Monday, and he's been scorching in January, averaging 30.3 points, 6.0 assists, 5.5 rebounds, and 3.5 threes per game through four January outings. The Cavs are now 9-1 when he puts up 35 or more.

Philadelphia (22-17) is licking their wounds and looking for revenge on their home floor. Tyrese Maxey has been incredible this season, ranking third in the league in scoring at 30.5 PPG and having arguably the best season of his career. The question for the Sixers is health, as keeping Joel Embiid and Paul George on the court together has been a challenge. Rookie V.J. Edgecombe has been one of the team's consistent contributors. Philly's two losses in January were both one-point overtime defeats to Denver and Toronto.

Cleveland (23-19) doesn't go on extended winning streaks but also doesn't lose more than three in a row. They've played better defense over the last two weeks after a step back in points allowed earlier. This game features two of the league's top five scorers: Mitchell (4th, 29.7 PPG) and Maxey (3rd, 30.5 PPG). The Cavaliers are 21-17-1 ATS this season while the Sixers are 15-27 ATS. The model slightly favors Cleveland to win outright at 51.2%.

Advanced Trends: Mitchell has scored 30+ in four of six January games and topped multiple threes in every outing this month. The Cavaliers are 5-2 ATS in their last 7 games against teams with winning records. Philadelphia is 2-5 ATS in their last 7 home games. The total has gone over in 5 of the last 6 meetings between these teams. Cleveland is 8-4 on the road against Eastern Conference opponents.

Bulls @ Nets

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center
Spread: BKN -2.5 | ML: CHI +125 / BKN -145 | O/U: 225.5

Two teams in transition square off at Barclays Center in a game that won't exactly move the needle nationally. Brooklyn's rebuild is in full swing, and they're banking on their young core to develop into something special. The Nets have been competitive at home, playing hard and giving fans glimpses of a brighter future. Nic Claxton has been a defensive anchor, and the modeling projects him for 1.0 steals tonight.

Chicago is stuck in NBA purgatory, not good enough to compete for a championship but not bad enough to tank for a top draft pick. The Bulls have some talented pieces but lack the star power to break through in the Eastern Conference. Their defensive metrics have been inconsistent, and they struggle to generate stops against teams that can create their own offense.

The 225.5 total is the lowest on tonight's slate, reflecting both teams' struggles to score efficiently. Brooklyn at -2.5 at home seems fair, though the Bulls are capable of stealing games against rebuilding teams. This could go either way, but expect a grind-it-out affair with possessions mattering more than usual in a low-scoring environment.

Advanced Trends: The under has hit in 5 of the last 7 Bulls road games. Brooklyn is 10-8 at home this season but just 4-7 ATS as home favorites. Chicago covers the spread 52% of the time as road underdogs of 3 or fewer points. The total has stayed under in 4 of the last 5 meetings between these franchises.

Clippers @ Raptors

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena
Spread: LAC -2.5 | ML: LAC -125 / TOR +105 | O/U: 215.5

Here's a matchup that defies the standings. The LA Clippers (17-23) are road favorites despite having a losing record, and they've earned that price with a four-game winning streak that has breathed life back into their season. When the Clippers are clicking, they're capable of beating anyone, and their recent form suggests they might be finding their identity. A fifth straight win would be a major statement.

Toronto (25-17) has been one of the pleasant surprises of the season, building a winning record despite questions about their roster construction entering the year. The Raptors play hard every night at Scotiabank Arena, and their young core has shown significant development. The model actually favors Toronto to win outright at 60.4%, suggesting the home team is undervalued here.

The 215.5 total is notably low for two teams capable of scoring. LA's defensive improvement during their winning streak partially explains the number, but Toronto has shown they can put up points in bunches. The Clippers at -125 with a 58.4% probability is the model's lean, but the Raptors at +105 offers intriguing value given their home record and overall form this season.

Advanced Trends: The Clippers have covered in 4 straight games. Toronto is 14-7 at home this season and 10-4 ATS at Scotiabank Arena. The under has hit in 4 of the last 5 Clippers road games. LA is 2-6 in their last 8 games against teams with winning records. The road team has covered in 5 of the last 7 meetings between these franchises.

Timberwolves @ Rockets

Friday, 9:30 PM ET | Toyota Center
Spread: HOU -3 | ML: MIN +130 / HOU -150 | O/U: 226.5

Kevin Durant continues to write history in Houston. On January 9th, he swished a three-pointer to pass Wilt Chamberlain for 7th on the NBA's all-time scoring list with 31,435 career points. Durant has been sensational in January, averaging 28.7 points, 6.7 rebounds, and 5.3 assists through six games on those absurd 52-40-89 shooting splits for the full season. For context, 52% from the field, 40% from three, and 89% from the line at his volume is generational efficiency.

The Rockets have hit a wall to start 2026, though. After closing 2025 with three straight wins, Houston has dropped three of their last five, including back-to-back losses in Portland. With Fred VanVleet out for the vast majority of the season with a torn ACL, Durant is absorbing even more usage and ball-handling duties. That's a lot to ask of a 37-year-old, even one as elite as Durant.

Minnesota's Anthony Edwards is one of just five players in January logging over 200 minutes while taking 32% of his team's shots when on the court. Edwards has managed the scoring load brilliantly, but the Timberwolves can't afford to burn him out in the second half. This is a fascinating clash of styles, with Houston's floor-spacing around Durant meeting Minnesota's defensive length. The 226.5 total suggests a moderate pace, which favors the Wolves.

Advanced Trends: Houston is 13-6 at home this season but just 6-8 ATS as home favorites. Minnesota has covered in 4 of their last 6 road games against Western Conference opponents. The over has hit in 5 of the last 7 meetings between these teams. Durant has scored 25+ in 9 consecutive games. Edwards has topped 30 points in 4 of his last 7 road games.

Wizards @ Kings

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | Golden 1 Center
Spread: SAC -6.5 | ML: WSH +220 / SAC -275 | O/U: 232.5

The late-night showcase brings us two of the league's most disappointing teams. Washington (10-29) is on a four-game losing streak and firmly in tank mode, playing for draft positioning rather than wins. The Wizards have some young pieces to develop, but this season is about securing a premium pick to accelerate the rebuild. They're the worst team in the Eastern Conference by a significant margin.

Sacramento (11-30) might be even more puzzling. After making the playoffs in 2023 and taking a step forward, the Kings have completely collapsed this season. Their 11-30 record is stunning given the talent on the roster, and questions about coaching, chemistry, and effort have swirled around the franchise all year. They should be much better than their record indicates, which makes them dangerous against fellow basement dwellers.

The Kings at -6.5 seems like a lot for a team with 11 wins, but the model gives Sacramento a 61.8% chance to win outright. Washington simply doesn't have the talent or motivation to compete with teams trying to salvage their season. The 232.5 total reflects both teams' defensive deficiencies. Sacramento should cover comfortably, but the final score could be ugly either way.

Advanced Trends: Washington is 2-8 ATS in their last 10 road games. Sacramento is 5-3 ATS in their last 8 home games against losing teams. The over has hit in 6 of the last 8 Wizards road games. The home team has won 7 of the last 9 meetings between these franchises. Kings games have averaged 235.2 total points over their last 10 home contests.
