Knicks @ Nets

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center, Brooklyn, NY
Spread
NYK -18.5
Moneyline
NYK -1567 / BKN +1329
Total
O/U 214.5

There's something deeply satisfying about a New York rivalry game even when the gap between the two teams is a canyon. The Knicks at 45-25 are riding a four-game win streak after demolishing the Pacers 136-110, and they come into Barclays Center playing some of their best basketball of the season. Jalen Brunson continues to be the heartbeat of this team, averaging 26.3 points per game on 48.1% shooting from the field and 38.8% from three. He's been remarkably efficient, and when Brunson is cooking, the Knicks' offense has a ruthless quality to it that very few teams in the East can match. Mitchell Robinson's 4.4 offensive rebounds per game rank among the league leaders, giving New York those precious second-chance opportunities that turn good offensive possessions into great ones.

Brooklyn at 17-52 is living a different reality entirely. The Nets have dropped 10 straight to the Knicks in this series, and there's nothing about this roster that suggests that streak is in danger. They're averaging just 106.4 points per game, the lowest-scoring offense in the entire NBA, and over their last 10 games, Brooklyn has gone 2-8 while managing only 102.4 points per outing. The injury list tells the story of a franchise in full rebuild mode: Day'Ron Sharpe is done for the season with a thumb injury, Egor Demin is out for the year with a foot problem, and Michael Porter Jr. remains day-to-day with an ankle issue. There's simply no player on this roster who can match up with Brunson's ability to get to his spots and create offense at will.

The 18.5-point spread is massive, and the -1567 moneyline essentially treats this like a scrimmage for the Knicks. But here's the interesting wrinkle: Brunson is listed as day-to-day with an ankle issue of his own. If he sits, the spread becomes a lot more interesting, though the Knicks' depth is good enough that they should still roll. New York is 7-3 over their last 10 games, averaging 116.3 points, and their fourth-ranked 12.9 offensive rebounds per game means they dominate the glass even when the shooting isn't falling. The Knicks' ability to punish teams on the boards is what makes these blowout spreads coverable, because they generate extra possessions that pile up the points.

The 214.5 total is the lowest on Friday's board, and it reflects the expectation that Brooklyn's anemic offense will keep this game from turning into a shootout. The Nets don't have the shot creation or the pace to push into the 110s against a quality defense, and the Knicks may well pull their starters in the fourth quarter if this goes sideways early. The real question isn't whether New York wins, it's whether they keep their foot on the gas long enough to cover a number that's approaching college football territory. In a rivalry game at Barclays, the Knicks' starters should want to make a statement before heading to the bench.

Warriors @ Pistons

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Spread
DET -8.5
Moneyline
DET -335 / GSW +300
Total
O/U 223

If you told anyone two years ago that the Detroit Pistons would be sitting at 49-19 and first in the Eastern Conference while the Warriors limped in at 33-36, you'd have been committed. But that's the reality of the 2025-26 season, and Detroit's rise has been one of the most thrilling stories in basketball. Cade Cunningham has been an MVP candidate this season, averaging 24.5 points and a staggering 9.9 assists per game, but the big concern heading into Friday is his health. Cunningham was diagnosed with a mild collapsed lung after leaving the Wizards game on March 17 and will be re-evaluated in two weeks. That's a significant blow for a team that was cruising toward the top seed, and it changes the complexion of this game entirely.

Golden State at 33-36 is in freefall, having lost eight of their last 10 games, and the Warriors looked lifeless in Wednesday's 120-99 loss to the Celtics. The team shot 40.0% from the field and a putrid 23.3% from three, and without Stephen Curry (knee) and Jimmy Butler (torn ACL, season-ending), there's simply no offensive engine driving this team forward. Brandin Podziemski and the young core are getting their developmental minutes, but this is a roster playing out the string with nothing to play for. The Warriors have essentially conceded this season, and the lack of urgency shows on both ends of the floor.

Even without Cunningham, Detroit won the first meeting 131-124, and the Pistons have the roster depth to handle a depleted Golden State team. Detroit's defense has been suffocating all season, and their identity doesn't change just because Cunningham is out. The question is how much of the offensive load the supporting cast can shoulder. The 8.5-point spread is reasonable if Cunningham sits, because the Warriors are bad enough that Detroit's depth advantage should still be decisive, but it would be a much larger number with Cunningham on the floor. The Pistons' home record at Little Caesars Arena has been dominant this season, and the crowd energy alone should carry Detroit through any early offensive struggles.

The 223 total sits in the middle of Friday's board, and it's tricky to project without knowing Cunningham's status definitively. If he's out, Detroit's offense loses its primary creator, and the pace should slow as the supporting cast runs more deliberate half-court sets. Golden State's offense has been anemic recently, and their inability to generate clean looks from three-point range limits their scoring ceiling. This has the feel of a game where Detroit wins comfortably but perhaps not spectacularly, with the Pistons' defense keeping Golden State in the low 100s while the home team does just enough offensively to cover.

Hawks @ Rockets

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -4.5
Moneyline
HOU -164 / ATL +156
Total
O/U 226.5

This is the game of the night, and it's not close. The Atlanta Hawks roll into Toyota Center riding an 11-game win streak that has catapulted them from the fringes of the play-in picture to a tie for seventh in the Eastern Conference at 38-31. Over those 11 wins, Atlanta has been absolutely electric, averaging 124.9 points while shooting 49.0% from the field with 31.3 assists per game. Those aren't just good numbers, they're historically good for a team on a run like this. Jalen Johnson has been the engine, posting 22.9 points, 10.5 rebounds, and 8.1 assists per game, and Jonathan Kuminga hit a 75-foot buzzer-beater in the win over Dallas that perfectly encapsulates the kind of magic this team is generating right now.

Houston at 41-27 desperately needs a win here after going 4-6 over their last 10 games, including getting swept at home by the Lakers. Kevin Durant's 25.7 points per game have been characteristically smooth, and Amen Thompson's development into a legitimate two-way force (17.9 PPG, 7.8 RPG) has been one of the season's best individual stories. But the Rockets miss Fred VanVleet badly. His torn ACL removed their defensive anchor and most reliable backcourt decision-maker, and Houston hasn't been the same team since. In the first meeting back in January, Durant went for 31 points and the Rockets cruised 104-86, but that was a completely different Hawks team than the juggernaut riding into Houston right now.

The 4.5-point spread feels like it might be undervaluing what Atlanta is doing right now. The Hawks haven't just been winning, they've been dismantling teams with a level of ball movement and defensive intensity that's genuinely surprising. CJ McCollum's 24-point effort in the Dallas win showed the kind of depth scoring Atlanta can generate, and Dyson Daniels has been a two-way revelation throughout the streak. But there's a reason Houston is still favored: home court matters, Durant is Durant, and the Rockets' defense at Toyota Center has been stingy enough to slow down good offenses all season. This is the kind of game where we find out if Atlanta's streak is the real deal or if it's been built against lesser competition.

The 226.5 total is the juiciest on the board, and it should deliver entertainment. Atlanta's playing at a pace and offensive efficiency that makes them one of the most fun teams in basketball right now, and Houston has enough offensive talent with Durant and Thompson to keep up. The Rockets' defense will be tested in ways it hasn't been over the past few weeks, because Atlanta's ball movement creates open shots at a rate that's difficult for even elite defenses to contain. Expect possessions to matter, expect big shots from Durant and Johnson, and expect the kind of atmosphere at Toyota Center that makes Friday night NBA basketball appointment viewing on NBA TV.

Celtics @ Grizzlies

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | FedExForum, Memphis, TN
Spread
BOS -15
Moneyline
BOS -950 / MEM +640
Total
O/U 228

The biggest storyline here isn't the lopsided matchup, it's Jayson Tatum's continued return from an Achilles injury that cost him the first five months of the season. Tatum has played four games since coming back in early March, averaging 19.7 points, 6.7 rebounds, and 3.7 assists while operating on a minutes restriction of around 27 minutes per game. He scored 24 points in Wednesday's win over the Warriors, and each game he looks more like the player who was on an MVP trajectory before the Achilles tear. The Celtics at 46-23 have won three straight and are pushing for the second seed, with Jaylen Brown's 28.2 points per game carrying the load magnificently in Tatum's absence and now serving as a devastating one-two punch alongside the returning star.

Memphis at 24-44 has been gutted by injuries all season, and the losses keep piling up. Ja Morant is out with left elbow discomfort and won't be re-evaluated for two weeks, Zach Edey is done for the season with an ankle injury, Kentavious Caldwell-Pope is out for the year with a finger issue, and Scotty Pippen Jr.'s season ended with toe surgery. The Grizzlies were blown out 131-95 when these teams met in November, and they don't have the personnel to make this version any more competitive. Memphis is running out young players and hoping for draft lottery luck at this point, and the FedExForum crowd has understandably thinned as the losses have mounted.

The 15-point spread is steep for a road team, but Boston has the kind of roster that justifies these massive numbers against the league's worst teams. With Tatum back alongside Brown, the Celtics have two elite wing scorers who can generate offense in any half-court situation. Derrick White and Payton Pritchard continue to provide high-level complementary scoring, and Boston's defense has the length and versatility to shut down Memphis's limited offensive options. The only concern for Boston is Nikola Vucevic, who's out with a finger injury, which removes some of their interior scoring and rebounding. But against a team as depleted as Memphis, that absence shouldn't matter much.

The 228 total is interesting because Tatum's minutes restriction could limit Boston's scoring ceiling, but Memphis plays at a pace that tends to inflate scoring even in lopsided games. The Grizzlies' young players push the tempo in transition regardless of the score, and Boston is more than happy to run when the opportunities present themselves. This should be a game where the Celtics build a comfortable lead by halftime and Tatum gets to play his 27 minutes in controlled conditions, which is exactly what Boston needs as they gradually ramp up their franchise player for the playoff push.

Trail Blazers @ Timberwolves

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Target Center, Minneapolis, MN
Spread
MIN -2.5
Moneyline
MIN -154 / POR +130
Total
O/U 229.5

This is the tightest spread on Friday's board, and it's a Western Conference play-in race game that carries genuine stakes for both teams. Minnesota at 43-27 holds the sixth spot in the West but is dealing with the massive absence of Anthony Edwards, who's sidelined with right knee inflammation and won't be re-evaluated for one to two weeks. Edwards' 29.5 points per game rank third in the entire league, and losing that kind of production leaves a hole that no amount of depth can fully fill. Julius Randle steps into the primary scoring role with 21.2 points, 6.9 rebounds, and 5.1 assists per game, and he's more than capable of carrying the load for stretches, but he can't replicate the explosive, game-breaking ability that Edwards brings.

Portland at 34-36 is a team on the rise, having won back-to-back games including a convincing 127-119 road win in Indiana where Deni Avdija went off for 32 points and Donovan Clingan added 28. Avdija has been a revelation this season, averaging 24.2 points per game and earning his first All-Star selection. He's a legitimate three-level scorer who can also facilitate with 6.7 assists per game, and his breakout season has transformed Portland from a rebuilding project into a legitimate play-in contender. Clingan's 11.6 rebounds per game rank third in the NBA, giving the Blazers a dominant interior presence on the boards that creates problems for any opponent.

The 2.5-point spread tells you everything you need to know about how Edwards' absence has leveled this matchup. With Edwards healthy, Minnesota would likely be favored by 7-8 points at home. Without him, this is essentially a coin flip. The Timberwolves still have Rudy Gobert anchoring the defense with elite rim protection and rebounding, and Donte DiVincenzo's 3.0 made threes per game provide perimeter scoring. But Portland's combination of Avdija's shot creation and Clingan's dominance on the glass gives them a path to winning this game outright. The Blazers are coming in with momentum and confidence, and they know this is exactly the kind of game they need to win to solidify their play-in positioning.

The 229.5 total is the second-highest on the board, and the pace both teams play at supports a high-scoring affair. Minnesota averages 118.8 points per game, ranking fourth in the league, while Portland puts up 115.2 per game. Without Edwards' defensive intensity, the Timberwolves are more vulnerable on the perimeter, and Avdija should be able to exploit that with his combination of size and shooting. This fourth meeting between the teams this season could easily be the most competitive, and it's a game that matters deeply to both franchises as the regular season enters its final stretch. Target Center will be rocking, but the energy might tilt more anxious than confident without Edwards in the building.

Raptors @ Nuggets

Friday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Spread
DEN -7
Moneyline
DEN -260 / TOR +215
Total
O/U 234.5

The nightcap features the best individual player on the planet operating in the altitude of Ball Arena, and if you enjoy watching basketball artistry, this is your game. Nikola Jokic is averaging 28.6 points, 12.7 rebounds, and 10.5 assists per game, which means he's flirting with a full-season triple-double average for a second time in his career. Jokic's combination of vision, touch, and basketball IQ remains unmatched in the sport, and playing at home where Denver's altitude advantage wears down opposing teams makes the Nuggets especially dangerous. Jamal Murray has been sensational recently, averaging 28.6 points while shooting 46.7% from three over his last seven March appearances, and his pick-and-roll chemistry with Jokic remains the most devastating two-man game in basketball.

Toronto at 39-29 has put together a quietly impressive season, sitting in the projected 4-5 range in the Eastern Conference. Brandon Ingram's 21.9 points per game have provided the go-to scoring punch the Raptors needed, and he's delivered 11 30-point performances this season. Scottie Barnes continues to develop into a versatile two-way force, averaging 19.3 points, 8.3 rebounds, and 5.5 assists per game, and his ability to guard multiple positions gives Toronto a defensive anchor that complements Ingram's offensive creativity. The Raptors lost the first meeting 106-103 on December 31 in a game that went down to the wire, proving they can compete with Denver when things break their way.

The 7-point spread reflects Denver's home-court advantage and the altitude factor that breaks so many visiting teams in the second half. Ball Arena is one of the toughest venues in the NBA for road teams, and the thin air at 5,280 feet takes a toll on legs and lungs that becomes apparent in the fourth quarter. Toronto has the talent to stay competitive through three quarters, but Denver's depth and conditioning advantage at altitude tends to turn close games into comfortable wins in the final 12 minutes. Murray's hot shooting makes the Nuggets' offense particularly lethal right now, because defenses can't load up on Jokic when Murray is hitting nearly half his threes.

The 234.5 total is the highest on Friday's entire board, and for good reason. Denver's offense generates elite looks at an absurd rate thanks to Jokic's passing, and Toronto has enough offensive talent to push the pace and trade buckets. Ingram and Barnes both want to score in transition, and Denver is more than happy to play fast when opponents push the tempo. The first meeting was a grinding 106-103 affair, but that was in Toronto. At Ball Arena, the pace tends to open up, and with Murray shooting like he is right now, the Nuggets' offense has the potential to put up 125+ in their own building. This should be the most entertaining game on Friday's slate for neutral fans who just want to watch beautiful basketball.
