11-Game Slate Determines Knockout Round - Expert Predictions

Friday, November 28, 2025

VS

Dallas Mavericks (5-14) @ Los Angeles Lakers (13-4)

10:00 PM ET Crypto.com Arena LAL -10.5 O/U 230.5 NBA Cup West Group B

This is the game everyone has been waiting for since the blockbuster trade that shook the NBA in February. Luka Doncic returns to face his former team for the first time at Crypto.com Arena, now leading a Lakers squad that looks like legitimate championship contenders. The February trade sent Doncic to LA for Anthony Davis in what many called the most lopsided deal in NBA history. The results have validated those critics. The Lakers sit at 13-4 and are rolling, while Dallas has completely collapsed to 5-14 and already fired GM Nico Harrison.

Luka's Dominance in Purple and Gold

Doncic has been absolutely transcendent in purple and gold, averaging 35.2 points, 9.2 assists, and 8.8 rebounds per game. In his most recent outing against the Clippers, he erupted for 43 points (14-28 FG, 7-12 3PT), 9 rebounds, 13 assists, and 2 steals in what has become routine dominance. He's delivered at least 30 points in four straight games and six of his last nine. The Lakers' star trio of LeBron James, Doncic, and Austin Reaves combined for 99 points in a recent game, showcasing the offensive firepower this team possesses. Doncic is on pace for career highs in scoring efficiency and has already signed a three-year, $165 million extension through 2028.

Dallas in Freefall

Meanwhile, Dallas (5-14) has won just twice as underdogs this season. P.J. Washington leads with 17.0 PPG and 8.4 RPG in his last five games, while rookie Cooper Flagg (the #1 overall pick) is averaging 20.5 PPG on 56.8% shooting since taking over as a starter. The Lakers are riding a four-game home winning streak and look virtually unbeatable at Crypto.com Arena. The spread of -10.5 is steep, but Dallas has been completely outclassed all season. This is a revenge game for Doncic even if he's publicly taken the high road, and expect him to put on a show. The under has hit in five of Dallas's last five games.

VS

Phoenix Suns @ Oklahoma City Thunder (18-1)

8:00 PM ET Paycom Center OKC -14.5 NBA Cup West Group A Jalen Williams Season Debut

The defending NBA champion Oklahoma City Thunder (18-1) welcome back All-NBA guard Jalen Williams for his season debut against a Phoenix Suns team in complete disarray. Williams has been recovering from right wrist surgery after playing through a torn ligament during OKC's championship run last season. Even without him, the Thunder have been historically dominant - their +16.5 point differential through 19 games is the best in NBA history, breaking the record held by the 1971-72 Lakers.

Shai's MVP Campaign

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, the reigning NBA MVP, is averaging 32.2 points, 6.6 assists, and 4.9 rebounds per game on 54.3/41.2/89.8 shooting splits. He's scored at least 20 points in 91 consecutive games - the third-longest streak in NBA history behind only Wilt Chamberlain's two streaks.

Phoenix in Rebuild Mode

Phoenix has dealt with constant roster churn since trading Kevin Durant in the offseason. The blockbuster sent Durant packing, and the franchise hit the reset button with a new coach in Jordan Ott and a roster centered around Devin Booker, Dillon Brooks, and rising young stars. But injuries have derailed any progress. Jalen Green re-injured his hamstring and will miss 4-6 weeks, adding to a list of health concerns that has this team limping through the early season. Booker can't do it alone against OKC's suffocating defense. The 14.5-point spread is massive but justified given the talent disparity.

VS

Cleveland Cavaliers (12-7) @ Atlanta Hawks (11-8)

7:30 PM ET State Farm Arena CLE -5.5 O/U 238.5 NBA Cup East Group A

The Cavaliers visit Atlanta in an NBA Cup East Group A matchup with major implications. Cleveland enters at 12-7 with Donovan Mitchell putting up monster numbers - 30.8 points, 5.8 rebounds, and 5.9 assists per game while shooting 50.3% from the field and 38.3% from deep. Mitchell was named Eastern Conference Player of the Week (Week 5) after averaging 31.8 PPG, 6.3 RPG, and 5.0 APG. Evan Mobley, the reigning NBA Defensive Player of the Year, continues to develop into a two-way force with 18.5 PPG, 9.3 RPG, and 1.6 BPG. Last season the Cavaliers stormed to 64 wins (the eighth-highest scoring team in NBA history at 9,999 points) and produced three All-Stars (Mitchell, Darius Garland, and Mobley).

Atlanta's Rising Star

Atlanta counters with Jalen Johnson, who has blossomed into a legitimate star with 21.5 points, 9.5 rebounds, and 7.0 assists per game. The Hawks also get production from Nickeil Alexander-Walker at 18.6 points per game. Both teams have been playing fast and pushing the pace, which should make for an entertaining, high-scoring affair. The total of 238.5 reflects that expectation, with both squads ranking among the league's best at going over.

Cleveland has been excellent on the road at 7-1 straight up, but just 5-8 ATS when favored by 4.5 or more points. Atlanta has covered as underdogs and could keep this one close in front of their home crowd. The Hawks' plus-14-5 over/under record suggests the over is the play.
