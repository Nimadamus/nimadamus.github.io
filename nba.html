Cleveland Cavaliers @ Chicago Bulls

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | United Center | CHSN, FanDuel SN OH
Line: CLE -5.5 | O/U: 242.5 | CLE: 15-12 | CHI: 10-15

The Cavaliers are dealing with something that matters: real adversity. After a historic 15-0 start to last season that ended with a conference semifinal exit, Cleveland entered 2025-26 with championship aspirations. Now at 15-12, injuries have derailed their momentum. Evan Mobley is out with a Grade 1 left calf strain, expected to miss 2-4 weeks. That's their best defender, a guy who changes the entire complexion of their defense when he's on the floor. Cleveland has dropped four of their last five games, and they'll need Jarrett Allen to shoulder a heavier load in Mobley's absence.

Donovan Mitchell has been carrying the offense throughout this skid. In December alone, he's averaging 33.0 points, 5.5 assists, 4.3 rebounds, and 4.0 threes per game. His 48-point explosion against Washington showed he can still take over a game when needed. The Cavs are still 6-0 against Central Division teams this season, and the Bulls fall into that category. That divisional dominance matters in a game they need to win.

Chicago's Injury Nightmare

The Bulls are beyond banged up right now. Rookie Noa Essengue is done for the season (shoulder surgery). Isaac Okoro (back), Jalen Smith (hamstring), Tre Jones (ankle), and Kevin Huerter (adductor) are all out or doubtful. That's six players who can't suit up, and it shows. Since starting 6-1, Chicago has gone 4-14, including losses to bottom-feeders like the Pacers, Pelicans, Hornets, and Nets.

Coby White is averaging 21.7 points per game since returning from his preseason calf strain, but he's been inconsistent. He shot 1-for-10 from three in Sunday's loss to New Orleans. The Bulls are 1-6 without Okoro, their best defender. Billy Donovan has talked about too much isolation ball, and with this depleted roster, it's hard to generate any offensive flow.

Why Cleveland Should Cover

Cleveland remains one of two teams in the NBA with at least 240 steals and 145 blocks this season. Even without Mobley, their defensive infrastructure is better than most teams'. They're 10-2 against teams with losing records, and Chicago definitely qualifies. The 5.5-point spread feels fair given the injury disparity, but a double-digit win wouldn't be surprising given how the Bulls have looked lately.

Memphis Grizzlies @ Minnesota Timberwolves

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Target Center | FanDuel SN
Line: MIN -7.5 | O/U: 229.5 | MEM: 12-14 | MIN: 17-9

Ja Morant is questionable. That's the headline here, and it matters a lot for how this game will play out. He left Sunday's win over the Clippers after stepping on an opponent's foot and spraining his left ankle. This is the same player who missed the entire preseason with an ankle sprain, one game with right ankle soreness, and ten games with a calf strain. When he's been available, he hasn't looked like himself, shooting just 35.9% from the field and 16.7% from three through 14 games.

Even when Morant played in his return game against Utah, he was limited to 25 minutes with 21 points and 10 assists. The production was there, but the efficiency wasn't. And now he might not even be available tonight. Memphis is asking a lot of Desmond Bane and Jaren Jackson Jr. to carry the scoring load when Morant is out or limited.

Minnesota Has the Better Roster Health

The Timberwolves are 17-9 and have been one of the more consistent teams in the Western Conference. Anthony Edwards is playing at an All-NBA level, and they have the kind of defensive personnel that can make life miserable for Memphis. Julius Randle has fit in well as the secondary scorer, and Rudy Gobert continues to be the anchor that makes everything work defensively.

The Historical Angle

These teams met in the 2022 playoffs with the Grizzlies winning in six games, and again in 2023 with Minnesota taking revenge in six. There's legitimate history and animosity between these rosters. Dillon Brooks may be gone from Memphis, but the competitive fire remains. The problem for the Grizzlies is that they're nowhere near as healthy or deep as they were during those playoff runs.

The 7.5-point spread is steep, but it accounts for Morant's uncertainty and Minnesota's home-court dominance. If Ja sits, this could get ugly. If he plays limited minutes, the Wolves should still control this game from start to finish.
