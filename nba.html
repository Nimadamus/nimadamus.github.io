Wizards @ Hornets

2025-12-24T00:00Z | Spectrum Center | MNMT, NBA League Pass
WSH: 5-23 | CHA: 10-20

Two of the league's worst teams meet in what should be a competitive tank battle. Washington's 5-23 record is the NBA's worst, but they've actually played better recently with Jordan Poole averaging 24 points over his last five games. The Hornets have been without LaMelo Ball for stretches this season, but when healthy he's a matchup nightmare.

Charlotte's home court advantage at Spectrum Center has been minimal this year - they're just 5-10 at home. Washington actually covers more often as underdogs, going 9-6 ATS when getting points. This is a pace-up spot for both teams who rank in the top 10 in pace, so expect a higher-scoring affair than the total might suggest.

Nets @ 76ers

2025-12-24T00:00Z | Xfinity Mobile Arena | YES, NBA League Pass
BKN: 8-19 | PHI: 16-11

Philly's finally healthy and it shows. Joel Embiid has been dominant since returning, averaging 33 and 12 over his last eight games. The Sixers are 16-11 and climbing, while Brooklyn continues to struggle without a true star on the roster. Cam Thomas has been solid but can't carry a team against elite competition.

The Nets have been brutal on the road this season, winning just twice away from Barclays Center. Philly's defense at home ranks top-5 in defensive rating, and they've covered in six of their last eight home games. Brooklyn's young roster just doesn't have the firepower to hang with a motivated Sixers squad.

Bulls @ Hawks

2025-12-24T00:30Z | State Farm Arena | CHSN, NBA League Pass
CHI: 13-15 | ATL: 15-15

Two .500-ish teams fighting for playoff positioning in the East. Trae Young has been cooking lately, putting up 28 and 11 assists per game over his last 10. The Hawks play at the fastest pace in the league and want to turn this into a track meet. Chicago's been inconsistent - they'll beat good teams then lose to bad ones.

DeMar DeRozan's midrange game should work against Atlanta's switch-heavy defense, but the Hawks' three-point shooting can bury teams in a hurry. Atlanta's 9-5 at home this season, while Chicago struggles on the road at 5-9. State Farm Arena gets loud, and Trae feeds off that energy.

Pelicans @ Cavaliers

2025-12-24T00:30Z | Rocket Arena | FanDuel SN OH, GCSEN
NO: 8-22 | CLE: 16-14

New Orleans has been decimated by injuries all season. Zion Williamson has missed significant time again, and the depth just isn't there to compete. At 8-22, the Pels are one of the league's biggest disappointments. Cleveland's been solid if unspectacular - Donovan Mitchell and Darius Garland form one of the better backcourts in the East.

The Cavs are a different team at home, going 10-5 at Rocket Arena this season. Their defense tightens up considerably in front of their crowd, holding opponents to just 106 PPG at home versus 114 on the road. New Orleans' offense ranks 27th in efficiency - they simply can't score enough to keep up.

Bucks @ Pacers

2025-12-24T00:30Z | Gainbridge Fieldhouse | FanDuel SN IN, FanDuel SN WI
MIL: 11-18 | IND: 6-23

What happened to these two teams? Milwaukee at 11-18 is shocking given their talent, though the Giannis Antetokounmpo injury situation has derailed their season. Indiana's even worse at 6-23 - losing Tyrese Haliburton to an Achilles injury in last year's Finals has been devastating. The Pacers just don't have a playmaker anymore.

This is a get-right spot for Milwaukee. Even a banged-up Bucks team should handle Indiana comfortably. Damian Lillard needs to step up as the primary option with Giannis limited, and this soft matchup is perfect for him. The Pacers rank dead last in defensive rating and give up open threes constantly.

Raptors @ Heat

2025-12-24T00:30Z | Kaseya Center | Sportsnet, NBA League Pass
TOR: 17-13 | MIA: 15-14

Toronto's been one of the pleasant surprises this season. Brandon Ingram's mid-season arrival from New Orleans transformed this team - he's averaging 23 and 6 since the trade and given them a legitimate go-to scorer. Scottie Barnes continues to develop into a star, putting up 21-8-6 on the year. At 17-13, they're firmly in the playoff picture.

Miami's been up and down. Jimmy Butler is Jimmy Butler - he'll show up in big moments - but the supporting cast is aging. Bam Adebayo anchors the defense but needs more offensive help. The Heat are just 7-8 at home, which is surprising for a team that usually dominates at Kaseya Center. Toronto's won three straight on the road.

Nuggets @ Mavericks

2025-12-24T01:00Z | American Airlines Center | NBC, Peacock
DEN: 21-7 | DAL: 11-19

This is the national TV showcase game and for good reason. Nikola Jokic is having another MVP-caliber season, averaging a ridiculous 27-13-10 triple-double. Denver's 21-7 record is the third-best in the West, and they've won eight straight. Meanwhile, Dallas is struggling at 11-19 despite Cooper Flagg's impressive rookie campaign. The #1 overall pick is putting up 18-7-4 but can't do it alone.

Luka Doncic has been dealing with various injuries and hasn't looked like himself. When Luka's not right, Dallas becomes very beatable. The Nuggets dominated this matchup last year and have the depth to wear down the Mavs. Jamal Murray and Michael Porter Jr. give Denver multiple scoring options that Dallas simply can't match right now.

Knicks @ Timberwolves

2025-12-24T01:00Z | Target Center | FanDuel SN North, MSG
NY: 20-8 | MIN: 19-10

A clash of Eastern powers meeting in the Twin Cities. The Knicks are rolling at 20-8 with Jalen Brunson playing like an All-NBA guard - he's putting up 26 and 7 assists while shooting 48% from the field. New York's added depth has been huge, and they're legit title contenders this year.

Minnesota's been excellent at home, going 12-3 at Target Center. Anthony Edwards is a superstar now, averaging 28 points with improved efficiency. The Wolves' defense with Rudy Gobert anchoring the paint is elite. This is a playoff atmosphere game between two teams who could meet in June.

Thunder @ Spurs

2025-12-24T01:30Z | Frost Bank Center | FanDuel SN OK, NBA League Pass
OKC: 26-3 | SA: 21-7

This might be the game of the night. OKC at 26-3 is the NBA's best team - the defending champs look even better this year. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is playing like an MVP, averaging 32 points on absurd efficiency. Chet Holmgren's development has been incredible, giving them a true two-way unicorn.

But San Antonio is no pushover at 21-7. Victor Wembanyama is everything we hoped he'd be - the 7'4" freak is averaging 24-11-4 with 3.5 blocks. The Spurs are legit now, not just a fun young team. This Texas showdown features two of the league's brightest young cores. Frost Bank Center will be electric.

Lakers @ Suns

2025-12-24T02:00Z | Mortgage Matchup Center | Spectrum Sports Net, Spectrum Sports Net +
LAL: 19-8 | PHX: 15-13

LeBron James in Year 22 is still doing LeBron things. The Lakers are 19-8 and he's averaging 25-7-7 at 40 years old. Anthony Davis has stayed healthy and dominant, and Austin Reaves has become a legitimate third star. LA looks like a real contender again.

Phoenix has been inconsistent but dangerous. Devin Booker can go off any night for 40+, and Bradley Beal has found his rhythm since the slow start. The Suns need this game more with their middling 15-13 record. Home court helps, but the Lakers have won their last four road games.

Grizzlies @ Jazz

2025-12-24T02:00Z | Delta Center | FanDuel SN SE, NBA League Pass
MEM: 13-16 | UTAH: 10-18

Memphis has been disappointing this season. Ja Morant's return hasn't sparked the turnaround they expected, and the defense that made them elite has regressed significantly. At 13-16, they're fighting just to stay in the play-in picture. The Grizzlies need to string some wins together.

Utah's in full rebuild mode at 10-18. Lauri Markkanen is their only real threat, and teams just load up on him. The Jazz are one of the worst home teams in the league. Memphis should win this one comfortably if they show up with effort - which hasn't been a given lately.

Magic @ Blazers

2025-12-24T03:00Z | Moda Center | KUNP 16, BlazerVision
Line: ORL -1.5 | O/U: 231.5 | ORL: 16-13 | POR: 12-17

Orlando's young core continues to impress. Paolo Banchero is a legit All-Star putting up 23-8-5, and Franz Wagner has become one of the best two-way wings in the league. The Magic's defense is suffocating - they rank top-5 in defensive rating and make teams work for every bucket.

Portland is rebuilding around Scoot Henderson, who's shown flashes but remains inconsistent. The Blazers play hard at home but lack the talent to compete with playoff teams. Orlando's length and athleticism should bother Portland's guards all night. The Magic have covered in four straight road games.

Pistons @ Kings

2025-12-24T03:00Z | Golden 1 Center | NBC Sports CA, FanDuel SN DET
Line: DET -9.5 | O/U: 227.5 | DET: 23-6 | SAC: 7-22

Detroit's turnaround is the story of the NBA season. The Pistons went from laughingstock to legitimate contender, now sitting at 23-6. Cade Cunningham has blossomed into an All-Star, averaging 24-5-8. Jaden Ivey's explosiveness and their young core's chemistry has been beautiful to watch. Nobody saw this coming.

Sacramento's fallen apart at 7-22. De'Aaron Fox has been in trade rumors all season, and the team chemistry is clearly fractured. The Kings can't defend anyone and have lost eight straight at home. Detroit's favored by nearly 10 on the road - that tells you everything about where these franchises are headed.

Rockets @ Clippers

2025-12-24T03:30Z | Intuit Dome | NBC, Peacock
Line: HOU -7.5 | O/U: 219.5 | HOU: 17-9 | LAC: 7-21

Kevin Durant's first season in Houston has been a massive success. The Rockets are 17-9 and KD is still KD - averaging 27 points on his trademark efficiency. Paired with the young core of Jalen Green and Alperen Sengun, Houston has the perfect mix of veteran leadership and youthful energy.

The Clippers are a disaster at 7-21. Kawhi Leonard's health issues continue, and without a star, this roster is lottery-bound. The new Intuit Dome is nice, but the product on the floor isn't. Houston should roll here - the Rockets have won five straight and the Clippers have no answer for Durant.
