Hawks @ Hornets

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Spectrum Center
Line: ATL -5 | O/U: 238.5 | ATL: 15-12 (10-6 Away) | CHA: 8-18 (5-8 Home)

The Hawks travel to Charlotte riding their strong road performance at 10-6 away from home against a struggling Hornets squad near the bottom of the Eastern Conference. Atlanta has been excellent against the spread at 14-13-0, and the key storyline centers around Trae Young's availability after returning to practice from a knee injury. If Young sits, the Hawks will lean heavily on Jalen Johnson, who has emerged as a triple-double threat averaging 22.8 points, 10.5 rebounds, and 8.2 assists per game.

Charlotte rookie Kon Knueppel has provided a spark recently with back-to-back impressive performances, including dropping 29 points in Cleveland. Miles Bridges leads the Hornets with 21.3 PPG and 6.3 RPG, while LaMelo Ball contributes 19.4 points and 8.5 assists. The under has hit in three of the last four meetings, and both teams are dealing with injury concerns that could impact scoring. Charlotte has stayed under the total in 17 of their 26 games this season.

Knicks @ Pacers

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Gainbridge Fieldhouse
Line: NY -4 | O/U: 225.5 | NY: 18-7 (4-6 Away) | IND: 6-20 (5-9 Home)

Fresh off their triumphant NBA Cup Final victory on Tuesday, the Knicks return to regular season action riding a six-game winning streak against the struggling Pacers, who have been decimated by injuries. New York comes in as 66% favorites according to the odds - they've covered the spread in all six games during their current win streak. The Knicks rank third in the NBA in scoring at 121.0 points per game while maintaining elite defense, allowing just 112.2 points (fourth in the league).

Indiana's season has been derailed with Tyrese Haliburton sidelined for the remainder of the year, joined by key rotation pieces Obi Toppin and Aaron Nesmith. The Pacers are dead last in offensive rating and average an NBA-worst 110.2 points per game. Pascal Siakam has tried to carry the load with nearly 24 points per contest, but it hasn't been enough. The Pacers are 1-3 straight up and against the spread in their last four home games. Jalen Brunson (28.8 PPG, 6.4 APG) should control the pace while the Knicks' superior depth overwhelms Indiana's depleted roster.

Heat @ Nets

Thursday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center
Line: MIA -7 | O/U: 228.5 | MIA: 14-12 (4-8 Away) | BKN: 7-18 (3-10 Home)

Two teams trending in opposite directions meet in Brooklyn. Miami enters desperately trying to snap a five-game losing streak - their longest of the season. December has been brutal for the Heat, who have managed just one game over 110 points during this skid. Norman Powell remains the bright spot, leading the team with 24.4 points per game.

Brooklyn, surprisingly, has played much better recently, winning four of their last six games. The Nets are coming off their biggest victory of the season - a stunning 127-82 demolition of the shorthanded Bucks. Michael Porter Jr. has been exceptional in December, averaging 34 points while shooting 56% from the field. While Miami should be motivated to end their skid, Porter Jr.'s hot hand and Brooklyn's recent defensive intensity (holding Milwaukee to 82 points) could produce an upset or at minimum a close game where the Nets cover.

Raptors @ Bucks

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum
Line: TOR -5 | O/U: 219.5 | TOR: 16-11 (8-5 Away) | MIL: 11-16 (8-7 Home)

The third of four regular season meetings between these division rivals, with the series split 1-1. Toronto just snapped a four-game losing streak with a convincing 106-96 victory over Miami, and they'll build momentum on the road where they've been impressive at 8-5. Brandon Ingram is paying dividends since his February trade - his 28-point performance against Miami showcased exactly why Toronto acquired him. Ingram shoots 47.2% from the field while averaging nearly 22 points per contest.

Milwaukee is in freefall without Giannis Antetokounmpo, losing 12 of their last 16 games. Their most recent embarrassment was a catastrophic 127-82 loss to Brooklyn - a 45-point drubbing that exposed their lack of depth. Kevin Porter Jr. has responded admirably with 19.3 points and 6.9 assists in games Giannis has missed, but it's not enough. The trends heavily favor Toronto, who have won each of their last six games against Central Division opponents. BetMGM's model gives Toronto a 55.5% win probability with a projected final score of Raptors 112, Bucks 106.

Rockets @ Pelicans

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Smoothie King Center
Line: HOU -10.5 | O/U: 235.5 | HOU: 16-7 (8-5 Away) | NO: 5-22 (3-12 Home)

The Rockets travel to New Orleans as substantial 10.5-point favorites against one of the league's worst teams. Houston sits at 16-7, ranking third in the NBA in points allowed at 111.2 per game. Their defense is suffocating, holding opponents to 46.0% shooting and 34.5% from three (both seventh-best). Kevin Durant has seamlessly integrated since his July trade, averaging 24.8 points and shooting 57.1% over the last 10 games. Alperen Sengun provides a second scoring option with 23.5 PPG, while Amen Thompson has stepped into the starting point guard role in Fred VanVleet's absence (torn ACL, out for season).

New Orleans is in complete disarray at 5-22, ranking 28th in defensive efficiency by allowing 122.9 points per game. Opponents shoot a league-worst 49.3% against the Pelicans. Zion Williamson has played in just 11 games this season. The talent and system disparity is massive. Houston's elite defense should stifle New Orleans' struggling offense. Expect Houston to cover the spread comfortably in a wire-to-wire victory.

Wizards @ Spurs

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center
Line: SA -15 | O/U: 241 | WSH: 4-20 (2-11 Away) | SA: 18-7 (9-2 Home)

One of the most lopsided games on tonight's slate. San Antonio hosts the league's second-worst team while riding a three-game winning streak and winning 10 of their last 13 regular season games. Victor Wembanyama continues his ascent as one of the NBA's premier talents, averaging 25.8 points, 12.6 rebounds, and 3.8 assists while shooting 50.4% from the floor. Wembanyama is ramping up after missing 12 games earlier this season - he scored 21+ points in 10 of 14 appearances.

Washington is in shambles. They allow an NBA-high 126.7 points per game, and their injury report reads like a hospital ward. Leading scorer Alex Sarr is out with a thigh injury, plus Corey Kispert (thumb), Bilal Coulibaly (oblique), and Khris Middleton (knee) are all sidelined. The Wizards have zero chance against a healthy Spurs squad. San Antonio should dominate from tip-off, with Wembanyama, Vassell (15.7 PPG), and Castle (18.4 PPG) exploiting Washington's league-worst defense. Predictions forecast Spurs 127, Wizards 110.

Clippers @ Thunder

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Paycom Center | NBA TV
Line: OKC -17.5 | O/U: 222.5 | LAC: 6-20 (3-12 Away) | OKC: 24-2 (12-0 Home)

The defending NBA champion Oklahoma City Thunder host the struggling Clippers in what should be a statement game. OKC enters at 24-2, leading the Western Conference and sporting a perfect 12-0 home record. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is playing at an MVP level with 32.4 points per game - even better in December at 32.0 PPG. The reigning Finals MVP relishes playing against the Clippers, the team that traded him, adding extra motivation.

Elite Defense Meets Depleted Offense

Oklahoma City's defense is absolutely elite. They're the league leader in defensive efficiency, giving up only 106.3 points per game while holding opponents to 42.6% shooting. The Thunder force an NBA-high 16.9 turnovers per game and convert those into a league-best 25 points off turnovers.

Los Angeles arrives in complete disarray, having lost four straight and nine of their last 10. The Clippers will be without James Harden, while Bradley Beal is done for the season with a hip injury. Kawhi Leonard provides 25 PPG, but it won't be nearly enough. The Thunder won the first meeting 126-107 on November 4 and are 6-0 straight-up with a 5-0-1 ATS record in the last six meetings. Oklahoma City should dominate, extending their perfect home record to 13-0.

Pistons @ Mavericks

Thursday, 8:30 PM ET | American Airlines Center
Line: DET -6.5 | O/U: 229.5 | DET: 21-5 (9-3 Away) | DAL: 10-17 (7-9 Home)

The Eastern Conference-leading Detroit Pistons travel to Dallas riding a four-game winning streak and an incredible 21-5 record. Cade Cunningham is playing at an All-NBA level, averaging 27.1 points, 6.0 rebounds, and 9.2 assists per game. In Detroit's recent victory over Boston, Cunningham was spectacular with 32 points and 10 assists. This is a homecoming game for Cunningham, who just had his number retired by Arlington Bowie High School. Expect maximum motivation and potentially a massive performance.

The marquee matchup features Cunningham versus Cooper Flagg (18.4 PPG). Flagg recently made history by becoming the youngest player in NBA history to score 40+ points with his 42-point explosion against Utah. The paint battle between Jalen Duren (18.0 PPG, 11.0 RPG) and Anthony Davis (20.0 PPG, 10.5 RPG - questionable) will be crucial. Dallas is struggling at 10-17, and Davis, D'Angelo Russell, and Brandon Williams are all questionable. The Pistons already own a victory over Dallas this season, 122-110 in Mexico City when Duren dominated with 33 points and 10 rebounds.

Magic @ Nuggets

Thursday, 9:00 PM ET | Ball Arena
Line: DEN -7 | O/U: 236.5 | ORL: 15-11 (5-6 Away) | DEN: 19-6 (7-4 Home)

Denver enters at 19-6 - the best start in franchise history - riding a five-game winning streak. Three-time MVP Nikola Jokic is putting up video game numbers: 29.8 points, 12.4 rebounds, and 10.8 assists on an absurd 61.3% shooting. He's coming off a monster triple-double of 39 points, 15 rebounds, and 10 assists in the Nuggets' overtime win over Houston. Jamal Murray complements Jokic with 24.9 points and 6.8 assists. The Nuggets lead the NBA in scoring at 125.6 PPG and shooting efficiency at 51.6% from the field.

Orlando is dealing with a brutal injury situation. Franz Wagner, their leading scorer at 22.7 PPG, is out for weeks with a high ankle sprain. Jalen Suggs suffered a hip contusion in the NBA Cup semifinals, ruling him out tonight. That leaves Paolo Banchero (20.5 PPG, 7.9 RPG) to carry a massive offensive load against Denver. The Magic have struggled historically in Denver, winning only once in nine visits. Jokic should feast on Orlando's depleted frontcourt, and Denver's superior shooting efficiency will prove the difference. Expect the Nuggets to pull away in the second half.

Warriors @ Suns

Thursday, 9:00 PM ET | Footprint Center
Line: GSW -1.5 | O/U: 231 | GS: 13-14 (6-10 Away) | PHX: 14-12 (8-5 Home)

Curry On Fire

Stephen Curry is playing out of his mind. The Warriors visit Phoenix after Curry dropped an incredible 48 points in Golden State's 136-131 loss to Portland. Over his last five games, Curry is averaging 34.0 points per game - 4.4 points higher than his season average. He's making an average of 12.0 three-pointers over the last 10 games while shooting 48.4% from the field. When Curry gets this hot, he's nearly impossible to stop.

The Suns are looking to snap a three-game home losing streak. Devin Booker leads with 25.1 points, 4.4 rebounds, and 6.7 assists. Dillon Brooks is having a career year in Phoenix, averaging a career-best 21.6 points as the No. 2 scoring option. The teams met earlier on November 5 with the Warriors winning 118-107, when Curry led with 28 points while Booker paced Phoenix with 38. This game has shootout potential - Curry versus Booker is must-watch basketball, with both capable of erupting for 40+ on any given night. Dimers' model gives the Warriors a 56% win probability.

Lakers @ Jazz

Thursday, 9:00 PM ET | Delta Center
Line: LAL -8 | O/U: 243 | LAL: 18-7 (11-3 Away) | UTAH: 10-15 (7-7 Home)

The Lakers travel to Utah as substantial favorites, riding an 18-7 record that places them third in the Western Conference. Los Angeles won both previous meetings - 140-126 on November 18 and 108-106 on November 23. Luka Doncic is putting up historically great numbers, leading the entire NBA with 34.7 points per game while adding 8.8 assists and 8.7 rebounds. When paired with LeBron James (17.6 PPG, 7.2 APG) as a secondary playmaker, the Lakers can manufacture quality shots even in broken possessions.

Utah just came off an emotional 140-133 overtime victory over Dallas on Monday, with Keyonte George exploding for 37 points and Lauri Markkanen adding 33. George has been excellent with 23.5 points and 6.8 assists, while Markkanen leads the Jazz with 27.8 PPG and 7.0 RPG. The Lakers are missing Austin Reaves (left calf strain) and Deandre Ayton (elbow), while Utah is without Walker Kessler for the season (shoulder surgery). Doncic's brilliance should be the difference-maker. The high total of 243 reflects both teams' offensive firepower. Expect a high-scoring affair that goes over the total.

This late-night West Coast matchup features two struggling teams. Portland hosts Sacramento as 9-point favorites after an impressive 136-131 victory over Golden State where Shaedon Sharpe scored 35 points. The Trail Blazers are 10-16 overall and 4-6 at home. Deni Avdija has been a revelation, leading the team with 25.5 points, 7.2 rebounds, and 6.3 assists - his all-around game provides Portland with a versatile weapon who can score, facilitate, and defend multiple positions.

Sacramento is in disarray at 6-20, tied for one of the worst records in the Western Conference. They've lost 19 of their last 24 games and are riding a three-game losing streak. Russell Westbrook provides veteran leadership with 13.8 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 7.3 assists, while DeMar DeRozan contributes 17.7 points. The injury situations heavily favor Portland. Sacramento is without Domantas Sabonis (knee) and Zach LaVine (ankle). Dimers' model gives Portland a 74% win probability with a predicted final score of Blazers 121, Kings 114. Expect Portland to cruise behind Avdija's playmaking and Sharpe's scoring.
