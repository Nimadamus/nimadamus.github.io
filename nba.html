NBA Cup Group Play Finale - 11-Game Slate

NBA Cup Group Play Finale: Friday's 11-Game Slate Determines Knockout Round

Posted: November 28, 2025, 10:00 AM ET | Cavaliers @ Hawks, 76ers @ Nets, Bulls @ Hornets, Magic @ Pistons, Wizards @ Pacers, Suns @ Thunder, Spurs @ Nuggets, Mavericks @ Lakers

Friday brings the final day of Emirates NBA Cup group play with massive implications across both conferences. Twelve teams are still alive for six Knockout Round spots, and tonight's games will determine who advances and who goes home. The headliner arrives late from Los Angeles where Luka Doncic and the Lakers host Anthony Davis and the struggling Mavericks in a reunion game dripping with storylines. Earlier in the evening, the Oklahoma City Thunder look to continue their historic season against a Phoenix Suns team playing without much of their top talent. This is championship-level basketball with everything on the line.

DALLAS MAVERICKS @ LOS ANGELES LAKERS | 10:00 PM ET

DALLAS MAVERICKS (5-14) @ LOS ANGELES LAKERS (13-4)
LAL -10.5 | Total 230.5 | NBA Cup West Group B
Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles | Late Night Showcase

This is the game everyone has been waiting for since the blockbuster trade that shook the NBA in February. Luka Doncic returns to face his former team for the first time at Crypto.com Arena, now leading a Lakers squad that looks like legitimate championship contenders. The February trade sent Doncic to LA for Anthony Davis in what many called the most lopsided deal in NBA history. The results have validated those critics. The Lakers sit at 13-4 and are rolling, while Dallas has completely collapsed to 5-14 and already fired GM Nico Harrison.

Doncic has been absolutely transcendent in purple and gold, averaging 35.2 points, 9.2 assists, and 8.8 rebounds per game. In his last outing against the Clippers, he erupted for 43 points in what has become routine dominance. The Lakers' star trio of LeBron James, Doncic, and Austin Reaves combined for 99 points in a recent game, showcasing the offensive firepower this team possesses. Meanwhile, Dallas ranks 29th in scoring at just 109.5 points per game and has won only twice in 12 tries as underdogs. P.J. Washington leads the Mavericks with 15.7 points per game, but the supporting cast is thin without a true star.

The Lakers are riding a four-game home winning streak and look virtually unbeatable at Crypto.com Arena. The spread of -10.5 is steep, but Dallas has been completely outclassed all season. This is a revenge game for Doncic even if he's publicly taken the high road, and expect him to put on a show. The under has hit in five of Dallas's last five games, suggesting they may struggle to keep pace offensively. The Lakers should cruise in what amounts to a coronation of Doncic's new chapter.

PHOENIX SUNS @ OKLAHOMA CITY THUNDER | 8:00 PM ET

PHOENIX SUNS @ OKLAHOMA CITY THUNDER
OKC -14.5 | NBA Cup West Group A
Paycom Center, Oklahoma City | Jalen Williams Season Debut

The defending NBA champion Oklahoma City Thunder welcome back Jalen Williams for his season debut against a Phoenix Suns team in complete disarray. Williams has been sidelined to start the season but is cleared to play in this crucial NBA Cup matchup. Even without him, the Thunder have been the best team in basketball, boasting a league-high point differential of plus-16.9. They've been blowing teams out all season with the league's top-ranked defense, and Williams' return only adds to their depth.

Phoenix has dealt with constant roster churn since trading Kevin Durant in the offseason. The blockbuster sent Durant packing, and the franchise hit the reset button with a new coach in Jordan Ott and a roster centered around Devin Booker, Dillon Brooks, and rising young stars. But injuries have derailed any progress. Jalen Green re-injured his hamstring and will miss 4-6 weeks, adding to a list of health concerns that has this team limping through the early season. Booker can't do it alone against OKC's suffocating defense.

The 14.5-point spread is massive but justified given the talent disparity. Oklahoma City has been dominant at home and desperate to clinch their NBA Cup group. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander continues his MVP-caliber campaign, and the Thunder should roll in this one. Phoenix's rebuild is just beginning while OKC is already at the mountaintop.

CLEVELAND CAVALIERS @ ATLANTA HAWKS | 7:30 PM ET

CLEVELAND CAVALIERS (12-7) @ ATLANTA HAWKS (11-8)
CLE -5.5 | Total 238.5 | NBA Cup East Group A
State Farm Arena, Atlanta

The Cavaliers visit Atlanta in an NBA Cup East Group A matchup with major implications. Cleveland enters at 12-7 with Donovan Mitchell putting up monster numbers - 29.9 points, 4.8 rebounds, and 5.5 assists per game while shooting 50.3% from the field and 38.3% from deep. Mitchell has been sensational all season, and Evan Mobley continues to develop into a two-way force with 18.7 points, 8.7 rebounds, and 1.6 blocks per game. The Cavaliers have the firepower to compete with anyone in the East.

Atlanta counters with Jalen Johnson, who has blossomed into a legitimate star with 21.5 points, 9.5 rebounds, and 7.0 assists per game. The Hawks also get production from Nickeil Alexander-Walker at 18.6 points per game. Both teams have been playing fast and pushing the pace, which should make for an entertaining, high-scoring affair. The total of 238.5 reflects that expectation, with both squads ranking among the league's best at going over.

Cleveland has been excellent on the road at 7-1 straight up, but just 5-8 ATS when favored by 4.5 or more points. Atlanta has covered as underdogs and could keep this one close in front of their home crowd. The Hawks' plus-14-5 over/under record suggests the over is the play, and this game should deliver entertainment value regardless of the outcome.

ADDITIONAL GAMES

The rest of Friday's slate features Philadelphia hosting Brooklyn in a battle of rebuilding teams, the Bulls visiting Charlotte, Orlando traveling to Detroit, Washington heading to Indiana, and San Antonio facing Denver. Each game carries NBA Cup implications as teams fight for knockout round positioning. The Spurs-Nuggets matchup features Victor Wembanyama against Nikola Jokic in a clash of generational talents at different career stages.
