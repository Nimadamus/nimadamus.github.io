6-Game Slate - Monday, December 02, 2025

Monday, December 02, 2025

@

Washington Wizards @ Philadelphia 76ers

7:00 PM ET Wells Fargo Center NBCS-PH

Look, I'm not going to sugarcoat this one. The Wizards are having an absolutely brutal season, and this road trip to Philly isn't going to do them any favors. Washington's been struggling to find any kind of rhythm, and facing a 76ers squad at home is a tall order no matter how you slice it.

The problem for Washington is simple: they can't defend, they can't shoot consistently, and they're walking into one of the tougher home environments in the Eastern Conference. Philadelphia's got the firepower to blow this game open in the second half, and historically, these matchups haven't been kind to the Wizards.

Here's the thing though. When the spread gets this wide, you've gotta think about potential backdoor covers. The Sixers might get complacent if they jump out to a big lead early, and Washington could sneak in some garbage time buckets. But banking on that is a dangerous game. The smart play here is to focus on the total or look at player props rather than trying to thread the needle on the spread.

Philadelphia should dominate this one from the opening tip. They're playing with confidence at home, and Washington just doesn't have the pieces to keep up for forty-eight minutes. This feels like a get-right game for the Sixers, and I expect them to take full advantage of it.

@

Portland Trail Blazers @ Toronto Raptors

7:30 PM ET Scotiabank Arena TSN

This is one of those matchups that doesn't get a lot of national attention, but there's serious value if you know where to look. The Raptors added Brandon Ingram back in February, and he's been exactly what this team needed. Averaging 21.5 points per game, Ingram's given Toronto a legitimate scoring threat who can create his own shot and take over when the offense stalls.

Portland's coming in on the road, and road games in Toronto are never easy. The Scotiabank Arena crowd gets loud, the Raptors play with energy at home, and the Trail Blazers have been inconsistent all season long. Portland's got talent, don't get me wrong, but they struggle with defensive rotations and Toronto's ball movement can expose that quickly.

Here's what excites me about this spot: Toronto's building something real with Ingram leading the way. He's meshed well with the young core, and the chemistry is starting to click at the right time. Portland, meanwhile, is still trying to figure out their identity. Are they a playoff team? Are they rebuilding? The lack of clear direction shows up in games like this.

I'm leaning Toronto here. They've got the home-court advantage, they've got the guy who can take over in crunch time, and Portland hasn't shown me enough consistency on the road to make me believe they can steal this one. The Raptors should handle business tonight, and if you're looking at the spread, I like Toronto to cover.

@

New York Knicks (13-6) @ Boston Celtics (11-9)

8:00 PM ET TD Garden ESPN

Listen, this is the game of the night, and it's not even close. You've got the red-hot Knicks rolling into Boston on a four-game winning streak, and you've got a Celtics team that's struggling without their superstar. Jayson Tatum is out with an Achilles injury, and that changes everything for Boston. This isn't a minor player missing a game. This is the guy who carries the offense in the clutch.

Let me break down why I love the Knicks here. First, Jalen Brunson is playing out of his mind right now, averaging 28.5 points per game and orchestrating this offense like a maestro. He's making everyone around him better, and this four-game win streak isn't a fluke. The Knicks are clicking on all cylinders. Now, yes, OG Anunoby is out with a hamstring injury, and that hurts their defensive versatility. But here's the thing: Boston's missing Tatum, and that's a way bigger blow.

Jaylen Brown is stepping up and leading this Celtics squad with 28.4 points per game, and he's been phenomenal. I'm not taking anything away from Brown. The guy's a superstar in his own right. But asking him to carry this team every single night without Tatum is a tall order, especially against a hungry Knicks team that's playing with serious confidence right now.

Here's what the matchup tells me: New York's got the momentum, they've got the depth, and they've got a point guard who's proven he can dominate on the biggest stages. Boston's home-court advantage is real, TD Garden is always tough, but this Celtics squad is 11-9 for a reason. Without Tatum, they're vulnerable, and the Knicks are the exact kind of team that can expose those weaknesses.

The spread here is going to be tight, but I'm riding with New York. They've got everything going for them right now, and Boston's dealing with too many question marks. Brunson's on fire, the team's rolling, and this feels like the perfect spot for the Knicks to make a statement on national television. Give me New York to cover, and I wouldn't be shocked if they win this one outright.

@

Minnesota Timberwolves (12-8) @ New Orleans Pelicans (3-18)

8:00 PM ET Smoothie King Center FanDuel SN North

This one's pretty straightforward, folks. The Pelicans are the worst team in the NBA right now with a 3-18 record, and they're missing Dejounte Murray, who's been one of their few bright spots this season. Minnesota's rolling in at 12-8, and Anthony Edwards is playing like a man possessed. Five straight games with 30-plus points, averaging 28.9 points per game on the season. This kid is special, and he's showing the entire league why he's a future MVP candidate.

New Orleans is in full tank mode at this point, whether they want to admit it or not. They've got injuries piling up, the chemistry's off, and they're getting blown out on a regular basis. Minnesota, on the other hand, is building momentum and trying to secure a playoff spot in the loaded Western Conference. This is a must-win game for the Timberwolves, and they know it.

Here's what I love about Minnesota in this spot: Edwards is going to get his points, you can book that right now. But it's the supporting cast that makes this team dangerous. The defense is stout, they rebound well, and they play with discipline. New Orleans can't match that intensity right now, especially without Murray running the point.

The spread's going to be heavy, and rightfully so. This is a massive talent mismatch. But here's the thing: Minnesota needs to make a statement. They need to dominate teams like this if they want to be taken seriously in the West. I expect them to come out aggressive, build a lead early, and never let New Orleans back in the game. This should be a comfortable win for the Timberwolves, and I like them to cover whatever number Vegas throws at us.

Don't overthink this one. It's Anthony Edwards against the worst team in the league. Take Minnesota and sleep easy tonight.

@

Memphis Grizzlies @ San Antonio Spurs

8:00 PM ET Frost Bank Center FanDuel SN SE

Now this is an interesting matchup between two young, hungry teams that are trying to establish themselves in the West. Memphis has always played with that grit-and-grind mentality, and even though the roster's different, that identity remains. San Antonio's in rebuild mode but they've got some exciting pieces, and home games at the Frost Bank Center give them a puncher's chance against anyone.

The Grizzlies are the more established team here, and they've got the experience edge in tight games. Memphis knows how to win on the road, they play physical defense, and they don't beat themselves with turnovers. San Antonio's still learning how to close out games, and that inexperience can be brutal in fourth-quarter situations.

Here's the angle I'm looking at: Memphis is trying to stay in the playoff hunt, and every road win matters. The Spurs are playing for development and future growth. That difference in urgency shows up in these kinds of games. Memphis is going to play harder, execute better down the stretch, and find ways to grind out possessions when the game gets tight.

I'm not saying San Antonio can't win this. They've got talent, they play hard at home, and young teams can surprise you on any given night. But Memphis has the veteran leadership and the defensive identity that should carry them through. This feels like a Grizzlies cover to me, and I wouldn't be shocked if they win outright by double digits. Take Memphis and trust the process.

@

Oklahoma City Thunder (20-1) @ Golden State Warriors

11:00 PM ET Chase Center NBA TV

This is it, folks. The nightcap, the primetime showcase, the defending NBA champions walking into Chase Center to face a Warriors team that's missing Stephen Curry. And let me tell you, this line is going to be absolutely juicy for OKC bettors. The Thunder are 20-1. Twenty wins and one loss. They're the defending champs, they beat the Pacers in seven games last season, and they're playing like they've got something to prove all over again.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is on an absolute heater right now. Twenty-plus points in ninety-one straight games. Think about that for a second. Ninety-one consecutive games. The consistency, the dominance, the sheer will to score every single night, it's legendary stuff. SGA is playing like a top-five player in the league, and the Thunder's supporting cast is locked in around him.

Now let's talk about Golden State. Curry's out with a quad injury, and he's not getting re-evaluated until December 6th. That's massive. Yes, they've got Jimmy Butler now, and he's a phenomenal player who can take over games. But asking the Warriors to beat the defending champs at home without Steph Curry is a monumental task. Butler's great, but he's not Curry. Nobody is.

Here's what this game comes down to: Oklahoma City is better, healthier, and more motivated. They're coming off a championship run, they're 20-1, and they're walking into a Warriors team that's dealing with injury problems and trying to figure out rotations without their franchise player. This is the exact kind of spot where championship teams make statements.

The Thunder's defense is elite, their pace is controlled, and SGA is going to get his buckets no matter who's guarding him. Golden State can try to slow things down, they can try to make it ugly, but OKC has too many weapons and too much championship pedigree. This feels like a Thunder blowout waiting to happen.

I'm hammering Oklahoma City here. Whatever the spread is, I'm on the Thunder. They're the defending champs playing at an elite level, and Golden State's missing their best player. This is one of the easiest calls of the night. Take OKC, lay the points, and watch the champions go to work on national television. This one's going to be special.
