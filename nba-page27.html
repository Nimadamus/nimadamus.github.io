Warriors @ Raptors

2025-12-28T20:30Z | Scotiabank Arena | NBC Sports BA, NBA League Pass
GS: 16-16 | TOR: 19-14

The Warriors' identity crisis continues as they hover at .500, but Stephen Curry remains one of the most dangerous players in the league when it matters. Golden State's offense still ranks in the top ten, averaging 117.4 points per game, but their defense has been inconsistent all season. On the road, they're just 6-10, which makes this trip to Toronto particularly challenging.

The Raptors have been one of the surprise stories of the season. Toronto's young core led by Scottie Barnes is clicking, and they've won seven of their last ten at Scotiabank Arena. Barnes is averaging 21.3 points and 8.7 rebounds while showing marked improvement as a playmaker. The Raptors' pace-and-space attack should challenge Golden State's struggling perimeter defense.

This matchup comes down to whether Curry can get hot enough to overcome Toronto's home court advantage. The Raptors have been excellent at protecting their home floor and their depth gives them an edge in late-game situations. Look for this to be a high-scoring affair with the total likely to push past 230.

76ers @ Thunder

2025-12-28T20:30Z | Paycom Center | FanDuel SN OK, NBC Sports Phil
PHI: 16-14 | OKC: 27-5

The Thunder are the class of the Western Conference and this game showcases why. OKC's 27-5 record is the best in the NBA, built on suffocating defense that ranks first in points allowed. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is playing at an MVP level, averaging 31.8 points while Chet Holmgren continues to evolve into a two-way force. At home, the Thunder are a ridiculous 15-1.

Philadelphia has been solid but inconsistent, which is par for the course with this franchise. The 76ers are dealing with the usual injury concerns but have shown they can compete with anyone when healthy. Tyrese Maxey has stepped up as a legitimate star, but the supporting cast has been hit or miss. On the road, Philly is just 7-9.

This spread is large for a reason. OKC's defense is elite and they rarely let up at Paycom Center. The 76ers simply don't have the firepower to hang with the league's best team on the road. Philadelphia might keep it close early, but expect the Thunder to pull away in the second half.

Grizzlies @ Wizards

2025-12-28T23:00Z | Capital One Arena | MNMT, FanDuel SN SE
MEM: 15-17 | WSH: 7-23

Ja Morant and the Grizzlies have been up and down all season, but they're still a dangerous team when healthy. Memphis is averaging 116.2 points per game and plays at one of the fastest paces in the league. Morant is putting up 25.4 points and 8.8 assists per game, and the young supporting cast of Desmond Bane and Jaren Jackson Jr. gives them legitimate playoff potential.

Washington is in full rebuild mode with one of the worst records in the NBA. The Wizards are allowing 120.3 points per game - dead last in the league - and their offense isn't much better. Jordan Poole is putting up numbers but the efficiency isn't there, and the lack of defensive effort is glaring every night. They're 2-13 at home.

This is about as close to a layup as you'll find in the NBA. Memphis's pace should absolutely demolish Washington's non-existent defense. The only question is the margin. The Grizzlies should win by double digits without breaking a sweat.

Celtics @ Blazers

2025-12-28T23:00Z | Moda Center | BlazerVision, NBC Sports BO
BOS: 19-12 | POR: 13-19

Boston is in a strange spot this season. At 19-12, they're a good team but nowhere near their championship form from just two seasons ago. The Celtics are still trying to find their identity without Jayson Tatum, whose Achilles injury changed everything. Jaylen Brown has shouldered the load admirably, but the supporting cast hasn't always delivered. Road trips out West have been brutal.

Portland is in year two of the rebuild and showing some progress. The Blazers have young talent in Anfernee Simons and Scoot Henderson, and they've been competitive at home with a 9-6 record at Moda Center. Simons is averaging 22.1 points and can get hot in a hurry. The pace of play in Portland could work in their favor against a Celtics team that prefers half-court sets.

This is a tricky West Coast game for Boston. The Blazers aren't good, but they play hard at home and the travel factor is real. The Celtics should still win, but Portland has the ability to make this interesting if their three-point shooting is on. Don't expect a blowout.

Pistons @ Clippers

2025-12-29T02:00Z | Intuit Dome | FanDuel SN DET Ext, NBA League Pass
Line: DET -3.5 | O/U: 224.5 | DET: 24-7 | LAC: 9-21

What a difference a year makes. The Pistons have completely transformed from the laughingstock of the league to one of the best teams in basketball. Detroit's 24-7 record is second only to OKC, and Cade Cunningham has emerged as a legitimate All-Star. Jaden Ivey's development has been huge, and the Pistons' defense has become one of the stingiest in the league. On the road, they're still an excellent 10-4.

The Clippers' season has been a disaster. Without Kawhi Leonard (again), LA has cratered to 9-21 and looks headed for the lottery. James Harden is still putting up numbers but the effort level on defense is embarrassing. The move to Intuit Dome hasn't provided the home court spark they hoped for, as they're just 5-10 in the new arena.

Detroit is the better team by a wide margin. The Pistons play with energy and purpose, while the Clippers look like they're just going through the motions. This line seems about right - Detroit should win by 4-7 points without much drama.

Kings @ Lakers

2025-12-29T02:30Z | crypto.com Arena | Spectrum Sports Net, Spectrum Sports Net +
Line: LAL -12.5 | O/U: 232.5 | SAC: 8-23 | LAL: 19-10

Sacramento's 2025-26 season has been nothing short of a disaster. The Kings went from a playoff team to the bottom of the Western Conference in stunning fashion. De'Aaron Fox is still producing individually, but the chemistry has completely evaporated. Domantas Sabonis looks frustrated and the defense has been atrocious, allowing 121.6 points per game. At 8-23, they're playing out the string.

The Lakers have been solid with a 19-10 record, and LeBron James continues to defy Father Time. Anthony Davis is playing at a DPOY level, and Austin Reaves has developed into a legitimate third option. At home, LA is 12-3 and crypto.com Arena has been rocking. The Lakers' defense has been particularly stingy at home, allowing just 106.2 points per game.

This line is large but justified. The Lakers are a legit contender at home while the Kings are in complete disarray. LeBron loves to put on a show in LA, and Sacramento offers little resistance. Expect the Lakers to cover comfortably in a double-digit win.
