8-Game Thursday Slate

NHL March 5, 2026

Thursday delivers a loaded 8-game NHL slate with storylines dripping from every corner of the league. The defending back-to-back champion Panthers, shockingly sitting last in the Atlantic Division at 30-28-3, visit a surging Columbus club. Tampa Bay, the Atlantic's best at 38-17-4, heads to Winnipeg where the Jets have completely imploded from their hot start. Buffalo and Pittsburgh clash in a massive Eastern Conference playoff battle on ESPN. And the night caps with the Islanders visiting the Kings in LA, where the home team's league-leading 14 overtime losses tell a story of a club that can't finish games.

Maple Leafs @ Rangers

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Puck Line
TOR +1.5 / NYR -1.5
Moneyline
TOR -110 / NYR -115
Total
O/U 5.5

Two teams in very different kinds of pain meet at Madison Square Garden on Thursday night, and the atmosphere inside the World's Most Famous Arena should reflect the misery surrounding both franchises right now. Toronto at 27-24-11 with 65 points has been maddeningly inconsistent all season, and the offseason departure of their top winger continues to haunt this roster in ways that are impossible to ignore. The Leafs' inability to find consistent secondary scoring behind Auston Matthews has been the defining flaw of their season, and the 11 overtime losses tell you that Toronto has been in plenty of games but simply can't close them out when things get tight. That's a confidence problem as much as it is a talent problem.

New York at 23-29-8 with 54 points is living through the kind of nightmare season that nobody at MSG saw coming. This was supposed to be a team competing for a Cup, and instead, the Rangers are 23 games below .500 and looking like sellers at the trade deadline rather than buyers. The defense has been leaky, the goaltending inconsistent, and the offensive firepower that was supposed to carry this club has sputtered for long stretches. Madison Square Garden hasn't exactly been a fortress either, and the frustration from the fans has been palpable during a season that's gone completely sideways from day one.

The TOR -110 / NYR -115 moneyline tells you the market sees this as a virtual coin flip with the slightest lean toward the home side, which makes sense given how mediocre both teams have been. Toronto is the "better" team by record, but 65 points versus 54 points doesn't exactly scream domination when you consider that the Leafs have been wildly unreliable on the road this season. The Rangers, for all their struggles, still have the kind of talent on the roster that can beat anyone on a given night when things click. The 5.5 total is a standard number for a game between two teams that haven't been lighting up the scoreboard with any consistency.

What makes this game genuinely interesting is the desperation angle. Neither team can afford to lose right now, albeit for different reasons. Toronto is fighting to stay relevant in a brutally competitive Atlantic Division where Tampa Bay has pulled away at 80 points. The Rangers are trying to salvage any shred of dignity from a season that's already been a catastrophic failure by their standards. When two frustrated teams collide in a building where the crowd is going to be restless from the opening faceoff, you tend to get a tense, physical, low-event game. The under has some appeal here given that neither offense has inspired confidence lately, and both clubs are more likely to grind through a 2-1 or 3-2 affair than engage in a track meet.

Utah @ Flyers

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center, Philadelphia, PA
Puck Line
UTA +1.5 / PHI -1.5
Moneyline
UTA -120 / PHI EVEN
Total
O/U 5.5

Utah at 32-25-4 with 68 points rolls into the Wells Fargo Center as the road favorite against a Flyers team that's been quietly competitive all season long. The Mammoth, in their inaugural season after relocating from Arizona, have established themselves as a legitimate playoff contender in the Western Conference, and their 32 wins through 61 games represent a pace that would've seemed wildly optimistic back in October. The offense has been the engine, and the roster that management assembled for this fresh start in Salt Lake City has proven it can hang with the best teams in the league on any given night.

Philadelphia at 28-21-11 with 67 points is just one point behind Utah in the standings, and the Flyers have been one of the more pleasant surprises in the Eastern Conference. The 11 overtime losses are a bit frustrating because they represent points left on the table, but you can also look at it the other way: this team is competitive in virtually every game it plays. The Wells Fargo Center has been a tough building for visitors this season, and the Philly crowd brings an energy that can tilt tight games in the home team's favor. The Flyers have found a way to hang around in the Metropolitan Division race, and games like this one at home are the kind they need to capitalize on.

The UTA -120 moneyline making Utah a modest road favorite reflects the slight edge in overall quality, but the PHI EVEN price is telling you the market doesn't see much separation here at all. When you can get a home team at even money in a matchup between two clubs separated by a single point in the standings, that's the kind of value that sharp bettors tend to gravitate toward. Philadelphia's home-ice advantage and their competitive track record this season make them a dangerous underdog in this spot.

The 5.5 total is a clean number for this matchup, and it could go either direction depending on how the goaltending performs. Both teams have the offensive talent to push this over, but they've also shown the ability to grind out lower-scoring affairs when the situation demands it. Utah's road game has been solid but not spectacular, and the Flyers' defensive structure at home has been good enough to keep most opponents in check. This feels like a classic 3-2 or 4-3 type of game where the result comes down to which team makes fewer mistakes in the third period, and the home-ice edge could be the difference in a contest between two evenly matched opponents.

ESPN

Sabres @ Penguins

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | PPG Paints Arena, Pittsburgh, PA
Puck Line
BUF +1.5 / PIT -1.5
Moneyline
BUF -115 / PIT -105
Total
O/U 6.5

This is the marquee matchup of the early window and it deserves the ESPN national broadcast treatment. Buffalo at 36-19-6 with 78 points has been one of the best stories in the entire NHL this season, sitting second in the Atlantic Division and playing the kind of hockey that Sabres fans have been waiting over a decade to see. After years of being the league's punchline, Buffalo has transformed into a legitimate contender with a balanced attack that can beat you in multiple ways. Their 36 wins are tied for the sixth-most in the NHL, and the Sabres have done it with a blend of high-end talent, depth scoring, and goaltending that's been consistently excellent. This is a team that believes it can compete with anyone, and the record backs that confidence up entirely.

Pittsburgh at 31-16-13 with 75 points has been good but fascinating for a completely different reason: those 13 overtime losses. That number is staggering, the second-highest in the NHL behind only the Kings' 14. What it tells you about the Penguins is that they're competitive in every single game they play, rarely getting blown out, but they have an almost pathological inability to close out tight contests. A 31-16 record in regulation and overtime games that end decisively is outstanding. But the 13 OTL games represent 13 occasions where Pittsburgh had the game in hand and let it slip away. That's a concerning trend for a team with legitimate playoff aspirations, because the postseason is nothing but tight, high-pressure games.

The BUF -115 moneyline making the Sabres slight road favorites speaks volumes about how the market views these two teams right now. Buffalo has earned that respect through consistent performance all season, while Pittsburgh's OTL habit has created a perception that the Penguins are a team you can't fully trust in close games. The 6.5 total is the highest on the entire Thursday slate, and it's justified given that both of these teams have offensive firepower to burn. This is a matchup where you can legitimately see four or five goals per side, and the pace should be fast with both clubs willing to play up-tempo hockey. The over is an intriguing look given the offensive talent on both rosters.

The atmosphere at PPG Paints Arena should be electric for a nationally televised game between two Eastern Conference contenders, and Pittsburgh's home crowd will be desperate to will their team to a regulation win rather than another agonizing overtime affair. Buffalo, though, has shown zero fear on the road this season and won't be intimidated by the environment. The puck line at PIT -1.5 is a tough ask given Pittsburgh's propensity for close games, which makes the BUF +1.5 look like a very comfortable cushion. Regardless of who wins, this game has the ingredients to be one of the most entertaining contests on the entire NHL schedule tonight, and it's well worth the national spotlight.

Panthers @ Blue Jackets

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | Nationwide Arena, Columbus, OH
Puck Line
FLA +1.5 / CBJ -1.5
Moneyline
FLA -105 / CBJ -115
Total
O/U 6.5

If you'd told anyone 12 months ago that the back-to-back Stanley Cup champion Florida Panthers would be sitting last in the Atlantic Division in March, they'd have called you absolutely insane. And yet, here we are. Florida at 30-28-3 with 63 points is 17 points behind division-leading Tampa Bay and, for the first time in three years, looking like a team that's going to be watching the playoffs from home. The defending champs have been undone by a combination of age, regression, and the simple reality that winning two consecutive Cups takes an enormous toll on a roster. The hunger that defined their championship runs has been replaced by a flatness that's been impossible to shake, and the results have been brutal.

Columbus at 31-21-8 with 70 points has been one of the most unexpected success stories in the Eastern Conference this season. The Blue Jackets have quietly built a 7-point cushion over Florida in the standings, and their performance at Nationwide Arena has been a major reason why. Columbus plays with an edge and a physicality that makes them tough to beat on their own ice, and the organization has found a level of consistency that seemed impossible a year ago. This is a team that's bought into its identity, plays hard for 60 minutes, and doesn't care about your championship pedigree.

The FLA -105 / CBJ -115 moneyline gives Columbus a slight edge as the home team, and the pricing reflects the current trajectories of these two franchises more than their historical reputations. Florida's name still carries weight, which is why they're not bigger underdogs, but the on-ice product this season doesn't justify backing them with any real confidence. Columbus has simply been the better team, the more consistent team, and the more motivated team for months now. The 6.5 total suggests the market expects a higher-scoring affair, and given that Florida's defensive structure has eroded significantly from their championship form, that's a reasonable expectation.

There's a poetic element to this game that shouldn't be ignored. Florida's dynasty, if you can even call two titles that, might officially end with a whimper in Ohio. The Panthers need a miracle run to even sniff a wild card spot at this point, and games against teams like Columbus that are firmly in the playoff picture are exactly the ones they can't afford to lose. But "can't afford to lose" and "are built to win" are two very different things, and the defending champs look more like the former than the latter. Columbus should feel confident at home in this spot, and the Nationwide Arena crowd will smell blood in the water against a former champion that's clearly lost its edge.

Bruins @ Predators

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Bridgestone Arena, Nashville, TN
Puck Line
BOS +1.5 / NSH -1.5
Moneyline
BOS -105 / NSH -115
Total
O/U 6.5

Boston at 34-21-5 with 73 points heads to Nashville in a comfortable playoff position but looking to keep momentum going in a crowded Eastern Conference race. The Bruins have been a steady, workmanlike team all season, rarely spectacular but almost never awful, and that consistency has kept them firmly in the wild card picture. Boston's defensive identity under Jim Montgomery remains the backbone of this team, and the Bruins' ability to limit high-danger chances has made them a tough out every single night regardless of the opponent. The question with Boston has always been whether the offense can generate enough to win games that the defense keeps close, and on most nights this season, the answer has been yes.

Nashville at 27-26-8 with 62 points has been one of the more frustrating teams to watch in the entire league. The Predators were supposed to be better than this after an aggressive offseason, but the results just haven't materialized. At 62 points through 61 games, they're barely on pace for a wild card spot and would need a scorching finish to actually secure one. Bridgestone Arena hasn't been the fortress it once was, and the energy in the building has waned as the season has gone on without the Predators establishing themselves as a legitimate playoff contender. There's talent here, no question, but the sum hasn't equaled the parts, and time is running out for Nashville to figure it out.

The BOS -105 / NSH -115 moneyline makes Nashville a slight home favorite, which feels like a nod to the venue more than a statement about team quality. Boston is the better team by the standings and by most analytical measures, but the market gives home ice a bump, especially in a building like Bridgestone that can get loud when the Predators are engaged. The 6.5 total is on the higher side for a game involving the Bruins, who tend to play tighter defensive games, but Nashville's inconsistency means they can be involved in shootouts just as easily as they can play a boring 2-1 affair.

The key dynamic here is that Boston doesn't really need this game the way Nashville does. The Bruins are safely in a playoff spot and can afford to play their methodical style without pressing. Nashville, on the other hand, is playing for its postseason life, and that desperation can manifest in two completely different ways: either the Predators come out with urgency and make this a difficult night for Boston, or they press too hard and make mistakes that the disciplined Bruins exploit ruthlessly. History suggests that disciplined teams tend to feast on desperate ones, and Boston's structure and composure on the road could be the deciding factor in a game where Nashville's emotional state is unpredictable.

Lightning @ Jets

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Canada Life Centre, Winnipeg, MB
Puck Line
TB -1.5 / WPG +1.5
Moneyline
TB -185 / WPG +154
Total
O/U 5.5

Tampa Bay at 38-17-4 with 80 points is the best team in the Atlantic Division and one of the best teams in the entire NHL, and the Lightning have earned every single one of those points through a season-long display of dominance that's reminded everyone why this franchise has been the gold standard in the Eastern Conference for the better part of a decade. The Lightning's balance is what separates them from the pack: they score, they defend, their goaltending is rock solid, and their special teams are among the best in the league. When everything is clicking for Tampa, they're virtually unbeatable, and it's been clicking at an elite level for most of this season. The 38 wins through 59 games puts them on pace for a franchise-caliber season.

Winnipeg at 24-26-10 with 58 points represents one of the most dramatic collapses in the NHL this season. Remember when the Jets started the year on a blistering hot streak and looked like they might run away with the Central Division? That feels like a lifetime ago now. Winnipeg has been in complete freefall, tumbling from the top of the standings to a position where they're barely clinging to relevance. The 10 overtime losses tell part of the story, but the bigger issue is that the Jets simply stopped competing at the level required to win in this league. The goals dried up, the defensive structure crumbled, and the confidence that fueled their early-season surge evaporated entirely.

The TB -185 moneyline is a hefty price, but it reflects the chasm between these two teams right now. Tampa is 22 points ahead of Winnipeg in the standings, and the gap in quality is even wider than that number suggests. The Lightning's road record has been excellent, and they're the kind of team that goes into difficult buildings and imposes its will regardless of the environment. Winnipeg's home-ice advantage, which was formidable early in the season, has eroded to the point where Canada Life Centre isn't nearly the fortress it used to be. The Jets' collapse has taken the crowd out of it, and a building that was rocking in October and November now has the feel of a team going through the motions.

The 5.5 total is the lowest available number on the slate, and it makes sense when you consider that Tampa's defensive structure is elite and Winnipeg's offense has dried up during their extended slump. This is more likely to be a controlled, low-event game where the Lightning take an early lead and manage the clock than a wide-open shootout. The puck line at TB -1.5 is tempting given the talent disparity, but the Lightning are also a team that's perfectly happy winning 3-1 or 2-1 rather than running up the score. If you're looking at the WPG +154 underdog price, you're essentially betting on the Jets to rediscover the magic from months ago, and there's very little evidence that's happening anytime soon.

OTT -162 / CGY +136
Total
O/U 5.5

Ottawa at 29-22-9 with 67 points heads west sitting right on the playoff bubble, and every single game from here to the end of the season carries enormous weight for a Senators team that's desperate to end their long postseason drought. The Sens have been a frustrating blend of brilliant stretches and maddening inconsistency, capable of beating anyone in the league on their best night but equally capable of laying an egg against inferior opponents. The 9 overtime losses are points that Ottawa simply can't afford to leave on the table, and at 67 points, they're in a dogfight with several other teams for the final wild card spots in the Eastern Conference. This is a team that knows its margin for error is razor thin.

Calgary at 24-29-7 with 55 points is having a season to forget, and the Saddledome has been more of a museum than a fortress for the Flames this year. The rebuild is in full swing whether Calgary's front office wants to call it that or not, and the results speak for themselves. At 55 points through 60 games, the Flames are well out of the playoff picture and playing out the string. The offensive inconsistency has been the biggest issue, with Calgary struggling to generate enough scoring on a nightly basis to compete with teams that have more firepower and more depth up front. Defensively, they've been passable but not good enough to compensate for an attack that goes cold far too often.

The OTT -162 moneyline makes Ottawa a clear road favorite, and the pricing reflects the gap between a team fighting for its playoff life and one that's already mentally checked into offseason mode. Ottawa has the kind of offensive talent, led by their top-end forwards, that should be able to exploit a Calgary defense that's been permissive all season. The Senators need these games against below-.500 teams to bank points, and the market's confidence in them here is well-placed. The CGY +136 underdog price gives you a decent return if you believe in Calgary's home-ice advantage, but the Flames haven't done enough at the Saddledome this season to inspire that kind of confidence.

The 5.5 total is standard fare for a game involving these two teams, and the direction it goes will depend largely on whether Ottawa's offense shows up ready to play or coasts into Calgary assuming the result will take care of itself. The Senators can't afford to take anyone lightly at this stage of the season, and their coaching staff knows that trap games against basement-dwelling opponents are the ones that can sink a playoff push. If Ottawa comes out with the urgency this game demands, they should handle Calgary comfortably. If they come out flat, the Flames have enough pride and enough individual talent to make this uncomfortable. The smart money says Ottawa's desperation carries the day, but stranger things have happened in March hockey.

Islanders @ Kings

Thursday, 9:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Puck Line
NYI +1.5 / LA -1.5
Moneyline
NYI +114 / LA -135
Total
O/U 5.5

The nightcap on ESPN features a genuinely fascinating cross-conference clash between two teams that couldn't be more different in terms of how they've gotten to this point in the season. The Islanders at 35-22-5 with 75 points have been one of the most consistent teams in the Eastern Conference, sitting comfortably in third place in the Metropolitan Division with a brand of hockey that's built on defensive structure, goaltending, and opportunistic scoring. New York doesn't beat you with flash; they beat you by suffocating you with discipline and making the most of their chances. It's not always pretty, but 35 wins and 75 points through 62 games speaks for itself.

Los Angeles at 24-22-14 with 62 points is the NHL's most confounding team, and those 14 overtime losses are the single most telling statistic in the entire league. Nobody, and I mean nobody, has found more creative ways to lose games they should have won than the Kings this season. Fourteen times, LA had the game either tied or within reach in the extra period and came away empty-handed. That's not bad luck at that volume; that's a systemic inability to finish games. If even half of those OTL results had gone the other way, the Kings would be sitting at 69 points and firmly in a playoff spot. Instead, they're 13 points behind the Islanders and staring at a long offseason because they simply cannot close the deal.

The LA -135 moneyline makes the Kings home favorites, which is a reflection of the venue and the general belief that home ice matters in late-season games. But there's a legitimate argument that the Islanders are the better team here by a significant margin. New York has 13 more points in the standings, a much better win percentage, and the kind of road resilience that suggests they won't be fazed by playing at Crypto.com Arena on a Thursday night. The NYI +114 underdog price is intriguing because you're getting a team that's been better in nearly every meaningful metric at plus money simply because they're on the road. That's the kind of line that experienced bettors circle immediately.

The 5.5 total is standard for a game featuring the Islanders, who tend to keep scores low through their suffocating defensive system. The Kings' offensive output has been inconsistent, and their struggles in overtime suggest they lack the kind of finishing ability that pushes games over higher totals. This could easily be a 2-1 or 3-2 contest where every goal matters enormously, and the team that gets the first lead might never give it back. If there's one thing the Islanders do exceptionally well, it's protecting leads in the third period, which is exactly the kind of game management that the Kings have been unable to demonstrate all season. The atmosphere should be great for a nationally televised late-night game, but atmospheres don't win hockey games. Execution does, and the Islanders have been far more reliable in that department than the Kings all year.
