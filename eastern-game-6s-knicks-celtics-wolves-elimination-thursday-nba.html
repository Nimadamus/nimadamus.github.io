Game 6 - NY Leads 3-2
ESPN

Knicks @ Hawks

Thursday, 7:00 PM ET | State Farm Arena, Atlanta, GA

The New York Knicks visit Atlanta with a chance to close out the first-round series after Tuesday's commanding 126-97 Game 5 win at Madison Square Garden. Jalen Brunson posted a 39-point, eight-assist, three-rebound line that pushed the Knicks back into control of the matchup, and the Garden crowd carried the energy from the opening tip to the final buzzer. The series score is now 3-2 New York, and Game 6 at State Farm Arena is the closeout window before a potential return trip to MSG for Game 7. Atlanta has dropped two of the last three despite having stolen Game 4 at home, and the structural read is that the Hawks need to do something fundamentally different on the defensive end to extend the series.

The Knicks' identity in this series has been the half-court shot creation profile that Brunson and Karl-Anthony Towns produce together. Brunson's pull-up game has been the closing argument, with the kind of clutch jumper sequences that have defined his postseason career, and Towns has stretched Atlanta's frontcourt to the point where Onyeka Okongwu cannot stay on the floor in fourth-quarter minutes. OG Anunoby's two-way wing minutes have been the matchup constant against Trae Young, and Mikal Bridges has produced the kind of supplementary scoring profile that the Knicks need to complement Brunson when Atlanta's defense tilts. Tom Thibodeau's rotation has been tight - eight-man with the occasional Deuce McBride wrinkle - and the bench production has been a structural plus.

Atlanta's path to forcing Game 7 is the Trae Young variance window. Young's series scoring profile has been the offensive engine, and the home-court rhythm at State Farm Arena tilts the way his pick-and-roll game opens up against the Knicks' switching coverage. Jalen Johnson's two-way wing line has been the secondary piece, and Zaccharie Risacher's rookie playoff minutes have produced flashes of the kind of perimeter scoring that Atlanta will need from a third option. Quin Snyder's adjustments after the Game 5 blowout will be the chess move - whether the Hawks come out in a more aggressive top-lock against Brunson or whether they double Towns earlier in the shot clock to force the ball out of the two stars' hands. The aging Hawks roster needs the home-court fourth-quarter execution that has historically defined their best playoff runs.

Game 6 - Boston Leads 3-2 - Featured
Peacock

Celtics @ 76ers

Thursday, 8:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center, Philadelphia, PA

The Boston Celtics visit Philadelphia for Game 6 with the series in their hands at 3-2, road moneyline at -218 with the 76ers at +180 to extend the series. The classic Atlantic Division rivalry has produced the kind of back-and-forth playoff hockey that defined the early-2000s Iverson era, with three of the five games decided in the final two minutes. Boston's structural advantage in the matchup has been the Jaylen Brown-Jayson Tatum shot creation profile, with the two All-NBA wings combining for the kind of late-game possession-by-possession scoring that has defined Joe Mazzulla's Celtics across two consecutive deep playoff runs. Philadelphia's response has been the Tyrese Maxey jet-fueled offensive line and the structural defensive identity built around a healthy Joel Embiid frontcourt.

The Celtics' close-out math runs through Tatum and the road-game rhythm. Tatum returned from his May 2025 Achilles surgery during the regular season and has been back in the rotation through the entire first round, and his series scoring profile has produced the kind of efficient shot diet that has defined his deepest playoff runs, with the mid-range pull-up and the rim-attack package both available against Philly's switching coverage. Brown's two-way wing minutes against Maxey have been the matchup constant, and Derrick White has produced the kind of playmaker-shooter profile that opens the floor when the Embiid double-team commits. Kristaps Porzingis's frontcourt minutes have been the variance piece - if his back and foot hold up for 32 minutes at the Wells Fargo Center, Boston has the structural edge that has defined the matchup. Al Horford's playoff minutes against Embiid have been the late-game closer profile, and the Celtics' bench has produced the kind of rotation depth that Mazzulla has leaned on across the four wins.

Philadelphia's path to forcing Game 7 is the Embiid health variance and the Maxey explosion ceiling. Embiid's series production has been the question mark all spring - he is the structural reason the 76ers can match Boston's frontcourt, and the home-court rhythm in front of a Wells Fargo Center elimination crowd is the variance window that opens his interior scoring profile. Maxey's series scoring has been the offensive engine, with the kind of pull-up three and rim-attack profile that has produced 35+ point nights when the matchup tilts. Paul George's two-way wing line has been the secondary piece, and Kelly Oubre's bench scoring has produced the kind of supplementary minutes that Nick Nurse has leaned on. The Wells Fargo Center crowd will lean into the closeout moment for Philly, and the Sixers need every fourth-quarter execution stop to push the series back to TD Garden for Game 7. The Celtics close on the road as -218 favorites - the heaviest road moneyline of the night.

Game 6 - Minnesota Leads 3-2
ESPN

Nuggets @ Timberwolves

Thursday, 9:30 PM ET | Target Center, Minneapolis, MN

The Denver Nuggets visit Minnesota for Game 6 with the Timberwolves up 3-2 and looking to close out the first-round series at Target Center after Game 5 in Denver swung the matchup back to the Wolves. This is the rematch of the 2024 Western Conference second round series that Minnesota stole from the defending champs in seven games, and the storyline has come full circle with the Wolves now one win from another second-round trip at the expense of Nikola Jokic and the Nuggets. Anthony Edwards has been the offensive engine for Minnesota, and the home-court Game 6 rhythm at Target Center is the structural variance window that the Wolves have leaned on across their best playoff runs.

The Wolves' close-out math runs through Edwards and Rudy Gobert. Edwards' series scoring profile has been the kind of pull-up three and rim-attack package that has carried Minnesota through the late-game possessions where the Jokic double-team commits, and his clutch shot-making at the Target Center is the variance piece that defines the matchup. Gobert's interior defensive line against Jokic has been the matchup constant - the four-time Defensive Player of the Year is the structural reason Minnesota can survive the Jokic post-up scoring profile, and his rebounding numbers have produced the second-chance scoring on the other end. Julius Randle's two-way forward minutes have been the secondary piece, and Jaden McDaniels' wing defense against Jamal Murray has produced the kind of perimeter shutdown that flips Denver's offensive rhythm. Naz Reid's bench scoring profile has been the rotation X-factor that Chris Finch has leaned on across the four wins.

Denver's path to forcing Game 7 in Denver is the Nikola Jokic ceiling and the Jamal Murray health variance. Jokic's series MVP-level production has been the offensive structural piece - he is the reason the Nuggets are alive at this stage, and his triple-double profile against Gobert has produced the kind of playmaking minutes that open the floor for everyone else. Murray's series scoring has been the second-engine question mark, with the kind of pull-up jumper profile that defines Denver's clutch closeouts when his calf and shoulder cooperate. Aaron Gordon's two-way forward line has been the matchup constant, and Christian Braun's wing minutes have produced the kind of secondary scoring that Denver needs to complement Jokic. Michael Malone's adjustments at the Target Center will be the chess move - whether Denver doubles Edwards earlier in the shot clock or whether they switch the pick-and-roll to take the ball out of Edwards' hands. If Jokic produces another 30-15-10 line, the Nuggets push the series back to Ball Arena for Game 7. If Edwards detonates at the Target Center, the rematch of 2024 ends the same way it did then.
