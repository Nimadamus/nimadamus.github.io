3-Game Sunday Showcase

NBA February 1, 2026

A premium Sunday slate headlined by the debut of Sunday Night Basketball on TNT, featuring the Lakers traveling to Madison Square Garden to face the Knicks. The action begins with a Bucks-Celtics matinee in Boston and concludes with a Thunder-Nuggets Western Conference showdown in Denver.

Game 1
ABC

Bucks @ Celtics

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Spread
BOS -13.5 (-110)
Moneyline
MIL +520 / BOS -714
Total
O/U 217.5

The Bucks are in serious trouble, and this massive 13.5-point spread tells the story. Giannis Antetokounmpo is officially OUT with a calf strain, and without the two-time MVP, Milwaukee transforms from a legitimate contender to a lottery-caliber team. We've seen this movie before. When Giannis sits, the Bucks are 3-9 this season, averaging just 104.2 points per game while allowing 117.8. Those numbers are ugly, and facing Boston's elite defense in TD Garden makes this situation even more dire. Damian Lillard will need a heroic performance just to keep this competitive.

Boston at 33-15 has navigated life without Jayson Tatum (Achilles) admirably. Jaylen Brown has embraced the alpha role, averaging 28.4 points over his last ten games while shooting 47% from the field. The Celtics' defense remains elite, ranking third in defensive efficiency at 108.2 points allowed per 100 possessions. Derrick White and Jrue Holiday continue to provide the two-way impact that makes this team so dangerous, and their ability to clamp down on opposing guards will be crucial against Lillard. Boston is 18-4 at home this season and has dominated inferior competition at TD Garden.

The 13.5-point spread looks massive, but consider this: Milwaukee without Giannis is essentially a play-in team masquerading as a contender. Their defense falls apart without his rim protection, and their offense becomes entirely dependent on Lillard creating something out of nothing. Boston should control this game from the opening tip, building a double-digit lead by halftime and cruising to victory. The Celtics have covered in 8 of their last 11 home games as double-digit favorites. This line is big, but it might not be big enough.

Lakers @ Knicks

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY

NYK -4.5 (-110)
Moneyline
LAL +160 / NYK -190

Welcome to the inaugural Sunday Night Basketball on TNT, and the league couldn't have picked a better matchup. The Lakers bring Luka Doncic to the world's most famous arena, and the Slovenian sensation has been absolutely electric since arriving via the February 2025 blockbuster trade from the Lakers roster (post-trade)is averaging a league-best 33.7 points per game this season, and his combination with LeBron James has given Los Angeles an offensive firepower that few teams can match. The Lakers at 30-17 are legitimate title contenders in the Western Conference, and their star-powered backcourt makes them a nightmare matchup for anyone.

New York at 31-16 has been exceptional at Madison Square Garden, posting a 19-5 home record that ranks among the best in the league. Jalen Brunson continues to play at an All-NBA level, averaging 27.8 points and 6.3 assists while shooting 39% from three. Karl-Anthony Towns has been everything they hoped for when they acquired him, providing 21.4 points and 12.1 rebounds per game with floor-spacing ability that opens up driving lanes for Brunson. The Knicks' defense ranks eighth in efficiency, and they've held opponents under 108 points in 12 of their last 15 home games.

This is a genuine marquee matchup between two playoff-caliber teams with star power on both sides. The 4.5-point spread reflects New York's home court advantage, but the Lakers have the best player on the floor in Luka Doncic. Expect a back-and-forth affair with multiple lead changes in the fourth quarter. Brunson vs. Doncic is the headliner, but LeBron's ability to impact winning in big moments could be the difference. This game should come down to the final possessions, making the 4.5 number feel about right for a pick'em situation at the Garden.

The total of 224.5 feels slightly low given both teams' offensive capabilities. The Lakers rank sixth in offensive efficiency at 117.3 points per 100 possessions, while the Knicks sit at 114.8. Both teams move the ball well and can get hot from three-point range. In a nationally televised showcase, expect both sides to bring their A-game offensively. The atmosphere at MSG will be electric, and the stars will deliver.

Game 3
ESPN

Thunder @ Nuggets

Sunday, 9:30 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Spread
OKC -4.5 (-110)
Moneyline
OKC -180 / DEN +155
Total
O/U 227.5

The defending NBA champions travel to the Mile High City, but they'll be without Jalen Williams, who is OUT with a hip injury. That's a significant loss for Oklahoma City, as Williams has emerged as their second-best player this season, averaging 21.8 points, 5.9 rebounds, and 5.2 assists while providing elite perimeter defense. Without him, the Thunder's offensive burden falls even more heavily on Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, who has been phenomenal at 31.2 points per game. OKC at 37-10 remains the best team in the Western Conference, but their depth will be tested in the altitude.

Denver at 32-16 has Nikola Jokic playing at an MVP level once again. The Serbian big man is averaging 27.8 points, 13.1 rebounds, and 9.6 assists per game, and his court vision continues to unlock Denver's entire offensive system. The Nuggets are 19-5 at home this season, and the altitude factor at Ball Arena is real. Visiting teams often fade in the fourth quarter, and Denver's experience playing at elevation gives them a significant advantage in close games. Jamal Murray has been inconsistent, but Michael Porter Jr. has provided reliable scoring off the bench.

The Thunder being 4.5-point road favorites against a quality Denver team tells you how much respect the defending champions have earned. OKC's defense ranks first in the league in efficiency at 105.8 points allowed per 100 possessions, and they've held opponents under 110 points in 28 of their 47 games. Without Williams, they'll need Chet Holmgren to step up defensively against Jokic, which is a massive ask for the sophomore big man. Holmgren's shot-blocking ability will be tested by Jokic's array of floaters and hook shots.

This game should be a chess match between two of the league's best coaches. Mark Daigneault has done exceptional work developing OKC's young core into a championship-caliber team, while Michael Malone knows exactly how to maximize Jokic's brilliance. The altitude, the Williams injury, and Denver's home dominance make this closer than the line suggests. Expect a grind-it-out fourth quarter where execution and star power will decide the outcome.
