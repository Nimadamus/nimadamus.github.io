There's something deeply poetic about seeing the Florida Panthers, back-to-back Stanley Cup champions in 2024 and 2025, sitting at 77 points and already eliminated from playoff contention. The salary cap, injuries, and the natural regression that comes after two championship runs have taken their toll, and Florida at 37-36-3 looks nothing like the team that hoisted the Cup last June.

Montreal has been one of the feel-good stories in the Eastern Conference this season, and the Canadiens have turned Centre Bell back into a fortress. The -218 moneyline suggests the market expects Montreal to handle this one comfortably, and given the Panthers' current state, that's a reasonable expectation. The puck line at MTL -1.5 (+114) is the most interesting angle here because it's essentially asking whether Montreal can win by two or more goals, and given the talent gap between these two rosters right now, that's not an unreasonable proposition. Florida has been playing with significantly less intensity since their elimination became official, and there's a tangible difference between a team fighting for something and a team just trying to get through the final week without anyone getting hurt. Montreal has everything to play for, and Centre Bell on a Tuesday night in April with playoff implications hovering over every shift is going to be a very difficult environment for a team that's already checked out mentally.
