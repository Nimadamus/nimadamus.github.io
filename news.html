Brazil Launches Regulated Sports Betting Market, Bet365 & Betano Go Live

2:01 AM, July 25, 2025

After years of anticipation, Brazil has officially launched its fully regulated sports betting market as of July 25, 2025. The move immediately transforms the online gambling landscape in Latin America's largest economy.

The Ministry of Finance has already issued its first licenses to a select group of top-tier operators, including industry giants Bet365, Betano, and the locally popular PixBet. Analysts project the new market could generate over $3 billion in annual revenue.

The new Brazil sports betting regulation establishes a 12% tax on gross gaming revenue (GGR) and enforces strict rules for advertising and responsible gaming. Brazilian bettors can now legally wager on football, MMA, and other global sports without resorting to gray-market websites.

Thailand Designates Poker as an Official Sport to Boost Tourism

2:40 AM, July 24, 2025

In a landmark decision, the Sports Authority of Thailand (SAT) has officially recognized poker as a sport. The move is part of a strategic effort to attract major international poker tournaments and enhance the country's sports tourism profile.

Officials were clear, however, that the designation comes with a critical rule: poker must be treated purely as a game of skill and strategy. To align with national laws, any association with gambling is strictly forbidden.

Alongside poker, American football was also granted official sport status, a timely decision as flag football prepares for its debut at the LA 2028 Summer Olympics.

ACC Joins SEC, Big Ten in Mandating College Sports Injury Reports

2:25 AM, July 23, 2025

In a major move to address the pressure of legal sports betting, the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) is implementing a new mandatory injury report policy for all teams starting this fall.

Following a trend set by the SEC and Big Ten, the new **ACC player availability rules** will require teams to release official reports the day before a game and again two hours before kickoff. The goal is to increase transparency and protect student-athletes from harassment from those seeking insider betting information.

By standardizing injury reporting, the ACC aims to level the playing field for bettors and reduce the speculation that can lead to toxic online behavior directed at players.

California Sports Betting Hopes Dashed as 2026 Ballot Measure Fails

2:47 PM, July 20, 2025

Hopes for legal sports betting in California have been delayed until at least 2028, after it was confirmed that no new measure will appear on the 2026 ballot. This comes as a major blow to bettors and operators in what is potentially the country's most valuable market.

Deep divisions between tribal gaming leaders and commercial sportsbooks like DraftKings and FanDuel remain the primary obstacle, preventing the unified backing needed for a successful voter initiative after the failed measures of 2022.

Opinion: Is Sports Betting Replacing the Soul of the Game?

5:33 AM, July 20, 2025

There's a growing feeling among longtime sports fans that the explosion of sports betting is changing what it means to watch a game. The focus is shifting from team loyalty and the drama of the final score to the outcome of individual player props and micro-bets.

Nowhere is this more apparent than in the NBA, where the viewing experience can feel more like watching a stock market than a basketball game. Fans are often more invested in cashing a parlay than in whether their team wins or loses. This shift, combined with scandals like the Tim Donaghy affair and a three-point heavy, homogenous style of play, has led many to feel the sport's soul is being replaced by spreadsheets and algorithms.

When loyalty to a team is replaced by loyalty to a bet, the emotional connection that underpins fandom begins to erode, leaving one to wonder what we're really cheering for.

MLB Officials Voice Concerns Over In-Game Betting's Impact

8:21 PM, July 17, 2025

While fans and sportsbooks have embraced the rise of in-game betting, some officials within Major League Baseball are growing concerned about its effect on the integrity of the viewing experience.

According to insiders, the fear is that the constant injection of live odds into broadcasts is changing the game's narrative. Instead of focusing on the sport, every pitch and play is scrutinized through a gambling lens, putting undue pressure on players and umpires. While MLB isn't planning a retreat from its betting partnerships, there is a push to find a better balance where "some parts of the game should still be sacred."
