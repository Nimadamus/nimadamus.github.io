Saturday ABC Doubleheader

NBA February 7, 2026

A two-game Saturday ABC doubleheader kicks off with the Houston Rockets visiting the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder in a Western Conference heavyweight clash, followed by the Golden State Warriors traveling to Los Angeles to face the Lakers. Both games are reshaped by staggering injury reports: SGA, Jalen Williams, Stephen Curry, Jimmy Butler, and Luka Doncic are all sidelined, turning these marquee matchups into fascinating tests of organizational depth.

Rockets @ Thunder

Saturday, 3:30 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK
Spread
OKC -4.5 (-110)
Moneyline
HOU +136 / OKC -165
Total
O/U 214.5

This is one of the most intriguing games of the entire NBA season, and it has nothing to do with the stars who will be on the court. It's about the ones who won't be. Oklahoma City at 40-12 is missing its entire offensive engine: Shai Gilgeous-Alexander (abdominal strain, out until after the All-Star break) and Jalen Williams (right hamstring strain, missed 8+ games) are both sidelined. SGA has been on an absolute tear this season, averaging 31.8 points, 6.4 assists, and 4.5 rebounds per game while riding a 121-game streak of scoring 20 or more points, the second-longest in NBA history behind only Wilt Chamberlain. Williams was averaging nearly 22 points per game before going down. Ajay Mitchell is also out with an abdominal strain. That's three of the Thunder's most important offensive creators watching from the sideline in street clothes.

Here's the thing though: Oklahoma City is still the defending NBA champion, and the culture Mark Daigneault has built runs deeper than any two players. They opened the season 24-1, tying the 2015-16 Warriors for the best 25-game start in NBA history, and even without their stars, the Thunder's defensive identity hasn't wavered. This is a team that will fight you for every possession, contest every shot, and make you earn everything at the rim. The 4.5-point home spread tells you that the market still respects OKC's depth and home-court advantage even in these dire circumstances. Chet Holmgren, Lu Dort, and Isaiah Hartenstein will need to carry an enormous load, but these are players built for exactly this kind of responsibility.

Houston at 31-19 has been one of the league's pleasant surprises all season, and Kevin Durant has looked locked in since returning from his ankle injury on February 4. Durant dropped 31 points against Charlotte on Thursday night, and his season averages of 25.9 points, 5.4 rebounds, and 4.5 assists on 50.8% shooting and 39.9% from three are vintage KD. The Rockets have the kind of two-way roster that can punish a shorthanded opponent without mercy, and this might be the best possible time for Houston to steal a road win at Paycom Center. Ime Udoka's squad has been building toward something special, and a statement victory over the defending champs, even without SGA, would resonate loudly through the Western Conference standings.

The 214.5 total is notably low for a game featuring two of the West's best teams, but it makes perfect sense given the context. Without SGA and Williams, Oklahoma City's offensive ceiling takes a massive hit, and the Thunder will likely lean even harder into their grinding, defensive-oriented style. Houston isn't exactly a run-and-gun outfit either, preferring to win through half-court execution and defensive toughness. Expect a physical, playoff-style game where every possession matters. The question isn't whether OKC can compete without its stars, it's whether their supporting cast can generate enough offense to cover 4.5 points against a Rockets team that smells blood in the water.

Warriors @ Lakers

Moneyline

The Warriors and Lakers have produced some of the most electric regular-season basketball of the past decade, but Saturday night's edition at Crypto.com Arena looks dramatically different from the star-laden matchups we've grown accustomed to. Golden State at 28-24 is absolutely decimated by injuries, and the headline says it all: Stephen Curry (right patellofemoral pain), Jimmy Butler (torn right ACL, season-ending), and Seth Curry (left sciatic nerve) are all out. That's three of the team's most important offensive players sidelined simultaneously. The Warriors are 4-8 without Curry this season, and adding Butler's absence on top of that strips this roster of its top three scorers. Brandon Podziemski is listed as probable with an illness, but even if he plays, Golden State is going to war with a skeleton crew.

Let's put the Butler situation in proper context. He was acquired to be the missing piece, the veteran closer who could pair with Curry to make a legitimate championship push. Before tearing his right ACL, Butler was averaging 20 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 4.9 assists per game, exactly the kind of production Golden State needed. That's gone now, for the entire season, and it fundamentally alters everything about what the Warriors are trying to accomplish. This team went from fringe contender to fighting for its playoff life in the span of a single injury report.

Los Angeles at 30-19 has its own star absence to deal with. Luka Doncic is out with a left hamstring strain, and when your leading scorer at 32.8 points per game (tops in the entire NBA), 7.8 rebounds, and 8.6 assists isn't available, it changes everything about how you operate offensively. But here's where the Lakers have a significant advantage: LeBron James is playing. LeBron at 21.8 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 6.7 assists per game in his 23rd season remains one of the most reliable floor-raisers in basketball history. When Doncic sits, LeBron becomes the unquestioned focal point, and the Lakers have enough supporting talent to weather the absence. Los Angeles sits sixth in the West and first in the Pacific Division, and their depth has been a quiet strength all season.

The 3.5-point spread feels reasonable when you weigh both injury situations against each other. The Lakers are missing their best player, but the Warriors are missing their three best players. That gap in available talent is significant, especially at Crypto.com Arena where the Lakers have been strong this season. The 225.5 total is elevated for two teams dealing with major absences, but both sides will likely try to push pace to compensate for the scoring voids in their lineups. Without Curry's gravity and Butler's shot creation on one side, and without Doncic's one-man offense on the other, this game could come down to which team's role players step up in the brightest spotlight. LeBron James has been winning games like this for two decades, and that experience edge could be the difference in a primetime ABC showdown.
