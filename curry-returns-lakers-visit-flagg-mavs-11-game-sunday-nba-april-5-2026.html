11-Game Sunday Slate

NBA Previews Today

The biggest Sunday slate of the season brings 11 games headlined by Stephen Curry's long-awaited return from a two-month knee injury as the Warriors host the Rockets on NBC, LeBron James visiting Cooper Flagg one night after the 19-year-old's historic 51-point explosion in Dallas, a 17.5-point spread in Cleveland, and the biggest number of the entire season with OKC laying 22.5 against Utah. Add Giannis vs. Ja in Milwaukee and a pair of ROY candidates squaring off across different games, and you've got a Sunday loaded from tip to buzzer.

Raptors @ Celtics

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Spread
BOS -10.5 (-108)
Moneyline
TOR +410 / BOS -550
Total
O/U 219.5

Boston is laying double digits at home, and if you told someone that before the season they'd have laughed. But the Celtics without Jayson Tatum for the majority of this campaign have somehow managed to hold down the second seed in the East, and now that Tatum is back from his Achilles injury and working his way toward full game shape, this team is starting to look genuinely terrifying again. Tatum has been averaging 21.9 points, 10.9 rebounds, and 5.5 assists over his last 10 games since returning in early March, and while the minutes restriction is still in play for back-to-backs, his impact on the court has been immediate. The Celtics have gone 3-1 since his return, and the defense, which held things together all season without him, now has an extra All-NBA wing creating havoc on the perimeter.

Toronto comes in at 42-33 and firmly in the playoff picture, which is a testament to how well Brandon Ingram has fit since his trade from the Pelicans. The Raptors have been a solid, if unspectacular, team all year, but a 10.5-point spread on the road against a team with championship aspirations is a steep climb. Toronto's offense ranks in the middle of the pack, and they don't have the kind of isolation creators who can consistently manufacture buckets against elite half-court defenses. The Celtics' length and switching ability on the perimeter make life miserable for teams that rely on dribble penetration, and Toronto's inconsistency from three has been their Achilles' heel on the road this season.

The 219.5 total is one of the lowest on the board today, and that's a reflection of both Boston's suffocating defensive identity and the pace these teams tend to play. The Celtics slow things down in the half court, methodically running sets and forcing opponents to grind through every possession. Toronto isn't a fast-paced team either, and when you combine two mid-tier pace teams with a Boston defense that ranks in the top five in defensive rating, you get a game that's more likely to be decided by stops than scoring explosions.

Boston's home court has been a fortress all season, and the crowd at TD Garden knows these final few regular season games are about building momentum for a playoff run. With Tatum's minutes gradually increasing and Jaylen Brown continuing to be the rock he's been all year, the Celtics are starting to look like the team that won the championship just two years ago. Toronto is a good team, but this is a bad matchup in a bad building for them.

Wizards @ Nets

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Barclays Center, Brooklyn, NY
Spread
BKN -3.5
Moneyline
WSH +142 / BKN -170
Total
O/U 230.5

Welcome to the tank bowl. Washington at 17-59 and Brooklyn at 18-58 are the two worst records in the NBA, and this game is essentially a battle for draft lottery positioning disguised as a professional basketball contest. Both of these franchises have been in full teardown mode for months, playing young guys extended minutes and treating every game like a glorified Summer League audition. The Wizards have lost 20 of their last 21, and without Trae Young in the lineup for stretches this season, they've been historically bad. Brooklyn isn't much better, having stumbled through a season defined by its own roster turnover and developmental growing pains.

The 3.5-point spread suggests the market gives Brooklyn a slight edge at home, and that probably comes down to the Nets' young core having shown marginally more cohesion in recent weeks than Washington's revolving door of lineups. Brooklyn has at least established some identity with its developing players, while the Wizards have looked lost offensively for most of the second half of the season. Neither team plays any meaningful defense, which is why the 230.5 total is one of the higher numbers on the board despite neither offense being particularly efficient. These are two teams that play fast, turn it over, and give up easy transition buckets.

For bettors, the intrigue here is purely in the total. When two bad teams with porous defenses meet, the scoring can get wild in both directions. You'll see stretches where both teams can't miss and stretches where neither team can execute a simple pick-and-roll. The variance in these tank matchups is enormous, which makes the total a coin flip and the spread almost impossible to handicap with any conviction. This is a game where the box score will look normal but the actual basketball will make your eyes bleed.

If there's a silver lining, it's the chance to evaluate young talent. Both rosters are littered with guys who are auditioning for roles next season, and scouts will be paying close attention to how the young players handle late-season pressure-free minutes. For Washington, it's about seeing whether any of their recent draft picks can become foundational pieces. For Brooklyn, the question is whether their rebuild has any sort of direction or whether this is just another year of treading water at the bottom of the standings.

Spread

PHX -520 / CHI +390

Phoenix rolls into Chicago as massive double-digit road favorites, and the Suns are playing with the kind of urgency that comes from knowing every game matters for playoff seeding down the stretch. Devin Booker has been sensational lately, averaging 27.5 points per game over his last 10 contests while shooting efficiently from all three levels. The Suns have the talent to bury teams when Booker gets going, and Chicago's defense has been porous enough to let far less talented teams have their way at the United Center this season.

The Bulls are in the midst of a rebuilding year that has been painful for their loyal fanbase. Chicago briefly showed life in early March when they strung together a couple of wins, including a gutsy 105-103 victory over Phoenix on March 5 when Collin Sexton poured in 30 points. But those moments have been the exception rather than the rule. The Bulls' roster lacks a true franchise centerpiece, and while Sexton has been a useful scorer, he's not the kind of player who can single-handedly keep you competitive against teams like the Suns. The defense has been one of the worst in the league, and when you can't get stops, 10.5-point spreads are going to keep showing up next to your name.

The 237.5 total is juiced because of the mismatch in talent and Chicago's inability to get stops. Phoenix's offense when it's humming ranks among the best in basketball, and Booker's ability to create for himself and others makes the Suns extremely difficult to slow down when they're clicking. Chicago will score some points because they play fast and shoot a lot of threes, but they're going to need to have one of their best offensive games of the season to keep this competitive. The Suns' supporting cast around Booker has been more consistent recently, and that depth advantage shows up in the third and fourth quarters when the Bulls' bench can't keep pace.

This is the kind of game where Phoenix can cruise to a 15-point lead by halftime and then coast through the second half with their starters getting rest ahead of more meaningful games. The Suns don't have the luxury of losing these games to tanking teams, not with the Western Conference playoff race as tight as it is. For Chicago, the only real motivation is playing spoiler, and this Bulls team hasn't shown it has the competitive fire to do that consistently against superior opponents.

Spread
MIL -5.5
Moneyline

This has the potential to be one of the more fascinating individual matchups on the board, even if both teams have seen better days. Giannis Antetokounmpo has been dealing with a knee injury that's kept him out of several recent games, but if the Greek Freak is available today, this becomes a must-watch battle between two of the most explosive athletes the league has ever produced. Giannis is averaging 27.6 points, 9.8 rebounds, and 5.4 assists this season, and when he's healthy and locked in, there is simply nobody in basketball who can replicate what he does driving to the rim. The question is whether that knee allows him to be that version of himself today.

Memphis comes in with Ja Morant done for the season due to a UCL sprain in his left elbow, and that absence has gutted the Grizzlies' ceiling as a competitive team. In just 20 games this year, Morant averaged 19.5 points and 8.1 assists, showing flashes of the brilliance that makes him one of the most dynamic point guards in basketball. Without him, Memphis has relied on a committee approach that lacks the explosiveness and creativity Ja brings every night. The Grizzlies have the young talent to compete on any given night, but sustaining that effort over 48 minutes against a team with Giannis-level talent is a different story entirely.

Milwaukee's season has been a massive disappointment, having lost 15 of their last 19 games and falling out of playoff contention entirely. The Bucks' roster construction around Giannis has failed to produce the contender that ownership and fans expected, and the coaching staff has been under fire for months. Still, a 5.5-point spread at home suggests the market respects what Giannis can do when he plays, and Milwaukee's home court has been one of the few places they've shown any fight during this dismal stretch. Fiserv Forum can still get loud when Giannis is attacking, and the Bucks' defense has shown flashes of competence even as the season has spiraled.

The 227.5 total is moderate, and that reflects the pace both teams play at when their stars are compromised by injury. Memphis without Morant tends to slow things down and grind through half-court sets, while Milwaukee without a healthy Giannis looks like a team that doesn't know where to go on offense. If Giannis plays and is even 80% of himself, the Bucks should have enough firepower to cover this number at home. But there's real risk in laying 5.5 with a team that has been this bad for this long, regardless of the name on the back of the jersey.

Pacers @ Cavaliers

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH
Spread
CLE -17.5
Moneyline
IND +1300 / CLE -2800
Total
O/U 240.5

A 17.5-point spread is rare in any sport, and in the NBA it borders on absurd, but the gap between these two teams right now is genuinely that wide. Cleveland is 48-29 and fighting for playoff positioning in the deep Eastern Conference, while Indiana has endured one of the most devastating seasons in franchise history at roughly 18-59. The Pacers have suffered through two of the longest losing streaks in their history this year, a 13-game skid from December to January and a 16-game nightmare from February to March, and the root cause of all of it traces back to one catastrophic moment: Tyrese Haliburton's torn Achilles in Game 7 of last year's Finals. Without their franchise point guard, Indiana has been an entirely different team, and not in any good way.

Cleveland, meanwhile, has been one of the more balanced and dangerous teams in the Eastern Conference all season. Donovan Mitchell continues to be one of the most lethal scorers in the league, and the Cavaliers' supporting cast has provided the kind of depth and consistency that keeps them competitive against anyone. Cleveland's defense has been elite at home, and when you combine that with a Pacers offense that struggles to create quality looks without Haliburton running the show, you get a recipe for a blowout. The Cavaliers want to lock down the best possible seed before the playoffs, and games like this are where they can build confidence and rhythm while simultaneously resting starters in the fourth quarter.

The 240.5 total is the highest on the board for good reason. Cleveland plays at a brisk pace, and their offense generates open looks in transition and in the half court with equal efficiency. Indiana, despite being terrible defensively, still has young players who can score in bunches, and the Pacers' fast pace means possessions fly by in a hurry. Even in blowouts, the combined scoring in Pacers games tends to be high because Indiana doesn't play the kind of disciplined half-court defense that keeps totals low. When the Cavaliers are running in transition against a team that can't get back, the points pile up fast.

For Indiana, this season has been about developing young talent and waiting for Haliburton's eventual return next year. Aaron Nesmith, Obi Toppin, and Ben Sheppard have all missed significant time with their own injuries, compounding the misery of losing their star player. The Pacers went to the Finals just last June, and now they're laying down 17.5-point spreads. That's how quickly things can change in the NBA when your franchise player goes down. Cleveland should cruise here, and the only real question is whether the margin of victory justifies a number this large.

Hornets @ Timberwolves

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Target Center, Minneapolis, MN
Spread
CHA -1.5
Moneyline
CHA -125 / MIN +105
Total
O/U 229.5

Here's an interesting wrinkle: the Charlotte Hornets are road favorites in Minnesota. That's not something you see every day, and it speaks volumes about the trajectories of these two franchises this season. Charlotte is 42-36 and in the thick of the Eastern Conference playoff race, fueled largely by the sensational rookie season of Kon Knueppel, who recently set the Hornets' franchise record for three-pointers in a season with 261, surpassing Kemba Walker's mark from 2018-19. Knueppel is averaging 18.8 points, 5.4 rebounds, and 3.4 assists while shooting 43.1% from deep, and his ability to space the floor and create from the wing has completely transformed Charlotte's offense into something legitimate.

Minnesota at 46-31 has the better record, but the Timberwolves must be dealing with something significant on the injury front for the market to have them as home underdogs against a team with a worse record. Minnesota's defense has been their calling card all season, anchored by one of the best rim-protecting frontcourts in the league. But defense alone doesn't win you games when your offense goes cold, and the Wolves' inconsistency on that end of the floor has been the story of their season. They'll have stretches where they look like a genuine contender and stretches where they can't buy a bucket, and that unpredictability is reflected in a line that doesn't give them much respect at home.

Knueppel's Rookie of the Year case has been building all season long, and games like this are where he can separate himself. He leads all rookies in total points with 1,430 and has five 30-point games, making him one of the most productive first-year players the league has seen in a decade. His shooting stretches defenses in ways Charlotte hasn't had since prime Kemba, and the spacing he creates opens up driving lanes for the rest of the roster. The Hornets' offense with Knueppel on the floor is a completely different machine than when he sits, and that kind of transformative impact is exactly what the ROY award is meant to recognize.

The 229.5 total suggests a moderate-paced game, which makes sense given Minnesota's defensive identity. The Wolves want to slow things down and turn every game into a grind, and Charlotte is happy to let the game flow at whatever pace Knueppel can create within. This feels like a game that stays close throughout, with neither team able to pull away for extended stretches. The 1.5-point spread is essentially a coin flip with a slight lean toward Charlotte, and in a game this tight, the team with the best individual creator might be the one that comes out on top in crunch time.

Orlando visits New Orleans riding high after a thrilling 138-127 win over Dallas on Friday night, a game where the Magic had to survive Cooper Flagg's historic 51-point explosion to escape with the victory. That kind of win, where your team is tested by an individual performance for the ages and still finds a way to close it out, can galvanize a locker room heading into the final stretch of the regular season. The Magic are 41-36 and fighting for their playoff lives in the Eastern Conference, and every game from here on out is essentially a playoff atmosphere for a team that can't afford to drop games to sub-.500 opponents.

New Orleans at 25-53 has been one of the league's more disappointing teams this season. The Pelicans' roster was supposed to be competitive, but injuries and inconsistency have turned this into a lost year in the Big Easy. Without a true franchise cornerstone after the Brandon Ingram trade sent their best forward to Toronto, the Pelicans have lacked offensive identity for most of the season. The young guys are getting their minutes, but there's a significant gap between playing developmental basketball and winning basketball, and New Orleans has been firmly in the former category since the All-Star break.

Orlando's defense has been their bread and butter all season, and they have the kind of length and athleticism on the perimeter that can turn a Pelicans offense with limited shot creation into a disaster. The Magic's ability to switch across positions and contest shots without fouling has been one of the reasons they've stayed in the playoff hunt despite some offensive limitations of their own. When they're locked in defensively and running in transition off turnovers, Orlando can blow games open quickly, and New Orleans' propensity for careless turnovers plays right into the Magic's hands.

The 235.5 total is interesting because Orlando tends to play in lower-scoring affairs while New Orleans' porous defense can inflate numbers against them. The Magic want this to be a grind-it-out affair in the low 100s, and if their defense shows up the way it has for most of the season, that's exactly what this will be. But the Pelicans' defense is bad enough that Orlando's offense could have an easier night than usual, which could push the combined score higher. At 4.5 points, this feels like a comfortable number for the Magic unless they come out flat after the emotional high of the Flagg game.

Twenty-two and a half points. Let that sink in. This is the biggest spread of the entire 2025-26 NBA season, and it's a testament to the chasm that separates the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder from the rebuilding Utah Jazz. OKC is 61-16 and has been the most dominant team in basketball from wire to wire, becoming the first franchise to hit 60 wins in back-to-back seasons. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is the runaway MVP favorite after receiving 88 of 100 first-place votes in ESPN's latest straw poll, and his 31.6 points per game on 55.3% shooting has been the engine behind the league's best record. The Thunder don't just beat teams. They demolish them. Their most recent victim was the Lakers, who they annihilated by 43 points on Thursday night.

Utah at 21-52 has been one of the league's worst teams, exactly as the front office designed it. The Jazz traded away every meaningful veteran piece over the last two seasons and are now a roster full of young players and minimum-contract guys learning how to play NBA basketball. The problem is that learning comes with a lot of losing, and when you walk into Paycom Center against the most talented team in the sport, the education gets expensive in a hurry. The Jazz lost to Houston by 34 points just two days ago, and there's nothing in their recent performance to suggest they can keep this game within shouting distance.

Here's the thing about 22.5-point spreads: they almost feel like a dare. The market is essentially saying the Thunder are going to win by four touchdowns, and while that sounds absurd, OKC's margin of victory in their wins has been staggering this season. Gilgeous-Alexander has the ability to put up 30 in three quarters and sit the entire fourth, and the Thunder's depth is so ridiculous that their second unit can outplay most team's starters. The question isn't whether OKC wins. It's whether they win by enough to cover a number that would be eye-popping in any sport, let alone a basketball game.

The 239.5 total reflects OKC's offensive firepower and the expectation that even with garbage time, a lot of points are going to be scored. The Thunder play fast, they share the ball beautifully, and their transition game is the best in basketball. Utah's defense is one of the worst in the league, and their inability to protect the rim or contest perimeter shots means OKC is going to get whatever they want on every possession. This has blowout written all over it, and the only drama will be whether the Thunder care enough to keep pushing the accelerator once they're up 30 in the third quarter.

This is going to be the most-watched game of the night, and for good reason. LeBron James, who just dropped 42 points and 12 assists on Cleveland a few days ago, walks into American Airlines Center to face Cooper Flagg one night after the 19-year-old became the youngest player in NBA history to score 50 points in a game. Flagg's 51-point masterpiece against Orlando on Friday, shooting 19-of-30 from the field and 6-of-9 from three, was the kind of performance that transcends a regular season game and enters the realm of sports history. He joined Michael Jordan as the only player with multiple 45-point games as a rookie, and he's still a teenager. The passing of the torch narratives are going to be unavoidable tonight, and rightfully so.

But here's the reality for the Lakers: they're in trouble. LA is 50-27 and sitting third in the Western Conference, but they'll be without both Luka Doncic, who is done for the regular season with a hamstring strain, and Austin Reaves, who suffered a Grade 2 oblique muscle strain against OKC on Thursday and is out for the rest of the regular season as well. That means LeBron, who is averaging 20.6 points, 6.0 rebounds, and 6.9 assists at age 41, is going to have to carry a significantly heavier load tonight. The Lakers got destroyed by 43 points in their last game without Reaves, and the supporting cast around LeBron has looked shaky when the other two creators are unavailable.

Dallas at 24-53 has nothing to play for in the standings, but Flagg has everything to play for individually. His Rookie of the Year campaign is neck-and-neck with Charlotte's Kon Knueppel, and another monster performance on national television against LeBron James would be the kind of signature moment that tips the scales in his favor. Flagg is averaging 20.8 points, 6.6 rebounds, and 4.5 assists on the season, and if those numbers hold through the final five games, he'll become just the fourth rookie ever to average 20-6-4, joining Larry Bird, Michael Jordan, and Luka Doncic. That's rarefied air for a 19-year-old on the worst team in the West.

The 2.5-point spread feels small given the talent gap between these rosters, but the Lakers' injury situation makes them vulnerable. Dallas has lost 14 straight home games, which is a franchise-worst streak, but Flagg's recent surge has injected genuine energy into a building that had given up on this season months ago. LeBron is going to get his, he always does, but can the rest of this depleted Lakers roster hold up against a young Mavericks team that just put up 127 points the night before? The total of 232.5 suggests a high-scoring affair, and with neither team's defense positioned to be elite tonight, expect a fun, wide-open game that could go either way in the final minutes.

The Clippers at 39-38 are fighting for their playoff lives in the Western Conference, sitting in the 8th seed and trying to avoid the play-in tournament. Every game carries massive weight for a team that has invested heavily in its current roster, and dropping a game to the league's worst team would be a devastating blow to their postseason aspirations. The Clippers have the defensive versatility and offensive balance to handle Sacramento without breaking a sweat, and their urgency provides an edge that the Kings simply can't match at this point of the season.

The 11.5-point spread is large, but it's justified by the massive gap in motivation and talent between these two teams. The Clippers need this game, and Sacramento couldn't care less about winning it. The Clippers' depth advantage is enormous, and their bench has been one of the more productive units in the league this season.

If LA can impose their half-court style on this game, the total might struggle to get over. Stop everything. Stephen Curry is back. After missing the last 27 games with patellofemoral pain syndrome in his right knee, the greatest shooter in basketball history is expected to be cleared for Sunday's nationally televised showdown against Kevin Durant and the Houston Rockets. Curry hasn't played since January 30, and the Warriors have been clinging to the 10th seed and a play-in spot with a 36-40 record, a campaign that has fallen far short of the 48-34 mark they posted last year. "Feels great, it's been a long process, longer than I thought," Curry said after scrimmaging this week. "I'm ready to get back out there." With just five games remaining in the regular season, the timing couldn't be more critical for a Golden State team that desperately needs its franchise cornerstone.

And then there's the storyline that makes this game even more electric: Kevin Durant returning to Chase Center. KD, now in his first season with the Rockets after the blockbuster trade from Phoenix last July, has been everything Houston hoped for and more. He's averaging 26.1 points on 50%+ shooting, and he scored 25 points on 8-of-12 shooting in a dominant 140-106 blowout of Utah just two days ago. The Rockets are 48-29, riding a five-game winning streak, and clinched their playoff spot when Phoenix lost to Charlotte on April 2. This is a Houston team that is rolling at exactly the right time, and Durant's calm, efficient dominance has been the catalyst for everything they've accomplished.

The emotional weight of this game is off the charts. Curry and Durant won two championships together at this very arena, and now they'll face each other with enormous stakes on both sides. Houston needs wins to secure the best possible playoff seed, while Golden State needs wins just to stay in the play-in picture. Curry coming back from injury against his former teammate in a nationally televised Sunday night game is the kind of narrative the NBA dreams about, and Chase Center is going to be absolutely rocking. The Warriors' crowd will welcome Curry back with a roar that you'll be able to hear across the Bay, and that energy might be enough to turn a 36-win team into a dangerous opponent for one night.

The 3.5-point spread favoring Houston reflects the reality that the Rockets are the significantly better team, but it also accounts for the Curry factor and the home-court adrenaline that comes with a moment this big. There's no telling how Curry will look after two months away. He could be rusty, on a minutes restriction, and visibly working his way back into game shape. Or he could be Stephen Curry, the guy who hits five threes in a quarter and makes the impossible look routine. The 225.5 total is the lowest of the day, suggesting a game that might be more about defensive intensity and half-court execution than scoring fireworks. But when Curry and Durant are on the same court, all bets on pace and scoring projections go out the window.
