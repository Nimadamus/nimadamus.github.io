Friday's European football slate delivers four compelling matches from across the continent's top leagues. Brest vs Marseille

This is the match everyone in French football will be watching, and not just for the on-pitch action. Marseille just appointed Habib Beye as their new permanent manager on a one-and-a-half year deal after the stunning dismissal of Roberto De Zerbi following that catastrophic 5-0 loss to PSG. The 1.90 price on Marseille to win reflects their overall quality and league position (4th, 40 points), but there's enormous uncertainty around a squad that's been through managerial upheaval in the last nine days. In their only match under caretaker Jacques Abardonado, Marseille drew 2-2 with Strasbourg, with Mason Greenwood and Amine Gouiri finding the net. Now Beye inherits a talented but mentally fragile group traveling to Brittany for his first competitive match.

Mason Greenwood remains the most dangerous player on the pitch regardless of who's managing Marseille. His 14 goals and 4 assists in 21 Ligue 1 appearances lead the league's scoring charts, and his ability to create something from nothing is exactly what Marseille need during this period of instability. The problem for Marseille is that everything behind Greenwood is uncertain. De Zerbi's tactical system has been ripped out at the roots, Beye hasn't had time to install his own ideas, and the squad's confidence took a devastating hit from that PSG result. Marseille are 12 points off pace-setters Lens (52 points) in the title race, and their Champions League campaign has ended. The motivation question is real.

Brest (12th, 27 points) are the kind of opponent that can be absolutely lethal in this situation. They're three games unbeaten heading into this match, and the Stade Francis-Le Ble is a compact, hostile ground where visiting teams routinely struggle. Romain Del Castillo has been excellent with 6 league goals and 7 total goal involvements in 20 appearances, and Ludovic Ajorque provides a physical aerial threat that can disrupt even the best organized defenses. Both teams scoring feels like a realistic outcome here, because Marseille will likely create chances through Greenwood's individual quality, but their defensive organization under a brand-new manager could be all over the place.

Head-to-head history favors Marseille with 19 wins in 34 meetings, and they've beaten Brest in three straight including a 2-1 victory at Marseille in November. But at the Stade Francis-Le Ble specifically, Brest have won 7 of 15 home meetings compared to just 4 for Marseille. Factor in the managerial chaos, the travel, and Brest's recent form, and the home side has plenty working in their favor despite the market's lean toward Marseille. Whether the new manager bounce materializes immediately or takes time to develop is the central question of this match.

Friday night Bundesliga brings us a fascinating mid-table clash between two clubs heading in opposite directions. Mainz (14th, 21 points) were absolutely demolished 4-0 by Borussia Dortmund in their last outing, a result that pulled them dangerously close to the relegation playoff spot with just two points separating them from 16th place. The 2.10 home price reflects a team that needs to respond urgently, and historically Mainz have been a difficult team to beat at the MEWA Arena. Hamburg (9th, 25 points) arrive in much better form with a five-game unbeaten run (2W-3D), including a dramatic 3-2 victory over Union Berlin most recently.

Mainz's attacking threat revolves around Nadiem Amiri, who has been in sensational form with 9 goals in 19 appearances this season and 4 goals in his last 4 before the Dortmund humiliation. If there's one player capable of sparking a response from this Mainz side, it's Amiri. The problem is that Mainz are absolutely ravaged by injuries and suspensions, with Benedict Hollerbach, Armindo Sieb, Lennard Maloney, Robin Zentner, Stefan Bell, and Anthony Caci all out, and Silvan Widmer suspended. That's a significant chunk of their first-choice lineup missing.

Hamburg's Ransford-Yeboah Konigsdorffer has been in hot form with 3 goals in his last 4 outings, including a brace against Union Berlin. The visitors also bring momentum from that unbeaten streak, but away from home Hamburg have been inconsistent this season. In the reverse fixture earlier this campaign, Hamburg won 4-0 at home, but that was a completely different context. At the MEWA Arena specifically, Hamburg have won just 1 of their last 5 visits with 3 losses, which suggests Mainz's home advantage could be the deciding factor.

Six of Mainz's last seven matches have seen Over 2.5 goals, a trend that speaks to how open and entertaining their matches have been. This is a Mainz side that either blows teams away at home (they had a three-game winning streak before the Dortmund disaster) or gets blown away themselves. Hamburg will be happy to trade blows in an open game given their attacking form, and with two evenly matched sides meeting in a match where Mainz desperately need points, this could develop into one of the more entertaining contests on Friday's slate.

San Mames is one of the most iconic and intimidating venues in all of European football, but Athletic Bilbao (9th, 31 points) are not the force they should be this season. The 1.72 home price makes them clear favorites, and under normal circumstances that would be justified against 16th-placed Elche (18 points). But these are not normal circumstances. Nico Williams, Athletic's most dynamic attacker and one of La Liga's most exciting young talents, is out indefinitely with pubalgia, a chronic groin problem that could sideline him for 6-8 weeks and even threatens his World Cup participation. Losing Williams completely changes the profile of this Athletic attack.

Without Williams stretching defenses with his pace and directness, Athletic's offensive patterns become much more predictable. Gorka Guruzeta remains the primary goalscoring threat, but the supporting cast around him will need to step up in Williams' absence. Athletic's home form has actually been surprisingly poor this season, managing just 1.00 points per game at San Mames with a recent sequence of L-D-W-L. For a club that traditionally makes San Mames a fortress, that's alarming, and it opens the door for visiting teams to be competitive.

Elche are in a genuine relegation fight, sitting 16th with just 18 points from 24 matches. They come to Bilbao with a miserable run of D-L-L-L-D in their last 5 and an even worse away record of W-D-L-L-L. Alvaro Rodriguez (7 goals) and Rafa Mir (6 goals) provide their primary attacking threat, but Elche's overall quality is simply below what Athletic can field even without Williams. The 5.75 on an Elche win reflects just how steep the mountain is for the visitors.

One trend worth noting: Athletic have seen both teams score in a staggering 90% of their recent matches, and they haven't kept a single clean sheet in that run. Elche might be fighting for survival, but they do score goals with regularity. Athletic's inability to shut teams out, combined with their attacking quality even without Williams, sets up a match where both sides should find the net regardless of the final result. The question is whether Athletic's offensive depth is enough to overwhelm Elche's desperate defending or whether this becomes a tighter affair than the market expects.

If you're looking for desperation, look no further than Hellas Verona. Dead last in Serie A with a catastrophic 15 points from 25 matches (2W-9D-14L), Verona have the worst defense in the league at 43 goals conceded and are running out of time to save their season. They travel to the MAPEI Stadium to face a Sassuolo side (10th, 32 points) that won't be a pushover, and the situation gets even worse for the visitors. Their top scorer Orban (7 goals) is suspended for this match, and the injury list reads like a medical textbook with Suslov, Bernede, Gagliardini, Lovric, Lirola, and Valentini all unavailable.

Sassuolo's attacking trio of Andrea Pinamonti (5 goals), Domenico Berardi (5 goals), and Lauriente (4 goals) gives them multiple options to punish what will likely be a makeshift Verona defense. The hosts have their own absentees with Boloca and Pieragnolo injured plus Muharemovic and Matic suspended, but the drop-off in quality is far less significant than what Verona are dealing with. Sassuolo's home advantage and superior squad depth make them the clear favorites, and the real question is whether Verona can muster enough defensive resilience to keep this competitive.

The most recent meeting between these sides saw Sassuolo win 1-0 away at Verona in October, and the home advantage should make them even more comfortable this time around. The tactical dynamic is fascinating: Verona may look to park the bus and try to steal a point, but with their top scorer suspended and their defense shipping goals at nearly two per game, holding out for 90 minutes would require a performance far beyond what they've shown this season. Verona are staring at the very real prospect of finishing dead last in Serie A, and this match represents one of their last realistic opportunities to close the gap before the situation becomes insurmountable.

Lancashire derbies are always about more than just three points, and this one at Ewood Park carries genuine emotional weight for both sets of supporters. Blackburn (2.00) are the slight favorites at home, but Preston sit eight places higher in the Championship table and have been the more consistent side this season. The 3.30 draw and 3.85 on Preston suggest the market sees this as a competitive match that could easily go either way, and that's exactly what you'd expect from a rivalry fixture where form often goes out the window.

Lancashire derbies in the Championship are traditionally tight, physical affairs where defensive organization and set-piece quality often decide the outcome. Both teams know each other inside out, the players understand the intensity of the occasion, and there's rarely much space for attacking freedom in matches like this. Preston's more clinical finishing and stronger overall season give them an edge in terms of quality, but Blackburn's home advantage and the emotional lift of a derby could level the playing field.

Despite the derby intensity, both teams have shown enough attacking quality this season to suggest goals aren't out of the question on either side. At the same time, the typically cagey nature of this rivalry means neither team will be taking unnecessary risks going forward. A low-scoring affair with both sides finding the net at least once would be perfectly in line with the historical pattern of these Lancashire meetings. This is the kind of match where the atmosphere does half the talking and the football fills in the rest.
