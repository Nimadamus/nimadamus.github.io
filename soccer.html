Match of Day
Sky Sports

Tottenham vs Manchester City

Sunday, 4:30 PM GMT (11:30 AM ET) | Tottenham Hotspur Stadium
Tottenham +343 | Man City -147 | Total Goals O/U 3.0

Here's the situation Spurs find themselves in: they've somehow qualified for the Champions League knockout rounds with a top-8 finish in the league phase, yet they sit a miserable 14th in the Premier League table without a league win in 2026. That's the kind of schizophrenic season that gets managers fired, and Ange Postecoglou has to be feeling the heat as second-placed Manchester City roll into North London. The Cityzens have won 14 of 22 matches where they've been favored this season, including 11 wins in 15 games when installed as -137 favorites or greater. This is a team that simply takes care of business against inferior opposition.

The numbers tell a fascinating story about this matchup's likely texture. Tottenham have seen under 2.5 goals in 9 of their last 10 home matches against City, a stat that screams caution for anyone looking at the 3.0-goal total. Pep Guardiola's side doesn't need to blow out a struggling Spurs, they just need to control the game and take their chances. With their Goal Supremacy spread set at 0.85-1.05, the market expects City to win by approximately one goal, and given Tottenham's attacking woes in league play this calendar year, that feels about right.

What makes the -147 on City compelling is Spurs' fundamental inability to build from the back under pressure. Postecoglou's insistence on playing out from the goalkeeper has led to countless turnovers in dangerous areas this season, and there's no team in world football better at pressing and punishing those mistakes than Manchester City. The home crowd will be desperate for any positive result, but desperation doesn't stop Erling Haaland. City have covered against teams with similar profiles all season long, winning by comfortable margins when the opposition sits deep and by narrow margins when they press. Either way, they're finding ways to win.

The one concern for City backers is the congested fixture schedule. They're juggling Champions League, Premier League, and domestic cup commitments, and rotation could be a factor. But Guardiola has managed these situations masterfully for years, and his squad depth is arguably the best in world football. Look for City to control possession, limit Spurs to speculative long-range efforts, and grind out a professional victory that keeps the pressure on league leaders Arsenal.

Early Kick-Off
USA Network

Aston Villa vs Brentford

Sunday, 2:00 PM GMT (9:00 AM ET) | Villa Park, Birmingham

Aston Villa return to domestic action after their European exertions, hosting a Brentford side that's been quietly efficient this season. Villa's fixture list has been brutal with Champions League commitments adding to their workload, and fatigue could be a factor as they face Thomas Frank's well-organized Bees. The home side will be desperate for three points to maintain their European qualification push, but Brentford have proven time and again they can punch above their weight against bigger opposition.

This match was rescheduled due to Villa's participation in European competition on Thursday night, meaning they'll have less than 72 hours of recovery time before this clash. Unai Emery will need to rotate his squad, which could open opportunities for Brentford's compact defensive setup to frustrate the hosts. Ivan Toney has been in excellent form leading the Brentford line, and his ability to hold up play and bring others into the game will be crucial to the visitors' counterattacking hopes.

Villa Park has been a fortress this season, but the combination of fixture congestion and a tricky opponent makes this a potential banana skin for Emery's men. Brentford's away form suggests they won't come to Birmingham looking for a point, they'll press high and look to capitalize on any defensive lapses from tired Villa legs. The first goal will be crucial in dictating how this match unfolds.

Old Trafford
Peacock

Manchester United vs Fulham

Sunday, 2:00 PM GMT (9:00 AM ET) | Old Trafford, Manchester

Manchester United host Fulham at Old Trafford in what should be a fascinating tactical battle between two sides with contrasting styles. The Red Devils have shown flashes of their potential this season but have struggled for consistency, while Marco Silva's Fulham have established themselves as a genuine mid-table force who can make life difficult for anyone. United need three points here to keep their top-four aspirations alive, but Fulham won't make it easy.

The Cottagers' counter-attacking prowess will test United's defensive organization, and with Andreas Pereira returning to his former club, there's added spice to this encounter. Fulham have taken points from some unlikely places this season, and their away record suggests they won't be intimidated by the Theatre of Dreams. United's home form has been patchy at best, and the pressure from supporters is palpable every time they step onto the pitch.

Expect a cagey first half as both teams look to establish control. United's best route to victory lies through their wide players stretching Fulham's back line, while Silva will want to keep things tight and hit on the break through Raul Jimenez and Rodrigo Muniz. This has the feel of a match that could swing either way depending on individual moments of quality.

Forest Fortress
Peacock

Nottingham Forest vs Crystal Palace

Sunday, 2:00 PM GMT (9:00 AM ET) | City Ground, Nottingham

Nottingham Forest continue their remarkable season at the City Ground against a Crystal Palace side still searching for consistency under Oliver Glasner. Forest have been one of the stories of the Premier League campaign, defying expectations with their aggressive, organized approach that has earned them a genuine shot at European qualification. The home supporters have turned the City Ground into one of the most intimidating venues in English football.

Crystal Palace's season has been a study in contrasts, brilliant attacking displays followed by defensive collapses, and they'll need to find balance at a ground where Forest simply don't lose. Eberechi Eze remains Palace's most dangerous creative force, and his ability to unlock defenses with a moment of individual brilliance could be the difference maker. However, Forest's midfield pressing has been relentless all season, and Eze will find himself hounded at every opportunity.

This match was also rescheduled due to Forest's European commitments, and they'll be looking to carry that continental confidence into domestic action. The atmosphere at the City Ground will be electric, and Palace's younger players in particular may struggle with the intensity. Forest should have enough to take all three points and continue their push for what seemed like an impossible dream at the start of the season.

Basque Derby
ESPN+

Athletic Club vs Real Sociedad

Sunday, 9:00 PM local (3:00 PM ET) | San Mamés, Bilbao
Athletic Club -128 | Real Sociedad +106 | O/U 2.5 goals

The Basque Derby is one of European football's most intense rivalries, and this edition arrives with both clubs in very different form trajectories. Athletic Club (13th, 24 points) have stumbled badly in recent weeks with consecutive La Liga defeats, while Real Sociedad (8th, 27 points) are riding high after a 3-1 victory over Celta de Vigo and four wins in their last five outings. La Real arrive at San Mamés with genuine belief they can claim bragging rights from a venue that's historically been unkind to them.

Here's the wrinkle: Athletic have won four straight home meetings against Sociedad, conceding just one goal during that stretch. San Mamés transforms for derby day, and whatever struggles Athletic have experienced on the road tend to evaporate when Los Leones play in front of their own supporters against their neighbors. The -128 moneyline reflects this historical dominance while acknowledging Sociedad's superior form. Manager Ernesto Valverde needs his side to rediscover their identity after that 2-1 defeat at Sevilla left them looking listless in the final third.

The card market deserves attention here. Across the last nine meetings between these clubs, there's been an average of 5.56 cards per game, and with the fixture's emotional intensity cranked to maximum, expect referee appointments to be busy. Igor Zubeldia is likely to return for Sociedad after his replacement Duje Caleta-Car saw red last week, and Zubeldia himself has been carded six times this season. The 9/2 odds on a sending off feel generous given recent history, with both clubs showing five reds in their last 18-19 competitive matches.

Both teams to score at 1.91 represents an interesting angle. Athletic's home record suggests they'll find the net, and Sociedad's attacking quality with Mikel Oyarzabal and the dangerous support cast makes them a threat to any defense. The under 2.5 goals at -150 reflects the defensive tension inherent to derby matches, but this particular edition could break pattern if Athletic's aggressive home support pushes them forward and creates space for Sociedad's counterattack. Either way, this is must-watch football between two proud Basque institutions.
