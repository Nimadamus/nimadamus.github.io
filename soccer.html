West Ham's record looks like a tire fire, but they've shown flashes of competence that make this line feel a bit steep. United's been solid at Old Trafford, sure, but -245 suggests they're some unstoppable force at home when they've had their share of head-scratching performances this season. The Hammers have nothing to lose here, which makes them dangerous in that unpredictable way that drives bettors insane.

That 3.5 total feels right on the money given both teams' tendencies. United's attack has been clinical when they get going, while West Ham's defense has been about as reliable as a chocolate teapot most weeks. The question isn't whether goals will come, it's whether they'll flow freely or get bottled up by nerves and tactics.

The real storyline here is whether United's home advantage actually means anything against a West Ham side that's got absolutely nothing to lose and everything to prove.
