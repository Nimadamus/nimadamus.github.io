Utah @ Lightning

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Amalie Arena
Puck Line
TB -1.5 (+150)
Moneyline
TB -175 / UTAH +145
Total
O/U 6.5

Utah's franchise continues to establish itself in the NHL, and road trips to Tampa Bay are never easy. The Lightning remain one of the Eastern Conference's more dangerous teams, and Nikita Kucherov's offensive brilliance continues to terrify opposing defenses. Kucherov leads the team in points and his ability to create scoring chances out of nothing makes Tampa's power play a constant threat. Brayden Point's two-way play has been exceptional, and Andrei Vasilevskiy remains an elite goaltender when locked in.

Utah is still finding its identity in its second NHL season. The franchise, formerly the Arizona Coyotes, has been competitive but inconsistent. Their goaltending situation has been a question mark all year, and the defensive corps is still developing chemistry. Road games against veteran-laden teams like Tampa present the toughest test for a young roster that can be prone to turnovers in the neutral zone.

Tampa Bay at -175 reflects their home-ice advantage and the talent disparity. The Lightning's home record has been strong, and their power play can exploit Utah's penalty kill, which has been below average this season. The 6.5 total is standard for a game featuring Tampa's offensive firepower against a Utah team that can struggle to generate consistent offense on the road. Expect the Lightning to control this one, though Utah's competitiveness could keep it within the puck line.

Game 2
NHL Network

Bruins @ Rangers

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden
Puck Line
BOS -1.5 (+205)
Moneyline
BOS -115 / NYR -105
Total
O/U 6.5

This is the kind of Original Six rivalry that makes Monday night hockey worth watching. The Bruins and Rangers at Madison Square Garden is hockey at its finest, and both teams have playoff implications riding on every point. Boston enters as a slight road favorite at -115, which tells you everything about the respect the market has for this team. David Pastrnak continues to be one of the league's premier goal-scorers, and Brad Marchand's competitive fire elevates everyone around him. The Bruins' defensive structure under Jim Montgomery has been solid, and their balanced scoring attack makes them difficult to game-plan against.

The Rangers have been a model of inconsistency this season. Artemi Panarin remains their most dangerous offensive weapon, capable of taking over a game with his vision and finishing ability. Igor Shesterkin's goaltending has been the backbone of their competitiveness, and when he's on, the Rangers can beat anyone. But their defensive lapses have been costly, and the blue line has struggled with consistency in front of Shesterkin. Adam Fox's offensive production from the back end is elite, but he can't do it all.

The near-even moneyline (BOS -115 / NYR -105) screams "coin flip," and that's exactly what this feels like. Boston's road record has been solid, and they don't get rattled by hostile environments. The Garden will be rocking, but the Bruins have won at MSG before. The 6.5 total could go either way depending on which version of Shesterkin shows up. If he's sharp, this is a 3-2 game. If not, the Bruins' depth could push it over. Either way, this is the best hockey game on the board tonight.

ESPN+

Islanders @ Flyers

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center
Puck Line
PHI -1.5 (+220)
Moneyline
PHI -115 / NYI -105
Total
O/U 6.5

Two Metropolitan Division teams battling for playoff positioning meet in what should be a grinding, physical affair. Philadelphia's slight home favorite status at -115 reflects the home-ice advantage more than any significant talent gap. The Flyers' rebuild has produced some exciting young talent, with Matvei Michkov emerging as a dynamic offensive threat and Travis Konecny continuing to provide veteran leadership. Their blue line has been inconsistent but competitive, and goaltending improvements have been the biggest factor in their improved results.

The Islanders bring their trademark defensive structure to this matchup. Mathew Barzal remains the engine that drives their offense, and his ability to create in transition is vital against a Flyers team that can be vulnerable on the rush. Ilya Sorokin's goaltending has been excellent when healthy, giving the Islanders a chance in every game. Their system under Patrick Roy focuses on limiting high-danger chances, and they rank among the league's better teams at suppressing shots from the slot.

The moneyline is essentially a pick'em (PHI -115 / NYI -105), which is fitting for a Metropolitan Division game between evenly matched teams. The 6.5 total feels slightly high for a game that could become a defensive struggle. Both teams play a tight-checking style, and games between the Islanders and Flyers have trended under this season. This is a game where special teams and goaltending could determine the winner, and the team that gets the first power play goal might ride it to a 3-2 victory.

Kings @ Blue Jackets

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Nationwide Arena
Puck Line
CBJ -1.5 (+215)
Moneyline
CBJ -118 / LA -102
Total
O/U 6.5

Columbus as a slight home favorite over the Kings is an interesting market assessment. The Blue Jackets have been competitive at Nationwide Arena, where the crowd energy gives them a noticeable boost. Their young core has taken steps forward, and the additions from recent drafts are starting to bear fruit. The goaltending tandem has been serviceable, and Columbus plays with an aggression at home that can catch traveling teams off guard, especially Western Conference opponents who rarely visit.

Los Angeles remains one of the steadier teams in the Pacific Division, led by the ageless Anze Kopitar and the goal-scoring prowess of Adrian Kempe. The Kings' defensive system is one of the league's best at limiting zone entries, and their goaltending has been a strength all season. LA's experience and structure make them a tough out in any building, and this cross-conference matchup eliminates any familiarity advantage. Drew Doughty's leadership on the blue line, while past his prime, still provides valuable minutes in key situations.

The line tells you these teams are separated by the thinnest of margins. Columbus at -118 gets the home-ice edge, while LA at -102 represents value for a team with more playoff experience and structural discipline. The 6.5 total accounts for two teams that can score when their power plays click. LA's road game has been one of their strengths this season, and they won't be intimidated by a trip to Nationwide Arena. This is a toss-up that could come down to the final minutes.

ESPN+

Ducks @ Oilers

Monday, 8:30 PM ET | Rogers Place
Puck Line
EDM -1.5 (-105)
Moneyline
EDM -250 / ANA +205
Total
O/U 6.5

Connor McDavid and Leon Draisaitl at Rogers Place against a young Anaheim team. That's as close to a guaranteed fireworks display as the NHL can offer. Edmonton at -250 is the heaviest favorite on the board tonight, and for good reason. McDavid remains the most electrifying player in hockey, capable of single-handedly destroying defensive systems with his speed and vision. Draisaitl's complementary scoring touch gives the Oilers arguably the most dangerous 1-2 punch in the sport, and their power play converts at a rate that makes opponents desperate to stay out of the penalty box.

Anaheim's rebuild has shown progress, with Leo Carlsson and Mason McTavish developing into legitimate NHL talents. The Ducks' young core plays with fearlessness, but road trips to Edmonton are a brutal test of maturity. Rogers Place is one of the loudest barns in hockey, and the Oilers feed off that energy. Anaheim's goaltending will need to be spectacular to keep this competitive, and even then, Edmonton's depth scoring can overwhelm young defensemen who aren't accustomed to the pace McDavid and Draisaitl create.

The -1.5 puck line at -105 (essentially even money) tells you the market expects a multi-goal Edmonton win. The 6.5 total leans toward the over given Edmonton's home offensive output, as the Oilers average over 3.5 goals per game at Rogers Place. Anaheim's defensive structure can break down against elite teams, and McDavid in his home barn is hockey's most unstoppable force. The Ducks at +205 offer value if you believe in their young talent, but Edmonton should control this one wire to wire.
