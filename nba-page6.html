The Matchup

Two teams devastated by injuries meet in Indianapolis. The Kings have gone 4-3 without Sabonis, while the Pacers have struggled mightily since losing Haliburton to a torn Achilles in the 2025 Finals. Both teams are playing without their offensive engines.

Star Watch: De'Aaron Fox

De'Aaron Fox has been shouldering the load in Sacramento, averaging 28.4 PPG with 6.8 APG on the season. Without Sabonis, Fox has been even more aggressive, scoring 35+ in two of his last five games. His speed in transition will be a factor against Indiana's league-worst transition defense (allowing 18.2 fast break PPG).

Pacers Without Haliburton

Since Haliburton's season-ending injury, Benedict Mathurin has stepped into a featured role, averaging 22.1 PPG over his last 10 games. Pascal Siakam remains the stabilizing force with 21.8 PPG and 7.4 RPG, but the Pacers' offense has dropped from 120.3 PPG (1st) to 108.6 PPG (18th) without their All-Star point guard.

Pace & Style

SAC: 102.8 Pace IND: 103.4
SAC: 116.2 Off Rating IND: 112.8
SAC: 113.8 Def Rating IND: 115.4

These remain two of the fastest teams in the league, which explains the 234.5 total. Both teams rank bottom-10 defensively, and neither has the personnel to slow down the opposing attack. This should be a track meet.

Betting Trends

  • Kings are 8-5 ATS as road favorites this season
  • Pacers are 5-8 ATS as home underdogs
  • Over is 15-9 in combined games involving these teams
  • Kings are 4-1 ATS in their last 5 road games
  • Head-to-head: Kings won both meetings last season by 10+ points

The Angle

The total of 234.5 reflects the expectation of a high-scoring affair, and both teams' recent history supports it. Without Sabonis, the Kings have actually played faster but struggle on the glass. Without Haliburton, the Pacers lack the orchestrator who made their offense hum. This feels like a game where both teams score 115+ but neither reaches 120.

The Matchup

The Suns travel to Minnesota without Devin Booker, tilting this matchup heavily in the Timberwolves' favor. Minnesota has been one of the best home teams in the NBA at 9-3, while Phoenix has struggled on the road without their full complement of stars.

Anthony Edwards Showcase

Anthony Edwards is having an MVP-caliber season, averaging 27.8 PPG, 5.9 RPG, and 4.2 APG. He's shooting 46.2% from the field and 38.4% from three, establishing himself as arguably the most complete two-way guard in the league. In his last 10 games, Edwards has averaged 30.2 PPG with the Wolves going 8-2.

Suns Without Booker

Kevin Durant is now in Houston (traded July 2025), so without Booker the Suns are down to Bradley Beal (if he plays) and role players. Beal has averaged 23.4 PPG this season but has been inconsistent. Grayson Allen (15.8 PPG) and Jusuf Nurkic (10.2 PPG, 10.8 RPG) will need to step up significantly.

Defensive Battle

PHX: 114.2 Def Rating MIN: 108.4
PHX: 44.8 Opp FG% MIN: 43.2
PHX: 35.8 Opp 3P% MIN: 33.6

Minnesota's defense ranks 4th in the NBA, anchored by Rudy Gobert's rim protection. The Wolves allow just 43.2% shooting and force 14.8 turnovers per game. Without Booker to create off the dribble, Phoenix may struggle to generate quality looks.

Betting Trends

  • Wolves are 9-3 SU and 7-5 ATS at Target Center
  • Suns are 3-5 ATS as road underdogs of 7+ points
  • Under is 13-10 in Timberwolves games this season
  • Suns are 2-4 SU without Booker this season
  • Wolves have covered 6 of their last 8 home games

The Angle

The 7.5-point spread feels about right given the circumstances. Without Booker, the Suns lack the shot creation to keep up with Edwards and the Minnesota offense. The total of 218.5 reflects expectations of a defensive grind - the Wolves will try to impose their style, and the depleted Suns don't have the firepower to push pace.

The Matchup

The Pelicans' nightmare season continues as they host a Spurs team also dealing with significant injuries. New Orleans at 5-19 is the worst record in the Western Conference, decimated by injuries to Zion and the mid-season trade of Brandon Ingram to Toronto. Even without Wembanyama, San Antonio should control this game.

Spurs Without Wemby

Without Victor Wembanyama (out with knee soreness), the Spurs turn to Keldon Johnson (18.4 PPG) and Jeremy Sochan (14.2 PPG, 7.8 RPG). Rookie Stephon Castle has impressed with 12.8 PPG in his expanded role. The Spurs have gone 3-2 in their last 5 without Wemby.

Pelicans' Historic Struggles

This is one of the worst seasons in Pelicans history. Without Zion, Ingram (traded), and Murray, CJ McCollum has been asked to carry an impossible load, averaging 24.8 PPG but shooting just 42.1% from the field. Trey Murphy III (16.4 PPG) provides some firepower, but the Pelicans rank dead last in Net Rating (-11.4).

By The Numbers

SA: 113.8 Off Rating NO: 107.2
SA: 112.4 Def Rating NO: 118.6
SA: +1.4 Net Rating NO: -11.4

The Pelicans have the worst defense in the NBA, allowing 118.6 points per 100 possessions. They've given up 120+ points in 8 of their last 12 games. Even a Wemby-less Spurs team should find offense easy to come by.

Betting Trends

  • Pelicans are 2-8 ATS in their last 10 home games
  • Spurs are 6-3 ATS as road favorites this season
  • Over is 14-10 in Pelicans games (they can't stop anyone)
  • Spurs have covered 4 straight games vs teams under .400
  • Pelicans have lost 8 straight games

The Angle

The 8.5-point spread is significant, but the Pelicans have shown no ability to compete recently. They've lost by double digits in 6 of their last 8 games. The concern for Spurs backers is motivation - San Antonio has nothing to prove against a depleted opponent. The total of 221.5 feels low given New Orleans' defensive woes; even without Wemby, the Spurs should score 115+.
