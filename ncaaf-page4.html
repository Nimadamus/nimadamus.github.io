Tigers @ Wolfpack

2025-12-19T19:30Z | Raymond James Stadium | ESPN
MEM: 8-5 | NCSU: 8-5

The Go Bowling Myrtle Beach Bowl features two programs with identical 8-5 records looking to end their seasons on a high note. Memphis comes in fresh off a solid Conference USA campaign, while NC State salvaged what started as a disappointing ACC season with four wins in their final six games.

The Tigers rely heavily on their ground game, averaging over 180 rushing yards per contest. Running back Mario Anderson II has been the engine of this offense, and he'll be tested against an NC State front seven that's been stout against the run all season. The Wolfpack's defense has held opponents under 120 rushing yards in five of their last seven games.

For NC State, this is about building momentum for 2026. Head coach Dave Doeren needs a win here to quiet the noise around his program. Expect a physical, low-scoring affair that comes down to which team can establish the run game first.

Dukes @ Ducks

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Autzen Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
JMU: 12-1 | ORE: 11-1

This is the matchup everyone's been waiting for - Group of Five darling James Madison gets their shot at a power conference giant in the College Football Playoff First Round. The Dukes come in riding a 12-game win streak, having dominated the Sun Belt with their suffocating defense and opportunistic offense under Curt Cignetti.

Oregon is one of the most complete teams in the country, boasting a Heisman-caliber quarterback in Dillon Gabriel and a defense that ranks in the top 15 nationally. The Ducks averaged 38.2 points per game this season, and their offense has proven capable of putting up 50-burgers against quality competition.

The 20.5-point spread feels steep, but Oregon has the talent advantage at virtually every position. JMU will need to control the clock, avoid turnovers, and hope their physical defense can slow down Gabriel's air attack. The Dukes have covered in 9 of their last 11 as underdogs, so don't count them out from at least making this competitive through three quarters.

Redbirds @ Wildcats

2025-12-21T00:30Z | Villanova Stadium | ESPN2
ILST: 11-4 | VILL: 12-2

FCS quarterfinal action as Illinois State travels to take on Villanova in what should be an absolute barnburner. The Redbirds come in as road warriors, having won five straight away from Normal, while the Wildcats have been nearly unbeatable at home with an 8-0 record at Villanova Stadium this season.

Illinois State's offense operates through their quarterback, who's thrown for over 2,800 yards this season. They'll need to exploit a Villanova secondary that's been susceptible to the deep ball. On the flip side, the Wildcats run a balanced attack that keeps defenses guessing play after play.

Home field advantage could be massive here. Villanova has outscored opponents by an average of 17 points at home, and the weather in Philadelphia can wreak havoc on visiting teams this time of year. Expect a closely contested game that comes down to turnovers and special teams.

Wave @ Rebels

2025-12-20T20:30Z | Vaught-Hemingway Stadium | TNT, HBO Max
TULN: 11-3 | MISS: 12-1

A rematch nobody expected to see in the College Football Playoff. Ole Miss already destroyed Tulane 45-10 in their September meeting, and now the Green Wave get another shot at the Rebels on college football's biggest stage. The question is: can Jon Sumrall's squad close the gap in just three months?

Ole Miss quarterback Trinidad Chambliss has been nothing short of sensational since transferring from Ferris State, throwing for 3,200 yards and 28 touchdowns. His dual-threat ability has made this Rebels offense one of the most explosive in the country. Lane Kiffin's play-calling has been masterful all season.

Tulane comes in with legitimate talent, don't get it twisted. They won the AAC championship and have playmakers on both sides of the ball. But the 35-point beatdown in September looms large. The Green Wave will need to execute flawlessly and hope for some Ole Miss turnovers to have any chance in this one. The Rebels are playing for a spot in the quarterfinals and won't be overlooking anyone.

Hurricanes @ Aggies

2025-12-20T17:00Z | Kyle Field | ABC, ESPN
MIA: 11-2 | TA&M: 11-2

Two 11-2 programs collide in what could be the best game of CFP First Round weekend. Miami's explosive offense meets Texas A&M's physical SEC defense in a matchup that has all the makings of an instant classic. Kyle Field will be rocking, and the Hurricanes are about to experience what 100,000 screaming Aggies sounds like.

Miami quarterback Cam Ward has been electric all season, throwing for over 3,600 yards and 32 touchdowns. He's the engine that makes this Hurricanes offense go, and his ability to extend plays and make something out of nothing will be crucial against A&M's aggressive pass rush.

Texas A&M's defense has been their calling card all season. They rank 12th nationally in scoring defense, allowing just 18.4 points per game. The Aggies also have a legitimate two-headed monster at running back that can grind down opposing defenses. This game will likely be decided in the trenches, and the home crowd advantage could push A&M over the top in a close one.

Grizzlies @ Bobcats

2025-12-20T21:00Z | Bobcat Stadium (MT) | ABC
MONT: 13-2 | MTST: 13-2

The Brawl of the Wild goes to the FCS Semifinals! Montana and Montana State meet for the second time this season with a trip to the national championship game on the line. The Bobcats won the regular season meeting 34-28 in Missoula, and now the Grizzlies get their revenge shot in Bozeman.

Both teams come in at 13-2, and there's genuine bad blood here. Montana State has dominated this rivalry recently, winning 5 of the last 6 meetings. But the Grizzlies have been on a tear through the playoffs, outscoring opponents by an average of 21 points per game in their last three contests.

This game will be decided by which defense can make stops in the red zone. Both offenses can move the ball, but Montana State's home field advantage at Bobcat Stadium could be the difference. Expect freezing temperatures, rowdy fans, and an absolutely electric atmosphere for one of the best rivalries in all of college football.

Bulldogs @ Panthers

2025-12-13T17:00Z | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ABC
SCST: 10-3 | PV: 10-4

The Cricket Celebration Bowl features SWAC champion Prairie View A&M hosting MEAC champion South Carolina State in college football's premier HBCU championship game. This is what these programs have been working toward all season, and the winner gets bragging rights as the best HBCU program in the country.

South Carolina State comes in with a physical defense that's allowed just 17.3 points per game this season. The Bulldogs are battle-tested from their MEAC schedule and know how to win close games. They've won 7 of their last 8 games by single digits.

Prairie View has the home crowd advantage at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, which will be packed with HBCU faithful. Their offense has averaged over 30 points per game during their current win streak, and they have the explosiveness to put up big numbers quickly. This should be an entertaining, back-and-forth affair with national HBCU pride on the line.

Broncos @ Huskies

2025-12-14T01:00Z | SoFi Stadium | ABC
BOIS: 9-5 | WASH: 9-4

The LA Bowl brings together Boise State and Washington in a matchup between two programs that had very different paths to get here. The Broncos finished strong after a rocky start, winning 6 of their last 7 games, while Washington is looking to bounce back after missing out on the CFP despite a solid Pac-12 campaign.

Boise State's running game has been their bread and butter all season, averaging 195 rushing yards per game. Ashton Jeanty has been one of the best backs in the country, and he'll be looking to cap off his stellar season with a big bowl performance against a Washington defense that's struggled at times against the run.

Washington has the talent advantage on paper, but motivation could be a question. The Huskies were expected to contend for a national title after last year's run, and playing in the LA Bowl isn't exactly where they hoped to be. Boise State treats every bowl game like the Super Bowl, and that edge in intensity could make the difference.

Trojans @ Gamecocks

2025-12-17T02:00Z | Cramton Bowl | ESPN
TROY: 8-6 | JVST: 9-5

The Camellia Bowl features a Sun Belt showdown as Troy takes on Jacksonville State in Montgomery. Both programs have made significant strides this season, and this bowl game represents a chance to end the year on a high note heading into the offseason.

Troy comes in with one of the more experienced rosters in the Sun Belt, featuring a veteran quarterback who's thrown for over 2,600 yards this season. Their passing attack ranks in the top 30 nationally, and they have the receivers to stretch defenses vertically.

Jacksonville State's defense has been the story of their season. The Gamecocks rank in the top 25 in scoring defense and have forced turnovers at an impressive rate. They'll need to generate pressure on Troy's quarterback and win the turnover battle to pull off what would be a statement win for their program.

Monarchs @ Bulls

2025-12-17T22:00Z | Camping World Stadium | ESPN
ODU: 10-3 | USF: 9-4

The Cure Bowl features Old Dominion and South Florida in Orlando, with both programs looking to cap off breakthrough seasons. ODU at 10-3 has had one of the best turnarounds in college football, while USF continues to build under Alex Golesh.

Old Dominion's offense operates through a dynamic rushing attack that's averaged over 200 yards per game. They control the clock, limit possessions, and play mistake-free football. The Monarchs are 7-1 when holding opponents under 24 points this season.

South Florida has the explosiveness to match up with anyone. Their quarterback has thrown for over 2,900 yards and their receiving corps is one of the most talented in the AAC. This game will come down to whether ODU can control the pace or if USF can turn it into a track meet. In a neutral-site game, the team that adjusts better will come out on top.

Cajuns @ Hens

2025-12-18T01:40Z | Hancock Whitney Stadium | ESPN
UL: 6-7 | DEL: 7-6

The New Orleans Bowl brings together Louisiana and Delaware in a matchup of teams that scratched and clawed their way to bowl eligibility. Neither had the season they envisioned, but both earned the right to play one more game, and that counts for something in college football.

Louisiana's offense has shown flashes of brilliance this season but struggled with consistency. They have the playmakers to put up points, but turnovers have plagued them all year. On defense, they've been solid against the run but vulnerable to the deep ball.

Delaware comes in as the underdog but has played inspired football down the stretch. They won 4 of their last 5 to earn this bowl bid, and momentum is a powerful thing this time of year. This should be a competitive, back-and-forth game between two evenly matched teams.

Bears @ Wolves

2025-12-19T02:00Z | Ford Center At The Star | ESPN2
MOST: 7-6 | ARST: 7-6

The Xbox Bowl from Frisco, Texas features Missouri State taking on Arkansas State in a battle of 7-6 teams looking to end the year above .500. Both programs have had their share of ups and downs this season, making this a fitting matchup for a lower-tier bowl game.

Missouri State runs a physical, ground-and-pound offense that controls the clock and wears down opposing defenses. Their offensive line has been one of the best in the Missouri Valley Conference, and they'll look to establish the run game early against an Arkansas State defense that's been susceptible to power football.

Arkansas State has the explosive playmakers to flip the script if they get going. Their passing attack features a veteran quarterback who can make all the throws, and their receivers have proven capable of making big plays in tight coverage. The Red Wolves need to get ahead early and force Missouri State out of their comfort zone.

Tide @ Sooners

2025-12-20T01:00Z | Memorial Stadium (Norman, OK) | ABC, ESPN
ALA: 11-3 | OU: 10-3

A historic matchup in the CFP First Round as Alabama travels to Norman to face Oklahoma. These two blue-blood programs have combined for 26 national championships, and now they meet in December with playoff advancement on the line. The SEC vs the new Big 12 - tradition meets transition.

Alabama comes in with a chip on their shoulder after missing the CFP bye. The Crimson Tide offense has found its rhythm in December, averaging 38 points per game in their last three contests. Their defense remains one of the most talented units in the country, with NFL prospects at every level.

Oklahoma's home-field advantage could be the great equalizer. Norman in December is a hostile environment, and the Sooners have lost just twice at home this season. Their balanced offense keeps defenses honest, and their front seven has shown the ability to generate pressure against anyone. This is exactly the type of game where the home crowd can make a 3-point difference.

Owls @ Broncos

2025-12-19T16:00Z | Brooks Stadium (SC) | ESPN
KENN: 10-4 | WMU: 10-4

The Myrtle Beach Bowl's second game features Kennesaw State and Western Michigan, both coming in with identical 10-4 records. For Kennesaw State, this represents another step in their transition from FCS powerhouse to FBS competitor. For WMU, it's a chance to end a solid season on a high note.

Kennesaw State still runs elements of their famous triple-option offense that dominated at the FCS level. They're physical, they're disciplined, and they don't beat themselves with turnovers. The question is whether that style translates against a MAC team that's seen plenty of option football this season.

Western Michigan has the speed advantage on defense to potentially neutralize KSU's ground game. The Broncos also have more experience playing in bowl games at this level. Home-field advantage goes to WMU here with the game being played at a neutral site where MAC fans are more likely to travel. Expect a grind-it-out game that stays close throughout.

Cougars @ Aggies

2025-12-22T19:00Z | Albertsons Stadium | ESPN
Line: USU -1.5 | O/U: 49.5 | WSU: 6-6 | USU: 6-6

The LA Bowl features two 6-6 teams that barely limped into bowl eligibility. Washington State and Utah State meet in Inglewood with pride on the line - neither team wants to end the season below .500. For both programs, this game represents a chance for redemption after disappointing campaigns.

Washington State's Air Raid offense has had its moments this season but struggled with consistency down the stretch. Their quarterback has thrown for over 3,000 yards but also double-digit interceptions. Against a Utah State defense that's created turnovers at a solid rate, ball security will be paramount.

Utah State's offense runs through their ground game, averaging over 160 rushing yards per contest. They'll look to control the clock and keep Washington State's offense on the sidelines. With a slight 1.5-point spread, oddsmakers see this as a coin flip. In games this close between evenly matched teams, the squad that commits fewer turnovers usually wins.
