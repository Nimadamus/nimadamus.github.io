Bucks @ Hawks

Monday, 1:00 PM ET | State Farm Arena | Peacock
Spread: ATL -2.5 | O/U: 231.5 | ML: ATL -118 / MIL +100

The MLK Day festivities begin in the hometown of Dr. King, where two teams desperately trying to snap losing streaks square off at State Farm Arena. Atlanta (20-24) and Milwaukee (17-24) have both dropped their last three games, making this a fascinating matchup of desperation. The Hawks enter as slight home favorites, a testament to how far the Bucks have fallen this season. Milwaukee's 17-24 record is their worst first-half mark since 2015-16, and the franchise that was hoisting the Larry O'Brien Trophy just a few years ago now finds themselves fighting for playoff positioning in a muddled Eastern Conference.

The post-Trae Young era has officially begun in Atlanta. The three-time All-Star was shipped to Washington in a blockbuster deal, leaving Jalen Johnson as the Hawks' new centerpiece. Johnson has embraced the increased responsibility, and his two-way versatility gives Atlanta a different look than the offense-first identity they had with Young running the show. Johnson's ability to guard multiple positions while creating his own shot provides Atlanta flexibility they didn't have before. The Hawks are 21-23-0 ATS this season, while Milwaukee's 18-23-0 ATS mark tells you all you need to know about their struggles against the number.

Giannis Antetokounmpo remains a force of nature, but even the Greek Freak can't will this roster to victories alone. The Bucks are 1-4 ATS in their last five games, and their defensive identity has completely disappeared. Milwaukee ranks in the bottom third in defensive rating, a far cry from the elite defenses that propelled them to championships. The Bucks' supporting cast has been inconsistent, and their half-court offense stagnates when opponents can pack the paint against Giannis. Atlanta's length and athleticism could bother Milwaukee on both ends, and the Hawks' transition attack should feast against a Bucks team that struggles to get back in transition. The total of 231.5 is elevated, but neither team defends at a level that suggests the under is in play.

Thunder @ Cavaliers

Monday, 2:30 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse | NBC, Peacock
Spread: OKC -6 | O/U: 230.5 | ML: OKC -235 / CLE +194

This is the marquee matchup of MLK Day, and it absolutely delivers on paper. The defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder (35-8) roll into Cleveland to face the Cavaliers (24-19) in what would have been a Finals preview last season. Both teams entered the 2025 playoffs as No. 1 seeds in their respective conferences, and this is their first meeting of the 2025-26 campaign. OKC has been utterly dominant, sitting atop the Western Conference with the league's best record, while Cleveland has been solid but inconsistent as the fifth seed in the East.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander (31.8 PPG, 2nd in NBA) versus Donovan Mitchell (29.2 PPG, 7th in NBA) is must-watch television. SGA has taken another leap this season, cementing himself as one of the two or three best players on the planet. His mid-range game is automatic, his three-point shooting has improved, and his ability to get to the rim through traffic is elite. Mitchell, meanwhile, has been carrying an enormous offensive burden for Cleveland, and his scoring prowess keeps the Cavs competitive against anyone. The chess match between these two elite guards will likely determine the outcome.

The Thunder's 15-5 road record demonstrates their ability to win in hostile environments, though Cleveland's 14-10 home mark shows they protect Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse adequately. The spread seems steep for a road game against a quality opponent, but OKC has been covering numbers all season. The Thunder are a league-best against the spread among elite teams, while Cleveland has struggled to cover at home, posting a 7-17 ATS record in their own building. The total of 230.5 suggests a moderate pace, but both offenses are capable of exploding. With two elite scorers trading buckets, this has shootout potential if either defense takes a quarter off.

Clippers @ Wizards

Monday, 3:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena
Spread: LAC -7 | O/U: 226.5 | ML: LAC -275 / WAS +225

The Clippers arrive in Washington as comfortable road favorites against a Wizards team that's been ravaged by injuries and poor play all season. Los Angeles entered January on a five-game winning streak, the longest active run in the league at the time, and they've continued to play solid basketball since. The Clippers' roster construction allows them to compete without relying on any single star, and their depth has been their calling card this season. Multiple players can step up on any given night, which makes them a tough out regardless of matchup.

Washington's season took an interesting turn when they acquired Trae Young in the blockbuster trade with Atlanta, but the three-time All-Star hasn't played yet due to MCL and quadriceps injuries. Young's debut in a Wizards uniform will have to wait at least another month, leaving Washington without their new franchise cornerstone. The Wizards have been playing developmental basketball, getting young players extended run while accepting short-term losing as the price of long-term growth. Their defensive rating is bottom-five in the league, and opponents have been scoring at will against their porous perimeter defense.

The Clippers' 72.98% win probability per projection models reflects their significant talent advantage. LA should control the pace and execute in the half-court while Washington struggles to generate quality looks. The total of 226.5 feels moderate given the Wizards' defensive struggles, but the Clippers aren't an elite offensive team by any measure. They win through grinding defense and opportunistic scoring, which could keep this in the 220s. The seven-point spread is the number to watch, as Washington's young legs can occasionally keep games competitive before the experience gap shows in crunch time.

Mavericks @ Knicks

Monday, 5:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden | NBC, Peacock
Spread: NYK -12 | O/U: 231.5 | ML: NYK -621 / DAL +460

Jalen Brunson returns to face his former team in what's become an annual highlight of the NBA calendar. The diminutive point guard has transformed into one of the league's premier players since leaving Dallas, earning his second consecutive All-Star start and positioning himself in MVP conversations. Brunson sustained a sprained right ankle last Wednesday against Sacramento but is expected to play through the injury, which speaks to both his toughness and the magnitude of this revenge game. When these teams met in November, Brunson dropped 28 in a narrow 113-111 Knicks victory in Dallas.

The Mavericks look dramatically different than they did a year ago. Luka Doncic was shipped to the Lakers in February's blockbuster three-team deal with Anthony Davis going the other way, leaving Dallas to rebuild around their lottery pick, Cooper Flagg. The phenom from Duke has been spectacular, making a strong case for Rookie of the Year while carrying an enormous offensive burden. Flagg's ability to create his own shot and make plays for others has been impressive, but the supporting cast around him remains thin. Dallas sits at 17-26, well outside playoff contention but playing competitive basketball most nights.

New York's massive spread reflects their talent advantage, but the Knicks have been struggling lately. They've dropped eight of their last ten games, a stunning fall for a team that was competing for the East's top seed earlier in the season. The injuries have piled up, and the execution that defined their early-season success has been missing. That said, this is still Madison Square Garden on MLK Day against a rebuilding team. The atmosphere will be electric, and Brunson typically rises to big moments. The 12-point spread feels steep given New York's recent form, but Dallas doesn't have the firepower to capitalize if the Knicks show up. The total of 231.5 suggests a moderately paced game with both offenses generating quality looks.

Jazz @ Spurs

Monday, 5:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center
Spread: SAS -16.5 | O/U: 238.5 | ML: SAS -1000 / UTA +660

Victor Wembanyama celebrates his first All-Star starting selection by hosting one of the league's worst teams. The generational talent was announced as an All-Star starter earlier today, becoming the eighth San Antonio player in franchise history to earn that honor. Wembanyama has been sensational in his sophomore campaign, showcasing the shooting range that makes him virtually unguardable while anchoring an improving Spurs defense. His development has exceeded even the loftiest expectations, and he's become appointment television every time the Spurs take the floor.

San Antonio's roster construction has improved dramatically since acquiring De'Aaron Fox in a midseason trade. Fox's speed and scoring punch give Wembanyama a legitimate running mate, and the Spurs' transition attack has become one of the league's most dangerous weapons. Stephon Castle and Dylan Harper provide additional young talent, creating a core that should compete for years to come. The Spurs have won their last two games heading into this matchup, building momentum as the second half of the season approaches.

Utah (worst record in the NBA) is in full tank mode, and this spread reflects the massive talent gap between these rosters. The Jazz are led by Keyonte George and a collection of young players getting extended development minutes. Jusuf Nurkic provides veteran presence in the post, but the supporting cast lacks the firepower to compete with playoff-caliber teams. The 16.5-point spread is enormous, but San Antonio has been dominant at home this season. The total of 238.5 is elevated, suggesting an up-tempo affair where both teams look to push pace. With Wembanyama likely receiving extended minutes in a celebration game, the Spurs should cover comfortably while the total trends toward the over.

Pacers @ 76ers

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center
Spread: PHI -7 | O/U: 227.5 | ML: PHI -260 / IND +215

The Pacers limp into Philadelphia still dealing with the aftermath of Tyrese Haliburton's Achilles injury. The electric point guard suffered the devastating injury during the 2025 Finals, and his recovery timeline remains uncertain. Indiana has been competitive without their floor general, but the ceiling of this team is dramatically lower without Haliburton orchestrating the offense. The Pacers rely on their pace and transition attack, but generating quality half-court offense has been challenging without a capable lead ball-handler.

Philadelphia's season has been defined by the usual injury concerns and inconsistency that have plagued this franchise in recent years. When healthy, the Sixers have the talent to compete with anyone in the East, but stringing together healthy stretches has been the challenge. Joel Embiid's availability remains game-to-game, and the team's chemistry suffers when their stars aren't building rapport on the floor. That said, the Wells Fargo Center provides a legitimate home-court advantage, and the Sixers have been solid in their own building this season.

The seven-point spread suggests Philadelphia's talent advantage should manifest in a comfortable win, but Indiana's pace can keep games competitive. The Pacers push tempo relentlessly, which generates easy transition opportunities and keeps opponents from setting their defense. If Philadelphia matches Indiana's pace, this could turn into a shootout that benefits the Pacers. The total of 227.5 feels low for a Pacers game, as they routinely play in games that exceed 230 points. The under might require Philadelphia controlling pace and executing in the half-court, which plays into their strength. Indiana's ATS record against quality opponents is poor, suggesting the spread has value if Embiid is healthy and engaged.

Suns @ Nets

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center
Spread: PHX -9.5 | O/U: 214.5 | ML: PHX -420 / BKN +330

The post-Kevin Durant era Suns visit the post-everything Nets in a matchup that highlights how dramatically both franchises have changed in recent years. Phoenix (25-17) has found their footing after shipping Durant to Houston last summer, building a more balanced roster that doesn't rely on a single superstar to carry the offensive load. Devin Booker has embraced the primary scoring role, and the Suns' offensive efficiency has actually improved without the ball-dominant Durant creating isolation opportunities. The defense remains a concern, but Phoenix has been winning enough games to position themselves for playoff success.

Brooklyn (12-28) is in full rebuilding mode, trading away veteran pieces and giving extended minutes to young players. The Nets lack a true go-to scorer, relying on committee offense that produces inconsistent results. Their defensive intensity fluctuates from quarter to quarter, and they're prone to extended droughts that allow opponents to build comfortable leads. The Barclays Center crowd has seen glimpses of competitiveness, but this roster lacks the talent to compete with playoff-caliber teams consistently.

The 9.5-point spread reflects Phoenix's significant talent advantage, and the Suns should control this game from the opening tip. Brooklyn's youth and inexperience typically shows against veteran teams that execute in the half-court. The total of 214.5 is surprisingly low for a game involving two teams with defensive deficiencies. The under might require Phoenix to control pace and limit possessions, which isn't necessarily their preferred style. Booker and company should cover this spread without much drama, though Brooklyn's young legs can occasionally keep things interesting in garbage time.

Celtics @ Pistons

Monday, 8:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena | NBC, Peacock
Spread: DET -2.5 | O/U: 223.5 | ML: DET -134 / BOS +114

This might be the most fascinating matchup of MLK Day. The Detroit Pistons (30-10) sit atop the Eastern Conference, a sentence that would have seemed absurd a year ago. Their remarkable turnaround has been built on elite defense, unselfish ball movement, and the development of their young core. The Pistons have been dominant at Little Caesars Arena, posting a 16-4 home record that makes them one of the toughest home teams in the league. They've won 11 of their last 12 games in their own building, creating a fortress that few opponents have been able to breach.

Boston (26-15) has been remarkably resilient despite losing Jayson Tatum to a season-ending Achilles injury. Jaylen Brown has shouldered the offensive burden magnificently, posting career-highs in points (29.5 PPG), assists (5.0), and field goal percentage (50.3%). Brown's six consecutive 30-point games matched the longest streak in Celtics history not named Bird, Havlicek, Pierce, or Tatum. The Celtics have rallied behind Brown, maintaining playoff positioning despite the devastating loss of their franchise cornerstone. Payton Pritchard's emergence as a third option (16.6 PPG, 5.4 APG) has been crucial, though he's questionable tonight with an ankle injury.

The injury report favors Detroit. Cade Cunningham is OUT with a knee injury, but that's been the case for weeks and the Pistons have continued winning. Boston might be without Pritchard, which would significantly impact their backcourt depth. The Pistons are 2-1 ATS against the Celtics this season, and Detroit's defensive intensity has been the difference. The total of 223.5 suggests a grinding, physical affair, which plays into Detroit's identity. This is a genuine measuring-stick game for both teams, and the atmosphere at Little Caesars Arena should be electric. The Pistons as short home favorites feels right, as their home-court advantage has been one of the best in the league.

Heat @ Warriors

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Chase Center
Spread: GSW -5 | O/U: 239.5 | ML: GSW -205 / MIA +172

The MLK Day slate concludes with a fascinating West Coast showdown that features the aftermath of last February's blockbuster trade. Jimmy Butler returns to face his former team for the first time since forcing his way out of Miami, and you can bet this one has been circled on the calendar since the schedule was released. Butler landed in Golden State after the Heat finally capitulated to his trade demands, and the fit alongside Stephen Curry and Draymond Green has been exactly what the Warriors needed. The veteran three-time All-Star provides defensive toughness and secondary playmaking that was sorely missing from the Warriors' roster.

Golden State's transformation since acquiring Butler has been dramatic. After floundering through the first half of last season, the Warriors went on a 23-8 run to close the regular season once Butler arrived. They made the playoffs, won their play-in game, knocked off the Rockets in the first round, and only fell to Minnesota after Curry suffered a hamstring injury. This season, the Warriors have championship aspirations again, and Butler's leadership has been the catalyst. His defensive intensity sets the tone, and his ability to create in the half-court gives Golden State options when Curry is being face-guarded.

Miami has moved on with Andrew Wiggins as the centerpiece of their return package. Wiggins has thrived in South Beach, averaging 17.8 points while shooting 52.8% from the field and 40% from three. His efficiency has been career-best, suggesting the Heat's system is maximizing his abilities. The Heat are 20-22-1 ATS this season, while Golden State has been similarly mediocre against the spread despite their talent. The total of 239.5 is elevated, reflecting both teams' offensive capabilities and the Warriors' uptempo style at Chase Center. Butler's revenge game narrative adds intrigue, but the Warriors' home-court advantage and talent edge should be the difference in a game that could come down to the final possessions.
