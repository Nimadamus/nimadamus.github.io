Baylor @ Kansas

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | Allen Fieldhouse | FOX
Spread: KU -7.5 | ML: BAY +280 / KU -350 | O/U: 155.5

Allen Fieldhouse is rocking again. Kansas just delivered a statement that reverberated across the college basketball landscape, demolishing No. 2 Iowa State 84-63 on Tuesday night. The 21-point margin was the largest home victory by Kansas over a Top-5 opponent in Phog Allen history. Let that sink in. For the first time ever, the Jayhawks were considered underdogs at home against Iowa State, and they responded by handing the Cyclones their first loss of the season in dominant fashion.

Bill Self's team had been called out by their own coach just days before. After a road loss to West Virginia, Self declared that his team "sucked." Well, they heard the message. Kansas is 24-5 under Self at Allen Fieldhouse against opponents ranked higher than themselves. The Phog is college basketball's most intimidating venue, and it showed Tuesday night. The Jayhawks' 16-year run under Self has produced 14 Big 12 regular season championships while allowing just 13 home losses total.

Baylor comes in trying to continue their own solid campaign, but walking into this environment three days after Kansas dismantled the nation's No. 2 team is a nightmare draw. The Bears are a quality program, but Kansas at -7.5 at home off that performance is completely justified. The crowd will be electric, the team is riding high, and Self has proven time and again he knows how to prepare his squad for Big 12 showdowns. The 155.5 total reflects both teams' defensive capabilities.

Advanced Trends: Kansas is 14-1 at home against Big 12 opponents under Self over the past two seasons. The Jayhawks are 8-3 ATS in their last 11 games as home favorites. Baylor is 3-5 ATS in their last 8 road games against ranked opponents. The under has hit in 4 of the last 6 meetings between these programs at Allen Fieldhouse. Kansas averages 82.4 PPG at home this season, well above their 76.1 road average.

Creighton @ Providence

Friday, 6:30 PM ET | Amica Mutual Pavilion | FS1
Spread: CREI -1.5 | ML: CREI -125 / PROV +105 | O/U: 162.5

Big East basketball is always a grind, and this Creighton-Providence matchup epitomizes what makes the conference so special. The Bluejays come in as slight road favorites, which tells you the respect Greg McDermott's program has earned across the league. Creighton plays beautiful basketball, moving the ball unselfishly and getting high-percentage looks through their motion offense. They're built to win in hostile environments.

Providence has been solid at Amica Mutual Pavilion, using their home court as a fortress in Big East play. The Friars play tough, physical defense that can slow down opponents' tempo and force them into half-court battles. Ed Cooley may have moved on to Georgetown, but the program's identity remains intact. They'll compete hard for 40 minutes and make Creighton earn every basket.

The 162.5 total is relatively high for a Big East road game, suggesting both offenses should find rhythm. Creighton at -1.5 on the road is essentially a pick'em, which feels right for two evenly matched teams. The Bluejays have the slight edge in talent and execution, but Providence's home-court advantage closes the gap. This could easily come down to the final possession.

Advanced Trends: Creighton is 6-3 ATS in their last 9 road games against Big East opponents. Providence is 7-4 at home this season. The over has hit in 5 of the last 7 meetings between these teams. Creighton covers the spread 55% of the time as a road favorite of 2 points or less. Providence is 4-2 ATS in their last 6 home games against winning teams.

Marquette @ DePaul

Friday, 8:30 PM ET | Wintrust Arena | FS1
Spread: DEP -3.5 | ML: MARQ +150 / DEP -180 | O/U: 142.5

This is not your typical Marquette team. The Golden Eagles have fallen on hard times, sitting at just 6-12 overall and 1-6 in Big East play. That's a stunning collapse for a program that has been a consistent Big East contender in recent years. Shaka Smart's squad has struggled to find consistency, and they've failed to win any of the eight games where they were listed as underdogs this season. That's a troubling pattern that speaks to mental fragility.

DePaul (10-7, 2-4 Big East) has actually been the better team this season, a sentence you don't often write. The Blue Demons are 8-3 at home and have gone 9-1 in games where they were favored on the moneyline, winning 90% of those matchups. They're 11-6 ATS overall and riding a 3-0 ATS streak in their last three games. When DePaul smells blood against a struggling opponent, they know how to finish.

Marquette at +3.5 as a road underdog to DePaul is surreal to type, but the numbers don't lie. The Golden Eagles are 5-13 ATS this season and 2-8 ATS in their last 10 games. They've covered just twice as underdogs of 3.5 or more points. Meanwhile, DePaul's implied winning margin of 3 points aligns perfectly with their home dominance. The 142.5 total is low, reflecting both teams' offensive struggles at times.

Advanced Trends: Marquette is 2-4 ATS when getting 3.5+ points this season (0-8 SU). DePaul is 7-3 ATS in their last 10 home games. The Blue Demons and Golden Eagles average 147.2 combined PPG, 4.7 points higher than tonight's total. The home team has covered in 5 of the last 7 meetings between these rivals. DePaul's defensive rating at Wintrust Arena is 8 points better than their road mark.

Loyola Chicago @ Dayton

Friday, 8:30 PM ET | UD Arena | ESPN2
Spread: DAY -17.5 | ML: LOY +1200 / DAY -2500 | O/U: 142.5

Dayton comes in as a massive 17.5-point favorite against Loyola Chicago, and for good reason. The Flyers have been one of the Atlantic 10's dominant forces, playing elite basketball at UD Arena where the crowd creates one of the best atmospheres in mid-major basketball. Anthony Grant has built a program that plays to their home-court advantage, and the Flyers consistently cover big spreads against overmatched A-10 opponents.

Loyola Chicago has had moments this season but simply doesn't have the firepower to compete with Dayton on the road. The Ramblers play disciplined defense and try to slow the pace, which is their only path to keeping games competitive against superior opponents. Drew Valentine's squad will make Dayton work for every possession, but the talent gap is significant.

The 17.5 spread is enormous, but Dayton has earned that number with their home dominance. The Flyers' offensive efficiency at UD Arena is elite, and they typically pull away in the second half against inferior competition. Loyola's best bet is to keep the pace slow and hope Dayton goes cold from three, but that's a lot to ask. The 142.5 total suggests the Ramblers' pace-controlling efforts will have some success.

Advanced Trends: Dayton is 10-2 at home this season and 7-4 ATS as double-digit home favorites. Loyola Chicago is 2-6 ATS in their last 8 road games against teams with winning records. The under has hit in 5 of the last 7 Ramblers road games. Dayton covers spreads of 15+ points 62% of the time at UD Arena under Grant. The total has gone under in 4 of the last 6 meetings between A-10 opponents at Dayton.

Colorado State @ Boise State

Friday, 10:30 PM ET | ExtraMile Arena | FS1
Spread: BSU -5.5 | ML: CSU +185 / BSU -225 | O/U: 141.5

The late-night Mountain West showdown brings us a matchup with serious conference implications. Boise State has been one of the conference's better home teams, using ExtraMile Arena as a launching pad for their tournament hopes. The Broncos play physical, defensive-oriented basketball that travels well but is especially effective in front of their home crowd. They understand how to win games in the 60s.

Colorado State is fighting for relevance in the Mountain West, a conference that's been extremely competitive top to bottom. The Rams have shown flashes of quality basketball but haven't been consistent enough to separate themselves from the pack. On the road against Boise State's stingy defense, they'll need to be efficient with their possessions and avoid turnovers that lead to easy transition points.

Boise State at -5.5 at home feels like the right number. The Broncos have earned respect in their building, and Colorado State's road struggles suggest they'll have trouble keeping pace over 40 minutes. The 141.5 total is the lowest on tonight's college hoops slate, which tells you everything about how these teams play. Expect a grind-it-out affair where every possession matters.

Advanced Trends: Boise State is 9-3 at home this season and 6-3 ATS as home favorites of 4-7 points. Colorado State is 2-5 ATS in their last 7 road games against conference opponents. The under has hit in 7 of the last 10 meetings between these Mountain West rivals. Boise State's home defensive rating is 8.4 points better than their road mark. The total has stayed under in 5 of Colorado State's last 6 road games.
