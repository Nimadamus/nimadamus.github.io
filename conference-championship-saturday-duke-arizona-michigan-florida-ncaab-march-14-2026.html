Championship Saturday

NCAAB March 14, 2026

Championship Saturday has arrived. The ACC Championship pits No. 1 Duke (31-2) against No. 2 Virginia (29-4) in Greensboro, while the Big 12 Championship features No. 1 Arizona (31-2) vs No. 2 Houston (28-5) in Kansas City. SEC Semifinals in Nashville and Big Ten Semifinals in Chicago round out a massive six-game slate with Selection Sunday less than 24 hours away.

SEC Semifinals - Bridgestone Arena, Nashville

Game 1
ESPN

Florida vs Vanderbilt

Saturday, 1:00 PM ET | Bridgestone Arena, Nashville, TN | ESPN
Spread
UF -6.5
Moneyline
UF -358 / VANDY +268
Total
O/U 160.5

Florida's 26-6 season and dominant 16-2 SEC record have made them the unquestioned class of the conference, and they arrive in Nashville as the tournament's No. 1 seed with legitimate Final Four aspirations. The Gators' offense has been relentless all season, running opponents off the floor with a pace that produces points in bunches and rarely lets the opposing team settle into a comfortable rhythm. Their 16-2 conference mark is the best in the SEC by a significant margin, and that level of sustained excellence against the deepest basketball conference in America deserves every ounce of respect the market is giving them. This is a team built for March, with depth, scoring balance, and the kind of defensive versatility that can adjust to whatever they see across the floor.

Vanderbilt at 25-7 with an 11-7 conference record has been one of the best stories in college basketball this season. The Commodores have transformed from a bottom-feeder into a legitimate contender, and their 4th-seed run through the SEC Tournament has included wins over Tennessee in the quarterfinals. Playing in Nashville gives Vandy a quasi-home court advantage that can't be understated. Bridgestone Arena will be rocking with gold and black, and that crowd energy in a conference semifinal can turn a 6.5-point game into a coinflip in a hurry. Vanderbilt's physical style and willingness to compete on the glass against bigger, more athletic teams has been their calling card, and they won't be intimidated by the moment.

The 160.5 total is one of the highest numbers on the board and reflects Florida's up-tempo attack. The Gators want to push the pace on every possession, generate transition opportunities, and turn defense into offense before the opposing team can get set. Vanderbilt has shown the ability to score efficiently in the half court, but matching Florida's pace for 40 minutes is a different animal entirely. The Commodores' best path to an upset runs through controlling tempo, limiting possessions, and turning this into an ugly, physical grind in the paint. If Vandy can keep this game in the low 70s, they're in the fight. If Florida pushes the pace and this becomes a track meet in the 80s, the Gators' depth and talent advantage will be too much to overcome.

Selection Sunday is tomorrow, and both of these teams are firmly in the NCAA Tournament field regardless of the outcome. But the stakes are still enormous. Florida is playing for a potential No. 1 seed, and a dominant semifinal win followed by an SEC championship would all but lock them into that top line. Vanderbilt is looking to cement their seeding, and a win over the No. 1 seed in a conference semifinal is the kind of signature victory that bumps you up a seed line or two. The 6.5-point spread suggests Florida pulls away in the second half, which aligns with how their games have played out against quality opponents all season. The Gators' conditioning and depth tend to wear teams down after halftime, and that's where this one could separate.

Ole Miss vs Arkansas

If you told anyone at the start of the SEC Tournament that the 15th-seeded Rebels would be playing in the semifinals, they'd have called you crazy. But Ole Miss has produced one of the most electrifying Cinderella runs in recent conference tournament history, knocking off three opponents including a stunning upset of No. 2 seed Alabama in the quarterfinals. There is something genuinely dangerous about a team playing with house money and zero pressure, and the Rebels have found a gear in Nashville that they couldn't find during the grind of the regular season. Their confidence is sky-high, their energy is off the charts, and they're playing with the kind of fearless abandon that makes them a nightmare matchup for any team trying to protect something.

Arkansas enters as the No. 3 seed and the clear favorite, with a roster built to play at a frenetic pace that can overwhelm opponents with sheer volume of possessions. John Calipari's system in Fayetteville thrives on pushing tempo, creating chaos in transition, and using athletic depth to wear teams down over 40 minutes. The Razorbacks' quarterfinal performance showcased exactly the kind of relentless attack that makes them one of the most difficult matchups in the SEC. Their ability to generate second-chance points, force turnovers, and finish in transition creates a scoring machine that doesn't depend on any one player having a great night. When the system is clicking, Arkansas can score on anyone.

The 157.5 total tells you everything about what this game is going to look like. Neither team is interested in slowing things down, and both have the offensive talent to put up big numbers. Ole Miss's tournament run has been fueled by hot shooting and an aggressive attacking style, while Arkansas's DNA is built around pace and pressure. This has all the makings of a high-scoring, back-and-forth affair that comes down to which team can get the crucial stops down the stretch. The Rebels have been getting those stops in their tournament wins, but doing it against Arkansas's relentless offensive machine is a different level of difficulty entirely.

The narrative around this game is irresistible. Ole Miss as the Cinderella 15-seed taking down giants versus Arkansas's powerhouse roster trying to reach the SEC Championship game. For the Rebels, every game from here is gravy, and that freedom is genuinely dangerous. For Arkansas, the challenge is avoiding the letdown trap, treating a hot 15-seed with the same respect you'd give a top-4 opponent. Calipari has been in these situations before, and his teams don't tend to overlook anyone. But tournament basketball has a way of producing magic, and Ole Miss has the kind of fearless, nothing-to-lose energy that can produce one more stunning result. The atmosphere in Bridgestone Arena for this one is going to be absolutely electric.

Big Ten Semifinals - United Center, Chicago

Game 3
CBS

Wisconsin vs Michigan

Saturday, 1:00 PM ET | United Center, Chicago, IL | CBS
Spread
MICH -11.5
Moneyline
MICH -800 / WIS +550
Total
O/U 161.5

Michigan arrives in this Big Ten semifinal as the tournament's No. 1 seed and one of the most dominant teams in the conference all season, but the Wolverines know better than to overlook Wisconsin. The Badgers already beat Michigan during the regular season, which makes this the kind of rematch that should have the Wolverines fully locked in and motivated. Michigan's quarterfinal win over Ohio State, 71-67, was tighter than expected and showed that even elite teams can get pushed in tournament settings. The Wolverines' talent advantage is clear on paper, but tournament basketball has a way of compressing that gap, and Wisconsin has already proven they can hang with this team when everything clicks.

Wisconsin's path to the semifinals included an overtime thriller against Illinois, winning 91-88, which tells you two things about this Badgers team. First, they have the offensive firepower to put up 90+ points against a quality opponent. Second, they have the mental toughness and clutch gene to survive and advance in pressure-packed tournament games. That overtime win will have given Wisconsin enormous confidence heading into this semifinal, and they'll walk into the United Center believing they can beat anyone on any given day. The Badgers' balanced scoring attack and disciplined half-court offense have been their trademark all season, and their ability to execute in the half court is exactly what you need to pull off a tournament upset against a more talented opponent.

The 11.5-point spread is enormous for a Big Ten semifinal, and it reflects the gap between these teams across the full body of work. Michigan has been the more complete team over the course of the season, with superior depth, athleticism, and versatility on both ends of the floor. But an 11.5-point spread in a conference semifinal between two teams that already split, with the underdog winning one of those games, feels like a lot. Tournament environments create chaos, and 40 minutes in a packed United Center can produce results that regular-season records wouldn't predict. Wisconsin's coaching staff will have a detailed game plan designed to slow Michigan down and control the pace, and if they execute it, 11.5 points could be a bridge too far for the Wolverines to cover.

The 161.5 total suggests a moderately paced game, which actually favors Wisconsin's preferred style. The Badgers don't want this to become an up-and-down track meet where Michigan's superior athletes can run wild in transition. If Wisconsin can keep this game in the mid-70s per team, they're right in the fight. Michigan needs to impose their tempo and use their depth advantage to wear Wisconsin down over the course of the game. The Wolverines' second-half performance has been elite all season, and they tend to pull away from opponents who can hang tough for the first 20 minutes. Whether Wisconsin can sustain their intensity for a full 40 minutes against Michigan's relentless pressure is the central question of this semifinal.

Purdue vs UCLA

Moneyline

O/U 147.5

Purdue punched their ticket to the Big Ten semifinals with a convincing 74-58 dismantling of Nebraska in the quarterfinals, the kind of dominant wire-to-wire performance that sends a message to the rest of the bracket. The Boilermakers' interior presence has been their calling card all season, and their ability to control the paint on both ends of the floor makes them a difficult out in tournament settings. Purdue's size advantage creates problems for teams that don't have the frontcourt depth to match up, and their halfcourt execution has been among the best in the Big Ten all season. When the Boilermakers are hitting shots from the perimeter to complement their inside game, they become nearly impossible to guard.

UCLA advanced with a hard-fought 88-84 win over Michigan State in the quarterfinals, a game that showcased the Bruins' resilience and clutch shotmaking. Playing their first season in the Big Ten, UCLA has proven they belong among the conference's elite, and their quarterfinal win over a proud Michigan State program solidified that statement. The Bruins' guard play has been exceptional, with their backcourt providing the kind of scoring punch and floor leadership that wins tournament games. UCLA's ability to create off the dribble and manufacture offense in tight games has been the difference in several of their biggest wins this season.

The PUR -6.5 spread tells you the market believes Purdue's size and interior dominance give them a clear edge in this matchup against UCLA's guard-driven offense. It's a classic basketball chess match. Can the Bruins' perimeter players create enough separation and find enough open looks to offset Purdue's rebounding advantage? Can the Boilermakers' big men dominate the paint against a UCLA team that doesn't have traditional interior size? There's also the injury concern surrounding Bilodeau that could factor into the outcome. If that player is limited or unavailable, it significantly changes the complexion of the game and the matchup dynamics that both coaching staffs will be game-planning around. Tournament basketball often comes down to which team can best adjust to adverse circumstances, and this semifinal could test both teams in that regard.

The 147.5 total sits right in the middle of the board and suggests a moderately paced game that could go either way stylistically. Both Purdue and UCLA are firmly in the NCAA Tournament field, but a trip to the Big Ten Championship game would provide a massive boost to their seeding and their confidence heading into March Madness. Selection Sunday is tomorrow, and the committee will absolutely be watching how these teams perform under the bright lights of the United Center. A dominant semifinal win could be the difference between a 4-seed and a 3-seed, or a 5-seed and a 4-seed, and those seed line differences can completely reshape your path through the NCAA Tournament bracket. The stakes couldn't be higher with the bracket reveal less than 24 hours away.

Houston vs Arizona

Spread

Moneyline
HOU +125 / ARIZ -150

O/U 137.5

This is the game of the day, and it might be the game of the entire conference tournament weekend. Arizona at 31-2 and Houston at 28-5 are two of the best teams in the country, and they're meeting in the Big 12 Championship with massive March Madness seeding implications hanging in the balance. Arizona has been on an absolute tear, riding an 8-game win streak into this title game, while Houston has been equally impressive on a 5-game win streak of their own. The Cougars' semifinal demolition of Kansas, 69-47, was a statement performance that reminded everyone why Kelvin Sampson's program has been among the elite in college basketball for the past half-decade. Arizona squeaked past Iowa State 82-80 in their semifinal, showing the kind of composure and clutch execution that separates contenders from pretenders.

Last year, Houston won this same Big 12 Championship game against Arizona, 72-64, so there's a revenge angle for the Wildcats that adds another layer of intensity to an already loaded matchup. Arizona knows what it feels like to come this close and fall short, and that memory is going to fuel their intensity from the opening tip. The Wildcats' balanced attack and defensive versatility have been the foundation of their 31-2 record, and they've proven throughout the season that they can beat you in multiple ways. Whether it's their half-court execution, their transition game, or their suffocating defense, Arizona has answers for virtually every challenge you throw at them.

The 137.5 total is remarkably low for a game featuring two of the most talented rosters in the country, and it speaks to the defensive identity of both programs. Houston under Kelvin Sampson has always been a defense-first program, and the Cougars' ability to lock teams down in the half court is what makes them so dangerous in tournament settings. Arizona's defense has been equally stingy this season, and when two elite defensive teams meet in a championship game, the result tends to be an ugly, physical, low-scoring affair decided in the final minutes. This isn't going to be a pretty game for casual fans, but for basketball purists, this is the kind of heavyweight chess match that defines March.

The Arizona -2.5 spread makes this essentially a pick'em, with the Wildcats getting a slight edge as the No. 1 seed. Houston at +125 on the moneyline represents legitimate value for a team that won this exact game last year and just demolished Kansas by 22 points in the semifinal. Both teams are likely No. 1 seeds in the NCAA Tournament regardless of the outcome, but winning the Big 12 Championship would cement their case and give them the psychological edge of entering March Madness on the highest possible note. The Wildcats have the home-court-adjacent advantage of being the top seed, but Houston's experience in big games and their championship DNA from last year makes this as close to a 50/50 game as you'll find on the entire Championship Saturday slate. This is going to be an absolute war.

ACC Championship - Greensboro Coliseum, Greensboro

Game 6

Virginia vs Duke

Saturday, 8:30 PM ET | Greensboro Coliseum, Greensboro, NC | ESPN
Spread
DUKE -8.5
Moneyline
DUKE -365 / UVA +281
Total
O/U 137.5

The ACC Championship features the two best teams in the conference all season long, and it doesn't get much bigger than Duke vs Virginia under the lights in Greensboro with a conference title and March Madness momentum on the line. Duke at 31-2 with a 17-1 ACC record has been the most dominant team in college basketball this season, a juggernaut that has steamrolled through the ACC with a combination of elite talent, depth, and Jon Scheyer's masterful coaching. The Blue Devils' 31-win season is a testament to their consistency, and they've shown the ability to beat opponents in every conceivable way. But tonight's game comes with a significant caveat that changes the calculus entirely.

Duke will be playing WITHOUT Caleb Foster (foot) and Patrick Ngongba II (foot), two of their top four scorers. That is a massive blow for a team trying to win a conference championship. Losing two key rotation pieces to injury fundamentally changes what Duke can do offensively and defensively, and it forces role players into expanded minutes in the biggest game of the season. The Blue Devils' depth will be tested like it hasn't been all year, and the question becomes whether Duke's remaining talent is still good enough to handle a Virginia team that has been the second-best program in the ACC all season. The 8.5-point spread would likely be 12-15 points with a healthy Duke roster, so the market has already baked in the injury discount.

Virginia at 29-4 with a 15-3 ACC record has earned this moment through a season of relentless defensive excellence and Tony Bennett's trademark disciplined approach. The Cavaliers' ability to slow the game down, control tempo, and grind opponents into submission has been the backbone of their 29-win season. When Duke beat Virginia 77-51 back on January 28th at Cameron Indoor, the Blue Devils had their full roster and dominated from start to finish as 10-point favorites. But that was a different Duke team. Without Foster and Ngongba, the Cavaliers have a legitimate shot at making this game competitive and potentially pulling the upset in the ACC Championship.

The 137.5 total is the lowest number on the entire Championship Saturday board, and it perfectly encapsulates what this game is going to look like. Virginia's pace is glacially slow by design, and with Duke missing two key offensive weapons, the Blue Devils' scoring output is going to be diminished from their usual standard. This game is going to be a grind, a physical, half-court battle where every possession matters and defensive stops are the currency that determines the winner. Virginia's best chance at the upset is to keep this game in the 60s, force Duke's reserves into uncomfortable offensive situations, and capitalize on the Blue Devils' shorthanded rotation. If Virginia can keep it close heading into the final five minutes, the pressure of playing for an ACC title with a depleted roster could be Duke's undoing. This is going to be the nightcap of Championship Saturday, and the atmosphere in Greensboro is going to be electric for this classic ACC rivalry showdown.
