The World Cup Final Comes To MetLife: Argentina And Spain Play For Everything

2026 FIFA World Cup Final and the NWSL
Published July 19, 2026

A month of soccer across three countries comes down to one Sunday afternoon in New Jersey. Defending champions Argentina and European champions Spain meet in the 2026 World Cup final at MetLife Stadium, Messi against Lamine Yamal, survival against control, with kickoff at 3:00 PM ET on FOX. And when the confetti settles, the NWSL plays on: the third-place Washington Spirit visit expansion side Boston Legacy FC in Rhode Island tonight. Verified routes, odds and standings below. Analysis only, no picks.

Everything the last month built toward happens this afternoon in East Rutherford. The defending world champions against the reigning European champions, in the first men's World Cup final the New York area has ever hosted, with a 39-year-old Lionel Messi captaining Argentina against a 19-year-old Lamine Yamal playing for his first world title. The pregame market could not split them cleanly: Spain closed at +125 on the 90-minute line at FanDuel, the draw at +195, Argentina at +260, with the total parked at 2.5 goals. When the draw is the second choice in a World Cup final, the market is telling you to expect ninety tight, nervous minutes.

Spain earned favorite status by being the tournament's most complete team. Luis de la Fuente's side went through the knockout rounds without a wobble: Austria 3-0, Portugal 1-0, Belgium 2-1, and France 2-0 in the semifinal. That is eight scored, one conceded, three clean sheets, and not a single minute of extra time. Captain Rodri dictates everything from the base of midfield, Dani Olmo works between the lines, Mikel Oyarzabal leads the press from the front, and Yamal is the one-on-one weapon no left-back at this tournament has solved.

Argentina's tournament has been an entirely different genre. Lionel Scaloni's champions beat Cape Verde 3-2 in extra time, Egypt 3-2, Switzerland 3-1 in extra time, and England 2-1 in the semifinal. Four knockout wins, all by one score, two dragged past the ninetieth minute. Julian Alvarez has carried the attacking load, Enzo Fernandez has run the midfield, and Messi has produced the moments, exactly as he did four years ago. The concern is in the legs: two extra-time matches is a full additional hour of soccer, and Spain are the worst possible opponent to face tired.

The tactical shape of the final is not a mystery. Spain will have the ball and Argentina will let them, sitting compact and springing Alvarez and Messi into the space behind the high line, the same transition game that punished England. Spain's answer is Rodri's positioning and a back line that has conceded once in four knockout matches. The team that has controlled every match meets the team that has survived every match, and something has to give at MetLife. Slavko Vincic of Slovenia referees, and FOX and Telemundo carry it in the United States. The full single-game breakdown is on the Featured Game page.

NWSL - League Table
NWSL

Washington Spirit @ Boston Legacy FC

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Centreville Bank Stadium, Pawtucket, RI

Hours after the World Cup final ends, the NWSL gives the day a second act in Rhode Island. The Washington Spirit arrive sitting third in the table on 27 points at 8 wins, 3 draws and 3 losses, and the number that separates them from the pack is the plus-11 goal difference, the best in the league. Ahead of them, Gotham FC lead on 30 points and San Diego sit second on 29, so every road trip like this one is a chance to keep the pressure on the top two.

Boston Legacy FC are living the expansion grind. The league's new club sits thirteenth on 16 points at 4 wins, 4 draws and 7 losses with a minus-5 goal difference, and home matches at Centreville Bank Stadium in Pawtucket are where the points have to come from. Four draws tells you this team hangs around in matches; seven losses tells you it has struggled to land the final punch against the league's established sides.

The gap in the table frames the assignment. Washington's league-best goal difference is built on beating the teams below them, which is precisely what a third-place side chasing two leaders has to keep doing down the stretch. For Boston, a first-year club hosting one of the league's best on the night the entire soccer world is watching finals highlights is a free swing: nobody expects the points, and an expansion side with four draws in fifteen matches has already shown it can make good teams uncomfortable for ninety minutes.
