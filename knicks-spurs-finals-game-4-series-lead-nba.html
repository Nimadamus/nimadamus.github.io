Game 4 at the Garden: Knicks Try to Build the Wall

The NBA Finals take a breath Tuesday before the loudest building in basketball reopens for Game 4 on Wednesday, 8:30 PM ET on ABC. New York leads San Antonio 2-1 but is coming off a sting, a 115-111 Game 3 loss on its own floor where Victor Wembanyama took over Madison Square Garden. The early market still trusts the Knicks, listing them a 1.5-point favorite at home with the total at 215.5.

NBA FinalsGame 4Knicks Lead 2-1

There is no game on the board Tuesday, June 9, which makes this the calm before a pivotal Game 4. New York leads the Finals 2-1, but the shape of the series shifted Monday night when San Antonio walked into Madison Square Garden and left with a 115-111 win to climb back within a game. Wednesday is the swing point of the entire series: win it, and the Knicks are one victory from a title with two of a possible three to come; lose it, and a 2-1 cushion becomes a 2-2 coin flip with home court already split.

How We Got Here

The Knicks grabbed control early, taking the opener 105-95 in San Antonio behind a closing kick that turned a tight game into a comfortable one, then carried that momentum to a 2-0 lead before the series swung back to New York. The Spurs answered the only way a team this talented could, stealing Game 3 on the road to make it 2-1. That 115-111 final was San Antonio's first win of the series and a reminder that a 2-0 hole is not a death sentence when you have the best young player on the floor.

Wembanyama Found The Garden

Game 3 belonged to Victor Wembanyama, who silenced a hostile Madison Square Garden crowd with the kind of two-way performance the Spurs had been waiting for all series. After a quiet, foul-line-dependent Finals debut, he looked comfortable attacking in space and protecting the rim, and San Antonio's offense flowed through him instead of around him. If that version of Wembanyama shows up again Wednesday, New York's margin for error shrinks dramatically, because the Knicks built this 2-1 lead largely by making him uncomfortable in the half court.

New York's Counterpunch

The Knicks are not a team that panics, and they will lean on the same identity that produced a deep playoff run: Jalen Brunson creating in crunch time, Karl-Anthony Towns stressing the Spurs on the glass and from the perimeter, and the wing defense of OG Anunoby and Josh Hart muddying San Antonio's actions. Head coach Mike Brown spent the aftermath of Game 3 pointing at a free-throw disparity, a sign New York feels it can win the physical battle if the whistle evens out. At home, with the crowd back on its side, the Knicks have every reason to believe they reset the tone in Game 4.

The Market And The Stakes

The number tells the story of a series the books see as razor-thin. New York is only a 1.5-point favorite despite leading 2-1 and playing at home, with a moneyline of minus-125 to San Antonio's plus-105, essentially a pick'em with a homecourt nudge. The total of 215.5 splits the difference between New York's grind-it-out defense and the 226 points the teams combined for in Game 3. For San Antonio, Wednesday is a must-win in every sense short of elimination; drop it and the Spurs are down 3-1 with the math turning brutal. For New York, it is the chance to put a foot on the throat of a series it has led from the opening night. Game 4 tips Wednesday at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.
