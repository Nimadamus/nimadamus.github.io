Manchester United vs West Ham United

Premier League 3:00 PM ET Old Trafford USA Network Line: MU -240 O/U: 3.5

Manchester United hosts West Ham at Old Trafford in a Premier League clash between two teams heading in opposite directions. The Red Devils are 6-3-4 (W-D-L) with 21 points and sitting in the top half of the table, while the Hammers are struggling at 3-2-8 with just 11 points.

United's Home Form: Old Trafford has been a fortress under Ruben Amorim, with United winning 4 of their last 5 at home. Marcus Rashford has found his form with 8 goals this season, and Bruno Fernandes continues to orchestrate the attack with 6 assists. The new 3-4-3 system is producing more cohesive football.

West Ham's Woes: The Hammers are in deep trouble. Just 3 wins from 13 matches has manager Julen Lopetegui on the hot seat. Their away record is particularly dire - 0 wins on the road with just 4 goals scored. Jarrod Bowen has 5 goals but the supporting cast has been invisible.

The Play: United -240 is steep, but West Ham's woeful away form makes it hard to back them. The value might be in United to win and Over 2.5 goals at plus money. Expect United to control possession and create chances against a demoralized West Ham defense.

Key Stat: Manchester United have scored in their last 12 home Premier League matches. West Ham have kept just 1 clean sheet on the road this season.

Arsenal vs Brentford

Premier League 2:30 PM ET Emirates Stadium Peacock Line: ARS -320 O/U: 2.5

Arsenal continue their title chase as they host Brentford at the Emirates. The Gunners sit at 10-3-1 with 33 points, just behind Liverpool in the table. Brentford are a solid mid-table side at 6-1-7 with 19 points and have shown they can be dangerous on the counter.

Arsenal's Title Charge: Mikel Arteta's side are playing some of the best football in Europe. Bukayo Saka (8 goals, 7 assists) is world-class, and Martin Odegaard's return from injury has lifted the entire team. Arsenal have won 6 of their last 7 at home, conceding just 4 goals.

Brentford's Challenge: The Bees upset Arsenal 2-0 at the Emirates last season, so they know how to frustrate the Gunners. Ivan Toney's physicality causes problems for any defense, and Bryan Mbeumo (7 goals) has been clinical. Thomas Frank sets up disciplined, compact defensive units on the road.

The Play: Arsenal should win, but -320 is too much juice. Look at Arsenal -1.5 (+110) if you believe in a comfortable home win. The Under 2.5 is also worth considering - Brentford will sit deep and make Arsenal work for every chance.

Key Stat: Arsenal have kept 5 clean sheets in their last 7 home matches. Brentford's away record is 3-0-4 (W-D-L).
Sources: ESPN, Premier League, Team Statistics

Brighton vs Aston Villa

Premier League 2:30 PM ET Amex Stadium Peacock Line: Pick'em O/U: 2.5

An intriguing match between two ambitious clubs. Brighton are 6-4-4 with 22 points under new manager Fabian Hurzeler, while Aston Villa are 8-3-3 with 27 points and competing on multiple fronts including the Champions League.

Brighton's Transition: The Seagulls have adapted well to Hurzeler's high-pressing style after losing Roberto De Zerbi. Kaoru Mitoma remains their most dangerous attacker, and the Amex is a tough place to visit. Brighton have lost just 1 home match this season.

Villa's European Hangover?: Unai Emery has Villa playing excellent football, with Ollie Watkins (9 goals) leading the line superbly. However, Champions League commitments can take a toll on squad depth. Villa's away form is strong at 4-1-2, but they've shown vulnerability when rotation kicks in.

The Play: This is a genuine pick'em match. Both teams play attractive, attacking football. The Over 2.5 goals at -110 looks solid - both teams want to play on the front foot and create chances. A high-scoring draw wouldn't surprise anyone.

Key Stat: These two teams combined for 6 goals in their last meeting. Brighton are unbeaten at home in their last 6.
Sources: ESPN, Premier League, Team Statistics

Wolves vs Nottingham Forest

Premier League 2:30 PM ET Molineux Peacock Line: FOR +150 O/U: 2.5

Wolves are in crisis at 0-2-12, sitting dead last in the Premier League with just 2 points. Nottingham Forest are 4-3-7 with 15 points and fighting to stay above the relegation zone. This is a massive six-pointer for both clubs.

Wolves' Disaster Season: Zero wins through 14 matches is historically bad. Gary O'Neil was sacked last week, and the club is in complete disarray. Matheus Cunha remains talented but has no support. Molineux has turned toxic, with fans booing the team regularly.

Forest's Survival Fight: Nuno Espirito Santo knows Molineux well from his time as Wolves manager. Forest have shown fight and Chris Wood (7 goals) has been excellent up front. Away from the City Ground, Forest are 2-2-3 - not great, but infinitely better than Wolves' home record of 0-1-6.

The Play: Forest at +150 is tremendous value. Wolves are broken and showing no signs of recovery. Back Forest to win or draw double chance at -170 for a safer play. The Under 2.5 is also worth considering - Wolves can't score.

Key Stat: Wolves have scored just 8 goals all season (worst in PL). Forest have won 3 of their last 5 away from home.
