6-Game Sunday Slate

NHL Previews Today

Sunday's 6-game slate is compact but loaded with playoff urgency. The Penguins and Capitals meet for the second straight day in a home-and-home series on TNT, with Pittsburgh ravaged by injuries and Washington clinging to wild card life. Boston's stunning 5-game losing streak puts their playoff spot in genuine jeopardy as they visit Columbus, Montreal pushes to close the gap on Buffalo for Atlantic 2, and Utah celebrates clinching WC1 with a trip to Calgary. Every game on this board carries weight heading into the final week of the regular season.

Game 1 - Marquee

Penguins @ Capitals

Sunday, 3:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena, Washington, DC
Puck Line
WSH -1.5 (+145)
Moneyline
WSH -160 / PIT +135
Total
O/U 6.5 (O -115/U -105)

This is the kind of game that makes the final week of the NHL season absolutely electric. Pittsburgh and Washington played each other yesterday in Pittsburgh, and now they flip venues for the back half of a home-and-home that has massive implications for the Capitals' playoff hopes. Washington at 41-30-9 with 91 points is fighting for its postseason life, sitting on the outside of the Metro playoff picture and needing every possible point down the stretch. The Capitals are a desperate team right now, and desperate teams playing at home with their season on the line are dangerous. The -160 moneyline reflects both Washington's urgency and the very real fact that Pittsburgh is playing this game with what can only be described as a MASH unit roster.

The Penguins' injury report reads like a casualty list from a war zone. Sidney Crosby, Evgeni Malkin, Bryan Rust, Erik Karlsson, and Kris Letang are all listed as day-to-day, and while some of them may gut it out for a Sunday matinee on national television, the sheer volume of banged-up elite talent is staggering. Pittsburgh at 41-23-16 with 98 points has already locked down the Metro 2 seed, so there's a legitimate argument for resting some of these guys and getting them healthy for the playoffs. That calculus makes Washington's -160 price look very reasonable, because even if Crosby or Malkin tries to play through whatever is ailing them, they're not going to be at 100%, and the Capitals will be skating like their season depends on it, because it does.

Washington has its own injury concerns with Pierre-Luc Dubois and Rasmus Sandin both dealing with issues, but the Capitals' depth has been tested all season and they've found ways to compete. The goaltending matchup features Tristan Jarry's backup Alex Nedeljkovic or potentially Skinner for Pittsburgh against Logan Thompson for Washington, and Thompson has been solid enough in the Capitals' crease to keep them in games against quality opponents. The 6.5 total with the over at -115 tells you the market expects this one to have some offense, and that makes sense when you consider Pittsburgh's defensive corps could be missing multiple regulars. If Karlsson and Letang are both out, the Penguins' blue line is going to have a very difficult time containing Washington's attack.

The TNT broadcast at 3:00 PM gives this game a playoff atmosphere before the playoffs even start. Washington's fans know what's at stake, and Capital One Arena should be rocking for what could be one of the most important regular season games in recent franchise history. Pittsburgh's locked into their seed and banged up beyond belief. Washington needs these two points like they need oxygen. The home-and-home format adds another layer of familiarity, both teams know exactly what the other did yesterday, what worked, what didn't, and now they get to adjust in real time. This is appointment viewing for any hockey fan, and the Capitals' desperation factor combined with Pittsburgh's injury crisis makes this one of the most compelling matchups on the entire Sunday slate.

Game 2
ESPN+

Canadiens @ Islanders

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | UBS Arena, Elmont, NY
Puck Line
MTL +1.5 (-270) / NYI -1.5 (+220)
Moneyline
MTL -110 / NYI -110
Total
O/U 6.5

Montreal at 47-23-10 with 104 points has been one of the best stories in the entire NHL this season, and the Canadiens are still pushing hard for the Atlantic 2 seed with Buffalo sitting at 106 points just two points ahead. Every game matters for Montreal down the stretch, and a win here would keep the pressure squarely on the Sabres in what has become a thrilling two-team race for second place in the Atlantic Division. The Canadiens have earned their 104 points through a combination of elite goaltending, a balanced offensive attack, and the kind of team-first culture that makes playoff teams dangerous. This is a squad that believes in itself, and with the Atlantic 2 seed still very much within reach, Montreal isn't going to coast into the postseason.

The Islanders at 43-32-5 with 91 points are chasing a Metro 3 spot and trailing Philadelphia in the standings, so New York has its own motivation here. This isn't an eliminated team going through the motions. The Isles need points to stay alive in the playoff race, and UBS Arena has been a tough building for visitors all season. Ilya Sorokin in net gives the Islanders a legitimate chance to steal any game, and his ability to keep the score close against high-powered offenses has been the backbone of New York's season. The pick'em moneyline at -110 on both sides tells you everything you need to know about how the market views this matchup. These are two evenly matched teams with significant playoff implications on the line for both sides.

The goaltending duel between Fowler for Montreal and Sorokin for New York has the potential to be the story of this game. Both netminders have been excellent this season, and in a game where both teams are playing with playoff desperation, you can expect tight checking and limited space through the neutral zone. The 6.5 total suggests the market thinks there's enough offensive talent on both sides to produce goals, but don't be surprised if this turns into a lower-scoring affair driven by goaltending and defensive structure. Montreal's two-way game has been their calling card all year, and the Islanders under their system play a style that can suffocate teams that rely on transition offense.

What makes this game particularly intriguing is the playoff positioning chess match happening in real time across the Eastern Conference. Montreal winning here doesn't just help the Canadiens, it also hurts the Islanders in the Metro race. And New York winning doesn't just help their own cause, it potentially creates breathing room for other teams chasing Atlantic positioning. These are the kinds of interconnected storylines that make the final week of the NHL season so compelling. Both teams are playing for something meaningful, both have the talent to win, and the pick'em line reflects a genuine toss-up that could go either way based on which goaltender has the better night.

Game 3 - Marquee
NHL Network

Bruins @ Blue Jackets

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Nationwide Arena, Columbus, OH
Puck Line
CBJ -1.5 (+165) / BOS +1.5 (-200)
Moneyline
CBJ -145 / BOS +118
Total
O/U 6.0

If you told anyone in October that the Boston Bruins would be sitting at 96 points in mid-April and still wouldn't have clinched a playoff spot, they would have laughed in your face. But here we are. Boston is in the middle of a genuinely alarming 5-game losing streak, going 0-2-2-1 over that stretch, and the Bruins' grip on a playoff position is loosening by the day. At 96 points, Boston still has a path to the postseason, but the margin for error has completely evaporated. The Bruins cannot afford another loss, especially not to a Columbus team that is 4 points behind Ottawa for the second wild card spot and playing with the kind of urgency that comes from knowing your season could end with one bad week.

Columbus at 40-28-12 with 92 points has been quietly impressive all season, and the Blue Jackets find themselves in a position that seemed unlikely back in October: legitimate playoff contenders in April. Columbus is 4 points behind Ottawa's 96 for WC2, which means they need wins and they need help, but crazier things have happened in the final week of an NHL season. Nationwide Arena is going to be electric for this one because the home crowd knows exactly what's at stake. The -145 moneyline makes Columbus a solid favorite, and that price reflects both the home ice advantage and the fact that Boston has looked like a team that's lost its identity over the past two weeks. When you're losing five straight and suddenly playing with your back against the wall, confidence becomes a very fragile thing.

The broader Eastern Conference picture makes this game even more fascinating. Colorado has already locked up the Presidents' Trophy at 115 points. Carolina leads the East with 110. Buffalo's at 106 in the Atlantic. But below that top tier, it's absolute chaos. Ottawa and Boston are both at 96 points. Columbus is at 92. The Islanders and Capitals are at 91. And the defending back-to-back Stanley Cup champion Florida Panthers? Eliminated at 80 points. That's right, the team that won the Cup two years in a row is going home, while the Bruins, Blue Jackets, and several other teams are fighting tooth and nail for the final playoff spots. This is what makes the NHL's parity so compelling and so agonizing for the teams involved.

The 6.0 total is notably lower than most games on this slate, and that tells you the market expects a tight, grinding affair between two teams that can't afford to play loose defensively. Boston's problems during this losing streak haven't been about a lack of talent. They still have the personnel to compete with anyone in the league. The issue has been execution, composure under pressure, and the kind of mental toughness that separates teams that make the playoffs from teams that collapse down the stretch. Columbus doesn't have the star power that Boston does, but the Blue Jackets play a structured, physical brand of hockey that can frustrate opponents into mistakes. This is a must-watch game on NHL Network, and whoever loses might be looking at their season ending before it even gets to the postseason.

Game 4
ESPN+

Senators @ Devils

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Prudential Center, Newark, NJ
Puck Line
OTT -1.5 (+185) / NJ +1.5 (-225)
Moneyline
OTT -130 / NJ +110
Total
O/U 6.5

Ottawa at 43-27-10 with 96 points is sitting in the WC2 position and cannot afford to let up with Boston and Columbus breathing down their necks. The Senators have put together a remarkable season that has them on the verge of their first playoff appearance in years, and the franchise's young core has finally started to deliver on the promise that Ottawa's front office has been betting on. At 96 points, the Senators are in a position of strength, but nothing is guaranteed when you're only 4 points ahead of Columbus and tied with a Boston team that, despite its current losing streak, has the talent to rattle off wins in a hurry. Ottawa needs to keep banking points, and a road game against an eliminated Devils team looks like a golden opportunity on paper.

New Jersey at 41-36-3 with 85 points has been eliminated from playoff contention, and the Devils' season has been one of unfulfilled potential. The biggest blow came when Jacob Markstrom, their number one goaltender, was shut down for the remainder of the season, leaving Nico Daws to handle the crease for the final stretch. That's a significant downgrade in net, and it's reflected in the +110 moneyline that makes New Jersey a modest home underdog despite playing in front of their own fans at the Prudential Center. The Devils still have offensive talent that can create problems for anyone, but without their starting goalie and without anything tangible to play for, the motivation gap between these two teams is massive.

The -130 moneyline on Ottawa feels like a fair price for a team that needs the win visiting a team that doesn't. The Senators have been one of the more consistent road teams in the Eastern Conference this season, and their ability to win away from home has been a key factor in getting them to 96 points. Tim Stutzle, Brady Tkachuk, and the Ottawa offense have the kind of firepower that can exploit a backup goaltender, and with Daws in net for New Jersey, the Senators should be able to generate quality scoring chances. The 6.5 total reflects the expectation of offense, and Ottawa's attack against a weakened Devils defense should be able to push toward four or more goals.

The biggest risk for Ottawa in this game is the classic letdown factor. When you're a team fighting for a playoff spot and you see an eliminated opponent on the schedule, there's a natural tendency to look ahead, to assume the points are already in your pocket before the puck even drops. The Devils still have pride, they still have young players auditioning for next season, and they still have enough talent to make life difficult for teams that don't bring their best effort. But if the Senators play with the urgency that their standings position demands, this should be a game they control from start to finish. Ottawa can't afford charity points right now, and a professional road performance against New Jersey would go a long way toward locking down that WC2 spot.

Canucks @ Ducks

Sunday, 8:00 PM ET | Honda Center, Anaheim, CA
Puck Line
ANA -1.5 (-125) / VAN +1.5 (+105)
Moneyline
ANA -305 / VAN +245
Total
O/U 6.5

Vancouver at 23-48-8 with 54 points owns the worst record in the entire NHL, and the Canucks' season has been an absolute catastrophe from start to finish. This is a team that came into the year with some optimism about their rebuild trajectory, and instead they've delivered the kind of season that gets people fired from top to bottom. At 54 points with the regular season winding down, Vancouver is playing purely for pride and draft lottery positioning at this point. The +245 moneyline reflects the enormous talent gap between these two teams, and while the Canucks will show up and compete because that's what professional athletes do, the reality is that this is a team that has been overmatched against quality opponents all year long.

Anaheim at 42-32-5 with 89 points is holding down the Pacific 3 spot and fighting to solidify their playoff positioning heading into the final week. The Ducks' resurgence this season has been one of the more pleasant surprises in the Western Conference, and Anaheim's young core has shown the kind of development that gives the franchise legitimate hope for the future. Lukas Dostal has been excellent in net, providing the Ducks with the kind of goaltending that keeps them competitive in tight games, and the team's offensive depth has been a key factor in their ability to pile up wins against teams at the bottom of the standings. The -305 moneyline makes Anaheim a massive favorite, and that's entirely justified given the gap in quality between these rosters.

The puck line at ANA -1.5 (-125) is actually the more interesting number in this game because it's asking whether Anaheim can win by two or more against the league's worst team, and the answer should be yes more often than not. The Ducks have been a strong home team this season, and the Honda Center crowd has embraced this team in a way that hasn't been felt in Anaheim for years. Dostal in goal against Tolopilo for Vancouver is a significant goaltending edge, and Anaheim's forwards should be able to create chances at will against a Canucks defensive structure that has been porous all season. Vancouver has been competitive in some games this year, but the Canucks simply don't have the depth or consistency to keep up with a team of Anaheim's caliber over a full 60 minutes.

For Anaheim, this is a business trip. Get in, get the two points, and move on to whatever comes next. The Ducks can't afford to overlook anyone with their playoff positioning still being determined, but this is about as favorable a matchup as you'll find on the Sunday slate. Vancouver's season is a cautionary tale about what happens when the rebuild goes sideways, and the Canucks are counting down the days until the offseason when they can start putting the pieces together for a more competitive future. In the meantime, Anaheim should handle this one comfortably and continue their push toward home ice in the first round of the Western Conference playoffs.

Utah @ Flames

Puck Line
UTA -1.5 (+160) / CGY +1.5 (-192)
Moneyline
UTA -162 / CGY +136
Total
O/U 5.5

Utah at 42-31-6 with 90 points has clinched the WC1 spot in the Western Conference, and the Mammoth can play this one with the comfort of knowing their playoff position is secured. That's a luxury that very few teams on this Sunday slate can afford, and it creates an interesting dynamic for this game. Does Utah rest key players and start preparing for the first round? Or do they try to build momentum and keep their game sharp heading into the postseason? The -162 moneyline suggests the market believes Utah will still compete at a high level regardless of the circumstances, and that's a testament to the organizational culture that has been built in Salt Lake City during the franchise's first full season.

Calgary at 32-38-9 with 73 points has been eliminated from playoff contention, and the Flames' season has been a disappointing one for a franchise that was hoping to be more competitive in a wide-open Western Conference. The Saddledome has seen better days, and the Calgary faithful have spent much of this season watching a team that doesn't quite have enough talent at the top of the roster to compete with the league's best. The +136 moneyline makes the Flames a moderate home underdog, and that's a reasonable price for an eliminated team that still has enough skill to compete on home ice. Calgary's young players will be motivated to finish the season strong and show the front office that they belong in the NHL going forward.

The 5.5 total is the lowest on the entire Sunday slate, and that tells you the market expects a tighter, more defensive game than the other five matchups on the board today. Utah has been built on structure and defensive responsibility all season, and even with their playoff spot clinched, the Mammoth aren't going to abandon the principles that got them here. Calgary's offense has been inconsistent all year, and the Flames' inability to score consistently has been one of the primary reasons they're already eliminated. When you combine Utah's defensive identity with Calgary's offensive struggles, a lower-scoring game makes a lot of sense.

For the broader NHL picture, the most fascinating storyline of this entire regular season might be the fact that the defending back-to-back Stanley Cup champion Florida Panthers are eliminated at just 80 points while the expansion-era Utah Mammoth have clinched a playoff spot in their first year. That's the beauty of hockey's parity. The Panthers' fall from grace has been stunning, and it serves as a reminder that nothing is guaranteed in this league, no matter how many championships you've won. Utah's accomplishment is remarkable, and regardless of what happens in this game against Calgary, the Mammoth have already exceeded every reasonable expectation for a franchise in its inaugural NHL season. This nightcap closes out a Sunday slate that has delivered exactly what the final week of the regular season is supposed to: meaningful hockey with playoff implications in almost every game.
