4-Game Monday Slate

NBA March 2, 2026

Monday's compact 4-game slate is headlined by a fascinating Celtics-Bucks showdown on NBA TV, where both teams will play without their franchise cornerstones as Tatum and Giannis continue rehabbing. Nikola Jokic and the NBA's highest-scoring offense in Denver visit Utah's league-worst defense. A historically depleted Warriors squad missing Curry, Butler, and Porzingis hosts the Clippers on Peacock. And the Rockets lay a massive 15.5-point number in Washington against the lowly Wizards.

Rockets @ Wizards

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena, Washington, D.C.
Spread
HOU -15.5
Moneyline
HOU -1200 / WAS +750
Total
O/U 224

Fifteen-and-a-half points on the road. That's the number hanging over this one, and the fact that Houston at 37-22 is laying that kind of lumber in somebody else's building tells you everything about how the market views the gap between these two rosters. The Rockets have been one of the best defensive teams in the NBA all season, allowing just 109.2 points per game, which ranks third in the entire league. That defense has been the foundation of everything Houston does, and it's the reason this team has been able to grind out wins even during stretches where the offense hasn't been firing on all cylinders. Alperen Sengun has been the engine of this operation, putting up 20.2 points and 9.1 rebounds per game with a polished post game and passing ability that makes him one of the most unique centers in basketball.

Washington at 16-43 is one of the worst teams in the NBA, and Monday night at Capital One Arena is going to feel like a developmental scrimmage against a team that's simply operating on a different level. The Wizards allow 123.0 points per game, which ranks 29th in the league, and their inability to get stops on the defensive end has turned most of their games into lopsided affairs where the outcome is decided by halftime. Kyshawn George has been a bright spot in an otherwise bleak season, averaging 14.8 points, 5.2 rebounds, and 4.5 assists per game, and rookie Will Riley has shown flashes with 14 points per game over his last 10. But individual performances on a roster this depleted don't translate into competitive basketball against a team of Houston's caliber.

The Rockets won the first meeting this season 135-112 back on November 13, and Houston is a perfect 6-0 ATS in their last six meetings with Washington. That trend reflects the reality that the Rockets simply have too much talent, too much depth, and too much defensive intensity for the Wizards to stay within striking distance. Reed Sheppard has given Houston another young weapon on the perimeter, and the Rockets' supporting cast has been reliable enough to maintain the defensive standard even when the starters sit. Houston is dealing with injuries to Fred VanVleet (season-ending torn ACL), Steven Adams (ankle), Jabari Smith Jr. (ankle), and Jae'Sean Tate (knee), but the depth of this roster has allowed them to absorb those losses without a significant dip in performance.

Washington's injury list is equally brutal, with Anthony Davis out for the season with a finger injury, Cam Whitmore done for the year with a shoulder issue, and Alex Sarr sidelined with a hamstring injury. Trae Young is day-to-day with a knee issue, and D'Angelo Russell is also listed as day-to-day. Even if Young and Russell play, neither one is going to single-handedly close a 15-point gap against a defense this suffocating. The 224 total is reasonable given that Houston's defense should keep the scoring contained on Washington's end, while the Rockets' 114.5 points per game on offense gives them plenty of firepower to run away with this one early. The Wizards are simply overmatched in every phase of the game, and this one has the feel of a blowout that starts in the first quarter and never gets interesting.

Celtics @ Bucks

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee, WI
Spread
BOS -7
Moneyline

This is the game of the night, and the storyline writes itself. Two of the NBA's most storied Eastern Conference franchises meet on NBA TV, and neither team will have its franchise player on the floor. Jayson Tatum continues rehabbing his Achilles injury in Boston, though the encouraging news for Celtics fans is that he's reportedly doing 5-on-5 scrimmages and eyeing a return sometime in March. Giannis Antetokounmpo is now in week six of recovery from a calf strain that's kept him out since late January. It's a strange game in that both teams are competing without the player their entire identity was built around, and yet both have managed to stay afloat through sheer effort and depth.

Boston at 39-20 has been remarkable without Tatum, and a 9-1 record over their last 10 games is the kind of stretch that should put the rest of the East on notice. Jaylen Brown has been carrying this team on his back, and the Celtics' defensive identity hasn't skipped a beat despite the roster upheaval. The system Joe Mazzulla has built is bigger than any single player, and Boston's ability to switch, contest, and protect the rim at a high level means opponents are still facing one of the toughest defensive environments in the league every time they step on the floor at TD Garden or on the road. Baylor Scheierman is questionable with a thumb fracture, which could thin the rotation slightly, but Boston has shown all season that next-man-up isn't just a cliche for this group.

Milwaukee at 26-32 is playing on the second night of a back-to-back after facing the Bulls on March 1, and fatigue could be a real factor for a team that's already operating with a short rotation. The Bucks have won 8 of their last 11, which is genuinely impressive given the circumstances, and they've gone 3-2 ATS over their last five games, showing they can hang with the number even when the market expects them to be outclassed. Ryan Rollins and Kevin Porter Jr. have emerged as unlikely contributors during Giannis' absence, and the collective effort on both ends of the floor has kept Milwaukee competitive in games where the talent disparity suggests they should be getting blown out. Taurean Prince is out with a herniated disc, further thinning the rotation on the wing.

The BOS -7 spread is significant, but it reflects the reality that the Celtics' depth and system are simply more sustainable than what Milwaukee can piece together with role players and reclamation projects. The 215.5 total is the lowest on Monday's board, which paints a picture of a grinding, defensive affair where possessions are precious and neither team is going to light up the scoreboard. That feels right given the personnel available to both teams. Boston's defense should clamp down on Milwaukee's limited offensive creation, and the Bucks' best chance at covering is making this an ugly, physical game where possessions get messy and the Celtics can't get into their half-court flow. The back-to-back factor for Milwaukee is the wild card here, because tired legs in the second half could turn a competitive game into a comfortable Boston victory in the final 12 minutes.

Nuggets @ Jazz

Monday, 9:00 PM ET | Delta Center, Salt Lake City, UT
Spread
DEN -11.5
Moneyline
DEN -535 / UTA +400
Total
O/U 244.5

The 244.5 total is the highest on Monday's board by a country mile, and one look at these two rosters tells you exactly why. Denver at 37-23 leads the entire NBA in scoring at 120.5 points per game, shooting 49.2% from the field and 39.3% from three, and they're walking into the Delta Center to face a Utah defense that allows 125.9 points per game, which is dead last in the league. That's the NBA's most explosive offense against the NBA's worst defense. The math doesn't require advanced analytics to understand. Nikola Jokic is putting up one of the most absurd statistical seasons in basketball history, averaging 29.4 points, 12.1 rebounds, and 10.7 assists per game on 60.5% shooting from the field. He's a legitimate triple-double machine who makes everyone around him significantly better, and against a Jazz defense that doesn't have the personnel to throw multiple bodies at him, Jokic could put up a 40-point triple-double without breaking a sweat.

Utah at 18-42 has been one of the worst teams in the league all season, but here's what makes this game interesting from a total perspective: the Jazz can score. They're averaging 118.0 points per game, and their 29.9 assists per game rank second in the entire NBA, which means they move the ball and generate open looks even if they can't get stops on the other end. That's the profile of a team that's going to push the pace, take a lot of shots, and make enough of them to keep the scoreboard spinning even while getting blown out. If Lauri Markkanen plays, and he's questionable with a groin issue, Utah has a genuine 27.8 points per game scorer who can go toe-to-toe with anyone offensively even if his team has no shot at winning the game.

Denver's injury situation is worth monitoring. Aaron Gordon has missed 15+ games with a hamstring injury, and Peyton Watson is out with a hamstring issue of his own. Cameron Johnson is probable with an ankle issue, and Spencer Jones is questionable. Even without Gordon, the Nuggets' offense hasn't missed a beat because Jokic elevates everyone around him to a level they can't reach without him. The Nuggets have gone 2-3 over their last five games, which suggests some inconsistency, but the losses have come against quality opponents, not teams on Utah's level. When Denver locks in against inferior competition, the results are usually decisive and emphatic.

Utah is similarly banged up, with Walker Kessler out for the season after shoulder surgery, Jaren Jackson Jr. sidelined following knee surgery, Kevin Love sitting with rest. That's a lot of frontcourt depth that's unavailable, which is going to be a massive problem when Jokic is operating in the post with nobody capable of even slowing him down. The DEN -11.5 spread feels like it accounts for Denver's recent inconsistency, because on paper, this mismatch is closer to a 15-to-20-point game. The Jazz don't have the defense to contain Jokic, don't have the rim protection to deter drives, and don't have the perimeter defense to slow down Denver's shooters when Jokic finds them with his ridiculous passing. This one could turn into a track meet where both teams score in the 120s, with the Nuggets pulling away decisively in the second half.

Clippers @ Warriors

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Chase Center, San Francisco, CA
Spread
LAC -1.5
Moneyline
LAC -118 / GS -102
Total
O/U 219.5

Golden State at 31-29 is the home team here, but the Warriors are listed as slight underdogs at their own Chase Center, and one glance at the injury report explains everything. This might be the most depleted roster in the league right now. Stephen Curry has missed 10 games with a knee injury. Jimmy Butler, acquired from the Heat in February 2025, is out for the season with a torn ACL. Kristaps Porzingis is sidelined with an Achilles issue and illness. Seth Curry is dealing with a back injury. LJ Cryer is out with a hamstring issue. And to make matters worse, Draymond Green is day-to-day with an illness, De'Anthony Melton is day-to-day with a knee problem, and Will Richard is dealing with an ankle issue. Golden State is 8-13 without Curry this season, and the list of players available for Monday night could be historically short for a team with championship aspirations.

The Warriors do have one thing going for them, and it's significant: they lead the entire NBA in three-point shooting at 42.4% from beyond the arc, and they're averaging 115.6 points per game. Even shorthanded, Golden State's system creates open threes through constant motion, screening, and ball movement, and the players who are available know the system well enough to keep the offense humming at a reasonable level. The Warriors have won three straight games despite the depleted roster, and they're a remarkable 20-11-1 ATS at Chase Center this season. The home crowd has been a legitimate factor, and the energy inside that building has willed this team to competitive performances even when the personnel says they should be getting blown out.

The Clippers at 27-31 arrive in San Francisco on a three-game losing streak, shooting just 32.9% from three, and dealing with their own set of injury concerns. Bradley Beal is done for the season with a hip injury. Darius Garland is out with a toe issue. Kawhi Leonard is questionable with an ankle problem, and John Collins is questionable with a head injury. LA is averaging 111.7 points per game, and their offense has been inconsistent at best when Leonard isn't on the floor. The Clippers need this game for their play-in positioning, and the urgency factor could work in their favor against a Warriors team that might be running on fumes by the fourth quarter.

The LAC -1.5 spread and the nearly even moneyline (LAC -118, GS -102) tell you the market sees this as a coin flip with the slightest edge to the visitors. That makes sense when you consider that the Clippers, even without Beal and potentially without Leonard, still have more available bodies and more reliable scoring options than whatever Golden State can piece together from the remains of their roster. The 219.5 total is the lowest on Monday's board, suggesting a slower-paced affair where both teams struggle to score consistently. The fascinating subplot here is whether Golden State's incredible home ATS record and three-point shooting can overcome one of the most brutal injury situations in recent NBA memory. Chase Center has been a fortress this season, and the Warriors have consistently outperformed expectations in that building, but asking them to compete without Curry, Butler, Porzingis, and potentially Green is an enormous ask even for a team with championship DNA.
