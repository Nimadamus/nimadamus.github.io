NFL Week 16 TNF

Epic NFC West showdown ends in overtime thriller at Lumen Field

Rams @ Seahawks

Thursday, December 19, 2025 | 8:15 PM ET | Lumen Field | Prime Video
Seahawks 38, Rams 37 (OT)
Final - Overtime

What. A. Game. The Seahawks pulled off one of the most incredible comebacks of the season, erasing a 16-point deficit in the final eight minutes to stun the Rams 38-37 in overtime. This was Thursday Night Football at its absolute finest, and it completely reshuffled the NFC West standings with two weeks remaining.

Matthew Stafford was absolutely brilliant for most of the night, throwing for 457 yards and 3 touchdowns. Puka Nacua went nuclear with a career-high 225 receiving yards—the most by a Rams player since Isaac Bruce in 1997. With Davante Adams nursing an injury, Stafford fed Nacua early and often, and it looked like LA had this game locked up at 30-14 with eight minutes left.

Then Seattle flipped the script. Kenneth Walker III exploded for a 55-yard touchdown run that ignited the comeback. Sam Darnold, who had struggled for stretches, found his rhythm when it mattered most. The Seahawks' defense, which had been shredded all night, came up with crucial stops when elimination loomed.

The Game-Winner

In overtime, the Rams struck first, but the Seahawks answered immediately. Darnold found Jaxon Smith-Njigba in the end zone for a 4-yard touchdown, then connected with tight end Eric Saubert on a perfect two-point conversion to seal the victory. The Lumen Field crowd erupted—Seattle had clinched a playoff berth and taken first place in the NFC West with one improbable sequence.

What This Means

The Seahawks (12-3) are now the No. 1 seed in the NFC with two games remaining. The Rams (11-4) dropped from the top seed to the No. 5 wild card slot in the span of three hours. That's the NFL—one game can change everything. Seattle's defense was a liability for most of the night, allowing Stafford to do whatever he wanted, but they made the plays when the season was on the line. That's what playoff teams do.
