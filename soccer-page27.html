Saturday La Liga Results Recap

Matchday 20 Results | January 17, 2026

Before diving into Sunday's slate, here's what happened in La Liga on Saturday. Real Madrid bounced back from their Copa del Rey embarrassment with a 2-0 win over Levante at the Bernabeu. Kylian Mbappe converted a penalty in the 57th minute for his 30th club goal of the season, and Asencio added a header from an Arda Guler corner. It was Alvaro Arbeloa's first league win as manager after replacing Xabi Alonso, though the atmosphere was hostile with fans whistling the players throughout following the shocking midweek loss to second-division Albacete.

Real Mallorca continued their impressive form with a 3-2 victory over Athletic Club, while Real Betis recorded a clean sheet in their 2-0 win against Villarreal. These results keep Madrid within striking distance of Barcelona, trailing by just one point in the standings heading into Sunday's action.

Getafe CF vs Valencia CF

Sunday, 4:00 AM PST | Coliseum Alfonso Perez | La Liga
3-Way ML: Getafe +155 / Draw +170 / Valencia +235 | Total Goals: O/U 2.5 (+240/-302)

The early kick-off features two clubs mired in relegation trouble. Getafe sit 13th with 21 points but have been in freefall, currently riding a six-match winless streak that has seen their cushion above the drop zone shrink dramatically. Valencia's situation is even more precarious in 18th place with just 17 points, desperately needing any positive results to climb out of the danger zone.

The numbers paint an interesting picture here. Getafe have been one of La Liga's most defensive sides, and matches at the Coliseum Alfonso Perez tend to be low-scoring affairs. Valencia won three of their last four meetings with Getafe, which is a curious trend given Los Che's struggles this campaign. That historical edge, combined with their unbeaten run in the head-to-head, gives Valencia some hope despite their table position.

This is the kind of desperate, cagey match that tends to produce few goals. Both sides lack genuine attacking firepower, and the stakes are too high for either manager to encourage an open, expansive style. The Under 2.5 at -302 is heavily juiced for good reason, but there might be value in the Draw at +170 given both teams' inability to win games consistently.

Wolverhampton vs Newcastle United

Sunday, 6:00 AM PST | Molineux Stadium | Premier League
3-Way ML: Wolves +360 / Draw +250 / Newcastle -139 | Total Goals: O/U 3.0 | BTTS: Yes -161

Newcastle make the trip to Molineux with European aspirations on the line. The Magpies sit 9th in the Premier League table and have been outstanding in head-to-head encounters, unbeaten in eight consecutive meetings against Wolves with six wins. That kind of dominance typically doesn't happen by accident, and Newcastle's quality in the final third has been the difference in these fixtures.

Here's what's fascinating about this matchup: Wolves have quietly become one of the toughest teams to score against in the league. Over their last six games, only Arsenal have posted a better expected goals against (xGA) rate than Wolves' 0.99 per 90. That defensive improvement hasn't translated to wins because they simply can't score, but it does suggest Newcastle might not have the easy path to goal that the odds imply.

Newcastle are a completely different proposition on the road compared to their fortress at St. James' Park. In their last 17 Premier League away games, they've scored more than 1.5 goals just three times. That's a genuinely stunning number for a team with this much attacking talent. The discrepancy between home and away output suggests potential value on the under or even Wolves at the massive +360 price.

All 10 Premier League head-to-head games at Molineux have featured both teams scoring, which is why BTTS Yes sits at -161. If you're looking for the most likely outcome based on historical patterns, it's probably a 2-1 Newcastle win. But at -139, you're laying significant juice for a team that struggles to score away from home against a defense that's been quietly excellent.

Atletico Madrid vs Deportivo Alaves

Sunday, 6:15 AM PST | Riyadh Air Metropolitano | La Liga
3-Way ML: Atletico -333 / Draw +425 / Alaves +950 | Total Goals: O/U 2.5 (-114/-114)

Atletico Madrid are absolutely lethal at the Metropolitano. Diego Simeone's side have posted eight wins and just one draw in their nine home league matches this season, creating a fortress that few visiting sides have been able to crack. Currently sitting 4th with 38 points from 19 matches, Los Colchoneros are very much in the title conversation if Barcelona or Real Madrid slip up.

Deportivo Alaves arrive in Madrid with minimal expectations, and rightfully so. The Basque club occupies 16th place with just 19 points and have lost their previous six away matches. That's not a typo. Six consecutive road defeats heading into a trip to one of Europe's most intimidating venues against a team that rarely drops points at home. The numbers paint a bleak picture.

The interesting market here is the total. Atletico's home matches haven't been goal-fests despite their dominance. Simeone's pragmatic approach often sees them score early and then suffocate opponents with their organized defensive structure. Alaves haven't shown the quality to threaten consistently, which could lead to a comfortable but low-scoring Atletico win. The BTTS No at -179 reflects that Alaves simply don't have the firepower to score at the Metropolitano.

Aston Villa vs Everton

Sunday, 8:30 AM PST | Villa Park | Premier League
3-Way ML: Villa -161 / Draw +350 / Everton +449 | Total Goals: O/U 3.0 (-140/+115)

Villa Park has become a genuine fortress under Unai Emery. Aston Villa have won eight consecutive home matches and 14 of their last 20 at home, establishing themselves as one of the toughest places to visit in the Premier League. Against Everton specifically, Villa are unbeaten in all 13 Premier League meetings since their return to the top flight in 2019. That's an absolutely dominant run that speaks to a clear quality gap between these clubs.

The injury report tells a concerning story for Everton. Seamus Coleman, Michael Keane, Idrissa Gueye, Charly Alcaraz, Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall, and Iliman Ndiaye are all confirmed out, with Jarrad Branthwaite and Tim Iroegbunam questionable. When you're already in a relegation battle and travel to face one of the league's form teams, those kind of absences are crippling. Sean Dyche will be working with a skeleton crew.

Villa have injury concerns of their own with Amadou Onana and Ross Barkley out, while Emiliano Martinez and Boubacar Kamara are listed as questionable. But the depth at Villa Park is significantly greater than what Everton can muster. Offensively, Villa rank 7th in the Premier League with 33 goals (1.6 per match), and they should be able to exploit an Everton defense that's missing key pieces.

The Over 3.0 at -140 is interesting because while Everton have been decent defensively this season (6th with 1.2 goals conceded per match), they haven't faced this kind of home dominance with this many players unavailable. Villa's win probability sits at 58% according to predictive models, and the question isn't whether they win but by how much.

RC Celta vs Rayo Vallecano

Sunday, 8:30 AM PST | Estadio de Balaidos | La Liga
3-Way ML: Celta +102 / Draw +210 / Rayo +285 | Total Goals: O/U 2.5 (+120/-152)

Celta Vigo are enjoying one of their best campaigns in recent memory. With 29 points from 19 matches, they've established themselves as a legitimate upper-half side with genuine ambitions. They're unbeaten in their last five La Liga games, winning four of them, and Balaidos has become a difficult venue for visiting teams.

Rayo Vallecano present an interesting case study. Despite being winless in their last four league matches (three defeats), they haven't lost to Celta in their previous five meetings. That head-to-head edge is the kind of stat that can matter in tight games, though it's worth noting that several of those matches came when Celta were in worse form than they currently enjoy.

The Asian Handicap market shows Celta -0.5 at +102, essentially backing them to win at even money. That seems reasonable given their home form and current trajectory. Rayo +0.5 at -127 protects you if they manage a draw, which isn't impossible given the historical trends. The Under 2.5 has a 57% implied probability, suggesting bookmakers expect a tight, tactical affair typical of Spanish football when neither side needs to chase a result desperately.

Real Sociedad vs FC Barcelona

Sunday, 11:00 AM PST | Reale Arena | La Liga
3-Way ML: Sociedad +400 / Draw +330 / Barcelona -185 | Total Goals: O/U 3.0 (+126/-152)

This is the marquee match of the day, and it's not particularly close. Barcelona arrive at the Reale Arena riding an absolutely scorching run of form, eight consecutive La Liga wins to take a four-point lead at the summit. They've controlled the title race since El Clasico in late October and haven't dropped a single point in the league since that showdown with Real Madrid. Hansi Flick has this team playing some of the most entertaining football in Europe.

The numbers back up the eye test. Barcelona have won 11 straight matches across all competitions, including a 3-2 victory over Real Madrid in the Supercopa de España final. Their 49 points from 19 matches represents a genuine title-winning pace, and the +33 goal difference demonstrates the gap between them and the chasing pack. They've covered the -1 Asian Handicap line in 9 consecutive games and 14 of their last 20. That kind of consistency against the spread is remarkable.

Real Sociedad have strung together a five-match unbeaten run of their own, but context matters. They've lost 12 of their last 14 head-to-head meetings with Barcelona. The clubs already met this season in Matchday 7, with Barcelona winning 2-1 after trailing 1-0. Real Sociedad took an early lead and then watched the Catalan giants methodically dismantle them in the second half. That pattern, La Real hanging around before Barcelona's quality tells, has repeated throughout this fixture's recent history.

Here's the betting angle worth considering: Barcelona have taken their time to get going on their travels this season, trailing at halftime on four occasions in La Liga away matches before ultimately winning. Real Sociedad HT/Barcelona FT (1/2) or Draw/Barcelona (X/2) double chance on the correct score market could offer significant value if you expect the visitors to start slowly before their class shows in the second 45 minutes.

The BTTS market sits at reasonable odds because Real Sociedad have shown they can score against elite competition at home. They're not going to roll over, but Barcelona's relentless attacking quality eventually wears down every opponent. Expect this to be competitive through an hour before the gulf in class becomes apparent in the closing stages.
