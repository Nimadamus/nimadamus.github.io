Arsenal vs Paris Saint-Germain

Monday, November 10 | 3:00 PM ET | Emirates Stadium | Champions League
Line: ARS -105 | O/U: 2.5 | ARS: 8-2-2 | PSG: 9-1-2

This is the kind of Champions League clash we live for. Arsenal at home in a European knockout scenario, facing a PSG side that's finally clicking under Luis Enrique. The Emirates will be absolutely rocking for this one, and Arteta has proven he can set up his side to compete against the very best in Europe.

Arsenal's defensive structure has been exceptional this season. Saliba and Gabriel form one of the best center-back pairings in world football, and Rice's ability to break up play in front of them gives the back line extra protection. Saka continues to terrorize left-backs, and Odegaard's creative vision makes Arsenal dangerous on every possession.

PSG, though, have their own weapons. Mbappe is simply unguardable when he's on form, and the supporting cast of Dembele and Barcola gives them pace to burn on the counter. But here's my concern: PSG have historically struggled in big away fixtures in Europe. Arsenal's press will test their buildup, and I like the Gunners to edge this one at home. Under 2.5 feels like the safer play - both teams will respect each other defensively.

Napoli vs Juventus

Monday, November 10 | 2:45 PM ET | Stadio Diego Armando Maradona
Line: NAP -120 | O/U: 2.0 | NAP: 9-3-1 | JUV: 7-5-1

The matchup everyone in Serie A circles for at the start of the season. Napoli at home against their most bitter rivals, in a stadium that's been renamed to honor the greatest player to ever wear the Partenopei shirt. The atmosphere will be absolutely electric, and the pressure on Juventus will be immense.

Antonio Conte has transformed Napoli from last season's disappointment into genuine title contenders again. Osimhen's replacement Lukaku has been surprisingly effective, using his physicality to bully Italian defenders. Kvaratskhelia remains the most exciting player in Serie A, capable of producing moments of individual brilliance that decide big matches.

Juventus are still finding their identity under Thiago Motta. The football is more attractive than the Allegri days, but results have been inconsistent. Vlahovic is their main threat, but he's been isolated too often this season. Away at the Maradona, I don't see Juve getting a result. Napoli's home form has been outstanding, and they'll want to make a statement here. Take the under though - both managers are pragmatic enough to not leave themselves exposed.

Tottenham Hotspur vs Aston Villa

Monday, November 10 | 12:30 PM ET | Tottenham Hotspur Stadium
Line: TOT -115 | O/U: 3.0 | TOT: 6-4-4 | VIL: 8-3-3

Two of the most entertaining teams in the Premier League go head to head, and I'm expecting goals. Spurs under Postecoglou play an incredibly aggressive, high-line style that creates chances at both ends. Son is still pulling the strings in attack, and Maddison's creativity from the 10 position has been a joy to watch when he's on form.

Villa, though, have been one of the stories of the season. Unai Emery has built something special at Villa Park, and they're genuine Champions League contenders. Watkins continues to score for fun, and the midfield trio of McGinn, Tielemans, and Douglas Luiz is as good as any in the league outside the top two.

Here's my take on this one: it's going to be chaotic. Both teams press high, both teams take risks, and both defenses can be exposed. The over 3.0 is where I'm looking. I could see this ending 3-2 either way, or even a wild 4-3. Spurs slight favorites at home makes sense, but Villa are more than capable of stealing all three points. Entertainment guaranteed.

RB Leipzig vs Bayer Leverkusen

Monday, November 10 | 2:30 PM ET | Red Bull Arena
Line: LEV +125 | O/U: 3.0 | RBL: 8-2-3 | B04: 9-2-2

The defending champions travel to Leipzig in what could be a title race defining fixture. Leverkusen were absolutely sensational last season under Xabi Alonso, going unbeaten domestically, but the question is whether they can maintain that level. So far, the answer is yes - they've picked up right where they left off.

Leipzig are always dangerous at home, though. The Red Bull Arena is a fortress, and Marco Rose has this team playing some outstanding counter-attacking football. Sesko has emerged as one of the most exciting young strikers in Europe, and Openda's pace gives them a constant outlet on the break. Defensively, they're organized and physical.

I actually like Leverkusen here despite being the away side. Xabi Alonso's tactical flexibility is elite - he can adapt his approach to any opponent. Wirtz continues to be the most talented German player since Ozil, and Grimaldo bombing forward from left-back creates overloads. Leipzig will make it difficult, but Leverkusen's quality should tell. The +125 feels like value.

Villarreal vs Athletic Bilbao

Monday, November 10 | 3:00 PM ET | Estadio de la Ceramica
Line: VIL +105 | O/U: 2.5 | VIL: 6-5-3 | ATH: 7-4-3

An underrated La Liga fixture between two clubs that always produce quality football. Villarreal at home are a tough out for anyone - the Yellow Submarine have a rich European pedigree and play with technical sophistication that makes them dangerous against any opponent.

Athletic Bilbao, though, have been resurgent. Their Copa del Rey triumph last season has injected genuine belief into the squad, and their unique identity - only signing Basque players - gives them an incredible team chemistry that money can't buy. Nico Williams is rapidly becoming one of the most exciting wingers in Europe, and Inaki keeps defying age with his physical prowess.

This screams draw to me. Both teams are evenly matched, both play controlled possession football, and neither has been blowing teams out. The 1-1 final feels most likely, with both teams creating chances but struggling to convert. If you're betting, the under 2.5 goals combined with both teams to score could be the move.

Newcastle United vs West Ham United

Monday, November 10 | 3:00 PM ET | St. James' Park
Line: NEW -175 | O/U: 2.5 | NEW: 7-4-3 | WHU: 4-4-6

St. James' Park is one of the toughest away days in English football, and Newcastle have been excellent at home this season. The Toon Army creates an atmosphere that opposing teams genuinely struggle to handle. Isak has been clinical when fit, and Gordon's emergence as one of the best left wingers in the league gives them legitimate attacking quality.

West Ham have been disappointing this season. The Moyes departure and Lopetegui appointment was supposed to bring more attractive football, but it's been a struggle to find consistency. Kudus has been their bright spot, capable of individual brilliance, but too often the Hammers look disjointed and easy to defend against.

Newcastle should handle this comfortably. Their home form has been outstanding, and West Ham's away record is concerning. The -175 line feels about right, maybe even light given how one-sided these matches at St. James' can get. Expect Newcastle to control possession and wear West Ham down in the second half.
