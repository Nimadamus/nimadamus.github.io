7-Game Sunday Slate

NHL Previews Today

Seven games on a loaded Sunday slate with playoff chaos everywhere you look. The Capitals and Rangers headline the night on ESPN with the special Inside Out Classic alternate broadcast on Disney Channel. Detroit's wild card hopes hang by a thread in Minnesota. The defending champion Panthers are shocking +195 underdogs in Pittsburgh. Nathan MacKinnon and the Avalanche look to pad their historic season against St. Louis. With 13 days left in the regular season, every point matters.

Wild @ Red Wings

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Puck Line
MIN -1.5 (+215) / DET +1.5 (-265)
Moneyline
MIN -120 / DET -105
Total
O/U 5.5

This is a fascinating early afternoon matchup between a Minnesota team that has already punched its ticket to the postseason and a Detroit club that desperately needs every single point it can scrape together. The Wild come in at 42-21-12 with 96 points, comfortably clinched and sitting third in the Central Division. Detroit, meanwhile, is clinging to life in the Eastern Conference wild card picture at 40-27-8 with 88 points, sitting just two points behind Boston for that second wild card spot. For the Red Wings, this game isn't just important. It's existential.

Minnesota's biggest weapon, as always, is Kirill Kaprizov. The Russian superstar is putting together another Hart Trophy-caliber campaign with 39 goals and 81 points through 72 games, and his ability to take over a game with his skating, shooting, and vision makes the Wild dangerous every single night. The Wild rank fourth in the NHL in goals against with just 213 allowed, and their defensive identity under John Hynes gives them the kind of floor that makes them tough to beat regardless of how the offense is clicking. At -120 on the road, the market sees Minnesota as the better team, and that's hard to argue with based on the full body of work this season.

But here's what makes Detroit so dangerous in this spot: desperation. The Red Wings have fought tooth and nail to stay relevant in the wild card race, and playing at Little Caesars Arena with their season potentially on the line is going to bring out a level of intensity that a clinched Minnesota team might not match. Detroit at -105 is essentially a pick'em price for the home side, and the Red Wings' 40 wins this season represent a franchise that has genuinely turned the corner from the rebuilding years. Lucas Raymond continues to develop into a legitimate top-line talent, and the depth throughout Detroit's lineup is miles better than it was even a year ago.

The 5.5 total feels tight for a game with this much on the line. Minnesota's defensive structure could keep this thing in the 2-1 range if they're locked in, but Detroit's desperation could also lead to an open, fast-paced affair where both teams trade chances. The matinee start on TNT adds visibility to what could be a season-defining game for the Red Wings. If Detroit can't find two points here at home against a team with nothing to play for in the standings, the wild card dream might slip away for good.

Game 2
ESPN+

Panthers @ Penguins

Sunday, 3:00 PM ET | PPG Paints Arena, Pittsburgh, PA
Puck Line
FLA +1.5 (-122) / PIT -1.5 (+102)
Moneyline
FLA +195 / PIT -238
Total
O/U 6.5

If you told anyone in June 2025 that the back-to-back Stanley Cup champion Panthers would be +195 road underdogs against the Penguins in April, they would have had you committed. But the 2025-26 season has been nothing short of a nightmare for Florida, and the 37-35-3 record with 77 points tells the story of a team that never recovered from the championship hangover. The Panthers hoisted the Cup just ten months ago and now they're sitting at home watching the playoffs from the outside. It's one of the most dramatic collapses in recent NHL history, and there's been no single moment to point to. The entire season has just felt flat, like a team running on fumes from two consecutive deep playoff runs finally ran out of gas.

Pittsburgh at -238 is a steep home favorite, and that price reflects a Penguins team that has been genuinely competitive this season at 38-22-16 with 92 points. Sidney Crosby, at 38 years old, continues to defy every expectation about aging in professional hockey. He's put up 28 goals and 69 points through 65 games, and in PPG Paints Arena, the crowd feeds off his every shift. Crosby surpassed Mario Lemieux for the franchise's all-time points lead earlier this season, and the man is playing like someone who knows there aren't many Aprils left. The supporting cast has stepped up around him, and Pittsburgh's home record has been strong enough to justify this kind of line against a team as depleted as Florida.

The Panthers' path to an upset is narrow but not impossible. Aleksander Barkov is still one of the best two-way centers in hockey, and Sergei Bobrovsky has shown flashes of the brilliance that backstopped two championship runs. But the energy, the desperation, the will to grind out wins that defined Florida's championship DNA simply isn't there this season. When your team is 37-35-3 in April, the body language tells you everything you need to know. Players are mentally checked out, thinking about the offseason, and that's a dangerous place to be when you're visiting a building where the home crowd is going to be electric for Crosby and company.

The 6.5 total suggests some fireworks, and that makes sense given Florida's defensive apathy down the stretch. The Panthers have been leaky defensively for months, and Pittsburgh's offense has enough weapons to exploit those breakdowns. This could turn into one of those 5-2 or 6-3 affairs where the Penguins put the game away early and cruise through the third period. If you're looking for the upset, Florida's +195 gives you a nearly 2-to-1 return, but this team hasn't given anyone a reason to trust them since October.

Game 3
TNT

Bruins @ Flyers

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center, Philadelphia, PA
Puck Line
BOS +1.5 (-218) / PHI -1.5 (+180)
Moneyline
BOS +120 / PHI -142
Total
O/U 5.5

The Flyers are favored at home at -142 against the Bruins, and that's a sentence that carries real weight given where both franchises are in April. Philadelphia at 37-24-12 with approximately 86 points is right in the thick of the Eastern Conference wild card battle, fighting alongside Ottawa, Detroit, and Columbus for every last playoff spot. Boston at 39-23-8 with 90 points currently holds one of those precious wild card positions, but the margin is razor-thin. This isn't just a rivalry game. It's a game where the loser's playoff hopes take a significant hit, and both teams know it.

Philadelphia being favored at home is a testament to how much this team has grown under a coaching staff that has instilled genuine belief in the locker room. Wells Fargo Center has been a fortress for the Flyers when they're playing with purpose, and the energy in that building during meaningful April hockey is going to be absolutely electric. Philly's transition game and forecheck have been relentless this season, and they play with the kind of physical, aggressive identity that wears opponents down over 60 minutes. Getting -142 at home in a game this important feels like the market trusts Philadelphia's trajectory.

Boston at +120 is a fascinating underdog price. The Bruins have been one of the more consistent teams in the Eastern Conference all season, and their disciplined, structured approach to the game can frustrate more talented rosters into sloppy play. The Bruins don't beat themselves. They play tight defensively, their penalty kill has been a weapon, and they win games by limiting mistakes and capitalizing on the few chances they create. That formula has worked in hostile buildings all season, and the +120 number gives you a team that's been excellent on the road against a Flyers squad that's still learning how to handle playoff-pressure situations.

The 5.5 total is set appropriately for a game between two teams that understand the value of defense in April. Boston wants this to be a 2-1 grinder where their structure suffocates Philadelphia's transition game, while the Flyers want to push the pace and use their home crowd to create momentum swings. The special teams battle could be decisive. If Boston stays disciplined and out of the penalty box, they have the formula to steal this one. If Philadelphia draws penalties with their aggressive forecheck, the power play could be the difference. A massive Sunday afternoon in Philly with real playoff implications on both sides.

ESPN+

Hurricanes @ Senators

Sunday, 5:00 PM ET | Canadian Tire Centre, Ottawa, ON
Puck Line
CAR -1.5 (+200)
Moneyline
CAR -125 / OTT +105
Total
O/U 6.5

Carolina has already clinched with a dominant 48-21-6 record and 102 points, sitting atop the Metropolitan Division and looking like a legitimate Stanley Cup contender. Ottawa, on the other hand, is at 39-26-10 with the most regulation wins of any wild card contender in the East at 32. The Senators have been playing with an urgency and intensity that has been genuinely impressive over the past few weeks, capped by a massive win over Buffalo recently that thrust them right back into the playoff conversation. This is a team that believes it belongs, and Canadian Tire Centre is going to be rocking for this one.

The Hurricanes at -125 on the road tells you the market still respects Carolina's talent and structure, even in a hostile building in Ottawa. The Canes have been the model of consistency all season, and their defensive system under Rod Brind'Amour remains one of the hardest things to score against in the NHL. Carolina's ability to control possession, limit quality chances, and strike on the transition has made them a nightmare for opponents, and they don't take games off just because they've already clinched. The Hurricanes play the same relentless, suffocating brand of hockey whether it's October or April.

But Ottawa at +105 as home underdogs in a game where their season could turn is the kind of spot that produces magical performances. Brady Tkachuk sets the tone with his relentless motor and physical presence, and the Senators' young core has bought into the identity of a team that competes on every shift. Ottawa's speed through the neutral zone has given opponents fits all season, and their home record has been strong enough to make Canadian Tire Centre a genuinely tough place to play. With 39 wins and 86 points, the Senators control their own fate for that second wild card, and a win here against the league's best would be an enormous statement.

The 6.5 total is set high for a game involving Carolina's stingy defense, but Ottawa's offensive talent and the emotional stakes of this game could push both teams into a higher-scoring affair. The Senators aren't going to sit back and try to grind out a 1-0 win against the Hurricanes. They're going to come out with energy, push the pace, and try to bury Carolina early with crowd momentum behind them. Whether the Hurricanes can match that intensity with nothing left to play for in the standings is the central question. Sometimes clinched teams coast in these spots. Sometimes they flex and remind everyone why they're the best. This game will tell us which version of Carolina shows up.

Devils @ Canadiens

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Bell Centre, Montreal, QC
Puck Line
NJ +1.5 (-192) / MTL -1.5 (+160)
Moneyline
NJ +136 / MTL -162
Total
O/U 6.5

Montreal at -162 hosting the Devils is a reflection of just how much the Canadiens have exceeded every expectation this season. The Habs sit at 44-21-10 with 98 points, a number that would have seemed like pure fantasy if you'd predicted it in September. Nick Suzuki has emerged as a legitimate superstar, posting 27 goals and 94 points through 75 games with the kind of all-around brilliance that makes him one of the most complete centers in the NHL. The Bell Centre has been absolutely electric this season, and Montreal playing meaningful April hockey has rejuvenated an entire city that's been starving for it.

New Jersey at +136 represents a Devils team that has been one of the season's biggest disappointments. At 39-34-2 with 80 points, the Devils are mathematically alive but functionally dead in the playoff race, sitting four points out of a wild card spot with just a handful of games remaining. Jack Hughes has been brilliant individually, but the roster around him has failed to hold up its end. The defensive structure has been inconsistent, the goaltending has been a revolving door, and the power play has gone cold at the worst possible times. This is a team with elite talent that simply hasn't figured out how to put it all together.

Suzuki's 94-point campaign has been the engine driving everything for Montreal. His vision, his defensive responsibility, and his ability to elevate the players around him have turned the Canadiens from a rebuilding curiosity into a legitimate contender. The supporting cast has bought into Martin St. Louis's system with infectious energy, and the speed through the neutral zone combined with an aggressive forecheck has been a nightmare for opponents all year. At home, the Habs are even more dangerous because the Bell Centre crowd amplifies every big play and turns momentum swings into avalanches.

The 6.5 total is set high because both teams can create offense. Montreal's creativity combined with New Jersey's defensive vulnerabilities creates a recipe for goals, and neither team's goaltending has been reliable enough to consistently keep the score low. This could easily be a 5-3 or 4-3 affair where the lead changes multiple times. Hughes vs. Suzuki is a fun matchup between two young stars at different points in their team's trajectory. One is carrying a contender, and the other is putting up numbers on a sinking ship. The contrast tells you everything about where these franchises stand.

Capitals @ Rangers

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Puck Line
WSH -1.5 (+235)
Moneyline
WSH -115 / NYR -105
Total
O/U 5.5

This is the headliner of the Sunday slate, and it's not even close. Washington at -115 visiting the Rangers at Madison Square Garden on ESPN, with a special Inside Out Classic alternate broadcast airing simultaneously on Disney Channel, Disney XD, and Disney+. The alternate broadcast will use the NHL's player and puck tracking system to render a real-time animated version of the game featuring characters from Pixar's Inside Out franchise skating alongside animated versions of the actual players. It's a spectacle, but underneath all the bells and whistles is a game with enormous stakes. The Capitals at 38-29-9 are fighting for their playoff lives in the Eastern Conference wild card race, and every remaining game feels like a playoff game for Washington.

Alex Ovechkin continues to add chapters to the greatest goal-scoring story in hockey history. Having broken Wayne Gretzky's all-time goals record last season, the 40-year-old captain has kept right on rolling, sitting at 29 goals this season and one away from a record 20th career 30-goal campaign. Earlier this season, Ovechkin became just the second player in NHL history to reach 1,000 combined regular season and playoff goals. Every time he steps onto the ice at MSG, the building buzzes with anticipation because fans know they're watching a living legend who refuses to slow down. Washington's playoff push has been fueled by Ovechkin's determination and the team's collective refusal to let their season die quietly.

The Rangers at -105 at home are essentially a pick'em with the visiting Capitals, and that tells you everything about where this franchise has fallen. New York's season started with Stanley Cup aspirations and has devolved into a rebuild-on-the-fly after a brutal trade deadline that stripped the roster of key pieces. The Garden faithful have watched their team get dismantled, and the atmosphere at MSG has shifted from playoff electricity to resigned frustration. That said, rivalry games against Washington have a way of bringing out something extra in the Rangers, and the players remaining on this roster have pride. Getting -105 at home in a rivalry matchup is a number that could attract some attention, especially with the national broadcast spotlight adding motivation.

The 5.5 total is set on the lower end, and that reflects the reality that both teams are capable of playing tight, defensive hockey when the situation calls for it. Washington's playoff desperation will have them locked in defensively, unwilling to give up easy chances, while the Rangers' depleted roster doesn't have the offensive firepower to consistently generate high-quality looks. The animated Inside Out Classic broadcast on Disney+ is going to bring a completely new audience to this game, with Riley's emotions experiencing the roller coaster of an NHL rivalry game in real time. It's a brilliant marketing play by ESPN and Disney. But strip away the animation and what you have is a classic Metropolitan Division rivalry game where the visiting team's season is on the line. Those are the best kind.

Game 7 - Marquee
ESPN

Blues @ Avalanche

Sunday, 9:30 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Puck Line
COL -1.5 (+114)
Moneyline
STL +190 / COL -230
Total
O/U 6.5

The nightcap on ESPN features one of the most dominant teams in the NHL hosting a divisional opponent with nothing left to lose. Colorado at -230 is a massive home favorite, and the Avalanche have earned every bit of that price through a season that has been nothing short of historically elite. The Avs lead the league in goals scored with 221 and have allowed the fewest in the NHL at just 146, a combination of offensive explosiveness and defensive suffocation that has made them the clear Stanley Cup favorite. Nathan MacKinnon became the first player in the league to reach 50 goals this season, and his 121 points through 73 games have him firmly in the Hart Trophy conversation for the second time in three years.

MacKinnon's 50-goal, 121-point campaign has been something special to watch. His ability to take over games single-handedly, combined with Cale Makar's transcendent blue-line play, gives Colorado a one-two punch that no other team in hockey can match. Makar continues to be the most complete defenseman in the sport, joining the rush, quarterbacking the power play, and defending at an elite level night after night. The Avalanche's depth throughout the lineup means they can roll four lines and keep coming at you in waves, and at Ball Arena, the altitude and the energy combine to create one of the most hostile environments in the Western Conference.

St. Louis at +190 is a significant underdog, and the Blues' 27-30-10 record with just 64 points tells you everything about a season that went sideways months ago. The Blues are playing out the string, evaluating young players, and thinking about the offseason. That's not an ideal mindset when you're visiting the best team in hockey on a Sunday night on national television. Colorado dismantled St. Louis 6-1 on New Year's Eve in what was one of the most dominant performances of the season, scoring the fastest four goals to start a game in franchise history. The Blues simply don't have the firepower or the defensive structure to hang with the Avalanche when Colorado is firing on all cylinders.

The 6.5 total reflects the expectation that Colorado's offense is going to eat, and it's hard to argue. The Avalanche generate an absurd volume of quality chances, and against a Blues defense that has been leaky all season, the goals could come in bunches. The question isn't whether Colorado wins this game. It's by how much. MacKinnon on national television with the chance to pad his MVP resume against a team he's tormented all season is appointment viewing for hockey fans. The +190 on St. Louis has appeal only if you believe the Blues can somehow find the defensive performance of a lifetime, and there's been very little evidence this season to suggest that's coming. Colorado closes out the Sunday slate in dominant fashion.
