The Morning After The World Cup: Malmo And Djurgarden Chase Points On Allsvenskan Away Days

Soccer Daily Board - July 20, 2026
Published July 20, 2026

The World Cup final was played at MetLife Stadium yesterday, and the club game does not pause to catch its breath. Monday's board belongs to Scandinavia and South America: Malmo FF visit Kalmar and Djurgarden travel to Orgryte in a pair of 1:00 PM ET Allsvenskan kickoffs, then Uruguay's Torneo Intermedio takes over the evening with Montevideo City Torque hosting winless Danubio and Defensor Sporting welcoming Liverpool to the Franzini. Verified standings and form below. Analysis only.

Marquee - Allsvenskan

Malmo FF @ Kalmar FF

Monday, 1:00 PM ET | Guldfageln Arena, Kalmar, Sweden

Sweden's most decorated club is having an identity crisis, and Monday in Kalmar is the kind of fixture that decides whether it deepens. Malmo FF sit sixth in the Allsvenskan with 19 points from 12 matches, a 6-1-5 record that reads nothing like the standard the club sets for itself, and the table above them is running away: IK Sirius lead the league with 35 points and an unbeaten 11-2-0 record through 13 rounds. Sixteen points is not a gap a champion shrugs off in July. Every dropped point from here forward converts the title race from difficult to arithmetic.

Kalmar FF are twelfth with 13 points from 12 matches at 4-1-7, closer to the relegation places than to mid-table comfort, and Guldfageln Arena is exactly the kind of ground where a wounded giant gets tested. Kalmar's minus-3 goal difference says the games have been close; seven defeats say they have found ways to lose them anyway. A home date against a Malmo side that has already lost five times is, from the hosts' perspective, the most winnable marquee fixture they will get all month.

The stylistic question is which Malmo shows up on the road. At their best the away side controls midfield and grinds out the sort of 1-0 and 2-0 wins that built two decades of titles; the five-loss version has been vulnerable to exactly the compact, counterattacking setup a home underdog will bring. With a game in hand on most of the pack, a win lifts Malmo to 22 points, past Djurgarden's current 19, and keeps faint title hopes breathing. A draw or worse in Kalmar and the conversation around this season changes for good.

Game 2 - Allsvenskan

Djurgarden @ Orgryte IS

Monday, 1:00 PM ET | Gamla Ullevi, Gothenburg, Sweden

The other Swedish fixture is a mismatch on paper with a trapdoor underneath it. Djurgarden are fifth with 19 points from just 11 matches, the best games-in-hand position of any contender, and their plus-11 goal difference is the third best in the league behind only runaway leaders Sirius and Hammarby. A 6-1-4 record with that scoring margin suggests a team whose defeats have been narrow and whose wins have been emphatic, and matches in hand mean Monday is worth more than three points; it is a chance to jump to fourth and put real pressure on the top three by midweek.

Orgryte are fifteenth of sixteen with 9 points from 12 matches, a 2-3-7 record, and a minus-17 goal difference that is the second worst in the Allsvenskan, ahead of only bottom club Halmstad. The traditional Gothenburg club has been beaten and beaten badly this season, and the relegation math is already stark: with Halmstad on 6 points and Degerfors on 10, every home match against a superior side is one Orgryte can afford to lose only if they are winning the games around it.

Gamla Ullevi at least gives the hosts a proper stage, and desperate teams at home in July are never free wins. But the profile gap is the widest of the Monday board: a top-five side with the division's best per-game points pace among the chasers, against a side conceding at a bottom-two rate. Djurgarden's task is to make the game look exactly as lopsided as the table says it should be, and their season so far says they usually do.

Game 3 - Torneo Intermedio

Danubio @ Montevideo City Torque

Monday, 4:00 PM ET | Estadio Charrua, Montevideo, Uruguay

Uruguay's midseason Torneo Intermedio gives the evening its first kickoff, and the Group B table frames it cleanly. Montevideo City Torque have taken 9 points from five matches at 3-0-2, an all-or-nothing profile with three wins, two defeats and not a single draw, and they sit fourth in the group with the leaders still catchable: Montevideo Wanderers top Group B at 12 points, with Deportivo Maldonado on 11 and Nacional on 10. With a match in hand on the leaders, a home win moves Torque squarely back into the race for the group.

Danubio have not won a game in the competition: 0-3-2 for 3 points, last in Group B alongside a minus-3 goal difference. Three draws in five matches tell you the historic club from Jardines del Hipodromo has been competitive in stretches, but the Intermedio is short, eight teams per group playing a single round, and a winless side running out of runway has to start converting draws into wins immediately or the tournament ends as a writeoff.

The Estadio Charrua fixture lines up as aggressor against survivor. Torque's no-draw profile suggests a team that commits numbers forward and lives with the consequences; Danubio's three stalemates suggest the opposite instinct. When a team that always produces a result meets a team that never quite loses by much, the opening goal is worth double, because Danubio have shown no evidence yet this tournament that they can chase a game from behind.

Game 4 - Torneo Intermedio

Liverpool @ Defensor Sporting

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Estadio Luis Franzini, Montevideo, Uruguay

The nightcap is a Group A meeting between two sides going nowhere fast, which is precisely what makes it desperate. Defensor Sporting are winless in the Intermedio at 0-3-2 with 3 points, dead last in Group A while Penarol lead the group at 13 points from six. The Franzini has seen three draws and two defeats' worth of frustration already, and the violet's minus-3 goal difference across five matches at least suggests the losses have been tight rather than humiliating.

Liverpool of Montevideo are barely better off at 1-2-2 for 5 points, sixth in the group with a minus-1 goal difference, and their one win is the only thing separating the two sides' tournaments. For the negriazules, the road trip to the Franzini is the most winnable remaining fixture on paper, and a victory would carry them past Cerro into the group's middle band with the arithmetic still alive for a late run.

Two teams with seven draws between them in ten combined matches is a strong signal about how this one is likely to breathe: cagey, low-event, decided by a set piece or an individual error if it is decided at all. Defensor's home crowd and Liverpool's marginally better form pull in opposite directions, and in a fixture where neither side has shown they can put teams away, the first goal, whenever it arrives, will feel like a match winner.
