#10 Nebraska @ Ohio State

Monday, January 5 | 6:30 PM ET | Value City Arena | FS1
Nebraska: 14-0 (3-0 Big Ten) | Ohio State: 10-3 (2-1 Big Ten)

This is it. The moment of truth for Fred Hoiberg's historic run. Nebraska hasn't been 14-0 since... well, ever in modern history. They haven't run the table in non-conference play in almost 100 years. They're ranked in the top 10 for the first time in program history. And now they have to prove it wasn't a cupcake schedule by going into Columbus and handling business.

The Cornhuskers barely escaped Michigan State 58-56 on January 2nd, and that was in Lincoln where they're 10-0 this season. This is a completely different animal - Value City Arena is a hostile environment, and Ohio State is motivated. The Buckeyes are 10-3 with Bruce Thornton leading the charge (he just dropped 20 and 10 against Rutgers to move into 8th on Ohio State's all-time scoring list).

Here's what worries me about Nebraska though: they've been winning ugly. That Michigan State escape required some late-game magic, and they haven't exactly blown anyone out in Big Ten play. Devin Royal (16 points vs Rutgers) and John Mobley Jr. give Ohio State legitimate scoring punch. The Buckeyes will pack the paint and make Nebraska uncomfortable. This is the first true road test of whether the Cornhuskers are for real or just a good story.

USC @ #12 Michigan State

Monday, January 5 | 8:30 PM ET | Breslin Center | FS1
USC: 12-2 (1-2 Big Ten) | Michigan State: 12-1 (2-1 Big Ten)

Both teams are coming off painful losses. USC got absolutely demolished by Michigan 96-66 in their first game of 2026 - a 30-point embarrassment on national television. Michigan State lost that heartbreaker to Nebraska 58-56, scoring a season-low 56 points in one of the ugliest offensive performances in Tom Izzo's tenure. Someone's getting a bounce-back win tonight, and history says trust the guy with 749 career wins.

Tom Izzo is in his 31st year at Michigan State, and he's seen this movie before. The Spartans are the defending Big Ten champions, they've got 27 straight NCAA Tournament appearances on the line, and the Breslin Center will be absolutely rocking. Izzo doesn't lose back-to-back games at home very often, and the program has too much pride to let that Nebraska loss spiral into something worse.

USC's had a great season overall - winning the Maui Invitational for the first time in program history was huge. But Eric Musselman's crew is in a vulnerable spot here. The Michigan beatdown exposed some serious defensive issues, and now they're traveling to one of the toughest environments in college basketball. Ezra Ausar and Chad Baker-Mazara can score, but can they handle the Izzo defensive pressure after getting exposed in Ann Arbor? The Breslin Center crowd will smell blood.

Oregon @ Rutgers

Monday, January 5 | 7:00 PM ET | Jersey Mike's Arena | Peacock
Oregon: 8-6 (1-2 Big Ten) | Rutgers: Post-Harper/Bailey rebuild

Oregon finally got their first Big Ten conference win on January 2nd, beating Maryland 64-54 in College Park. Nate Bittle was tremendous with 16 points, 7 rebounds, and 5 blocks - exactly the kind of dominant interior presence the Ducks need to compete in this league. Takai Simpkins matched him with 16 points, and suddenly Oregon looks like a team that figured something out.

Rutgers is in full rebuild mode after losing Dylan Harper (2nd overall pick to the Spurs) and Ace Bailey (5th overall to the Jazz) - the two highest NBA draft picks in school history. Steve Pikiell brought in the fourth-best recruiting class in the Big Ten to replace them, but that's a lot of inexperience to throw into the fire. The Scarlet Knights finished 15-17 last year even with those two stars.

The Jersey Mike's Arena environment has been solid for Rutgers historically, but this team is still learning how to play together. Oregon's coming in with momentum and a clear game plan - pound it inside with Bittle and make Rutgers' young guards prove they can score consistently. The Ducks aren't world-beaters at 8-6, but they might be catching the Scarlet Knights at the perfect time while Pikiell's new roster is still gelling.

William & Mary @ Charleston

Monday, January 5 | 7:00 PM ET | TD Arena | CBSSN
CAA Conference Matchup | TV: CBS Sports Network

The Colonial Athletic Association gets some national TV shine on CBS Sports Network tonight, and this is exactly the kind of mid-major matchup that flies under the radar but delivers entertaining basketball. William & Mary has been a scrappy program that competes hard, while Charleston brings the TD Arena home court advantage that can swing close games.

The Cougars have traditionally been one of the better programs in the CAA, and playing at home should give them an edge in this spot. TD Arena crowds get loud for conference games, and visiting teams often struggle to adjust to the atmosphere. Look for Charleston to control tempo and use their familiarity with the building to create separation.

William & Mary will need to shoot efficiently to stay in this one - their margin for error is smaller on the road. The Tribe typically rely on team basketball and smart shot selection, but road games in conference play are always tougher than they look on paper. Charleston should have enough at home to take care of business.

Columbia @ Cornell

Monday, January 5 | 5:00 PM ET | Newman Arena | ESPN+
Ivy League Matchup | Newman Arena, Ithaca, NY

Ivy League basketball might not get the headlines, but these conference games matter for NCAA Tournament positioning. With only the conference tournament winner earning an automatic bid, every league game carries enormous weight. Columbia and Cornell are both trying to position themselves for a potential run at the Ivy Tournament title come March.

Cornell's Newman Arena is one of the more intimate venues in Division I basketball. The Big Red's student section gets into games, and visiting teams often find the atmosphere more challenging than expected. The drive to Ithaca in early January isn't exactly a vacation either - the weather factor can impact shooting for teams not used to the conditions.

Columbia typically brings disciplined, well-coached basketball to these conference matchups. The Lions rely on execution and taking care of the ball against Ivy opponents. This should be a competitive, low-possession game where every trip down the floor matters. Home court should lean Cornell's way, but Ivy games are notorious for going down to the wire.
