NBA Playoffs First Round - Monday

Wolves Chase A Closeout Without Edwards, Thunder Hunt The Sweep In Phoenix, And A Cunningham-Banchero Game 4 Anchors The Monday Slate

Three NBA First Round games Monday across NBC, Peacock, and TNT. Pistons at Magic Game 4 with Detroit trailing 1-2 and Cade Cunningham averaging 31 PPG. Thunder at Suns Game 4 sweep watch with OKC up 3-0. Wolves at Nuggets Game 5 closeout window with Minnesota up 3-1, Anthony Edwards out with a knee injury, and Ayo Dosunmu fresh off a 43-point Game 4 explosion.

Game 5 - Closeout Watch - Featured
TNT

Timberwolves @ Nuggets

Monday, 10:30 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO

The Minnesota Timberwolves visit Denver with a 3-1 series lead chasing the closeout on the road. Denver is an 11.5-point home favorite with the moneyline at minus-550 and the total at 221.5. The Wolves built the lead with a Saturday Game 4 win that came at enormous roster cost. Anthony Edwards has been ruled out for Game 5 with a left knee bone bruise and hyperextension that imaging cleared of ligament damage but is expected to keep him out multiple weeks. Donte DiVincenzo is done for the season with a torn Achilles. Ayo Dosunmu's 43-point Game 4 explosion on 13-of-17 shooting and 5-of-5 from three was the kind of bench-guard line that doesn't repeat in volume but earned him the lead-creator role for Monday.

Denver's home-court desperation premium is the structural piece of the heavy spread. Nikola Jokic was ejected late in Game 4 for instigating a confrontation with Jaden McDaniels, drawing Julius Randle in for the escalation. Jokic was fined $50,000, Randle $35,000, and neither was suspended, so both stars play Monday. Jokic's series averages of 26.5 points, 12.8 rebounds, and 9.5 assists set the gravity of the matchup. Jamal Murray's 30-point Game 4 line gave Denver its scoring profile, but the Nuggets' role-player support across four games has been spotty - Christian Braun, Michael Porter Jr., and the Russell Westbrook second unit have all underperformed regular-season baselines. The full deep-dive matchup breakdown lives on the Featured Game of the Day page.

Minnesota's path is the half-court grind. Mike Conley's pace control has been the structural piece of the series, slowing Denver into the kind of low-possession environment where the Wolves' defensive identity around McDaniels and Rudy Gobert thrives. Jaden McDaniels has been the matchup defender on Jokic on the perimeter switches, and the Wolves' defensive rating across four games sits at the top tier of any first-round series. The Edwards absence leaves the offense to Conley as the steadying ball-handler, Dosunmu as the scoring spark, Randle as a high-post initiator, and Naz Reid's three-point spacing as the geometry that keeps Jokic from sealing the paint. The closeout-window pressure defines every possession.

Game 4
NBC / Peacock

Pistons @ Magic

Monday, 8:00 PM ET | Kia Center, Orlando, FL

The Detroit Pistons visit Orlando trailing the series 1-2 in a must-win Game 4 spot. Detroit is a 2.5-point road favorite with the moneyline at minus-148 and the total at 214.5, the lowest total of any first-round game on the Monday slate. The series-price inversion is the structural read - Detroit was the East one-seed, but Orlando has tagged the Pistons twice in three games and is now sitting on the chance to push the top seed to a 3-1 hole. Cade Cunningham has been the most prolific playoff scorer in the East through three games, with series averages of 31.0 points, 5.3 rebounds, and 8.0 assists, leading all postseason scorers in points-per-game. Game 3 saw Cunningham produce 27 points but the Magic still pulled away late behind a Banchero-Bane combo of 25 points each.

Orlando's structural advantage has been wing depth. Paolo Banchero's series averages of 22.0 points, 9.0 rebounds, and 7.0 assists give the Magic a primary creator who can match Cunningham possession-for-possession. Desmond Bane's mid-range scoring profile has been the secondary piece, and Franz Wagner's two-way wing role has tightened the team's defensive rating in the half-court. The Magic have produced the kind of point-of-attack defense that has forced Cunningham into mid-range looks against longer defenders, and the team's bench rotation around Cole Anthony has kept the Pistons' second-unit minutes neutral at best. Orlando's home court in Game 4 is the structural piece that the Magic lean on to extend the lead.

The Pistons' bounce-back math is the rotational health. Jalen Duren's interior coverage held Banchero to 4-of-12 shooting in Game 2. Tobias Harris has produced the kind of veteran scoring contribution that gets the team through tight stretches. Ausar Thompson's defensive activity on Banchero has been the structural piece of the Pistons' Game 2 win. Detroit's path is to limit Cunningham's turnover rate, get the bench scoring profile back above the regular-season baseline, and exploit Orlando's interior depth without Wendell Carter Jr. at full strength. A Magic Game 4 win produces a 3-1 series lead heading back to Detroit for Game 5. A Pistons win evens the series at 2-2 and shifts the home-court advantage back to the East one-seed.

Game 4 - Sweep Watch
NBC / Peacock

Thunder @ Suns

Monday, 9:30 PM ET | Mortgage Matchup Center, Phoenix, AZ

The defending-champion Oklahoma City Thunder visit Phoenix with a 3-0 series lead chasing the four-game sweep. Oklahoma City is a 10.5-point road favorite with the moneyline at minus-500 and the Suns at plus-380. The total reflects the high-pace Thunder profile against a Suns team whose defensive rating across the three games has been bottom-tier. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been the playoff scoring profile - efficient mid-range work, attacking the rim against Phoenix's switching defense, and a series of late-game closing possessions that have produced the kind of fourth-quarter scoring runs that defined the regular-season MVP-caliber play. The Thunder's 64-18 regular-season record produced a 31-10 road profile, and the team's playoff version has carried that road performance into the first round.

Phoenix's structural problem is the matchup math. Devin Booker has produced 28-plus point lines in two of three games, but the Suns' supporting cast has been below the level needed to make Booker's scoring profile meaningful in playoff variance. Bradley Beal's secondary scoring has been spotty, the bench has produced negative net ratings across the series, and the team's interior defense has been picked apart by the Thunder's pick-and-roll. Kevin Durant is now in Houston after the July 2025 trade, which removed the team's secondary creator from the rotation that took Phoenix to a play-in spot last season. Ryan Dunn's rookie defensive profile has produced minutes against Gilgeous-Alexander but not the kind of stops that change the matchup math.

The 0-3 elimination spot has historically produced one of two things - a closeout sweep, which has been the historical norm, or a desperation-spot home win that pushes the series to a 3-1 game-five spot. No NBA team has ever climbed back from 0-3, but the Suns at home with the elimination premium and the bench-rotation depth that becomes more workable in a win-or-go-home spot is the kind of variance environment that produces a competitive game even at a 10.5 spread. Chet Holmgren's interior depth alongside Isaiah Hartenstein has given the Thunder the kind of two-big lineup that has held Phoenix's pick-and-roll defense well below the regular-season baseline. The closing-game rotation is the structural piece of the Thunder's playoff identity, and the Sunday Game 4 sweep window is the kind of spot where the closing-game rotation has produced the late-game execution.
