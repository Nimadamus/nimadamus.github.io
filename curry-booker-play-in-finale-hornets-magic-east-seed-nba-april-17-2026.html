NBA Play-In Tournament - Final Night

Curry, Booker, and the Last Two Playoff Spots

Two games tonight. Two win-or-go-home eliminations. In the East, the Charlotte Hornets visit the Orlando Magic with the 8 seed and a first-round date with the 60-22 Pistons on the line. In the West, Stephen Curry and the Warriors face Devin Booker and the Suns with the final Western Conference playoff spot up for grabs. Winners face the 1 seeds tomorrow. Losers clean out their lockers.

Hornets @ Magic

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Kia Center, Orlando, FL

The 8-seed Orlando Magic (45-37) host the 9-seed Charlotte Hornets (44-38) in a single-elimination game for the final Eastern Conference playoff spot. Orlando opens as a 3-point favorite with the total set at 218.5, which is a small number against a Hornets roster that has played like one of the best two-way teams in basketball since the All-Star break. Charlotte arrives here after outlasting the Miami Heat 127-126 in overtime on Tuesday, with LaMelo Ball pouring in 26 points and 6 assists and carrying the offense across crunch-time possessions. The Magic walk in bruised. They lost 109-97 to the Joel Embiid-less Sixers on Wednesday in the play-in's 7-vs-8 game, which means they arrive with the demoralizing knowledge that they couldn't handle a Philadelphia team missing its entire offensive gravity well. That's not the energy you want going into a must-win.

The context matters because Orlando has been struggling publicly. Reports over the past week have described "major turmoil" in the locker room, with at least one unnamed player willing to demand a trade if head coach Jamahl Mosley isn't relieved. Paolo Banchero (22.3 PPG, 6.8 RPG, 4.7 APG) is the franchise cornerstone and has been productive all year, but the Magic's offense has cratered in clutch situations. Desmond Bane (acquired from Memphis) averaged 20.5 PPG and Franz Wagner posted 20.4 PPG, which gives Orlando a legitimate three-headed scoring attack on paper. The problem is they haven't shown they can consistently produce against a switching defense in late-game possessions, and that's exactly what Charlotte has been this season. Jonathan Isaac is out with a knee injury, which subtracts one of Orlando's key defensive identities and forces Mosley to go small in high-leverage lineups.

Charlotte's case is both structural and momentum-based. LaMelo Ball has been the engine for everything Charlotte does offensively, and his ability to create in ball-screen actions puts Orlando's bigs in conflict sets they haven't been able to solve this year. Brandon Miller has been electric, projected for 22.5 points on Friday and coming off scoring runs of 25 and 20 in his last two meetings with the Magic. The Hornets won the season series 3-1, with the three wins coming by an average margin of 20.3 points. That's not parity. That's dominance. Charlotte boasts a 13-3 SU mark as road favorites this season, they're entering in rhythm off the Tuesday comeback, and they match up structurally well with Orlando's frontcourt. Moussa Diabate is listed questionable for the Hornets with left hip soreness, which is the only real cloud over this Charlotte roster.

The 218.5 total feels fair given that both teams have had trouble scoring in half-court possessions throughout the year. Orlando's offense has dragged in clutch situations, and Charlotte's defense has been elite since February. The season-series margin of 20.3 tells you that when these two teams play, one side takes over decisively. The market pricing Orlando at -3 despite the head-to-head record and the Charlotte momentum is a nod to home-court, rest, and the reality that the Magic's roster is deeper on paper than their Wednesday loss suggested. Whether that's enough against a Hornets team that's been sharper for longer is the question. The winner draws Detroit and its 60-22 record on Saturday. The loser's season is over.

Warriors @ Suns

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | Mortgage Matchup Center, Phoenix, AZ

The 10-seed Golden State Warriors (37-45) walk into Mortgage Matchup Center as 3.5-point underdogs against the 7-seed Phoenix Suns (45-37) in the final play-in elimination game of the season. The winner claims the No. 8 seed and a first-round date with the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder (64-18). The loser goes home. The total sits at 219.5 with Phoenix at -175 on the moneyline and Golden State at +145. Those numbers reflect home court, a day of rest, and the recognition that Stephen Curry is still shaking off two months of rust after missing 27 games with a right knee injury. They do not reflect the head-to-head reality: the Warriors took three of four regular-season meetings this year, including a 101-97 February 5 comeback on Phoenix's floor without Curry in the lineup.

Curry's return has redefined the Warriors' ceiling. He dropped 35 points in Tuesday's 114-110 overtime survival against the Clippers, scored 27 of those in the second half, and erased a 13-point fourth-quarter deficit to keep Golden State's season alive. He played 36 minutes on what should have been a controlled workload, and he's off the injury report for Friday. Jimmy Butler is done for the year with a torn ACL, Moses Moody is out with a ruptured patellar tendon, and Kristaps Porzingis is questionable with right ankle soreness after finishing with 20 points on 8-of-12 shooting Tuesday before rolling the ankle. If Porzingis plays, the Warriors get a second shot-creator and a rim deterrent Phoenix's bigs cannot handle alone. If he sits, Draymond Green and Al Horford have to cover Mark Williams and Phoenix's second unit, and Golden State's margin for error disappears entirely.

Phoenix's story is equally complicated. Devin Booker sat out the final two regular-season games for precautionary right ankle management and is back, cleared, off the injury report, and ready to carry the offensive load he's been handling all year at 25.6 points, 7.1 assists, and 4.1 rebounds. Jalen Green dropped 35 on Tuesday against Minnesota but watched Phoenix blow an 11-point fourth-quarter lead with 7:13 to play in a 114-110 loss that dropped them from a guaranteed playoff spot to this single-elimination game. Booker's foul trouble derailed the back half of that game, and the Suns' transition defense collapsed. Dillon Brooks, acquired to give Phoenix perimeter defensive bite, had 19 and 6 on Tuesday and draws the unenviable primary Curry assignment tonight.

The coaching adjustments here matter as much as the injury report. Phoenix has to keep Booker out of foul trouble, has to find the bench scoring that carried them during regular-season stretches, and has to defend the rim without over-helping on Curry's pick-and-roll actions. Golden State has to manage Curry's minutes, hope Porzingis is available, and get the auxiliary scoring from Podziemski and Gui Santos that they got in the Clippers survival. The 3.5 line and 219.5 total feel about right given the injury variables, but the head-to-head record suggests this game should be closer than the market is projecting. A Curry in his element plus Porzingis active is a live dog. A Curry running on fumes and Porzingis out is a comfortable home cover. Tip-off 10:00 PM ET. Winner sees Oklahoma City on Saturday. Loser stares at a long offseason that starts immediately.
