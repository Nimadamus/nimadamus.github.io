Sunday, January 5, 2026 | Off-Season Coverage

The Big Picture

We're deep into the Hot Stove season now, and the landscape of Major League Baseball is shifting dramatically. The biggest names have found new homes, but there's still plenty of intrigue as teams look to fill out their rosters before Spring Training opens in mid-February. Here's where things stand as we head into the final stretch of the off-season.

The Dodgers, as always, have been aggressive. They've reloaded their pitching staff after losing key arms to free agency last year, and their lineup remains one of the most feared in baseball. The Yankees responded with their own splashy moves, while the Mets continue to spend like money grows on trees in Queens.

World Series Odds Update

Heading into the 2026 season, the early futures market has settled into some familiar positions. The Dodgers lead the pack at +450, with the Yankees right behind at +550. The Braves (+700) and Phillies (+800) round out the favorites in what looks like another wide-open season.

The most interesting value might be in the middle tier. Teams like the Orioles (+1200), Guardians (+1400), and Padres (+1600) have all made significant off-season improvements and could be dark horses come October. If you're looking to take a shot on a longer price, those are the spots to watch.

Spring Training Preview

Pitchers and catchers report in just over a month, with full squad workouts beginning shortly after. The Cactus League and Grapefruit League schedules have been released, and we'll start getting our first look at the new rosters in late February. Until then, the Hot Stove continues to simmer with trades and minor signings rounding out contending rosters.

Championship Recap

The 2025 World Series delivered everything we could have asked for - drama, clutch performances, and a series that went the distance. For those looking ahead to 2026, understanding what made the champions successful provides crucial context for evaluating the upcoming season's contenders.

The winning formula hasn't changed: elite pitching, timely hitting, and a bullpen you can trust in October. Teams that can check all three boxes will be in the mix. Teams that can only check one or two will be watching from home.

Key Off-Season Storylines

Several franchise-altering moves have already reshaped the competitive balance. The trade market has been particularly active, with contenders adding pieces and rebuilding teams stockpiling prospects. The deadline for teams to tender contracts has passed, and non-tender season gave us some surprising names hitting the open market.

Keep an eye on the international signing period as well. Some of the game's next great stars will be signing in the coming months, and smart organizations are investing heavily in their international scouting departments.
