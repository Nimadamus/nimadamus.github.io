Champions
Dodgers defeat Yankees 4-2 | World Series MVP: Shohei Ohtani

The Los Angeles Dodgers captured their second World Series title in four years, defeating the New York Yankees in six thrilling games. Shohei Ohtani delivered a legendary postseason performance, becoming the first player to win World Series MVP as a designated hitter. Ohtani hit .400 with 5 home runs and 12 RBIs across the Fall Classic, including a go-ahead grand slam in Game 5.

The Dodgers rotation, anchored by Yoshinobu Yamamoto and Tyler Glasnow, held the vaunted Yankees lineup to just 3.2 runs per game. Manager Dave Roberts became the first manager to win multiple World Series titles for the franchise since Tommy Lasorda.
