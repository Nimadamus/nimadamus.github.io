Bankroll Management by Sport

Optimized Strategies for NFL, NBA, NHL, MLB & College Sports

Different sports have different characteristics that should inform your bankroll management approach. Season length, scoring patterns, variance, and market efficiency all vary significantly. Here's how to optimize your bankroll strategy for each major sport.

Why Sport-Specific Bankroll Management Matters

A $10,000 bankroll betting NFL requires a completely different approach than the same bankroll betting MLB. The NFL season has 17 games per team while MLB has 162. Your edge estimation accuracy differs by sport. Market efficiency varies. Treating all sports the same is a recipe for suboptimal results.

Key Considerations: The small sample size means you can't prove your edge within a single season. Key injuries, weather, and motivation factors create high game-to-game variance. Conservative bet sizing is essential.

Recommendation: Use quarter Kelly for most bets, half Kelly only for your highest-conviction plays. Never exceed 5% on any single NFL game. Cap weekly exposure at 15-20%.

Separate Bankrolls: Yes or No?

Recommendation: Keep separate mental (or actual) bankrolls for each sport you bet. This allows you to track your edge by sport, apply appropriate Kelly fractions, and prevent a losing streak in one sport from affecting your ability to bet another.

If you're betting $20,000 total across multiple sports, a reasonable allocation might be:

‚Ä¢ Your best sport (proven edge): 35-40% ($7,000-8,000)

‚Ä¢ Secondary sports: 25-30% each ($5,000-6,000)

‚Ä¢ Speculative/learning sports: 10-15% ($2,000-3,000)

Cross-Sport Correlation Warning

Be careful about "big sports days" where you might bet NFL, NBA, and NHL all on the same day. If you're betting 3% on each of 10 games across different sports, you have 30% of your total bankroll at risk on one day. Reduce individual bet sizes on heavy betting days.
