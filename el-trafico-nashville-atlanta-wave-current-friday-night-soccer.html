El Trafico Closes A Friday Night That Starts With Nashville, The Best Team In The East, Hosting Atlanta

MLS and NWSL - Friday Night Analysis
Published July 17, 2026

Three matches worth your Friday evening, and one of them is a derby that has never once respected the table. Nashville SC have lost a single match all season and host the club sitting fourteenth in their own conference. The San Diego Wave take the best defence in the NWSL to a Kansas City side that has not drawn a match all year. And El Trafico closes it out in Carson, where the Galaxy have contrived a record of exactly five wins, five draws and five losses. Verified standings for every match below. Analysis only, no picks.

El Trafico - Western Conference Derby
MLS

LAFC @ LA Galaxy

Friday, 10:25 PM ET | Dignity Health Sports Park, Carson, CA

The Galaxy have played fifteen matches this season and finished with a 5-5-5 record: five wins, five draws, five losses. They have scored 22 goals and conceded 22. A goal difference of exactly zero. You could not build a more literal picture of a team that has decided nothing about itself, and it sits ninth in the Western Conference on 20 points.

LAFC arrive as the better side and not by a landslide. They are 7-3-5 across their own fifteen matches, fifth in the West on 24 points, with 24 goals scored and 17 conceded for a plus-seven difference. Four points and four places separate them from their neighbours, which in a derby is another way of saying nothing separates them.

El Trafico has never especially cared about the table, and this is the version of the fixture where it matters least. The Galaxy's problem is legible in the numbers: a defence that has conceded as often as the attack has delivered. LAFC's edge is that they concede seven fewer goals across the same number of games. In a derby that historically refuses to stay tidy, the side that has been harder to score against is the side with the better argument, and it kicks off after ten o'clock on the East Coast for anyone still awake.

Eastern Conference - First vs Fourteenth
MLS

Atlanta United @ Nashville SC

Friday, 8:10 PM ET | GEODIS Park, Nashville, TN

Nashville SC have lost one match all season. One. Across fourteen games they are 10-3-1 with 33 points, top of the Eastern Conference, and they have scored 31 goals while conceding 11. A plus-20 goal difference in fourteen matches is not form, it is a level.

Atlanta United are the opposite pole of the same conference. They are 3-2-9 on 11 points, fourteenth in the East, with 14 goals scored and 23 conceded. Twenty-two points separate these two clubs in the same table after roughly the same number of games.

The gap is the story and the trap is that GEODIS Park has a way of making Nashville's opponents commit numbers they do not have. Atlanta have to defend a side averaging better than two goals a game with a back line already leaking at a rate of more than one and a half. Realistically, the visitors' route is a low block and a set piece, because they cannot win this by playing.

NWSL - League Leaders On The Road
NWSL

San Diego Wave @ Kansas City Current

Friday, 8:00 PM ET | CPKC Stadium, Kansas City, MO

Here is a genuine oddity: the Kansas City Current have played fourteen NWSL matches this season and drawn zero of them. Their record is 8-0-6. Every single match has produced a winner. That is not a stylistic footnote, it is a club that plays every game like a knockout tie, and it has left them sixth on 24 points with 21 goals scored and 20 conceded.

San Diego Wave come to CPKC Stadium sitting first in the NWSL at 9-1-5 on 28 points, with 21 goals for and 15 against. Both clubs have scored exactly 21 goals. The difference between first and sixth is entirely at the other end: five fewer goals conceded across fifteen matches.

That is the whole match in one line. Kansas City can score with the league leaders and cannot stop conceding. San Diego are top of the table because they defend, not because they are more dangerous. The Current's total absence of draws suggests this will not be a cagey evening, and Prime Video has the broadcast.
