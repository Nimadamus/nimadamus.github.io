The Final-Day Picture: What Is Still Live in Matchweek 38

All 10 matches kick off 11:00 AM ET | Sunday, May 24, 2026

Premier LeagueMatchweek 38Final Day

Here is the thing about final day in the Premier League: the title can be done and the season can still feel like it is hanging by a thread. Arsenal wrapped up the championship before this round even arrived, clinching their first league crown in 22 years when Manchester City could only draw 1-1 at Bournemouth on May 19. The Gunners sit on 82 points with a plus-43 goal difference, comfortably clear of City on 78. That top of the table is settled, and so is the Champions League quartet behind it: City, Manchester United on 68, and Aston Villa on 62 are all locked into Europe's top competition alongside the champions. What is not settled is everything in the middle and at the bottom, and that is why all ten games kick off at the same time, 11:00 AM ET on Sunday, so no club gets to peek at another's score before deciding how to play.

The European race below the top four is genuinely tangled. Liverpool hold fifth on 59 points and the Europa League place that comes with it, but Bournemouth sit sixth on 56 and can still apply pressure, while Brighton on 53 in seventh are clinging to a Conference League hope. Final-day European math in England always carries a wrinkle from cup outcomes, so the exact prize attached to fifth and sixth can shift depending on results elsewhere in the European finals, but the league order itself is what these clubs control on the pitch. The relegation battle is even sharper. Wolves on 19 points and Burnley on 21 are already down. The third and final drop spot is a straight fight between Tottenham, 17th on 38 points, and West Ham, 18th on 36, both at home, both kicking off at the same second.

That simultaneous start is what makes the day special. Spurs need only to avoid losing at home to Everton to be safe, because their goal difference sits above West Ham's. West Ham's path is brutal in its simplicity: they have to beat Leeds at the London Stadium and hope Tottenham lose to Everton. Anything less and the Hammers go down. Below, we break down the matches that actually decide the European places and the relegation scrap, with the verified market prices woven in. No picks, just the analysis of where the drama lives on a day when the whole league plays at once.

Tottenham vs Everton

11:00 AM ET | Tottenham Hotspur Stadium

Relegation BattleNorth LondonSurvival

This is the game that defines Tottenham's season. Spurs go into the final day 17th on 38 points, one rung above the drop, and the assignment could not be clearer: avoid defeat against Everton and they stay up, because their goal difference is superior to West Ham's. A draw is enough. A win removes all doubt. Only a loss, paired with a West Ham victory, sends them down. For a club of Tottenham's stature, sweating survival in the 38th game is a remarkable place to be, and the home crowd will feel every minute of it.

The market frames Spurs as narrow favorites at home, priced around minus-114 on the moneyline, with Everton a clear underdog near plus-310 and the draw around plus-132. Both teams to score has been bid up to roughly minus-159, which tells you the books expect goals at both ends rather than a clean sheet shutout. Everton arrive as mid-table spoilers with nothing of their own to chase, which can cut both ways: free of pressure, a visiting side can play loose and dangerous, or it can drift in a game that means everything to one bench and little to the other. For Tottenham, the danger is overthinking a result they can secure simply by not losing.

The reason this is must-watch is the simultaneous clock. Spurs cannot manage the game off West Ham's scoreboard, because they will not reliably know it. If Everton score early and West Ham are beating Leeds at the same moment, Tottenham could spend long stretches sitting inside the relegation zone in real time. That is the cruelty of final day, and it is exactly why a comfortable-looking minus-114 home price still carries genuine tension.

West Ham vs Leeds

11:00 AM ET | London Stadium

Relegation BattleMust-WinLondon Stadium

West Ham's situation is the harsh one. Sitting 18th on 36 points, two behind Tottenham with the goal-difference tiebreaker against them, the Hammers have to win and need help. Beating Leeds is the part they control. The other half, Tottenham failing to avoid defeat against Everton, is entirely out of their hands. That combination, win plus a Spurs slip, is the only route that lifts West Ham to 39 points and drops Tottenham into the bottom three on the final whistle.

The London Stadium crowd at least gets a favorable price to cheer. West Ham are priced around minus-135 on the moneyline, with Leeds a long shot near plus-335. The totals market leans toward goals: the Over 2.5 sits close to a coin flip with a slight lean over, and the Over 3.5 is available around plus-160 against the Under 3.5 near minus-225, which paints a picture of a match the books expect to be open rather than locked down. That makes sense for a home side that must chase a result and a visiting Leeds team with no league stakes of its own on the final afternoon.

The emotional weight here is unique because West Ham can do their job perfectly and still go down. They could be winning comfortably and watching the stadium screens for news from Tottenham that never turns their way. For Leeds, the role is pure spoiler, and there is a long tradition of final-day visitors with nothing to lose throwing a wrench into a desperate host's plans. The minus-135 favorite tag reflects the urgency, not certainty.

Liverpool vs Brentford

11:00 AM ET | Anfield

European RaceAnfieldFifth Place

Liverpool come into the final day holding fifth on 59 points and the Europa League place that goes with it. They are not chasing the title and they are not in relegation peril, but the European order behind the top four is still live, and a Liverpool win at Anfield is the cleanest way to keep their finishing position firmly where they want it rather than leaving the door ajar for Bournemouth below them. There is real motivation here, even if it lacks the life-or-death edge of the relegation games.

The hosts are favored at home, priced around minus-115 to minus-125 on the moneyline, with Brentford a sizable underdog near plus-280 and the draw out around plus-320. The total leans cautious, with one projection putting the Under 2.5 goals slightly ahead of the Over, a reminder that a Liverpool side managing its European position does not always need to throw caution to the wind. Brentford arrive as a well-organized visiting team with no table stakes of their own, the kind of side that can frustrate a favorite if the home crowd grows anxious.

What makes Anfield worth watching on this particular Sunday is the chain reaction. If Bournemouth win their match at the same time, Liverpool's result suddenly matters more for the fifth-versus-sixth pecking order and the European prize attached to each. A comfortable Liverpool win settles it. A draw or a slip, with Bournemouth winning, is exactly the kind of simultaneous swing that final day specializes in.

Nottingham Forest vs Bournemouth

11:00 AM ET | The City Ground

European RaceSixth PlaceCity Ground

This is the European-hopeful's final exam. Bournemouth sit sixth on 56 points and have already secured European football for the first time in the club's history, a milestone sealed by that late draw with Manchester City. Now they want more. A win at the City Ground is the only way Bournemouth can keep pressure on Liverpool in the race for fifth, and depending on how the European finals shake out elsewhere, sixth place itself could carry a meaningful continental reward. For a club of Bournemouth's size, this is uncharted, exciting territory.

Interestingly, the road team is favored here. Bournemouth are priced around plus-102 on the moneyline as narrow favorites, with Nottingham Forest the home underdog near plus-235, an unusual look that reflects how strong Bournemouth's season has been relative to a Forest side playing out a quieter final day. The draw is the third option, and the spread of three-way prices suggests a competitive, tight match rather than a one-sided afternoon.

The watch-along value is in the math. Bournemouth need their own result and then need Liverpool to stumble to climb into fifth, so the City Ground scoreboard and the Anfield scoreboard are effectively linked in real time. A Bournemouth goal at the same instant Brentford are level at Anfield is the sort of simultaneous twist that makes a single 11:00 AM ET kickoff window so compelling, even in a match that on paper looks like a mid-table fixture.

Crystal Palace vs Arsenal

11:00 AM ET | Selhurst Park

Champions' SendoffSelhurst ParkBrighton Watch

Arsenal arrive at Selhurst Park as confirmed champions, which changes the texture of this fixture entirely. The pressure that defined their title run is gone, replaced by the looser mood of a side that has already done the job. The intrigue shifts to Crystal Palace, a mid-table host with a chance to test themselves against the best team in England on a day when that team has nothing left to prove on the table. For Palace, a result against the champions would be a fine note to end the season on; for Arsenal, it is a celebration as much as a contest.

Even with the title settled, the market still respects the champions. Arsenal are favored around minus-120 on the moneyline, with Crystal Palace the home underdog near plus-320 and the draw the alternative in between. Predictive models put Arsenal's win probability in the high-50s percent range, which is consistent with a road favorite carrying genuine quality even in a low-stakes setting. The question is always whether a freshly crowned champion brings full intensity to a dead rubber or eases off with the trophy already secured.

Crystal Palace also matter to the wider final-day picture because of the European race feeding off these results. Brighton, sitting seventh and chasing a Conference League hope, will be tracking what happens at clubs around them. Palace pulling off an upset against Arsenal, or even taking a point, ripples into the seventh-place conversation in ways that a quiet 11:00 AM ET window suddenly makes interesting. It is the champions' party with a side plot the rest of the league cannot ignore.

Brighton vs Manchester United

11:00 AM ET | Amex Stadium

European RaceAmex StadiumSeventh Place

Brighton close the season at home on 53 points in seventh, holding onto a Conference League hope that the final day will resolve one way or the other. The Seagulls have built their reputation on being awkward opponents who play on the front foot regardless of who visits, and ending the campaign in front of their own supporters with a European place potentially on the line is the kind of stage they relish. Manchester United, meanwhile, arrive with their Champions League finish already banked on 68 points, which strips the result of stakes for the visitors and hands the urgency to the home side.

The market sees Brighton as the favorite at the Amex, priced around minus-128 on the moneyline, with Manchester United the underdog near plus-290 and the draw in between. That is a notable line, a host favored over Manchester United, and it speaks to both Brighton's home strength this season and United's lack of anything to chase on the final day. Models put Brighton's win probability in the high-40s to mid-50s percent range, which matches a deserved-favorite tag rather than a heavy one.

The reason to keep this game on the multi-screen final-day grid is the seventh-place thread. Brighton's reward for finishing seventh depends on results filtering through the European finals and the clubs immediately around them, so a Brighton win is necessary but not always sufficient on its own. With United playing for pride and Brighton playing for a continental ticket, the contrast in motivation is exactly what makes a simultaneous kickoff worth watching beyond the relegation and title-adjacent storylines.
