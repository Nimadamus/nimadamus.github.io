Syracuse @ #16 North Carolina

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Dean E. Smith Center, Chapel Hill, NC
Spread
UNC -10.5 (-110)
Moneyline
SYR +450 / UNC -650
Total
O/U 155.5

The Dean E. Smith Center has been a house of horrors for ACC opponents this season, with North Carolina posting a perfect 12-0 record on their home floor. The #16 Tar Heels at 17-4 (5-3 ACC) are clicking at exactly the right time, coming off an impressive 91-75 win over Georgia Tech on Saturday. Hubert Davis has this team playing connected basketball on both ends, and their offensive efficiency at home has been among the best in the conference.

Syracuse at 13-9 (4-5 ACC) is coming off an encouraging 86-72 win over Notre Dame, but this is a massive step up in competition. The Orange are just 2-2 on the road in ACC play and have struggled against ranked opponents. Jim Boeheim's iconic 2-3 zone still gives opponents fits at times, but North Carolina has the shooters and ball movement to pick it apart if they're patient.

The Tar Heels lead the all-time series 18-7 and have won 14 of the last 17 meetings against Syracuse. This dominance is particularly pronounced in Chapel Hill, where the atmosphere is electric for Big Monday games. The 10.5-point spread is substantial, but North Carolina's home form and the historical dominance in this matchup justify the number. The 155.5 total suggests a moderately paced game, with both teams capable of scoring in transition when opportunities present themselves.

Syracuse will need a near-perfect shooting night from three-point range to keep this game competitive. Their zone defense can frustrate opponents, but North Carolina's depth and experience should wear down the Orange over 40 minutes. Expect the Tar Heels to pull away in the second half as their superior athleticism and home crowd push them toward another impressive victory.

#14 Kansas @ #11 Texas Tech

Monday, 9:00 PM ET | United Supermarket Arena, Lubbock, TX
Spread
TTU -4.5 (-110)
Moneyline
KU +158 / TTU -190
Total
O/U 154.5

This is tonight's Featured Game of the Day, and for good reason. Two top-15 teams clash in what promises to be one of the most intense atmospheres in college basketball. United Supermarket Arena in Lubbock is one of the toughest places to play in the country, and Texas Tech at 16-5 has been nearly unbeatable at home this season. Grant McCasland's team plays suffocating defense and has the guard play to match up with anyone.

Kansas at 16-5 brings the historical pedigree that few programs can match. Bill Self's squad has been inconsistent at times this season, but their ceiling is as high as anyone's in college basketball. The Jayhawks are battle-tested in hostile environments, and their experience in big games should serve them well in this spot. However, playing at altitude in Lubbock against an elite defensive team presents a significant challenge.

Texas Tech's defensive metrics are staggering, ranking in the top 10 nationally in adjusted defensive efficiency. They force turnovers, contest every shot, and rarely give opponents easy looks at the rim. Kansas will need to be extremely patient on offense and take care of the basketball to have any chance on the road. The Red Raiders are 4.5-point favorites, and their home court advantage combined with their defensive prowess makes them a formidable obstacle.
