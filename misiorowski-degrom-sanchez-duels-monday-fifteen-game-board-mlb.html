Misiorowski, deGrom And Sanchez: Three Aces Anchor A Fifteen-Game Monday

MLB Daily Board - July 20, 2026
Published July 20, 2026

Every team in baseball plays tonight, and the pitching is the story of the full fifteen-game card. Jacob Misiorowski and his 1.62 ERA host the Mets in Milwaukee, the 63-37 Dodgers open at Citizens Bank Park against 11-4 Cristopher Sanchez, Jacob deGrom takes his 0.86 home WHIP into a date with the surging White Sox, and Nick Martinez meets Dylan Cease in the night's best pure duel at Rogers Centre. Ten breakdowns below, every number verified. Analysis only.

Marquee - Misiorowski Night

Mets @ Brewers

Monday, 7:40 PM ET | American Family Field, Milwaukee, WI
Brewers ML
-208
Total
7.5 Runs

The widest current-form gap of the entire Monday board lives in Milwaukee. Jacob Misiorowski is 10-4 with a 1.62 ERA, a 0.76 WHIP and 167 strikeouts against 27 walks in 111 innings, holding opponents to a .148 average, and his home version is somehow sharper: a 1.59 ERA at American Family Field with a .153 opponent average and better than fourteen strikeouts per nine. He has been the most dominant starter in the National League this season by nearly any rate measure, and the market prices Monday accordingly at -208 with a 7.5 total.

The 42-58 Mets are a difficult watch right now. The team is hitting .234 with a .686 OPS, the lowest average of any club on the marquee side of tonight's board, and the offense's 409 runs in 100 games leans heavily on the home-run ball, 117 of them, which is a rough profile against a starter who barely allows baserunners at all. Freddy Peralta takes the ball for New York at 5-8 with a 4.66 ERA, and the road splits are the concern: a 5.58 ERA and 1.54 WHIP away from Citi Field with opponents hitting .283 and homers flying at 1.79 per nine.

The 62-37 Brewers have won with exactly the formula on display tonight: elite run prevention fronting a patient offense that hits .253 with a .337 on-base percentage and has scored 502 runs. Milwaukee owns the National League Central's best record and the deepest gap between its rotation's results and its opponents' expectations. A 7.5 total with a -208 favorite tells you how the market sees the shape of this one: low scoring, one-sided, and driven entirely by whether anyone touches Misiorowski at all.

Game 2

Dodgers @ Phillies

Monday, 7:10 PM ET | Citizens Bank Park, Philadelphia, PA
Phillies ML
-136
Total
8.5 Runs

Tonight's Featured Game puts baseball's best record in the underdog role. The 63-37 Dodgers bring the sport's best team OPS at .772 into Citizens Bank Park, and the 55-45 Phillies are favored anyway at -136, because Cristopher Sanchez is 11-4 with a 2.62 ERA and Emmet Sheehan is carrying a 4.81. Two starters this far apart move a market more than an eight-game gap in the standings does.

Sanchez's profile is the exact counter to what Los Angeles does best. The Dodgers grind at-bats and stack baserunners behind a .342 team on-base percentage; Sanchez has walked 25 hitters in 127.1 innings while striking out 144. When a lineup that feeds on free passes meets a left-hander who refuses to issue them, something gives, and that collision is the whole game. Sheehan's job on the other side is simpler to describe and harder to do: keep a 128-homer Phillies lineup in the park at home.

The series stakes are real for both sides, Philadelphia chasing its division race and the Dodgers looking to keep the best-record pace, and the 8.5 total splits the difference between the league's best OPS and one of its best Monday starters. Game 3

Rays @ Blue Jays

Monday, 7:07 PM ET | Rogers Centre, Toronto, ON
Blue Jays ML
-156
Total
7.5 Runs

The best pure pitching duel of the night is in Toronto, and the market's 7.5 total respects it. Nick Martinez arrives at 8-2 with a 2.65 ERA and a 1.12 WHIP, with just 19 walks in 105.1 innings, the profile of a veteran strike-thrower at the peak of his command. Dylan Cease answers at 6-4 with a 2.56 ERA and 148 strikeouts in 98.1 innings, holding hitters to a .190 average, and his home work at Rogers Centre has been electric: 14.29 strikeouts per nine with a .196 average against.

The standings tension makes this series bigger than a July Monday. The 56-42 Rays own one of the American League's best records and a .257 team average built on contact and one of the sport's lowest team strikeout counts, while the 46-53 Blue Jays are running out of season and playing home games they cannot afford to drop. Toronto being favored at -156 despite a ten-game record deficit is a Cease-at-home number, and it says the market believes the strikeout stuff neutralizes Tampa Bay's contact machine.

Both offenses profile modestly against this level of pitching. Toronto hits .242 with a .687 OPS and scores barely four a game; Tampa Bay's low-power, 98-homer attack needs sequencing to produce crooked innings, and sequencing against Cease at home is the hardest single assignment on tonight's board. A tight, starter-driven game is the market's expectation and the reasonable one.

Game 4

Twins @ Guardians

Monday, 6:40 PM ET | Progressive Field, Cleveland, OH
Twins ML
-121
Total
7.5 Runs

A road favorite in a divisional game is always worth a second look, and this one is a pitching statement: Joe Ryan is priced at -121 in Cleveland because his road work has been outstanding, a 2.47 ERA and 1.00 WHIP away from Target Field with a .199 opponent average. His full season line, 2.85 with 128 strikeouts against 25 walks in 110.1 innings, makes him one of the American League's most reliable starts, and the 49-51 Twins have stayed on the fringe of the race largely on nights he pitches.

The 52-48 Guardians hold the better record and the worse everything else offensively. Cleveland is hitting .230 with a .678 OPS, the lowest marks of any team on tonight's card, and 392 runs in 100 games rounds to under four a night. Tanner Bibee's 3-9 record wildly understates his actual pitching, a 3.90 ERA with a 1.15 WHIP, and that disconnect is the cleanest single illustration of what this Cleveland offense has done to its rotation all season.

The one crack in the low-scoring script is Bibee's home-run tendency at Progressive Field, better than two per nine at home this season, against a Twins lineup with 123 homers. The 7.5 total prices a duel; Bibee's gopher balls are the argument it turns into something louder. First pitch at 6:40 PM ET starts the night's schedule.

Game 5

Orioles @ Red Sox

Monday, 7:10 PM ET | Fenway Park, Boston, MA
Red Sox ML
-149
Total
8.5 Runs

An AL East basement battle with real strikeout appeal. The 50-48 Red Sox are favored at -149 behind Payton Tolle, who has quietly built a 3.11 ERA and 1.07 WHIP across 84 innings with 87 strikeouts, and at Fenway the young left-hander misses bats at better than eleven per nine. The 49-51 Orioles counter with Shane Baz, whose season has been a house divided: a 4.19 ERA overall and a 4-9 record, but a road profile, .242 opponent average, 1.30 WHIP, a home-run rate of just 0.88 per nine, that plays notably better than his home work.

Neither offense inspires much fear on current form. Boston hits .244 with a .708 OPS and has scored 413 runs in 98 games, about 4.2 a night; Baltimore hits .237 with more punch, a 119-homer season, but leads this game in strikeouts by a wide margin with 918 on the season. Both lineups have underperformed their talent for long stretches, which is a big part of why two former contenders are playing .500-or-worse baseball in late July.

Fenway is the wild card, as always. Baz's road success is built on keeping the ball in the park, and this is the one park where fly-ball contact turns into doubles without leaving the yard. The 8.5 total leans toward respect for both starters; the Monster leans the other way. How Baz manages left-center field is the whole game.

Game 6

White Sox @ Rangers

Monday, 8:05 PM ET | Globe Life Field, Arlington, TX
Rangers ML
-175
Total
7.5 Runs

Jacob deGrom at home is still one of the great sights in the sport: a 2.51 ERA, an 0.86 WHIP and a .178 opponent average at Globe Life Field this season. His overall line, 7-5 with a 3.49 ERA and 122 strikeouts against 22 walks in 100.2 innings, carries the usual deGrom command signature, and the -175 price with a 7.5 total is the market betting on the home version showing up. When he is on in Arlington, entire lineups disappear for six innings at a stretch.

The surprise of this game is who is on the other side. The 52-46 White Sox are six games over .500 in late July, hitting .240 as a team but with legitimate power, 131 homers, and 469 runs scored, more than the Rangers themselves have managed. Erick Fedde starts for Chicago at 5-6 with a 4.15 ERA, and his splits are the fault line of the night: a 2.91 ERA in his home park against a 5.48 mark with a 1.45 WHIP on the road.

Texas at 50-49 continues to hover at the break-even line, scoring 4.2 runs a game on a .245 average, and the offense's inconsistency is why a deGrom season this good has produced only seven wins. A 7.5 total with a heavy home favorite prices a classic deGrom script: low scoring, tightly managed, decided by two swings or fewer. Fedde's road form is the loudest argument against it.

Game 7

Pirates @ Yankees

Monday, 7:05 PM ET | Yankee Stadium, Bronx, NY
Yankees ML
-120
Total
7.5 Runs

Do not let the franchises' reputations fool you about which rotation arm is the draw here. Braxton Ashcraft is 9-3 with a 3.49 ERA and a 1.11 WHIP across 113.1 innings, striking out 128 against just 27 walks, and the 52-48 Pirates have climbed above .500 in large part behind his consistency. A -120 home price against a nine-game winner is one of the shortest Yankee Stadium lines of the season, and it reflects real respect for the Pittsburgh right-hander.

The 55-44 Yankees counter with left-hander Ryan Weathers, 3-7 with a 4.15 ERA and a 1.24 WHIP, whose 110 strikeouts in 97.2 innings show the stuff has never been the problem; run support and the long ball have. New York's lineup remains the reason to lay any price in the Bronx, and the short porch against a Pirates pitching staff that has carried its team all season is the central collision of the game.

Pittsburgh's offense is the season's quiet story: 533 runs scored with a .769 team OPS, production few saw coming, and a big part of why the team sits at 52-48 and within shouting distance of a wild-card race it was supposed to be nowhere near. A 7.5 total in Yankee Stadium is a low number for this building, and it tells you the market expects Ashcraft to keep doing exactly what he has done to everyone: throw strikes, avoid the middle of the plate, and make the game small.

Game 8

Tigers @ Cubs

Monday, 8:05 PM ET | Wrigley Field, Chicago, IL
Cubs ML
-116
Total
11.5 Runs

An 11.5 total is the second-highest number on the entire Monday board, and it is not hard to see why the market expects a shootout at Wrigley. Jack Flaherty arrives for Detroit at 3-8 with a 4.48 ERA and a 1.41 WHIP, walking 40 in 82.1 innings, while Jameson Taillon counters for Chicago at 2-5 with a 5.19 ERA. Two starters allowing this much traffic, in this ballpark, with a wind-aided July evening in play, is the recipe for exactly the number the books hung.

The 56-43 Cubs remain one of the National League's best stories, chasing the Brewers in the Central with 502 runs scored on the season, level with Milwaukee's own total. The 46-53 Tigers have slipped badly from their early-season pace, and Flaherty's individual struggles mirror the team's: talent intact, results cratering, a 3-8 record that has as much to do with walks as with luck.

A near pick-em price with a monster total frames this as a coin-flip slugfest, and the bullpens will decide it: whichever manager gets five competent innings from his starter wins the leverage battle in a game everyone expects to reach the middle relief early. For run-environment watchers, only Coors Field tonight carries a bigger number.

Game 9

Nationals @ Rockies

Monday, 8:40 PM ET | Coors Field, Denver, CO
Nationals ML
-123
Total
12 Runs

The biggest total in baseball tonight, 12 runs at Coors Field, and every input points the same direction. Washington brings the most productive offense on the Monday board, 545 runs and 144 home runs through 100 games on a .251 average, into the sport's friendliest run environment. Colorado answers with a lineup that hits .255 with a .747 OPS, numbers that swell at altitude, where the Rockies have played far better baseball than their 40-61 record suggests.

The pitching matchup is two arms bracing for impact. Rookie left-hander Andrew Alvarez carries a 4.04 ERA and a 1.45 WHIP across his first 49 big-league innings, with 22 walks that loom much larger at 5,200 feet, where free passes turn into runs at the highest rate in the league. Kyle Freeland's season line is rough, 2-8 with a 7.36 ERA, though the honest read of his splits is that the veteran has at least been more survivable at home, 6.38 at Coors against 8.28 with an 0-6 record on the road.

The market's slim -123 price on a 50-50 team against a 40-61 team is the Coors effect in one number: nobody lays a real price in this building, because the game state can flip on any half-inning. The exactly .500 Nationals have the season-long offensive edge; the Rockies have the environment and the experience of playing 81 games in the chaos. Twelve runs is the market saying both things are true.

Game 10

Athletics @ Diamondbacks

Monday, 9:40 PM ET | Chase Field, Phoenix, AZ
Diamondbacks ML
-163
Total
9 Runs

The late window belongs to the Springs problem. Jeffrey Springs is 3-9 with a 6.08 ERA and a 1.42 WHIP across 97.2 innings, and the home-run rates are the alarming part: 2.65 per nine at his home park and 2.08 on the road. Now the left-hander gets Chase Field, a building that rewards elevated contact, in front of a 42-57 Athletics club drifting through the second half. The -163 price on Arizona is less about the home team's brilliance and more about the visiting starter's season-long generosity.

The 51-48 Diamondbacks are quietly hanging on the edge of the National League race, and they are doing it with pitching depth rather than offense; the lineup hits just .237 with a .695 OPS. Tonight's start is an unusual one: left-hander Mitch Bratt makes only his third big-league appearance, which points toward an aggressive bullpen plan behind a short opening stint. Against an Athletics offense hitting a respectable .246 with a 122-homer season, the Arizona bullpen's night matters more than most.

The 9-run total captures the tension: a hitter-friendly park and a homer-prone starter argue for runs, while a bullpen-forward Arizona plan and an Athletics team that has faded argue for control.
