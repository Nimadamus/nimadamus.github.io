Guardians vs Brewers

Thursday, 2:10 PM ET | American Family Field, Milwaukee

The marquee on the Thursday board sends one of the National League's hottest teams onto its home field, as the 39-35 Cleveland Guardians visit the NL-best 45-26 Milwaukee Brewers in a getaway-day matinee. Milwaukee has been the surprise of the senior circuit, riding the most productive offense in this breakdown to the top of the standings, while Cleveland has hovered around contention on the strength of pitching and defense. The contrast in identities, a thumping lineup against a run-prevention club, makes this the headliner.

The headline arm belongs to Cleveland. Parker Messick takes the mound carrying a sparkling 2.68 ERA across 14 starts and 80.2 innings, with a 1.09 WHIP, 82 strikeouts, and a .216 opponent average. The left-hander has been one of the better run-prevention starters in baseball this season, missing bats and limiting hard contact, and his assignment against a Milwaukee lineup averaging a board-best 5.37 runs per game is the central test of the afternoon. If Messick is sharp, he gives Cleveland a real chance to quiet the league's most dangerous offense.

Milwaukee counters with Shane Drohan, who carries a 3.59 ERA and a 1.17 WHIP across his early body of work this season. The left-hander has shown solid run-prevention in a smaller sample, and against a Cleveland lineup that ranks as the quietest in this breakdown at 3.97 runs per game, his job is to keep the Guardians off the board long enough for a deep Brewers order to do the rest. A first-place team at home with the better offense is exactly where Milwaukee wants to be, and the duel between Messick's command and the Brewers' bats is the story of the day.

Giants vs Braves

Thursday, 7:15 PM ET | Truist Park, Atlanta

The best record in this breakdown belongs to Atlanta, and the 46-27 Braves welcome a struggling 31-43 Giants club to Truist Park. The gap in the standings is enormous, and Atlanta's blend of power and pitching has made them one of the class acts of the National League. San Francisco, by contrast, has been one of the season's bigger disappointments, and a road trip into one of the league's tougher environments is a difficult spot for a team trying to find traction.

Atlanta hands the ball to veteran left-hander Martin Perez, who has been excellent with a 2.90 ERA, a 1.05 WHIP, and a .202 opponent average across 10 starts and 62 innings. Perez has been keeping the ball off the barrel all year, and against a Giants lineup that makes plenty of contact but lacks big-time power, his command profile is well-suited to a quiet start. Behind one of the better records in baseball, the Braves do not need a masterpiece, just a steady outing in front of a deep lineup.

San Francisco counters with Landen Roupp, who carries a 4.24 ERA and a 1.29 WHIP across 14 starts and 74.1 innings with 82 strikeouts and a 5-7 record. Roupp can miss bats, but the elevated WHIP shows he allows his share of traffic, and against an Atlanta lineup with this much thump, runners on base are dangerous. The Giants do own the best team average in this breakdown at .258, so their contact-heavy approach gives them a puncher's chance, but the matchup on paper leans heavily toward the home side. This is the kind of game a first-place team is expected to take care of.

White Sox vs Yankees

Thursday, 7:05 PM ET | Yankee Stadium, New York

The Bronx hosts a clash of clubs heading in different directions as the 38-34 Chicago White Sox visit the 45-27 New York Yankees, who own a share of the best record on the board and lead the American League East. The Yankees have powered their way to the top behind a board-best 109 balls hit over the fence and a .775 team OPS, while Chicago has overachieved expectations to stay above .500. Yankee Stadium's short porch and New York's thunderous lineup make this a difficult road assignment for the White Sox.

New York hands the ball to Ryan Weathers, who carries a 4.36 ERA across 13 starts and 74.1 innings with 81 strikeouts and a 1.17 WHIP. The surface ERA is ordinary, but the strikeout-per-inning rate and sub-1.20 WHIP show a pitcher who limits traffic, and backed by the most dangerous home offense in this breakdown, he does not need to be dominant. The Yankees lineup is built to punish mistakes over the fence, and at home in a launching pad, that profile is the engine of the New York attack.

Chicago counters with Sean Burke, a 4.15 ERA and 1.26 WHIP across 11 starts and 73.2 innings with 73 strikeouts. Burke has missed bats, but the WHIP shows he allows baserunners, and that is a dangerous trait to carry into Yankee Stadium against a lineup that leads this breakdown in power. The White Sox have cleared the fence 100 times of their own, so they are not without thump, but they also carry the most strikeouts in this game at 655, a profile that can go quiet against a Yankee bullpen protecting a late lead. The matchup tilts toward the home side.

Blue Jays vs Red Sox

Thursday, 1:35 PM ET | Fenway Park, Boston

An AL East matinee at Fenway Park sends the 36-38 Toronto Blue Jays in to face the 29-42 Boston Red Sox in a meeting of two clubs trying to find their footing. Toronto has hovered just below .500 as a pitching-led group, while Boston has struggled to score all season and sits well back in the division. The historic ballpark and a compelling pitching matchup, a steady veteran against a promising young arm, give this day game more intrigue than the records suggest.

Boston leans on veteran right-hander Sonny Gray, who has been excellent with a 3.03 ERA, a 1.20 WHIP, and an 8-1 record across 12 starts and 62.1 innings. Gray has been the steadying force in the Boston rotation, and his ability to mix pitches and limit damage gives the Red Sox a real chance to win a low-scoring game despite their offensive woes. Against a Toronto lineup that does not slug in bunches, Gray's command profile is a strong fit, and he is exactly the kind of arm that can keep a struggling team in every start.

Toronto counters with young right-hander Trey Yesavage, who carries a 3.78 ERA and a 1.26 WHIP across 9 starts and 47.2 innings with 47 strikeouts and a .207 opponent average. Yesavage has been keeping hitters off the barrel, and against the lowest-scoring offense in this breakdown, his contact-suppression profile is a difficult assignment for a Boston lineup that has hit just 59 home runs all year. The contrast between a proven veteran and a rising young arm, both pitching well, sets up the kind of tense, low-scoring duel Fenway day games often produce.

Orioles vs Mariners

Thursday, 9:40 PM ET | T-Mobile Park, Seattle

The nightcap on the board heads to the Pacific Northwest, where the 35-40 Baltimore Orioles visit the 38-37 Seattle Mariners in a matchup of two clubs trying to climb in their respective races. Seattle plays in one of the most pitcher-friendly parks in baseball, and T-Mobile Park has a way of turning even modest pitching matchups into low-scoring affairs. Both teams have talented young cores, and a late-night West Coast game often produces the kind of grind that rewards pitching and defense.

Seattle hands the ball to Bryan Woo, the steady right-hander carrying a 4.28 ERA across 14 starts and 82 innings with 79 strikeouts and an elite 1.04 WHIP. The surface ERA undersells him; a sub-1.05 WHIP and a .227 opponent average show a pitcher who simply does not allow traffic, and pitching in front of his home crowd in a park that suppresses offense gives him a real edge. The Mariners need length from Woo to protect a bullpen that has carried a heavy load, and against a Baltimore lineup that can be streaky, he is well-positioned to keep the visitors quiet.

Baltimore counters with Shane Baz, who carries a 4.06 ERA but a bloated 1.39 WHIP across 14 starts and 82 innings as he works through a 4-6 season. Baz has the stuff to miss bats, with 67 strikeouts on the year, but the elevated WHIP and a .264 opponent average show he has been allowing too much traffic. In a spacious park against a disciplined Seattle lineup, his margin for error is thin. This is a matchup of a steady home arm against a talented but inconsistent visitor, and the pitcher-friendly setting tilts the night toward a tense, low-scoring finish.
