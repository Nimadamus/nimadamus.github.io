World Cup Day 6

France, Argentina And Norway Open Groups I And J

Day 6 of the 2026 World Cup launches two more groups with four opening matches across the United States, from France meeting Senegal at MetLife Stadium to Iraq facing Erling Haaland and Norway in Foxborough, reigning champions Argentina opening against Algeria in Kansas City, and Austria taking on Jordan in Santa Clara. Group I Opener

France vs Senegal

Tuesday, 3:00 PM ET | East Rutherford, New Jersey
Group
I
Venue
MetLife Stadium
Broadcast
Fox / Telemundo

The headline match of Day 6 carries a heavy dose of history, as France opens its World Cup campaign against Senegal at MetLife Stadium. The fixture instantly recalls 2002, when Senegal stunned the then-defending champions France 1-0 in the opening match of that tournament. This time France arrives ranked number two in the FIFA world rankings and installed as one of the favorites to lift the trophy, a deep and dangerous side led by Kylian Mbappe, whose pace and finishing make him the focal point of everything the French attack does. The market reflects that gap, pricing France as clear favorites at -225 with the draw at +350 and Senegal out at +550.

Senegal, for all that France carry the pedigree, are exactly the kind of opponent capable of making a favorite uncomfortable. The Lions of Teranga combine real pace and physicality with genuine quality in attack, and they have the athletes to trouble any back line in transition. The tactical question is whether Senegal can stay compact and disciplined enough to weather France's possession while picking their moments to break, or whether the depth and individual brilliance of the French squad tells over ninety minutes. As a Group I opener in the New York metropolitan area, it is the marquee fixture of the day and a true test of France's title credentials.

Group I Opener

Iraq vs Norway

Tuesday, 6:00 PM ET | Foxborough, Massachusetts
Group
I
Venue
Gillette Stadium
Broadcast
FS1 / Telemundo

The other half of Group I meets in Foxborough, where Norway opens against Iraq with Erling Haaland leading the line. Norway arrived at this World Cup in dominant qualifying form, winning Group I of UEFA qualification with a perfect 8-0-0 record while outscoring opponents 37-5. That goal difference tells the story of a team built to attack, and in Haaland they have one of the most lethal finishers on the planet, a striker whose movement and power can decide a group-stage match on a single chance. As the clear favorite, Norway will look to assert control early and bank three points in its opener.

Iraq returns to the World Cup stage as a side that earned its place through resilience and organization, the kind of team that defends in numbers and looks to frustrate more fancied opponents. The Lions of Mesopotamia will need to be compact and disciplined to limit the supply into Haaland, because giving a striker of his quality even a handful of clear looks is a dangerous proposition. The tactical battle centers on whether Iraq's structure can blunt Norway's attacking firepower, or whether the gap in individual quality up front proves decisive. In a passionate New England soccer setting, this opener sets the early tone in Group I.

Group J Opener

Argentina vs Algeria

Tuesday, 9:00 PM ET | Kansas City, Missouri
Group
J
Venue
Arrowhead Stadium
Broadcast
Fox / Telemundo

The reigning world champions begin their title defense in Kansas City, where Argentina opens Group J against Algeria at Arrowhead Stadium. Argentina arrives as the clear favorite to top the group, priced at -260, and as a side that blends tournament-winning experience with the kind of midfield control that lets it dictate the rhythm of a match. La Albiceleste are accustomed to managing the weight of expectation, and a composed, professional opener is exactly what a defending champion wants before the group tightens.

Algeria returns to the World Cup for the first time since 2014, and they do so in genuinely impressive form, having won all seven of their friendly matches in the build-up, including victories over Mauritania and Zambia. The Desert Foxes carry confidence and a clear attacking identity, and they will not simply sit back and absorb pressure against Argentina. The tactical intrigue is whether Algeria's form and front-foot approach can test a champion that prefers to control proceedings, or whether Argentina's quality and game management prove too much. At Arrowhead in a primetime slot, it is one of the most anticipated openers of the group stage.

Group J Opener

Austria vs Jordan

Tuesday, 12:00 AM ET | Santa Clara, California
Group
J
Venue
Levi's Stadium
Broadcast

The late finish in Santa Clara rounds out Group J, where Austria meets Jordan at Levi's Stadium. Austria arrives as a well-drilled European side that built its qualification on structure, work rate, and an organized pressing game, the kind of team that does not beat itself and asks opponents to break down a disciplined block. In a group headlined by Argentina, three points in this opener would be a meaningful step toward keeping Austria's knockout ambitions on track, and the Austrians will see Jordan as exactly the sort of fixture they need to start with a win.

Jordan reaches the World Cup as one of the tournament's notable newcomers, a side that earned its place through a strong qualifying run and arrives with little to lose and plenty of belief. The team plays with discipline and looks to make itself difficult to break down, and a positive result here would be a landmark moment for Jordanian football. The tactical battle centers on whether Jordan's organization can frustrate an Austria side that wants to dominate the ball, or whether Austria's greater tournament experience tells in the latter stages. It is a fitting close to a Day 6 slate that launched two fresh groups across the host nation.
