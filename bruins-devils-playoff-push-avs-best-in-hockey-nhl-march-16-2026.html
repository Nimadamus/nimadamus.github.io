5-Game Monday Slate

NHL March 16, 2026

Monday delivers a tight but loaded 5-game NHL slate with two nationally televised marquee matchups on ESPN. The Boston Bruins and New Jersey Devils collide in a crucial Eastern Conference playoff-push battle, while the Colorado Avalanche, owners of the best record in hockey at 44-12-9, host a slumping Pittsburgh Penguins squad. Three more games round out the evening across all four time zones.

Flames @ Red Wings

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Puck Line
DET -1.5 (-162)
Moneyline
DET -180 / CGY +150
Total
O/U 5.5 (O -130 / U +110)

Detroit has been one of the most pleasant surprises in the Eastern Conference this season, and hosting Calgary on a Monday night at Little Caesars Arena should feel like a layup for a team firing on all cylinders. The Red Wings (36-23-5, 77 points) are comfortably in a playoff spot, and a massive reason for that is the renaissance of John Gibson between the pipes. Since December 2, Gibson has been nothing short of sensational, posting a 19-5-1 record with a 2.08 GAA and a .925 save percentage. That's elite-tier goaltending, and it has transformed Detroit from a fringe team into a legitimate contender in the East.

Calgary (26-32-7, 59 points) is going nowhere fast, and the numbers paint a bleak picture. The Flames own the worst scoring offense in the entire NHL at just 2.5 goals per game, which is a death sentence against a Detroit team that doesn't need to score five to win. When you can't put the puck in the net, every defensive breakdown becomes a catastrophe, and Calgary simply doesn't have the firepower to keep up in a shootout. Dustin Wolf has shown flashes of being a capable NHL goaltender, but even the best netminder in the world can't win games when his team can't score.

The -180 moneyline for Detroit reflects just how wide the gap is between these two teams right now. The Red Wings have the superior goaltending, the better offense, the home-ice advantage, and the motivation of a team fighting for playoff positioning. Calgary is playing out the string, and while there's professional pride on the line, there's no tangible reward for winning in mid-March when you're 18 points out of a wild card spot. The 5.5 total with the over juiced at -130 is interesting, because Detroit has the firepower to put up three or four goals against a Calgary team that struggles to keep the puck out of its own net.

The puck line at DET -1.5 (-162) tells you the market expects a comfortable Detroit win, and it's hard to argue with that assessment. When you pair the worst offense in the NHL against a goaltender who has been one of the best in hockey over the last three and a half months, the recipe is pretty clear. Detroit should control the tempo, get the early goal, and make Calgary chase the game all night. And when the Flames are chasing, they're even more vulnerable to giving up odd-man rushes because they simply don't have the talent to play from behind with any consistency.

Game 2
ESPN

Bruins @ Devils

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Prudential Center, Newark, NJ
Puck Line
NJD -1.5 (-245)
Moneyline
NJD -125 / BOS +105
Total
O/U 5.5

This is one of the most consequential games on the Monday slate, and ESPN made the right call putting it on national television. Boston (37-23-6, 80 points) travels to Newark to face a New Jersey team (33-31-2, 68 points) that desperately needs every point it can get if it wants to claw its way into the playoff picture. The Bruins finally snapped a brutal 7-game road losing streak on March 14 with a 3-2 shootout victory over the Capitals, and the question now is whether that win was a genuine turning point or just a one-night reprieve from the misery of playing away from TD Garden.

New Jersey has been excellent on this homestand, going 4-2-0 and getting monster performances from Nico Hischier, who exploded for 2 goals and 2 assists against the Kings on March 14. The Devils are 14-7-2 in one-goal games this season, which is the hallmark of a team that knows how to compete when things get tight in the third period. That kind of clutch performance in close games doesn't happen by accident. It comes from having a veteran core that stays composed under pressure and a goaltending tandem that can make the big save when it matters most. The Prudential Center crowd has been electric during this homestand, and the energy should be cranked up even higher with a playoff rival in town on national TV.

Boston's road woes are a legitimate concern heading into this matchup. Seven consecutive road losses before the Washington win is a staggering slide for a franchise that prides itself on being tough to play against in every building. The Bruins are built to grind, to play structured hockey that wears opponents down, but that identity has completely evaporated on the road. Whether it's fatigue, lack of focus, or just the natural regression that comes with an aging core, Boston has been a different team away from home. The +105 moneyline gives you plus money on a team with 80 points, which tells you the market doesn't trust the Bruins to perform in hostile territory.

The 5.5 total feels about right for a game that should be tightly contested. Both teams play responsible defensive hockey when they're at their best, and neither squad is going to open things up and turn this into a track meet. Expect a chess match where special teams could be the deciding factor. New Jersey's power play has been clicking during the homestand, and if Boston takes undisciplined penalties on the road, the Devils have the skill to make them pay. This has the feel of a 3-2 or 4-3 game where the outcome hangs in the balance until the final minutes, and in that scenario, New Jersey's 14-7-2 record in one-goal games gives them a real edge.

Game 3
ESPN+

Kings @ Rangers

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Puck Line
LAK -1.5 (+195)
Moneyline
LAK -123 / NYR +103
Total
O/U 6.5

Madison Square Garden hosts what should be a fascinating cross-conference clash, but it's the visiting Kings who come in as slight favorites, and that tells you everything you need to know about where these two franchises stand right now. Los Angeles (27-23-15, 69 points) has been maddeningly inconsistent all season, but they still bring more overall talent and structure to the ice than a Rangers team (24-29-8, 56 points) that has been one of the biggest disappointments in the NHL. New York's season has been a slow-motion train wreck, and the fact that they're underdogs at home in the World's Most Famous Arena speaks volumes about how far this team has fallen.

The Rangers' offensive struggles have been the defining story of their season. New York is tied with Calgary for the fewest high-danger goals in the entire league at just 41, which is a staggering indictment of a team that was supposed to have dynamic offensive talent. When you can't generate quality scoring chances, you're relying entirely on your goaltender to bail you out, and that's not a sustainable way to win hockey games in the modern NHL. The Rangers have been among the worst offensive teams in the league for months now, and there's been no sign of a turnaround. The skill is theoretically there, but the execution and the chemistry simply aren't clicking.

LA brings a more balanced attack and a road-warrior mentality that has kept them in the Pacific Division hunt despite the inconsistency. The Kings' 15 overtime and shootout losses are the most telling stat about their season. This is a team that competes in almost every game but can't find the finishing touch when it matters most. That said, playing at MSG against a struggling opponent feels like a game where LA's veterans should be able to control the pace and dictate terms. Anze Kopitar continues to be the heartbeat of this franchise, and his leadership in big moments is exactly what you need in a building like Madison Square Garden.

The 6.5 total is the highest number on the board tonight, which is a bit surprising given the Rangers' offensive woes. But this game profiles as one where defensive breakdowns from both sides could lead to a high-scoring affair. New York's defensive structure has been leaky all year, and when the Kings get transition opportunities, they have the speed and skill to capitalize. Meanwhile, LA's defense isn't exactly airtight either, and even a struggling Rangers offense can find goals when the opposition gives them room to operate. The LAK -1.5 puck line at +195 suggests the market doesn't expect LA to run away with this, and in a game at MSG, anything can happen. But the talent gap favors the visitors.

ESPN+

Utah @ Stars

Monday, 8:00 PM ET | American Airlines Center, Dallas, TX
Puck Line
DAL -1.5 (+170)
Moneyline
DAL -159 / UTA +134
Total
O/U 5.5

Dallas has been an absolute machine in the Central Division this season, and hosting Utah on a Monday night at the American Airlines Center should be another opportunity for the Stars (42-14-10, 94 points) to flex their muscle. Dallas sits second in the Central and is one of the best teams in the entire Western Conference, built on a foundation of elite defensive structure, balanced scoring, and goaltending that can steal games when the offense isn't humming. The Stars play the kind of suffocating, detail-oriented hockey that makes opponents feel like they're skating through quicksand, and Utah is going to need to bring their absolute best effort to stay in this one.

Utah (34-27-6, 74 points) is having a solid but unspectacular first full season, sitting in a wild card position and trying to hold on as the stretch run heats up. The Mammoth have shown they can compete with good teams, but there's a significant gap between being competitive and being dominant, and Dallas falls firmly into the dominant category. These two teams have combined for 6.6 goals per game in their season series, which suggests that the meetings between them have been more wide open than you'd typically expect from a Stars game. Utah's offense has enough talent to put pucks behind the Dallas goaltender, but the question is whether they can do it consistently enough to overcome the Stars' relentless attack.

The -159 moneyline for Dallas tells you this isn't a slam dunk, and the market respects Utah as a team that won't go quietly. The Mammoth have playoff aspirations of their own, and every game against a Western Conference contender is a chance to prove they belong in the postseason conversation. But there's a 20-point gap in the standings between these two teams, and that gap exists for a reason. Dallas is deeper, more experienced, and plays with the confidence of a team that knows it's going to be playing meaningful hockey well into May. Utah is still figuring out what it takes to be that kind of team.

The 5.5 total is standard for a Dallas home game, but given the 6.6 combined GPG in the season series, there could be more offense in this one than the number suggests. The Stars' power play has been a weapon all year, and if Utah gives them opportunities with the man advantage, Dallas has the skill to make them pay. On the flip side, Utah's speed in transition can create problems for any defense, and they've shown they won't be intimidated by the big-market atmosphere at AAC. This is one of those games where the final score could go either way in terms of total goals, but the Stars' ability to control possession and limit quality chances should ultimately be the difference.

Game 5
ESPN

Penguins @ Avalanche

Monday, 9:30 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Puck Line
COL -1.5 (-130)
Moneyline
COL -229 / PIT +188
Total
O/U 6.5

Here it is, the nightcap on ESPN, and it features the best team in hockey hosting a Pittsburgh squad that has been spiraling in the wrong direction. Colorado (44-12-9, 97 points) isn't just good this season. They're historically good. The Avalanche have scored 221 goals, the most in the NHL, while allowing just 146, the fewest in the league. That's an absurd combination. When you lead the league in both goals scored and fewest goals allowed, you're not just playing hockey, you're playing a different sport than everyone else. The Avs have been the most complete team in the NHL from wire to wire, and there's no sign of them slowing down as the regular season winds toward the playoffs.

Pittsburgh (33-18-15, 81 points) has a respectable point total, but the recent trajectory is alarming. The Penguins have dropped 5 of their last 7 games, and when you're heading into Ball Arena riding that kind of losing skid, the last thing you want to see is the league's most dominant team waiting for you on the other side of the ice. Pittsburgh's 15 overtime and shootout losses tell you this is a team that hangs around in games but can't close the deal, and that pattern of coming up short in tight games becomes a mental burden as the season wears on. The Penguins are still in a playoff position, but the cushion is shrinking, and games like this one against Colorado can accelerate a slide if things go sideways early.

Mackenzie Blackwood has been outstanding between the pipes for Colorado, posting an 18-7-1 record with a 2.42 GAA, .908 save percentage, and 3 shutouts. Having a goaltender who can consistently give you a chance to win is the foundation of any championship team, and Blackwood has provided exactly that stability while the Avalanche's high-powered offense does its thing at the other end of the ice. The 221 goals scored aren't just a product of one line going crazy. Colorado attacks in waves, rolling four lines that can all create offense and pressuring opponents into mistakes with relentless forechecking and transition speed. It's the kind of system that grinds you down over 60 minutes.

The COL -1.5 puck line at -130 is priced aggressively, and the market clearly expects the Avalanche to win this game comfortably. The 6.5 total reflects the offensive firepower on display, particularly from the home side, and this has the feel of a game where Colorado puts up four or five goals without breaking a sweat. Pittsburgh will need to play a near-perfect road game to have any chance in this building, and given the 5 losses in their last 7, that kind of performance feels like a tall order. The Avs are playing for the Presidents' Trophy and home-ice advantage throughout the playoffs, and they're not going to take their foot off the gas in mid-March. Expect Colorado to come out firing and put this one away before the third period even begins.
