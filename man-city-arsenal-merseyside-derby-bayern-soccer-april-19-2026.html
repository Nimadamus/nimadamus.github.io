Everton vs Liverpool

Sunday, 9:00 AM ET | Hill Dickinson Stadium, Liverpool

The Merseyside Derby in the first season at Everton's Hill Dickinson Stadium. David Moyes' Everton side arrives carrying the form line of the year in the bottom half of the Premier League, unbeaten in four of their last five including a 4W-1D run that has reshaped their European-place conversation. Liverpool arrive in the opposite trajectory, with Arne Slot's side coming off a 0-2 home defeat to PSG in the Champions League that knocked them out of the competition and a run of form that reads 1W-4L in their last five. Odds reflect the trajectory collision: Liverpool -140 on the moneyline, draw +275, Everton +238. Under 2.5 goals at +100, both teams to score 7/10.

Everton's recent progress has been built on defensive discipline and set-piece threat. Jarrad Branthwaite has been one of the most consistent young center-backs in the league across the second half of the year. James Tarkowski's aerial dominance has been the structural piece Moyes has leaned on in every moment of pressure. The attack leans on Iliman Ndiaye's wide work and Beto's hold-up play in transition. Liverpool's issues have centered on rotation fatigue from the Champions League run and the loss of midfield intensity that plagued the latter stretch of the season. Mohamed Salah remains the offensive centerpiece, and his ability to produce in the biggest moments is still the key differential.

The Asian handicap of Everton +0.25 at 1.84 is the directional value on the slate if you believe Everton's home form continues to hold. The draw at +275 is a live position for handicappers who expect a grind-it-out, low-event match typical of recent derbies. Under 2.5 is the cleanest totals direction given Everton's defensive solidity and Liverpool's fatigue. Kickoff 9:00 AM ET on USA Network. Both teams need points for different reasons: Liverpool to hang on to top-four, Everton to keep the European dream alive for another week.

NBC

Man City vs Arsenal

Sunday, 11:30 AM ET | Etihad Stadium, Manchester

The Premier League title race distilled into 90 minutes at the Etihad. Arsenal sit top of the table on 70 points. Manchester City are second on 64. The gap is six with six rounds remaining, which means an Arsenal loss cuts the margin to three and reopens the title race entirely, while a draw keeps the Gunners comfortably in the driver's seat. Odds reflect the home-ground advantage: Manchester City -117 moneyline, draw +270, Arsenal +390. Total under 2.5 at -122, both teams to score 17/20. This is Mikel Arteta's biggest fixture of the season and Pep Guardiola's path to a comeback title swing.

Arsenal's attacking production has dipped across the last five matches. Bukayo Saka's workload has accumulated to the point that his final-third decision-making has become inconsistent, and Gabriel Jesus' injury absence has forced Kai Havertz into a more permanent center-forward role. Declan Rice's deep-lying midfield work continues to be the stabilizer, and William Saliba plus Gabriel Magalhaes remain one of the best center-back pairings in the league. The question for Arteta is whether his team can absorb sustained City possession for 70-plus minutes and still convert on transition chances.

Manchester City enter with their most important home fixture in two seasons. Rodri remains the central midfield anchor, and his ability to control tempo in build-up situations against Arsenal's press is the key tactical variable. Erling Haaland's goal-scoring form has been steady rather than exceptional, but his penalty-box threat against Arsenal's backline has been as disruptive as any striker in the league. Phil Foden's creative work on the left and Kevin De Bruyne's continued excellence in late-clock situations give City the kind of chance-creation depth that Arsenal's midfield has to account for across the full match.

The under 2.5 goals at -122 is the directional totals lean. Premier League title-decider matches between these two clubs have historically played tight, and both managers will accept a conservative 1-0 or 1-1 outcome over a 3-2 loss. The moneyline market suggests City as the marginal favorite with a legitimate 43-percent implied probability of winning, which is the highest that number has been for a City-Arsenal fixture in two years. The draw at +270 is a live position if you believe the tactical conservatism plays out. Kickoff 11:30 AM ET on NBC. This is the title race moment for both clubs.

Bayern Munich vs VfB Stuttgart

Sunday, 11:30 AM ET | Allianz Arena, Munich

Bundesliga Matchday 30. Bayern Munich (76 points, 29 played) host third-place VfB Stuttgart (56 points) at the Allianz Arena in a fixture that has title-ceremony atmosphere on one side and Champions League qualification stakes on the other. Bayern open at -250 moneyline, with Stuttgart as a heavy underdog. Bayern's recent form has been imperious: four straight home wins including a 5-0 over St. Pauli in the last round, a 4-3 Champions League win at Real Madrid in midweek that further cemented their continental credentials. Harry Kane's goal-scoring form continues to be elite, and his ability to produce in the biggest fixtures is why his Munich move has worked as cleanly as it has.

Stuttgart under Sebastian Hoeness has been one of the Bundesliga's best stories across the last three seasons. Their 4-0 win over Hamburg in the last round extended their push into third place. The pace and counter-attacking profile led by Deniz Undav and Jamie Leweling has produced some of the best transitional football in Germany. The problem for Stuttgart is that Bayern at home after a Champions League high against Real Madrid is the most difficult fixture in the league, and Hoeness will likely take a cautious approach early before seeing what Bayern's game-state looks like at 60 minutes.

Bayern's path to a three-goal cover is through Kane continuing his Bundesliga-leading goal-scoring form and Jamal Musiala producing a goal or assist. Stuttgart's path to keeping the match competitive is Undav creating at least three high-quality chances and Alexander Nubel making saves on the underlying expected-goal profile. The most likely outcome is a 2-0 or 3-1 Bayern win with one Stuttgart goal coming from a transition or set-piece. Kickoff 11:30 AM ET on ESPN+. Bayern's title procession continues.

Aston Villa vs Sunderland

Sunday, 9:00 AM ET | Villa Park, Birmingham

Aston Villa host newly-promoted Sunderland at Villa Park in a Premier League fixture that carries more weight than the table positions suggest. Villa are fifth-place and pushing for a Champions League return under Unai Emery. Sunderland are 6th or 7th depending on week and have emerged as the surprise story of the Premier League season after their promotion. Villa open at -165 moneyline (implied 59 percent win probability), draw at +275, Sunderland as a +350 dog. Villa arrive off a 7-1 aggregate demolition of Bologna in the Europa League quarterfinal that booked their semifinal date against fellow English side Nottingham Forest.

Emery's rotation strategy is the central variable. Villa face Forest in the Europa League semifinal on April 30, and the manager will likely protect certain legs in this fixture. Ollie Watkins remains the central striker, with Morgan Rogers behind him providing the creative attacking-midfield profile that has been one of the Premier League's most productive this year. Leon Bailey on the right flank gives Villa the wide-attacking dimension that stretches opposing defensive lines. Sunderland under Regis Le Bris have been unbeaten in 4 of their last 5 and have produced 7 points from their last 3 away matches.

Sunderland's unlikely push into the top six has been driven by the excellent front-three play of Wilson Isidor, Patrick Roberts, and the creative work of Habib Diarra in midfield. The Black Cats will come to Birmingham looking for the kind of away point that continues the European-place pursuit. The likely outcome is a Villa win by one or two goals, but Sunderland's over-under dog value is the directional bet for handicappers who believe in the away form. Kickoff 9:00 AM ET on Paramount+. Villa manage rotation while chasing both Europe and Europa League silverware.

Nottingham Forest vs Burnley

Sunday, 9:00 AM ET | City Ground, Nottingham

Nottingham Forest host Burnley at the City Ground with Forest's Europa League semifinal against Aston Villa looming on April 30. Forest booked their semifinal spot with a 2-1 aggregate win over Porto, with Morgan Gibbs-White producing the decisive second-leg goal. Nuno Espirito Santo is going to manage his rotation carefully because the Europa League run is the most meaningful modern-era European campaign Forest has had, and the Burnley match is sandwiched between Premier League matchdays that matter and the two-leg semifinal.

Forest's season has been the kind of overachievement that reshapes a mid-sized club's identity. The defensive discipline led by Ola Aina and Murillo has been the structural foundation, and the attacking efficiency of Chris Wood and Gibbs-White has produced chances from every line-up combination Nuno has used. Burnley under Scott Parker have been in a relegation fight for most of the season, and the Clarets' ability to get a result at the City Ground depends on defensive rigor and set-piece opportunism against a Forest side that may be managing legs.

The moneyline favors Forest heavily given the City Ground home advantage. The directional play for handicappers is either the Forest moneyline at relatively short juice or the under 2.5 goals, which reflects the reality that Forest will likely play conservatively to preserve European legs. Burnley's clean-sheet and relegation-fight mentality also favors the under. Kickoff 9:00 AM ET on USA Network. Forest's manager chooses between the Premier League three points and the Europa League semifinal preparation.

Juventus vs Bologna

Sunday, 2:45 PM ET | Allianz Stadium, Turin

Serie A Matchday 33. Juventus host Bologna at the Allianz Stadium in a fixture with Champions League-qualification stakes for both clubs. Juventus sit fourth on 60 points from 32 games, clinging to a two-point lead over fifth. Bologna are eighth on 48 points and require wins to extend their European-place hopes into the final weeks. Juventus open at +130 moneyline, implied win probability around 43.5 percent, Bologna's Asian handicap +1.25 at -122. Juve are unbeaten in seven (5W-2D) across their last seven matches, and the head-to-head against Bologna has produced 19 matches without a Juve defeat with 5 of the last 6 ending in draws.

Juventus under Thiago Motta have finally produced the kind of possession-based attacking football the club targeted when Motta was hired. Dusan Vlahovic's form has stabilized, and Nicolo Savona's emergence at right-back has given Juve the wide-attacking dimension they lacked in prior seasons. Bologna under Vincenzo Italiano have continued the possession-plus-pressing profile that Thiago Motta established in the prior regime, and Santiago Castro's form up top has been one of Serie A's more consistent striker stories. Castro's aerial threat on set pieces is particularly difficult for Juve's back line.

The 1-1 correct-score at +700 is the value position on the slate given the 19-match unbeaten streak for Juve at home vs Bologna and the recent draw frequency. Bologna +1.25 Asian handicap is the directional lean if you believe Bologna competes but doesn't lose outright. Juventus' path to a three points is Vlahovic's goal plus a clean sheet, and Bologna's path to a result is Castro producing a goal and denying Juve in key chance-creation windows. Kickoff 2:45 PM ET on CBS Sports. Juve's Champions League push continues.

Hellas Verona vs AC Milan

Sunday, 9:00 AM ET | Stadio Marcantonio Bentegodi, Verona

Serie A Matchday 33. Hellas Verona (19th place, 18 points, relegation zone) host third-place AC Milan (63 points) at the Bentegodi in a fixture where the stakes are opposite for each side. Milan are -175 moneyline favorites at 1.57, draw at 3.90 (+290), Verona at 6.25 (+525). Milan cling to a five-point lead over fifth-place for the final Champions League qualification spot, while Verona need every point they can scrape to close the nine-point gap to safety. Milan have lost three of their last four, and Verona have lost four straight. Both trajectories point down.

Milan's recent struggles have centered on Rafael Leao's inconsistency in the final third and the midfield losing shape without Tijjani Reijnders' full fitness. Sergio Conceicao's substitutions have been questioned in the Italian media, and the collective chemistry has looked strained. Christian Pulisic's injury absence has removed the primary creative chance-creator from the right flank. Verona are dealing with their own injury crisis: Armel Bella-Kotchap is out, Lovric is out, and Serdar is out. Paolo Zanetti's rotation depth has been pushed to its breaking point across the last month.

Verona need a miracle to avoid relegation, and Milan need results to stay in the top four. The moneyline suggests Milan as the sensible favorite, but the team's recent form makes +525 on Verona a live position for handicappers who believe in home-ground momentum swings in relegation-fight fixtures. The under 2.5 goals is the directional total lean because both teams are struggling to produce chances with consistency. Kickoff 9:00 AM ET on Paramount+. This is the kind of match where both teams walk off frustrated at a 1-1 result.
