NHL Stanley Cup Playoffs - First Round

Playoffs Tip Off: Three Game 1s to Kick Off the Cup

The Stanley Cup chase is officially underway. Three first-round Game 1s on Saturday: Senators at Hurricanes at 3 PM ET, Wild at Stars at 5:30, and the Battle of Pennsylvania in prime time at 8 when the Flyers visit the Penguins at PPG Paints Arena. All three games air on ESPN. New goalie duels, classic rivalries, and the road to the Cup starts tonight.

Senators @ Hurricanes

Saturday, 3:00 PM ET | Lenovo Center, Raleigh, NC

The Carolina Hurricanes open their 2026 Cup chase at home against the Ottawa Senators in Game 1 of a first-round series that has more intrigue than the seeding suggests. Carolina opens at -148 on the moneyline with the Senators at +124. Puck line Carolina -1.5 (+180). Total 5.5 goals with the over at -110 and the under at -110 in most books. This is the Hurricanes' 8th straight playoff appearance under head coach Rod Brind'Amour, and their structural identity remains the hardest-forechecking, highest-pressuring team in the league. Carolina finished top-five in Corsi For percentage and is one of the few rosters that can dictate five-on-five zone time for 40-plus minutes a game.

Ottawa arrived as a legitimate Cup contender rather than a feel-good Cinderella. The Sens have been building for five years, and the core of Tim Stutzle, Brady Tkachuk, and Jake Sanderson has finally crossed into its prime. The big variable for Ottawa in Game 1 is Linus Ullmark in net. He's been 5-1 across his last six starts with a .927 save percentage, and he's the kind of veteran goalie who thrives in high-pressure Game 1 atmospheres on the road. Opposite him, Frederik Andersen carries a career .914 postseason save percentage for the Canes and has made the kinds of breakaway saves that Cup runs get built around. The save-percentage advantage is narrowly Ottawa, but the volume advantage is heavily Carolina.

Carolina's path to a Game 1 cover is through their forecheck and their ability to create high-danger chances off zone-entry pressure. Sebastian Aho leads a first line that has been one of the most productive forward trios in the league. Seth Jarvis has emerged as a 40-goal scorer who adds bite to the second line. The back end features Jaccob Slavin and Brent Burns, and Slavin specifically is going to draw Tkachuk for most of his shifts. Ottawa's first line is Tkachuk-Stutzle-Drake Batherson, and they've combined for over 230 points this year. If that line doesn't produce in Game 1, the Sens are going to struggle to match Carolina's structural depth.

The 5.5 total sits in the sweet spot for a playoff Game 1, where both teams traditionally tighten defensively but where the pressure system Carolina plays can produce high-event hockey. Ottawa's path to an outright upset is Ullmark at .950, Tkachuk's line producing two-plus goals, and a power-play conversion rate above 20%. The ice is Carolina's, the crowd is Carolina's, and the first goal of the 2026 Stanley Cup Playoffs is likely to come in this game. Opening faceoff is 3:00 PM ET on ESPN. Game 2 is Monday in Raleigh.

Wild @ Stars

Saturday, 5:30 PM ET | American Airlines Center, Dallas, TX

The Dallas Stars open their Cup chase against the Minnesota Wild in a series that has the potential to turn into one of the most competitive first-round matchups in the bracket. Dallas is favored at -120 on the moneyline with Minnesota at +100, total 5.5 with the over at -130 and the under at +106. Puck line Minnesota +1.5 is the chalk side. The Stars have been one of the Western Conference's most consistent teams under head coach Pete DeBoer, and Jake Oettinger's goaltending has been the central reason Dallas has reached the postseason in every year of his tenure. Oettinger enters his 11th career playoff series as the starter, which is a ridiculous resume for a 26-year-old goalie.

The storyline on the other side is Jesper Wallstedt making his NHL postseason debut in net for Minnesota. Filip Gustavsson has been benched after a late-season slide that shook the Wild's confidence in the veteran, and John Hynes is going to the 23-year-old Swedish prospect as his Game 1 starter. Wallstedt has a .917 save percentage this year in limited NHL action, but the leap to a playoff Game 1 against Dallas' elite offensive group is significant. If Wallstedt can stand on his head for Game 1, the Wild have a real chance to steal the opener in a crowd environment that is going to be loud.

Minnesota's offense is Kirill Kaprizov at the center and everything else orbiting him. Kaprizov has been the most reliable pace-of-play driver Minnesota has, and Mats Zuccarello, Joel Eriksson Ek, and Matt Boldy give the Wild a legitimate four-up-front attack against most opponents. The question is whether their defensive pairs can hold up against the Dallas top-six. Jason Robertson and Roope Hintz are one of the best even-strength duos in the league, and Mikko Rantanen has been a playoff-point-per-game contributor going back years. If Dallas gets typical production from their top line, it's a hard road for the Wild no matter what Wallstedt gives them.

The 5.5 total reflects the reality that both teams play responsible defensive hockey and both goalies are elite in their respective ways. Playoff Game 1 totals tend to clear the under because rotations tighten and coaches prioritize structure over ice time. Minnesota needs Kaprizov to produce two-plus points to have a chance in Game 1, Wallstedt to match Oettinger save for save, and the Wild's penalty kill to limit Dallas to zero power-play goals. The Stars should win outright at home, but the Minnesota +1.5 puck line is a live position if you believe in Wallstedt's Game 1 poise. Opening faceoff is 5:30 PM ET on ESPN.

Flyers @ Penguins

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | PPG Paints Arena, Pittsburgh, PA

The Battle of Pennsylvania in prime time. The Pittsburgh Penguins host the Philadelphia Flyers in the first playoff meeting between these two franchises in over a decade. Pittsburgh opens at -145 on the moneyline with Philadelphia at +122. Total 6.0 in most books. This series has "Original Six energy" even though neither team is one, because the blood feud between these two organizations is real, loud, and goes back generations. Sidney Crosby at 38 years old and Evgeni Malkin at 39 are headlining their franchise's most meaningful playoff series since 2018. Danny Briere's Flyers rebuild has finally produced a roster that crossed over into legitimate contender territory.

The goalie storyline is the most important piece of the series. Stuart Skinner, acquired from Edmonton in the December 12, 2025 trade that sent Tristan Jarry the other way, has been steady for Pittsburgh since his arrival and gives the Penguins a starter they trust in high-leverage games. Opposite him, Dan Vladar has been the best goalie Philadelphia has had in five years. He posted a career-best 2.42 goals-against average and .906 save percentage this season while playing the bulk of the starts. The Flyers' playoff hopes rest on Vladar producing .925-plus save percentage in this series, because Philadelphia's offense is going to need every goal it can scrape together against a Pittsburgh team that can still score in bunches.

Pittsburgh's offensive depth is better than it's been in years. Crosby remains a point-per-game anchor. Malkin's power-play work is still one of the league's most dangerous assets. Kris Letang is healthy and has been one of the Penguins' most productive defenseman. The supporting cast features younger legs that have finally complemented the core rather than just filling space around it. Philadelphia counters with Travis Konecny's sniping, Sean Couturier's two-way work, and a second line built around Matvei Michkov that has been one of the best surprises in the league. Michkov's first career playoff series is going to be something to watch.

The 6.0 total is higher than most Game 1 numbers because these two rosters score. Both teams have top-10 power plays. Both teams have elite shot volume. The history of the rivalry includes 6-5 games, 7-4 games, and overtimes that were decided at 4-3. The one scenario that produces an under here is Vladar standing on his head and the Flyers grinding out a 2-1 win. Every other realistic outcome clears 6. Pittsburgh at home with Crosby's experience and Skinner's recent form is the cleanest Game 1 path. Whether that holds for a full seven-game series is the question that defines this bracket. Opening faceoff is 8:00 PM ET on ESPN. Game 2 is Monday at PPG Paints Arena.
