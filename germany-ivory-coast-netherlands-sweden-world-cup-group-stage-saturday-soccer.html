Germany Meets Ivory Coast For Group E Control, And The Netherlands Chase Sweden In Group F

A four-match Saturday at the 2026 World Cup is headlined by Germany and Ivory Coast meeting with both sides on three points for control of Group E, the Netherlands chasing group leaders Sweden in Group F, Ecuador and Curacao both needing a result, and Tunisia facing Japan. Germany vs Ivory Coast

Saturday, 4:00 PM ET | BMO Field, Toronto | Group E

The headline match of the Saturday slate is a Group E meeting at BMO Field in Toronto, where Germany and Ivory Coast arrive level on three points after both won their openers. Germany opened with an emphatic 7-1 win over Curacao, while Ivory Coast edged Ecuador 1-0 on a late goal from Amad Diallo. With both sides already holding maximum points, the winner here takes firm control of the group and moves to the brink of a knockout berth, which gives this match the feel of an early decider.

Germany carry the deeper pool of attacking talent, with the creativity of Florian Wirtz and Jamal Musiala at the heart of their build-up and a forward line that shared the goals in a dominant opening performance. Their challenge is the structure they will face: Ivory Coast are organized, physical, and disciplined defensively, exactly the profile that can frustrate a possession-heavy side. The betting market has priced Germany as clear favorites around -200 with the draw near +350 and Ivory Coast in the +450 range, a reflection of the gap in attacking quality even against a well-drilled opponent.

Ivory Coast will lean on the kind of compact, counterattacking approach that delivered three points against Ecuador, with Amad Diallo a constant threat in transition. Their path is to stay tight, weather Germany's early pressure, and strike on the break or from a set piece. With the total set around 2.5 goals, the market expects an open game, but Ivory Coast's organization and the high stakes of a match between two three-point sides could just as easily tighten it into a tense, low-scoring affair where one moment decides the group.

Group F Showdown
FOX

Netherlands vs Sweden

Saturday, 1:00 PM ET | Houston | Group F

A heavyweight Group F clash in Houston sends the Netherlands in against a Sweden side that leads the group after a dominant start. Sweden opened with a 5-1 demolition of Tunisia to sit top on goal difference, while the Netherlands were held to a 2-2 draw by Japan and have just one point to show for their opener. That result has put the Dutch under early pressure, and a win here would lift them level with or above Sweden in a tightly contested group.

The Netherlands carry the pedigree and the defensive anchor in Virgil van Dijk, who scored in the draw with Japan and remains central to how they control matches. Their issue in the opener was failing to close out a lead, and against a Sweden side brimming with confidence and firepower, the Dutch will need to be sharper in both boxes. The market has installed the Netherlands as moneyline favorites around -135, with the draw near +280 and Sweden in the +360 range, reflecting Dutch quality balanced against Sweden's early momentum.

Sweden's threat is obvious and dangerous, built around the attacking talent of Alexander Isak and Viktor Gyokeres, the pair that tore through Tunisia. They arrive with belief and the freedom that comes from already banking three points and a healthy goal difference. For the Netherlands, the task is to impose control in midfield, protect against Sweden's pace in transition, and finally turn territorial dominance into the goals that escaped them against Japan. This is the match that will likely shape the entire complexion of Group F.

Must-Win Pressure
FS1

Ecuador vs Curacao

Saturday, 8:00 PM ET | Kansas City | Group E

A Group E meeting in Kansas City carries the weight of early elimination for both sides, as Ecuador and Curacao arrive without a point after losing their openers. Ecuador fell 1-0 to Ivory Coast in a tight contest, while Curacao were overwhelmed 7-1 by Germany. Both teams sit on zero points with negative goal differences, which turns this into something close to a knockout match this early in the tournament: the loser faces near-certain elimination, while the winner stays alive.

Ecuador enter as heavy favorites, with the market pricing them around -800 and a total set near 3.5 goals, a reflection of the talent gap between an established South American side and a Curacao team making history simply by reaching the tournament. Ecuador's defensive solidity was evident even in defeat to Ivory Coast, and the priority here is to turn that organization into a result by finally converting their chances. The pressure of a must-win match against a lesser-ranked opponent can weigh on a favorite, and Ecuador will want to settle the game early.

Curacao, the smallest nation ever to qualify for a World Cup, will take heart from the occasion itself even after a chastening loss to Germany. Their realistic path is to defend deeply, stay compact, and look to frustrate an Ecuador side that will dominate possession. For a team with little to lose and everything to gain, a disciplined, low-block performance is the formula that keeps them in the contest. Against an Ecuador team carrying the burden of expectation, Curacao's best hope is to make the game uncomfortable and pounce on any opening.

Group F Battle
Telemundo

Tunisia vs Japan

Saturday, 12:00 AM ET | Monterrey, Mexico | Group F

The late-night kickoff from the Monterrey area in Mexico, technically rolling into the early hours of Eastern time, sends Tunisia in against Japan in a Group F match with real stakes for both. Japan opened with an encouraging 2-2 draw against the Netherlands to bank a point, while Tunisia were beaten 5-1 by Sweden and sit on zero. For Tunisia, this is close to a must-win; for Japan, a victory would push them firmly toward a knockout berth and into a strong position in the group.

Japan arrive with momentum and a clear identity, built on quick passing, intelligent movement, and a fearlessness that served them well in holding the Netherlands. Having already taken a point off one of the tournament favorites, the Samurai Blue carry belief into a match they will be expected to control. Their pressing and tempo can overwhelm opponents who sit back, and against a Tunisia side reeling from a heavy defeat, Japan will look to dictate from the opening whistle.

Tunisia must respond after a difficult opener, and their approach will likely center on defensive discipline and a more compact shape to avoid the spaces Sweden exploited. The North African side has the organization and tournament experience to make life difficult when they are focused, and with their tournament hopes on the line, motivation will not be an issue. Billed as a milestone fixture in World Cup history, this match pits Japan's momentum and quality against Tunisia's need to dig in and salvage their campaign.
