4-Game Monday Slate

NHL Previews Today

A compact but consequential 4-game Monday slate with massive playoff implications on both ends. Tampa Bay's 102-point juggernaut visits a Buffalo team that just clinched its first postseason berth since 2011. Nashville and Los Angeles meet deadlocked at 81 points in a winner-takes-all wild card showdown. The Western Conference playoff picture could look dramatically different by midnight.

Game 1 - Marquee
NHL Network

Lightning @ Sabres

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | KeyBank Center, Buffalo, NY
Puck Line
TB -1.5 (+215)
Moneyline
TB -115 / BUF -105
Total
O/U 6.5

This is the kind of game that deserves the national television spotlight. Tampa Bay rolls into Buffalo with 102 points and 273 goals scored, the second-highest total in the entire NHL, and they're going to face a building that hasn't seen postseason hockey in 15 years. The Sabres clinched a playoff spot for the first time since 2011, and if you don't think that building is going to be absolutely electric tonight, you haven't been paying attention to what this franchise has endured. The drought is over. KeyBank Center is going to be rocking, and Tampa has to walk into that atmosphere and try to play their game against a team riding an emotional tidal wave.

The goaltending matchup here is fascinating and it heavily favors the visitors. Andrei Vasilevskiy gets the start for Tampa Bay, and his .917 save percentage tells you everything about why this team is one of the most dangerous in the league heading into the postseason. Big Cat has been there, done that, won multiple Cups, and he's the kind of backstop who elevates his game when the lights get brighter. Buffalo counters with Alex Lyon and his .903 save percentage, which is a significant step down from what the Sabres would want in a game of this magnitude. Lyon has been serviceable, but there's a world of difference between serviceable and elite, and that gap could be the deciding factor tonight.

Tampa's power play has been humming at 21.74% this season, and Buffalo's penalty kill will need to be disciplined to avoid handing the Lightning free opportunities. The Sabres aren't slouches on the man advantage themselves at 20.78%, but the concern for Buffalo is that Tampa is simply a more complete team from top to bottom. The Lightning have the offensive firepower to put up crooked numbers on any given night, and their 273 goals scored means they're averaging well north of 3.5 per game. When you combine that kind of offensive output with Vasilevskiy's steadiness, you've got a team that doesn't need to steal games because they can just overwhelm you with talent.

The 6.5 total feels about right for a game featuring two teams that can both score in bunches. Tampa's offensive prowess is well documented, and Buffalo's crowd-fueled energy could translate into an up-tempo, emotionally charged affair where both teams are pressing. The -115/-105 moneyline essentially makes this a coin flip with a slight lean toward Tampa, and that feels accurate. The Lightning are the better team on paper, but the Sabres have home ice, a sold-out building hungry for playoff hockey, and the kind of adrenaline that only comes from ending a decade-and-a-half of misery. This is going to be a phenomenal hockey game regardless of which way it falls.

Game 2
ESPN+

Kraken @ Jets

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | Canada Life Centre, Winnipeg, MB
Puck Line
WPG -1.5 (+150)
Moneyline
SEA +140 / WPG -166
Total
O/U 5.5

Two teams watching the playoff picture slip away from them, and at this point you'd have to imagine both fanbases are running out of patience. Seattle limps into Winnipeg on a brutal 2-6-2 run in their last 10 games, sitting at 75 points and essentially needing to win out just to have a prayer. The Kraken's season has been a study in missed opportunities, and their 72.77% penalty kill, which ranks dead last in the NHL at 31st, tells you everything about why they can't close games. When you're giving up goals on the man disadvantage at that rate, it doesn't matter how well you play at even strength because you're handing teams free offense every single night.

Winnipeg isn't in much better shape at 78 points, though they at least have the luxury of playing at home where Canada Life Centre can provide a spark. The Jets are averaging just 2.80 goals per game, which ranks 25th in the league and paints the picture of an offense that has struggled to generate consistent scoring threats all season. It's hard to make a deep playoff run when you can't score, and it's even harder to get into the playoffs in the first place. The one bright spot for Winnipeg is their defensive structure, allowing 3.00 goals against per game, which ranks 10th in the NHL. They're not leaky, they just can't finish.

The 5.5 total reflects two offenses that aren't exactly setting the world on fire. Seattle's inability to stay out of the penalty box could be the X-factor here, because even though Winnipeg's power play hasn't been elite, any team is going to convert at a reasonable clip when you're handing them opportunity after opportunity. The Kraken are going to need to play an incredibly disciplined, tight-checking game to have any shot in this one, and based on their recent form, that feels like a big ask. Seattle's road woes have been well-documented, and traveling to Winnipeg in early April to play a desperate team in front of a hostile crowd is about as tough an assignment as it gets.

Winnipeg enters as a -166 moneyline favorite, and while that's not an overwhelming price, it reflects the reality that the Jets simply need this game more and have the home ice advantage to back it up. Neither of these teams inspires a ton of confidence right now, and it's entirely possible that both are watching the playoffs from the couch in two weeks. But in a one-game snapshot, Winnipeg's defensive structure and home environment should be enough to handle a Kraken team that has been in freefall. The real question is whether 78 points will be enough to get in, and based on what Nashville, LA, and San Jose are doing, the Jets may need to run the table regardless of tonight's result.

Game 3
ESPN+

Blackhawks @ Sharks

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | SAP Center, San Jose, CA
Puck Line
SJ -1.5 (+155)
Moneyline
CHI +142 / SJ -170
Total
O/U 6.5

San Jose is playing some of the most inspired hockey of any team in the league right now, and it's happening at the exact right time. The Sharks have gone 4-1 in their last 5 games and they're sitting at 79 points, right on the doorstep of the Western Conference wild card picture with 10 days left in the regular season. This is a franchise that has been in rebuild mode, and the fact that they're even in the playoff conversation is a testament to how well this group has come together down the stretch. Chicago, meanwhile, has lost 5 of their last 6, sits at 70 points, and is essentially playing out the string with nothing meaningful left to play for.

The home ice dominance in this series has been staggering. San Jose is 8-4 in their last 12 home meetings against Chicago, and when you factor in the current trajectories of both teams, that number makes even more sense tonight. The Sharks are fighting for their playoff lives with every ounce of energy they have, while the Blackhawks are limping toward the finish line of a disappointing season. Motivation matters enormously in the NHL, especially in April, and there's a massive gap between a team that believes it can make the postseason and a team that's already thinking about the offseason draft lottery.

The 6.5 total is interesting given the contrasting styles. San Jose's offense has been clicking in recent weeks, and the Blackhawks' 1-5 run in their last 6 games suggests a team that has stopped defending with any real urgency. When a club checks out mentally, the goals allowed tend to pile up in bunches, and Chicago's young roster doesn't have the veteran presence to maintain defensive structure when the stakes stop mattering. That's a recipe for a game that could get out of hand quickly if San Jose jumps out to an early lead, which they've been very good at doing during this hot stretch.

San Jose at -170 feels like the right price for a team playing must-win hockey at home against a lifeless opponent. The Sharks can't afford to drop games to teams like Chicago when they're staring at Nashville (81 pts), LA (81 pts), and Winnipeg (78 pts) in the rearview mirror. Every single point matters, and the urgency factor alone should give San Jose a significant edge tonight. Chicago will have their moments because they have talented young players who compete regardless of standings, but talent without urgency rarely beats a desperate team on home ice in a playoff race. The Sharks should handle business here.

Game 4
ESPN+

Predators @ Kings

Monday, 10:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Puck Line
LA -1.5 (+180)
Moneyline
NSH +115 / LA -138
Total
O/U 5.5

This is as close to a playoff game as you'll find in the regular season. Nashville and Los Angeles are both sitting at exactly 81 points, deadlocked in the Western Conference wild card race with just 10 days remaining before the postseason begins on April 18. There is no margin for error for either team tonight. The loser doesn't just drop a game, they drop behind in a race where every single point could be the difference between playing in the playoffs and watching from home. And to make this even more compelling, these two teams just played four days ago on April 2, with Nashville winning that one. The Kings know what it feels like to lose to the Predators. They don't want to feel it again.

Los Angeles has set an NHL record this season with 31 overtime appearances, picking up 19 of those as overtime losses. That's both incredible and incredibly frustrating for Kings fans, because it means this team has been in a ridiculous number of tight games and has found a way to lose a staggering amount of them in extra time. On one hand, you can argue the OTL points have kept them in the race, banking loser points game after game. On the other hand, this is a team that clearly struggles to close out games, and that inability to finish is the reason they're fighting for a wild card spot instead of comfortably sitting in the bracket. In a game where the result matters this much, the Kings' track record in tight situations has to give you pause.

Nashville's confidence has to be sky-high heading back to LA after beating the Kings just days ago. There's a psychological edge that comes from having just taken a game from your direct competitor for a playoff spot, and 96% of spread bets landing on Nashville +1.5 tells you that the betting public sees it the same way. The Predators don't need to be the better team for 60 minutes, they just need to keep it close, and the public is banking on their ability to do exactly that. Nashville's road game has been respectable, and their ability to execute in high-pressure moments has been better than LA's overtime-plagued track record would suggest.

The 5.5 total is set low because both of these teams understand what's at stake. In games with this kind of intensity, you tend to see tighter defensive play, fewer odd-man rushes, and goalies standing on their heads because their seasons literally depend on it. Don't expect a wide-open track meet. This is going to be a grind, a physical, emotional, 60-minute battle where every shift matters and every shot could be the difference between playoff hockey and a long summer. The Kings have the home ice advantage, but Nashville has the momentum and the recent head-to-head victory. This is the best game on the Monday slate and it's not particularly close.
