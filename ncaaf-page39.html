Rice @ Texas State

Friday, January 2, 2026 | 1:00 PM ET | Amon G. Carter Stadium | ESPN
Line: TXST -17.5 | O/U: 55.5 | RICE: 5-7 | TXST: 6-6

What happens when mediocre meets mediocre and only one team gets to sleep in their own beds?

Texas State's home field advantage tells a story here – that 8% jump from 42% to 50% win rate might seem modest, but it's pure gold when you're dealing with two teams scraping for bowl eligibility. The Bobcats have been grinding out ugly wins at home while Rice has been that classic road underdog that looks decent on paper but wilts under pressure away from Houston.

Here's the pattern everyone's missing: 17.5 points is massive for two teams separated by one win, but home cooking changes everything in college football's final weeks. Rice's defense has been Swiss cheese on the road – they're giving up 31+ per game away from home while Texas State's been methodically wearing down opponents in front of their crowd.

The total screams under with both offenses sputtering, but that spread? Texas State covers this comfortably. Sometimes the books just hand you a gift wrapped in obvious tape.

**Take: Texas State -17.5**

Navy @ Cincinnati

Friday, January 2, 2026 | 4:30 PM ET | Simmons Bank Liberty Stadium | ESPN
Line: NAVY -7.5 | O/U: 54.5 | NAVY: 10-2 | CIN: 7-5

Navy's 83% road record screams danger for bettors – that's the number that matters most here.

This is a classic trap brewing in Memphis. Navy rolls in riding high after their dominant season, but Cincinnati's getting disrespected at home despite being bowl-eligible. The Bearcats have had extra time to prepare for Navy's triple-option attack, which historically gives teams fits on short notice but becomes manageable with proper preparation.

Here's the situational wrinkle: Navy's motivation is purely pride-based now. They're already locked into their bowl destination, while Cincinnati needs this performance to build momentum heading into their postseason. The Bearcats' 58% home record isn't spectacular, but they've been competitive in familiar surroundings.

The market's falling in love with Navy's road dominance, but that 83% win rate is exactly why this line feels inflated. Cincinnati gets the benefit of preparation time against a gimmicky offense, plus they're playing with house money as dogs at home.

**Take the points with Cincinnati +7.5.** The trap is real.

Arizona @ SMU

Friday, January 2, 2026 | 8:00 PM ET | Snapdragon Stadium | FOX
Line: ARIZ -1.5 | O/U: 52.5 | ARIZ: 9-3 | SMU: 8-4

Look, 1.5 is about as neutral as it gets - barely off the key number but not sitting on anything that screams value either way. The vig's normal, so books aren't sweating this one.

Here's what jumps out: Arizona's been the better team all year, but they're walking into a buzzsaw. SMU's playing with house money in their first playoff appearance ever, at a neutral site that might as well be home turf in Southern California. That 75% road win rate for Arizona looks impressive until you realize they haven't faced this kind of stage pressure.

SMU's offense can move the ball, and their defense has been scrappy enough to keep them in games. Arizona's got more talent on paper, but college football playoffs are about who shows up mentally ready to fight.

The total at 52.5 feels right - both teams can score but also tighten up when it matters.

Give me SMU +1.5. They're desperate, they're loose, and sometimes that beats pure talent.

Wake Forest @ Mississippi State

Friday, January 2, 2026 | 8:00 PM ET | Bank of America Stadium | ESPN
Line: MSST -3 | O/U: 53.5 | WAKE: 8-4 | MSST: 5-7

**STRONGEST TAKE: The home cooking angle is completely dead here.**

Mississippi State gets zero benefit playing at Bank of America Stadium in Charlotte - this is a neutral site bowl game, not Davis Wade Stadium in Starkville. The line makers are hanging -3 on the Bulldogs purely based on conference perception, not any actual home advantage.

Wake Forest has been the road warriors all season at 67% away from Winston-Salem, while Mississippi State has been pathetic at home (42%). The Demon Deacons thrive in hostile environments and bowl atmospheres where they can play spoiler. State's season has been a disaster under Jeff Lebby's first year, and their motivation is questionable after limping to 5-7 just to reach bowl eligibility.

Wake's experience in these neutral site games gives them the edge. They've got nothing to lose and everything to prove against an SEC opponent that's been getting by on reputation alone. The venue doesn't favor either side, but momentum and desperation favor the underdog.

**LEAN: Wake Forest +3** - grab the points with the hungrier team.
