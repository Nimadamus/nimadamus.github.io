Utah @ Rangers

2026-01-06T00:00Z | Madison Square Garden | MSG, ESPN+
Line: NYR -175 | O/U: 6.0 | UTAH: 18-19-5 | NYR: 25-14-3

Utah makes their inaugural trip to the World's Most Famous Arena, and the Rangers are treating this like a playoff game. New York's home dominance has been absurd - they're 15-5-2 at MSG and Igor Shesterkin has been a brick wall between the pipes.

The Mammoth have been road warriors themselves, going 9-10-3 away from Salt Lake. That's respectable for a first-year franchise finding its footing. Clayton Keller has been their offensive engine, but he'll face the toughest defensive assignment of his career against Adam Fox and company.

The market's pricing this correctly. Rangers are the clear favorite, but -175 on the moneyline is rich. The puck line at -1.5 feels like a trap because Utah plays tight defensive games and keeps things close. Under 6 is where the value lives - both teams trend toward low-scoring affairs.

**LEAN: Under 6.0** - Shesterkin shuts the door, Utah limits damage.

Ducks @ Capitals

2026-01-06T00:00Z | Capital One Arena | MNMT, ESPN+
Line: WSH -185 | O/U: 6.5 | ANA: 21-18-3 | WSH: 29-10-3

Washington's been the surprise of the NHL this season. At 29-10-3, the Capitals are legitimate Stanley Cup contenders, and Alex Ovechkin's chase for Gretzky's goal record has the entire hockey world watching. They're a different beast at home - dominant, physical, and relentless.

Anaheim's rebuild is ahead of schedule with some exciting young talent, but they're not ready to compete with the league's elite on the road. The Ducks are 8-11-2 away from Honda Center, and that record gets uglier against top competition.

The juice at -185 is steep, but Washington's earned it. They're covering spreads at an absurd rate at home. The puck line at -1.5 is the better play - Caps win by two or more against inferior opponents with alarming consistency.

**Take Washington -1.5 (+120)** - Better value than the bloated moneyline.

Red Wings @ Senators

2026-01-06T00:30Z | Canadian Tire Centre | Prime Video
Line: OTT -140 | O/U: 6.5 | DET: 18-20-4 | OTT: 22-17-3

Prime Video gets another Original Six rivalry for their Monday Night Hockey showcase. Detroit's been maddeningly inconsistent this season - they'll beat a contender one night and lose to a bottom-feeder the next. That's the life of a young, developing team.

Ottawa's been the more stable operation. The Senators are firmly in the playoff picture with Brady Tkachuk playing MVP-caliber hockey and Tim Stutzle emerging as one of the game's elite playmakers. Their home record is solid at 12-7-1.

The key matchup is in goal. Detroit's inconsistent netminding has cost them games all season, while Linus Ullmark has been steady for Ottawa. That's the difference-maker in a tight divisional game.

**LEAN: Ottawa -140** - Better goaltending and home-ice advantage swing this one.

Kraken @ Flames

2026-01-06T02:30Z | Scotiabank Saddledome | Sportsnet, ESPN+
Line: CGY -130 | O/U: 6.0 | SEA: 19-21-3 | CGY: 20-17-6

Two Pacific Division teams fighting for playoff positioning meet in Calgary. The Flames have been better at the Saddledome this season, and their defensive structure under Ryan Huska has made them tough to beat at home.

Seattle's road struggles continue. The Kraken are just 8-12-2 away from Climate Pledge Arena, and they've particularly struggled against Western Conference opponents. Matty Beniers has been their lone offensive bright spot, but he can't do it alone.

Calgary's goaltending tandem of Dustin Wolf and Dan Vladar has stabilized after early-season concerns. Wolf in particular has been excellent at home, and he's the likely starter against a Seattle offense that can't score.

**Under 6.0 is the play** - Both teams grind games down in the third period.

Wild @ Kings

2026-01-06T03:30Z | Crypto.com Arena | FDSNW, ESPN+
Line: LA -145 | O/U: 5.5 | MIN: 24-13-5 | LA: 24-13-5

Identical records, different styles. Minnesota grinds you down with suffocating defense and elite goaltending from Filip Gustavsson. LA overwhelms you with offensive firepower and the league's most dangerous power play. Something's got to give in this late-night clash.

The Wild have been road warriors all season, posting a 12-7-3 mark away from the Xcel Energy Center. They're not fazed by hostile environments, and Crypto.com Arena won't intimidate a team with legitimate Stanley Cup aspirations.

Los Angeles loves this matchup stylistically. They've got the talent to break down Minnesota's defensive shell, and Anze Kopitar's line has been eating teams alive at home. The Kings are 14-6-2 at their rink this season.

This is a coin flip game, which is why the total is the play. Under 5.5 at even money is a gift. Two defensively responsible teams that don't allow many second chances. Gustavsson vs. the Kings' goalie is a battle of stingy netminders.

**LEAN: Under 5.5** - Defensive chess match ends 2-1 or 3-2.
