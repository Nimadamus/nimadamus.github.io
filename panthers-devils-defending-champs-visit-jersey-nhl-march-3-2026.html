11-Game Tuesday Slate

NHL March 3, 2026

Tuesday delivers a monster 11-game NHL slate packed with marquee matchups. Pittsburgh visits Boston without Sidney Crosby, who's on IR after an Olympic injury. Buffalo hosts Vegas in a clash of 75- and 70-point squads. Dallas carries an absurd franchise-record 9-game winning streak into Calgary. Tampa Bay and Minnesota, both sitting on 80 points atop their respective conferences, collide on TNT. And Colorado, the best team in hockey at 89 points, visits a surging Anaheim squad riding a 5-game heater.

Penguins @ Bruins

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Puck Line
BOS -1.5 (+195)
Moneyline
PIT +100 / BOS -118
Total
O/U 6.5

This game has a completely different complexion than it would have had two weeks ago. Sidney Crosby is on injured reserve with a lower-body injury suffered at the Olympics, and the timeline is roughly four weeks before he returns. That's a gut punch for a Pittsburgh team that's been legitimately excellent this season at 31-15-13 with 75 points. But here's the thing: even without Crosby, the Penguins have kept rolling. Pittsburgh is 16-3-4 over its last 23 games, and they just blanked Vegas 5-0 in their most recent outing. This isn't a team that's going to collapse the moment their captain goes down. They've built enough depth and identity to survive this stretch.

Boston at 33-21-5 with 71 points is in decent shape but hasn't exactly been dominant. The Bruins have their own injury concerns, with Patrice Bergeron sidelined by a fractured foot. Jeremy Swayman is expected to start in net, and his play will be critical in a game where both teams are missing key pieces. On the positive side for Boston, Hampus Lindholm and Pavel Zacha have both returned from their respective injuries, which adds some badly needed depth to a Bruins lineup that was running thin for a while. TD Garden should be rocking for this Original Six clash, and that home-ice energy matters in spots like this.

What makes this game fascinating from a betting perspective is the moneyline. Pittsburgh at +100 is essentially being treated as a coin flip on the road despite missing the best player on their roster. That tells you how much respect the market has for what the Penguins have built this season beyond Crosby. This team's system, their depth scoring, and their defensive structure have all been strong enough to withstand the loss of their franchise centerpiece, at least in the short term. The 16-3-4 run over their last 23 is not a fluke; it's the product of a well-coached, well-constructed team playing complete hockey.

The BOS -1.5 at +195 is a big number that reflects the tightness of this matchup. Boston is favored at -118, but only slightly, and the puck line payout tells you that a multi-goal Bruins win is far from guaranteed. The 6.5 total is on the higher side, which makes sense for two teams that can both generate offense and have had some defensive inconsistencies without their full lineups available. This is a classic "pick your poison" game where both sides have legitimate cases, and the near-even moneyline confirms it. TD Garden will be hostile, but Pittsburgh has proven on the road all season that hostile buildings don't rattle them.

Golden Knights @ Sabres

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | KeyBank Center, Buffalo, NY
Puck Line
BUF -1.5 (+165)
Moneyline
VGK +122 / BUF -145
Total
O/U 6

Buffalo at 35-19-6 with 76 points is having the kind of season that Sabres fans have been waiting over a decade to see, and KeyBank Center is going to be electric for this one. This is a team that has legitimately arrived. Tage Thompson has been an absolute force with 32 goals and 63 points, and when he's rolling at this level, Buffalo's offense is one of the most dangerous in the Eastern Conference. The Sabres are 9-3 in their last 12 games and just demolished Tampa Bay 6-2, which tells you this isn't a team riding a lucky streak. They're playing genuinely dominant hockey right now.

Vegas at 28-18-14 with 70 points has been a frustrating team to watch this season. Those 14 overtime losses are a staggering number that tells a story of a team that consistently plays close games but can't finish them off. The Golden Knights are dealing with their own injury issues: Mark Stone is day-to-day with an upper-body concern, while William Karlsson, Brendan Rondbjerg, and Brett Howden are all out. Jack Eichel has been brilliant with 68 points, and the addition of Mitch Marner has given Vegas another high-end playmaker, but the depth has been tested to its limits with this many bodies missing from the lineup.

There's a fascinating subplot with Eichel returning to Buffalo. He spent parts of five seasons with the Sabres before the messy divorce that sent him to Vegas, and KeyBank Center always brings extra juice when Eichel is on the visiting roster. The Buffalo fans have complicated feelings about their former franchise player, and that emotional energy tends to translate into an even louder, more intense atmosphere than usual. For the current Sabres squad, this is a chance to show the old guard what the future looks like, and they've got the record and the roster to back it up.

The BUF -145 moneyline reflects Buffalo's clear home-ice advantage and their superior form coming into this game. The puck line at BUF -1.5 (+165) is an interesting option given that the Sabres have been winning by comfortable margins during this 9-3 stretch, with the 6-2 demolition of Tampa being the latest example. The 6 total is interesting, sitting slightly below the 6.5 you see in most games, which suggests the market thinks this could be a slightly tighter affair despite Buffalo's recent offensive explosion. Vegas has the talent to keep it close if their stars show up, but they're walking into a building that's been a fortress for one of the league's hottest teams.

This is about as close to a true pick'em as you'll find on tonight's board. Florida at -110 and New Jersey at -109 are essentially even money, and the market is telling you that neither team has a meaningful edge in this one. Both squads are fighting for their lives in the Eastern Conference, and both are dealing with significant absences that have altered the trajectory of their seasons. This is a TNT game that might not have the star power of the Tampa-Minnesota matchup later, but the stakes and the desperation factor make it must-watch hockey.

Florida at 30-27-3 with 63 points is a far cry from the back-to-back Stanley Cup champions that dominated the last two postseasons. Aleksander Barkov has been out all season after ACL/MCL surgery, and Seth Jones is also sidelined. Those are two cornerstone players, and losing them has transformed the Panthers from perennial favorites into a team fighting to stay relevant. Anton Lundell has returned, which helps, but the Panthers sit 12 points out of a wild card spot, and the math is getting increasingly grim. Every game from here on out is essentially an elimination game for Florida.

New Jersey at 29-29-2 with 60 points has had its own disaster of a season. Dougie Hamilton is done for the year after a pectoral injury, and Jonas Siegenthaler is also out for the season. Those defensive losses have been devastating, and the Devils' blue line has been a sieve for stretches of this campaign. The silver lining? New Jersey snapped a painful 5-game losing streak on Saturday, which at least stops the bleeding heading into this nationally televised matchup. The Devils have gone under in 7 of their last 7 games, which is a staggering trend that speaks to how tight and low-event their recent games have been.

The 5.5 total is the lowest on tonight's board, and it makes perfect sense given the context. Both teams are playing tight, desperate hockey with depleted rosters, and neither offense has been firing consistently enough to expect a high-scoring affair. New Jersey's 7-of-7 under trend is hard to ignore, and Florida's offense without Barkov is a shadow of what it was during their championship runs. This game has "2-1 grinder" written all over it, and the pick'em moneyline means the market sees it as a genuine toss-up where the team that makes fewer mistakes will walk away with two points.

Utah at 31-25-4 with 66 points currently holds a top wild card position in the Western Conference, and the Hockey Club has been one of the more intriguing stories of the 2025-26 season. In just their second year of existence after relocating from Arizona, Utah has built a competitive roster that's been in the playoff hunt all season. This is a team that plays with an edge and an identity, and they've earned the respect of the market, which has them as only slight underdogs at +105 despite playing on the road at Capital One Arena.

Washington at 31-24-7 with 69 points has been a solid but unspectacular team this season. The Capitals have dealt with some health concerns, as John Carlson has been nursing a lower-body issue and his status for tonight is uncertain. Alex Ovechkin had a maintenance day recently but is expected to be in the lineup, because of course he is. The man is still chasing Wayne Gretzky's all-time goals record, and nothing short of a catastrophic injury is keeping him off the ice. Logan Thompson has been confirmed as the starter in net, and his performance between the pipes will be a key factor in a game that's expected to have some offensive fireworks.

The 6.5 total is the highest number on tonight's 7 PM slate, and the market is clearly expecting an up-tempo game between two teams that can score. Both Utah and Washington have offenses that can put pucks in the net in bunches, and neither defense has been airtight enough this season to consistently hold opponents to low totals. Washington's home-ice advantage at Capital One Arena is the primary reason they're favored at -125, because on paper, these two teams are remarkably similar in terms of overall quality and results.

What's particularly interesting about this game is the playoff implications for both sides. Utah needs every point they can get to maintain their wild card position in the brutally competitive Western Conference, while Washington is trying to create some separation in the Metro Division race. Both teams have something tangible to play for, which should elevate the intensity and the stakes. The tight moneyline reflects how evenly matched these squads are, and the high total suggests this could be one of the more entertaining games on Tuesday's loaded board.

Columbus at 30-21-8 with 68 points continues to be one of the surprise contenders in the Eastern Conference, and Nationwide Arena has become a genuinely difficult place for visiting teams to play this season. The Blue Jackets are coming off an overtime win, which means they've got momentum and confidence heading into this matchup against a Nashville team that's been trending in the wrong direction. Zach Werenski has been the engine of this Columbus team with 65 points, and he's expected back in the lineup after dealing with an illness, which is a huge boost for a squad that relies heavily on his ability to generate offense from the blue line.

Nashville at 27-25-8 with 62 points is stuck in that frustrating middle ground where they're too good to tank but not quite good enough to feel comfortable about their playoff positioning. The Predators are on a 2-game losing streak, and while Steven Stamkos has been excellent with 30 goals on the season, he can't carry this team alone. Nashville's inconsistency has been the defining story of their season, and they've struggled particularly on the road, where they don't have the comfort of Bridgestone Arena's famously hostile atmosphere to lean on.

The CBJ -127 moneyline tells you the market views Columbus as a moderate home favorite, which tracks with their overall season performance and the fact that they have the better record of the two teams. Nashville at +108 offers a decent number for a team with Stamkos-level scoring talent, but the Predators' recent form and road woes make it hard to trust them in this spot. The 6.5 total suggests the market expects a game with some offense, and both teams have the firepower to deliver on that expectation.

The underlying story here is the playoff race. Columbus at 68 points is building a nice cushion in the Eastern Conference wild card picture, while Nashville at 62 points is looking up at teams and running out of runway to make up ground. That disparity in urgency could be a factor tonight. The Blue Jackets are playing to solidify their position, while the Predators are playing to stay alive. Sometimes that desperation fuels a team, and sometimes it leads to pressing and poor decisions. Werenski's return from illness gives Columbus their full complement of weapons, and at Nationwide Arena, that should be enough to maintain their home-ice edge.

Moneyline

Total
O/U 5.5

This is a game between two teams that have been grinding through difficult stretches, and neither one is going to inspire a lot of confidence from bettors right now. Chicago at 23-28-9 with 55 points is firmly in the lottery conversation, while Winnipeg at 23-26-10 with 56 points is barely a point better and has been dealing with their own frustrations all season. The Jets are favored at -159 largely because of home ice at Canada Life Centre, but the number isn't nearly as big as you'd expect for a home team against a sub-.500 opponent, which tells you the market doesn't have a ton of faith in either side.

The Blackhawks' season has been about development and finding silver linings, and Tyler Bertuzzi has been the biggest one. With 26 goals and 19 assists, Bertuzzi has been one of the few consistent offensive threats on a Chicago team that's often struggled to score. The Blackhawks are 2-6-2 in their last 10, which is rough, but they just posted a 4-0 shutout over Utah in their most recent game, proving they can still put together a dominant performance when everything clicks. The question is whether that Utah win was a one-off or the start of something more consistent.

Winnipeg is banged up and struggling. Josh Morrissey is out with an upper-body injury, and Nino Niederreiter is sidelined for 4-6 weeks after surgery. Those are significant losses for a team that was already having trouble finding its footing. Mark Scheifele has been doing his part with 27 goals and 43 assists, but the supporting cast hasn't been able to provide enough consistent production to keep the Jets in the playoff picture. The under has hit in 5 of Winnipeg's last 7 games, and the head-to-head history between these two teams has gone under in 8 of the last 11 meetings.

The 5.5 total combined with those under trends makes this one of the more interesting totals on the board tonight. Both teams have struggled offensively at times, both are missing key contributors, and the recent history between these franchises has produced low-scoring affairs more often than not. The WPG -159 moneyline is the least exciting number you'll find on Tuesday's slate, but the under trend is genuinely compelling if you believe that two struggling offenses with depleted lineups will produce a tight, grinding game rather than an offensive showcase.

Stars @ Flames

Tuesday, 9:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Saddledome, Calgary, AB
Moneyline
DAL -123 / CGY +105
Total
O/U 5.5
Road Fav
DAL -123

Stop and appreciate what Dallas is doing right now. The Stars are on a franchise-record 9-game winning streak. Nine straight wins. In the NHL, where parity makes it nearly impossible to string together even five or six wins in a row, Dallas has won nine consecutive games and they've done it while outscoring opponents 37-22 during the run. That's not squeaking by with one-goal wins. That's domination. The Stars just beat Vancouver 6-1 on Monday night, and they're riding into Calgary with the kind of confidence and momentum that makes them one of the most dangerous teams in hockey right now.

Here's what makes the streak even more impressive: Dallas is doing this without Tyler Seguin, Roope Hintz, Mikko Rantanen, and Radek Faksa. That's an absurd amount of talent sitting on the shelf, and the Stars haven't just survived those losses, they've thrived. Miro Heiskanen has been on a tear with 9 assists in his last 6 games, and the depth throughout the Dallas lineup has stepped up in ways that most teams can only dream about. At 37-14-9 with 83 points, the Stars have the second-best record in the Western Conference, and they're doing it with a MASH unit roster. That's a testament to coaching, culture, and organizational depth that goes far beyond the names on the injury report.

Calgary at 24-28-7 with 55 points is having a tough season, and things haven't gotten any better recently with back-to-back losses, including a shootout defeat to the Ducks. The Flames don't have the firepower or the defensive structure to match what Dallas is bringing to the Saddledome tonight, and the gap between these two teams is evident in the standings. Calgary has been competitive at home at times this season, and the Saddledome crowd can create energy, but asking the Flames to be the team that snaps a franchise-record winning streak is a tall order for a roster that's been struggling to beat average opponents, let alone elite ones.

The DAL -123 moneyline is surprisingly modest for a team on a 9-game heater. You'd think the market might price Dallas higher given the streak and the record, but the road disadvantage and the fact that streaks are inherently fragile in the NHL keeps the number somewhat reasonable. The 5.5 total is standard for a game involving Dallas, which has been one of the better defensive teams in the league all season. The Stars don't need to win 6-1 every night; they're perfectly capable of winning 3-1 or 2-0 with their defensive structure. But given the offensive avalanche they've produced during this 37-22 run, there's clearly more firepower available when they need it.

Here's a game with massive playoff implications, and the fact that both teams are sitting on identical 66-point totals makes it feel like a playoff game already. Ottawa at 29-22-8 and Edmonton at 29-24-8 are mirror images of each other in the standings, and the winner of this one gains a critical edge in the wild card race. Both teams know exactly what's at stake, and the intensity at Rogers Place should reflect the desperation that comes with being this close to the postseason bubble with the stretch run approaching.

Edmonton's offensive firepower is terrifying when it's clicking, and right now, it's clicking at an elite level. Leon Draisaitl has been on an absolute heater with 18 points in his last 10 games, including 6 goals and 12 assists. Evan Bouchard has added 21 points in his last 10 from the blue line, giving Edmonton a two-pronged offensive attack that's nearly impossible to contain. And then there's the power play: at 32.3%, the Oilers have the most dangerous man-advantage unit in the entire NHL. When Edmonton gets a power play, it's not a matter of if they'll score, it's a matter of when.

Ottawa's concern in this matchup is their penalty kill, which has been sitting at a woeful 73.1%. That's a massive vulnerability against the best power play in the league. If the Senators take more than two or three penalties tonight, they're going to be in serious trouble, because Edmonton will cash in on those opportunities. Tim Stutzle has been solid with 12 points in his last 10 games, but he'll need to be even better if Ottawa is going to steal two points on the road in Edmonton.

The 6.5 total is the joint-highest on tonight's slate, and it's well-earned given the offensive talent on both sides and Ottawa's leaky penalty kill. Edmonton at -125 is a moderate home favorite, and the number suggests the market respects Rogers Place as a difficult venue for visiting teams but doesn't see a massive gap between these two clubs. The OTT +115 price is juicy enough to attract some attention, but asking Ottawa's 73.1% penalty kill to survive against a 32.3% power play on the road is asking a lot. This game could easily come down to special teams, and Edmonton has a decisive advantage in that department.

This is the headliner of the entire Tuesday slate, and TNT has this one in prime time for a reason. Tampa Bay at 38-16-4 with 80 points and Minnesota at 35-16-10 with 80 points are deadlocked at the top of their respective conferences, and both teams come in having dropped their last two games. That's an unusual situation, two elite teams both looking to snap mini-slumps against each other on national television, and the urgency from both sides should produce an incredible hockey game. The Xcel Energy Center is going to be absolutely electric for this one.

Tampa Bay's firepower is absurd. Nikita Kucherov leads the way with a staggering 95 points on the season, with 31 goals and 64 assists. Let that number breathe for a second. Ninety-five points with a full month still to play. Kucherov has been the most productive player in hockey this season, and his ability to create offense from anywhere on the ice makes Tampa Bay's attack virtually impossible to contain consistently. Brayden Point has chipped in 28 goals, and Andrei Vasilevskiy has been his usual dominant self in net at 28-8-3 with a .917 save percentage. This is a team that's built to win in any environment.

Minnesota's calling card has been Kirill Kaprizov, who has 34 goals and remains one of the most electrifying players in the NHL. The Wild have built a complete team around Kaprizov's brilliance, with solid goaltending from Filip Gustavsson at a 10-6 record and .910 save percentage. The one concern for Minnesota is that Marcus Foligno is on injured reserve, which removes some of that physical, grinding element from the bottom of the lineup. The Wild's identity is built on being tough to play against, and losing Foligno's sandpaper hurts that identity even if the stats don't fully capture his impact.

The TB -125 moneyline makes the Lightning slight road favorites, which tells you the market trusts Tampa Bay's overall body of work slightly more than Minnesota's despite the identical point totals. The puck line at TB -1.5 (+190) is a big number that reflects how tight this game is expected to be. The 6 total is reasonable for two teams that can both score and both defend, and the TNT cameras should be treated to a back-and-forth affair between two legitimate Stanley Cup contenders. Both teams have dropped their last two, so something has to give, and the loser falls behind in what's shaping up to be a fascinating conference race.

Colorado at 40-10-9 with 89 points is the best team in hockey, and it's not particularly close. Forty wins through 59 games is an astonishing pace, and the Avalanche are tracking toward one of the great regular seasons in modern NHL history. Nathan MacKinnon has been the engine of this juggernaut with 91 points on 41 goals and 51 assists. Yes, 41 goals. MacKinnon leads the entire NHL in goals scored, and his ability to take over games single-handedly makes Colorado the scariest team in the league every single night they take the ice.

But here's where it gets interesting: Anaheim is not the pushover the standings might suggest. The Ducks at 33-23-3 with 69 points are on a scorching 5-game winning streak and are averaging 4.7 goals per game during that run. Leo Carlsson has been a revelation with 20 goals and 28 assists, and Anaheim's home record of 20-8-1 is one of the best in the entire NHL. The Honda Center has become a fortress this season, and the Ducks already proved they can hang with Colorado when they won the previous meeting between these teams 2-1 in a shootout. This is not going to be an easy road game for the Avalanche.

The COL -149 moneyline is lower than you might expect for the league's best team, and that's a direct reflection of how well Anaheim has been playing at home. When a team goes 20-8-1 in their own building and is riding a 5-game heater with nearly 5 goals per game, the market adjusts. The Ducks aren't going to be intimidated by Colorado's record or reputation, and they've already shown they can beat this team. The 6.5 total is the highest number on the late-night portion of the slate, and with both offenses humming right now, there's a real possibility this turns into a track meet.

The contrast in narratives makes this game fascinating. Colorado is the establishment powerhouse, methodical and dominant, grinding through the regular season with the kind of consistency that makes them the clear Presidents' Trophy favorite. Anaheim is the upstart, the team nobody expected to be relevant, riding confidence and home-ice energy into a matchup against the best team in the world. MacKinnon's 41 goals against Anaheim's 4.7 goals per game during the streak is an unstoppable force meeting an immovable object, and late-night hockey fans are going to be treated to a genuinely compelling game at the Honda Center.
