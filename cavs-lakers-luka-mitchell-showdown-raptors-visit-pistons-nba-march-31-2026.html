7-Game Tuesday Slate

NBA March 31, 2026

Tuesday's seven-game slate is headlined by a Cavaliers-Lakers showdown on Peacock featuring Donovan Mitchell against Luka Doncic in a battle of two of the league's most electric scorers. Brandon Ingram and the surging Raptors visit the league-best 54-20 Pistons, also on Peacock. The Knicks head to Houston to face Kevin Durant and the Rockets. Cooper Flagg and the rebuilding Mavericks visit the struggling Bucks. The Suns travel to Orlando, the Hornets face the tanking Nets, and the Trail Blazers visit the Clippers.

Game 1

Suns @ Magic

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | Kia Center, Orlando, FL
Spread
ORL -2.5
Moneyline
PHX +114 / ORL -135
Total
O/U 223.5

Phoenix at 42-33 is fighting tooth and nail for playoff positioning in a packed Western Conference, and this road trip to Orlando is the kind of game that separates contenders from pretenders. Devin Booker continues to be one of the most lethal scorers in the NBA, and his ability to create his own shot in clutch moments gives the Suns a baseline of competitiveness in every game they play. Phoenix's offense has been humming when healthy, and the Suns' perimeter shooting can stretch any defense to its breaking point. But playing on the road against a young, hungry Orlando team that's protecting home court with real urgency adds a layer of difficulty that Booker and the Suns can't afford to take lightly.

Orlando at 39-35 has been one of the most pleasant surprises in the Eastern Conference this season, and Paolo Banchero has emerged as the kind of two-way force that can anchor a playoff team. Banchero's combination of size, skill, and competitive fire makes him a matchup nightmare for opposing power forwards, and the Magic's supporting cast has provided just enough around him to keep Orlando in the postseason conversation. The Kia Center has been a tough place to play this season, and Orlando's defensive identity at home gives them a real edge against teams that rely heavily on half-court offense to generate points.

The 2.5-point spread suggests this is essentially a coin flip with home-court advantage tipping the scales toward Orlando, and that feels about right for two teams at similar stages of development. Phoenix has more star power with Booker, but Orlando's depth and defensive cohesion at home have been enough to neutralize more talented rosters throughout the season. The 223.5 total hints at a game where defense dictates the pace, and both teams grind through possessions rather than lighting up the scoreboard in a track meet.

This is a fascinating clash of styles and timelines. Phoenix is in win-now mode with Booker at the center of everything, while Orlando is building something sustainable around Banchero and a young core that's improving rapidly. The Magic's home-court advantage and defensive toughness make them a justifiable favorite, but Booker's ability to take over a game at any moment keeps the Suns dangerous regardless of the venue. Expect a physical, competitive contest that comes down to execution in the fourth quarter.

Game 2

Hornets @ Nets

Tuesday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center, Brooklyn, NY
Spread
CHA -15.5
Moneyline
CHA -1200 / BKN +750
Total
O/U 218.5

A 15.5-point spread in the NBA is borderline absurd, and it tells you everything you need to know about the state of the Brooklyn Nets. At 18-57, the Nets aren't just bad, they're historically bad, and this season has been a painful exercise in enduring losses while waiting for draft lottery ping pong balls to determine the franchise's future. Brooklyn's roster is a collection of end-of-bench players and G-League call-ups at this point, and there's simply no matchup on the floor where the Nets can compete with a competent NBA team. The Barclays Center has gone from one of the league's premier destinations to a place where opposing teams come to pad their stats and build confidence.

Charlotte at 39-36 has been one of the feel-good stories of the NBA season, and LaMelo Ball's health has been the single biggest factor in the Hornets' resurgence. When Ball is on the floor and feeling it, he's one of the most electrifying players in basketball, with the kind of passing vision and shot-making ability that can break down any defense. The Hornets have found a winning formula around Ball's playmaking, and their young core has grown into a group that plays with genuine confidence and cohesion. Charlotte is fighting for playoff seeding, and every win matters as the regular season enters its final stretch.

The 15.5-point spread is massive, but Brooklyn has done nothing to suggest they can keep games competitive against teams that are playing for something meaningful. The Nets rank near the bottom of the league in virtually every statistical category that matters, and their defensive effort on most nights has been underwhelming to put it charitably. Charlotte's pace and transition offense should exploit Brooklyn's lack of defensive intensity, and Ball's ability to push the tempo and find open shooters in transition could make this ugly early. The 218.5 total is relatively low, reflecting the Nets' inability to score enough to push games into higher-scoring territory even when they're getting blown out.

There's always a risk in laying double-digit points in the NBA because the three-point line is the great equalizer, but the talent gap in this game is so significant that the Hornets should be able to control proceedings from start to finish. Charlotte's starters should handle their business in the first three quarters, and the only question is whether the bench unit can maintain the margin when the regulars sit. Brooklyn's season has been a long, painful rebuilding exercise, and Tuesday night at Barclays figures to be more of the same.

Peacock

Raptors @ Pistons

Tuesday, 7:30 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Spread
DET -2.5
Moneyline
TOR +120 / DET -142
Total
O/U 219.5

The Pistons at 54-20 have been the story of the NBA this season, and Little Caesars Arena has become one of the toughest venues in basketball. Detroit's rise from perennial lottery team to the best record in the league is the kind of transformation that doesn't happen often, and the Motor City faithful have embraced this team with an energy that rivals any arena in the country. The Pistons' success has been built on an elite defense, unselfish ball movement, and the kind of team-first mentality that coaches dream about but rarely achieve. With Cade Cunningham sidelined due to a collapsed lung, however, Detroit is navigating uncharted waters, and the roster's ability to maintain this level of play without their franchise cornerstone is the biggest storyline of the final weeks of the regular season.

Toronto at 42-32 has been quietly excellent this season, and Brandon Ingram's acquisition from New Orleans has been the catalyst for the Raptors' resurgence. Ingram's ability to create in the mid-range, operate as a secondary playmaker, and score in isolation situations gives Toronto an offensive weapon that most teams in the East can't match. The Raptors have built a balanced roster around Scottie Barnes and Ingram, and their defensive versatility and length make them a difficult matchup for anyone in a playoff series. This road trip to Detroit is a measuring-stick game for Toronto, and a win at Little Caesars Arena would send a statement to the rest of the conference.

The 2.5-point spread reflects Detroit's home-court advantage more than any perceived talent gap, especially with Cunningham unavailable. The Pistons have shown remarkable resilience in Cunningham's absence, but asking a team to sustain the best record in the NBA without its best player is a significant challenge. Toronto has the kind of roster depth and defensive versatility that can exploit the offensive void Cunningham leaves behind, and Ingram's shot creation becomes even more valuable against a Detroit team that may struggle to generate points in the half court without its primary playmaker.

This Peacock matchup should be a legitimate chess match between two of the East's best teams. Detroit's defensive identity doesn't change without Cunningham, and the Pistons' home crowd will provide the energy that makes Little Caesars Arena so daunting. But Toronto has the star power with Ingram and Barnes to take advantage of the offensive opportunities that Cunningham's absence creates. The 219.5 total suggests a grind-it-out affair where defense dominates, and both teams will need to earn every bucket in what should be one of the best games on Tuesday's slate.

Game 4

Knicks @ Rockets

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
NYK -1.5
Moneyline
NYK -125 / HOU +105
Total
O/U 218.5

This is one of the more intriguing matchups on Tuesday's board. The Knicks at 48-27 have established themselves as legitimate contenders in the Eastern Conference, and their physical, defense-first identity under Tom Thibodeau has made them one of the toughest outs in the league. Jalen Brunson continues to orchestrate the offense with the kind of poise and scoring efficiency that has elevated him into the conversation of the NBA's best point guards, and New York's depth and toughness have been evident throughout the season. The Knicks are road favorites in Houston, which speaks to the respect the market has for their overall consistency and talent level.

Houston at 45-29 has been one of the best stories in the Western Conference, and Kevin Durant's arrival from Phoenix has transformed the Rockets from an exciting young team into a genuine championship contender. Durant's scoring versatility, defensive presence, and veteran leadership have been exactly what this young Rockets core needed to take the next step, and his partnership with Jalen Green and Alperen Sengun has given Houston one of the most dynamic offensive attacks in the league. The Toyota Center has been rocking this season, and Houston's home-court advantage has been a significant factor in their strong record.

The Knicks being 1.5-point road favorites in Houston is a testament to how much the market respects New York's overall body of work this season. The Knicks' defense has been elite, and their ability to slow down high-powered offenses through physicality and scheme versatility is the kind of trait that translates well in the postseason. But Houston's offense with Durant as the focal point is capable of scoring on anyone, and the Rockets' young legs and transition game can punish teams that don't get back in transition. This has the feel of a playoff-intensity game between two teams that could very well meet in the Finals.

The 218.5 total suggests a defensive slugfest, and that's likely driven by the Knicks' identity more than anything else. Thibodeau teams grind you down, limit possessions, and make you work for every point, and Houston will need to match that intensity on the defensive end to keep this game close. Durant's ability to get a bucket whenever he wants gives Houston a trump card in crunch time, but Brunson has shown he can go toe-to-toe with anyone in late-game situations. This should be a physical, competitive, and thoroughly entertaining game from start to finish.

Cavaliers @ Lakers

Tuesday, 10:00 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Spread
LAL -1.5
Moneyline
CLE +110 / LAL -130
Total
O/U 237.5

Here it is, the game of the night. Donovan Mitchell brings his Cleveland Cavaliers into Crypto.com Arena to face Luka Doncic and the Los Angeles Lakers in a nationally televised Peacock showdown that has legitimate Finals preview potential. Mitchell has been absolutely sensational this season, carrying the Cavaliers to a 47-28 record with his combination of scoring explosion, clutch shot-making, and improved playmaking that has put him firmly in the MVP conversation. When Mitchell gets into one of his scoring zones, there are very few players in basketball who can stay in front of him, and his ability to hit contested threes, get to the rim through traffic, and convert at the free-throw line makes him one of the most complete offensive players in the sport.

On the other side, Luka Doncic has turned the Lakers into the most exciting team in the Western Conference since arriving in the blockbuster trade from Dallas. The Lakers at 48-26 are rolling, and Doncic's partnership with Anthony Davis has been the most devastating one-two punch in basketball. Doncic's court vision, scoring volume, and ability to control the pace of a game are generational talents, and having Davis as a lob threat, pick-and-roll partner, and defensive anchor gives Luka the kind of running mate he never had in Dallas. The Crypto.com Arena crowd has been electric this season, and Lakers games have taken on a playoff atmosphere since the Doncic acquisition transformed this franchise from a fringe contender into a legitimate title threat.

The 237.5 total is the highest on Tuesday's board by a significant margin, and for good reason. Mitchell and Doncic are two of the highest-usage, highest-volume scorers in the NBA, and when they share the floor in opposite jerseys, the result is typically a track meet where both teams trade haymakers for 48 minutes. Cleveland's defense has been solid this season, but containing Doncic when he's operating in his bag is a challenge that even the league's best defensive teams struggle with. Likewise, the Lakers' defense will have their hands full with Mitchell, who can get to his spots against any defensive scheme and convert at an elite clip.

This is the kind of game that reminds you why the NBA regular season matters. Two elite guards going head-to-head, both playing at an MVP-caliber level, both leading teams with genuine championship aspirations. The 1.5-point spread tells you this is essentially a pick'em game with a slight lean toward the home team, and that feels right. Doncic's familiarity with the Crypto.com Arena crowd and his chemistry with Davis give the Lakers a marginal home-court edge, but Mitchell has proven time and again that he elevates his game on the biggest stages. Tune into Peacock for this one, because it has all the ingredients for an instant classic.

Game 6

Mavericks @ Bucks

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee, WI
Spread
DAL -1.5
Moneyline
DAL -125 / MIL +105
Total
O/U 227.5

Two of the NBA's most disappointing teams meet in Milwaukee, and the primary attraction for basketball fans everywhere is Cooper Flagg. The presumptive number one overall pick in this summer's draft has been thrust into a starting role for the rebuilding Mavericks, and his performance since Dallas traded Luka Doncic to the Lakers has been nothing short of electric. Flagg brings a combination of size, skill, court vision, and competitive fire that has scouts comparing him to a young LeBron James, and his willingness to attack the biggest moments despite being a rookie speaks to the kind of mentality that franchise players are built from. Dallas at 24-51 isn't winning many games, but Flagg is making every single one of them worth watching.

Milwaukee's fall from grace has been one of the most stunning developments of the 2025-26 season. At 29-45, the Bucks are a shadow of the team that won the championship just a few years ago, and Giannis Antetokounmpo's frustration has been palpable throughout a season marred by injuries, roster turnover, and organizational dysfunction. Giannis is still Giannis, an unstoppable force when healthy and motivated, averaging dominant numbers on both ends of the floor. But the supporting cast has crumbled around him, and the Bucks' defense has been a liability that even Giannis's individual brilliance can't paper over. Fiserv Forum has been a somber place this season, a far cry from the electric atmosphere that defined Milwaukee's championship era.

Dallas being a 1.5-point road favorite in Milwaukee tells you everything about where these franchises are right now. The Bucks, with Giannis on the roster, should never be home underdogs to a 24-win team, but the market is telling you that Milwaukee's supporting cast is so depleted that even the Greek Freak's superhuman talents aren't enough to overcome the depth advantage that even a rebuilding Dallas team possesses. Flagg's energy, athleticism, and fearlessness give the Mavericks a spark that the Bucks' tired roster simply can't match, and Dallas's young legs should be a factor as the game wears on.

The 227.5 total is moderate, and it reflects two teams that are capable of scoring but also prone to extended droughts when their stars aren't carrying them. The Flagg vs. Giannis matchup is fascinating from a generational standpoint, the torch-passing narrative writes itself, and there's genuine intrigue in watching the NBA's present (Giannis) clash with its future (Flagg). Neither team is playing for anything meaningful in the standings, but individual pride and professional competitiveness should ensure that both superstars bring their best in what could be a sneaky-entertaining game.

Game 7

Trail Blazers @ Clippers

Tuesday, 10:30 PM ET | Intuit Dome, Inglewood, CA
Spread
LAC -4.5
Moneyline
POR +165 / LAC -195
Total
O/U 227.5

The late-night window brings a Western Conference matchup between two teams hovering around .500 with very different trajectories. Portland at 38-38 has been one of the NBA's most unpredictable teams this season, capable of beating anyone on a good night and losing to anyone on a bad one. The Trail Blazers' young core has shown flashes of real potential throughout the season, and their willingness to play at a fast pace and let it fly from three-point range makes them a dangerous opponent when the shots are falling. But consistency has been the issue all year, and Portland's road record hasn't been strong enough to inspire confidence as a 4.5-point underdog in Los Angeles.

The Clippers at 39-36 are fighting for their playoff lives in a stacked Western Conference, and every game from here on out carries enormous weight. Kawhi Leonard's health remains the variable that determines the Clippers' ceiling, and when he's available and engaged, LA has the defensive identity and half-court execution to compete with anyone in the conference. The Intuit Dome has given the Clippers a genuine home-court advantage for the first time in recent memory, and the fans have created an atmosphere that makes late-season games feel like playoff contests. LA needs this game badly, and the urgency should show in their effort and intensity from the opening tip.

The 4.5-point spread is reasonable given the Clippers' home-court advantage and their desperation factor in the playoff race. Portland doesn't have the same level of urgency, sitting exactly at .500 with a fuzzy postseason picture, and that motivational gap can matter in a game between two similarly talented teams. LA's defense, when locked in, is capable of stifling Portland's transition offense and forcing the Trail Blazers into the kind of half-court possessions that favor the Clippers' length and physicality. Leonard's ability to take over possessions on both ends of the floor gives LA a trump card that Portland simply doesn't have.

The 227.5 total suggests a moderately paced game, and that should favor the Clippers' preferred style. LA wants to slow things down, execute in the half court, and use their defensive versatility to create turnovers and transition opportunities. Portland would prefer to push the pace and create chaos, but on the road against a motivated Clippers team, getting into that up-tempo rhythm is easier said than done. This could end up being a grinder where the Clippers' defensive effort and Leonard's shot-making make the difference in the fourth quarter.
