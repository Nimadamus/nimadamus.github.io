Warriors @ Celtics

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Spread
BOS -12.5
Moneyline
GSW +440 / BOS -600
Total
O/U 215.5

The early ESPN window delivers one of the league's most storied rivalries, but the circumstances couldn't be more different from the Larry Bird vs Magic Johnson days. Boston at 45-23 is rolling as the first seed in the Atlantic Division, with Jaylen Brown shouldering the offensive load brilliantly in Jayson Tatum's extended absence. Brown's 28.4 points per game have been among the most impressive individual performances in the league this season, and he's proven that this Celtics team doesn't crumble without its co-star. Derrick White (17.5 PPG) and Payton Pritchard (16.7 PPG) have provided the kind of complementary scoring that keeps Boston's offense humming even without Tatum's 20.0 PPG contributions.

Golden State at 33-35 is a shell of what this franchise has been over the past decade, and losing Stephen Curry to a knee injury has essentially ended any remaining playoff hopes. Curry's 27.2 points per game this season reminded everyone that he's still one of the most electric scorers in basketball history, but he's been limited to just 39 games and is currently sidelined again. Jimmy Butler was supposed to provide a secondary star alongside Curry when he arrived in Golden State, but his torn ACL in February ended both his season and the Warriors' fading dreams. Brandin Podziemski (13.0 PPG) has been a bright spot for the future, but this is a team playing out the string.

The 12.5-point spread is massive for an ESPN game, but it's entirely justified when you look at the talent disparity. Boston's defense at TD Garden has been suffocating all season, and without Curry to punish them from deep, the Warriors don't have a player who can consistently create high-quality offense against an elite defense. The Celtics' home record has been outstanding, and they've been particularly ruthless against sub-.500 teams when the national spotlight is on. Brown tends to raise his game in these nationally televised matchups, and his ability to score from all three levels gives Boston an offensive floor that Golden State simply can't match right now.

The 215.5 total is the second-lowest on the board, reflecting the expectation that Golden State's offense will struggle mightily without its two best players. Boston's defensive efficiency at home has been among the best in the league, and they have the length and athleticism to disrupt every action the Warriors try to run. This should be a game where the Celtics build a double-digit lead by halftime and manage the clock in the second half, keeping the final score relatively contained. The entertainment value here comes from watching Brown operate as the undisputed alpha on a national stage, not from competitive tension.

Thunder @ Nets

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center, Brooklyn, NY
Spread
OKC -18.5
Moneyline
OKC -2400 / BKN +1200
Total
O/U 214.5

The reigning NBA champions roll into Brooklyn as 18.5-point road favorites, and honestly, that number might still be too low. Oklahoma City at 54-15 has been the most dominant team in basketball this season, and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's 31.6 points per game have cemented his status as the best player in the world right now. SGA combines an impossibly smooth mid-range game with elite finishing at the rim and a defensive intensity that makes him a nightmare for opposing guards. Even with Jalen Williams sidelined by a hamstring injury, the Thunder have the kind of roster depth that allows them to absorb absences without missing a beat. Chet Holmgren's 17.3 points and 9.0 rebounds provide a perfect complement to SGA's perimeter brilliance.

Brooklyn at 17-51 is one of the worst teams in the NBA, and this season has been about development and draft positioning rather than winning games. The Nets have been ravaged by injuries and roster turnover, with Michael Porter Jr. out with an ankle issue and Day'Ron Sharpe done for the season after UCL surgery. There's simply no player on this roster who can match up with SGA's combination of size, skill, and speed, and the defensive schemes that might slow him down require a level of coordination and talent that Brooklyn doesn't possess. The Nets will compete hard for stretches because professional basketball players don't quit, but the talent gap here is about as wide as it gets in the NBA.

The 18.5-point spread is the largest on Wednesday's board, and the -2400 moneyline essentially treats this as a foregone conclusion. OKC's defense, even without Williams, has the versatility and athleticism to shut down Brooklyn's limited offensive options. The Thunder switch everything on the perimeter and protect the rim with Holmgren's length, creating an environment where generating clean looks feels like solving a Rubik's cube in the dark. Brooklyn's offensive efficiency against top-10 defenses has been among the worst in the league, and OKC's defense is the best of the best.

The 214.5 total is the lowest on the board, and it should stay under if OKC's starters play their usual minutes. The Thunder have been content to grind games down defensively against inferior opponents, and their bench units typically maintain the same defensive intensity even when the starters are resting. The only scenario where this total goes over is if OKC builds a massive lead early and the garbage-time units on both sides start playing up-tempo basketball. But with the Thunder holding a 54-15 record, they've shown a disciplined approach to managing these kinds of games all season long.

Game 3

Trail Blazers @ Pacers

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Gainbridge Fieldhouse, Indianapolis, IN
Spread
POR -10.5
Moneyline
POR -470 / IND +360
Total
O/U 234.5

Portland at 33-36 is in the thick of the Western Conference play-in race, and every game from here on out carries enormous weight for a franchise trying to return to the postseason. The Trail Blazers have been one of the more surprising teams this season, building a competitive roster around their young core even after losing Damian Lillard to an Achilles injury and Shaedon Sharpe to a calf strain. Portland's identity this season has been built on defense and toughness, qualities that tend to translate well on the road against inferior competition. Coming into Gainbridge Fieldhouse as 10.5-point favorites speaks to how far Indiana has fallen this season.

The Pacers at 15-54 have endured one of the most painful seasons in franchise history. Losing Tyrese Haliburton to an Achilles injury suffered in Game 7 of the 2025 Finals gutted this roster of its creative engine, and the dominos have fallen all season. Pascal Siakam is out with a knee issue, and the list of players questionable or unavailable on any given night reads like a who's who of their rotation. Indiana became the first team mathematically eliminated from playoff contention, and the atmosphere at Gainbridge Fieldhouse has gone from the raucous energy of last year's Finals run to the somber quiet of a team waiting for the lottery.

The 234.5 total is the highest on the early window, and it reflects the pace both teams tend to play at in these kinds of matchups. Portland will push the tempo when they sense the opportunity to extend a lead, and Indiana's defense doesn't have the personnel to slow down capable offenses even when they're trying their hardest. The Pacers' young players, getting extended developmental minutes, tend to play fast and loose, which inflates scoring on both sides. This could easily be one of those games that finishes 125-108 with both teams trading runs throughout the middle quarters.

For Portland, this is a game they have to have. Sitting at 33-36 with the play-in tournament just weeks away, road wins against the league's worst teams are the baseline expectation, not bonus victories. The Trail Blazers' play-in hopes depend on stacking these kinds of wins and building momentum heading into the final stretch. If they can't handle a depleted Indiana roster by double digits on the road, it raises serious questions about their readiness for the intensity of a play-in game. Portland's defense should be the difference, as Indiana's offense lacks the shot creation to sustain scoring against organized defensive schemes.

Game 4

Raptors @ Bulls

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | United Center, Chicago, IL
Spread
TOR -7.5
Moneyline
TOR -305 / CHI +245
Total
O/U 232.5

Toronto at 38-29 has been one of the best stories in the Eastern Conference this season, and the acquisition of Brandon Ingram has taken this roster from intriguing to genuinely dangerous. Ingram's 21.9 points per game lead the team, and his ability to create his own shot from the mid-range and get to the free throw line gives Toronto a go-to scorer they desperately needed. Pair that with Scottie Barnes' all-around brilliance (18.7 PPG, 7.9 RPG, 5.4 APG) and RJ Barrett's continued development (19.0 PPG), and the Raptors have a three-headed offensive attack that can compete with anyone in the East. Immanuel Quickley (17.1 PPG, 6.0 APG) has been the perfect floor general to orchestrate this offense.

Chicago at 28-40 has been a disappointment, and the absence of their key players has made winning consistently nearly impossible. Jaden Ivey is sidelined with a knee issue that requires re-evaluation in a week, and the Bulls' roster simply doesn't have the depth to overcome significant absences. The United Center crowd has grown restless watching a team that's been out of playoff contention for weeks, and the energy in the building for a midweek game against a superior opponent is going to be noticeably flat. Chicago's offense struggles to create quality looks against set defenses, and Toronto's defense has been good enough this season to exploit those limitations.

The 7.5-point spread feels reasonable given the trajectories of these two franchises. Toronto is fighting for seeding position in a crowded Eastern Conference, where the difference between the 5th and 8th seed could be just two or three games. Every road win against a sub-.500 team matters for the Raptors, and they've shown the kind of focus and professionalism that winning teams bring to these supposed "easy" games. Barnes' versatility as a point-forward creates matchup problems that Chicago doesn't have the defensive personnel to solve, and Ingram's isolation scoring gives Toronto a bail-out option when the offense gets stagnant.

The 232.5 total suggests a moderately paced game with both teams capable of putting up points. Toronto's offense has been efficient all season, ranking in the top half of the league in offensive efficiency, and their four-pronged scoring attack makes it difficult for opposing defenses to focus on any single player. Chicago's defense has been below average, and without their full complement of perimeter defenders, they'll struggle to contain Toronto's wing players in isolation and pick-and-roll situations. This has the feel of a game where the Raptors control the pace, build a comfortable lead by the third quarter, and cruise to a victory that's never really in doubt.

Jazz @ Timberwolves

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Target Center, Minneapolis, MN
Spread
MIN -11.5
Moneyline
UTA +490 / MIN -675
Total
O/U 233.5

Minnesota at 42-27 is in a dogfight for Western Conference positioning, and the Timberwolves host a Utah team that's been one of the worst in the league all season. The big storyline here is Anthony Edwards' absence, as the 29.5 PPG scorer is sidelined with right knee inflammation and won't be re-evaluated for another week or two. Edwards has been the engine that drives everything Minnesota does offensively, and his absence creates a massive void that no single player can fill. But this is a roster built with enough depth to handle games against the league's bottom-feeders, even without their franchise cornerstone.

Julius Randle steps into the primary scoring role with Edwards out, and his 21.1 points, 6.9 rebounds, and 5.1 assists per game make him more than capable of carrying the offensive load against a team like Utah. Rudy Gobert's defensive presence (11.2 RPG, elite rim protection) ensures that Target Center remains a fortress regardless of who's scoring the points, and Jaden McDaniels (14.5 PPG) and Donte DiVincenzo (12.5 PPG, 38.9% from three) provide enough perimeter scoring to keep defenses honest. The Timberwolves' identity is built on defense first, and that identity doesn't change when Edwards sits.

Utah at 20-48 has been in full rebuild mode all season, and the losses of Lauri Markkanen (hip) and Jaren Jackson Jr. (season-ending knee condition) have removed whatever veteran stability this roster had. The Jazz are playing their young players heavy minutes and accepting the growing pains that come with development, which means inconsistency, turnovers, and defensive breakdowns against quality opponents. Utah's offense has been among the least efficient in the league, and their inability to consistently create quality looks in the half court makes it nearly impossible to stay competitive against teams with Minnesota's defensive caliber.

The 11.5-point spread reflects the gap between these teams, even with Edwards out. Minnesota's home-court advantage at Target Center has been excellent this season, and the Timberwolves have been particularly dominant against teams below .500 when the crowd is engaged. The 233.5 total is interesting because Edwards' absence should slow Minnesota's transition attack, but the Timberwolves' offense has enough half-court creation through Randle and their supporting cast to maintain a respectable scoring pace. Utah's defense is porous enough that even a slightly diminished Minnesota offense should be able to put up 115+ at home comfortably.

Clippers @ Pelicans

Spread
NOP -1.5
Moneyline
LAC +110 / NOP -130
Total
O/U 232.5

This is the tightest spread on Wednesday's entire board, and it's a fascinating matchup between two franchises with very different trajectories but similar records of underachievement this season. The Clippers at 34-34 are sitting right at .500 and clinging to the 8th spot in the Western Conference play-in picture, while New Orleans at 23-46 has been one of the league's biggest disappointments. The 1.5-point home-court spread for the Pelicans tells you the market views this as essentially a toss-up on neutral ground, with New Orleans getting the slightest bump for playing at the Smoothie King Center.

LA's season has been defined by injuries and inconsistency. Bradley Beal's season-ending hip surgery removed their second-best scorer, and Kawhi Leonard remains questionable with the ankle issues that have plagued his career. When Leonard plays, the Clippers look like a legitimate playoff team. When he doesn't, they look like a .500 squad with no clear offensive identity. The uncertainty around Leonard's availability on any given night makes it difficult to project how this team will perform, and the market's tight spread reflects that ambiguity. If Kawhi suits up, the Clippers should be favored outright. If he sits, New Orleans has the edge at home.

The Pelicans have had a brutal season, but they've played with more energy and competitiveness at home than their 23-46 record suggests. The Smoothie King Center crowd hasn't had much to cheer about, but home games against beatable opponents have still generated some atmosphere, and New Orleans' young players have responded to that energy. The Pelicans' roster has enough talent to compete in individual games, even if the season-long body of work has been poor. This is a spot where the home team's familiarity with their court and the Clippers' road inconsistency could create a genuinely competitive game.

The 232.5 total projects a moderately high-scoring affair, which makes sense given that neither team's defense has been particularly stingy this season. The Clippers' defensive rating on the road has been below average, and New Orleans' home offense has shown flashes of life throughout the season. This is one of those games where the outcome feels genuinely uncertain, and the betting market's reluctance to lay more than 1.5 points reflects that reality. Play-in implications add urgency for the Clippers, while the Pelicans are playing for pride and development down the stretch.

Atlanta at 37-31 is in the middle of a heated battle for Eastern Conference play-in positioning, and every road win is crucial for a team trying to solidify their spot in the postseason picture. The Hawks have been one of the most improved teams in the league this season, with Jalen Johnson emerging as a legitimate All-Star caliber player. Johnson's 23.0 points, 10.4 rebounds, and 8.1 assists per game have been jaw-dropping, and his ability to function as a point-forward who can guard multiple positions has transformed Atlanta's identity on both ends of the floor. He's the kind of player who makes everyone around him better, and the Hawks' record reflects his impact.

Dallas at 23-46 has endured a brutal season, and Kyrie Irving's season-ending knee injury was the final blow to whatever playoff aspirations remained. The Mavericks are now built around the development of Cooper Flagg, the 19-year-old rookie who's been thrust into a starring role far earlier than anyone anticipated. Klay Thompson and Khris Middleton provide veteran guidance, but this is fundamentally a team that's focused on the future rather than the present. Daniel Gafford is also out with an illness, further depleting a frontcourt that was already thin on talent compared to the league's best teams.

The 8.5-point spread reflects Atlanta's clear talent advantage, but the Hawks have been inconsistent on the road this season, and Dallas, despite their terrible record, plays with energy at American Airlines Center where the young players are eager to prove themselves. Jonathan Kuminga's questionable status for Atlanta adds a wrinkle, as his defensive versatility and transition scoring have been valuable in the Hawks' rotation. If Kuminga sits, Atlanta's depth takes a hit, but they should still have more than enough firepower to handle Dallas comfortably.

The 238.5 total is the highest on Wednesday's board outside of Denver-Memphis, and it reflects the pace both teams prefer to play at and the defensive shortcomings on Dallas's end. The Mavericks' defense has been among the worst in the league this season, and they lack the rim protection and perimeter versatility to slow down Atlanta's multi-faceted offense. Johnson, in particular, should feast against Dallas's defensive schemes, creating open looks for teammates and finishing at the rim with his combination of size and skill. This could easily be a game where both teams push 120+ points in an entertaining, up-tempo affair.

Nikola Jokic comes to Memphis on a mission, and the numbers he's putting up this season border on absurd. Jokic is averaging 28.6 points, 12.7 rebounds, and 10.5 assists per game, which means he's literally averaging a triple-double for the entire season. Let that sink in. The greatest passing big man in NBA history is also one of the most efficient scorers in the league, and his ability to dissect defenses from the high post creates an offensive ecosystem that makes every Denver player better. Jamal Murray's resurgence (25.4 PPG, 7.1 APG) has given the Nuggets the dynamic backcourt partner Jokic needs, and Aaron Gordon's 17.0 points per game provide the kind of two-way forward play that rounds out Denver's starting five beautifully.

Memphis at 23-44 has been decimated by Ja Morant's ongoing injury issues. Morant is currently sidelined with left elbow discomfort and won't be re-evaluated for two weeks, robbing the Grizzlies of their most explosive player and the heartbeat of their offense. Without Morant's ability to attack the rim and create chaos in transition, Memphis becomes a much more predictable team offensively, relying on half-court sets and individual shot creation from players who aren't designed to be primary options. Scotty Pippen Jr.'s season-ending toe surgery removed another ball-handler from the equation, leaving the Grizzlies desperately thin at guard.

The 13.5-point spread is one of the largest on the board, and Denver's offense is the reason why. The Nuggets at 42-27 are fighting for positioning in a loaded Western Conference, and they can't afford to take their foot off the gas against any opponent, regardless of record. Jokic's triple-double average means that every game is an opportunity for him to put on a statistical clinic, and Memphis's undermanned defense doesn't have the personnel to limit his passing lanes or his scoring touch in the low post. Murray's pick-and-roll partnership with Jokic is the most devastating two-man game in basketball, and the Grizzlies' defense simply can't handle both players simultaneously.

The 240.5 total is the highest on the entire board, and it's entirely justified. Denver's offense generates high-quality looks at an elite rate, and Memphis, despite their struggles, still plays at a pace that inflates scoring. The Grizzlies' young players will push the tempo in transition even when it's not strategically sound, and Denver is more than happy to play fast when the opportunities are there. Jokic's outlet passing in transition is devastating, and Murray's ability to finish in the open court makes the Nuggets one of the best fast-break teams in the league when opponents give them opportunities to run. This could be the highest-scoring game on Wednesday's slate.

Lakers @ Rockets

Wednesday, 9:30 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -2.5
Moneyline
LAL +114 / HOU -135

O/U 222.5

The nightcap on ESPN delivers the game of the night, and it's not particularly close. Luka Doncic vs Kevin Durant. Two of the most gifted offensive players of this generation going head-to-head in a game with massive Western Conference seeding implications. The Lakers at 43-25 are third in the Pacific Division and firmly in the playoff picture, with Doncic's 32.9 points, 8.5 assists, and 7.9 rebounds per game making him the most complete offensive player in basketball. Doncic has been transcendent since arriving in Los Angeles, and his partnership with LeBron James (21.2 PPG) and Austin Reaves (23.8 PPG) has given the Lakers a three-headed offensive monster that can beat you in a dozen different ways.

Houston at 41-26 has been one of the league's best stories this season, and Kevin Durant's arrival transformed this franchise from rebuilding hopefuls into legitimate contenders. Durant's 25.8 points per game have been as effortless as ever, and the way he's integrated with the Rockets' young core has been remarkable. Alperen Sengun (20.2 PPG, 8.9 RPG, 6.1 APG) has developed into one of the most skilled big men in the league, and Amen Thompson's 17.8 points, 7.7 rebounds, and 5.3 assists make him one of the most exciting young players in basketball. The Rockets' biggest loss has been Fred VanVleet, whose torn ACL ended his season and removed their defensive anchor in the backcourt, but this team has been resilient enough to maintain their position near the top of the West.

The 2.5-point home-court spread for Houston creates one of the most compelling betting matchups on the board. The Lakers have Doncic, who can single-handedly take over any game at any moment. The Rockets have Durant and a supporting cast that's deeper and more versatile. This is the kind of game that comes down to which star makes the biggest plays in the fourth quarter, and both Doncic and Durant have extensive track records of delivering in those moments. LeBron James, at 40 years old, adds another layer of star power to the Lakers' side, and his ability to facilitate and score at an elite level gives LA an x-factor that Houston has to account for on every possession.

The 222.5 total is the lowest on the late-night slate, and it reflects the defensive intensity both teams bring to games with seeding implications. Houston's defense, anchored by Thompson's length and athleticism on the perimeter and Sengun's improving rim protection, has been stingy against elite offenses all season. The Lakers, despite their offensive firepower, tend to play at a controlled pace that limits possessions and emphasizes half-court execution over fast-break chaos. This should be a chess match between two teams that respect each other's offensive capabilities, with each defensive stop feeling like a small victory. The atmosphere at Toyota Center will be electric for this one, and it's the perfect way to close out a nine-game Wednesday slate.
