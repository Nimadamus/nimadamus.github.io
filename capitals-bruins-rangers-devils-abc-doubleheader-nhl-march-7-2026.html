11-Game Saturday Slate

NHL March 7, 2026

Saturday's massive 11-game slate features an ABC doubleheader with the Capitals visiting the Bruins in a matinee and the Rangers heading across the Hudson to face the Devils. The Atlantic-leading Lightning (38-18-4) visit the Maple Leafs. The Flyers and Penguins renew the Pennsylvania rivalry. The league-best Hurricanes (40-16-6) travel to Calgary. Capitals @ Bruins

Saturday, 12:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Puck Line
BOS -1.5 (+195)
Moneyline
BOS -130 / WSH +110
Total
O/U 6.5

The ABC matinee opens with a terrific Metropolitan-Atlantic crossover between two playoff-caliber clubs. Boston at 34-22-5 (73 points) holds the fifth spot in the Atlantic and has been solid at TD Garden, where the home crowd provides a tangible energy boost. The Bruins are averaging 3.36 goals per game this season while allowing 3.18, suggesting a team that wins through offense as much as defense. This isn't the suffocating defensive Bruins of recent memory; this is a team that's comfortable in shootouts and trading chances.

Washington at 31-25-7 (69 points) is firmly in the wild card conversation in the Metropolitan Division, and every point matters from here on out. The Capitals are averaging 3.16 goals per game with 3.00 against, making them a team that plays tight, competitive games more often than not. Alex Ovechkin's relentless pursuit of Wayne Gretzky's all-time goals record remains the overarching storyline in Washington, and every road game is an opportunity to inch closer to history. The Capitals have shown they can compete in hostile environments this season.

The 6.5 total is the highest on the early slate, and it reflects two teams that score freely and play at an uptempo pace. This has the feel of a 4-3 or 5-3 game where both goalies are tested frequently and special teams play a significant role. The BOS -130 moneyline suggests the Bruins are slight favorites but not prohibitive ones, making this a genuine toss-up that should deliver entertaining hockey for the ABC audience. The puck line at +195 for Boston reflects that covering 1.5 goals is never easy in games between evenly matched teams.

The Hudson River rivalry takes center stage on ABC's afternoon window, and the Devils enter this one with significantly more at stake. The Devils need to stockpile points down the stretch, and the Prudential Center crowd will be invested in every shift knowing that the playoff picture in the Metro is incredibly tight.

The roster has underperformed relative to expectations, and the 24-29-8 record tells the story of a team that couldn't find consistency when it mattered most. Walking across the river into a hostile Prudential Center against a desperate Devils squad isn't the recipe for a turnaround performance.

The NJD -162 moneyline and -1.5 (+150) puck line tell you the market sees this as a comfortable Devils win, and it's hard to argue. New Jersey has the talent, the motivation, and the home-court advantage in a rivalry game where the desperation gap between the two teams is enormous. The 5.5 total is modest, reflecting the defensive nature of rivalry games where both sides play physical, tight-checking hockey. Tampa Bay's 38-18-4 record reflects a team that's playing with purpose and conviction, and their road game has been strong enough to justify being favored at Scotiabank Arena.

Toronto at 27-25-11 (65 points) has been one of the most disappointing teams in the NHL this season. The Maple Leafs are 8th in the Atlantic and staring at the possibility of missing the playoffs altogether. For a franchise with Stanley Cup aspirations and a roster loaded with talent, a 27-25-11 record is a crisis. Home games against division leaders like Tampa Bay are the exact kind of games Toronto needs to win to save their season, but the TBL -180 moneyline suggests the market doesn't have faith in the Leafs to rise to the occasion.

The 6.5 total reflects two teams that generate offense, but this game carries more weight for Toronto than it does for Tampa Bay. The Lightning can afford a loss and still maintain their Atlantic lead. The Maple Leafs can't afford many more losses without their playoff hopes evaporating entirely. That desperation could make Toronto a dangerous opponent at home, but Tampa Bay's overall quality and Vasilevskiy's steadiness give them a clear edge. This is a fascinating game with enormous implications for both teams' postseason positioning.
