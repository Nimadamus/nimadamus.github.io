West Round 2 - Game 4 - Featured
Amazon Prime Video

Thunder @ Lakers

Monday, 10:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA

The Oklahoma City Thunder arrive at Crypto.com Arena with a chance to close out the Western Conference Semifinals in four games. Oklahoma City opens as a 10.5-point road favorite, the kind of road number that books rarely set on the Lakers in their own building and a price that reflects how completely the defending-champion Thunder have dominated the matchup across the first three games. The series sits 3-0 after Oklahoma City's 131-108 Game 3 win on Saturday night, and no team in NBA history has come back from a 3-0 deficit to win a playoff series. Tip-off is 10:30 PM ET on Amazon Prime Video as Prime's first round of NBA postseason coverage continues to anchor the late window.

The Thunder's path to the sweep runs through Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's MVP-caliber scoring engine and the kind of two-way wing depth Sam Presti assembled around him. Oklahoma City's 131-108 Game 3 win was a complete-team performance built on the SGA scoring profile, the secondary creation of Jalen Williams and Chet Holmgren, and the perimeter defense that has held the Lakers below their regular-season offensive efficiency in each of the first three games. The matchup against a Lakers roster missing Luka Doncic for the 15th consecutive game with the hamstring injury sustained in early April has tilted the entire bracket geometry. Oklahoma City's depth advantage has shown in fourth-quarter sequences in all three games and the kind of late-game shot-making that closes playoff series.

The Lakers' Game 4 path has narrowed to LeBron James and Austin Reaves carrying an offensive load that the roster shape was never built to absorb without Doncic. LeBron's sustained scoring profile at 41 years old is one of the great basketball stories of the modern era, but the structural deficit of running the playoffs without a primary co-star is the kind of geometry that flat-out cannot beat the depth of the Thunder. The pace, perimeter shooting, and turnover differential have all tilted the wrong way in this series, and Game 4 is the kind of elimination-night spot where the Lakers either find a third option offensively or the season ends on Crypto.com Arena home court. Tip-off is 10:30 PM ET on Amazon Prime Video.

East Round 2 - Game 4
NBC / Peacock

Pistons @ Cavaliers

Monday, 8:00 PM ET | Rocket Arena, Cleveland, OH

The Eastern Conference Semifinals Game 4 between the Detroit Pistons and Cleveland Cavaliers returns to Rocket Arena Monday night with the series sitting 2-1 in Detroit's favor and Cleveland looking to even the bracket before it heads back to Detroit. The Pistons captured Game 1 in Detroit 111-101 and Game 2 in Detroit 107-97 before the Cavaliers responded with a 116-109 Game 3 home win on Saturday afternoon. The home team has won five straight games in this head-to-head dating across the regular season and into the playoffs, and Cleveland is 31-12 straight up as the home favorite this season. The 8:00 PM ET tip-off airs on NBC and Peacock as part of NBC's return to the NBA broadcast lineup.

The Cavaliers' Game 4 path runs through the full return of Darius Garland, Evan Mobley, and De'Andre Hunter alongside Donovan Mitchell. Game 3 was the first complete-roster game of the series for Cleveland after Garland and Mobley missed parts of Games 1 and 2, and the 116-109 home win confirmed the read that the Cavaliers' regular-season offensive identity reassembles around the Mitchell-Garland backcourt with Mobley anchoring the half-court geometry on both ends. The Cavaliers' offensive efficiency in Game 3 ran at the level that produced the 52-30 regular-season record, and the front-court rotation of Mobley with Jarrett Allen produced the kind of two-big closing lineup that Detroit did not see in the first two games of the series.

The Pistons walk into Game 4 with a 2-1 series lead and an injury report that turned sideways on Sunday night. Kevin Huerter is listed as questionable with the left adductor strain that has now sidelined him from large parts of the series, and Caris LeVert was added to the questionable list with a right heel contusion. Detroit's depth profile around Cade Cunningham, Tobias Harris, Jalen Duren, Jaden Ivey, and Ausar Thompson is the structural piece that has produced the 2-1 lead in this round, and the Game 4 read is whether the Pistons absorb the Cavaliers' full-roster Game 3 response or whether Detroit pulls the road split that swings the series back to Cleveland on the brink. Tip-off is 8:00 PM ET on NBC and Peacock.
