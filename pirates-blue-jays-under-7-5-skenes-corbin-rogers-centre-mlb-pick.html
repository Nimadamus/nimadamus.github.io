Here's the thing about betting Paul Skenes games: the market already knows he's special, but it still can't fully price what he does to a run total. He walks into Rogers Centre at 6-3 with a 2.62 ERA, and that number alone should be dragging this total toward 7 flat. Instead we get 7.5, and the under at a fair price. I'll take that all day.

Toronto is the trap name here. The Blue Jays sound dangerous, but a 23-27 club that has spent the year scuffling does not scare you when it has to solve the best young arm in the sport. That's the whole sell. Skenes locks down one half of the scoreboard, and Toronto's offense has to do something it hasn't done consistently all season to push this over.

And no, the under doesn't need Patrick Corbin to be an ace. He's 1-1 with a 4.23 ERA, a serviceable lefty, nothing more. All Corbin has to do is keep a middle-of-the-road Pittsburgh offense (26-24) from hanging a crooked number early. Pair a competent start from him with six dominant innings from Skenes and 7.5 looks like a generous gift.

Why The Under, Not A Side

Look, the moneyline already has Pittsburgh at -154. The market isn't hiding anything on the side, and laying that price to sweat a one-run outcome is not where I want my money. The cleaner edge is the run environment. Skenes starts tend to live in the low-scoring band, the Pirates are 6-3 when he pitches, and the total lets you ride his arm without paying favorite freight.

I'm not pretending this is free. Rogers Centre can play big when the ball is carrying, and one swing changes everything. But the pitching edge is real enough to keep us on the right side of a key number. A 3-2 game cashes. A 4-3 game cashes. The only way this dies is a late bullpen leak or a multi-run inning against Skenes, and that just hasn't been the script this year.

Number, Price And Unit Size

The price is -120, and that's fair for an under anchored by a sub-3.00 ERA starter at a key total. We don't need a shutout. We need a controlled afternoon that finishes at seven runs or fewer, which is exactly the type of game Skenes produces.

Stake is 2 units. Confident, not reckless. The pitching matchup is lopsided in Pittsburgh's favor, so this earns more than a token lean, but Toronto still has the home lineup and the last at-bat, so we're not treating it like a lock either. Two units is the right number for a strong-but-not-bulletproof spot.

What Beats The Bet

One messy inning. That's the whole nightmare. A leadoff walk, a defensive miscue, a two-run swing in a ballpark where the air helps, and suddenly the math tightens. Toronto gets the home half and the last at-bat, so a late bullpen blowup on either side is the classic way a 7.5 under goes up in smoke. And if Skenes gets pulled early or grinds through traffic, that cushion we're leaning on shrinks fast.

Bottom Line

Published pick stays Pirates/Blue Jays under 7.5 at -120 for 2 units. Strip away the noise and it's simple: Skenes at a 2.62 ERA versus a 23-27 Blue Jays lineup, with a soft 7.5 hanging up there, is the cleanest angle on the board. The side is overpriced at -154. Let the best young arm in baseball carry the ticket. Play the total.
