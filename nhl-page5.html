The Matchup

A classic Central Division rivalry reignites at Enterprise Center as two Original Six franchises battle it out. The Blackhawks have been a pleasant surprise this season at 13-11-6, playing competitive hockey after years of rebuilding. St. Louis has struggled at 11-14-7, caught between their championship window closing and a necessary rebuild they've been reluctant to commit to.

Key Storyline

Chicago's Connor Bedard has lived up to the generational hype, averaging 1.12 PPG and quarterbacking one of the league's best power plays. The kid is the real deal. For St. Louis, Jordan Kyrou (22 goals) has carried the offense, but the supporting cast has underperformed. Robert Thomas has regressed, and the Blues' goaltending carousel between Jordan Binnington and Joel Hofer has been suboptimal.

By The Numbers

Chicago ranks 14th in goals per game (3.12) and 19th in goals against (3.08). St. Louis is 22nd offensively (2.78 GPG) and 20th defensively (3.16 GAA). The Blues are just 4-8-2 at home this season - one of the worst home records in the NHL. Chicago is 7-4-2 on the road. The 5.5 total reflects two teams that play relatively low-event hockey.

The Angle

St. Louis as a home favorite feels like a trap. The Blues have been dreadful at Enterprise Center, and the Blackhawks have genuine star power with Bedard. The -125 line on St. Louis implies a 56% win probability, but their underlying metrics suggest they're closer to a .500 team. Chicago's offensive upside with Bedard makes them the more interesting side here.

The Matchup

Seattle travels to Salt Lake City to face Utah in the Mammoth's inaugural season. The expansion franchise has exceeded expectations at 14-15-3, giving their passionate new fanbase competitive hockey. The Delta Center has been electric, and Utah has actually played better at home than on the road. Seattle at 12-10-6 is the definition of mediocre - not good enough to be a playoff team, not bad enough to get a premium draft pick.

Key Storyline

Utah's Dylan Guenther has been a revelation, scoring 18 goals in his first 32 games as a Mammoth. Karel Vejmelka has stabilized the goaltending after early-season struggles. For Seattle, Matty Beniers has taken a step back in his sophomore season, and the Kraken's lack of offensive firepower continues to be their Achilles heel. They rank 27th in goals per game (2.61).

By The Numbers

Utah is 9-6-1 at home this season and 14-8-2 ATS overall - one of the best covers in the Western Conference. Seattle is 4-7-2 on the road and has struggled to score, averaging just 2.3 goals per game away from Climate Pledge Arena. The -185 line on Utah is heavy, implying a 65% win probability. The 5.5 total suggests oddsmakers expect a low-scoring, defensive affair.

The Angle

The expansion Mammoth have been a legitimate cover machine this season, and the home crowd in Salt Lake is passionate and loud. Seattle has been a disappointment, especially on the road. The -185 is steep, but Utah's home record and Seattle's road struggles justify the juice. This feels like a grind-it-out 3-1 or 2-1 type game where the Mammoth find a way.
