Madison Square Garden gets the Eastern Conference Finals opener on Tuesday night with Cleveland walking in as the higher-seeded road team and New York as a 6.5-point home favorite. ESPN's broadcast window starts at 8 ET. The Knicks closed out Philadelphia in the second round; the Cavaliers needed a Game 7 to put away a stubborn Detroit team and now have to win on the road in a building that has not lost much through two rounds.

What The Knicks Have Built

New York's postseason advanced numbers explain why this line lives on the home favorite side. The Knicks own a playoff offensive rating of 119.8 (third), a defensive rating of 113.3 (seventh) and a net rating of plus-6.5 (fifth). The deeper read is even sharper: since January 20 the Knicks have a plus-10.3 net rating (third in the league) with a defensive rating of 108.2 that ranks second. That is the version of New York that closed Philadelphia, and that is the version Cleveland is now trying to crack in a Game 1 on the road.

The personnel piece matters. Jalen Brunson is averaging 26.0 points per game this season (10th in the NBA). Karl-Anthony Towns gives the Knicks frontcourt scoring against any defensive coverage. Mikal Bridges and OG Anunoby give head coach the long, switchable wing pairing he needs to throw at Donovan Mitchell. Anunoby is listed as probable for Game 1 with a right hamstring strain that lingered from the Philadelphia series. Probable is not cleared, but it is the strongest signal yet that he will be available, and it changes the math on how Mitchell can attack matchups in the half court.

Cleveland's Profile And The Road-Game Problem

Cleveland are not at all out of place in this round. Since the James Harden trade the Cavaliers have a record of 22-9 with a 120.8 offensive rating (fourth) and a plus-5.7 net rating (ninth). The Donovan Mitchell version of the offense is dangerous in any building. The issue is splits: the Cavaliers are averaging just 109.4 points per 100 possessions on the road with a minus-2.4 net rating away from home. That is the swing that makes the market list New York at minus-6.5 even though the regular-season records are not far apart.

Mitchell carries the highest-usage burden, averaging 27.9 points per game (sixth in the NBA). Evan Mobley is averaging 9.0 rebounds per game (ninth) and is the defender Cleveland is going to lean on against Towns and inside the paint when Brunson finally gets to a finish. Darius Garland needs to handle the second-side ball-handling against a Knicks defense that switches and recovers as well as anyone in the East. Larry Nance Jr. is listed as questionable with an illness, a depth note that affects the bench picture more than the starting lineup.

Mitchell vs Bridges and Anunoby

The defining matchup of Game 1 is Mitchell against the Bridges and Anunoby pair. New York will throw fresh, long wings at him every time he picks up the ball and try to keep him from getting downhill rhythm against Brunson on cross-matches. The counter from Cleveland is to move Mitchell off the ball, hunt switches, and let Garland or a spot-up shooter like Sam Merrill attack the Brunson defensive assignment. If the Cavaliers get into that pattern, the offense looks like the Harden-era version that ran up the regular-season net rating; if they get stuck in isolation against Bridges and Anunoby, the offense gets slow and the Knicks defense will dictate.

The Total Sits At 213.5

The 213.5 total reads like a four-quarter, half-court game. Both teams are top-10 defensively in the playoffs by points allowed per 100 possessions. Game 1 of a conference finals routinely comes in slightly under regular-season pace as both staffs guard against transition. A 110-104 type game lands well under the number, while a 116-110 type game pushes the over. The market is not asking for a track meet.

WCF Watch

The other half of the bracket sits idle on Tuesday after a wild Game 1 in Oklahoma City. San Antonio took double-overtime Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals from the Thunder on Monday night, with Victor Wembanyama dropping 41 points and 24 rebounds. WCF Game 2 is Wednesday in OKC and gets the next national window. That puts even more weight on the East opener tonight: this is the only basketball on the board, and ESPN treats it like a true marquee.

Things To Watch In The First Six Minutes

The opening minutes are where Game 1s historically decide their tone. Three checkpoints to track: first, how often Cleveland generate paint touches in the half court rather than settling for early-clock pull-ups against Bridges and Anunoby. Second, whether New York protect the defensive glass against Mobley or give up second-chance possessions that crack the home crowd open. Third, the live-ball turnover battle. Cleveland have been a top defense in the playoffs at forcing live-ball giveaways; the Knicks have been one of the best at avoiding them. Whichever team wins that micro-battle tilts the night.

Final Thoughts

This is the kind of Game 1 the league waits for: 1 seed at home, 2 seed on the road by record, both top-six MVP-tier scorers on the floor, both rosters in their best version since January. New York is favored because home court matters and the defensive profile holds up, but Cleveland are not a team you fade lightly with Mitchell carrying the ball. The smart frame is process, not result: track shot quality, turnover differential and rebounding, and the basketball will tell the story. The Eastern Conference Finals starts here.
