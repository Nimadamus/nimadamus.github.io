Bills @ Browns

2025-12-21T18:00Z | Huntington Bank Field | CBS
Line: BUF -10.5 | O/U: 40.5 | BUF: 10-4 | CLE: 3-11

Look, this is about as big a mismatch as you'll find on a Week 16 slate. The Bills are rolling at 10-4 and fighting for the AFC's top seed, while Cleveland is limping to the finish line at 3-11. Josh Allen has been in MVP form all season, and he's not slowing down against a Browns defense that's given up 25+ points in six of their last eight games.

Cleveland's offense has been a disaster without consistent quarterback play. They're averaging just 14.2 points per game over their last month, and Buffalo's defense is opportunistic enough to create multiple turnovers. The Bills have covered 10.5-point spreads in three of their last four games as heavy favorites.

The only concern here is garbage time scoring, but Buffalo's been putting games away early and keeping their foot on the gas. The Browns have quit on this season, and it shows on film. Take the Bills to handle business on the road.

Chargers @ Cowboys

2025-12-21T18:00Z | AT&T Stadium | FOX
Line: DAL -1.5 | O/U: 50.5 | LAC: 10-4 | DAL: 6-7-1

This is one of the more intriguing matchups of the day. Jim Harbaugh has the Chargers playing disciplined, physical football at 10-4, while Dallas continues to frustrate with their inconsistency at 6-7-1. The Cowboys getting points at home against a team of LA's caliber feels like a trap, and that's exactly what it is.

Justin Herbert has been surgical this season when given time, and Dallas's pass rush hasn't been nearly as disruptive as years past. Meanwhile, the Cowboys' offense has been boom-or-bust all year. Dak Prescott can put up 300 yards one week and look completely lost the next. You can't trust this team at home.

The Chargers control the clock, limit mistakes, and win with defense. That's a recipe for covering a small spread on the road. Dallas has covered just twice in their last seven home games as underdogs. Give me LA here.

Chiefs @ Titans

2025-12-21T18:00Z | Nissan Stadium | CBS
Line: KC -3.5 | O/U: 37.5 | KC: 6-8 | TEN: 2-12

Here's something you don't see every December: the Kansas City Chiefs at 6-8 and fighting for their playoff lives. The dynasty is on life support, and they're facing a Tennessee team that's been terrible but plays hard at home. This 3.5-point spread feels small for a reason.

Patrick Mahomes is still Patrick Mahomes, but the supporting cast has let him down all season. The Chiefs' offensive line has been a turnstile, and the defense has regressed significantly from championship form. Tennessee at 2-12 is bad, but they've actually covered in three of their last five home games.

The total of 37.5 is the real story here. Both teams have struggled to score consistently, and this has all the makings of an ugly, low-scoring affair in Nashville. I'd lean under rather than picking a side in this mess.

Bengals @ Dolphins

2025-12-21T18:00Z | Hard Rock Stadium | CBS
Line: CIN -3.5 | O/U: 48.5 | CIN: 4-10 | MIA: 6-8

Two disappointing teams meet in Miami, with the Bengals as slight road favorites despite their 4-10 record. Cincinnati's record is misleading - they've lost six games by a field goal or less. Joe Burrow has been playing at an elite level, but the defense and special teams have been a disaster.

Miami's season fell apart after their early promise. Tua Tagovailoa has been inconsistent, and the running game that was supposed to complement the passing attack never materialized. At home, the Dolphins have managed to keep games close, but they haven't been able to finish.

The 48.5 total suggests books expect points, and they should get them. Neither defense can stop anyone, and both quarterbacks are capable of putting up 300+ yards. This could be a fun shootout in South Florida. I like the over more than either side.

Jets @ Saints

2025-12-21T18:00Z | Caesars Superdome | CBS
Line: NO -6.5 | O/U: 40.5 | NYJ: 3-11 | NO: 4-10

The battle of broken dreams in the Big Easy. Both the Jets and Saints entered 2025 with playoff aspirations and both have been complete disasters. New York at 3-11 has been arguably the biggest disappointment in football, while New Orleans at 4-10 has dealt with injuries and inconsistent play all season.

The Saints are 6.5-point home favorites, which feels about right. The Superdome is still a tough place to play, and New Orleans has at least shown flashes of competence at home. The Jets, meanwhile, have been an embarrassment on the road, covering just once in their last eight away games.

This is a stay-away game if I've ever seen one. Neither team is worth trusting, and the 40.5 total reflects how ugly this could get. If forced to pick, I'd take the Saints to cover at home, but I'd rather just skip this one entirely.

Vikings @ Giants

2025-12-21T18:00Z | MetLife Stadium | FOX
Line: MIN -2.5 | O/U: 41.5 | MIN: 6-8 | NYG: 2-12

Minnesota's playoff hopes are hanging by a thread at 6-8, and they desperately need wins down the stretch. The good news? They're facing a Giants team that's been one of the worst in football at 2-12. New York has been outscored by double digits in their last six games and shows no signs of life.

Sam Darnold has been better than expected this season for Minnesota, and Justin Jefferson remains one of the most dangerous receivers in football. The Vikings' offense can put up points when they're clicking, and the Giants' defense is too banged up to slow them down.

The 2.5-point spread feels way too low. Minnesota should win this game by double digits if they show up focused. The Giants are playing out the string and have nothing to play for. Take the Vikings to handle business in the Meadowlands.

Buccaneers @ Panthers

2025-12-21T18:00Z | Bank of America Stadium | FOX
Line: TB -3 | O/U: 45.5 | TB: 7-7 | CAR: 7-7

This NFC South showdown has sneaky playoff implications with both teams sitting at 7-7. Tampa Bay has been the more consistent team this season, with Baker Mayfield putting together another solid campaign. Carolina, though, has exceeded expectations and plays with serious energy at home under Dave Canales.

The Panthers' improvement has been one of the feel-good stories of the year. Bryce Young has started to look like the first overall pick they thought they were getting, and the running game has provided balance. At home, Carolina has been tough to beat, going 4-2 in their last six at Bank of America Stadium.

Tampa as a 3-point road favorite feels about right, but I'm not laying points with the Bucs on the road in a divisional game. Carolina has the home crowd, the motivation, and enough talent to make this a coin flip. I'd take the Panthers plus the points here.

Jaguars @ Broncos

2025-12-21T21:05Z | Empower Field at Mile High | FOX
Line: DEN -3.5 | O/U: 46.5 | JAX: 10-4 | DEN: 12-2

This is one of the premier matchups of Week 16. Denver at 12-2 has been the surprise of the NFL this season, with Sean Payton building a legitimate contender around Bo Nix. Jacksonville at 10-4 has also exceeded expectations, but traveling to Mile High in late December is no picnic.

The Broncos are nearly unbeatable at home, where the altitude gives them a significant edge. Their defense has been suffocating, and Nix has avoided the rookie mistakes that typically plague first-year quarterbacks. Jacksonville's offense is talented, but Trevor Lawrence will need to be perfect to keep up.

Denver laying just 3.5 points at home against a good team feels like a gift. The Broncos have covered this number in five of their last six home games, and their defense should create problems for Jacksonville. Take Denver to win and cover.

Falcons @ Cardinals

2025-12-21T21:05Z | State Farm Stadium | FOX
Line: ATL -3 | O/U: 48.5 | ATL: 5-9 | ARI: 3-11

Two disappointing teams square off in the desert. Atlanta's 5-9 record is a far cry from the playoff expectations they had entering the season. Raheem Morris's first year has been a learning experience, and the Falcons have struggled to find consistency on either side of the ball.

Arizona at 3-11 has been even worse, but they're getting points at home. Kyler Murray has shown flashes when healthy, but the supporting cast just isn't good enough to compete consistently. The Cardinals have been competitive in losses at home, rarely getting blown out in State Farm Stadium.

Atlanta as a 3-point road favorite is about right. The Falcons need wins to salvage something from this season, and they should have enough firepower to handle Arizona. However, the Cardinals' ability to keep games close makes me nervous about laying points. Lean Atlanta, but don't go big.

Steelers @ Lions

2025-12-21T21:25Z | Ford Field | CBS
Line: DET -7.5 | O/U: 52.5 | PIT: 8-6 | DET: 8-6

This is the game of the week, no question. Two 8-6 teams with playoff aspirations in a late-season showdown at Ford Field. Detroit has been one of the most fun teams to watch all season, while Pittsburgh's defense has carried them through a grinding campaign.

The Lions at home are a different animal. Dan Campbell has created a culture where the crowd becomes a legitimate factor, and Detroit's offense can score with anyone. Jared Goff has been excellent in the dome, and Amon-Ra St. Brown continues to be one of the best receivers in football.

Pittsburgh's defense is their calling card, but I'm not sure it's enough to slow down Detroit's high-powered attack. The Steelers have struggled on the road this season, going just 3-4 away from Heinz Field. Detroit -7.5 is a lot to lay, but I think the Lions win by 10+ in a playoff-atmosphere game.

Raiders @ Texans

2025-12-21T21:25Z | NRG Stadium | CBS
Line: HOU -14.5 | O/U: 38.5 | LV: 2-12 | HOU: 9-5

Houston is a massive 14.5-point favorite, and for good reason. The Texans at 9-5 are playoff-bound and looking to lock up the AFC South, while Las Vegas at 2-12 is already thinking about the draft. This is about as big a mismatch as you'll see in Week 16.

C.J. Stroud continues to prove he's the real deal, and Houston's offense has found its rhythm in the second half of the season. The defense has improved as well, giving DeMeco Ryans exactly what he needs to control games. Against a Raiders team with nothing to play for, expect the Texans to dominate.

The only question is whether Houston covers this massive spread. Vegas has been covering as big underdogs recently, keeping games closer than expected through pure effort. But 14.5 points against a team this bad feels justified. Take Houston to cruise.

Patriots @ Ravens

2025-12-22T01:20Z | M&T Bank Stadium | NBC, Peacock
Line: BAL -3 | O/U: 48.5 | NE: 11-3 | BAL: 7-7

Sunday Night Football closes out Week 16 with a fascinating matchup. New England at 11-3 has been the AFC's most surprising team, completely reinventing themselves under a new coaching regime. Baltimore at 7-7 is fighting for playoff positioning and needs every win they can get.

The Patriots have won with defense and ball control, limiting turnovers and playing smart football. It's a stark contrast to the chaos of recent years, and Drake Maye has shown flashes of franchise quarterback potential. Their defense has been opportunistic, forcing turnovers at a league-leading rate.

Baltimore has the talent to win this game, but Lamar Jackson has been inconsistent down the stretch. The Ravens' defense has given up too many points, and playing from behind doesn't suit their run-heavy offensive identity. New England getting 3 points on the road in primetime feels like value. I'll take the Patriots to cover and potentially win outright.
