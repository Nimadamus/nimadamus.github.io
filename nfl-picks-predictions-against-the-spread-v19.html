Packers @ Lions

2025-11-30 | Thursday, 12:30 PM ET | Ford Field | FOX

The NFC North's finest meet on Turkey Day in what could be a preview of January. Detroit has been dominant at home, while Green Bay's young roster continues to mature. Jordan Love has looked like a franchise quarterback, but facing the Lions defense in Ford Field is his toughest test of the season.

Dan Campbell has this team believing they're Super Bowl contenders, and they're playing like it. The Lions' rushing attack is punishing, and their defense has gotten timely stops all season. Green Bay needs to keep pace in the division race, making this effectively a must-win.

Thanksgiving Stakes

Detroit hasn't won a Thanksgiving game against a division rival in years, and they're hungry to change that narrative. The Lambeau mystique doesn't travel well, and Ford Field will be absolutely electric. Expect a physical, hard-fought game that could come down to the final possession.

Chiefs @ Cowboys

Thursday, 4:30 PM ET | AT&T Stadium | CBS

Patrick Mahomes brings his back-to-back Super Bowl winning Chiefs (2023-24) to Jerry World for the afternoon showcase. Kansas City has been navigating through adversity all season but remains dangerous with Mahomes under center. The Chiefs find ways to win close games, and their Super Bowl experience shows in crunch time.

Dallas desperately needs a statement win. The Cowboys have underperformed relative to expectations, and a nationally televised victory over the champs would silence some critics. Dak Prescott has the weapons, but the offensive line needs to give him time against Steve Spagnuolo's pressure packages.

Primetime Spotlight

Mahomes vs. Prescott in front of 90,000 fans on Thanksgiving is appointment television. Both quarterbacks have the arm talent to make any throw, and this game could become a shootout. Watch for Travis Kelce to find soft spots in the Cowboys' zone coverage.

Bengals @ Ravens

Thursday, 8:20 PM ET | M&T Bank Stadium | NBC

The AFC North rivalry caps Thanksgiving night in Baltimore. Lamar Jackson has been running away with MVP consideration, making plays with both his arm and legs that no other quarterback can replicate. Derrick Henry has been the perfect complement, wearing down defenses and keeping the chains moving.

Joe Burrow refuses to let the Bengals die. Despite injuries and inconsistency around him, Burrow keeps making elite throws and leading comebacks. Ja'Marr Chase is his usual dominant self, and when these two connect, defenses have no answers.

Division Implications

Every AFC North game matters in this ultra-competitive division. The Ravens have home field advantage, but Burrow has a history of playing his best football in prime time. Expect fireworks as two of the league's best quarterbacks battle for division positioning.
