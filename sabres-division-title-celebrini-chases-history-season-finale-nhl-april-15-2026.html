6-Game Wednesday Slate

Celebrini Chases History, Season Finale Eve

The penultimate day of the 2025-26 NHL regular season delivers a loaded 6-game Wednesday slate. Macklin Celebrini sits just 2 points from Joe Thornton's all-time Sharks franchise record in San Jose's visit to Chicago, where he'll face fellow generational talent Connor Bedard. Buffalo celebrates its first division title in 15 years and first playoff berth since 2011 as the Sabres host Dallas in a 1999 Stanley Cup Final rematch. Nikita Kucherov continues padding his 130-point masterpiece against the eliminated Rangers, and the Battle of Ontario pits a surging Ottawa squad against a Toronto team mired in a brutal L6 streak. Out west, Vegas jockeys for playoff positioning under new coach John Tortorella.

Stars @ Sabres

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | KeyBank Center, Buffalo, NY
Puck Line
DAL +1.5 (-265) / BUF -1.5 (+215)
Moneyline
DAL +100 / BUF -115
Total
O/U 6.5

If you're looking for the most emotionally charged building in the NHL tonight, it's KeyBank Center in Buffalo, and it's not even close. The Sabres have clinched their first division title since 2010 and their first playoff berth since 2011, ending what was the longest active playoff drought in the entire league. Fifteen years of heartbreak, bad lottery luck, coaching carousels, and rebuilds that never quite turned the corner have finally given way to a legitimate contender, and the Buffalo faithful are going to make tonight feel like a Stanley Cup celebration. The -115 moneyline makes the Sabres a slight home favorite, and honestly, with the emotion in that building and the way this team has been playing, that price feels like a bargain.

And then there's the storyline that makes this game appointment television for any hockey historian. Dallas Stars. Buffalo Sabres. The 1999 Stanley Cup Final. "No Goal." Brett Hull's controversial Cup-clinching goal in triple overtime of Game 6, the most debated play in NHL history, happened with these two franchises on opposite benches. Now they meet again with Dallas sitting at 49-20-12, 110 points, locked into second in the West, and Buffalo riding the highest of highs. Lindy Ruff, who coached the Sabres in that 1999 Final, is back behind Buffalo's bench, and you can bet the memories are flowing in Western New York tonight. Both teams are riding 4-game winning streaks, and neither wants to blink first heading into the postseason.

Tage Thompson has been the engine of everything Buffalo has accomplished this season. He's over 40 goals and 90+ points, the first Sabre to hit those marks since Daniel Briere in 2006-07, and his three hat tricks this year have been signature moments in the franchise's resurrection. Thompson's combination of size, skating, and shot is nearly impossible to contain, and he's elevated his game to a level that puts him in the conversation with the best centers in the NHL. On the other side, Jason Robertson has been brilliant for Dallas with 40 goals and 47 assists for 87 points, but the Stars are dealing with significant injuries. Roope Hintz has been out since March 6 with a lower-body injury, and Miro Heiskanen went down April 10 with a lower-body issue of his own, though there's hope he returns for the playoff opener on April 18.

The question for Dallas is whether they treat this as a tune-up or a rest day. The Stars have nothing left to play for in the standings, and with two of their key players already nursing injuries, there's a real argument for pulling back and making sure the group is fresh for Round 1. But Dallas has been on a 4-game tear despite those absences, and sometimes maintaining that rhythm is more valuable than rest. For Buffalo, this is a coronation. The Sabres aren't going to coast, not with this crowd, not with this opponent, and not with the ghosts of 1999 hanging over the matchup. KeyBank Center is going to be absolutely electric, and if you're a hockey fan, you don't want to miss this one.

Rangers @ Lightning

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Amalie Arena, Tampa, FL
Puck Line
NYR +1.5 (-205) / TB -1.5 (+170)
Moneyline
NYR +110 / TB -130
Total
O/U 6.5

Nikita Kucherov has been the best player in the world this season, and tonight gives him another chance to pad what has been an absolutely absurd stat line. With 44 goals, 86 assists, and 130 points, Kucherov has separated himself as the NHL's undisputed scoring champion, and every game left in the regular season is an opportunity to push that number even higher. The Lightning are locked into a first-round matchup against Montreal with home ice advantage, but there's zero chance Kucherov takes his foot off the gas. He's the kind of player who wants every point, every goal, every assist, and when you're in the middle of a 130-point season, you don't coast into the playoffs.

Tampa at 50-25-6, 106 points, is riding into this game with the confidence of a team that's been to the top of the mountain before. This is their 9th consecutive playoff appearance, a streak that speaks to the organizational stability and culture that Jon Cooper has built. Jake Guentzel has posted a career-high 88 points in his first full season with the Lightning, and the depth throughout the lineup is the kind of balanced attack that makes Tampa dangerous in a seven-game series. The -130 moneyline at home against an eliminated team feels like a very fair price, and the Lightning should be able to control this one from start to finish.

The Rangers, on the other hand, are limping toward the finish line. At 33-39-9, 75 points, New York has been eliminated and is staring at a second consecutive missed postseason, a stunning fall for a franchise that was in the Eastern Conference Final not that long ago. The L3 streak tells you where this team is mentally, and a brutal 9-18-7 home record has been one of the defining failures of the season. On the road in Tampa against a team with this much firepower, it's hard to see how the Rangers keep this competitive for 60 minutes.

The 6.5 total makes sense when you consider Tampa's offensive machine against a Rangers team that has shown very little defensive resistance down the stretch. Kucherov's vision and playmaking ability create chances at an elite rate, and Guentzel has the finishing touch to convert those opportunities. This should be a comfortable night for the Lightning, a chance to stay sharp before the playoff machine turns on. For the Rangers, it's just another game in a season they'll want to forget, and the real work begins this summer when the front office has to figure out how to stop the bleeding.

Red Wings @ Panthers

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Amerant Bank Arena, Sunrise, FL
Puck Line
DET -1.5 (+180) / FLA +1.5 (-218)
Moneyline
DET -130 / FLA +110
Total
O/U 6.5

This is what a dynasty looks like when the hockey gods say "not this year." Florida, the back-to-back Stanley Cup champion, has been eliminated from playoff contention as of April 4, becoming the first defending champion to miss the postseason since the 2014-15 Los Angeles Kings. It's not that the Panthers suddenly forgot how to play hockey. It's that injuries absolutely gutted this roster. Aleksander Barkov, the heart and soul of the franchise, is done for the season. Brad Marchand has been shut down. Seth Jones missed 26 games. When you lose your best players for extended stretches, no amount of championship pedigree can save you. The banners will hang forever, but this season in Sunrise has been a painful reminder of how thin the line is between champion and also-ran.

Detroit rolls into Amerant Bank Arena as a -130 road favorite, and that price reflects the massive gap in motivation between these two teams. The Red Wings at 41-30-10, 92 points, have had a solid season even though they fell short of the playoffs, and there's genuine pride in this group. Patrick Kane just reached 1,400 career points, a milestone that cements his legacy as one of the greatest American-born players in NHL history. At this stage of his career, every point is a gift to the sport, and Kane has shown flashes of brilliance this season that remind you why he's a future first-ballot Hall of Famer.

Lucas Raymond has been one of the brightest spots in Detroit's season, pushing past 25 goals and establishing himself as a legitimate top-line forward. And then there's Moritz Seider, whose recent 5-point game was a statement of arrival for one of the best young defensemen in the league. The Red Wings' core is young and getting better, and while the playoffs didn't happen this year, the trajectory is undeniably positive. Games like tonight give the young players a chance to build confidence and develop winning habits against a team with championship experience, even a depleted one.

For Florida, these final games are about getting healthy, evaluating the depth pieces, and starting to plan for a championship defense next season. The Panthers' organizational depth was tested beyond its limits this year, and the young players who got extended opportunities will be better for it. The crowd at Amerant Bank Arena won't be at its playoff peak, but the fans in South Florida have earned the right to celebrate what this team accomplished over the past two years. Two Stanley Cups don't disappear because of one injury-plagued season. The Panthers will be back, and they'll be hungry.

Maple Leafs @ Senators

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Canadian Tire Centre, Ottawa, ON
Puck Line
TOR +1.5 (-162) / OTT -1.5 (+136)
Moneyline
TOR +160 / OTT -192
Total
O/U 6.5

The Battle of Ontario has never carried this kind of emotional baggage. Toronto at 32-35-14, 78 points, is in the middle of a 6-game losing streak, eliminated from playoff contention, and watching their season unravel in the most painful way imaginable. Auston Matthews is done for the year with a knee injury sustained on a hit from Radko Gudas, and without their franchise center, the Maple Leafs have completely fallen apart. This is the first time Toronto will miss the playoffs since 2015-16, the very year they drafted Matthews first overall. The symmetry is cruel. The franchise that was supposed to be built around Matthews reaching the promised land is instead watching him in street clothes while the season dies.

Ottawa, meanwhile, is everything Toronto was supposed to be. The Senators clinched a playoff berth on April 2, and they're sitting at 43-27-11, 97 points, in the WC1 spot with a 4-point cushion over Columbus. Tim Stutzle has been magnificent with 34 goals and 49 assists for 83 points, and he needs just 1 more assist to hit 50 for the fourth consecutive season. That kind of consistency from a 24-year-old is special, and Stutzle has become the undisputed leader of this Senators team. Brady Tkachuk is listed as questionable with an illness, and Ottawa fans will be watching the pregame warmup closely to see if their captain takes the ice. Even without Tkachuk, the Sens have the depth and structure to handle a depleted Toronto team.

The -192 moneyline on Ottawa tells the whole story. The Senators are heavy favorites at home, and they should be. This isn't the typical Battle of Ontario where both sides bring equal intensity and the rivalry hatred fuels a tight game. This is a playoff-bound team hosting a broken one, and the gap in quality, motivation, and cohesion is enormous. Toronto's L6 streak isn't just about talent, it's about a team that has mentally checked out, and the absence of Matthews has removed any remaining reason to compete. The Leafs' young players will play hard because their careers depend on it, but the overall product on the ice has been embarrassing for a franchise of Toronto's stature.

Canadian Tire Centre is going to be rocking tonight, and the Senators fans will enjoy every minute of watching their rivals suffer. There's no sugarcoating it. The Toronto-Ottawa rivalry is fueled by mutual disdain, and when one side is up and the other is down, the knife gets twisted. Ottawa's playoff clinch has already made this season a resounding success, and a convincing win over the Maple Leafs on the penultimate night of the regular season would be the cherry on top. For Toronto, the off-season can't come fast enough. The questions about this franchise's direction, their cap structure, their coaching, and whether Matthews will be healthy for next season are all going to dominate the summer conversation. But first, they have to survive 60 minutes in a hostile building where nobody is going to show them any mercy.

Sharks @ Blackhawks

Wednesday, 8:30 PM ET | United Center, Chicago, IL
Puck Line
SJ -1.5 (+195) / CHI +1.5 (-238)
Moneyline
SJ -125 / CHI +105

O/U 6.5

Forget the standings. Forget that both teams are eliminated. This game is about two kids who are going to be the face of the NHL for the next 15 years, and tonight might be the most compelling individual matchup on the entire final week's schedule. Macklin Celebrini sits at 44 goals and 112 points, just 2 points away from Joe Thornton's 2006-07 franchise record of 114 points. He's 19 years old. Let that sink in. A teenager is about to break a record set by a player who is widely regarded as one of the greatest playmakers in hockey history, and he's doing it in his first full NHL season. Nine goals and 16 points in his last 10 games tell you he's not slowing down for anyone.

On the other side, Connor Bedard has quietly put together a season that would be the talk of the league in any other year. Thirty goals and 72 points in 66 games gives him a 1.09 points-per-game average, an absurd number for a 20-year-old on one of the worst teams in the NHL. Bedard's 41 assists show his playmaking growth, and while a 4-game losing streak and 7 points in his last 10 suggest Chicago's overall quality has dragged him down, the individual talent is undeniable. The United Center faithful have watched Bedard develop into a legitimate NHL star, and tonight's head-to-head against Celebrini is the kind of marquee individual matchup that sells tickets and creates memories.

Here's what makes this so special. We're watching the future of hockey play out in real time. Celebrini and Bedard are the two most talented young players on the planet, and when they're on the ice at the same time tonight, you're looking at a preview of what the NHL's premier rivalry could be for the next decade and a half. Celebrini's combination of speed, vision, and finishing ability is something the league hasn't seen from a teenager since Connor McDavid, and the fact that he's 2 points from a franchise record that most people assumed would stand for another generation is extraordinary. The -125 moneyline on San Jose reflects the Sharks' overall edge in roster quality right now, but this game is about individual brilliance, not team depth charts.

If Celebrini gets those 2 points tonight, the celebration will be well-deserved. Joe Thornton's 114-point season came when he was 27, in the prime of his career, on a Sharks team loaded with talent. Celebrini is doing it at 19, on a team that's still building, and his trajectory suggests that this franchise record won't just be broken, it'll eventually be obliterated. But tonight, in this moment, the entire hockey world will be watching to see if the kid can pull it off. Chicago's crowd will appreciate the history they're witnessing, even if it comes at their team's expense. And Bedard will be right there, matching Celebrini shift for shift, reminding everyone that the future of this sport belongs to both of them. Don't miss this one.

Kraken @ Golden Knights

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas, NV
Puck Line
SEA +1.5 (-108) / VGK -1.5 (-112)
Moneyline
SEA +230 / VGK -285
Total
O/U 6.5

Vegas closes out Wednesday's slate at T-Mobile Arena, and the Golden Knights are a fascinating team right now. They just fired Bruce Cassidy and hired John Tortorella, and the early returns have been excellent with a 3-game winning streak under the new coach. Tortorella's intensity, his no-nonsense approach, and his ability to get every ounce of effort out of his roster are exactly what Vegas needed in the final stretch, and the players have responded. At 38-26-17, 93 points, the Golden Knights are clinched but still jockeying for the best possible first-round matchup, and Tortorella won't let this group take its foot off the gas for a single shift.

The Mitch Marner trade has been one of the best moves of the entire season. Since arriving via trade last July, Marner has averaged roughly a point per game in Vegas, posting 77-78 points and proving that a change of scenery was exactly what he needed. Marner's playmaking, his compete level, and his ability to drive possession have given the Golden Knights' top six a dimension they were lacking, and the chemistry between Marner and Jack Eichel (82 points) has been instant. Add in Pavel Dorofeyev's breakout 35-goal, 61-point campaign, and Vegas has the kind of offensive arsenal that can beat anyone in a playoff series. The -285 moneyline is steep, but it's justified for a team playing this well at home with this much talent.

Seattle is wrapping up its third straight season without a playoff berth, and the Kraken's weaknesses have been on full display. The penalty kill at 71.6% is the worst in the NHL, and when you can't kill penalties against a Vegas power play featuring Eichel, Marner, and Dorofeyev, you're going to have a very long night. Jordan Eberle has been a bright spot at 35 years old with 55 points, squeezing every last drop out of a career that has been more productive than people give him credit for. But one veteran having a solid individual season doesn't compensate for the systemic issues that have kept the Kraken out of the postseason picture.

The +230 moneyline on Seattle tells you everything about the talent disparity in this game. T-Mobile Arena is going to be electric as the Golden Knights prepare for a playoff run under their new coach, and Tortorella's emphasis on defensive structure and competitive fire has already transformed this team's identity. Vegas isn't just talented, they're playing with a renewed purpose and discipline that wasn't always there under the previous regime. Seattle will compete, because that's what professional athletes do, but the gap between where these two franchises are right now is significant. This should be a comfortable night for the Golden Knights as they continue building toward what they hope will be a deep postseason run.
