10-Game Sunday Slate

NBA March 8, 2026

Sunday's massive 10-game slate features an ABC doubleheader with the Celtics visiting a devastated Cleveland team missing Mitchell, Allen, and Strus at 1 PM, followed by the Knicks at the Lakers at 3:30 PM with Jalen Brunson facing Luka Doncic. The Rockets visit the 46-17 Spurs, with Kevin Durant facing De'Aaron Fox in San Antonio on NBC Peacock. The league-best 45-16 Pistons visit Miami. Plus Orlando at Milwaukee, Dallas at Toronto, Washington at New Orleans, Indiana at Portland, Chicago at Sacramento, and Charlotte at Phoenix.

Game 1
ABC

Celtics @ Cavaliers

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH
Spread
BOS -1.5
Moneyline
BOS -105 / CLE -115
Total
O/U 223.5

This is supposed to be a showdown between two Eastern Conference heavyweights, but Cleveland's injury report has turned it into something else entirely. Donovan Mitchell is out with a groin injury, Jarrett Allen is sidelined with a knee issue, and Max Strus is dealing with a foot problem that keeps him off the court. That's three key pieces of a 39-24 team gone in one fell swoop, and it fundamentally changes the calculus of this matchup. Cleveland at home still has some fight, but without their leading scorer and their defensive anchor in Allen, the Cavaliers are essentially asking their supporting cast to take on a Celtics team that remains one of the most talented rosters in basketball despite their own injury issues.

Boston at 42-21 isn't at full strength either. Jayson Tatum remains out with the Achilles injury that's defined their season, and Nikola Vucevic is sidelined with a finger issue. But here's the difference: the Celtics have been navigating life without Tatum for months now, and their system, built on ball movement, three-point shooting, and defensive versatility, has continued to function at a high level. Jaylen Brown has shouldered the offensive burden admirably, and Boston's depth has been their saving grace. Derrick White, Jrue Holiday, and the rest of this supporting cast know how to win games without needing a 30-point scorer every night.

The 1.5-point spread feels surprisingly tight for a game where Cleveland is missing this much talent. The respect the market is showing the Cavaliers is rooted in their home-court advantage at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, where they've been excellent this season, and the fact that Boston has been inconsistent on the road. But Mitchell's absence alone should be worth more than 1.5 points, and the Allen loss compounds the problem by removing Cleveland's rim protection and interior presence. The 223.5 total reflects the expectation that both depleted rosters will struggle to generate elite offense, and it's a reasonable number given the circumstances.

The ABC afternoon window gives this game national visibility, and the storyline is clear: can Cleveland's depth hold up against a Celtics team that's battle-tested and experienced in navigating adversity? Darius Garland will need to be spectacular as the primary creator, and the Cavaliers' bench will need to punch above its weight class. Boston's defensive length and versatility should be able to disrupt whatever Cleveland tries to run without Mitchell orchestrating the offense, and the Celtics' experience in big moments gives them an edge in a game where both teams are compromised.

Game 2
ABC

Knicks @ Lakers

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Crypto.com Arena, Los Angeles, CA
Spread
NYK -2.5
Moneyline
NYK -148 / LAL +124
Total
O/U 227.5

The ABC doubleheader saves the best for the late afternoon window, and this one is a genuine star showcase. Luka Doncic leads the entire NBA in scoring at 32.5 points per game, and since arriving in Los Angeles via the blockbuster trade from Dallas, he's transformed the Lakers into a legitimate Western Conference contender at 38-25. Jalen Brunson on the other side is averaging 26.5 points per game for a Knicks team sitting at 41-23, one of the best records in the league. This is a matchup between two elite point-of-attack creators who can carve up defenses from every level of the floor, and the 227.5 total tells you the market expects fireworks.

New York's road credentials are what make them the 2.5-point favorite here. The Knicks have been one of the most consistent teams in the NBA this season regardless of venue, and Brunson's ability to control tempo, get to his spots in the midrange, and create for teammates gives New York an offensive identity that travels well. The Knicks' defense, anchored by their physicality and toughness on the perimeter, should pose problems for a Lakers team that relies heavily on Doncic creating in isolation and pick-and-roll situations. If New York can force the Lakers' secondary options to beat them, the Knicks' depth becomes the decisive factor.

The big question mark for Los Angeles is LeBron James, who's listed as day-to-day with an elbow issue. Even at 41, LeBron's presence on the floor changes everything for the Lakers. He's the connective tissue of their offense, the veteran who keeps the defense organized, and the player who can take over in crunch time when the game tightens. If he sits, the Lakers become a Luka Doncic one-man show offensively, which is formidable but not enough against a Knicks team with this much talent. If LeBron plays, even at less than 100%, the Lakers have the kind of star power that can swing any game.

This has all the ingredients of a game that lives up to the ABC national TV billing. Two passionate fanbases, two elite lead guards going head-to-head, and playoff implications for both teams. The Knicks want to keep climbing for the top seed in the East, while the Lakers are trying to solidify their standing in a brutally competitive Western Conference. The atmosphere at Crypto.com Arena should be electric, especially if LeBron suits up, and the Doncic-Brunson duel should be appointment television regardless of outcome.

Game 3

Pistons @ Heat

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Kaseya Center, Miami, FL
Spread
DET -1.5
Moneyline
DET -120 / MIA EVEN
Total
O/U 229.5

The best record in the NBA belongs to Detroit, and that's still a sentence that stops people in their tracks. The Pistons at 45-16 have been the story of the 2025-26 season, and Cade Cunningham has been the engine driving the most remarkable turnaround in recent NBA history. But Cunningham is listed as day-to-day with a quad issue, and if he sits, this game shifts dramatically. Without Cunningham's 25.2 points, 9.8 assists, and 5.8 rebounds per game, the Pistons lose their primary creator and the heartbeat of their offense. Detroit has enough depth to compete without him, but the margin for error shrinks considerably on the road against a desperate Miami team.

The Heat are dealing with their own injury woes. Norman Powell is out, and Terry Rozier is sidelined as well, leaving Miami with a depleted backcourt that limits their offensive versatility. But Miami's culture under Erik Spoelstra always seems to find a way to extract maximum effort from whoever is available. The Heat play hard at home at Kaseya Center, and their defensive intensity doesn't fluctuate based on the personnel. If Cunningham sits, this becomes a genuine toss-up between two short-handed teams, which makes the 1.5-point spread and the even money on Miami's moneyline look very reasonable.

The 229.5 total is one of the higher numbers on Sunday's board, and it suggests the market expects both teams to score freely despite the injury absences. Miami's pace tends to slow down at home, but Detroit's offensive firepower, even without Cunningham, can push the tempo and create transition opportunities. The Pistons have been excellent at sharing the ball this season, and their collective offensive approach means they don't collapse when one player is out. But the road environment, the Heat's defensive pride, and the potential Cunningham absence all combine to make this a much more competitive game than the season records would suggest.

Detroit's -1.5 road spread is remarkably small for the league's best team, and it reflects both the Cunningham uncertainty and the respect the market has for Spoelstra's Heat at home. Miami has been a team that plays up to competition all season, and a primetime spot against the league's best record is exactly the kind of game where Spoelstra's preparation and the Heat's competitive DNA make them dangerous. This one could go either way, and the tight spread reflects that uncertainty perfectly.

Game 4

Mavericks @ Raptors

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena, Toronto, ON
Spread
TOR -9.5
Moneyline
TOR -425 / DAL +330
Total
O/U 229.5

Dallas has been gutted. That's the only word for it. Kyrie Irving is done for the season, Dereck Lively is out for the year, and Cooper Flagg, the prized rookie, is day-to-day with a foot issue that could sideline him tonight. The Mavericks went all-in on a championship window that slammed shut when they traded Doncic to the Lakers, and the roster that remains is a collection of developmental pieces and placeholder veterans trying to navigate the final months of a lost season. Walking into Scotiabank Arena as 9.5-point underdogs is the reality of where this franchise currently stands.

Toronto has Brandon Ingram now, acquired from the Pelicans, and his presence gives the Raptors a legitimate go-to scorer who can create his own shot and elevate the offense. Ingram is listed as day-to-day with an illness, so his availability matters, but even without him, Toronto has built a roster that's competitive and deep enough to handle a depleted Dallas team. Scottie Barnes continues to develop into a franchise cornerstone, and the Raptors' home-court advantage at Scotiabank Arena has been significant this season. The crowd feeds off the energy of a team that's finally trending in the right direction.

The -425 moneyline on Toronto tells you this game is essentially a foregone conclusion on the betting board, and the 9.5-point spread feels right for a matchup between a team building toward something and a team that's been stripped for parts. Dallas doesn't have the shot creation to stay with Toronto over 48 minutes, and their defense has been porous all season without the defensive infrastructure that Lively provided inside. The 229.5 total is moderate, reflecting the likelihood that Toronto controls the pace and doesn't need to push into the 120s to win comfortably.

If Flagg plays, he provides the Mavericks with their most exciting young talent and a reason for fans to tune in, but one rookie, no matter how talented, can't overcome the roster disparity in this matchup. Toronto's depth, home-court advantage, and defensive versatility should allow them to build a lead early and maintain it throughout. This has garbage-time-in-the-fourth-quarter written all over it.

Game 5

Wizards @ Pelicans

Sunday, 7:00 PM ET | Smoothie King Center, New Orleans, LA
Spread
NOP -9.5
Moneyline
NOP -440 / WAS +340
Total
O/U 242.5

The 242.5 total is the highest on Sunday's entire board, and it tells you everything you need to know about what to expect from this game. Neither of these teams is going to stop anybody. Washington and New Orleans are both in the lottery conversation, both prioritizing development over results, and both playing with the kind of loose, up-tempo style that leads to high-scoring affairs with minimal defensive resistance. This is a game for the scoreboard watchers and the totals bettors, because both teams are going to get up and down the floor and trade buckets all night.

New Orleans is the 9.5-point home favorite, which reflects their edge in talent more than any defensive superiority. The Pelicans have some interesting young pieces who can score in bunches, and playing at the Smoothie King Center gives them a comfort level that Washington won't have on the road. The Wizards have been one of the worst teams in the NBA this season, and their road record is particularly dismal. They lack a true go-to scorer who can take over a game, and their defense has been among the league's worst in terms of points allowed per game.

The 9.5-point spread is significant for a game between two teams at the bottom of the standings, but home-court advantage tends to be magnified in matchups between bad teams. New Orleans has more talent, more motivation at home, and better offensive weaponry to exploit a Washington defense that doesn't have the personnel to stop anyone consistently. The Wizards might keep it interesting in the first half, but over 48 minutes, New Orleans' edge in shot creation and depth should assert itself.

This isn't going to be featured on any highlight reels or end-of-season montages, but for the pace-and-space enthusiasts and anyone looking at over/under angles, it's one of the more interesting games on the board. The 242.5 total is massive, but these two teams have the offensive capability and defensive deficiency to push past it without breaking a sweat. Expect a lot of possessions, a lot of points, and not a lot of defense.

Game 6

Magic @ Bucks

Sunday, 8:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee, WI
Spread
ORL -3.5
Moneyline
ORL -155 / MIL +130
Total
O/U 221.5

Orlando is a road favorite in Milwaukee, and that reality speaks volumes about where both franchises are right now. The Magic have established themselves as one of the more disciplined defensive teams in the NBA, and their structure on that end of the floor carries them in games where the offense isn't flowing. Franz Wagner being out with a high ankle sprain hurts their ceiling offensively, but Orlando's defense-first identity means they can win ugly without needing 115 points. Paolo Banchero carries the offensive load, and his ability to create in the post and from the midrange gives Orlando a reliable half-court option against any defense.

Milwaukee's season has been a profound disappointment, and the Bucks are now in a position where they're the underdog at home against a team that would have been a routine win two years ago. Taurean Prince being out further thins an already depleted supporting cast around Giannis Antetokounmpo, and the Bucks' inability to surround their franchise player with consistent contributors has been the story of their season. Giannis at 27.6 points and 9.9 rebounds is still a force of nature, but one player, no matter how dominant, can't overcome a roster that lacks shooting, depth, and defensive consistency.

The 221.5 total is the lowest on Sunday's board, and it's a reflection of two teams whose strengths lie more on the defensive end. Orlando's defensive rating has been elite this season, and even without Wagner, they have the length and physicality to make life difficult for Milwaukee's offense. The Bucks' defense has been inconsistent, but Giannis's individual presence at the rim provides enough deterrence to keep the game from becoming a track meet. This should be a grind-it-out affair where every possession matters and the team that gets more defensive stops wins.

There's something poignant about watching Giannis labor through a season like this. He's one of the five best players on the planet, and his effort level never wavers, but the supporting cast simply isn't good enough to turn individual excellence into team success. Orlando's collective approach, their defensive discipline, and Banchero's continued growth as a scorer make them the right side in this matchup, even on the road.

NBC/Peacock

Rockets @ Spurs

Sunday, 8:00 PM ET | Frost Bank Center, San Antonio, TX
Spread
SAS -5.5
Moneyline
SAS -218 / HOU +180
Total
O/U 222.5

This is a Texas showdown with legitimate playoff implications, and the NBC Peacock audience gets a treat. The Spurs at 46-17 have the second-best record in the entire NBA, and De'Aaron Fox has been the catalyst since arriving from Sacramento. Fox's speed, finishing ability, and playmaking have given San Antonio the dynamic lead guard they've needed to complement Victor Wembanyama's generational defensive talent. The Spurs are on a three-game winning streak, and their home-court advantage at Frost Bank Center has been one of the most formidable in the league this season. San Antonio's defense, anchored by Wembanyama's rim protection and Fox's on-ball pressure, is suffocating when it's clicking.

Houston at 39-23 is a playoff team in their own right, and Kevin Durant has been outstanding since arriving in Houston. Durant's scoring versatility, his ability to get a bucket from anywhere on the floor at any time, gives the Rockets an offensive weapon that can compete with anyone in a half-court setting. But Houston is missing Fred VanVleet for the season with a torn ACL, and that loss has left a hole in their backcourt that hasn't been fully addressed. Without VanVleet's ball-handling, shooting, and defensive toughness, the Rockets' offense can become one-dimensional, leaning too heavily on Durant's individual brilliance.

The 5.5-point spread reflects San Antonio's home dominance and their overall superiority this season. Scottie Barnes is out for the Spurs along with Mason Plumlee, but the depth of talent in San Antonio's rotation means those absences are manageable. The Spurs have the kind of defensive identity that can contain Durant in ways that most teams cannot, and Wembanyama's length and shot-blocking ability force opposing scorers into difficult looks. Durant will get his points because he always does, but San Antonio's team defense can limit the supporting cast and win the battle of the rest-of-roster.

The 222.5 total is modest, and it's a nod to both teams' defensive capabilities. This should be a physical, competitive game where possessions are contested and points are earned rather than given. Fox vs. Durant is one of those matchups that feels like it should be on a bigger stage, and NBC Peacock subscribers who tune in should be rewarded with high-level basketball between two teams that have championship aspirations. San Antonio's three-game streak, their home record, and their defensive excellence make them the clear favorite, but Houston has the star power to keep it close.

Sunday, 9:00 PM ET | Moda Center, Portland, OR
Spread
POR -8.5
Moneyline
POR -360 / IND +285
Total
O/U 236.5

Indiana's season has been defined by one devastating injury. Tyrese Haliburton tore his Achilles during the 2025 NBA Finals against the Thunder, and the Pacers have been a different team without their All-Star point guard. Now they're also missing Ivica Zubac, and Pascal Siakam is day-to-day, which leaves Indiana with a roster that's barely recognizable from the group that made the Finals just months ago. Walking into Portland as 8.5-point underdogs is the consequence of losing your franchise player and watching the supporting cast deteriorate around the void he left.

Portland being an 8.5-point home favorite is a testament to how far this Trail Blazers team has come. The Blazers have young, exciting talent that can score in bunches, and their home-court advantage at Moda Center has been a factor all season. Portland plays with the kind of youthful energy and pace that can overwhelm depleted teams, and Indiana without Haliburton, potentially without Siakam, and without Zubac simply doesn't have the offensive firepower to keep pace. The 236.5 total is one of the higher numbers on the board, and it tells you the market expects a fast-paced game where defense is optional.

The -360 moneyline on Portland is steep, reflecting the significant talent gap in this matchup. Indiana's defense has been among the worst in the league this season, partly due to the constant roster shuffling caused by injuries, and Portland's offense can exploit those weaknesses with pace, transition buckets, and three-point shooting. The Pacers might have some fun moments from their young players getting extended minutes, but the overall quality of the roster simply can't compete with a Portland team that has more talent, more depth, and home-court advantage.

For Indiana, this is the kind of game where the front office is evaluating who can contribute moving forward and who is simply filling minutes. The season has become about development and draft positioning, not wins and losses. Portland should handle this one comfortably, and the only question is whether they cover the 8.5-point number or let off the gas in the fourth quarter.

Game 9

Bulls @ Kings

Sunday, 9:00 PM ET | Golden 1 Center, Sacramento, CA
Spread
CHI -2.5
Moneyline
CHI -155 / SAC +130
Total
O/U 234.5

Sacramento's season has turned into a nightmare. Zach LaVine, acquired from Chicago, is done for the season with a hand injury. Domantas Sabonis is out for the year with a torn meniscus. Dejounte Murray is sidelined as well. Three of the Kings' best players are gone, and what's left is a team that's playing out the string with a depleted roster that can't compete with most NBA opponents. The irony of the Bulls coming to town, the same franchise that traded LaVine to Sacramento, is not lost on anyone. LaVine was supposed to be the piece that put the Kings over the top, and instead his season ended before it could reach its full potential.

Chicago at -2.5 on the road tells you how severely the Kings have been impacted by injuries. The Bulls aren't world-beaters, but they have a functional roster with players who can fill roles and compete. Being a road favorite in Sacramento, a building that was rocking with energy just two years ago during the Kings' playoff push, shows how far this franchise has fallen in a short period. Chicago's defense gives them a foundation to win games even when the offense isn't spectacular, and against a Sacramento team that's missing its three best offensive players, the Bulls should be able to control the tempo and execute their game plan without much resistance.

The 234.5 total is elevated despite Sacramento's offensive limitations, and it suggests the market expects a fast-paced game where neither team is invested in grinding out stops. The Kings' young players will shoot with freedom since there's nothing at stake, and Chicago's defense may not bring its full intensity against a non-threatening opponent. This has the feel of a game where both teams play loose, the score gets high, and the outcome is never really in doubt for the Bulls.

Sacramento's front office has to be looking at this season as a reset. The LaVine trade, the Sabonis injury, Murray's absence, it's all added up to a campaign that's been derailed beyond repair. Chicago will take the win and move on, while Sacramento turns its attention to the draft and whatever roster construction changes are coming this summer.

Hornets @ Suns

Sunday, 10:00 PM ET | Footprint Center, Phoenix, AZ
Spread
CHA -4.5
Moneyline
CHA -192 / PHX +160
Total
O/U 218.5

Charlotte being a 4.5-point road favorite in Phoenix captures everything about where the Suns are right now. After the Suns traded Kevin Durant to the Rockets, the franchise entered full rebuild mode, and the results have been predictably rough. Dillon Brooks is out with a broken hand, and Jalen Williams is sidelined as well, leaving the Suns with a roster that's thin on talent and short on shot creation. The Footprint Center, once one of the loudest buildings in the NBA during the KD-Booker era, has become a much quieter place as the Suns navigate the early stages of a rebuild that could take years.

Charlotte is relatively healthy by comparison, and that health advantage is reflected in the 4.5-point spread. The Hornets have young, talented players who can score, defend, and compete on both ends, and their roster construction gives them a clear edge in overall talent against a Phoenix team that's been stripped of its best players. LaMelo Ball's ability to create offense in transition and half-court sets, combined with the Hornets' athleticism on the wing, should be more than enough to handle a Suns team that doesn't have the firepower to keep pace.

The 218.5 total is the lowest on Sunday's entire board, and it reflects two teams that will struggle to generate consistent offense. Phoenix's scoring has cratered without their top options, and Charlotte, while more talented, doesn't have the kind of offensive juggernaut that pushes totals into the 230s. This should be a lower-possession game where defense plays a prominent role, and the Hornets' edge in shot creation should be the difference in a game that stays in the low-to-mid 200s.

There's not much drama in this one. Charlotte has more talent, more health, and more offensive versatility than a Suns team that's playing for draft positioning and development reps. The only question is whether the Hornets take care of business by the 4.5-point spread or let the game stay closer than it should through inconsistent effort. The late Sunday window in Phoenix tends to produce strange results from time to time, but the talent gap here is wide enough that Charlotte should cover without much trouble.
