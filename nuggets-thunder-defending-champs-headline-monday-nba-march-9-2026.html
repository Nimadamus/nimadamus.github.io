5-Game Monday Slate

NBA March 9, 2026

Monday's five-game slate is headlined by a blockbuster showdown in Oklahoma City as Nikola Jokic and the Nuggets visit the defending champion Thunder on Peacock. SGA's 31.7 points per game meets Jokic's triple-double machine at 28.8/12.5/10.3 in what could be a Western Conference playoff preview. The 76ers visit the Cavaliers as 11.5-point underdogs, the Knicks close out their West Coast trip in LA against the Clippers on Peacock, and the Warriors visit Utah while the Grizzlies battle the Nets in Brooklyn.

Game 1

76ers @ Cavaliers

Monday, 7:00 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH
Spread
CLE -11.5
Moneyline
CLE -520 / PHI +390
Total
O/U 226.5

An 11.5-point spread in a game between two teams with winning records is absolutely staggering, and it tells you everything about how the market views Philadelphia right now. The 76ers at 34-29 are technically in the playoff picture, but their season has been a revolving door of injuries, inconsistency, and frustration. Joel Embiid's availability has been a constant question mark, and the 76ers' inability to string together a run of healthy, competitive basketball has left them dangling at the edge of the play-in conversation rather than contending for a top seed the way the preseason projections suggested.

Cleveland at 39-25 is a different animal entirely at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. The Cavaliers have been one of the better home teams in the Eastern Conference, and their combination of size, shooting, and defensive versatility makes them a nightmare matchup for a Philadelphia team that has lacked cohesion all year. Donovan Mitchell anchors the offense with his scoring explosiveness, and Cleveland's depth throughout the rotation gives them options that Philly simply can't match on a nightly basis. The Cavs' defensive structure, built around rim protection and switching on the perimeter, is designed to take away the things that make Philadelphia's offense click when healthy.

The -520 moneyline is as steep as it gets in a regular season NBA game, and it reflects a market that sees this as close to a foregone conclusion. Philadelphia might keep it respectable early, but over 48 minutes, Cleveland's home-court advantage, depth, and defensive identity should be too much. The 226.5 total is moderate, suggesting a game where Cleveland controls tempo and doesn't need to get into a shootout to cover comfortably. The Cavaliers should be able to dictate the pace, force Philadelphia into tough half-court possessions, and pull away in the second half.

For the 76ers, games like this one define their season. They're fighting for playoff positioning, and walking into Cleveland as double-digit underdogs exposes just how far this team has fallen from the championship expectations that surrounded them in October. If Philadelphia can't compete in environments like this, the play-in tournament is going to be a short stay. Cleveland should handle this one with authority.

Game 2

Grizzlies @ Nets

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | Barclays Center, Brooklyn, NY
Spread
MEM -1.5
Moneyline
MEM -112 / BKN -108
Total
O/U 220.5

This might be the most depressing game on Monday's board, and that's saying something. Memphis at 23-39 and Brooklyn at 16-47 are both deep into lottery territory, and the injuries have made matters exponentially worse. Ja Morant is out with an elbow injury, stripping the Grizzlies of their most electric player and the primary reason casual fans would tune in. Zach Edey is done for the season with an ankle injury, removing the rim protection and interior scoring that Memphis desperately needs. Multiple other Grizzlies are also sidelined, turning this into a showcase of G League-adjacent talent trying to prove they belong on an NBA roster.

Brooklyn's 16-47 record speaks for itself. The Nets are in full teardown mode, and every game at this point is about evaluating young talent and getting the most favorable draft positioning possible. Their roster is a patchwork of development projects and journeyman veterans, and the Barclays Center has become one of the quieter buildings in the NBA as the franchise navigates the post-Durant, post-Kyrie era with no clear star to build around. The Nets play hard under their coaching staff, but effort alone can't overcome the talent deficit they face on a nightly basis.

The 1.5-point spread and essentially even moneylines tell you this game is a pure coin flip. Memphis has slightly more talent on paper, even with all the injuries, but neither team has the kind of roster that inspires confidence. The 220.5 total is on the lower end, which makes sense for two teams that will struggle to generate quality offense without their best players. This is a game that will feature a lot of turnovers, contested jumpers, and possessions that don't lead to quality shots. The pace will likely be chaotic rather than deliberate.

For draft lottery purposes, both teams have an incentive to lose, though the players on the floor will obviously compete. Memphis's edge, if you can call it that, is having slightly more organizational infrastructure and coaching stability. But in a game between two teams combining for 39 wins, anything can happen, and the 1.5-point line reflects the total uncertainty of this matchup. This is a game you watch if you're scouting young talent, not if you're looking for high-level basketball.

Game 3
NBCSN/Peacock

Nuggets @ Thunder

Monday, 7:30 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK
Spread
OKC -7.5
Moneyline
OKC -290 / DEN +235
Total
O/U 231.5

This is the game of the night, and it's not even close. The defending NBA champion Thunder at 50-15 host the Nuggets at 39-25 in a matchup that features two of the most transcendent talents in basketball going head-to-head. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, averaging a blistering 31.7 points, 4.4 rebounds, and 6.5 assists per game, has cemented himself as one of the most unstoppable offensive forces in the league. On the other side, Nikola Jokic is doing Nikola Jokic things at 28.8 points, 12.5 rebounds, and 10.3 assists, operating as the most complete offensive player in NBA history and making a strong case for another MVP trophy. When these two share the floor, you're watching generational basketball.

Oklahoma City's 50-15 record is the best in the NBA, and their 26-6 home record at Paycom Center makes them a fortress. The Thunder have already won both prior meetings against Denver this season, and their combination of SGA's offensive brilliance, elite defensive depth, and young, hungry contributors makes them the gold standard in the Western Conference. The Thunder's defense is suffocating when healthy, though the injury report raises some questions. Jalen Williams is out, Chet Holmgren is questionable, Isaiah Hartenstein is sidelined, and Alex Caruso is questionable. That's significant missing talent on the defensive end, particularly Holmgren's rim protection, and it could narrow the gap between these two teams considerably.

Denver at 39-25 with a 22-12 road record has been one of the better traveling teams in the league, and Jokic's ability to orchestrate the offense against any defense gives the Nuggets a floor that most teams can't match. The question mark is Jamal Murray, who's listed as questionable with an ankle issue. Murray's presence alongside Jokic is what elevates Denver from a good team to a championship-caliber one. Without Murray, the Nuggets' half-court offense becomes more predictable, and OKC's defensive length can key in on Jokic and force the supporting cast to beat them. If Murray plays, Denver has the firepower to keep this game competitive throughout and potentially challenge the 7.5-point spread.

The 231.5 total is the highest on Monday's board, and it reflects the offensive talent on both sides. SGA and Jokic are going to get their numbers regardless of the defensive effort, and the supporting casts have enough shooting and creation to push the score into the 115-120 range on both sides. This is national television basketball at its finest, a showcase of the league's present and future, and the Peacock audience should be treated to a high-level Western Conference showdown. OKC's home dominance and overall record make them the clear favorite, but Jokic has a way of making any game competitive through sheer basketball genius.

Game 4

Warriors @ Jazz

Monday, 9:00 PM ET | Delta Center, Salt Lake City, UT
Spread
GSW -5.5
Moneyline
GSW -205 / UTA +170
Total
O/U 226.5

Golden State at 32-31 is the definition of a team on the bubble, and every game from here to the end of the season carries playoff-or-bust implications. The Warriors are clinging to play-in tournament positioning, and a trip to Salt Lake City to face the 21-43 Jazz should be a game they circle as a must-win. Stephen Curry remains the engine of everything Golden State does offensively, and his ability to stretch defenses with his gravitational pull from beyond the arc creates space for the Warriors' secondary options. But the roster around Curry has question marks, and Golden State's inconsistency has been the story of their season.

Jimmy Butler's presence on the roster, acquired from Miami, was supposed to be the missing piece that elevated Golden State back into championship contention. Instead, Butler is out for the season with a torn ACL, and the Warriors have had to adjust their plans and identity without him. That injury robbed Golden State of the defensive toughness, secondary scoring, and playoff experience that Butler was supposed to provide, and it's a big reason why the Warriors are fighting for a play-in spot rather than a top-six seed. The Warriors still have Curry, and Curry alone can carry any team on a given night, but the supporting cast needs to step up consistently for Golden State to close out the regular season on a positive note.

Utah at 21-43 is firmly in tank mode, and the Jazz's priority is development and draft positioning, not wins. Lauri Markkanen remains the Jazz's most talented player, and his shooting ability gives Utah a competitive element on offense even in games where the overall talent gap is significant. But the Jazz lack the depth, defensive cohesion, and overall roster quality to compete with a Warriors team that has Curry and playoff urgency. The Delta Center crowd has been watching a rebuilding product all season, and this game should follow the pattern of most Jazz home games against competent opponents: competitive early, separation by halftime, and a comfortable Warriors lead by the fourth quarter.

The 5.5-point spread feels about right for a game between a desperate play-in contender and a team that's mailing it in. Golden State's motivation edge alone should be worth a few points, and Curry's ability to take over a game against Utah's defense, which ranks among the worst in the league, makes the Warriors the clear side here. The 226.5 total is moderate, reflecting the Warriors' inconsistent offense and the Jazz's limited scoring punch without a fully healthy roster. If Curry has one of his signature shooting nights, this game could get out of hand quickly.

Game 5
NBCSN/Peacock

Knicks @ Clippers

Monday, 10:00 PM ET | Intuit Dome, Inglewood, CA
Spread
NYK -2.5
Moneyline
NYK -135 / LAC +114
Total
O/U 220.5

The Knicks close out their West Coast road trip in Inglewood against a Clippers team that's fighting for its postseason life. New York at 41-24 has been one of the most consistent teams in basketball this season, and Jalen Brunson's ability to control the pace of a game, get to his spots in the midrange, and create for teammates gives the Knicks an offensive identity that travels remarkably well. The Knicks' defense has been elite, built on physicality, toughness on the perimeter, and a collective effort that makes them one of the harder teams in the NBA to score against. Even on the second night of a back-to-back road swing, New York's identity doesn't change.

The Clippers at 31-32 are right on the play-in bubble, and every game carries enormous weight for a franchise that moved into the brand-new Intuit Dome with championship aspirations. Those aspirations have been tempered by a season of inconsistency, and the Clippers find themselves in a dogfight for postseason positioning in a stacked Western Conference. Without Kawhi Leonard for most of the year, the Clippers have leaned on James Harden's playmaking and the collective effort of their supporting cast. Harden has been solid as the primary creator, but the Clippers lack the defensive anchor and two-way star that Leonard provides when healthy, and it shows in their inability to win close games against top-tier opponents.

The 2.5-point spread in favor of the visiting Knicks is a testament to how the market views these two rosters. New York is simply the better team, and their road credentials back that up. The Knicks win on the road because their system, their defense, and their star player don't need a home crowd to function at a high level. The Clippers need the Intuit Dome crowd energy to close the gap, and the 10:00 PM start time gives them the advantage of a late West Coast game where the Knicks might be feeling the fatigue of their road trip.

The 220.5 total is the lowest on Monday's board, and it reflects two teams whose identities are built more on the defensive end. Both teams prefer to play in the half court, control tempo, and grind out possessions rather than getting into track meets. This should be a physical, competitive game that stays close throughout, with possessions being contested on both ends and the final margin determined by which team executes better in the fourth quarter. Brunson vs. Harden is a fascinating matchup of two elite point-of-attack creators, and the Peacock late-night audience should be treated to a competitive, intense finish to Monday's NBA action.
