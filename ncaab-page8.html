Light Monday Slate

No ranked matchups on the board today. The college basketball schedule picks back up Tuesday with several marquee games. Today's slate features mostly mid-major and buy-game action, with the best game being Lafayette at Penn in The Palestra.

The Matchup

The best game on Monday's college basketball slate takes place in one of the sport's most historic venues. The Palestra - "The Cathedral of College Basketball" - hosts its first game since 1971 when built, though this version has seen countless classics. Lafayette enters struggling at 3-7, while Penn has been solid at 5-4.

Penn's Edge

The Quakers have been competitive in the Ivy League under coach Steve Donahue. They're averaging 72.4 PPG and have shown the ability to defend, holding opponents to 69.8 PPG. Senior guard Tyler Perkins (14.2 PPG) leads the offense, while Nick Spinoso (11.8 PPG) provides secondary scoring.

Lafayette's Struggles

The Leopards have had a rough start, going 3-7 with losses to mid-majors. Their defense has been porous, allowing 76.4 PPG. Leo O'Boyle (15.6 PPG) is the main offensive threat, but Lafayette lacks depth and consistent shooting.

Betting Trends

  • Penn is 6-3 ATS this season
  • Lafayette is 4-6 ATS
  • Under is 5-4 in Penn games
  • Penn is 4-1 at The Palestra
  • Lafayette is 1-4 on the road

The Angle

Penn at home in The Palestra is always tough. The 8.5-point spread is reasonable given the venue advantage and Lafayette's road woes. The total of 152.5 reflects two teams that prefer slower-paced basketball. Penn should handle this one, but covering 8.5 on a Monday night isn't a gimme.

The Matchup

A typical buy game for the Longhorns as they host Southern from the SWAC. Texas at 6-3 has had an inconsistent start under Rodney Terry, but the talent gap is substantial. Southern (4-4) has been competitive in the SWAC but faces a massive step up in competition.

Texas Talent

The Longhorns' roster features several high-profile recruits and transfers. Tre Johnson, the highly-touted freshman, has been adjusting to the college game with 16.2 PPG. Arthur Kaluma provides veteran leadership with 12.8 PPG and 6.4 RPG. Texas has the athletes to overwhelm Southern.

Betting Trends

  • Texas is 4-5 ATS this season (struggling to cover big numbers)
  • Southern is 5-3 ATS
  • Texas is 3-1 ATS at home
  • Under is 5-4 in Texas games

The Angle

The 24.5-point spread is massive, and Texas has struggled to cover big numbers this season. They let Incarnate Word hang around, only winning by 18 when favored by 30. Southern's competitiveness in the SWAC suggests they won't completely roll over. The under at 155.5 might be the play if Texas controls pace.

The Matchup

Mississippi Valley State enters Manhattan with a brutal 1-8 record. The Delta Devils are one of the weakest programs in Division I, while Kansas State (5-4) is looking to build confidence before Big 12 play heats up. Jerome Tang's Wildcats should dominate.

Kansas State's Season

The Wildcats have been up and down after making the Elite Eight two seasons ago. Dug McDaniel (transfer from Michigan) has been solid at point guard, while David N'Guessan (12.4 PPG, 7.2 RPG) provides interior presence. This is a get-right game before a brutal Big 12 schedule.

Betting Trends

  • K-State is 5-4 ATS this season
  • MVSU is 3-6 ATS
  • K-State is 4-1 at home
  • Under is 6-3 in K-State games

The Angle

The 28.5-point spread is enormous but justified given the talent gap. MVSU is one of the worst teams in college basketball. K-State should cruise here - the question is whether they stay engaged long enough to cover. Games like this often get sloppy in the second half.
