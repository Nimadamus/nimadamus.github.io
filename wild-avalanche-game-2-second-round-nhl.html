Wild @ Avalanche

Tuesday, 8:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO

The Minnesota Wild visit Ball Arena Tuesday night for Game 2 of the Western Conference second round, a bounceback spot after Sunday's 9-6 loss in Game 1 that sits as one of the highest-scoring playoff openers in years. Colorado opens as a heavy -265 moneyline favorite, which translates to an implied probability around 72.6 percent, with the puck line at -1.5 priced around +131. The line shape reflects both the home-ice advantage at Ball Arena and the Avalanche's structural identity as the offense-first powerhouse that defined the regular season - the 9-6 Game 1 score was on the high end of the over-under projection but was not surprising given the way both teams played in the late regular season.

Colorado's structural identity is built around Nathan MacKinnon's primary scoring profile and the supporting cast that has been the franchise's championship-window template across the Cale Makar era. MacKinnon's playoff goal-scoring and assist numbers anchor the half-ice power-play geometry, and Makar's puck-moving from the back end provides the transition-creation that turns defensive-zone retrievals into rush chances. Mikko Rantanen's wing scoring complements the MacKinnon line, and the depth scoring from Artturi Lehkonen, Valeri Nichushkin, and the Ross Colton-led third line absorbed the kind of variance windows that show up in playoff games. The Game 1 9-6 score reflected both Colorado's offensive ceiling and the kind of penalty-driven open-ice geometry that the home team punished in Sunday's win.

Minnesota's path to a Game 2 win starts with goaltending. The Wild gave up nine goals in Game 1, the kind of structural breakdown that does not match the season-long defensive identity Minnesota has built under coach John Hynes. Filip Gustavsson is the projected starter and the structural variable in the bounceback geometry - the Wild need a goaltending performance closer to a save percentage in the 0.920 range to compete in a Game 2 road environment against the Avalanche power play. Kirill Kaprizov anchors the offense as the franchise's primary scorer with the kind of playoff-shooting profile that has been the structural piece of Minnesota's identity in the postseason, and Mats Zuccarello, Marcus Foligno, and the Joel Eriksson Ek-led line provide the supporting cast scoring. Matt Boldy and Marco Rossi round out the rotation. The Wild's road playoff record across the regular season was the kind of competitive bench-mark that suggests Game 2 will be tighter than the 9-6 Game 1 score implies, but the line shape reflects the home favorite's structural edges. Puck drop is 8:00 PM ET on ESPN.
