6-Game Wednesday Slate

NBA February 25, 2026

Wednesday delivers six matchups headlined by the league-leading Pistons hosting an OKC Thunder squad gutted by the absences of SGA and Jalen Williams. Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox bring the Spurs to Toronto, and the late window features Jaylen Brown and the Celtics visiting Nikola Jokic's Nuggets in what should be a spectacular show.

Thunder @ Pistons

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Little Caesars Arena, Detroit, MI
Spread
DET -7
Moneyline
DET -280 / OKC +230
Total
O/U 218.5

This is a fascinating game on paper, but on the court, it could be borderline ugly for the Thunder. OKC rolls into Little Caesars Arena without Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and Jalen Williams, and losing both of those guys simultaneously is the kind of blow that turns a championship-caliber team into something barely recognizable. SGA has been the engine of everything Oklahoma City does offensively, and Williams is their most versatile two-way weapon. Without them, you're asking role players to carry the load against the best team in the NBA by record. That's a brutal ask on a Wednesday night in Detroit.

And make no mistake, Detroit at 42-14 isn't just the best team in the league right now, they're playing with the kind of swagger and confidence that comes from knowing you're the best. Cade Cunningham has been having an MVP-caliber season, orchestrating an offense that moves the ball with purpose and punishes opponents who overhelp or lose focus for even a split second. The Pistons' defensive identity has been their calling card all year, suffocating opponents with length, activity, and a commitment to effort on every possession. They don't beat you with one superstar going nuclear; they beat you with five guys on the floor who all know their roles and execute them at an elite level.

The 218.5 total is the lowest number on tonight's board, and that makes sense when you consider what OKC's offense looks like without its two best players. The Thunder still have depth, guys like Chet Holmgren, Lu Dort, and Isaiah Hartenstein who can contribute, but the shot creation vacuum left by SGA and Williams is enormous. OKC's offense in minutes without those two this season has been significantly worse, and asking that version of the team to go on the road against Detroit's suffocating defense is a recipe for a rock fight. The Pistons should be able to control the tempo, dictate terms on both ends, and grind out a comfortable home victory.

Here's the real question: can OKC keep this close enough to make it interesting? The Thunder's organizational depth and coaching under Mark Daigneault have been legitimately impressive all season, and they've shown an ability to win games they probably shouldn't by maximizing effort and execution from the 6th through 10th guys on the roster. But there's a ceiling to what role players can do against a team as well-coached and defensively intense as Detroit. The Pistons at home with the crowd behind them, against a depleted opponent, on national television? This has all the ingredients for a statement performance from Cunningham and company.

Spurs @ Raptors

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena, Toronto, ON
Spread
SA -7.5
Moneyline
SA -310 / TOR +250
Total
O/U 229.5

San Antonio at 41-17 has been one of the best stories in basketball this season, and the combination of Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox has turned the Spurs into a genuine title contender far ahead of schedule. Fox, who arrived in San Antonio via a blockbuster three-team trade back in February 2025, has been absolutely electric in the Spurs' backcourt, bringing the kind of speed, scoring, and playmaking that gives Wembanyama a running mate capable of generating offense in the half court and in transition. The two-man game between Fox and Wemby has become one of the most devastating pick-and-roll combinations in the league, because how do you defend a 7'4" alien who can shoot from anywhere and a point guard who's one of the fastest players alive?

Wembanyama continues to look like a generational talent in every sense of the word. His shot-blocking, his ability to stretch the floor as a 7'4" center, and his growing comfort as an offensive hub make him the most unique player in basketball. Defensive coordinators around the league have no blueprint for stopping him, because there's never been a player like him before. When you pair that defensive anchor and offensive weapon with Fox's ability to create off the dribble and collapse defenses, the Spurs have an offense that can hurt you from every angle and a defense that revolves around the most intimidating rim protector since prime Rudy Gobert. San Antonio being second in the West behind only Detroit's league-wide pace is a testament to how quickly this pairing has gelled.

Toronto at 34-24 has been a pleasant surprise this season, and a lot of that credit goes to Scottie Barnes and the addition of Brandon Ingram via trade from New Orleans. Ingram has provided the Raptors with a legitimate secondary scorer who can create his own shot in the half court, and his ability to get to the mid-range and finish over smaller defenders has given Toronto an offensive versatility they were missing. The concern tonight is Barnes' health, as he's listed as questionable, and his absence would be a significant blow to a team that relies heavily on his playmaking, rebounding, and defensive versatility to set the tone.

The 7.5-point spread is steep for a road game, but the talent gap is real. San Antonio has two players who can individually take over a game in Fox and Wembanyama, and their supporting cast has been excellent all season. If Barnes sits, this number might even be too low, because Toronto without their best player against a Spurs team firing on all cylinders is a mismatch at virtually every position. Even if Barnes plays, the Raptors would need an exceptional shooting night and some defensive stops against Wemby to keep this competitive. The 229.5 total suggests both teams will push the pace, and San Antonio's ability to score in transition off turnovers and missed shots could make this one get away from Toronto early.

Warriors @ Grizzlies

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | FedExForum, Memphis, TN
Spread
GS -3.5
Moneyline
GS -155 / MEM +132
Total
O/U 227

Welcome to the "neither franchise player is available" game of the night. Stephen Curry is out for Golden State, and Ja Morant is out for Memphis, which transforms what could've been a must-watch individual duel into a fascinating test of supporting casts and depth. Without Curry, the Warriors become a team built around Draymond Green's playmaking, defense, and basketball IQ, plus whatever offensive contributions they can coax out of Andrew Wiggins... wait, Wiggins was traded to the Heat last February in the Jimmy Butler deal. The Warriors' roster construction without Curry is a collection of solid pieces who need someone to elevate them, and that someone isn't walking through the door tonight.

Golden State at 30-27 has been a profoundly inconsistent team this season, capable of looking like a playoff contender one night and a lottery team the next. Curry's availability has been the primary swing factor, and when he's out, the Warriors' offense tends to stagnate into isolation-heavy, low-efficiency possessions. Draymond remains one of the smartest defenders alive, but he can't score enough to punish opponents who load up against the Warriors' other options. The silver lining is that Golden State's defensive identity, anchored by Green's communication and positioning, tends to keep them in games even when the offense is struggling.

Memphis at 21-35 has been one of the most disappointing teams in the league, and Morant's continued health issues have been the defining factor. Without Ja, the Grizzlies lean heavily on Desmond Bane's scoring and Jaren Jackson Jr.'s two-way versatility. Bane has been solid as a secondary option this year, capable of scoring in bunches from the perimeter, but he's not the kind of player who can carry a team night in, night out. Jackson's ability to protect the rim while also stretching the floor on offense gives Memphis a legitimate building block, but the infrastructure around him simply isn't good enough to compete consistently without a healthy Morant running the show.

The 3.5-point spread in Golden State's favor feels right for a game where both teams are missing their most important players. The Warriors have the better overall roster depth and the coaching advantage with Steve Kerr, who's proven time and again that he can scheme competitive performances out of imperfect rosters. Memphis will have the home crowd at FedExForum, and Jackson's interior presence could give Golden State problems if they can't generate enough offense to force the Grizzlies out of their half-court defensive sets. This is going to be a grind-it-out, ugly, competitive game that probably comes down to which team's second and third options have the better shooting night. The 227 total is calibrated for exactly that kind of contest.

Kings @ Rockets

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -14.5
Moneyline
HOU -875 / SAC +575
Total
O/U 223

There's really no gentle way to say this: Sacramento at 13-46 is one of the worst teams in basketball, and this game has the potential to be genuinely painful for anyone wearing purple and white. The Kings' freefall since their franchise point guard was dealt to San Antonio in February 2025 has been staggering, and the Zach LaVine acquisition that was supposed to provide a scoring punch hasn't materialized as hoped since LaVine is out for the season with an injury. Without their franchise point guard and without LaVine, Sacramento is essentially a development team at this point, playing out the string while hoping the ping pong balls bounce their way in the lottery.

Houston at 35-21, on the other hand, is a team with genuine championship aspirations. Kevin Durant, acquired from Phoenix in a blockbuster trade last July, has given the Rockets exactly what they needed: a closer, a floor-raiser, and a player who can score from literally anywhere on the court against any defender the league can throw at him. Durant's presence has elevated everyone around him, particularly the young core of Jalen Green, Alperen Sengun, and Amen Thompson, who are all playing with increased confidence and purpose knowing that KD can bail them out when possessions break down. The Fred VanVleet injury (torn ACL, out for season) has been a significant loss at point guard, but the Rockets have adapted by leaning more heavily on Durant's playmaking.

The 14.5-point spread is one of the largest numbers you'll see in the NBA this season, and while double-digit spreads always carry risk, this one feels earned. Houston is the significantly better team in every measurable way, and they're playing at home in Toyota Center where the energy has been phenomenal all season. Sacramento doesn't have the personnel to slow down Durant in isolation, and they certainly don't have the depth to match Houston's rotation when Ime Udoka goes to his bench. The Kings might have a few young players who flash some potential, but individual bright spots don't translate into competitive basketball against a team this talented.

The 223 total is interesting because you might expect a blowout to push the number higher, but Houston tends to play defense-first basketball that keeps the overall scoring in check even when they're winning by 20. The Rockets' identity under Udoka has always been built on defensive intensity and controlled possessions, and they're unlikely to turn this into a track meet even if Sacramento's defense invites it. Look for Houston to build a lead in the first half through stifling defense and efficient offense, then manage the game through the third and fourth quarters with their depth advantage. This is less about whether Houston wins and more about whether they cover a number that large.

Cavaliers @ Bucks

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee, WI
Spread
CLE -8.5
Moneyline
CLE -375 / MIL +295
Total
O/U 227.5

If you told someone three years ago that Cleveland would be an 8.5-point road favorite at Milwaukee, they would've asked what you were drinking. But here we are. The Cavaliers at 37-22 have been one of the best teams in the Eastern Conference, and the pairing of James Harden and Donovan Mitchell has given Cleveland an offensive arsenal that's borderline unfair. Harden, acquired on February 4, has settled into his role beautifully, providing the kind of half-court orchestration and pick-and-roll mastery that complements Mitchell's aggressive scoring perfectly. When those two are sharing the floor, Cleveland's offense hums with a rhythm and efficiency that very few teams can match.

Mitchell continues to be the heartbeat of this team, and his ability to score from all three levels with equal effectiveness makes him one of the most difficult covers in the league. Harden's arrival has actually made Mitchell even more dangerous, because opponents can no longer sell out to stop him in isolation. Now they have to account for Harden's playmaking, which opens driving lanes and creates catch-and-shoot opportunities that Mitchell feasts on. The chemistry between these two has developed faster than anyone expected, and Cleveland's offensive ceiling with both of them healthy is genuinely terrifying for the rest of the conference.

Milwaukee at 25-31 is in the midst of what can only be described as a franchise crisis. Well below .500 and staring at the possibility of missing the playoffs entirely, the Bucks have been a shell of the team that dominated the Eastern Conference for years. The potential absence of Giannis Antetokounmpo, who is listed as out or questionable, would be the final blow for a team that's already struggling to generate anything resembling competitive basketball. Without Giannis, Milwaukee's offense devolves into Damian Lillard trying to create for himself against loaded defensive schemes, and while Lillard is still a talented scorer, he can't carry this team alone. The supporting cast has been inconsistent at best, and the defensive effort that defined Milwaukee's championship years has completely evaporated.

The 8.5-point spread is enormous for a road team, but Cleveland has earned that level of respect with their play over the last month. If Giannis sits, this number probably should be even higher, because the Bucks without their best player have been one of the worst teams in the league. Even if Giannis plays, he'd have to be at full strength and produce a masterclass to keep Milwaukee within striking distance of a Cavaliers team that's rolling right now. The 227.5 total suggests a moderate-paced game, but Cleveland's offensive firepower could push this higher if Milwaukee's defense continues to perform at the porous levels they've shown recently. This has the look of a game Cleveland controls from start to finish.

Celtics @ Nuggets

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | Ball Arena, Denver, CO
Spread
DEN -4.5
Moneyline
DEN -190 / BOS +158

O/U 227.5

This is the game you circle on the calendar, pour yourself a drink, and settle in for. Nikola Jokic versus Jaylen Brown. Denver's altitude against Boston's depth. Two of the best coaches in basketball squaring off on TNT with the entire basketball world watching. Jokic has been doing Jokic things all season, flirting with a triple-double average in the most casual, effortless way imaginable, and his case for MVP is as strong as it's ever been. His ability to control the game from the center position, orchestrating Denver's offense like a 6'11" point guard while also dominating the glass and scoring with absurd efficiency, is a nightly reminder that we're watching a generational talent at the peak of his powers.

Denver at 36-22 has been excellent at Ball Arena all season, and the altitude factor is real, especially for a Boston team traveling west. Playing at 5,280 feet takes a toll on visiting teams, particularly in the second half when legs start to feel heavy and lungs start to burn. The Nuggets know how to exploit this advantage, pushing the pace in the third and fourth quarters when opponents are most vulnerable. Jamal Murray's health and availability is always the swing factor for Denver, and when he's right, the Murray-Jokic two-man game is one of the most devastating offensive weapons in basketball. Michael Porter Jr. provides the spacing and scoring punch that stretches defenses to their breaking point.

Boston at 38-19 has navigated the Jayson Tatum Achilles injury with remarkable resilience, and that's almost entirely because Jaylen Brown has elevated his game to MVP-level territory. Brown is putting up career-best numbers across the board, scoring efficiently, rebounding at a high rate, and defending the opponent's best perimeter player on a nightly basis. He's proven beyond any reasonable doubt that he can be the number one option on a championship-caliber team, and the Celtics' system under Joe Mazzulla has adapted beautifully to maximize Brown's strengths. The supporting cast, Derrick White, Kristaps Porzingis, and Jrue Holiday, gives Boston one of the deepest and most versatile rosters in the league even without Tatum.

The 4.5-point spread respects Denver's home court advantage while acknowledging that Boston is one of the elite teams in the league regardless of venue. This game will likely be decided in the fourth quarter, when the altitude starts to take its toll and the execution gap between good and great becomes magnified. Jokic will get his numbers, that's a certainty, but Boston's defensive scheme and the versatility of defenders like Brown, White, and Holiday give them as good a chance as anyone at containing Denver's offensive machine. The 227.5 total reflects two teams that play at a deliberate pace and value each possession, making this more of a chess match than a track meet. This is exactly the kind of late-night showcase game that reminds you why you love basketball.
