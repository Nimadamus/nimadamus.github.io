8-Game Opening Week Slate

MLB Previews Today

Opening Week rolls into Day 2 with an absolutely loaded eight-game Friday slate. The defending champion Dodgers host Arizona at Dodger Stadium, the Royals bring Cole Ragans' elite strikeout stuff to Atlanta for a showdown with Chris Sale, the Yankees look to go 2-0 behind rising star Cam Schlittler at Oracle Park, and Framber Valdez makes his highly anticipated Tigers debut in San Diego after signing a $115 million deal.

NYY (1-0) @ SF (0-1)

Friday, 4:35 PM ET | Oracle Park
Run Line
NYY -1.5
Moneyline
NYY -123 / SF +106
Total
O/U 7

The Yankees looked absolutely dominant in their Opening Day 7-0 demolition of the Giants, and they'll try to make it two straight behind Cam Schlittler as they return to Oracle Park for the Friday matinee. That shutout win was a statement from a New York lineup that looks every bit as dangerous as advertised, and Juan Soto's presence in the middle of the order continues to change the entire complexion of every at-bat around him. The Bombers came out swinging on Thursday and showed zero signs of early-season rust.

Schlittler gets the ball for Game 2, and while he's still establishing himself at the big league level, the Yankees' front office clearly trusts the young arm in a high-profile early-season spot. He'll face a Giants lineup that looked completely overmatched on Opening Day, mustering zero runs and struggling to generate any consistent hard contact. San Francisco's bats need to wake up in a hurry, because falling into an 0-2 hole against this Yankees team would set a grim tone for the series.

Robbie Ray takes the mound for San Francisco, and the left-hander needs a big bounce-back performance after the Giants' offense gave him absolutely nothing to work with in the opener. Ray has the stuff to keep hitters honest with his fastball-slider combination, but he'll need pinpoint command against a Yankees lineup that doesn't chase much and punishes mistakes. The Oracle Park dimensions should help him somewhat, as the spacious outfield can turn some of those Yankee line drives into flyouts.

This total sitting at just 7 is fascinating given the offensive firepower in New York's lineup, but Oracle Park has always been a pitcher's haven and both starters have the ability to miss bats. The question is whether San Francisco can muster any offense at all after getting blanked on Opening Day, or if the Yankees' pitching staff continues to dominate a Giants team that may be in for a long 2026. Keep an eye on the wind conditions at Oracle Park, because they can completely transform the run-scoring environment from one day to the next.

OAK (0-0) @ TOR (0-0)

Friday, 7:07 PM ET | Rogers Centre
Run Line
TOR -1.5
Moneyline
OAK +132 / TOR -160
Total
O/U 8.5

Both teams open their 2026 campaigns in Toronto, and the Blue Jays are heavy home favorites as they welcome the Athletics to Rogers Centre. Toronto's roster underwent a significant makeover this offseason, and the energy in the building should be electric for the home opener. The Blue Jays are looking to prove that last year's World Series appearance wasn't a fluke, and this Oakland squad is the kind of opponent they need to handle convincingly if they want to be taken seriously as contenders again.

Kevin Gausman toes the rubber for Toronto, and the veteran right-hander is exactly the kind of steady presence you want for a home opener. His split-finger fastball remains one of the nastiest pitches in baseball, and he should have a clear edge over an Athletics lineup that's still building toward competitiveness. Gausman's ability to generate swings and misses in the zone makes him a nightmare for a young Oakland team that can be prone to chasing.

Luis Severino gets the start for Oakland, and the former Yankee is trying to reinvent himself after bouncing around in recent seasons. He still has the raw arm talent that made him a star in the Bronx years ago, but consistency has been the issue, and facing a Toronto lineup in a raucous Rogers Centre atmosphere is no easy assignment. The Blue Jays' right-handed hitters could give Severino problems if he can't locate his slider early in counts.

The 8.5 total reflects the expectation that this could turn into an offensive affair, and that makes sense when you consider the Rogers Centre dimensions and Oakland's pitching staff beyond Severino. Toronto's lineup has legitimate power from top to bottom, and if Gausman can keep the A's bats quiet through six innings, the Blue Jays' bullpen should be able to close this out. The gap in talent between these two rosters is significant, and it should show on the scoreboard.

COL (0-0) @ MIA (0-0)

Friday, 7:10 PM ET | LoanDepot Park
Run Line
MIA -1.5
Moneyline
COL +149 / MIA -181
Total
O/U 7.5

Two teams searching for an identity open their seasons in Miami, and the Marlins are significant home favorites for a matchup that features two of the more intriguing pitching storylines in baseball. Neither Colorado nor Miami is expected to contend this season, but these early games matter for player development, organizational confidence, and setting the tone for what both front offices hope will be a year of meaningful progress from their young players.

Sandy Alcantara takes the mound for Miami, and this is the start that Marlins fans have been circling for months. The 2022 NL Cy Young winner has been working his way back from Tommy John surgery, and if he's anywhere close to his pre-injury form, he could dominate this Colorado lineup. Alcantara's sinker-slider combination at its best is borderline unhittable, and a Rockies team away from Coors Field tends to look even more overmatched against elite pitching. This is a massive moment for the franchise.

Kyle Freeland gets the ball for Colorado, and the left-hander has quietly been a reliable innings-eater when healthy. His success has always been tied to generating ground balls and keeping the ball in the yard, and LoanDepot Park's dimensions should be more forgiving than Coors Field. Still, Freeland will need to be at his sharpest because any mistake pitches to Miami's young hitters could end up in the seats, and the Rockies' bullpen depth remains a concern if he can't go deep into the game.

The 7.5 total is one of the lower numbers on the board, and that speaks to the pitching matchup and the venue. LoanDepot Park has always suppressed offense, and with Alcantara's return potentially stealing the show, this game could easily stay under. Colorado's lineup away from the thin Denver air historically sees significant drops in production, and if Alcantara's stuff is sharp, the Rockies could be in for a long, quiet night at the plate.

KC (0-0) @ ATL (0-0)

Friday, 7:15 PM ET | Truist Park
Run Line
KC -1.5
Moneyline
KC -145 / ATL +125
Total
O/U 9

This is one of the best pitching matchups of Opening Week, and it's not even close. Cole Ragans versus Chris Sale at Truist Park is appointment baseball, with two of the most dominant left-handers in the sport going head to head in a game that has massive early-season statement potential. The Royals are road favorites here, which tells you everything about how the market views Kansas City's trajectory after their breakout 2025 campaign. This is a team that believes it belongs among the American League elite, and they're backing it up with how aggressively they're priced.

Ragans has transformed himself into one of the best pitchers in baseball, and his strikeout numbers are absolutely filthy. His slider generates whiffs at an elite rate, and he pairs it with a fastball that plays up because of his deceptive delivery. The Braves' lineup has the names to scare you on paper, but Ragans has shown he can neutralize even the most dangerous offenses when his secondary stuff is working. Atlanta's hitters will need to be incredibly disciplined early in counts to have any chance against him.

Chris Sale on the other side is still Chris Sale, and that means electric stuff, devastating sliders, and the kind of competitiveness that makes him one of the most entertaining pitchers to watch in baseball. The veteran lefty had a brilliant 2025 campaign in Atlanta, and he'll want to set the tone for the new season in front of the Truist Park faithful. Sale's ability to punch out hitters in bunches gives the Braves a legitimate shot in any game he starts, regardless of who's in the other dugout.

The 9 total feels high for a game featuring two aces, and that's the market telling you it expects the lineups to have their say too. Both teams can hit, and both bullpens have question marks behind their starters, so if either Ragans or Sale exits early, this thing could blow open in the middle innings. The Royals' offensive improvement has been one of baseball's best stories, and their ability to grind at-bats and work counts could be the difference in a game where every run will feel enormous.

The Angels pulled off the Opening Day stunner with a 3-0 shutout of Houston, and now they head to Minute Maid Park riding that momentum into Game 2. Los Angeles came out with energy and purpose on Thursday, and that kind of start can do wonders for a young team's confidence. The Astros, meanwhile, were blanked on their home turf in a performance that has to be concerning for a team with legitimate postseason aspirations. Houston doesn't get shut out often, and the way the Angels pitching staff silenced that lineup was genuinely impressive.

Yusei Kikuchi gets the start for the Angels, and the lefty can be a real handful when he's commanding his fastball to both sides of the plate. His stuff plays well against right-handed heavy lineups, and while the Astros have some tough lefty bats too, Kikuchi's ability to change speeds and work the edges gives him a chance to keep Houston off balance for a second straight night. The key for Kikuchi will be keeping the ball in the yard, because Minute Maid Park's short left field porch can punish any mistake that leaks over the middle.

Colton Burrows takes the mound for Houston, and the Astros are counting on the young right-hander to right the ship after that demoralizing Opening Day loss. Burrows will need to match the intensity from the jump, because this Astros lineup does not handle losing streaks well and the pressure to perform at home is real. The energy at Minute Maid should be intense as Houston's faithful demand a response, and Burrows will either thrive under that spotlight or wilt under the weight of expectations.

Houston is a significant favorite at -175 despite getting blanked on Opening Day, and that tells you the market still believes in this roster's talent floor. The Astros' lineup is simply too talented to get shut out in back-to-back games, and you'd expect Jose Altuve and company to come out swinging with a vengeance. The Angels showed they can compete, but doing it twice in a row against a motivated Houston team at Minute Maid Park is a different animal entirely. The 8 total is right in the sweet spot for a game that could go either way on the run-scoring front.

That 6.5 total is the lowest number on tonight's entire MLB board, and it perfectly captures what this game is all about: pitching, defense, and hoping somebody finds a way to scratch across a few runs. The Guardians took Game 1, and now Seattle needs to respond at home behind George Kirby, who is one of the best young arms in the American League. Cleveland has always been a team that plays tight, disciplined baseball, and their ability to win low-scoring games is practically an organizational identity at this point.

Gavin Williams gets the ball for Cleveland, and the hard-throwing right-hander has the kind of stuff that can dominate in a pitcher-friendly environment like T-Mobile Park. His fastball velocity and slider combination give him swing-and-miss potential in any count, and if he can keep the walks in check, the Mariners' offense, which has historically struggled to manufacture runs, could be in for another frustrating night. Williams doesn't need to be perfect here; he just needs to keep the ball down and let his defense work behind him.

George Kirby is the kind of pitcher who makes you tear your hair out as an opposing hitter. His command is absurd for someone his age, he attacks the zone relentlessly, and he rarely beats himself with walks. Kirby's ability to induce weak contact and pitch deep into games gives Seattle a huge advantage in a contest where every run is precious. The Guardians' contact-oriented approach could actually work in their favor against Kirby, as putting balls in play and forcing the defense to make plays is a sound strategy against a guy who doesn't give you many free passes.

This game has all the makings of a 2-1 or 3-2 type of affair, and that's exactly what the total suggests. T-Mobile Park's marine layer and spacious outfield should keep the ball in the yard, and with two starters who are capable of dominating, the offenses will need to be opportunistic with whatever chances they get. Seattle's home-field advantage in the pitching department is real, and the Mariners need this one to avoid falling into an early 0-2 hole against a Guardians team that knows how to win ugly.

The Tigers absolutely hammered San Diego 8-2 on Opening Day, and now Framber Valdez makes his highly anticipated Detroit debut in Game 2 at Petco Park. Valdez signed a massive $115 million deal to come to the Motor City, and the expectations on his shoulders are enormous. Detroit's front office made a statement with that signing, essentially telling the baseball world that they're done rebuilding and ready to compete right now. After the way the Tigers dismantled the Padres on Thursday, the energy around this franchise is at a level we haven't seen in years.

Valdez is the kind of pitcher who changes everything for a rotation. His sinker generates ground balls at an elite rate, he eats innings like a workhorse, and he brings a playoff pedigree from his years in Houston that this young Tigers team desperately needs. Petco Park should be a fantastic venue for his ground-ball heavy approach, as the expansive outfield and cooler evening air make it tough for hitters to elevate the ball with authority. If Valdez can replicate even 80% of what he did in Houston, the Tigers have a genuine ace at the top of their rotation.

Michael King takes the mound for San Diego, and the Padres need him to deliver after that embarrassing Opening Day performance. King had a breakout season in 2025 after coming over from the Yankees, and his ability to generate swings and misses with his slider gives San Diego a fighter's chance in any matchup. But the Tigers proved on Thursday that they can hit, and King will need to be sharp from the first pitch because Detroit's lineup showed no mercy in Game 1.

This is essentially a pick'em with Detroit at -102 and San Diego at +104, and that tells you the market sees these teams as almost perfectly evenly matched right now. The Tigers have the momentum after their dominant Opening Day win, but the Padres are too talented to get blown out in consecutive home games. Petco Park's pitcher-friendly dimensions should help keep the scoring in check, and the 7.5 total reflects the expectation that this will be a tighter, more competitive game than Thursday's blowout. Don't be surprised if this one comes down to the bullpens.

ARI (0-1) @ LAD (1-0)

Friday, 10:10 PM ET | Dodger Stadium
Run Line
LAD -1.5
Moneyline
ARI +220 / LAD -260

O/U 9

The defending World Series champions rolled to an 8-2 victory on Opening Day, and now the Dodgers look to push the Diamondbacks into an 0-2 hole under the Dodger Stadium lights. Los Angeles is absolutely loaded again, and the addition of Kyle Tucker on a four-year, $240 million deal has somehow made this already stacked lineup even more terrifying. Tucker's presence gives the Dodgers another elite left-handed bat that forces opposing managers into impossible pitching decisions, and the ripple effect through the entire batting order is immense. This team looks like it's built to repeat.

Tanner Sheehan gets the ball for Los Angeles, and while he may not carry the name recognition of some Dodger aces past, the organization's track record of developing pitching talent speaks for itself. Sheehan will need to attack the strike zone against a Diamondbacks lineup that showed some fight in 2025 but was thoroughly outclassed on Opening Day. Arizona's hitters can be aggressive early in counts, which actually plays into a pitcher's hands if he can locate his off-speed stuff on the corners.

Ryne Nelson starts for Arizona, and the young right-hander faces arguably the toughest assignment in baseball: pitching at Dodger Stadium against this lineup. Nelson has the arm talent to compete at the highest level, but the margin for error against Mookie Betts, Shohei Ohtani, Freddie Freeman, and now Kyle Tucker is essentially zero. One mistake pitch to any of those hitters can turn a competitive game into a blowout, and that's exactly what happened to Arizona's staff on Opening Day. Nelson needs to be surgical with his location and keep the Dodgers' bats off balance with a steady diet of off-speed stuff early.

The Dodgers at -260 are the biggest favorite on the entire Friday slate, and honestly, it's hard to argue with the market on this one. This is a historically talented roster with championship experience, elite depth, and now even more star power with Tucker in the fold. The 9 total reflects the offensive upside from both sides, as Arizona does have bats that can do damage, but the Diamondbacks need Nelson to be nearly perfect to have any chance of stealing one at Dodger Stadium. The late-night West Coast window makes this the nightcap of the slate, and it could be fireworks under those Chavez Ravine lights.
