MLB - Sunday July 19

A Bronx Nightcap Between The Dodgers And Yankees Headlines The Sunday Board

Sixteen games fill the first Sunday of the second half, and the day saves its best for last. The Dodgers hammered the Yankees 8-2 behind Yoshinobu Yamamoto in the afternoon opener in the Bronx, and the two heavyweights run it back in a 7:20 PM ET nightcap with Will Klein getting the ball for Los Angeles. Around it: Paul Skenes takes his 138 strikeouts to Cleveland, Eduardo Rodriguez and his 2.27 ERA host the Cardinals in the desert, and the Nationals visit an Athletics club that has lost nine of ten. Verified pitching lines, records and form below. Analysis only, no picks.

Dodgers @ Yankees

Sunday, 7:20 PM ET | Yankee Stadium, Bronx, NY

The best record in the National League spent Sunday afternoon making a statement in the Bronx. The 63-36 Dodgers took the doubleheader opener 8-2, with Yoshinobu Yamamoto, now 10-6 with a 2.78 ERA and a 0.88 WHIP, outpitching Cam Schlittler, whose brilliant 2.20 ERA season absorbed a rare beating. For the 54-44 Yankees, now losers of two straight, the nightcap is a pride game: split the day, or get swept at home by the team everyone measures against.

Los Angeles hands the nightcap to Will Klein, and the profile is more interesting than the name recognition. Klein is 3-4 with a 2.41 ERA across 37.1 innings, striking out 43 batters at a 10.37 per nine clip, with just two traditional starts in 2026, which means the Dodgers are effectively playing a bulk-and-bullpen game with the deepest roster in the sport behind it. The Yankees had not named a starter for the nightcap as of this writing, a normal doubleheader shuffle after burning Schlittler in the opener, and that uncertainty is baked into the -132/+108 market with the total at 9 at DraftKings.

The road team being favored at Yankee Stadium tells you everything about how the market weighs these rosters right now. The Dodgers are 32-17 on the road, the best travel record in baseball, while the Yankees are a startling 23-22 at home against a 31-22 mark away from the Bronx. New York's season-long run differential of plus-84 is the argument that this team is better than it has looked lately; an 8-2 afternoon loss and back-to-back defeats are the counterargument playing out in real time. Nightcaps after lopsided openers are their own animal, and a Yankees lineup that just watched Yamamoto carve it up gets a very different, more matchable look in game two.

Desert Duel
Local

Cardinals @ Diamondbacks

Sunday, 4:10 PM ET | Chase Field, Phoenix, AZ

Two teams parked on either side of the wild card conversation close their weekend set in the desert. The 51-46 Cardinals have quietly been one of the better road teams in the league at 25-20, but they arrive cooling off at 4-6 over their last ten. The 50-48 Diamondbacks are the opposite trend line, 6-4 in their last ten and a stout 28-21 at Chase Field, where the roof and the still air have suited this roster all year.

The pitching matchup is the draw. Arizona sends out Eduardo Rodriguez, who has put together one of the most underdiscussed seasons in the National League: 8-3 with a 2.27 ERA across 115 innings, a 1.16 WHIP, and a .222 average against. He does not miss a ton of bats at 6.26 strikeouts per nine, but he controls contact and works deep, and at home he has been the stabilizer of the Arizona rotation. St. Louis counters with Andre Pallante, 10-6 with a 3.95 ERA, a ground-ball workhorse whose 100.1 innings have kept the Cardinals' bullpen honest all summer.

Stylistically this leans quiet: a contact-managing lefty at home against a ground-ball righty, two offenses that rank mid-pack in runs scored, and a building that plays fair with the roof shut. The market agreed strongly enough that this game anchors today's BetLegend card, and the full reasoning lives on the flagship pick page. On pure analysis, the gap between Rodriguez's 2.27 and Pallante's 3.95 is the edge Arizona is paying for, and St. Louis needs its road-tested lineup to make Rodriguez throw strikes early rather than letting him cruise into the seventh.

Ace On The Lake
Local

Pirates @ Guardians

Sunday, 1:40 PM ET | Progressive Field, Cleveland, OH

Any Sunday with Paul Skenes on the mound is appointment viewing, and this one comes with actual stakes attached. The 51-48 Pirates have won seven of their last ten and have climbed into the National League periphery on the strength of a lineup that has scored 526 runs, second only to Washington in all of baseball, a wild sentence to type about a franchise that spent years starving its pitchers. Skenes brings a 3.43 ERA, a 1.04 WHIP and 138 strikeouts in 115.1 innings into Cleveland, a 10.77 per nine rate that leads this board, and his 8-8 record remains one of the great annual arguments against caring about pitcher wins.

The 52-47 Guardians live the opposite offensive life. Cleveland has scored just 391 runs all season, the lightest attack of any team on today's board, and survives on run prevention and late-inning leverage. Left-hander Joey Cantillo has been a real part of that formula at 8-4 with a 3.74 ERA and 112 strikeouts, a 9.51 per nine bat-misser whose 1.42 WHIP hints at the traffic he keeps escaping. Progressive Field remains one of the tougher places to cash a big offensive afternoon.

The shape of this game is legible from the season lines: a strikeout artist against a low-wattage offense on one side, a wobblier lefty against a hot-hitting Pirates group on the other. Cleveland's best path is Cantillo surviving the first five while the Guardians scratch out two or three off the Pittsburgh bullpen once Skenes departs, because scoring off Skenes himself has been a losing proposition for most of the sport. Pittsburgh's recent 7-3 stretch says this team has learned to win the low-scoring games its ace demands.

Road Warriors vs A Free Fall
Local

Nationals @ Athletics

Sunday, 4:05 PM ET | Sutter Health Park, West Sacramento, CA

The 49-50 Nationals are one of the strangest splits in baseball: 29-19 on the road against a miserable 20-31 at home. Whatever ails this team in Washington disappears when the plane takes off, and the offense is no illusion, with 540 runs scored, the most of any team playing today. They hand the ball to the pitcher who has defined their season: left-hander Foster Griffin, 10-2 with a 2.77 ERA, a 1.02 WHIP and a .210 average against across 110.1 innings, one of the most efficient seasons in the National League.

The 42-56 Athletics are in a legitimate free fall, having lost nine of their last ten before salvaging a win to open this series stretch, and the run prevention explains it: 552 runs allowed, the most in the American League. Left-hander Jacob Lopez has absorbed much of that damage, sitting at 4-3 with a 6.71 ERA and a 1.79 WHIP, with opponents hitting .289 against him. Sutter Health Park has played hitter-friendly in the summer heat, which is not the environment a struggling pitcher wants against the highest-scoring offense on the slate.

Every verified number points the same direction: the best road record in the National League, the day's best offense and a 2.77 ERA arm against a team allowing runs at the worst rate on the board. That alignment is exactly why the Nationals side features on today's BetLegend card. The honest counterweight is that Washington is just 3-7 over its last ten and lopsided lines have a way of humbling obvious reads, but on pitching profile alone, Griffin against Lopez is the largest single mismatch today's schedule offers.

Ray vs Gilbert
Local

Giants @ Mariners

Sunday, 4:10 PM ET | T-Mobile Park, Seattle, WA

Two identical 3.38 ERAs walk into the pitcher's park in Seattle. The 42-56 Giants send out veteran lefty Robbie Ray, 8-6 with a 3.38 ERA and a .217 average against, returning to the mound where he once won an American League strikeout crown. The 49-50 Mariners counter with Logan Gilbert, whose 3.38 ERA comes with a 1.00 WHIP and 121 strikeouts in 114.2 innings, a 9.50 per nine rate and the kind of command profile that has made him the steadiest arm on the Seattle staff.

The season context is grim on one side and frustrating on the other. San Francisco is 20-31 on the road and has scored just 405 runs, a bottom-tier attack that has wasted a decent rotation for months. Seattle is better positioned at 28-21 at home, but the Mariners are 4-6 over their last ten and their 396 runs scored is barely ahead of the Giants, which is how a team with this pitching sits a game under .500 in July. T-Mobile Park, the most run-suppressing environment in the American League, compounds all of it.

Two low-scoring offenses, two quality starters, and the stingiest park on the board is a formula that speaks for itself, and the total on this game features on today's BetLegend card on the flagship pick page. As pure analysis, the watch point is length: Gilbert's 1.00 WHIP means baserunners are rare, so San Francisco's best inning is likely its first look at him, while Ray's job is to keep the Seattle bats in the yard long enough for a late-inning battle of bullpens.

Best Record In Baseball
Local

Marlins @ Brewers

Sunday, 2:10 PM ET | American Family Field, Milwaukee, WI

The 61-37 Brewers own the best record in the National League Central and the second-best in the league behind only the Dodgers, winners of two straight to open this home stand against Miami and 31-18 at American Family Field. The formula has not changed: Milwaukee has allowed just 370 runs, among the fewest in baseball, and pairs it with 499 scored. Left-hander Robert Gasser, 2-4 with a 4.91 ERA, is the soft spot in an otherwise dominant run-prevention machine, and games he starts are where opponents have to cash in.

The 52-47 Marlins arrived in Milwaukee as one of the season's better stories and are leaving the weekend in a skid, riding a five-game losing streak that has knocked them back in the wild card race. Their answer is their most electric arm: Eury Perez, 5-7 with a 3.53 ERA, 104 strikeouts in 91.2 innings and a .199 average against, the lowest opponent batting mark of any starter on today's board. When Perez is on, Miami can beat anyone; his 10.21 strikeouts per nine are the reason this game is closer than the standings gap suggests.

The matchup inverts the usual script: the juggernaut starts its most vulnerable pitcher while the skidding visitor starts its most dangerous one. If Miami's slump-breaking blueprint exists, it is exactly this, Perez trading zeros while the lineup attacks Gasser early before Milwaukee's elite late-inning relief closes the door. The Brewers' counter is depth and home cooking, and a 31-18 home record says the building itself has been worth something close to a run a game all season.

Sunday At Wrigley
Local

Twins @ Cubs

Sunday, 2:20 PM ET | Wrigley Field, Chicago, IL

The 55-43 Cubs continue one of the better home seasons in the National League, 28-20 at Wrigley and 6-4 over their last ten, with an offense that has stacked 492 runs, top five among all teams in baseball. They hand Sunday to Shota Imanaga, whose 5-8 record hides a 3.94 ERA and a 1.11 WHIP across 114.1 innings. The left-hander's walk-allergic style plays anywhere, but the record tells you how little run support he has gotten in a lineup that scores plenty for everyone else.

The 49-50 Twins are sneaky-live, winners of seven of their last ten despite hovering below .500, with 478 runs scored keeping them dangerous on any given afternoon. The concern is on the mound: Zebby Matthews is 4-6 with a 5.40 ERA across 70 innings, and Wrigley Field in July with the wind blowing out is an unforgiving classroom for a young pitcher allowing a .268 average against. Minnesota's recent surge has been built on outslugging teams, not shutting them down.

The gap in starting pitching is the whole read. Imanaga against a streaky Twins lineup projects manageable; Matthews against a top-five scoring offense in its own park projects loud. For Minnesota to keep its 7-3 run going, the formula is early damage before Imanaga settles, because a bullpen-versus-bullpen finish at Wrigley favors the home team's depth. Chicago, meanwhile, keeps one eye on Milwaukee: at 55-43 the Cubs remain the closest chaser to the Brewers in the Central, and Sundays like this are the games contenders are supposed to bank.
