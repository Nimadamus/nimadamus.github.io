Schlittler and Luzardo Headline a Loaded Fifteen-Game MLB Friday

The full slate is back, all 15 games of it, and it opens with an ace duel at Citizens Bank Park where Cam Schlittler and his 2.20 ERA bring the Yankees in to face Jesus Luzardo and the Phillies. Roki Sasaki takes the ball for the league-best Dodgers at 65-38 in Queens, the streaking Guardians visit the Rays in an AL contender clash, and the 64-38 Brewers keep rolling at home. The two hottest rosters on the board, Los Angeles and Milwaukee, set the tone, and every game gets a full breakdown below.

New York Yankees at Philadelphia Phillies

Friday, 6:45 PM ET | Citizens Bank Park
First Pitch
6:45 PM ET
Venue
Citizens Bank Park
Top Arm
Schlittler 2.20

Marquee MatchupAce DuelAL vs NL

This is the game of the night and it isn't especially close. The Yankees hand the ball to Cam Schlittler, who has been the best starter on this entire board with a 2.20 ERA, a 0.96 WHIP, and 145 strikeouts across 123 innings over 21 starts. Philadelphia counters with lefty Jesus Luzardo, no slouch himself at 9-4 with a 3.43 ERA and 143 punchouts in 115.1 innings. Two power arms, nearly identical strikeout totals, and a ballpark that can turn a mistake into a souvenir, that's a pitching matchup worth setting your night around.

Look at the standings and the gap is real. New York sits 57-45 with a plus-88 run differential and owns the stingiest staff in the slate at a 3.38 team ERA, and they walk in on a 7-3 run over their last 10. The Phillies are a good club at 56-47, but their run differential is actually minus-15, a quirk that says they've won a lot of close ones, and their staff ERA of 4.27 is nearly a full run higher than the Yankees'. Philadelphia has also dropped two straight, so this is a spot where the home crowd wants a statement.

The swing here is whether Luzardo's swing-and-miss stuff can match Schlittler pitch for pitch, because the Yankees rotation depth and that sub-one WHIP from their starter give New York the cleaner path to a low-scoring win. Philadelphia's lineup has the thump to change that in one swing at Citizens Bank Park, which is exactly why this one carries the marquee.
