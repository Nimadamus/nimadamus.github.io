Chanticleers @ Bulldogs

2:00 PM ET | Independence Stadium, Shreveport | ESPN
Line: LT -10.5 | O/U: 49.5 | CCU: 6-6 | LT: 7-5

Everyone's fixating on Louisiana Tech's home record and that 7-5 season, but they're missing the key data point: this line screams trap game.

The sharps are hammering Coastal Carolina at +10.5, and here's why - Louisiana Tech's wins came against absolutely nobody. Their strength of schedule is tissue paper compared to Coastal's Sun Belt grind. That 58% home win rate? Inflated by cupcakes.

Smart money recognizes what the public doesn't: Coastal Carolina's 6-6 record is actually more impressive given their competition level. They've been in dogfights all season while Louisiana Tech padded stats against Conference USA bottom-feeders.

The total is where the real value sits though. Both teams struggled to crack 20 points in meaningful games down the stretch. Bowl rust, limited prep time, and two offenses that rely more on opponent mistakes than explosive plays.

The sharp action is clear - they're betting the under and taking the points with Coastal Carolina.

**Lean: Under 49.5**

Volunteers @ Illini

5:30 PM ET | Nissan Stadium, Nashville | ESPN
Line: TENN -3.5 | O/U: 61.5 | TENN: 8-4 | ILL: 8-4

**TRAP GAME SCREAMING LOUD HERE.**

Tennessee's getting way too much respect at -3.5. Yeah, they're 8-4, but look closer - this team limped to the finish line. Bowl season is where motivation separates pretenders from contenders, and Illinois has everything to prove while Tennessee's just happy to be here after a disappointing SEC campaign.

The Illini are 8-4 with a chip on their shoulder, playing in what's essentially a home region game at Nissan Stadium. They've been covering spreads all year because nobody believes in them. Meanwhile, Tennessee's been overhyped since August and consistently failed to meet expectations against quality competition.

Both teams identical records, but the market's still pricing Tennessee like they're some elite program. They're not. Illinois has been the better value play all season, and that 67% home win rate shows they respond well when expectations are low.

The disrespect continues, and Illinois cashes again.

**TAKE: Illinois +3.5**

Trojans vs Frogs

9:00 PM ET | Alamodome, San Antonio | ESPN
Line: USC -6 | O/U: 56.5 | USC: 7-5 | TCU: 8-4

The Alamo Bowl caps off a 3-game bowl day, and this one's got some juice. USC limped into bowl season at 7-5 after a disastrous start under Lincoln Riley that had fans calling for his head. The Trojans lost three straight in October before rallying late to salvage bowl eligibility. Meanwhile, TCU's been the pleasant surprise of the Big 12, going 8-4 under Sonny Dykes with a physical, gritty style that nobody expected.

Here's the thing - bowl games in Texas favor the Big 12 team almost universally. TCU gets a de facto home game at the Alamodome while USC's dealing with all the distractions that come with being a program in chaos. The transfer portal has decimated the Trojans' roster, and several key players have already opted out. Dykes knows how to prepare his team for bowl games - his track record is excellent in these situations.

The 6-point spread feels inflated based on name recognition alone. TCU has the better defense, the better motivation, and the crowd advantage. USC might have more raw talent, but bowl games reward preparation and cohesion. The Horned Frogs have both in spades.

**LEAN: TCU +6** - This stays close or TCU wins outright.
