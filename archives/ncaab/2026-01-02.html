8 Featured Games - Friday, January 2, 2026

Friday, January 2, 2026

VS

#24 USC Trojans @ #2 Michigan Wolverines

7:00 PM ET Crisler Center MICH -12.5 Peacock

Michigan's Historic Start

The Wolverines are putting together one of the most dominant seasons in Big Ten history. Under second-year head coach Dusty May, Michigan sits at 12-0 and has set a conference record with six victories of 40 or more points already this season. Let that sink in: half of their wins have been absolute blowouts. They're ranked #1 in both the NET and KenPom rankings, boasting the nation's best defense and 11th-best offense.

Trojans' Challenge

USC comes in ranked #24 and has had a solid season, but this is a completely different level of competition. The Trojans have talent, but Michigan's suffocating defense has been the story of college basketball so far. The Wolverines have won their last three games by 30+ points each, with three of those being 40+ point blowouts.

Here's the thing about this Michigan team: they don't just beat you, they embarrass you. The Crisler Center has been a house of horrors for opponents, and the atmosphere will be electric. USC is talented enough to keep this respectable, but Michigan's defensive pressure will be overwhelming. The double-digit spread is steep against a ranked opponent, but the Wolverines have been covering with ease all season.

VS

West Virginia Mountaineers @ #3 Iowa State Cyclones

9:00 PM ET Hilton Coliseum ISU -14.5 ESPN2

Cyclones Rolling

Iowa State is 13-0 and riding an incredible wave of momentum into Big 12 play. The Cyclones have won 42 consecutive non-conference home games, an absolute school record, and this team is projected as a one-seed in the NCAA Tournament. They reached #2 in the AP Poll earlier this month, the highest ranking in program history. This isn't the Iowa State of old; this is an elite program operating at peak efficiency.

Hilton Magic

Hilton Coliseum is one of the toughest places to play in college basketball, and the Cyclones have made it a fortress. Iowa State's 86 wins over the last four years tie for the sixth-best four-year stretch in program history. Their signature win this season came on the road against a #1 Purdue team, proving they can beat anyone anywhere.

West Virginia has had its struggles this season and faces a brutal road environment. The Mountaineers typically play physical, grind-it-out basketball, but Iowa State's depth and offensive firepower should be too much. The 14.5 spread is large, but the Cyclones have been dominant at home and are hungry to make a statement in their Big 12 opener.

VS

#9 Michigan State Spartans @ #13 Nebraska Cornhuskers

9:00 PM ET Pinnacle Bank Arena MSU -2.5 Peacock

Nebraska's Historic Season

Nebraska is UNDEFEATED. That's right, the Cornhuskers are one of just six teams in the country without a loss, and they're playing the best basketball in program history. Fred Hoiberg has completely transformed this program, and the energy in Lincoln is absolutely electric. Pinnacle Bank Arena will be rocking for this top-15 showdown.

Spartans' Pedigree

Tom Izzo brings his #9 Michigan State team to Lincoln in what should be an absolute war. The Spartans have the experience and pedigree that comes with being one of college basketball's elite programs. Izzo's teams always seem to rise to the occasion in big games, and this is as big as it gets for a January conference matchup.

This is the game of the night in college basketball. Two ranked teams, both undefeated, with a massive home crowd advantage for Nebraska. The Cornhuskers have earned this moment, and they'll be ready. Michigan State is only laying 2.5 points on the road, which feels like a disrespect to what Nebraska has accomplished. This one should come down to the final minutes, and the home team has the edge.

VS

#16 Louisville Cardinals @ Stanford Cardinal

8:00 PM ET Maples Pavilion LOU -6.5 ACC Network

Louisville's Resurgence

Pat Kelsey has brought excitement back to Louisville basketball. The Cardinals are ranked #16 and have been one of the more impressive teams in the ACC this season. Louisville plays with tremendous energy and passion, and Kelsey's system generates high-quality shots through ball movement and spacing. This is a program that's firmly back on the national stage.

Stanford Struggles

Stanford's first year in the ACC has been a rough transition. The Cardinal have had difficulty competing with the physicality and depth of their new conference opponents. Maples Pavilion won't provide much of a home court advantage against a Louisville team that travels well and brings energy wherever they go.

The cross-country trip is always a factor for East Coast teams heading to the West Coast, but Louisville has too much firepower for Stanford. The Cardinals should be able to control the tempo and get the stops they need defensively. Expect Louisville to win comfortably and cover the spread in this ACC matchup.

VS

Oregon Ducks @ Maryland Terrapins

7:30 PM ET Xfinity Center MD -4.5 Peacock

Big Ten Clash

Oregon's first year in the Big Ten continues with a trip to College Park to face Maryland. The Ducks have been adjusting to the physical style of play in their new conference, and this road trip will be another test of their mettle. Oregon has talent and athleticism, but the grind of Big Ten play is different from what they experienced in the Pac-12.

Maryland at Home

The Terrapins have been solid at home this season, and Xfinity Center provides a strong atmosphere for Big Ten basketball. Maryland's fans are passionate and loud, which should give the home team an edge. The Terps play physical defense and will look to control the tempo against Oregon's up-tempo style.

This is a crucial game for both teams as they navigate the challenging Big Ten schedule. Maryland's home court advantage and physical style should be the difference. Oregon will compete, but the Terps' familiarity with Big Ten basketball gives them the edge. Take Maryland to cover at home.

VS

Seattle U Redhawks @ #7 Gonzaga Bulldogs

9:00 PM ET McCarthey Athletic Center GONZ -24.5 ESPN+

Gonzaga's Dominance

Mark Few's #7 Gonzaga Bulldogs are once again among the nation's elite. The Zags play beautiful basketball, moving the ball with precision and attacking the rim with purpose. They have multiple NBA-caliber players and play at a pace that overwhelms lesser opponents. McCarthey Athletic Center is one of the toughest environments in college basketball, with the Kennel being notoriously difficult for visitors.

Local Rivalry

Seattle U makes the short trip east for this local rivalry game. The Redhawks have improved as a program, but they're simply not on Gonzaga's level. This is a showcase game for the Zags to display their talent in front of their home fans, and they typically do so with style.

The 24.5-point spread is massive, but Gonzaga has been known to run up the score in these types of matchups. The Zags' depth and talent advantage is overwhelming, and they should control this game from start to finish. Expect Gonzaga to win big and likely cover in the process.

VS

Ohio State Buckeyes @ Rutgers Scarlet Knights

8:00 PM ET Jersey Mike's Arena OSU -3.5 Peacock

Buckeyes on the Road

Ohio State hits the road for a tough Big Ten matchup in Piscataway. The Buckeyes have been inconsistent this season, showing flashes of brilliance mixed with head-scratching losses. Road games in the Big Ten are always a grind, and Jersey Mike's Arena provides one of the toughest environments in the conference.

Rutgers' Grit

Steve Pikiell's Rutgers teams always play hard-nosed, physical basketball. The Scarlet Knights may not have the most talent in the Big Ten, but they compete every night and make life miserable for opponents. Rutgers' defense is suffocating, and they'll look to slow the pace and grind out possessions.

This has all the makings of a classic Big Ten rock fight. Expect a low-scoring, physical affair where every possession matters. Rutgers at home is always dangerous, and Ohio State has struggled with consistency. The 3.5 spread is tight, and this one could go either way. Take the points with Rutgers at home in what should be a close game.

VS

Notre Dame Fighting Irish @ California Golden Bears

11:00 PM ET Haas Pavilion ND -5.5 ESPN2

ACC Road Trip

Notre Dame heads to the West Coast for a late-night ACC matchup in Berkeley. The Fighting Irish have been competitive this season and bring talent to every game. The late tip-off gives Notre Dame plenty of time to adjust to the time zone, but playing at 11 PM ET is never easy for an East Coast team.

Cal's Struggles

California's transition to the ACC has been rocky. The Golden Bears have struggled to find their footing against the increased level of competition, and Haas Pavilion hasn't provided the home court advantage it once did in the Pac-12. Cal needs to improve defensively to have any chance in this one.

Notre Dame should be able to control this game with superior talent and execution. The Fighting Irish play smart basketball and should handle the late tip without too much trouble. Cal will compete, but they don't have the depth or firepower to hang with Notre Dame for 40 minutes. Take the Irish to cover on the road.
