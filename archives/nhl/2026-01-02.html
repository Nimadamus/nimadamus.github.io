VS

2026 NHL Winter Classic: New York Rangers vs Florida Panthers

8:00 PM ET LoanDepot Park, Miami FLA -135 O/U 6.0 TNT

Historic Outdoor Hockey in Miami

This is the southernmost outdoor game in NHL history, and it's going to be absolutely spectacular. The Florida Panthers host the New York Rangers at LoanDepot Park, home of the Miami Marlins, in the 2026 Discover NHL Winter Classic. The NHL has gone all out with a "Snowy Winter Comes to Sunny Florida" theme, featuring a split design with the Panthers' side decorated like a beach complete with sand and lifeguard towers, while the Rangers' side looks like a traditional winter wonderland.

Panthers' Dynasty

Florida is the two-time defending Stanley Cup champion and has been to the Cup Final each of the past three seasons. That's dynasty territory. The Panthers are 21-15-3 with 45 points, sitting 5th in the Atlantic Division but playing excellent hockey. Aleksander Barkov leads this team with his elite two-way play, and Sam Reinhart has been scoring at will, recently putting up two goals in a 5-3 win over Washington. Florida has won 9 of their last 12 games.

Rangers' Outdoor Dominance

Here's a fascinating stat: the Rangers are 5-0-0 all-time in outdoor games, including 2-0-0 in Winter Classics. They've never lost an outdoor game in franchise history. New York has the pedigree and experience in these showcase events, and they'll bring their A-game for this nationally televised spectacle.

This is the Panthers' first ever outdoor game, and they'll be playing in front of a massive home crowd in one of the most unique settings in NHL history. The ice will be 2.5 inches thick (half an inch more than standard) to handle Miami's warm climate, and the retractable roof will likely be open for the event. Florida's championship experience should shine through, but the Rangers' outdoor magic makes this a fascinating matchup. Take the Panthers at home in their historic moment.

VS

Vegas Golden Knights @ St. Louis Blues

3:00 PM ET Enterprise Center VGK -145 O/U 6.0

Golden Knights Rolling

Vegas continues to be one of the NHL's elite teams. The Golden Knights have Mitch Marner now after his July trade from Toronto, adding even more offensive firepower to an already loaded roster. Vegas plays a fast, skilled game that can overwhelm opponents, and their depth is remarkable. They've been one of the most consistent teams in the Western Conference.

Blues' Struggles

St. Louis has had a difficult season, currently sitting at 9-10-7 and ranking near the bottom of the league in both goals scored (27th at 2.6 per game) and goals allowed (28th at 3.4 per game). Jim Montgomery's squad has shown some life with two consecutive wins, including a 1-0 shutout against Utah, but they've struggled to find consistent offense all season.

The Golden Knights are clearly the better team here, and they should be able to impose their will on a struggling Blues team. Vegas has the speed and skill to exploit St. Louis's defensive issues, and their goaltending has been solid. The Blues might keep it close with the home crowd behind them, but Vegas should win this one comfortably. Take the Golden Knights on the road.

VS

Seattle Kraken @ Vancouver Canucks

10:30 PM ET Rogers Arena VAN -165 O/U 6.0

Pacific Division Rivalry

The battle of the Pacific Northwest continues as Seattle makes the short trip north to Vancouver. These teams have developed a genuine rivalry since the Kraken entered the league, and the proximity between the cities adds extra juice to every matchup. This one should be physical and intense, with both teams fighting for playoff positioning.

Canucks' Home Ice

Vancouver has been solid at home this season, and Rogers Arena provides one of the best atmospheres in the NHL. The Canucks have a talented roster led by Elias Pettersson and Quinn Hughes, and they play a structured system under coach Rick Tocchet. Vancouver's power play has been dangerous, and they'll look to capitalize on any discipline issues from Seattle.

Kraken's Road Woes

Seattle has struggled on the road this season, and playing late on a Friday night in a hostile environment is never easy. The Kraken have talent but haven't been able to put together consistent stretches of winning hockey. They'll need to be disciplined and stay out of the penalty box to have a chance in this one.

Vancouver at home is a tough out, and Seattle's road struggles make this a favorable spot for the Canucks. The home team should control the pace and use their power play to put the game away. Take Vancouver to win and cover at home in this Pacific Division showdown.

VS

Minnesota Wild @ Anaheim Ducks

10:30 PM ET Honda Center MIN -145 O/U 5.5

Wild's Consistency

Minnesota has been one of the more consistent teams in the Western Conference this season. The Wild play a structured, defensive system that limits opponents' chances and capitalizes on transition opportunities. Kirill Kaprizov continues to be one of the league's most dangerous players, capable of taking over games with his elite skill and finishing ability.

Ducks' Youth Movement

Anaheim has been a pleasant surprise this season at 15-9-1, sitting atop the Pacific Division with 31 points. The Ducks rank second in the entire league in goals scored, with Cutter Gauthier and Leo Carlsson leading a talented young core that's ahead of schedule in their rebuild. However, Anaheim is 2-3-0 in their last five games and trying to snap a four-game road losing streak.

Wait, the Ducks are at home here, so that road losing streak doesn't apply. But they have been inconsistent lately. Minnesota's structure and experience should be the difference in this matchup. The Wild are built to grind out wins on the road, and their goaltending has been solid. The low total suggests a tight, defensive game, and Minnesota has the edge in that type of contest. Take the Wild to win outright.
