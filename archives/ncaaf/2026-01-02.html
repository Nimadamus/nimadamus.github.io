4 Bowl Games - Final Non-CFP Bowls - Friday, January 2, 2026

Friday, January 2, 2026

VS

Lockheed Martin Armed Forces Bowl: Rice Owls vs Texas State Bobcats

1:00 PM ET Amon G. Carter Stadium, Fort Worth TXST -7.5 ESPN

Texas Showdown

Friday's bowl slate kicks off with an all-Texas affair at Amon G. Carter Stadium in Fort Worth. This is a rematch of the 2023 First Responder Bowl, when Texas State dominated Rice 45-21 in Dallas. The Bobcats will be looking to make it two straight against their in-state rivals, while Rice hopes to flip the script and end their season on a high note.

Texas State's Rise

Texas State has become one of the better Group of Five programs in Texas, and they've earned their spot in this game. The Bobcats play physical football and have playmakers on both sides of the ball. Their rushing attack has been particularly effective, and they should be able to control the clock against a Rice defense that has struggled at times.

Rice's Challenge

Rice has improved under their coaching staff, but they're still building toward consistent competitiveness. The Owls have talent, particularly in the passing game, but they'll need everything to go right to pull off the upset. The 7.5-point spread suggests oddsmakers expect a close game, which aligns with how these Texas programs typically match up.

Texas State has recent history on their side with that 45-21 beatdown in 2023. The Bobcats are the more physical team and should be able to impose their will. Rice will keep it competitive, but Texas State's rushing attack and overall depth give them the edge. Take the Bobcats to cover.

VS

AutoZone Liberty Bowl: Navy Midshipmen vs Cincinnati Bearcats

4:30 PM ET Simmons Bank Liberty Stadium, Memphis NAVY -3.5 ESPN

Military Dominance

Navy comes into this game at an impressive 10-2, and here's a stat that should terrify Cincinnati: Navy, Army, and Air Force have won seven straight bowl games dating back to 2021. The service academies have completely dominated the bowl season in recent years, and there's no reason to believe that streak is in danger here. The Midshipmen are well-coached, disciplined, and play a unique triple-option offense that's nearly impossible to prepare for in bowl season.

Cincinnati's Struggles

The Bearcats come in at 7-5, a far cry from their 2021 College Football Playoff days. Cincinnati has been inconsistent this season, and preparing for Navy's triple-option in limited bowl practice time is a nightmare scenario. Teams that don't see this type of offense regularly almost always struggle against it, regardless of talent level.

The Option Advantage

Navy's triple-option is a ball-control offense that eats clock and keeps opposing offenses on the sideline. The Midshipmen will methodically move the ball down the field, and Cincinnati's defense will be gassed by the fourth quarter. This is a classic example of a system team having a massive advantage in a bowl setting.

Don't overthink this one. Navy at 10-2 with the service academy bowl streak, facing a 7-5 Cincinnati team that can't prepare for the option in two weeks? The Midshipmen should handle business and continue the military's bowl dominance. Take Navy to cover and extend the streak to eight straight service academy bowl wins.

VS

Duke's Mayo Bowl: Wake Forest Demon Deacons vs Mississippi State Bulldogs

8:00 PM ET Bank of America Stadium, Charlotte MSST -3.5 ESPN

ACC vs SEC

The 24th annual Duke's Mayo Bowl features an ACC-SEC showdown under the lights in Charlotte. Wake Forest represents the ACC, while Mississippi State carries the SEC banner. These programs have had up-and-down seasons, but bowl games are about finishing strong and building momentum for the following year.

Wake Forest's Identity

The Demon Deacons have been known for their offensive creativity and willingness to take chances. Wake Forest has playmakers in the passing game and can put up points when things are clicking. However, they've been inconsistent defensively, which has led to some tough losses against better competition.

Mississippi State's Physicality

The Bulldogs bring SEC physicality to this matchup. Mississippi State plays hard-nosed football and can grind out wins with their running game and defense. The SEC is typically more physical than the ACC, and that strength advantage could show up in this game, particularly in the trenches.

This game comes down to whether Wake Forest can match Mississippi State's physicality. The Bulldogs have the advantage up front, and their ability to run the ball should control the clock and keep Wake's offense off the field. The 3.5-point spread is tight, suggesting a close game, but Mississippi State's SEC pedigree gives them the edge. Take the Bulldogs.

VS

Trust & Will Holiday Bowl: #17 Arizona Wildcats vs SMU Mustangs

8:00 PM ET Snapdragon Stadium, San Diego ARIZ -6.5 FOX

Game of the Day

The marquee matchup of January 2nd features #17 Arizona taking on SMU at beautiful Snapdragon Stadium in San Diego. This is the best bowl game of the day by far, featuring two talented teams with explosive offenses. Arizona comes in ranked, while SMU has had an impressive first year in the ACC after leaving the American Athletic Conference.

Arizona's Offensive Firepower

The Wildcats have been one of the most exciting teams in college football this season. Arizona's offense can put up points in bunches, and they've got playmakers all over the field. Head coach Brent Brennan has this program rolling in Tucson, and a Holiday Bowl win would be the perfect cap to an excellent season.

SMU's ACC Debut Season

SMU made the jump to the ACC this season and held their own against Power Four competition. The Mustangs have talent, particularly on offense, and they can match Arizona's scoring ability if this game turns into a shootout. This is exactly the type of high-profile bowl game SMU was hoping for when they made the conference move.

This has "over" written all over it. Both teams can score, and neither defense is particularly stingy. Arizona's ranking suggests they're the better team, but SMU is dangerous and capable of pulling off the upset. The 6.5-point spread feels about right for two evenly matched offensive teams. Expect points and a close game, but Arizona's overall talent should be the difference in the fourth quarter.
