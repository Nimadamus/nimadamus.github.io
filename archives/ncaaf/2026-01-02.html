Rice Owls @ Texas State Bobcats

Friday, Jan 2 | 1:00 PM ET | Amon G. Carter Stadium | ESPN
Lockheed Martin Armed Forces Bowl | Texas State -7.5 | O/U 52.5

Friday's bowl slate kicks off with an all-Texas affair at Amon G. Carter Stadium in Fort Worth. This is a rematch of the 2023 First Responder Bowl, when Texas State dominated Rice 45-21 in Dallas. The Bobcats will be looking to make it two straight against their in-state rivals, while Rice hopes to flip the script and end their season on a high note.

Texas State has become one of the better Group of Five programs in Texas, and they've earned their spot in this game. The Bobcats play physical football and have playmakers on both sides of the ball. Their rushing attack has been particularly effective, and they should be able to control the clock against a Rice defense that has struggled at times.

Rice has improved under their coaching staff, but they're still building toward consistent competitiveness. The Owls have talent, particularly in the passing game, but they'll need everything to go right to pull off the upset. Texas State has recent history on their side with that 45-21 beatdown in 2023. The Bobcats are the more physical team and should be able to impose their will.

Navy Midshipmen vs Cincinnati Bearcats

Friday, Jan 2 | 4:30 PM ET | Simmons Bank Liberty Stadium | ESPN
AutoZone Liberty Bowl | Navy -3.5 | O/U 47.5

Navy comes into this game at an impressive 10-2, and here's a stat that should terrify Cincinnati: Navy, Army, and Air Force have won seven straight bowl games dating back to 2021. The service academies have completely dominated the bowl season in recent years, and there's no reason to believe that streak is in danger here. The Midshipmen are well-coached, disciplined, and play a unique triple-option offense that's nearly impossible to prepare for in bowl season.

The Bearcats come in at 7-5, a far cry from their 2021 College Football Playoff days. Cincinnati has been inconsistent this season, and preparing for Navy's triple-option in limited bowl practice time is a nightmare scenario. Teams that don't see this type of offense regularly almost always struggle against it, regardless of talent level.

Navy's triple-option is a ball-control offense that eats clock and keeps opposing offenses on the sideline. The Midshipmen will methodically move the ball down the field, and Cincinnati's defense will be gassed by the fourth quarter. Don't overthink this one. Navy at 10-2 with the service academy bowl streak, facing a 7-5 Cincinnati team that can't prepare for the option in two weeks? The Midshipmen should handle business.

Wake Forest Demon Deacons vs Mississippi State Bulldogs

Friday, Jan 2 | 8:00 PM ET | Bank of America Stadium | ESPN
Duke's Mayo Bowl | Mississippi State -3.5 | O/U 49.5

The 24th annual Duke's Mayo Bowl features an ACC-SEC showdown under the lights in Charlotte. Wake Forest represents the ACC, while Mississippi State carries the SEC banner. These programs have had up-and-down seasons, but bowl games are about finishing strong and building momentum for the following year.

The Demon Deacons have been known for their offensive creativity and willingness to take chances. Wake Forest has playmakers in the passing game and can put up points when things are clicking. However, they've been inconsistent defensively, which has led to some tough losses against better competition.

The Bulldogs bring SEC physicality to this matchup. Mississippi State plays hard-nosed football and can grind out wins with their running game and defense. The SEC is typically more physical than the ACC, and that strength advantage could show up in this game, particularly in the trenches. This game comes down to whether Wake Forest can match Mississippi State's physicality.

#17 Arizona Wildcats vs SMU Mustangs

Friday, Jan 2 | 8:00 PM ET | Snapdragon Stadium | FOX
Trust & Will Holiday Bowl | Arizona -1.5 | O/U 51.5

The marquee matchup of January 2nd features #17 Arizona taking on SMU at beautiful Snapdragon Stadium in San Diego. This is the best bowl game of the day by far, featuring two talented teams with explosive offenses. Arizona comes in ranked and riding a five-game winning streak, while SMU has had an impressive first year in the ACC after leaving the American Athletic Conference.

The Wildcats bring the #4 pass defense in the nation, allowing just 155.9 yards per game through the air. Head coach Brent Brennan has transformed this program in just two years, taking Arizona from 4-8 to 9-3 and a top-20 ranking. Noah Fifita has been excellent at quarterback with 26 touchdowns against just 5 interceptions.

SMU made the jump to the ACC this season and held their own against Power Four competition, beating Miami and Clemson. Kevin Jennings has thrown for 3,363 yards and 26 touchdowns, but those 10 interceptions are concerning against Arizona's turnover-generating defense. SMU has lost five straight bowl games, a trend they desperately need to break. This is a classic clash of styles - elite defense vs explosive passing.
