10-Game Slate - Friday, January 2, 2026

Friday, January 2, 2026

VS

San Antonio Spurs @ Indiana Pacers

7:00 PM ET Gainbridge Fieldhouse SA -3.5 O/U 229.5

Wembanyama Watch

Victor Wembanyama is questionable for this game after hyperextending his left knee Wednesday against the Knicks. The MRI came back clean, but he stayed in San Antonio for additional treatment rather than traveling with the team. If Wemby plays, the Spurs are a different animal. If he sits, this line will move dramatically. Keep an eye on the injury report before placing any bets.

Spurs Defense

When healthy, San Antonio has the best defense in the NBA with a 103.9 defensive rating. Without Wembanyama, that number balloons to 116.1, dropping them from elite to below average. That's the impact of a generational defender. Wemby is averaging 26.2 points, 12.9 rebounds, 4.0 assists, and 3.6 blocks per game this season, and his presence completely changes the game.

Pacers Struggling

Indiana has been one of the most disappointing teams in the league this season. The Pacers are winless in their last 10 games and have struggled to find any consistency. Their defense has been particularly problematic, and even a Wemby-less Spurs team might be enough to handle them. This is a must-win for Indiana to show any signs of life.

If Wembanyama plays, take the Spurs comfortably. If he's out, this becomes a much closer game and the Pacers might have value at home. Monitor the injury news and adjust accordingly. The spread assumes Wemby is playing, so expect movement if he's ruled out.

VS

Brooklyn Nets @ Washington Wizards

7:00 PM ET Capital One Arena BKN -2.5 O/U 224.5

Battle of the Bottom

This is two of the NBA's worst teams facing off, and neither franchise is trying to win games at this point. Both Brooklyn and Washington are in full tank mode, developing young players and accumulating draft picks. The talent level is low, the motivation is questionable, and this game will be ugly to watch.

Nets' Youth

Brooklyn is running out young players and seeing what they have. Cam Thomas has been dealing with injuries, and the Nets simply don't have the firepower to compete against decent teams. They're playing hard but losing close games, which is exactly what a tanking team wants.

Wizards' Rebuild

Washington is in Year 2 of their rebuild and has shown some improvement from their young core. The Wizards have dealt with injuries all season, particularly to Alex Sarr, but they're developing talent for the future. Home court gives them a slight edge in this matchup.

These games are unpredictable because neither team is trying to win. The 2.5-point spread is essentially a pick'em. Washington at home might have the edge simply because they're playing in front of their fans, but don't put much money on either side. This is a stay-away game for serious bettors.

VS

Denver Nuggets @ Cleveland Cavaliers

7:30 PM ET Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse CLE -4.5 O/U 230.5 Prime Video

Jokic vs Cleveland

Nikola Jokic brings his MVP candidacy to Cleveland for this nationally televised matchup on Prime Video. The big man is putting up ridiculous numbers again, and the Nuggets remain one of the West's elite teams despite roster changes. Denver plays beautiful basketball with Jokic orchestrating everything, and they're dangerous on any given night.

Cavaliers' Home Court

Cleveland is 17-15 and sitting 7th in the Eastern Conference. The Cavaliers are averaging 120.2 points per game, 6th in the league, with Donovan Mitchell leading the charge. Mitchell recently dropped 34 points in a win over Phoenix, and Jarrett Allen has been dominant inside with 27 points and 10 rebounds against San Antonio. The Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse is a tough place to play.

Key Matchup

Jokic vs Allen is the matchup to watch. Both are elite big men, but they play completely different styles. Jokic is a playmaking wizard who can score from anywhere, while Allen is a rim-running, rim-protecting monster. Whoever wins this battle will likely determine the outcome of the game.

Cleveland at home with Mitchell playing well should be able to hang with Denver. The Cavaliers have enough firepower to match the Nuggets' offense, and their home court advantage is real. This should be a competitive game, and the 4.5 spread feels about right. Lean Cleveland at home, but don't be surprised if Jokic takes over in the fourth quarter.

Atlanta Hawks @ New York Knicks

7:30 PM ET Madison Square Garden NYK -8.5 O/U 227.5

MSG Showdown

The Knicks bring the Hawks to Madison Square Garden, and the World's Most Famous Arena will be rocking. New York already beat Atlanta earlier this week, and the Hawks have dropped 8 of their last 10 games. This is a bad spot for Atlanta, playing on the road against a superior team in a hostile environment.

Knicks Rolling

New York has been playing excellent basketball at home, and their defense has been suffocating. The Knicks play physical, grind-it-out basketball that wears opponents down over 48 minutes. Their depth allows them to maintain intensity throughout the game, and tired legs late in the fourth quarter often spell doom for opposing teams.

Hawks Fading

Atlanta's season has been a disappointment. Jalen Johnson has shown flashes of brilliance, including a 41-point outburst recently, but the consistency isn't there. The Hawks' defense has been atrocious, and they can't seem to string together wins. This feels like a fade-the-Hawks situation.

New York at home laying 8.5 points is steep, but the Hawks have been so bad that it might not matter. The Knicks should control this game from start to finish, and their defensive intensity should keep Atlanta from getting into a rhythm. Take the Knicks to cover at home.

Orlando Magic @ Chicago Bulls

8:00 PM ET United Center ORL -5.5 O/U 217.5

Magic's Defense

Orlando has been one of the best defensive teams in the league, and they're doing it without Paolo Banchero. The Magic have won 8 of their last 10 games and are proving they're more than just one player. Their defensive system is elite, and they make life miserable for opposing offenses. The low total reflects the expectation that this will be a grinding, defensive battle.

Bulls Rebuilding

Wait, the Bulls are at home here. Let me reconsider. Orlando being favored on the road speaks to how much better they are than Chicago right now. The Magic's defense should travel, and they have the depth to handle the Bulls even without Banchero. Take Orlando to cover on the road in what should be a low-scoring affair.

Charlotte Hornets @ Milwaukee Bucks

8:00 PM ET Fiserv Forum MIL -10.5 O/U 225.5

Giannis Dominates

Giannis Antetokounmpo is back and healthy, and the Bucks are starting to look like themselves again. Milwaukee snapped a seven-game losing streak recently with Giannis in the lineup, and they've been dominant at home. The Greek Freak is averaging 30.9 points, 10.9 rebounds, and 6.6 assists on the season, and he's virtually unstoppable when healthy.

Hornets Overmatched

Charlotte has been competitive this season, with Miles Bridges and LaMelo Ball leading the charge. Bridges has been phenomenal, scoring 35 points in a recent overtime win, but the Hornets simply don't have the depth to compete with Milwaukee's star power. This is a massive mismatch on paper.

The Bucks have won 19 of their last 22 games against the Hornets, and there's no reason to believe that trend will change. Milwaukee at home with a healthy Giannis should dominate this game. The 10.5 spread is large, but the talent disparity is even larger. Take the Bucks to cover.

Pelicans' Struggles

The Champs Visit

The Warriors are fighting to stay relevant in the West, and games against elite teams like OKC expose their weaknesses. This is a measuring stick game for Golden State.

Prime Matchup

This is the game of the night. The defending champs against the dynasty that dominated the previous era. Curry vs SGA is a fantastic matchup, and Chase Center will be electric. The Thunder are favorites on the road, which tells you everything you need to know about the state of these two franchises.

OKC is the better team, but Curry at home in a nationally televised game is always dangerous. The Thunder should win, but the Warriors have the star power to keep it close. Take the points with Golden State in what should be an entertaining game.

Lakers Rolling

Late Night Battle

This is a fantastic late-night matchup between two talented teams. The Lakers' momentum and home court advantage give them the edge, but Memphis is dangerous and won't go away quietly. Expect a physical, competitive game that could come down to the final minutes.

The Lakers at home with their current momentum should be able to handle Memphis. Doncic and Reaves are playing at an elite level, and the Crypto.com Arena crowd will be hyped. Take the Lakers to cover at home and extend their winning streak.
