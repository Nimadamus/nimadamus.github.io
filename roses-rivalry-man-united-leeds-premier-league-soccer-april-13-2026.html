Manchester United vs Leeds United

Monday 3:00 PM ET | Old Trafford | Premier League

The War of the Roses returns to Old Trafford on Monday afternoon, and the stakes for both clubs couldn't be more divergent or more desperate. Manchester United sit third on 55 points, level with Aston Villa and firmly in the Champions League conversation after a strong run of form that's seen them go unbeaten in nine home league matches since October. With a record of 15 wins, 10 draws, and just 6 losses from 31 games, United have scored 56 and conceded 43, building a plus-13 goal difference that reflects a squad finally finding consistency under the current system. Their recent form of W-W-L-W-D includes convincing victories over Everton and Crystal Palace, and only a 1-2 stumble at Newcastle has blemished what's been an excellent stretch. Bruno Fernandes continues to orchestrate everything with 8 goals and a remarkable 16 assists, while Benjamin Sesko (9 goals) and Bryan Mbeumo (9 goals) have provided the cutting edge in the final third.

Leeds, by contrast, are staring into the abyss. Sitting 15th with 33 points from 31 matches (7W-12D-12L), they're just one point above West Ham in 17th and the relegation zone. The numbers tell a grim story: 37 goals scored, 48 conceded, a minus-11 goal difference, and a form line that reads winless in their last six league games with a failure to score in four consecutive Premier League outings. That's a drought that would terrify any fanbase, and the underlying data suggests it's not just bad luck. However, there's a fascinating psychological wrinkle here. Leeds just knocked off West Ham on penalties to reach their first FA Cup semi-final in 39 years, and that kind of emotional lift can do strange things to a squad that's been drowning in negativity. Dominic Calvert-Lewin (10 goals) and Felix Nmecha (6 goals) have the quality to hurt anyone on their day, but "their day" hasn't arrived in the league for over a month.

The betting market has Manchester United priced between -170 and -185 on the moneyline, with the draw at +310 to +330 and Leeds available at +425 to +475. The over 2.5 goals line is juiced at -135 to -142, and BTTS Yes sits at -132, both of which reflect the expectation that United will create plenty going forward but that this fixture has a history of producing moments of chaos. United's -1 spread is available between -102 and -112, which is an interesting line given their dominance at home and Leeds' inability to find the net recently. The head-to-head record is brutally one-sided in United's favor: unbeaten in the last seven meetings (4W, 3D), and Leeds haven't won at Old Trafford since 1981. That's 45 years of hurt at the Theatre of Dreams, and the weight of that history sits heavy on a club already buckling under pressure.

Here's what makes this match genuinely compelling beyond the rivalry: United need every point they can get to secure a top-four finish and Champions League football, while Leeds need points simply to survive. Harry Maguire is suspended after his red card, and Matthijs de Ligt remains out with a back injury until early May, which thins United's defensive options. Leeds are missing Junior Firpo replacement Joe Rodon (ankle) and creative midfielder Stach (ankle), along with winger Daniel James (groin). The atmosphere at Old Trafford will be intense, and while the Roses rivalry guarantees passion from both sides, the tactical reality is that Leeds will need to find a way to end their scoring drought against a defense that's been resolute on home soil. United's unbeaten home run stretching back to October suggests they have the quality and the confidence to handle even the most emotionally charged opponent, but Leeds' FA Cup momentum and the desperation of a team fighting for survival make this more dangerous than the odds suggest.

Fiorentina vs Lazio

Monday 2:45 PM ET | Stadio Artemio Franchi | Serie A

Fiorentina's season has been nothing short of miserable by their standards, and a home fixture against a banged-up Lazio side at the Artemio Franchi represents one of their better opportunities to grab three points and put some daylight between themselves and the relegation conversation. Sitting 16th with 36 points from 32 games (8W-12D-12L), Stefano Pioli's side have scored just 23 goals all season, the worst attacking output in the top half of the table and a number that would be embarrassing for a club with Fiorentina's ambitions. The goal difference of minus-17 (23 scored, 40 conceded) tells the story of a team that simply cannot find the net consistently, and the recent 3-0 Conference League loss to Crystal Palace only amplified the frustration around the club.

Lazio arrive in Florence sitting ninth on 44 points (11W-11D-9L from 31 games), which is respectable but far from where they'd hoped to be. Their form over the last 10 matches reads 4W-4D-2L, showing a side that's competitive but lacks the killer instinct to put together a sustained winning run. More concerning for Monday is the injury list: goalkeeper Ivan Provedel is out long-term with a shoulder problem, Mattia Zaccagni is nursing a thigh injury, and defenders Gila, Pellegrini, and Gigot are all unavailable. That's a significant chunk of Lazio's spine missing for a tricky away fixture, and the bookmakers have noticed, pricing Fiorentina as slight favorites at +132 with Lazio out at +245 and the draw at +190.

The under 2.5 goals market is heavily favored at -167, and honestly, that feels about right. Fiorentina's 23 goals in 32 games works out to barely 0.72 per match, and while Lazio are slightly more productive at roughly a goal per game, the absence of Zaccagni removes one of their most creative outlets. Both teams have the ability to defend in numbers when they need to, and this has all the ingredients of a cagey, low-scoring affair where the first goal changes everything. BTTS Yes at -112 offers some value if you believe Lazio's attacking quality can bypass a Fiorentina defense that's hardly been watertight, but the overall tempo of this match is likely to be patient and tactical rather than open and expansive.

The head-to-head record adds intrigue: the last 10 meetings are split 4-4-2 between Fiorentina wins, Lazio wins, and draws, but Fiorentina are unbeaten in the last four at home against Lazio (2W, 2D), including a 2-2 draw in Rome back in January. Fiorentina will be without Gudmundsson (suspended) and Fagioli (accumulated yellows), while Lamptey remains a long-term absence with a cruciate ligament injury. For Fiorentina, this is a match they simply have to win at home to stabilize their season and avoid getting sucked any closer to the danger zone. For Lazio, grabbing a point with a depleted squad would feel like a good day's work, and the way they've been grinding out draws recently suggests they won't make it easy for the hosts.

Levante vs Getafe

Monday 3:00 PM ET | Estadi Ciutat de Valencia | La Liga

Levante's return to La Liga as Segunda Division champions has turned into a nightmare. Dead last in 20th place with 26 points from 30 matches (6W-8D-16L), they're five points from safety with just eight games remaining, and the math is becoming increasingly unkind. Their goal difference of minus-16 (34 scored, 50 conceded) reflects a team that can create chances but simply cannot keep the opposition out, and their home record is the worst in the division with just 14 points from 15 home matches. That's a staggering failure rate at the Estadi Ciutat de Valencia, a ground that should be a fortress for a team fighting for their lives. Promoted teams are supposed to lean on home form as their lifeline, and Levante's inability to do so is the single biggest reason they're in this position.

Getafe, meanwhile, have been one of La Liga's most impressive stories this season. Sitting eighth on 41 points (12W-5D-13L), they've won four of their last five matches, including an impressive 2-0 victory over Athletic Bilbao, and possess the third-best defense in the entire league with just 31 goals conceded. Their away form ranks fifth-best in La Liga, and the last 10 games show a run of 6W-2L-2D that screams a side hitting its stride at exactly the right time. However, there's a significant caveat for Monday: Mauro Arambarri, their top scorer with 6 goals, is suspended, and midfielder Milla is also serving a ban. Borja Mayoral (knee) and Juanmi (muscle) are both injured as well, leaving Getafe without four key contributors for a match they'd normally be expected to grind out.

The betting market reflects genuine uncertainty, with Levante at +160 to +170, the draw at +200 to +205, and Getafe at +188 to +190. That's about as close to a pick-em as you'll find in La Liga, and the suspensions to Getafe's personnel are clearly baked into the price. The under 2.5 goals is heavily favored at -233, which makes sense given Getafe's defensive DNA (they've allowed the third-fewest goals in the league) and the fact that neither team is particularly prolific going forward. Getafe's 27 goals in 30 games is one of the lowest totals in the top half, and while Levante have been slightly more adventurous with 34, their inability to convert at home has been their downfall all season.

The all-time head-to-head record between these two is one of football's most perfectly balanced rivalries: 9 wins apiece with 9 draws across 27 meetings. Even more intriguing, Levante are unbeaten at home against Getafe since 2012, which is the kind of historical trend that takes on extra significance when a team is desperate for anything to cling to. The most recent meeting ended 1-1 at Getafe back in September. For Levante, this is a genuine lifeline, a home match against a side missing their best scorer and two other key players. If they can't generate energy and urgency in front of their own fans with the situation this dire, then the relegation is deserved. For Getafe, the challenge is maintaining their excellent form without several first-choice players, and a point on the road at a ground where they haven't won in 14 years would be a perfectly acceptable result given the circumstances.
