Brighton & Hove Albion vs AFC Bournemouth

Monday, 7:00 AM PST | American Express Stadium | Premier League
3-Way ML: Brighton -119 / Draw +260 / Bournemouth +325 | Total Goals: O/U 3.5 (+125/-155) | BTTS: Yes -150

If you're looking for goals on MLK Day, this is your match. Brighton host Bournemouth in what promises to be an absolute shootout at the American Express Stadium, and the numbers backing this up are genuinely staggering. Bournemouth's away games this season are averaging 4.8 goals per match. That's not a typo. Nearly five goals per game on the road. They're conceding plenty, but they're also causing absolute chaos for opposing defenses.

Here's what makes this fascinating from a betting perspective: Brighton under Fabian Hurzeler have both scored and conceded in 41 of their 59 Premier League games, a ratio of 69.5% that represents the highest of any manager to take charge of 50+ games in the competition's history. That stat alone tells you everything you need to know about what to expect. This isn't a side that grinds out 1-0 results. They're going to score, they're going to concede, and the entertainment value is essentially guaranteed.

The head-to-head history favors Brighton decisively. They've won 6 of their last 10 meetings against the Cherries, including four of the last five at home. Kaoru Mitoma has been an absolute menace in this fixture, scoring five goals in his last six appearances against Bournemouth. When one player has that kind of psychological edge against a particular opponent, it tends to manifest on the pitch. Mitoma is available at 5/2 for anytime scorer, which represents genuine value given his recent form and this specific history.

The injury situation adds intrigue. Brighton are without Adam Webster, Mats Wieffer, Solly March, and Stefanos Tzimas, with Yankuba Minteh questionable. Bournemouth's list is even longer: Will Adams, Tyler Adams, Justin Kluivert, and Enes Unal are all confirmed out, with David Brooks and Ryan Christie questionable. Both sides are somewhat depleted, which typically leads to more defensive errors and more goals. The Over 3.5 at +125 offers solid value in what should be an entertaining Monday afternoon fixture.

Elche CF vs Sevilla FC

Monday, 6:00 AM PST | Estadio Martinez Valero | La Liga
3-Way ML: Elche +140 / Draw +210 / Sevilla +210 | Asian Handicap: Elche -0.25 (+104) | Total Goals: O/U 2.5 (-150/+120)

Sevilla are in absolute freefall, and the numbers are genuinely shocking for a club of their stature. Four consecutive defeats without scoring a single goal. Let that sink in. This is Sevilla, a club with multiple Europa League titles this decade, currently failing to even find the net over 360 minutes of football. They've lost 7 of their last 10 matches overall and 4 of their last 5 on the road. When you're going through a drought this severe, the last thing you want is a trip to a venue where you haven't won in over a decade.

Here's the fascinating context: Sevilla haven't beaten Elche at the Estadio Martinez Valero since October 2014. That's more than 11 years of futility at this ground. While Sevilla are unbeaten in their last six overall meetings with Elche (W3, D3), those results haven't come in this particular stadium. Home advantage matters in Spanish football, and Elche have been outstanding in their backyard. They've lost just one of their 10 La Liga home games this season, collecting 19 of their 23 total points at home.

The betting market has priced this as essentially a coin flip, with Elche slight favorites at +140 and Sevilla at +210. That's remarkable when you consider the historical prestige of these clubs, but the current form table tells a completely different story than the all-time standings. Elche sit 10th with 23 points while Sevilla languish in 16th with just 20. Three points separate them, but the trajectory couldn't be more different.

Predictive models are giving Elche a 41-43% win probability, with Sevilla at just 31-32% and the draw around 27%. The Under 2.5 at -150 reflects expectations of a cagey, tactical affair. Both sides lack firepower, and Elche's strength has been built on defensive organization rather than attacking brilliance. If you're looking for value, the Elche Asian Handicap -0.25 at +104 essentially offers you a full payout on a win with half your stake returned on a draw. Given Sevilla's current inability to score, that looks like a reasonable spot.

Weekend Results Context

La Liga & Premier League | January 17-18, 2026

Before we dive into Monday's action, here's what shaped the weekend narrative heading into these fixtures. In La Liga, Real Madrid bounced back from their Copa del Rey embarrassment against Albacete with a 2-0 home victory over Levante. Kylian Mbappe converted from the spot for his 30th club goal of the season, and substitute Asencio added a header from an Arda Guler corner. It was Alvaro Arbeloa's first league win as manager since replacing Xabi Alonso, though the Bernabeu faithful were less than forgiving, whistling the players throughout despite the positive result.

Barcelona maintain their four-point cushion at the summit after Sunday's dominant performance at Real Sociedad. The Catalan giants have now won 11 straight across all competitions, including their Supercopa triumph over Real Madrid. Their +33 goal difference tells the story of a team playing at a completely different level than the rest of the league. They've covered the -1 Asian Handicap in 14 of their last 20 matches, a genuinely remarkable rate of margin victories.

In the Premier League, Manchester City's continued struggles opened the door for the chasing pack. The defending champions dropped points again, leaving Liverpool in pole position at the top of the table. Brighton's 1-1 draw at the Etihad demonstrated both their quality on the road and their frustrating inability to close out matches against elite opposition. Kaoru Mitoma registered the goal in that contest, continuing his excellent run of form heading into today's meeting with Bournemouth.
