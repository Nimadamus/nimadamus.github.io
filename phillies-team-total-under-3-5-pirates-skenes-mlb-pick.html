This is the more targeted version of the Phillies/Pirates pitching angle: instead of needing both offenses quiet, it asks Philadelphia to stay at three runs or fewer against Skenes and the Pittsburgh run-prevention plan.

Why This Pick Fits The Slate

Skenes' verified 2026 line is the anchor. A 1.98 ERA and 0.64 WHIP through 50 innings gives this bet a real starting-point edge, and the 56 strikeouts matter because they limit the productive-out innings that can beat a 3.5 team total. Philadelphia is a .500 club entering the day, and this is not a soft landing spot for a road lineup against an ace with both swing-and-miss and baserunner suppression.

Market Read

The -135 price is materially lighter than the first-five under, and the tradeoff is bullpen exposure. That is acceptable here because the team total remains tied to a relatively low Phillies ceiling. If Skenes handles the first two times through and Pittsburgh does not hand away extra baserunners, Philadelphia has to be efficient late to clear the number.

The risk is bullpen leakage or one high-leverage mistake after Skenes exits. Team-total unders can lose even when the starting handicap is correct if the final third of the game gets messy.
