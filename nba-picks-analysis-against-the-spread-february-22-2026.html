Cavaliers @ Thunder

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK
Spread
OKC -6.5
Moneyline
OKC -250 / CLE +200
Total
O/U 234.5

Welcome back from the All-Star break with a Sunday blockbuster. Cleveland at 36-21 rolls into Oklahoma City riding a scorching 7-0 straight-up winning streak, and the addition of James Harden from the Clippers on February 4 has given this roster a completely different look. Harden brings 19.3 points and 8.7 assists per game to a backcourt that already features Donovan Mitchell's 28.8 PPG scoring punch. The question is how quickly this new pairing finds its rhythm after the break. Cleveland has been excellent ATS recently, going 8-3 against the number in their last 11 games, and they've looked like a team that genuinely believes they can compete with anybody in the league.

But here's the problem: the Thunder at 43-14 are the best team in basketball, and it's not particularly close. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, the reigning MVP, is putting up 31.8 points, 4.4 rebounds, and 6.4 assists per game, and he does it with a surgical efficiency that makes everything look effortless. SGA was dealing with an abdominal strain heading into the break, so his availability is the one X-factor to monitor. OKC has been dominant in Sunday games, going 14-6 ATS in their last 20, and their home court has been a house of horrors for visitors all season. The Thunder defend at an elite level, and their depth is suffocating for opposing benches.

The head-to-head history is brutal for Cleveland. The Cavaliers are just 1-5 straight up in their last six meetings with OKC, and the Thunder have owned this matchup consistently. The 234.5 total is worth watching closely, as four of the last five meetings between these teams have gone over. Both offenses are capable of putting up 120+ on any given night, and the All-Star layoff could mean rusty defenses early. The 6.5-point spread is the biggest question here. Cleveland is a legitimate contender with Harden now in the fold, but OKC at home with SGA is a different beast entirely.

This is the kind of game that sets the tone for the second-half stretch run. If Cleveland can hang with OKC on the road, it sends a message to the rest of the league that the Harden trade was worth the price. If the Thunder blow them out, it reinforces what we already suspected: OKC is in a class of their own heading toward the playoffs.

Betting lines weren't available at post time for this one, but you don't need Vegas to tell you which way this game is leaning. Atlanta at 27-31 is a significantly better team than Brooklyn at 15-40, and the Hawks should control this game comfortably at State Farm Arena. Trae Young continues to be one of the most dynamic offensive players in the league, and the Hawks' supporting cast gives them enough firepower to overwhelm a depleted Nets roster that has been in full teardown mode all season.

Brooklyn is one of the worst teams in basketball by virtually every metric. Their 15-40 record tells the story of a franchise that traded away its stars and is playing out the string with developmental pieces and expiring contracts. The Nets lack a true go-to scorer, their defense is porous, and their effort level on the road has been inconsistent at best. They've been competitive in some games, but facing a Hawks team at home that has legitimate playoff aspirations is a tall order for this group.

The Hawks need wins like these to solidify their play-in positioning. At 27-31, they're hovering around that 9th-10th seed range in the Eastern Conference, and dropping games to the league's worst teams would be catastrophic for their postseason hopes. Expect Atlanta to come out with energy in front of their home crowd, attack Brooklyn's poor perimeter defense early, and build a comfortable lead. The projected total around 228 feels about right, as the Hawks can score in bunches but Brooklyn's lack of offensive firepower keeps this from becoming a shootout.

If you told anyone two years ago that Toronto would be favored on the road in Milwaukee, they'd have laughed you out of the room. But here we are. The Raptors at 33-23 have been one of the league's most pleasant surprises this season, and the acquisition of Brandon Ingram from the Pelicans in February 2025 has been the catalyst for their transformation. Ingram has given them a legitimate second star alongside Scottie Barnes, and his ability to create in isolation and draw fouls has been exactly the offensive punch Toronto needed. This team is playing with confidence and cohesion, and they look like a legitimate Eastern Conference threat.

Milwaukee at 24-30 is in crisis. The Bucks are below .500, sitting 11th in the Eastern Conference, and their title window appears to be slamming shut. Giannis Antetokounmpo can still dominate on any given night, but the supporting cast has deteriorated, and the defense that once defined this franchise has become a liability. Damian Lillard's fit in Milwaukee has never quite clicked the way everyone hoped, and the Bucks' lack of depth and wing defense makes them vulnerable against teams with multiple scoring options. Being 6 games below .500 this deep into the season is alarming for a franchise that expected to contend.

The moneyline at Toronto -223 tells you the market respects what the Raptors have built this season. Milwaukee's home court advantage at Fiserv Forum isn't what it used to be, and the Bucks have struggled mightily against teams with winning records. Toronto's defense has been underrated all season, and their ability to switch across positions and contest shots at the rim gives them a schematic advantage over Milwaukee's half-court offense. This feels like a game where the Raptors assert their dominance and make a statement about where they stand in the Eastern Conference hierarchy.

Nuggets @ Warriors

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Chase Center, San Francisco, CA
Spread
DEN -6.5 (-105)
Moneyline
DEN -203 / GSW +194

O/U 232.5

The Warriors are a shell of themselves for this one, and the injury list reads like a casualty report. Stephen Curry is sidelined with a knee issue, Jimmy Butler is out for the entire season after tearing his ACL, and Seth Curry is dealing with a back problem. That's three of their most important players gone, and the absence of Curry alone completely changes the math for Golden State. Without the greatest shooter in NBA history on the floor, the Warriors' offensive spacing collapses, and their ability to generate quality looks in the half-court diminishes drastically. At 29-27, they're fighting to stay in the playoff picture, and losing Curry for any extended time could be devastating.

Denver at 36-21 has their own injury concerns, with Aaron Gordon and Peyton Watson both out with hamstring injuries. But when you have Nikola Jokic, you always have a chance. Jokic continues to put up numbers that don't look real, orchestrating Denver's offense with a level of passing and court vision that no big man in NBA history has ever matched. He's the engine that makes everything run, and his ability to exploit mismatches against any defensive scheme gives the Nuggets an inherent advantage in every game. Without Curry to punish Denver's defense on the other end, the Nuggets should be able to control the tempo and dictate the terms of engagement.

The 6.5-point spread reflects the massive talent gap with these injuries in play. Golden State without Curry at home is a fundamentally different team than the Warriors most people envision. Draymond Green and the remaining core will try to keep things competitive, but the offensive burden shifts to role players who aren't built for that kind of responsibility. The 232.5 total feels a touch high with Curry out, as the Warriors' offense will likely struggle to generate points without its primary creator. Denver should win this one going away, and covering 6.5 feels very achievable for a team of their caliber.

Two of the league's most struggling teams meet in Indianapolis in what projects as a genuinely toss-up game. Dallas at 19-36 is on a brutal 10-game losing skid that has buried any remaining hope of postseason basketball this year. Life after their former superstar's departure has been a nightmare for the franchise. Cooper Flagg, the #1 overall pick in the 2025 draft, is getting his education in NBA basketball the hard way, learning on a roster that's in full rebuild mode. Flagg has shown flashes of the talent that made him the consensus top selection, but asking a rookie to carry an entire franchise is a recipe for exactly this kind of record.

Indiana at 15-42 is the worst team in the Eastern Conference, and their season has been defined by the absence of Tyrese Haliburton. The former All-Star has dealt with an Achilles injury that has either kept him off the floor entirely or severely limited his effectiveness when he does play. Without Haliburton orchestrating the offense, the Pacers lack a true primary ballhandler, and their high-octane pace-and-space attack has devolved into a disorganized mess. They have young talent in the pipeline, but right now, this roster simply doesn't have the pieces to compete on a nightly basis.

Lines weren't available at post time, and honestly, it's easy to see why oddsmakers might have trouble pricing this one. Both teams are bad. Both teams are tanking, whether they admit it or not. The projected total of around 231 suggests the market expects some offense, which tracks considering neither team plays any defense. Dallas has a slight edge in overall talent even without their former superstar, and the Mavs' losing streak has to end eventually. But betting on either of these teams with any conviction requires a stronger stomach than most bettors have. This is a stay-away spot for the smart money.

The 233.5 total is interesting considering the spread implies a blowout, and Charlotte's offense should get plenty of open looks against a Wizards defense that simply doesn't have the personnel to contest shots consistently. This has all the makings of a comfortable wire-to-wire Hornets victory.

The greatest rivalry in NBA history gets a fresh chapter, and this one has a completely different feel. The Lakers at 34-21 now feature Luka Doncic, who has been absolutely ridiculous since arriving in Los Angeles, averaging 33.0 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 8.6 assists per game. Doncic dealt with a hamstring strain before the break but is expected to return for this marquee matchup. The combination of Luka and LeBron James, when both are healthy, gives LA two of the most gifted playmakers in NBA history sharing the floor. LeBron's status is the one thing to watch here, as he's been managing sciatica and foot issues, but the prospect of him missing a nationally televised Celtics game seems unlikely.

Boston at 36-19 is navigating one of the most challenging stretches in recent franchise history. Jayson Tatum remains out with an Achilles injury, and the Celtics have had to completely reinvent their offensive identity. Jaylen Brown has answered the bell in spectacular fashion, pouring in 29.2 points, 7.0 rebounds, and 4.8 assists per game as the undisputed number-one option. Brown has proven he can carry a team, and his two-way impact has been the primary reason Boston remains a top seed despite losing their best player for an extended period. Jrue Holiday and Derrick White continue to provide the defensive backbone that makes this team competitive every night.

The Celtics have historically dominated this matchup recently, going 5-2 straight up in the last seven meetings. But those games didn't feature Luka Doncic in a Lakers uniform. His arrival fundamentally changes the dynamic of this rivalry, and the home crowd at Crypto.com Arena will be electric for this one. The moneyline at Lakers -117 and Celtics +113 tells you this is essentially a pick'em game, which is exactly what you'd expect from two elite teams in a rivalry game.

This has everything: star power, history, playoff implications, and a national broadcast on NBC. Brown vs. Doncic as the primary scoring matchup is appointment television, and the chess match between coaching staffs should be fascinating. The under might have some value here given both teams' defensive capabilities, but these are two offenses capable of erupting at any moment. Circle this one on your calendar.

This is a fascinating clash of styles between two teams with legitimate postseason aspirations. Minnesota at 35-22 has been one of the best defensive teams in the league all season, and Anthony Edwards continues to establish himself as one of the NBA's most electrifying players. Edwards' ability to attack the basket, pull up from three, and create for teammates has elevated the Wolves into consistent Western Conference contenders. Their defensive identity, anchored by strong rim protection and switchable perimeter defenders, makes Target Center one of the tougher places to play in the league.

Philadelphia at 30-25 is a team that looks dangerous when healthy, and the one-two punch of Joel Embiid and Tyrese Maxey gives them a ceiling that few teams can match. Embiid's dominance in the paint is well-documented, and his ability to draw fouls and get to the line makes him a nightmare for any defense to gameplan against. Maxey's evolution into a true star has been the story of the 76ers' season, and his speed and shooting create problems for defenses that can't keep up with his pace. The question, as always with Philly, is health. If both stars are available and fully engaged, this team can beat anyone.

The head-to-head trends are worth noting here. The over has hit in five of the last six meetings between these teams, which makes sense given the offensive talent on both rosters. The home team has covered in four straight matchups, which bodes well for Minnesota in this spot. The Wolves' home court advantage at Target Center has been substantial this season, and their crowd feeds off the energy Edwards brings on both ends. This should be an intense, competitive game between two teams that both view themselves as genuine title contenders, and the post-All-Star break intensity should be cranked up to another level.

New York at 35-21 is steamrolling everyone in their path right now. The Knicks have won 8 of their last 10 games and are averaging a ridiculous 124.5 points per game during that stretch. Jalen Brunson continues to be one of the best point guards in the league, Karl-Anthony Towns provides a dominant inside-out scoring presence, and the depth of this roster means there's no letup when the starters go to the bench.

Chicago at 24-32 is heading in the opposite direction. The Bulls are mired in a four-game losing streak and have shown very little fight against quality opponents this season. The 12.5-point spread is massive, but it feels warranted given where these two teams are right now. New York's offensive explosion over the last 10 games has been one of the most impressive stretches by any team this season, and Chicago simply doesn't have the defensive tools to slow them down. The 217.5 total looks low given New York's scoring prowess, but the Bulls' inability to generate offense on the other end keeps this from becoming a track meet. Expect the Knicks to build a big lead early, potentially hit cruise control in the fourth quarter, and make the spread interesting late. The question isn't whether New York wins, it's whether they win by enough to justify laying double digits on the road.

The nightcap on NBA TV features an Orlando team at 29-25 that has been quietly impressive this season despite dealing with significant injuries to their roster. The Magic's defensive identity has been their calling card all year, and Paolo Banchero has taken another leap as the franchise cornerstone. Orlando's ability to grind out low-scoring games and suffocate opponents' offenses makes them a tough out every single night, and their road record has been surprisingly strong. They're a team built for playoff basketball, and the post-All-Star break stretch gives them a chance to lock in their positioning.

The Clippers at 27-29 are going through a significant transition. The February 4 trade that sent James Harden to Cleveland brought Darius Garland to LA, and the Clippers are still figuring out how to integrate their new point guard into the system. Garland is a talented playmaker and scorer, but chemistry takes time, and coming out of the All-Star break, this roster is still learning to play together. The loss of Harden removes a proven ball-handler and shot creator, and while Garland is younger and offers upside, the adjustment period could lead to some growing pains in the short term.

Orlando being a 5.5-point road favorite tells you a lot about where the market thinks these teams are. The Magic's defense travels well, and their half-court execution has been methodical and efficient all season. The Clippers' new look roster needs time to gel, and facing a suffocating Orlando defense in their first game back from the break is a tough ask. The 218.5 total is the lowest on the board alongside the Portland-Phoenix game, reflecting both teams' defensive tendencies and the Clippers' potential offensive struggles with a new-look backcourt. Expect a grind-it-out affair that Orlando controls from start to finish.
