7-Game Sunday Slate

NBA March 15, 2026

Sunday's seven-game slate is headlined by a marquee ABC showdown in Oklahoma City where the defending champion 50-15 Thunder host the 40-24 Timberwolves. The surging 48-18 Detroit Pistons, owners of the best record in the East, travel to Toronto. The Warriors visit the 43-25 Knicks on NBC. Portland enters Philadelphia as a rare road favorite. Cleveland hosts the rebuilding Mavericks, Milwaukee looks to snap Indiana's 12-game skid, and the Jazz visit the Kings in a late-night West Coast battle of lottery teams.

Game 1

Timberwolves @ Thunder

Sunday, 1:00 PM ET | Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, OK
Spread
OKC -9.5
Moneyline
OKC -420 / MIN +330
Total
O/U 226.5

This is the headliner of Sunday's slate, and it carries the weight you'd expect from a potential Western Conference playoff preview. The defending champion Thunder at 50-15 have the best record in the NBA and have been absolutely ruthless at Paycom Center this season. OKC's Defensive Rating has been among the league's elite all year, and their ability to suffocate opponents with length, athleticism, and relentless switching makes them a nightmare for any team walking into their building. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander continues to play at an MVP level, orchestrating an offense that ranks among the top five in efficiency while anchoring a defense that gives opposing guards nightmares in the half court.

Minnesota at 40-24 is a legitimate contender in the West, and this is exactly the kind of measuring stick game that tells you where you stand in March. The Timberwolves have the defensive identity and physicality to hang with anyone, and Anthony Edwards has been brilliant in his continued ascension as one of the league's most dynamic two-way players. But here's the problem for Minnesota: OKC at home is a different animal. The Thunder's home record has been absurd, and the 9.5-point spread reflects a market that fully believes OKC can separate from quality opponents at Paycom Center. The Wolves will need to be elite defensively and limit transition opportunities, because the Thunder are devastating in the open floor.

The 226.5 total is moderate for two teams that can both score in bunches, but it speaks to the defensive caliber of this matchup. Both teams rank in the top ten defensively, and the half-court chess match between these two coaching staffs should be fascinating. Minnesota has the size and length to contest at the rim, but OKC's ball movement and three-point shooting create open looks that even the best defenses struggle to eliminate. This game will likely be decided by which team executes better in the clutch, and the Thunder's home crowd will be a factor that can't be overstated on a Sunday afternoon national TV stage.

What makes this so compelling is the playoff subtext. These teams could easily meet in the second round or the Western Conference Finals, and how Minnesota performs in this environment, down nearly double digits before the ball is tipped, will say everything about their readiness for a deep postseason run. The Thunder have earned their status as the class of the league, but the Wolves are one of the few teams in basketball with the personnel and the mentality to push OKC to the limit. The question is whether they can do it on the road against a team that's been nearly unbeatable in its own building.

Mavericks @ Cavaliers

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, OH
Spread
CLE -13.5
Moneyline
CLE -750 / DAL +525
Total
O/U 236.5

CLE -13.5 is one of the largest spreads on Sunday's board, and it's entirely justified when you look at where these franchises are. Dallas at 22-45 is deep in the rebuild after trading Luka Doncic to the Lakers in February 2025, and the results have been painful. Anthony Davis, who came to Dallas as the centerpiece return in that blockbuster deal, has been injured for much of the 2025-26 season, leaving the Mavericks without the star talent they'd hoped would ease the Luka transition. Cooper Flagg, the No. 1 overall pick in the 2025 draft, has shown flashes of generational ability, but rookies don't win games in March against teams fighting for playoff positioning. Dallas is playing for ping pong balls at this point, and it shows.

Cleveland at 41-26 has been one of the steadier teams in the Eastern Conference, and their home court at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse has been a fortress. Donovan Mitchell continues to be one of the most explosive scorers in the league, and the supporting cast around him has been dependable enough to keep the Cavaliers in the thick of the East playoff race. The Cavs' Offensive Rating at home has been well above league average, and their ability to pour points on teams without the defensive personnel to slow them down makes this spread feel manageable. Dallas doesn't have the backcourt defenders to stay in front of Mitchell, and that's going to be a problem from the opening whistle.

The 236.5 total is the highest on Sunday's board, reflecting the expectation that Cleveland's offense will feast against Dallas's porous defense while the Mavericks' young players will try to run and gun to stay competitive. That's a recipe for a high-scoring game where the final margin is likely larger than the running score suggests. Dallas will compete in stretches, because young teams always do, but the talent gap here is massive. When one team has a franchise scorer and the other has a rookie learning on the fly, games tend to get out of hand in the third quarter when the home team's depth and experience take over.

The -750 moneyline tells you everything about the market's confidence in Cleveland. This is the type of game where the Cavaliers should be able to empty the bench in the fourth quarter and still cover comfortably. Flagg's development is the only real storyline worth watching from Dallas's perspective, and while his talent is undeniable, he's being asked to carry an impossible burden on a roster that simply isn't built to compete yet. Cleveland will take care of business here, and the question is whether the margin eclipses the 13.5-point spread or falls just short.

Game 3

Pistons @ Raptors

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena, Toronto, ON
Spread
DET -3.5
Moneyline
DET -156 / TOR +150
Total
O/U 220.5

If you told anyone three years ago that the Detroit Pistons would have the best record in the Eastern Conference in March 2026, they would have laughed you out of the room. And yet here we are. Detroit at 48-18 has been the story of the NBA this season, a team that went from laughingstock to legitimate championship contender in a stunningly short window. Cade Cunningham's development into an All-Star caliber player has been the catalyst, and the roster construction around him has been masterful. The Pistons play with a defensive intensity and offensive cohesion that belies their youth, and they've been dominant enough to hold the top seed in the East with 16 games remaining.

Toronto at 37-29 is no pushover, and Brandon Ingram has given the Raptors a legitimate offensive weapon since arriving from New Orleans in February 2025. Ingram's ability to score from all three levels, create for himself in isolation, and operate as a secondary playmaker has transformed Toronto's ceiling. At 37-29, the Raptors are firmly in the playoff picture, and Scotiabank Arena will be electric for a Sunday afternoon matchup against the team with the best record in the conference. Toronto's home record has been solid this season, and they have the kind of length and versatility that can bother Detroit's offensive actions.

The DET -3.5 spread on the road tells you everything about how the market views the Pistons' dominance this season. Being a 3.5-point road favorite in a hostile building like Toronto is a statement. Detroit's defense has been one of the best in the league, ranking in the top five in Defensive Rating, and their ability to switch, contest, and protect the rim makes them a matchup nightmare for most opponents. Toronto will need Ingram to be brilliant, and they'll need their supporting cast to hit shots, because the Pistons don't give you many easy looks.

The 220.5 total is the lowest on Sunday's board, and it reflects the defensive identity that both teams bring to the table. Detroit's defense is elite, and Toronto has been surprisingly stingy at home this season. This has the feel of a grind-it-out affair where possessions matter, turnovers are costly, and the team that executes in the half court with more precision will walk away with the win. The Pistons have proven all season that they can win games exactly like this, but playing at Scotiabank Arena against a Raptors team fighting for seeding is a different beast than beating up on bottom feeders at Little Caesars Arena.

Game 4

Pacers @ Bucks

Sunday, 3:30 PM ET | Fiserv Forum, Milwaukee, WI
Spread
MIL -7.5
Moneyline
MIL -294 / IND +235
Total
O/U 228.5

Indiana's 12-game losing streak is one of the most depressing storylines in the NBA right now, and it's hard to see the light at the end of the tunnel when you're 15-52 and walking into Fiserv Forum on a Sunday afternoon. The Pacers have been gutted by Tyrese Haliburton's Achilles injury, which robbed them of their franchise point guard and the heartbeat of their offense. Without Haliburton, Indiana's playmaking has cratered, their half-court offense has become stagnant, and the young players left to pick up the pieces simply don't have the experience to close games. Twelve straight losses tells you everything about where this roster is without its best player.

Milwaukee at 27-39 has had a profoundly disappointing season by their standards, but the Bucks still have Giannis Antetokounmpo, and that alone makes them a formidable home opponent against a team in Indiana's state of disarray. The Bucks haven't lived up to expectations this year, and at 27-39, the playoff picture is slipping away. But there's a difference between being disappointed in your season and being flat-out bad, and Milwaukee has shown they can still beat the teams they're supposed to beat, especially at home. Giannis's ability to attack the rim and generate easy baskets should overwhelm an Indiana defense that has been among the worst in the league.

The MIL -7.5 spread feels right for a matchup between a desperate home team and a squad on a 12-game slide, but the 228.5 total is the number that catches the eye. Indiana's games have trended toward higher totals because they can't get stops, and Milwaukee with Giannis driving to the basket in transition should generate plenty of easy points. The Pacers will try to run, because that's all they know, but their lack of a true floor general means the offense devolves into isolation ball and contested jumpers when the pace slows down. Milwaukee should control this game from the second quarter on.

Here's the uncomfortable truth for Indiana: even at 27-39, the Bucks are a significantly more talented team from top to bottom. Giannis is a cheat code when he's engaged, and Milwaukee's role players have enough experience to punish a young team that's mentally and physically exhausted from losing 12 straight. The Pacers need the season to end, and games like this are the painful reminder that March basketball can be cruel when you're on the wrong side of the ledger. Milwaukee should handle this without too much stress, and the real question is whether the margin is comfortable enough to cover the 7.5.

Trail Blazers @ 76ers

Sunday, 6:00 PM ET | Wells Fargo Center, Philadelphia, PA
Spread
POR -6.5
Moneyline
POR -260 / PHI +210
Total
O/U 222.5

This is the most fascinating number on Sunday's board. Portland at 32-35, a team that's been hovering around .500 for much of the season, is a 6.5-point road favorite in Philadelphia. Let that sink in. The Trail Blazers, a team you'd expect to be a slight underdog in most road games, are laying nearly a touchdown at Wells Fargo Center. That tells you everything about the current state of the 76ers, and it's not a pretty picture. Philadelphia at 36-31 has the better overall record, but the spread strongly suggests the market is pricing in significant injury or rest concerns that have flipped the power dynamic in this matchup.

Portland's season has been a strange ride. At 32-35, the Trail Blazers aren't bad, but they aren't particularly good either. They've hovered in the play-in range in the Western Conference, winning enough to stay relevant without ever stringing together the kind of run that would push them into legitimate contender status. Being installed as a 6.5-point road favorite is unusual territory for Portland, and it speaks to whatever Philadelphia is dealing with on the health front. When the road team is laying this kind of number against a team with a better record, the market is screaming that something is off with the home team.

The intrigue here is entirely in the line. Historically, road favorites of 6.5 or more points in the NBA are uncommon, and they almost always correlate with a major injury or rest situation for the home team. Whatever is happening in Philadelphia's locker room or training room has shifted the dynamics of this game dramatically, and Portland is in position to take advantage. The Trail Blazers play hard, they compete on both ends, and they have enough offensive firepower to exploit a shorthanded opponent. The question is whether they can maintain focus as a heavy road favorite, a role they're not accustomed to playing.

Philadelphia at 36-31 has had a rollercoaster season regardless of whatever is happening today. The 76ers' inconsistency has been a recurring theme, and their inability to stay healthy has plagued them for multiple seasons running. When fully healthy, Philly is a dangerous team with the talent to beat anyone. But health has been the achilles heel of this franchise, and games like this, where they're massive home underdogs, are the painful consequence. Portland should approach this with the confidence of a team that's been given a gift by the market, and execute accordingly.

Warriors @ Knicks

Moneyline

Sunday night at the Garden. There are few stages in basketball that match Madison Square Garden under the lights for a primetime NBC broadcast, and the Knicks at 43-25 have given their fans plenty to cheer about this season. New York has been one of the best teams in the Eastern Conference, and their physicality, toughness, and defensive identity have made them a legitimate championship contender. Playing at home against the Warriors on national television is exactly the kind of game this roster thrives in, because this team feeds off the energy of the MSG crowd and plays with a purpose that elevates when the spotlight is brightest.

Golden State at 32-34 is clinging to play-in positioning in the Western Conference, and the loss of Jimmy Butler to a torn ACL has been devastating. Butler came to the Warriors in February 2025, and for a brief stretch, it looked like his veteran presence and two-way brilliance would transform Golden State's trajectory. But the ACL tear ended his season and left the Warriors without their best acquisition in years. Stephen Curry is still Stephen Curry, capable of erupting for 40 on any given night, but the supporting cast around him has been inconsistent, and the Warriors' record reflects a team that's been fighting just to stay afloat in a brutally competitive Western Conference.

The Knicks as home favorites makes perfect sense given the records and circumstances. New York's home record has been outstanding, and the MSG crowd creates an atmosphere that visiting teams genuinely struggle with. The Warriors, without Butler, are a perimeter-oriented team that lives and dies by the three-point shot, and the Knicks' physical, switch-heavy defense is designed to take away the exact looks Golden State craves. Curry will get his moments, because he always does, but the question is whether anyone else on the Warriors' roster can generate enough offense to keep pace with a deep, balanced New York attack.

This has the feel of a game that New York controls with defensive intensity and rebounding dominance. The Knicks are bigger and more physical at nearly every position, and they have the kind of half-court offense that can grind possessions and keep the score in a range that favors the home team. Golden State's best chance is to turn this into a track meet, because the Warriors still have the shooting to light up the scoreboard in transition. But the Knicks don't allow transition offense. They force teams into the half court, where New York's size and toughness become overwhelming advantages. The Garden should be rocking for this one.

Game 7

Jazz @ Kings

Sunday, 10:00 PM ET | Golden 1 Center, Sacramento, CA
Spread
SAC -2.5
Moneyline
SAC -138 / UTA +118
Total
O/U 230.5

The late-night West Coast special features two teams that are firmly in the lottery race, and this is the kind of game that only the most dedicated NBA fans will still be watching at midnight on the East Coast. Utah at 19-45 has been in full rebuild mode all season, developing young talent and accumulating draft assets for a future that looks brighter than the present. Sacramento at 15-50 has the worst record in the NBA, and the trade that sent De'Aaron Fox to San Antonio in February 2025 was the moment the Kings officially hit the reset button. Zach LaVine came back in that deal, but he hasn't been the answer Sacramento needed, and the Kings have been historically bad this season.

The fascinating subplot here is Sacramento's complete organizational upheaval. The Spurs' current point guard was the face of the Kings franchise before the February 2025 trade, the player who made the Kings relevant again, and his departure left a void that LaVine simply hasn't been able to fill. LaVine is a talented scorer, there's no question about that, but he doesn't elevate the players around him the way Fox did, and the Kings' record reflects a team that lost its identity when its best player was traded away. At 15-50, Sacramento is on pace for one of the worst seasons in franchise history, and that's saying something for a franchise that has seen its share of dark years.

Utah's rebuild has been more methodical, and at 19-45, the Jazz are at least playing competitive basketball more consistently than their record suggests. The young players in Utah's pipeline have shown enough growth to suggest the foundation is being laid correctly, even if the wins aren't there yet. This is a game where both teams are essentially auditioning young talent for the future, and the outcome matters more for draft lottery positioning than for any meaningful standings implications. Neither team has anything to play for besides development and pride, and those games can be entertaining or unwatchable depending on the night.

Whatever the spread lands at, this game is likely going to be a coin flip between two teams that are evenly matched in their mediocrity. Sacramento has home-court advantage, for whatever that's worth when you're 15-50 and Golden 1 Center is half-empty on a Sunday night. Utah will bring their typical competitive effort, and the Jazz's young players will try to use this game as a proving ground. For the rest of the basketball world, this is the game you fall asleep to, and that's perfectly fine. Not every game needs to be a marquee matchup. Sometimes the NBA's late-night offering is exactly what it looks like: two rebuilding teams grinding through a long season.
