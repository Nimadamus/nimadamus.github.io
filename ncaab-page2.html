Players Era Festival 2025

Elite Matchups Headline Las Vegas Showcase

The 2025 Players Era Festival delivers one of college basketballs most anticipated early-season events at the MGM Grand Garden Arena and Michelob ULTRA Arena in Las Vegas. With $1 million in NIL funds to all participating teams and an extra $1 million to the winner, the stakes are sky-high. Monday features a loaded slate headlined by No. 11 Alabama vs No. 13 Gonzaga at 9:30 PM ET on TNT, plus Kansas-Notre Dame, Houston-Syracuse, Oregon-Auburn, and more. This is where programs make statements and March narratives begin to form.

Todays slate spans from noon through midnight with 60 college basketball games across the country, but the Players Era Festival games are the crown jewels. Tennessee faces Rutgers, Baylor meets Creighton, St. Johns battles Iowa State, and Michigan clashes with San Diego State before the marquee Alabama-Gonzaga showdown. Were breaking down the biggest matchups with complete verified stats and key players to watch.

Featured Game: Alabama vs Gonzaga

vs

#11 Alabama Crimson Tide vs #13 Gonzaga Bulldogs

9:30 PM ET | TNT | MGM Grand Garden Arena, Las Vegas

This matchup has been ranked as the best college basketball game of feast week, and for good reason. Alabama enters at 3-1 with the nations seventh-best offense according to KenPoms adjusted offensive efficiency. The Crimson Tide boast an elite backcourt led by All-American candidate Labaron Philon, who exploded for 24 points in their win over No. 8 Illinois. Aden Holloway, Houston Mallette, and Latrell Wrightsell add depth and shooting, while 6-foot-10 forwards Taylor Bol Bowen and Aiden Sherrell can stretch the floor with their three-point shooting.

Gonzaga enters a perfect 5-0 with one of the nations top defensive units. Graduate student forward Graham Ike leads the team at 17 points and 9.2 rebounds per game. Coach Nate Oats compared Gonzagas frontcourt of Graham Ike and Braden Huff to NBA legends Kevin McHale and Robert Parish. The Bulldogs have allowed only 57.8 points per game while averaging 94 points themselves, and they lead with an elite 46.4 rebounds per game.

Alabama shoots 35.3 three-pointers per game - the sixth highest mark in college basketball. That volume meets Gonzagas adjusted defensive efficiency ranked at No. 5 overall, while Alabamas defense sits at No. 37. The Bulldogs length and versatility with Jalen Warley, Emmanuel Innocenti, Tyon Grant-Foster, and Mario Saint-Supery will test Alabamas perimeter attack. This is an elite matchup with national championship implications.
