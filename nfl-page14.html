Eagles @ Commanders

2025-12-20T22:00Z | Northwest Stadium | FOX
Line: PHI -7 | O/U: 44.5 | PHI: 9-5 | WSH: 4-10

The Eagles can clinch the NFC East title with a victory today at Northwest Stadium. Philadelphia is a 7-point road favorite, and this line reflects the massive blow Washington just suffered. Jayden Daniels has been ruled out for the rest of the season, leaving Marcus Mariota to start under center for the Commanders. That's a devastating loss for a Washington offense that was built around Daniels' dual-threat ability.

Jalen Hurts has been exceptional down the stretch, and the Eagles' offense is clicking at the perfect time. Philadelphia's defense has been opportunistic all season, and against a backup quarterback making his first meaningful start, expect aggressive play-calling from Vic Fangio's unit. The Commanders are 0-2 ATS as 7-point underdogs or more this season, and that trend should continue here.

Washington has actually covered in 4 of the last 5 meetings between these teams, but those games featured Daniels at quarterback. With Mariota under center and the Eagles desperate to lock up the division, Philadelphia should handle business and cover the spread in a game that could get ugly by the fourth quarter.

Packers @ Bears

2025-12-21T01:20Z | Soldier Field | FOX
Line: CHI -1.5 | O/U: 44.5 | GB: 9-4-1 | CHI: 10-4

This is the game everyone's been waiting for. The oldest rivalry in the NFL takes center stage on Saturday Night Football with first place in the NFC North on the line. The Bears lead the division by half a game over the Packers, and a Chicago win combined with a Lions loss would clinch a playoff spot. The stakes couldn't be higher at Soldier Field tonight.

Caleb Williams has been phenomenal in year two under Ben Johnson, throwing for 3,150 yards with 21 touchdowns and only 6 interceptions. Last week's 31-3 demolition of Cleveland saw Williams post a career-best 90.2 PFF passing grade. Meanwhile, Green Bay is dealing with significant injuries. Josh Jacobs is expected to miss the game, Zach Tom is likely out with a knee injury, and Jordan Love is playing through a separated left shoulder.

The line opened with Green Bay favored at -2.5 but has swung all the way to Bears -1.5 at most books. That movement tells you where the sharp money is going. Chicago is 9-2-1 ATS in their last 12 games and has won five straight at Soldier Field. The Packers have owned this rivalry for 17 years, going 15-2 SU at Soldier Field since 2008, but this Bears team is different. Ben Johnson's offense has transformed Chicago into a legitimate contender.
