Crystal Palace vs West Ham

Monday, 8:00 PM BST / 3:00 PM ET | Selhurst Park, London

Oliver Glasner's Crystal Palace welcome Graham Potter's West Ham to Selhurst Park in a London derby that doubles as a relegation-zone measuring stick for the Hammers. Palace are priced at 29/20 to win. West Ham are 2/1. The draw is 11/5. Palace come off a 2-1 home win over Newcastle where Jean-Philippe Mateta scored both goals with five shots on target, and they also qualified for the Europa Conference League semi-finals with a 4-2 aggregate win over Fiorentina despite losing the second leg 2-1 in Italy. The double-competition calendar has tested Glasner's rotation options, but the current form trend is the strongest of any club in the bottom half of the Premier League table.

West Ham's 4-0 home win over Wolves last weekend put the Hammers in position to climb out of the relegation zone, and a result at Selhurst would essentially guarantee Premier League survival with five games left. Potter's tactical pivot toward a compact mid-block with quick transition through Mohammed Kudus has produced the kind of possession-light, chance-heavy attacking pattern that their roster was actually built for. Jarrod Bowen's goal-threat numbers continue to be Premier League-elite per ninety minutes. The core question for the Hammers is whether their defensive structure holds against a Palace attack that has been scoring at a well-above-par rate across the last six weeks.

Mateta is the Palace player of the season. His aerial presence in the box, ability to hold up play in tight spaces, and finishing efficiency have pushed his goal tally into double digits across all competitions. Eberechi Eze remains the creative engine, though his usage has been managed across the European fixtures. Marc Guehi's central-defensive leadership sets the tone for Palace's defensive structure, and Dean Henderson in goal has produced save-percentage numbers among the league's best since February. The central midfield partnership of Adam Wharton and Daichi Kamada has given Palace more control in games where they dominate possession without being able to finish chances.

The betting markets are pricing the game close. Both-teams-to-score at 1.63 reflects the expectation that West Ham will generate their chances through Bowen and Kudus even if Palace dominate overall shape. The Racing Post and William Hill previews both suggest Palace home win with goals is the modal outcome given the Hammers' need to chase the game. Palace enter with more rest tactically but a heavier fixture load overall. West Ham's six-day rest from the Wolves win is the kind of preparation window that suits Potter's training-ground approach. Kickoff 8:00 PM BST / 3:00 PM ET.
