#17 UNC @ #1 Duke

Saturday, 6:30 PM ET | Cameron Indoor Stadium, Durham, NC
Spread
DUKE -17.5
Moneyline
UNC vs DUKE
Total
O/U 146.5

This is the game everyone circles on the calendar, and the 2026 regular season finale delivers the Tobacco Road Rivalry with everything on the line. The top-ranked Duke Blue Devils (28-2, 16-1 ACC) host #17 North Carolina (24-6, 12-5 ACC) at Cameron Indoor Stadium in a 6:30 PM tip on ESPN. Duke has been the best team in college basketball for virtually the entire season, and the Blue Devils are looking to lock up the overall No. 1 seed in the NCAA Tournament heading into conference tournament play. But there's an extra layer of intensity here, because UNC handed Duke one of its only two losses this season with a 71-68 victory in Chapel Hill on February 7th. That defeat still stings, and Cameron Indoor will be a cauldron of noise for the regular season finale.

The 17.5-point spread is massive for a rivalry game, and it tells you everything about the talent gap that's been created by one crucial injury. Caleb Wilson, who scored 23 points in UNC's February victory over Duke, is done for the season with a thumb injury. Wilson was the Tar Heels' best player and their primary offensive weapon, and without him, UNC is a fundamentally different team. The Tar Heels have still managed to win games in his absence thanks to their defensive intensity and Hubert Davis's ability to get production from his remaining roster, but the ceiling is dramatically lower when your best scorer is in street clothes on the bench. That February game looks like a lifetime ago now.

Duke's depth and firepower make them a nightmare matchup for a diminished UNC roster. The Blue Devils have been dominant at home in Cameron Indoor all season, and the combination of the rivalry atmosphere, the revenge factor from that February loss, and UNC's personnel limitations could create the conditions for a blowout. Duke's defense has been suffocating in ACC play, and without Wilson to stretch the floor and create his own shot, UNC is going to struggle to generate consistent offense against the most talented team in the country. The 146.5 total is relatively low, which makes sense given Duke's defensive identity and the likelihood that the Blue Devils control tempo and pace in front of their home crowd.

Even at 17.5 points, this feels like a game where Duke comes out with something to prove. The Blue Devils remember that February loss, and the Cameron Indoor crowd will be at absolute maximum intensity for the final home game of the regular season. Rivalry games always carry an element of unpredictability, and UNC has the kind of pride and toughness that makes them fight to the final whistle regardless of the score. But talent wins in March, and Duke has an enormous talent advantage in this matchup without Wilson on the floor for the Tar Heels. This should be a coronation of Duke's regular season at Cameron Indoor.

The Big East's best travel to Milwaukee for a noon window showcase on FOX as the #4 UConn Huskies (27-3, 17-2 Big East) visit Marquette at Fiserv Forum. UConn has been the class of the Big East all season long, and this game represents a chance to put an exclamation point on a dominant regular season. The Huskies already handled Marquette in their first meeting, winning 73-57 in Storrs behind a stifling defensive performance that held the Golden Eagles to just 38.5% shooting. Dan Hurley's team is playing with the kind of confidence and swagger that comes from knowing you're the best team in the building every night.

Marquette has been inconsistent in Big East play, and the gap between their ceiling and floor has been frustrating for Shaka Smart's coaching staff all season. When the Golden Eagles are locked in defensively and sharing the basketball, they can hang with almost anyone in the conference. But the 73-57 loss in Storrs exposed the fundamental talent gap that exists between these programs right now. UConn's depth, size, and defensive versatility are simply on a different level, and the Huskies have the kind of lineup flexibility that makes them hard to game plan against.

The 9.5-point spread on the road feels like a statement about where UConn stands relative to the rest of the Big East. Laying nearly double digits in a road conference game is a lot to ask, but UConn has earned that kind of respect with their performance this season. The 143.5 total is one of the lower numbers on the board, and that's a reflection of UConn's suffocating defense and the likelihood that this game is played in the half-court at a methodical pace. Marquette's home crowd at Fiserv Forum will make this louder than the first meeting, but it's hard to see the Golden Eagles bridging a 16-point gap in talent and execution from the first encounter.

The SEC regular season finale sends #5 Florida (24-6, 15-2 SEC) into the hostile environment of Rupp Arena to face Kentucky (19-11, 10-7 SEC) in a 4:00 PM tip on ESPN. The Gators have been the SEC's best team this season and arrive in Lexington riding a stunning seven-game road winning streak that underscores just how dangerous this team is away from Gainesville. Florida's 15-2 conference record has them locked into a top seed in the SEC Tournament, but Todd Golden's squad isn't the type to cruise into the postseason on autopilot. They've played with an edge and intensity all year that's made them one of the most complete teams in the country.

Kentucky's season has been a tale of unfulfilled expectations under Mark Pope. At 19-11 overall and 10-7 in the SEC, the Wildcats are firmly on the NCAA Tournament bubble and desperately need quality wins in their final opportunities. A victory over #5 Florida at home would be a massive resume boost and could be the difference between dancing in March and watching from home. The 158.5 total is the highest on the NCAAB board today, and it reflects the up-tempo style that both teams prefer. This should be a fast, high-scoring affair with both offenses pushing in transition whenever possible.

Florida being a 5.5-point road favorite at Rupp Arena tells you everything about the current state of both programs. The Gators are simply the more talented, deeper, and better-coached team right now, and their road record backs that up. Kentucky will get an energy boost from the Rupp Arena crowd, and Senior Night emotions can fuel some improbable performances, but Florida has proven all season that environment doesn't rattle them. The Gators have won at hostile venues throughout the SEC schedule, and the 5.5-point spread feels like a fair assessment of the gap between these teams. Florida is the better team, and they've earned the right to be favored even in the most intimidating road environment in college basketball.

The #7 Houston Cougars travel to Gallagher-Iba Arena to close out the Big 12 regular season against Oklahoma State in a noon tip on CBS. Houston has been one of the most dominant defensive teams in college basketball all season, and Kelvin Sampson's squad is looking to secure the No. 2 seed in the Big 12 Tournament with a strong finish. The Cougars' identity hasn't changed since they became a national powerhouse: relentless defensive pressure, elite rebounding, and just enough offense to grind opponents into submission. That formula has produced another outstanding season and a legitimate Final Four contender.

Oklahoma State has been rebuilding in Stillwater, and the Cowboys find themselves as massive 12.5-point home underdogs in their own building. The -835 moneyline on Houston tells you just how wide the talent gap is in this matchup. Gallagher-Iba Arena is one of the great old-school college basketball venues in the country, and the atmosphere can make life uncomfortable for visiting teams, but the Cowboys simply don't have the personnel to compete with Houston over 40 minutes. The 148.5 total is modest, which makes sense given Houston's defensive intensity and their tendency to slow games down to a crawl when they want to.

Houston's defense is the story here. The Cougars rank among the top five nationally in adjusted defensive efficiency, and they make life miserable for every team they face. Oklahoma State will struggle to generate clean looks against Houston's switching and help defense, and the turnover battle should be lopsided in the Cougars' favor. The 12.5-point spread is steep for a road game, but Houston has the kind of defensive ceiling that can produce blowouts against overmatched opponents. If the Cougars get even average offensive production on top of their elite defense, this one could get ugly early.

The Sunflower Showdown arrives at Allen Fieldhouse as the #14 Kansas Jayhawks (21-9, 11-6 Big 12) host Kansas State in a 2:00 PM tip on CBS. Kansas has been virtually unbeatable at home this season with a 12-2 record inside Allen Fieldhouse, one of the most daunting environments in all of college basketball. The Jayhawks' season has been somewhat uneven by their historically elite standards, as 21-9 overall and 11-6 in the best conference in America suggests a team that's been good but not great. Still, Bill Self's ability to get his team playing its best basketball in March is well documented, and a dominant Senior Day performance against the rival Wildcats would generate valuable momentum heading into the Big 12 Tournament.

Kansas State has had a difficult season in the Big 12, and the 16.5-point spread reflects just how challenging it is to walk into Allen Fieldhouse and compete with a motivated Kansas team on Senior Day. The rivalry adds emotional intensity to this matchup, but the talent gap is significant. Kansas has the kind of size, depth, and defensive versatility that can overwhelm smaller conference opponents, and the Jayhawks tend to treat rivalry games with an extra level of focus and intensity. The 155.5 total suggests a fast-paced game where Kansas's transition offense could create separation in a hurry.

Allen Fieldhouse on Senior Day is one of the toughest tickets in college basketball, and the atmosphere will be electric from the opening tip. Kansas has historically dominated this rivalry at home, and the combination of Senior Day emotions, the rivalry factor, and Kansas's home court dominance creates a perfect storm for the Jayhawks. Kansas State will compete and fight, because that's what rivalry games demand, but they're simply outmatched in terms of talent and depth. Look for Kansas to come out aggressively, establish their interior presence early, and feed off the Allen Fieldhouse energy to pull away in the second half.

Iowa State's defensive identity is built on pressure, switching, and forcing opponents into difficult decisions with the shot clock winding down, and that formula has been devastatingly effective in Big 12 play.

Arizona State is coming into Ames as a 15.5-point underdog with a +875 moneyline, and those numbers accurately reflect the enormous gap between these programs right now. The Sun Devils have struggled mightily in their first season of Big 12 play after the conference expansion, and a trip to Hilton Coliseum, Hilton Magic as it's known, is arguably the toughest road assignment in the league. Iowa State feeds off the energy of their home crowd like few programs in the country, and the Cyclone faithful will be fired up for the final home game of the regular season.

The 148.5 total is low, and that's a direct reflection of Iowa State's suffocating defense. The Cyclones rarely allow opponents to score comfortably, and they control pace and tempo in ways that limit the total number of possessions in a game. Arizona State is going to struggle to find any offensive rhythm against Iowa State's switching defense, and the Cyclones have the kind of defensive depth where they can throw different looks at opponents without sacrificing intensity. This should be a wire-to-wire Iowa State victory in front of a raucous Hilton Coliseum crowd.

This is the best rivalry game nobody outside the SEC is talking about. #23 Tennessee hosts #24 Vanderbilt at Thompson-Boling Arena in a 2:00 PM tip on ESPN, and both teams are ranked and playing for NCAA Tournament seeding. This is a game that carries genuine stakes for both programs. Tennessee has been grinding through a competitive SEC schedule all season, and the Volunteers' defensive identity under Rick Barnes has been their calling card. At home in Knoxville, Tennessee is a completely different team, and the Rocky Top faithful create one of the loudest environments in the SEC.

Vanderbilt's emergence as a ranked team this season has been one of the best stories in college basketball. The Commodores have been competitive in every game they've played in the SEC, and earning a ranking heading into the final weekend of the regular season is a testament to Jerry Stackhouse's program building in Nashville. However, traveling to Thompson-Boling Arena is a different proposition entirely. Tennessee's home court advantage is real, and the 4.5-point spread suggests the market respects both teams but gives Tennessee the edge in their own building.

The 141.5 total is one of the lowest on the board, and that tells you everything about how these teams play. This game is going to be physical, it's going to be intense, and it's going to be played in the 60s. The rivalry adds an extra layer of emotion that can lead to sloppy possessions and missed shots from the pressure, which further supports the under. Tennessee's home court advantage should be the deciding factor in a close, grind-it-out SEC battle.

The Commonwealth Clash sends Virginia Tech to John Paul Jones Arena to face #13 Virginia in a noon tip on the CW Network. Virginia's season has been a return to form under Tony Bennett, and the Cavaliers have played the kind of disciplined, patient basketball that made them national champions in 2019. Virginia's pack-line defense remains one of the most effective defensive systems in college basketball, and opponents routinely struggle to generate efficient offense against the Cavaliers' halfcourt structure. At home in Charlottesville, Virginia has been dominant, and the 11.5-point spread reflects their clear superiority in this in-state rivalry.

Virginia Tech has been one of the more disappointing teams in the ACC this season, and closing the regular season with a trip to JPJ against a ranked Virginia team is a tough draw. The Hokies simply haven't been able to match Virginia's consistency and defensive intensity, and the rivalry factor alone won't be enough to bridge the talent and execution gap that exists between these programs right now. The total of 124.5 is the lowest number on the entire college basketball board today, and that's quintessential Virginia basketball. Bennett's teams don't allow possessions to go to waste, and they're perfectly content to play a 55-52 game if that's what it takes to win.

Virginia's defense will dictate the tempo and the outcome. The Cavaliers force opponents into long possessions where every pass and cut has to be perfect, and that level of defensive discipline wears teams down over 40 minutes. Virginia Tech is going to struggle to generate any offensive rhythm against the pack-line defense, and the Hokies' frustration could lead to forced shots and turnovers as the game progresses. Virginia should control this one from start to finish in front of their home crowd, making the Senior Day celebration a comfortable one in Charlottesville.
