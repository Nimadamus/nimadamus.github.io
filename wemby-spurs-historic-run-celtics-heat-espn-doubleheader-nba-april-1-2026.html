9-Game Wednesday Slate

NBA Previews Today

Wednesday's nine-game slate features an ESPN doubleheader anchored by the 57-18 Spurs visiting Golden State as Victor Wembanyama rides a scorching 9-game winning streak into a Warriors team gutted by injuries. The early ESPN window brings Celtics at Heat in a classic Eastern Conference rivalry. Massive spreads dot the board with five double-digit lines, the Nuggets and Jazz project for a 249-point total, and the Pacers and Bulls promise a defense-optional affair at 247. Philadelphia visits Washington, Atlanta travels to Orlando in a playoff-race battle, the Knicks head to Memphis, Toronto hosts Sacramento, and Milwaukee faces Houston.

Game 1

76ers @ Wizards

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena, Washington, DC
Spread
PHI -15.5
Moneyline
PHI -1250 / WAS +850
Total
O/U 240.0

A 15.5-point spread in the NBA is the kind of number that makes you do a double take, but one look at Washington's 17-58 record and it starts to make perfect sense. The Wizards own the worst record in basketball and they've earned it, playing a brand of basketball that would generously be described as developmental and more accurately described as unwatchable for anyone not directly invested in the franchise's lottery odds. Washington has been gutted by injuries tonight with Trae Young (quad), Anthony Davis (hand), and D'Angelo Russell all sitting out, plus three more players listed as questionable. There simply isn't enough talent on the floor to compete with a motivated Philadelphia squad.

The 76ers at 41-34 are getting healthier at exactly the right time, with both Paul George and Tyrese Maxey returning to the lineup for this one. Philadelphia's season has been a rollercoaster of injuries and inconsistency, but when George and Maxey are both on the floor together, this team has the kind of offensive firepower and defensive versatility that can give anyone trouble. Johni Broome remains out, but the returning star power more than compensates. Maxey's speed and scoring ability combined with George's two-way impact should overwhelm a Wizards defense that ranks near the bottom of the league in virtually every meaningful category.

The 240-point total is elevated and reflects the expectation that both teams will score freely, albeit for very different reasons. Philadelphia should generate quality looks with ease against Washington's porous defense, and the Wizards' offense tends to play at a frantic pace that produces possessions even when the quality isn't there. This game projects to be a track meet where the Sixers control the proceedings from the opening tip while Washington's young players chuck up shots with reckless abandon. Don't be surprised if the first quarter alone tells you everything you need to know about how this one is going to unfold.

Laying 15.5 points always feels uncomfortable in the NBA because of the three-point line and garbage time, but the talent disparity here is genuinely staggering. Philadelphia has playoff positioning to fight for and returning stars looking to shake off rust, while Washington is playing out the string with a depleted roster that would struggle in the G League on some nights. The Sixers should treat this as a glorified practice session, build a massive lead early, and the only real drama will be whether the bench unit can maintain the margin in the fourth quarter when the starters get an early rest day.

Game 2

Hawks @ Magic

Wednesday, 7:00 PM ET | Kia Center, Orlando, FL
Spread
ATL -4.5
Moneyline
ATL -185 / ORL +150
Total
O/U 233.5

This is one of the more consequential games on Wednesday's slate for the Eastern Conference playoff picture. Atlanta at 43-33 has put together a strong season and is firmly in the postseason conversation, but every game matters when you're jostling for seeding in a packed East. The Hawks have been built around offensive firepower this season, and their ability to put points on the board in bunches makes them a tough matchup for any team that can't match their scoring output. With Jock Landale out due to illness, Atlanta loses some depth in the frontcourt, but the impact should be minimal against an Orlando team that's dealing with far more significant roster issues.

Orlando at 40-35 finds itself in a precarious position, clinging to playoff positioning while missing several key contributors. Franz Wagner (ankle), Anthony Black, and Jonathan Isaac are all out, and that's a devastating blow to the Magic's depth, versatility, and defensive identity. Wagner in particular is Orlando's most important player, and his absence strips the Magic of their primary offensive creator and a player whose two-way impact touches every aspect of the game. Without him, Orlando's offense tends to become stagnant, and the supporting cast has to shoulder a creative burden that few of them are equipped to handle consistently.

The Hawks being 4.5-point road favorites tells you everything about how much Wagner's absence tilts this matchup. Atlanta has the offensive weapons to exploit Orlando's depleted roster, and the Magic's defense, which has been their calling card all season, loses significant teeth without Wagner and Isaac available. The 233.5 total is moderate, and it reflects Orlando's defensive-minded identity even in a diminished state. But the Hawks should be able to generate quality looks against a Magic team that's scrambling to fill rotational holes with players who aren't accustomed to significant minutes.

This feels like a game where Atlanta's talent advantage manifests early and Orlando spends the entire night trying to claw back from a deficit they never erase. The Hawks have the kind of offensive diversity that punishes undermanned teams, and the Magic's best players, the ones who give them a fighting chance in games like this, are watching from the bench in street clothes. Orlando's home-court advantage and defensive culture will keep this from becoming a total blowout, but the outcome shouldn't be in serious doubt if Atlanta brings anything close to their best effort on a Wednesday night in Florida.

Celtics @ Heat

Wednesday, 7:30 PM ET | Kaseya Center, Miami, FL
Spread
BOS -5.5
Moneyline
BOS -210 / MIA +170
Total
O/U 229.0

Celtics-Heat on ESPN is appointment television for basketball fans, and this rivalry has delivered some of the most memorable postseason moments in recent NBA history. Boston at 50-25 leads the Atlantic Division and has been one of the most consistent teams in the league all season. The big news here is Jayson Tatum being cleared to play after dealing with his Achilles issue, and his return gives the Celtics their full complement of offensive weaponry for the stretch run. Tatum's ability to score at all three levels, create for teammates, and anchor the defense makes Boston a completely different animal when he's on the floor. Nikola Vucevic remains out, but that's a secondary concern when you're rolling out a lineup this talented.

Miami at 40-36 is fighting for its postseason life, and the Heat have made a living out of proving doubters wrong in the second half of the season. The Heat Culture narrative isn't just marketing, it's a real competitive edge that shows up when the games matter most, and Miami's ability to flip a switch in the playoffs has burned better regular-season teams before. But tonight's challenge is steep, with Terry Rozier out, Norman Powell dealing with illness, and Andrew Wiggins questionable with a toe injury. The Heat's depth is being tested at the worst possible time, and asking a short-handed roster to hang with a fully loaded Boston team on national television is a tall order even for an organization with Miami's pedigree.

Boston being a 5.5-point road favorite in Miami is significant. The Kaseya Center is typically a fortress for the Heat, and Miami's home crowd brings an energy that has fueled comebacks and upsets throughout the season. But the Celtics' overall talent advantage is simply too large to ignore, especially with Tatum back in the fold and Miami dealing with multiple absences. Boston's defense, which has been elite for years, should be able to contain a Heat offense that's missing key rotation pieces, and the Celtics' three-point shooting can blow any game open in a hurry when the shots start falling from deep.

The 229-point total suggests a game where defense plays a significant role, and that's typically what happens when these two teams meet regardless of the stakes. Boston and Miami have a physical, grinding history in the postseason, and even in the regular season, their matchups tend to feature more half-court execution than transition fireworks. The Celtics' size, shooting, and defensive versatility should be too much for a depleted Heat roster, but don't count out Miami's ability to make this uncomfortable. Erik Spoelstra always has his team prepared, and the Heat have a way of turning nationally televised games into dogfights that keep viewers glued to their screens until the final minutes.

Game 4

Knicks @ Grizzlies

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | FedExForum, Memphis, TN
Spread
NYK -14.5
Moneyline
NYK -910 / MEM +700
Total
O/U 227.5

The Knicks at 48-28 roll into Memphis as massive 14.5-point road favorites, and that number tells you everything about the state of the Grizzlies this season. Memphis at 25-50 has been decimated by injuries throughout the year, with 10 players currently on the injury report and four dealing with season-ending ailments. What was supposed to be a bounce-back year for the Grizzlies has instead turned into another lost season, and FedExForum has gone from one of the loudest buildings in basketball to a place where opposing teams come to pad their stats and build momentum heading into the postseason.

New York has been one of the most complete teams in the Eastern Conference all season, and Jalen Brunson's evolution into a legitimate franchise point guard has been the driving force behind the Knicks' success. Brunson's mid-range game is virtually unstoppable, and his ability to control the pace, make the right read in pick-and-roll situations, and deliver in clutch moments has elevated him into the conversation of the league's elite guards. The Knicks' physical, defensive-minded identity under Tom Thibodeau translates well on the road, and their depth and toughness should overwhelm a Memphis roster that's essentially running a G League lineup at this point in the season.

Fourteen and a half points is an enormous spread, but the Grizzlies have given opponents zero reason to believe they can compete in games against playoff-caliber teams. Memphis's healthy bodies are playing hard, you can't question the effort, but effort alone doesn't bridge a talent gap this wide. The Knicks have the luxury of using this game as a tune-up for the postseason, working on sets, building chemistry with their full rotation, and getting younger players some run while still maintaining a comfortable lead throughout the game.

The 227.5 total is relatively moderate for a game featuring the Knicks, and it reflects the possibility that New York builds such a large lead that the pace slows dramatically in the second half with starters resting. Brunson and company should have their way with Memphis's defense in the first half, and the question becomes whether the Grizzlies' young players can generate enough offense to push this toward the over when the Knicks go to their bench. This should be a stress-free night for New York and another long, painful evening for Memphis fans counting down the days until the draft lottery.

Game 5

Kings @ Raptors

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Scotiabank Arena, Toronto, ON
Spread
TOR -13.5
Moneyline
TOR -900 / SAC +575
Total
O/U 226.5

Sacramento's nightmare season has reached the point where the numbers almost don't look real. At 19-57, the Kings are on pace for one of the worst records in the Western Conference in recent memory, and the franchise's decision-making that led to this point will be dissected for years. The trades that sent away core pieces have backfired spectacularly, and the returns haven't panned out the way Sacramento hoped. Domantas Sabonis, Zach LaVine, and De'Andre Hunter are all done for the season, stripping the Kings of whatever veteran competence remained on the roster. What's left is a skeleton crew of young players and end-of-rotation guys who are simply overmatched against NBA-caliber competition on a nightly basis.

Toronto at 48-28 has been one of the best stories in the Eastern Conference, and the Raptors' resurgence has been built on the foundation of Scottie Barnes's development and Brandon Ingram's addition to the roster. Ingram's shot creation, mid-range artistry, and ability to carry the offense through dry spells has given Toronto a second star who complements Barnes perfectly. The Raptors are vastly healthier than Sacramento in every conceivable way, and Scotiabank Arena has been rocking this season as the Canadian fanbase has embraced this team's return to relevance with genuine passion. Toronto's defensive versatility and length create problems for opposing offenses, and against a Kings team this depleted, the Raptors should be able to suffocate whatever Sacramento tries to run.

The 13.5-point spread is enormous but probably doesn't do justice to the talent gap between these rosters right now. Toronto has legitimate playoff aspirations and is trying to secure the best possible seeding for a deep postseason run, while Sacramento is playing meaningless games with a roster that would struggle to win consistently in any professional basketball league on earth. The Raptors should control this game from the opening tip, build a double-digit lead by halftime, and spend the second half resting starters and getting younger players valuable experience in a comfortable environment.

The 226.5 total is interesting because it depends entirely on how quickly Toronto takes its foot off the gas. If the Raptors' starters play into the third quarter, this total could get pushed easily as they'll score at will against Sacramento's defense. But if the lead balloons early and Barnes and Ingram sit the entire fourth quarter, the pace could slow to a crawl as bench units exchange possessions without much efficiency on either side. Either way, the outcome of this game was essentially decided before tipoff, and the only question is the margin of victory.

Game 6

Pacers @ Bulls

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | United Center, Chicago, IL
Spread
CHI -4.5
Moneyline
CHI -195 / IND +165
Total
O/U 247.0

If you're looking for defense, look elsewhere. The Pacers and Bulls are projected for a 247-point total, the second-highest on Wednesday's board, and for good reason. Both of these teams play at a pace that would make track coaches proud and defend with an intensity that would make defensive coordinators weep. Indiana at 17-58 has been devastated by Tyrese Haliburton's torn Achilles, an injury that ripped the heart out of this franchise and turned a team with genuine playoff aspirations into one of the worst in basketball. Without Haliburton's playmaking, court vision, and ability to orchestrate the Pacers' high-octane offense, Indiana has been rudderless all season.

Chicago at 29-46 isn't exactly thriving either, but the Bulls have enough competent NBA players on the roster to be competitive on most nights, which is more than you can say for the current version of the Pacers. The Bulls' season has been a slow march toward the lottery, but they've shown flashes of the kind of young talent that could be interesting in the right developmental environment. Chicago's home-court advantage at the United Center still carries some weight, and the crowd, while not nearly as electric as it was during the team's competitive years, provides enough energy to give the Bulls a marginal edge in close games against inferior opponents.

The 4.5-point spread suggests Chicago is clearly the better team in this matchup, but not by a massive margin. Both teams are flawed in similar ways, particularly on the defensive end, and the game could easily devolve into a shootout where whoever has the ball last wins. Indiana's young players will compete because that's what NBA players do regardless of the standings, and the Pacers' pace-and-space philosophy ensures they'll generate enough possessions to keep the score elevated. Chicago's edge comes from having more reliable shot-makers and a slightly better defensive effort at home.

The 247-point total is almost comically high, but it reflects the reality that neither team has any interest in or capability for playing defense at a high level. This is a game where both sides will push the ball, shoot early in the shot clock, and create the kind of back-and-forth scoring that's entertaining for fans even if it makes basketball purists cringe. If you enjoy fast-paced, high-scoring basketball without much defensive sophistication, this is your game. If you prefer grinding, physical basketball, tune in to literally any other game on the board.

Game 7

Bucks @ Rockets

Wednesday, 8:00 PM ET | Toyota Center, Houston, TX
Spread
HOU -17.5
Moneyline
HOU -2100 / MIL +1100
Total
O/U 228.0

The biggest spread on Wednesday's board, and arguably the most depressing storyline for Bucks fans who remember this franchise winning a championship just a few years ago. Milwaukee at 29-45 is guaranteed its first losing season since 2015-16, and with Giannis Antetokounmpo sidelined by a knee hyperextension and bone bruise, the Bucks have zero chance of competing against a Houston team that's playing some of the best basketball in the Western Conference. Giannis's absence strips Milwaukee of its only player capable of single-handedly keeping games competitive, and the supporting cast has been so thoroughly gutted by trades and poor roster construction that what's left couldn't compete in most international leagues, let alone the NBA.

Houston at 45-29 has been one of the league's best stories this season, and Kevin Durant has been the catalyst for everything since joining the Rockets. Durant at 25.9 points per game has been vintage KD, using his 7-foot frame and silky-smooth jumper to score over, around, and through every defensive scheme opponents throw at him. His partnership with Jalen Green and Alperen Sengun has given the Rockets a big three that can beat you in multiple ways, and the Toyota Center has been electric all season as Houston's fanbase has embraced this team's rapid ascent from rebuilding project to legitimate championship contender. The Rockets are playing with purpose, confidence, and the kind of defensive intensity that champions are built on.

Laying 17.5 points against an NBA team feels absurd until you look at the Bucks' roster sheet for tonight. Without Giannis, Milwaukee is essentially asking a collection of role players and developmental prospects to compete against one of the best teams in the Western Conference in a hostile environment. The Rockets' defense has been suffocating this season, and they'll swarm whoever the Bucks throw out there as the primary ball-handler with the kind of length, athleticism, and scheme versatility that makes life miserable for even the league's best offensive players, let alone Milwaukee's replacement-level options.

The moneyline says it all. Houston at -2100 means you'd need to risk $2,100 just to win $100, and even that might feel like a gamble given how unpredictable the NBA can be in garbage time. But the reality is that the Rockets should build a 20-plus point lead by halftime and cruise through the second half with their starters resting on the bench. Durant might play 28 minutes and still score 25 points, because that's just how easy this matchup is for a player of his caliber against a team of Milwaukee's current quality. This is an NBA game in name only.

Game 8

Nuggets @ Jazz

Wednesday, 9:00 PM ET | Delta Center, Salt Lake City, UT
Spread
DEN -17.0
Moneyline
DEN -1800 / UTA +850
Total
O/U 249.0

The highest total on the entire Wednesday board, and it's not particularly close. Denver at 48-28 travels to Salt Lake City to face a Jazz team at 21-54 that has been completely gutted by season-ending injuries and a front office clearly focused on the draft lottery. Walker Kessler, Quentin Jackson, Jusuf Nurkic, and Lauri Markkanen (out since February) are all done for the year, leaving Utah with a roster so thin that even the 249-point total might be underselling the offensive explosion that's likely coming from the Nuggets' end. Denver should score at will against whatever defensive scheme Utah trots out with a skeleton crew.

The Nuggets have been rolling this season behind Nikola Jokic's otherworldly playmaking and the kind of team chemistry that only comes from years of playing together. Jokic continues to do things on a basketball court that shouldn't be physically possible for a player his size, and Denver's offensive system, which runs through the big man's passing and decision-making, is essentially impossible to stop when it's clicking. Aaron Gordon is listed as probable, which is good news for a Denver team that values his defensive versatility and transition finishing. The Nuggets are locked into the playoff picture and have the luxury of using games like this to fine-tune rotations and build momentum for a deep postseason run.

The 17-point spread and the 249 total paint a picture of a game where Denver plays their normal offensive style and it just happens to produce an avalanche of points against a defense that lacks the personnel to resist. Utah's defenders, to their credit, will compete and try to make things difficult, but the talent gap is so enormous that Denver's passing, movement, and shooting will create open looks on virtually every possession. Jokic could probably score 40 without breaking a sweat in this one, but he's more likely to dish out 15 assists and let his teammates feast on the easy looks his gravity creates.

The over/under at 249 is the real story here. That number screams "we think Denver is going to score 130 and Utah is going to score 119 by virtue of playing fast and taking a lot of shots." And honestly, that feels about right. Utah's young players won't stop running and shooting regardless of the score, which keeps possessions moving and the clock from becoming a factor in slowing things down. Denver's starters might rest the fourth quarter if this gets out of hand, which could depress the final number, but three quarters of Jokic against this defense should be more than enough to push this toward or past the total. Basketball fans who love offense should circle this one.

Spurs @ Warriors

Wednesday, 10:00 PM ET | Chase Center, San Francisco, CA
Spread
SA -13.5
Moneyline
SA -850 / GS +575
Total
O/U 226.5

Here it is, the game of the night. The San Antonio Spurs at 57-18 ride a scorching 9-game winning streak into Chase Center to face a Warriors team at 36-39 that is a shell of its former dynasty self. Victor Wembanyama has been the most transformative force in basketball this season, a 7-foot-4 alien who blocks shots, drills threes, handles the ball in transition, and does things defensively that the sport has never seen from a single player. The Spurs' historic record is a testament to Wemby's dominance and the organizational culture that Gregg Popovich has cultivated over decades. San Antonio hasn't just been good this season, they've been historically good, and the 9-game winning streak heading into this one suggests they're only getting better as the postseason approaches.

Golden State at 36-39 is living through the painful twilight of an era. Stephen Curry has missed 25 games this season, and his absence has exposed just how dependent the Warriors' entire operation has been on the greatest shooter in basketball history. Without Curry, and without Jimmy Butler (ACL), Kristaps Porzingis, Al Horford, and Moses Moody, Golden State's roster is impossibly thin. The Warriors who took the floor a decade ago and changed the sport forever are gone, and what's left is a franchise caught between honoring its past and accepting its future. Chase Center will be full because it always is, but the energy will be subdued, the kind of respectful appreciation for an opponent rather than the rabid, belief-driven roar that defined the dynasty years.

San Antonio being a 13.5-point road favorite at Chase Center is a sentence that would have gotten you committed to an institution five years ago, but here we are. The Spurs' defense, anchored by Wembanyama's shot-blocking and rim protection, is suffocating, and Golden State's diminished offensive options will struggle to generate quality looks against San Antonio's length and discipline. The Warriors will run their sets and try to move the ball, but without Curry's gravity pulling defenders out of position and creating space for everyone else, the offense becomes predictable and easy to game-plan against. Wemby doesn't even need to score 30 to dominate this game. His defensive presence alone will turn the paint into a no-fly zone.

The ESPN cameras will be there to capture what feels like a symbolic changing of the guard. The Warriors built a dynasty that will be talked about for generations, but the Spurs, with their generational talent in Wembanyama, represent the future of NBA dominance. San Antonio should control this game from start to finish, and the 9-game winning streak should extend to 10 without much drama. The 226.5 total reflects the Spurs' defensive prowess and the Warriors' offensive limitations, and this should be a relatively low-scoring affair where San Antonio's defense dictates the pace and Wemby adds another masterpiece to what has been the most impressive individual season in recent NBA history. Tune into ESPN for this one. It's must-watch basketball.
