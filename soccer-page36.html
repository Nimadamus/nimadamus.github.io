Macclesfield FC vs Crystal Palace

Saturday, January 10 | 7:15 AM ET | BBC One, TNT Sports 1
FA Cup Holders Begin Defence | Non-League vs Premier League

This is what the FA Cup is all about. Crystal Palace, the defending champions, begin their title defence at sixth-tier Macclesfield FC in potentially the biggest upset opportunity of the round. The romance of the cup is on full display here - a non-league club with dreams of giant-killing against a Premier League side fresh off their historic triumph.

Palace won the 2025 FA Cup in stunning fashion, and that success has raised expectations at Selhurst Park. But the magic of this competition is that anything can happen, and Macclesfield's players will be treating this as the biggest match of their lives. The energy from the home crowd will be absolutely electric.

Oliver Glasner needs to respect this tie while also managing his squad for Premier League priorities. Rotation is expected, but Palace still have enough quality on their bench to handle non-league opposition. The question is whether complacency creeps in - that's when FA Cup upsets happen.

Everton vs Sunderland

Saturday, January 10 | 7:15 AM ET | discovery+
Championship vs Premier League | Northeast Visitors at Goodison

Everton welcome Championship high-flyers Sunderland to Goodison Park in what shapes up as one of the more competitive Third Round ties. The Black Cats have been flying in the second tier and will fancy their chances against a Toffees side that's been inconsistent in the league.

Sunderland's young, energetic squad plays without fear, and their pressing intensity can cause problems for teams not ready for it. The atmosphere at the Stadium of Light has been incredible this season, and that confidence travels. This is exactly the kind of tie where Championship clubs cause problems.

Everton's home form has been patchy, and Sean Dyche will know his side cannot afford to take this lightly. The FA Cup represents a realistic trophy opportunity for both clubs, and expect a cagey, competitive affair. Sunderland have genuine quality - this won't be easy.

Newcastle United vs AFC Bournemouth

Saturday, January 10 | 10:00 AM ET
Premier League Clash | Two Top-Flight Sides Meet

This is one of four all-Premier League ties in the Third Round, and it's a cracker. Newcastle's ambitions have grown since the Saudi takeover, and the FA Cup represents a genuine trophy opportunity. St. James' Park will be rocking for this one.

Bournemouth have been one of the league's pleasant surprises under Andoni Iraola. The Cherries play attractive football and punch above their weight against bigger clubs. They won't be intimidated by the atmosphere or the occasion - they've earned their place in the top flight.

Eddie Howe faces his former club in a match that adds personal stakes to an already compelling tie. Newcastle's attacking firepower should be the difference, but Bournemouth's defensive organization and counter-attacking quality makes them dangerous opponents. This could easily go the distance.

Manchester City vs Exeter City

Saturday, January 10 | 10:00 AM ET | Etihad Stadium
League One vs Premier League | Major Mismatch on Paper

Manchester City welcome League One Exeter City to the Etihad in what should be a routine exercise for Pep Guardiola's men. City have won multiple FA Cups in recent years and treat this competition with respect, even against lower-league opposition.

Exeter will arrive with nothing to lose and everything to gain. These cup ties are what players at their level dream about - the chance to test themselves against the best in the world at an incredible stadium. The Grecians will compete and make the most of the experience.

City's squad depth is ridiculous, and even their second-string side would beat most Premier League teams. Guardiola will rotate heavily, but names like Cole Palmer's replacement and other fringe players are still world-class talents. Exeter will make this a party, but the result feels inevitable.

Tottenham Hotspur vs Aston Villa

Saturday, January 10 | 12:45 PM ET | BBC One, TNT Sports 1
All-Premier League Tie | Two Trophy Hunters Collide

This is the marquee Third Round fixture - two of the Premier League's most ambitious clubs going head-to-head for a place in the Fourth Round. Tottenham and Aston Villa both see the FA Cup as a realistic trophy opportunity, and neither will treat this lightly.

Spurs' trophy drought continues to hang over the club, and every cup competition represents a chance to finally break through. The pressure on Ange Postecoglou to deliver silverware grows with each passing season. Their home form at the new stadium has been generally strong.

Villa under Unai Emery have become genuine contenders for everything. Their Champions League qualification last season proved this club is on an upward trajectory, and adding the FA Cup would be a statement. Ollie Watkins and their attacking firepower can hurt anyone.

Expect a proper battle between two quality sides. This has the feel of a tie that could go to extra time or even penalties. Neither manager will want to exit this early, and both have the squad depth to rotate while still fielding competitive lineups.

Charlton Athletic vs Chelsea

Saturday, January 10 | 3:00 PM ET | TNT Sports 1
League One vs Premier League | South London Derby

Chelsea travel across South London to face League One Charlton Athletic in what should be a routine win on paper. But the FA Cup has a way of producing shock results, and Charlton will raise their game significantly for this local derby.

The Valley will be absolutely bouncing for this one. Charlton fans have dreamed of this draw - a chance to face their Premier League neighbors in a cup competition with the spotlight on them. The atmosphere will be hostile and partisan in the best possible way.

Chelsea's rebuilding project continues under new management, and the FA Cup provides a chance for silverware while the Premier League title race moves on without them. Enzo Maresca needs to navigate the balance between respecting the tie and resting key players. Charlton won't make it easy.

Sheffield Wednesday vs Brentford

Saturday, January 10 | 10:00 AM ET | Hillsborough
Championship vs Premier League | Hillsborough Hosts the Bees

Hillsborough will be rocking when Brentford come to town. Sheffield Wednesday supporters create one of the best atmospheres in English football, and the FA Cup brings out their passion even more. This is a genuine banana-skin tie for Thomas Frank's side.

Wednesday have quality in their Championship squad and know how to make life difficult for top-flight opposition. Danny Rohl has organized them well, and their physicality in the middle of the park can disrupt Brentford's possession-based approach.

Brentford's model continues to produce results, but the Bees can be inconsistent against well-organized opposition that makes them uncomfortable. Ivan Toney's departure left a hole that's been patched rather than truly replaced, and Wednesday will test their defensive resolve.

Wolverhampton vs Shrewsbury Town

Saturday, January 10 | 7:15 AM ET | Molineux
Local Derby | West Midlands Pride on the Line

Local bragging rights are on the line when Wolves host Shrewsbury Town in this West Midlands derby. League One Shrewsbury will bring thousands of traveling supporters for the short trip, and the atmosphere at Molineux will be split between two passionate fanbases.

Wolves have been struggling in the Premier League this season, and the FA Cup provides a chance to build some momentum and confidence. But these local derbies can be tricky - the intensity rises, and anything can happen when neighbors collide.

Shrewsbury will be up for this one. A win against their Premier League neighbors would be the biggest result in years for the club, and that motivation can override the talent gap. Gary O'Neil needs his players to match that intensity and treat this with the respect it deserves.
