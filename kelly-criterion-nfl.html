Kelly Criterion for NFL Betting

Optimal Bet Sizing for Football Point Spreads, Totals & Moneylines

The Kelly Criterion is a powerful tool for NFL bettors who want to maximize long-term bankroll growth while managing risk. But NFL betting has unique characteristics that require specific adjustments to the standard Kelly approach.

Why NFL Betting Requires Special Kelly Considerations

NFL betting differs from other sports in several key ways that affect how you should apply Kelly:

Limited Sample Size: With only 17 regular season games per team (plus playoffs), NFL bettors have fewer opportunities to prove their edge compared to NBA (82 games) or MLB (162 games). This means more conservative Kelly fractions are wise.

High Variance: NFL games are decided by key plays, injuries, and weather. A fumble on the goal line or a last-second field goal can swing any game. This inherent variance means even the sharpest NFL bettors experience significant swings.

Line Movement: NFL lines move significantly from open to close. Getting the best number is crucial, and your edge estimation should account for where you got the line versus the closing number.

NFL Point Spread Kelly Example

Example: Chiefs -3.5 (-110) vs Raiders

Your Analysis: You estimate the Chiefs have a 56% chance to cover the spread.

Bankroll: $2,000

Kelly % = (Decimal Odds × Win Prob - 1) / (Decimal Odds - 1)
Kelly % = (1.909 × 0.56 - 1) / (1.909 - 1) = 7.0%
Half Kelly: 3.5% = $70 bet

At standard -110 juice, the breakeven win rate is 52.4%. Your 56% estimate gives you a 3.6% edge. Using half Kelly, your recommended bet is $70.

NFL Moneyline Kelly Example

Example: Bills +145 vs Ravens

Your Analysis: You estimate the Bills have a 42% chance to win outright.

Bankroll: $2,000

Decimal Odds = 2.45 (from +145)
Kelly % = (2.45 × 0.42 - 1) / (2.45 - 1) = 2.1%
Half Kelly: 1.05% = $21 bet

Underdog moneylines can offer value when public perception underestimates teams. The higher payout means smaller Kelly percentages, which naturally limits exposure on these higher-variance bets.

Recommended Kelly Fractions for NFL

Quarter Kelly (25%): Best for most recreational bettors and those new to bankroll management. Provides solid growth with minimal risk of significant drawdowns.

Half Kelly (50%): Recommended for experienced bettors with proven track records. Offers 75% of full Kelly's growth rate with much lower variance.

Full Kelly (100%): Generally not recommended for NFL betting due to the sport's inherent variance. Only consider if you have years of documented edge.

Pro Tip: Track your closing line value (CLV) to validate your edge. If you're consistently beating the closing line, your win probability estimates are likely accurate enough to trust your Kelly calculations.

NFL-Specific Bankroll Rules

Never exceed 5% on any single NFL game regardless of what Kelly suggests. NFL games are too unpredictable for larger single-game exposure.

Cap weekly exposure at 15-20% of your bankroll across all NFL bets. This prevents a bad Sunday from devastating your season.

Separate your NFL bankroll if you bet multiple sports. Your edge in NFL may differ from other sports, so track and size them independently.
