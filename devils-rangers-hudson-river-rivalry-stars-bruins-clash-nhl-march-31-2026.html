Game 1 - Marquee
Heavyweight

Stars @ Bruins

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Puck Line
DAL -1.5 (+205) / BOS +1.5 (-250)
Moneyline
DAL -120 / BOS +100
Total
O/U 5.5

This is the kind of cross-conference showdown that feels like a Stanley Cup Final preview, and the market agrees. Dallas (44-18-12, 100 points) has been one of the most complete teams in the NHL all season, sitting near the top of the Western Conference standings with a roster that can beat you in every possible way. The Stars don't have a single glaring weakness. Their defense is elite, their goaltending has been outstanding, and their forward group combines high-end skill with relentless forechecking. Coming into TD Garden as a slight -120 road favorite tells you everything about the respect this Dallas team has earned over the course of the season.

Boston (42-24-8, 92 points) isn't going to be intimidated by anyone in their own building, and the Bruins have been rolling all season with one of the best home records in the Eastern Conference. TD Garden has been a fortress, and the Boston faithful create the kind of atmosphere that can rattle road teams and elevate the home squad. The Bruins have the veteran leadership, the defensive structure, and the goaltending to compete with anyone on any given night, and getting them at even money as a home underdog against a team they match up well with is the kind of number that grabs your attention. This is a team with 92 points for a reason.

The chess match between these two coaching staffs is going to be fascinating. Dallas plays a patient, structured game that prioritizes puck possession and limiting high-danger chances against. Boston plays a similar style, which means this game could come down to which team wins the special teams battle and which goaltender makes the crucial save at the crucial moment. Neither team is going to give up easy goals, and the 5.5 total reflects the market's expectation of a tight, low-scoring affair. When two elite defensive teams meet in a game with playoff-level intensity, the margins become razor thin.

The puck line tells an interesting story here. Dallas at -1.5 (+205) pays over two-to-one, which means the market doesn't expect the Stars to win this game comfortably even though they're the moneyline favorite. That's a sign of respect for Boston's ability to keep games close, and it's also a reflection of the fact that games between two good teams rarely end in blowouts. This has the feel of a 2-1 or 3-2 game where every shift matters and every goal feels like it carries enormous weight. If you're a hockey purist, this might be the best game on the entire slate tonight.

Game 2 - Rivalry
Hudson River

Devils @ Rangers

Tuesday, 7:00 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Puck Line
NJD -1.5 (+205) / NYR +1.5 (-250)
Moneyline
NJD -120 / NYR +100
Total
O/U 5.5

The Hudson River Rivalry never needs a reason to be heated, but tonight's edition at Madison Square Garden carries an extra layer of intensity because these two franchises are heading in completely opposite directions. New Jersey (38-33-2, 78 points) is right in the thick of a wild card chase and desperately needs every point they can scrape together. The Rangers (30-35-9, 69 points), meanwhile, are mired in a deeply disappointing season that has left the MSG faithful restless and frustrated. When the Devils walk into the Garden tonight, they'll be facing a team with nothing to lose and a building full of fans who are looking for any reason to get loud. That's a dangerous combination.

New Jersey being the -120 road favorite at the Garden is a testament to how far the Rangers have fallen this season. There was a time not long ago when being a road favorite at MSG would have been unthinkable for the Devils, but New York's 30-35-9 record has erased whatever mystique the Garden once held for opponents. The Rangers' nine overtime losses are particularly frustrating because they show a team that competes hard but can't finish games when it matters most. Their inability to close out tight contests has been the defining weakness of their season, and it's left them watching the playoff race from the outside looking in.

The Devils come in with much more urgency and much more to play for. At 78 points with a handful of games remaining, New Jersey can't afford to drop road games against struggling teams in their own division. Jack Hughes has been everything the Devils hoped he'd become, and the young core around him has developed into a group that can compete with anyone in the Metro Division. New Jersey's speed and transition game can cause problems for any defense, and the Rangers' blue line has been vulnerable to fast-paced attacks all season. If the Devils get their legs going early and force New York to chase the game, this could get uncomfortable for the home side quickly.

The 5.5 total reflects the rivalry factor. Devils-Rangers games tend to be physical, emotional, and sometimes messy, with the intensity level pushing both teams into tighter defensive postures than they might normally play. But there's also the possibility that New York's defense, which has been inconsistent all year, gives New Jersey too many quality looks, and the game opens up in the Devils' favor. The even-money price on the Rangers is interesting for bettors who believe the Garden atmosphere and the rivalry factor can level the playing field, but from a pure talent and motivation standpoint, the Devils look like the better team coming into this one.

Buffalo's remarkable season continues to be one of the best stories in all of hockey. The Sabres (45-21-8, 98 points) are on pace for a 106-point season that would obliterate franchise records and officially end what has been the longest playoff drought in NHL history. KeyBank Center has been rocking all year, and the energy in that building when a quality opponent comes to town is something you have to experience to believe. The Sabres aren't sneaking up on anyone anymore. They're a legitimate powerhouse, and the -198 moneyline reflects the market's respect for what this team has built.

The Islanders (42-28-5, 89 points) are no pushovers, and their 89-point season puts them firmly in the playoff picture. New York plays the kind of structured, defense-first hockey that can stifle any opponent, and their ability to limit chances and play patient, mistake-free hockey has been the foundation of their success all season. Ilya Sorokin continues to be one of the best goaltenders in the league, and when you have a netminder of that caliber behind a committed defensive group, you always have a chance to steal a road game. The +164 price on the Islanders offers value for bettors who believe New York's system can neutralize Buffalo's home-ice advantage.

What makes Buffalo so dangerous is their depth. The Sabres don't rely on one line to carry the offense. They roll four lines that can all contribute, and their defensive corps has matured into one of the most reliable units in the conference. The 6.5 total is the highest number on the board for the early-window games, and it makes sense given the offensive firepower both teams possess. Buffalo's home games have been particularly entertaining this season, with the Sabres pushing the pace and creating an up-tempo environment that favors the home team. The crowd feeds off the energy, and the players feed off the crowd.

This is a matchup of contrasting styles that could go in multiple directions. If the Islanders are able to slow the game down, clog up the neutral zone, and force Buffalo to grind through their defensive structure, this could be a tight 3-2 affair that goes either way. But if the Sabres are able to push the pace and get their transition game rolling early, the Islanders could find themselves chasing the game in a hostile building where playing from behind is almost impossible. Buffalo at home as a significant favorite against a playoff-caliber opponent feels right, and the 6.5 total suggests both teams have the talent to produce goals.

This might be the single most fascinating storyline on the entire slate. The Florida Panthers are the back-to-back Stanley Cup champions, winners in 2024 and 2025, but their title defense has completely collapsed this season. At 35-35-3 with just 73 points through 73 games, the Panthers are a .500 team that is watching the playoff race from the outside. Defending a championship is the hardest thing to do in professional sports, and Florida has discovered that in the most painful way possible. The fatigue, the injuries, the regression, the target on your back every single night, it's all caught up to a roster that looked invincible just nine months ago.

Ottawa (38-25-10, 86 points) comes in as the road favorite at -170, which tells you everything about how far the Panthers have fallen. The Senators are having a strong season and are firmly in the playoff picture, with a young, hungry roster that has the talent and the depth to compete with anyone in the Eastern Conference. Brady Tkachuk continues to be the heart and soul of this team, and the supporting cast around him has stepped up all year. Ottawa plays an aggressive, up-tempo style that generates a ton of offensive chances, and against a Panthers team that has struggled defensively for much of the season, the Senators should have plenty of opportunities to put the puck in the net.

What makes Florida's collapse so shocking is the talent still on this roster. Aleksander Barkov, Matthew Tkachuk, Sam Reinhart, and a core that won back-to-back championships are all still here. These are proven winners who know what it takes to perform at the highest level. But something has been fundamentally broken this season, and the Panthers haven't been able to fix it. The goaltending has been inconsistent, the defensive structure has cracked repeatedly, and the offensive production hasn't come close to matching the championship-caliber output of the past two years. At some point, talent alone can't overcome the issues, and Florida has reached that point.

The 5.5 total is interesting given Ottawa's offensive tendencies and Florida's defensive struggles. The Senators have the firepower to push this game over by themselves if they get rolling early, and the Panthers have shown that they can be beaten to the punch in their own building. But there's also the chance that Florida's championship DNA kicks in for 60 minutes and they play the kind of desperate, focused hockey that made them champions in the first place. The +142 moneyline on the Panthers offers interesting value for anyone who believes the back-to-back champs still have some fight left in them, but the evidence this season suggests that ship has sailed.
