Lakers @ Wizards

Friday, 7:00 PM ET | Capital One Arena, Washington, DC
Spread
LAL -6 (-110)
Moneyline
LAL -400 / WAS +317
Total
O/U 225

The Lakers come into this one licking their wounds after getting absolutely demolished by Cleveland 129-99 in their last outing. That's the kind of loss that wakes a team up, and LeBron James and Anthony Davis won't let another embarrassment happen on back-to-back games. LA at 28-18 is still very much in the Western Conference playoff picture, and they've been excellent on the road this season, particularly against bottom-feeders. The Lakers have won 4 of their last 5 road games against teams with losing records, and Washington at 11-34 certainly qualifies.

Washington is in full rebuild mode, and while they showed some fight in their last game, knocking off Portland 115-111, let's not get carried away. The Wizards are one of the worst teams in the league by any metric you want to use. Their defense ranks 28th in efficiency, and they don't have anyone who can slow down LeBron or AD in the post. Jordan Poole continues to put up empty stats on a losing team, but his shot selection and defensive effort make it hard to see how this roster competes with legitimate playoff teams.

The Lakers have historically dominated in DC, and their last three road games at Washington have gone OVER the total. With LA likely looking to run early and often to wash away the taste of the Cleveland debacle, expect a track meet that favors the more talented team. The 225 total feels about right, and the Lakers should cover this spread with relative ease. This is a get-right game for LA.

ESPN+

Raptors @ Magic

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Kia Center, Orlando, FL
Spread
ORL -1.5 (-110)
Moneyline
TOR +110 / ORL -129
Total
O/U 229

Toronto has quietly become one of the more interesting teams in the Eastern Conference. The Raptors at 29-20 are riding a four-game road winning streak and playing with a confidence that belies their market size. The Brandon Ingram acquisition from New Orleans in February 2025 has been transformational, giving them a legitimate second scoring option alongside Scottie Barnes. Ingram's ability to create his own shot and draw fouls has been exactly what this young core needed to take the next step. Toronto is 25-24 ATS this season, and they've covered in 13 of 21 games as underdogs of 1.5 points or more.

Orlando at 24-22 has had an up-and-down campaign. Franz Wagner remains out, and Jalen Suggs is doubtful for this one, which significantly hampers the Magic's offensive ceiling. Paolo Banchero has been carrying a massive load, but even he can't do it all without his running mates. Orlando's defense has been their calling card, ranking in the top 10 in efficiency, but they've struggled to generate offense consistently when their perimeter players are sidelined. The Magic are 18-28 ATS this season, and they're just 12-20 against the spread as favorites.

This looks like a classic trap line. Orlando is at home and slightly favored, but they're missing key pieces and facing a Toronto team that's playing with house money on the road. Jakob Poeltl is still sidelined for the Raptors, but their guard play has been electric. Give me Toronto's experience and recent form over a depleted Magic squad. This game could go either way, which is exactly why the 1.5-point spread feels like a gift for the dog.

Kings @ Celtics

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA
Spread
BOS -10.5 (-110)
Moneyline
SAC +420 / BOS -550
Total
O/U 225.5

Sacramento's season has been a nightmare from start to finish. At 12-36, they're the worst team in the Western Conference by a significant margin, and the February 2025 trade that sent De'Aaron Fox to San Antonio in a three-team deal has left them without an identity. Zach LaVine, acquired from Chicago in that same transaction, hasn't been the answer. LaVine's scoring punch is still there, but he's not a floor general, and Sacramento's offense ranks dead last in the league in efficiency. They've been outscored by double digits in 20 games this season.

Boston at 26-15 is navigating life without Jayson Tatum, who remains sidelined with his Achilles injury. It's been a challenge, but Jaylen Brown has stepped into the alpha role admirably, and the Celtics' depth has kept them afloat. Derrick White and Jrue Holiday continue to provide the two-way impact that made this team so dangerous, and their defense remains elite even without Tatum's presence. The Celtics are 14-6 at home this season and have dominated inferior competition at TD Garden.

The 10.5-point spread is massive, but Sacramento has given no indication they can hang with quality teams. Their road record is abysmal, their defense is a sieve, and they lack any coherent offensive system. Boston will control the pace, pound the ball inside, and grind Sacramento into dust. The Celtics have covered in 6 of their last 8 home games against teams with losing records. This is a mismatch on paper and on the court.

Trail Blazers @ Knicks

Friday, 7:30 PM ET | Madison Square Garden, New York, NY
Spread
NYK -7.5 (-105)
Moneyline
POR +225 / NYK -270

O/U 227.5

Portland is trying to stop a three-game losing skid, but walking into Madison Square Garden to face the Knicks isn't exactly the cure they need. The Trail Blazers at 23-25 are a middle-of-the-pack team that competes hard but lacks the firepower to beat quality opponents consistently. Jerami Grant (18.8 PPG) and Anfernee Simons are their primary scoring options, but neither is an elite number-one option, and Portland's defense has been inconsistent all season. They're 12-12 ATS on the road, which tells you everything about their reliability in this spot.

New York at 29-18 is rolling right now, and the Garden has been a fortress. Jalen Brunson continues to play at an All-NBA level, averaging 27.6 points and 6 assists while shooting 38% from three. Karl-Anthony Towns has been everything they hoped for when they acquired him, providing 20.2 points and 11.6 rebounds per game with floor-spacing ability that opens up driving lanes for Brunson. The Knicks are 11-5 ATS when favored by 7.5 points or more, and they've won 26 of 38 games when listed as favorites.

The Knicks should control this game from the opening tip. Their defense has been stout at home, and Portland doesn't have the secondary creators to exploit New York's switching scheme. Expect Brunson to carve up Portland's guards, Towns to dominate on the glass, and the Knicks to build a double-digit lead by halftime. The 227.5 total could go either way, but the spread feels solid for the home team in front of a raucous MSG crowd.

Two struggling teams meet in the Smoothie King Center, and this one has the makings of an ugly, back-and-forth affair. Memphis at 18-27 has been one of the league's biggest disappointments this season. The injuries that have plagued them in recent years continue to take their toll, and their young core hasn't developed as quickly as hoped. Jaren Jackson Jr. remains their anchor at 19.0 points and 1.5 blocks per game, but the supporting cast has been inconsistent. The Grizzlies are 9-11-1 ATS on the road, showing they can hang with inferior competition.

New Orleans at 12-37 is flat-out bad. Without Zion Williamson healthy for any sustained stretch, the Pelicans have been rudderless. Their defense ranks 28th in the league, allowing 121.2 points per game, and their offense isn't much better despite having capable scorers. The Pelicans have gone 4-6 in their last 10 games, though they've actually covered at a 6-4 clip in that span, suggesting they've been competitive despite the losing. Home cooking has helped them stay within striking distance of road teams.

The 234 total is the highest on Friday's board, and it reflects two teams that simply can't stop anyone. Memphis's defense has regressed significantly this season, and New Orleans has been a turnstile all year. Expect a track meet with little regard for defensive fundamentals. The 3-point spread feels about right for a home team that's slightly less bad than the visitors, but this could easily swing either way in a sloppy, high-scoring contest.

Cavaliers @ Suns

Spread
CLE -4.5 (-110)
Moneyline
CLE -144 / PHX +127
Total
O/U 223

This is the marquee matchup of the Friday night slate, and it's easy to see why ESPN scooped it up. Cleveland at 29-20 just destroyed the Lakers by 30 points and is playing their best basketball of the season. Donovan Mitchell continues to be one of the league's elite scorers, pouring in 30.6 points per game with the efficiency and shot-making ability of a true superstar. He dropped 25 on 9-of-21 shooting against LA, and he'll be looking to continue that dominance against a Phoenix defense that has been solid but not elite. The Cavaliers are averaging 119.2 points per contest, fourth-best in the league.

Phoenix at 28-19 is in a different place than they were a year ago. The Kevin Durant trade to Houston last summer changed the identity of this franchise, and while Devin Booker remains an elite scorer, the supporting cast has been in flux. Jalen Green, acquired in the Durant deal, provides scoring punch but has been inconsistent as a second option. The Suns' defense ranks fifth in points allowed, but they haven't faced a scorer quite like Mitchell recently. Booker will need to match Mitchell bucket for bucket to keep Phoenix in this game.

Cleveland being 4.5-point road favorites tells you everything about where these franchises stand. The Cavaliers have hit the over in 52 of their last 93 games, and their offensive firepower suggests this game should be entertaining. The Suns will make this competitive at home, but Cleveland's depth and offensive execution give them the edge. Expect Mitchell vs. Booker to be a showdown, but the Cavaliers' supporting cast should make the difference down the stretch.

Moneyline

The Clippers travel to Denver significantly shorthanded. Bradley Beal is done for the season after hip surgery, Derrick Jones Jr. is day-to-day, and Bogdan Bogdanovic didn't play in their last game. LA at 22-24 has been fighting just to stay in the play-in picture, and losing their second-best player for the year is a devastating blow. They beat Utah 115-103 in their last outing, but facing the Nuggets in Denver is a completely different challenge. The altitude alone saps visiting teams' legs in the fourth quarter.

Denver at 31-16 is cruising through the Western Conference behind Nikola Jokic's brilliance. Jokic is putting up another MVP-caliber campaign: 27.2 points, 12.8 rebounds, and 9.4 assists per game. His court vision unlocks Denver's entire offensive system, and no team in the league has figured out how to stop him consistently. The Nuggets are 18-4 at home this season, and they just dropped a close one to Detroit, which means they'll be hungry to get back on track. Michael Porter Jr. and Jamal Murray provide the complementary scoring Denver needs to blow teams out.

The 210.5 total is the lowest on Friday's board, reflecting Denver's defensive improvement and the Clippers' personnel losses. This should be a grind-it-out affair where Jokic controls the pace and the Clippers struggle to generate quality looks without Beal. Denver has won 9 of their last 16 matchups against LA, and they have a massive home court advantage. Expect a methodical Nuggets victory by double digits.

This is the kind of game that could swing 10 points either way based on who hits their open threes. The 226 total feels high given both teams' offensive limitations, but the defensive apathy could easily push this over. Proceed with caution.

Pistons @ Warriors

Friday, 10:00 PM ET | Chase Center, San Francisco, CA
Spread
GSW -1.5 (-110)
Moneyline
DET +105 / GSW -125
Total
O/U 224.5

The nightcap on ESPN features the best record in basketball visiting Chase Center, and this should be must-watch TV. Detroit at 33-11 has been the story of the season, transforming from perennial lottery team to legitimate title contender in stunning fashion. Cade Cunningham has emerged as an MVP candidate, orchestrating an offense that hums with precision while anchoring a defense that allows just 110.0 points per game, second-best in the league. The Pistons just knocked off the Nuggets 109-107 on the road, proving they can win anywhere against anyone.

Golden State at 27-22 is still figuring out their identity in the post-Klay Thompson era. Stephen Curry remains an unstoppable force, leading the league in three-pointers made, but the supporting cast has been inconsistent. Jimmy Butler's torn ACL in January ended any chance of that experiment panning out this season, and the Warriors have been relying heavily on their remaining veterans to stay competitive. They blasted Utah 140-124 in their last game, but facing Detroit's elite defense is a completely different challenge than facing the Jazz's traffic cones.

The Warriors being 1.5-point favorites at home against the league's best team feels like disrespect. Yes, Golden State has the home court advantage, and Curry in Chase Center is always dangerous. But Detroit has been the best team in basketball by a significant margin, and their defensive identity travels well. Cunningham's poise in big moments has been remarkable for a young player, and this team has no fear of any environment. This feels like a coin flip, but Detroit's resume suggests they deserve to be favored, not laying points on the road.
