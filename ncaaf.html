Cure Bowl

Ohio Bobcats vs Jacksonville State Gamecocks

The Matchup

The Cure Bowl kicks off bowl season with two programs riding late-season momentum. Ohio (9-4) from the MAC faces Conference USA's Jacksonville State (8-4). The Bobcats have been one of the most consistent mid-major programs in recent years, featuring a balanced offense that can run and throw. JSU is in just their second year of FBS transition but has exceeded expectations with an explosive passing attack.

Key Numbers

Ohio averages 28.6 PPG and allows 24.2, winning the scoring margin battle. Their rushing attack averages 185 yards per game. Jacksonville State is more pass-heavy, averaging 278 passing yards per game but just 112 rushing. The Gamecocks score 31.2 PPG but allow 27.8. The total of 51.5 feels low given both teams' offensive capabilities.

The Angle

Bowl games are about motivation, and both teams should be fired up to close their seasons with a win. Ohio's experience edge matters - they've been here before. JSU is still adjusting to FBS bowl culture. The 3-point spread is tight, suggesting Vegas sees this as a toss-up. The Over looks appealing if both offenses click.

49.5O/U

The Matchup

This is a fascinating matchup between underachieving SEC talent and an overachieving AAC squad. Florida (6-6) barely made bowl eligibility in a disappointing season under Billy Napier. Tulane (9-4) has been one of the best Group of 5 programs the past few years under Willie Fritz. The Green Wave won back-to-back AAC titles and knows how to prepare for big games.

Key Numbers

Florida's talent advantage is significant on paper - they have more NFL prospects than Tulane combined. But the Gators rank 65th in SP+ efficiency while Tulane is 32nd. The Green Wave's rushing attack averages 205 yards per game, which matches up well against Florida's porous run defense (155 allowed). Florida scores 27.2 PPG but has been inconsistent against quality competition.

The Angle

This is a classic trap game for Florida. The Gators' motivation is questionable after a brutal season, while Tulane has everything to prove against an SEC opponent. The 6.5-point spread feels like a lot given Tulane's program strength. Take the Green Wave and the points - this could be a one-score game either way.

The Matchup

UNLV's remarkable turnaround continues as they face California in the LA Bowl. The Rebels (10-3) were one of the biggest surprises in college football, nearly making the College Football Playoff. Their high-octane offense features one of the nation's best dual-threat QBs. Cal (6-6) barely reached bowl eligibility in their first ACC season. The Golden Bears have been inconsistent but can compete when clicking.

Key Numbers

UNLV averages 35.4 PPG (top 20 nationally) and has an offense that can score in bunches. The Rebels' passing attack ranks 12th nationally with 297 yards per game. Cal allows 26.8 PPG but has faced better competition than UNLV's Mountain West schedule. The Bears rank 87th in SP+ defense, which could be exploited.

The Angle

UNLV has been excellent all season, but 7 points is a significant spread against a Power 5 opponent. Cal has the athletes to keep up, and bowl games often see underdogs cover. That said, UNLV's motivation is sky-high after narrowly missing the playoff. The Rebels should win, but Cal keeping it within a touchdown wouldn't be shocking.

The Matchup

Two FBS newcomers meet in the New Orleans Bowl. Georgia Southern (8-4) from the Sun Belt faces Conference USA's Sam Houston (9-3). Both programs have exceeded expectations in their FBS transitions. The Eagles run the triple-option attack, controlling tempo and wearing down defenses. Sam Houston has been dominant in CUSA, featuring a balanced offense that can score in multiple ways.

Key Numbers

Georgia Southern averages 33.8 PPG with a rushing attack that gobbles up 248 yards per game. Their time of possession average of 34 minutes leads the Sun Belt. Sam Houston scores 32.4 PPG and has been explosive, averaging 6.2 yards per play. The Bearkats' defense allows 22.1 PPG, but they haven't faced an option attack like Georgia Southern's.

The Angle

This is essentially a pick'em. The 2.5-point spread reflects how evenly matched these teams are. Georgia Southern's unique offense is always tough to prepare for in bowl season. Sam Houston has the more explosive offense, but controlling tempo could neutralize that. The Over 55.5 looks appealing if both offenses get rolling. Consider the Under if you think Georgia Southern dominates time of possession.

The Matchup

James Madison (11-2) has been a college football sensation since joining FBS. The Dukes have won 22 of their last 24 games and are on the verge of Power 5 consideration. Their defense is elite, ranking top-20 nationally. Western Kentucky (7-5) is a solid CUSA program but is outmatched here. The Hilltoppers can score but have been inconsistent defensively.

Key Numbers

JMU's defense allows just 17.2 PPG (15th nationally) and forces turnovers at an elite rate. The Dukes score 34.8 PPG with a balanced attack. WKU averages 28.4 PPG but allows 29.6 - they're losing the scoring margin battle. The Hilltoppers' passing attack is dangerous (285 YPG) but their rushing game averages just 98 yards.

The Angle

Ten points is a significant spread, but JMU is simply the better team. The Dukes have dominated every week against similar competition. WKU's one-dimensional offense (pass-heavy) plays into JMU's hands - they can tee off on passing downs. The Under might be the play if JMU's defense controls the game. Take the Dukes if you're betting the spread.
