CFP Semifinal: Hurricanes vs Rebels

January 8, 2026 | 7:30 PM ET | Fiesta Bowl | State Farm Stadium | ESPN
FINAL: Miami 31, Ole Miss 27 | Miami advances to National Championship

Both teams entered with identical 13-2 records, but their December trajectories told different stories.

Miami closed the regular season stumbling through a 42-38 loss to Syracuse, then needed overtime to survive Iowa State 14-13 in their bowl game. The Hurricanes' offense, once explosive behind Cam Ward, managed just 13 points in regulation against a middling Big 12 defense. Their 87% win rate looks impressive until you realize how many close calls went their way late in the season.

Ole Miss took a different path, demolishing Duke 52-20 in their bowl after throttling Mississippi State to end the regular season. The Rebels' offense found another gear in December, averaging 45 points across their final three games. Jaxson Dart's mobility gives them a dimension Miami struggled to contain against dual-threat quarterbacks all year.

The neutral site levels the playing field somewhat, removing Ole Miss's raucous home atmosphere advantage. Miami's experienced secondary should challenge Dart's downfield accuracy, while the Rebels' improved run defense could force Ward into uncomfortable pocket situations. Both defenses showed vulnerability to big plays in recent weeks, suggesting an offensive showcase might be brewing in Glendale.

CFP Semifinal: Ducks vs Hoosiers

January 9, 2026 | 7:30 PM ET | Peach Bowl | Mercedes-Benz Stadium | ESPN
FINAL: Indiana 55, Oregon 26 | Indiana advances to National Championship

You know what separates playoff football from regular season? Everyone's banged up, but who's hiding what?

Oregon's been relatively healthy through their run, though they've managed some nagging issues with their offensive line depth. The Ducks rotate heavily anyway, so minor bumps don't derail their system. Their speed on both sides creates matchup problems when everyone's moving at full capacity.

Indiana's perfect record masks the typical wear of a long season. They've dealt with some secondary concerns and linebacker depth questions, but nothing that's shown up on game film. The Hoosiers have thrived on their defensive cohesion - guys knowing exactly where teammates will be.

Here's the reality: both teams have kept injury reports tight heading into this stage. Oregon's up-tempo offense can exploit any communication breakdowns if Indiana's missing key defensive signals. But the Hoosiers' home crowd advantage gets amplified when they're at full strength defensively.

The team that's genuinely healthier in the trenches - not just on paper, but guys who can play through contact for four quarters - controls this game's tempo and physicality.
