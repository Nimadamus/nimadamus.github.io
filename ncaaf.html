Old Dominion Monarchs vs South Florida Bulls

Wednesday, 5:00 PM ET | Camping World Stadium, Orlando | ESPN
StaffDNA Cure Bowl | Line: USF -4 | O/U: 52.5 | ODU: 9-3 | USF: 9-3

Here's the thing about this Cure Bowl matchup that makes it genuinely unpredictable: both teams are rolling into Orlando without their starting quarterbacks. South Florida's Byrum Brown, who became just the 12th player in FBS history to pass for 3,000 yards and rush for 1,000 in a season, is opting out as he weighs his future options between the transfer portal, staying at USF, or testing the NFL waters. Old Dominion's Colton Joseph, the Sun Belt Offensive Player of the Year, is entering the transfer portal and won't play. This one comes down to which backup can handle the moment.

For South Florida, senior Gaston Moore steps in for his final college game. The Tennessee transfer has appeared in seven games this season, going 14-for-20 for 54 yards and a touchdown in limited action. Not exactly a confidence-inspiring sample size, but he's a veteran who knows what pressure feels like. The bigger issue for USF is the coaching chaos. Alex Golesh took the Auburn job, so interim head coach Kevin Patrick and tight ends coach Jack Taylor are running things. New hire Brian Hartline is still with Ohio State for their playoff run. It's a mess.

Old Dominion's Ground Game Is the Key

The Monarchs are the real story here. This 9-3 season is their best since going 10-3 in 2016, and they've done it by running the football. Old Dominion set a school season rushing record with 2,843 yards, averaging 236.9 per game (7th nationally). Trequan Jones has rushed for 736 yards on 97 carries at 7.6 per rush. Without Joseph's dual-threat ability, they'll lean even harder on the ground game with Quinn Henicle making just his second career start.

The Defensive Matchup Favors ODU

Old Dominion's defense has been outstanding, allowing just 19.3 points per game (20th in FBS). They're top 20 nationally in EPA per play allowed, yards per drive, and points per drive. Jerome Carter and Mario Easterly lead the team from safety with 72 and 71 tackles respectively, and Carter has four interceptions. Against a backup quarterback making his first meaningful start, that kind of ball-hawking secondary could be devastating.

South Florida comes in with plenty to play for. A win would mark only the third time in school history they've reached double-digit wins, joining Charlie Strong's 2017 squad (10-2) and Willie Taggart's 2016 team (11-2). But asking a backup quarterback to deliver that milestone against a legit defense is a tall order. The total of 52.5 feels right for a game where neither offense will have its primary playmaker.

Louisiana Ragin' Cajuns vs Delaware Blue Hens

Wednesday, 8:30 PM ET | Hancock Whitney Stadium, Mobile | ESPN
68 Ventures Bowl | Line: UL -1.5 | O/U: 60.5 | UL: 6-6 | DEL: 6-6

This game is historic for Delaware, and I mean that literally. The Blue Hens are playing in their first-ever bowl game after completing their first season at the FBS level. For a program that was an FCS powerhouse for decades, this transition year was supposed to be about growing pains and learning experiences. Instead, they went 6-6 and earned a bowl bid when there weren't enough bowl-eligible teams to fill all 82 spots. It's an incredible story.

Louisiana, meanwhile, has made this an annual tradition. This is their eighth straight bowl appearance, a school record. But getting here wasn't easy. The Ragin' Cajuns started the season 2-6 with a mountain of injuries, looking like a team headed nowhere. Then something clicked. They've won four straight games to get to 6-6 and become bowl eligible. A win tonight would be their fifth consecutive victory after that brutal start.

Delaware's Quarterback Is Special

Nick Minicucci wasn't supposed to be the starter. He opened 2025 as the backup before Zach Marker went down in the opener. Over 12 games, Minicucci has thrown for 3,507 yards and 22 touchdowns while adding 231 rushing yards and 10 scores on the ground. That's FBS-level production from a guy thrust into the role unexpectedly. He's been the engine driving Delaware's surprisingly competitive season.

Louisiana's Running Game vs Delaware's Defense

Louisiana's ground attack ranks sixth in the Sun Belt at 189.8 yards per game, powered by the one-two punch of Bill Davis (743 yards) and Zylan Perry (664). QB Lunch Winfield adds another dimension with 627 rushing yards on 138 carries. The question is whether Delaware's defense, which has been inconsistent in the first year of FBS competition, can slow it down. The Blue Hens went 3-1 at home in conference play but 1-3 on the road.

Both teams are led by alumni of their respective schools. Michael Desormeaux played at Louisiana and is in his third year leading the program. Ryan Carty is a Delaware guy through and through. This is the first-ever meeting between these two programs, and for Delaware, no matter the outcome, just being here is validation that the FBS transition can work faster than anyone expected.
