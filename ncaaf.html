Troy Trojans @ Jacksonville State Gamecocks

Tuesday, 9:00 PM ET | Cramton Bowl, Montgomery, AL | ESPN
Line: TROY -1.5 | O/U: 49.5 | TROY: 8-5 | JVST: 8-5

Well, this is a long time coming. Troy and Jacksonville State haven't played each other in 24 years. Think about that for a second. The last time these two programs met on the field, Troy was still an FCS program. They've since moved up to FBS, won Sun Belt titles, and established themselves as a legitimate Group of Five contender. And now they're meeting again in the Salute to Veterans Bowl, separated by less than 200 miles on Interstate 20 in the heart of Alabama.

Here's the storyline that makes this game fascinating: Troy won the Sun Belt West with a 6-2 conference record and reached the Sun Belt Championship game, where they fell to James Madison 31-14. That loss stings, but this Trojans squad has plenty to be proud of. An 8-5 record with five straight wins at one point shows this team can put together a run when they need to. The problem? They'll be without their leading rusher Tae Meadows, who rushed for 695 yards and six touchdowns this season but announced he's entering the transfer portal. Jordan Lovett steps into that role with 317 yards on the season.

Jacksonville State's Ground Game Is Lethal

Jacksonville State comes in with the second-most rushing yards in all of Division I football this season. Let that sink in. The Gamecocks have piled up 3,354 rushing yards, led by Conference USA Player of the Year Cam Cook, who has been absolutely ridiculous. Cook has rushed for 1,659 yards and 16 touchdowns on 295 carries. And here's the kicker: Jacksonville State has TWO thousand-yard rushers this season. That's rare at any level of football. Under first-year head coach Charles Kelly, this program has maintained the winning culture from their FCS days and is playing in their third straight bowl game since moving up.

The Matchup Problem for Troy

Troy has struggled against the run all season, allowing 192 rushing yards per game, which ranks 115th in the nation. Against a Jacksonville State team that runs the ball better than almost anyone in college football? That's a recipe for trouble. The Gamecocks don't need to do anything fancy. They can line up and pound the rock, control the clock, and keep Troy's offense on the sideline. That's exactly the kind of game plan that works in bowl season when you've had extra weeks to prepare.

What Troy Needs to Win

Quarterback Goose Crowder has thrown for 1,314 yards, 11 touchdowns, and five interceptions. He's not going to light up the scoreboard, but he's capable enough to keep defenses honest. The Trojans need to establish some kind of run game with Lovett and keep Jacksonville State's offense off the field. If this turns into a shootout, Troy has a chance. If it turns into a ball-control game where the Gamecocks just run it down their throats, the Trojans are in trouble.

Historical Context

These two programs have met 63 times historically, making this one of the most-played rivalries that nobody talks about anymore. Troy leads the all-time series, but Jacksonville State was competitive back in the day. The last meeting in 2001 feels like ancient history, but there are still fans who remember when this was a marquee game in Alabama football circles. The Salute to Veterans Bowl in Montgomery brings this rivalry back to life, and the proximity of both schools ensures plenty of fans from both sides will make the trip.

The total of 49.5 feels about right for two programs that play solid defense but have some offensive limitations in the passing game. Jacksonville State's rushing attack is the X-factor here. If they can control the trenches and keep the chains moving, they'll keep this game close or win outright. Troy laying just 1.5 points against a team that can run like that is razor thin.
