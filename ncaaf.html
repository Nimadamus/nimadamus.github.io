Week 11 Friday Night Triple Header November 7

Complete breakdown of Friday night's three game slate featuring Big 12 and American Conference battles

@
Houston Cougars (7-2, 4-2 Big 12) @ UCF Knights (4-4, 1-4 Big 12)
Friday, November 7, 2025 • 8:00 PM ET • FS1
Line: Houston -1.5 | Total: 47.5

Houston Bounces Back After West Virginia Loss

Houston enters Friday night at 7-2 overall and 4-2 in Big 12 play, sitting in 4th place in the conference standings. The Cougars just lost to West Virginia 45-35 in a shootout, which snapped their three game winning streak. Before that loss, Houston had rattled off impressive wins over Arizona State 24-16, Arizona 31-28, and Oklahoma State 39-17. Quarterback Conner Weigman has thrown for 1,890 yards with 16 touchdowns and a 70.0 QBR, which are solid numbers for a Big 12 quarterback. Running back Dean Connors has been productive with 657 yards on 148 carries and 4 touchdowns, giving Houston a balanced offensive attack.

The Cougars average 29.4 points per game, which ranks 62nd nationally, while allowing 21.6 points per game, tied for 38th in the country. Those are both respectable marks that indicate Houston can play defense and score enough to win games. The passing offense ranks 79th nationally at 222.0 yards per game, while the rushing attack is 58th at 161.3 yards per game. Houston is efficient without being explosive, which makes them a tough matchup for teams that can't execute consistently.

UCF Desperate After Blowout Loss to Baylor

UCF comes into this game at 4-4 overall and 1-4 in Big 12 play, sitting 13th in the conference. The Knights just got absolutely demolished by Baylor 30-3 six days ago, which was one of the most embarrassing performances of the season. UCF managed just 3 points and couldn't move the ball at all against Baylor's defense. Before that disaster, UCF had beaten West Virginia 45-13, which looked like a breakthrough moment, but they followed that with losses to Cincinnati 20-11 and Kansas 27-20.

Quarterback Tayven Jackson has thrown for 1,334 yards with 5 touchdowns and a 58.5 QBR, which are mediocre numbers at best. Running back Myles Montgomery has been the bright spot with 572 yards on 112 carries and 3 touchdowns, providing some semblance of a ground game. UCF averages 27.3 points per game, ranking 74th nationally, while allowing 18.8 points per game, which ranks 21st defensively. The Knights can defend when they're locked in, but their offensive inconsistency is killing them.

The Line and Betting Angle

Houston is favored by just 1.5 points on the road, which is essentially a pick'em given how small that spread is. The total is set at 47.5, which suggests a moderate-scoring game. Here's what matters: Houston is the better team with a better record, better quarterback play, and more consistent offensive production. UCF just got embarrassed by Baylor and their confidence has to be shaken. The Knights are 1-4 in Big 12 play, which means they're not competitive in conference games.

Houston should win this game outright. The small spread of 1.5 points suggests the market is respecting UCF's home field advantage at Bounce House Stadium, but the Cougars have proven they can win on the road with victories at Arizona State and Oklahoma State. UCF's offensive struggles are real, and Houston's defense should be able to contain Tayven Jackson and force him into mistakes. The total at 47.5 feels about right, and the under has value if Houston controls tempo and pounds the ball with Dean Connors. This projects as a low to mid 20s type game where Houston wins 27-17 or something similar.

@
Northwestern Wildcats (5-3, 3-2 Big Ten) @ USC Trojans (6-2, 4-1 Big Ten)
Friday, November 7, 2025 • 9:00 PM ET • FOX
Line: USC -13.5 | Total: 51.5

USC's High-Powered Offense Rolls into Week 11

USC enters Friday night at 6-2 overall and 4-1 in Big Ten play, sitting 4th in the conference. The Trojans just beat Nebraska 21-17 in a close game, but before that they had a tough loss at Notre Dame 34-24 where they couldn't keep up with the Irish offense. USC's signature win came against ranked Michigan 31-13, proving they can dominate quality opponents. Quarterback Jayden Maiava has thrown for 2,315 yards with 15 touchdowns and an elite 88.2 QBR, which are excellent numbers that rank him among the top quarterbacks in the conference.

USC averages 39.8 points per game, ranking 8th nationally, which is elite offensive production. The Trojans throw for 302.3 yards per game, ranking 10th nationally, and rush for 203.6 yards per game, ranking 19th. This is a balanced, explosive offense that can beat you through the air or on the ground. Running back Waymond Jordan has 576 rushing yards on 88 carries with 5 touchdowns, while wide receiver Makai Lemon has 776 receiving yards on 50 catches with 6 touchdowns. USC has weapons everywhere and the talent to outscore most teams.

Northwestern Grinds Out Wins with Defense

Northwestern comes into this game at 5-3 overall and 3-2 in Big Ten play, sitting 8th in the conference. The Wildcats just lost to Nebraska 28-21, which was a tough setback after they had won four straight games. Northwestern's strength is their defense, which allows just 16.8 points per game, tied for 13th nationally. The Wildcats beat Purdue 19-0 with a shutout, and pulled off a huge upset at Penn State 22-21, proving they can win in hostile environments.

Quarterback Preston Stone has thrown for 1,372 yards with 10 touchdowns and a 47.6 QBR, which are pedestrian numbers. Running back Caleb Komolafe has been productive with 608 rushing yards on 124 carries and 7 touchdowns, giving Northwestern a ground game to lean on. The Wildcats average just 22.5 points per game, ranking 102nd nationally, which is terrible offensive production. Northwestern ranks 118th nationally in passing offense at 172.4 yards per game, but they're 30th in rushing offense at 187.9 yards per game. This is a run-first team that tries to control clock and limit possessions.

The Spread and Total Analysis

USC is laying 13.5 points at home, which is a massive spread that reflects the talent gap between these teams. The Trojans are explosive and the Wildcats are plodding. USC scores 39.8 points per game while Northwestern scores 22.5. That's a 17-point difference in offensive production, which justifies this spread. The total at 51.5 suggests a moderate-scoring game, but if USC gets rolling early, this could fly over.

Here's the thing: Northwestern's defense is legitimate, ranking 13th nationally in points allowed. The Wildcats can slow down offenses and force them to execute in the red zone. USC is talented enough to score regardless, but Northwestern has proven they can keep games close against better opponents. The Wildcats covered at Penn State and hung around against Nebraska. The question is whether USC blows them out or if Northwestern's defense keeps it within two touchdowns.

The smart play is USC to win, but 13.5 points is a lot to lay at home. Northwestern will run the ball, control clock, and try to keep this game in the 30s. If the Wildcats can execute their game plan and limit USC's possessions, they can cover even in a loss. The total at 51.5 leans under if Northwestern successfully grinds out long drives and keeps USC's offense on the sideline. This projects as USC winning 34-20 or something in that range, where they cover but it's not a blowout.

@
Tulane Green Wave (6-2, 5th AAC) @ Memphis Tigers (8-1, 2nd AAC)
Friday, November 7, 2025 • 9:00 PM ET • ESPN
Line: TBD | Total: TBD

Memphis Tigers Dominating American Conference

Memphis enters Friday night at 8-1 overall and 4-1 in American Conference play, sitting 2nd in the conference standings. The Tigers just demolished Rice 38-14 and have won three straight games by a combined score of 117-52. Memphis beat South Florida 34-31 in a shootout, crushed Tulsa 45-7, and destroyed Florida Atlantic 55-26. This is an offense that's clicking on all cylinders right now. Quarterback Brendon Lewis has thrown for 1,869 yards with 10 touchdowns and added 562 rushing yards with 8 touchdowns on the ground, giving Memphis a dual-threat element that's tough to defend.

The Tigers average 37.7 points per game, ranking 12th nationally, which is elite offensive production. Memphis allows 19.2 points per game, ranking 24th defensively, which means they can play defense when needed. The Tigers rank 73rd in passing offense at 228.7 yards per game but are tied for 21st in rushing offense at 201.1 yards per game. Memphis is balanced and efficient, with wide receiver Cortez Braham Jr. leading the way with 632 receiving yards on 39 catches and 7 touchdowns.

Tulane Fighting for American Conference Position

Tulane comes into this game at 6-2 overall and sitting 5th in the American Athletic Conference. The Green Wave just beat Northwestern 23-3 in a dominant defensive performance, and before that they escaped South Alabama 33-31 in a shootout. Tulane has quality wins over Duke 34-27 and East Carolina 26-19, but they also got destroyed by Ole Miss 45-10 in their only game against a Power 4 opponent. Quarterback Jake Retzlaff has thrown for 1,622 yards with 7 touchdowns, while wide receiver Omari Hayes has 376 receiving yards on 25 catches.

Tulane averages 25.9 points per game, ranking 81st nationally, while allowing 25.5 points per game, ranking 79th defensively. The Green Wave are mediocre on both sides of the ball, which is why they're 6-2 instead of competing for conference titles. Tulane throws for 233.1 yards per game and rushes for 175.1 yards per game, which are both middle-of-the-road numbers. This is a team that can beat mid-tier opponents but struggles against elite competition.

American Conference Battle with Huge Implications

This game matters enormously for both teams' conference championship hopes. Memphis is 8-1 and needs to keep winning to stay in contention, while Tulane at 6-2 needs an upset to prove they belong in the conversation. The line and total weren't available yet, but Memphis should be favored at home given their offensive firepower and home field advantage.

Memphis has the better quarterback in Brendon Lewis, the better offense overall, and the momentum of three straight blowout wins. Tulane's defense held Northwestern to 3 points, but Memphis is a completely different animal offensively. The Tigers can score in multiple ways and have the playmakers to exploit Tulane's mediocre defense. This projects as Memphis winning by double digits, something like 38-24, where they control the game and Tulane hangs around but can't close the gap.

When the line is released, Memphis should be favored by 7 to 10 points at home. If the number is in that range, the Tigers offer value as the more complete team with the better offense and home field advantage. Tulane will compete and make this interesting, but Memphis has too much firepower and should pull away in the second half. The total will likely be in the mid 50s given both teams' offensive capabilities, and the over should be in play if both offenses execute.
