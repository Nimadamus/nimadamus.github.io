Georgia Southern vs App State

Monday, 2:00 PM ET | Protective Stadium, Birmingham | ESPN
Line: Georgia Southern -10 | O/U: 57.5 | GASO: 6-6 | APP: 5-7

The Sun Belt's most heated rivalry gets a bowl game stage in Birmingham. Georgia Southern and Appalachian State have been going at it since App State joined the Sun Belt in 2014, and these games are always physical, emotional affairs. The Eagles come in as 10-point favorites, but don't let that line fool you - this is the kind of game where the spread means nothing and both teams will be leaving everything on the field.

Georgia Southern finished the regular season 6-6 after a rollercoaster year under Clay Helton. The Eagles' triple-option attack remains one of the most unique offenses in college football, averaging 215 rushing yards per game behind a physical offensive line. Quarterback JC French has been solid managing the offense, and running back Jalen White has been a workhorse carrying the ball 180+ times this season. When Georgia Southern controls the clock and keeps the chains moving, they're extremely difficult to stop.

App State's Struggles

The Mountaineers limp into this bowl game at 5-7, which would normally mean they're sitting at home. But with not enough bowl-eligible teams to fill all 43 bowl slots, App State gets a lifeline. It's been a disappointing season in Boone - this program won 10+ games in four of the last six years, and the standard here is much higher than a losing record. Head coach Shawn Clark needs a strong showing to quiet some of the noise around his program.

App State's offense has been inconsistent all year, ranking outside the top 80 nationally in scoring. Quarterback Joey Aguilar has flashed at times but hasn't found the consistency the Mountaineers needed. The run game hasn't been able to carry the load like it has in years past, and the offensive line has struggled against physical fronts - which is exactly what Georgia Southern brings.

Keys to the Game

For Georgia Southern, it's simple: run the ball, control the clock, and don't turn it over. The triple-option is built for games like this where you want to shorten the game and limit possessions. If the Eagles can sustain long drives and keep App State's offense on the sideline, they'll win comfortably. The Mountaineers' defense has been susceptible to the run, allowing over 170 yards per game on the ground - that plays right into Georgia Southern's hands.

App State needs to find a way to create explosive plays and get Georgia Southern out of their rhythm. The Mountaineers have to win the turnover battle and somehow generate negative plays in the backfield to disrupt the option. Easier said than done against a team that's been running this system for years. If this game turns into a slugfest in the trenches, Georgia Southern has the advantage.
