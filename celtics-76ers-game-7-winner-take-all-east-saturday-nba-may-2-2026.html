NBC / Peacock

76ers @ Celtics

Saturday, 7:30 PM ET | TD Garden, Boston, MA

The Philadelphia 76ers visit Boston Saturday night for the only Game 7 on the NBA's Saturday slate, the kind of one-night-decides-everything finale that has defined first-round drama since the round expanded to a best-of-seven format in 2003. Boston is a 7.5-point home favorite with the moneyline at minus-310, the total at 206.5, and Philadelphia at plus-250 on the moneyline. The spread opened at 9.5 on Friday and has been climbing back as the market priced in Joel Embiid's return to full minutes and Philadelphia's back-to-back closing punch in Games 5 and 6. The 7.5 is the structural midpoint between the home-court premium for Boston at TD Garden and the series-specific Sixers strength that produced 16-point and 13-point road wins in the last two games.

The series got here through two distinct halves. Boston blew Philadelphia out by 32 in Game 1 and again by 32 in Game 4, the kind of variance environment that defined the regular-season Celtics' offensive ceiling. The Sixers stole Game 2 in Boston by 14 and split the Philadelphia games to keep the series alive at 3-1. Then Joel Embiid, returning from appendicitis since Game 4, produced a 33-point Game 5 in Boston and a 30-point Game 6 in Philadelphia, the kind of vintage MVP-caliber stretch that flipped the bracket math. Across Games 5 and 6, Boston scored 190 points combined while Philadelphia scored 219, a 29-point swing across two games that brought the series back to TD Garden for a winner-take-all night.

Tyrese Maxey has been the structural piece of Philadelphia's offense across all six games at 26.3 points and 6.5 assists per game. Paul George shot 9-of-18 from three across the last two games as the secondary perimeter scorer, and Embiid's 26-33-19 line across his three return games has anchored the half-court offense. The Sixers' switching perimeter defense produced sub-100 Celtics scoring nights in three of the six games, and Boston's role-player shooting collapsed at the wrong time - Payton Pritchard at 1-of-13 from three across the Boston losses and Derrick White at 25.6 percent from three for the entire series. Boston's response will be the regression-to-the-mean three-point variance and the Tatum two-way superstar profile that produced the Game 4 30-7-11. Boston was 31-13 at home in the regular season with a 120.8 offensive rating that ranked second league-wide, and TD Garden in a Game 7 has been one of the most decisive home venues in modern playoff history. The winner advances to face the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference semifinals starting next week.
