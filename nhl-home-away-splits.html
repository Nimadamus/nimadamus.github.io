Observations

Colorado Home Dominance

Colorado's 23-6-5 home record represents a 67.6% win rate and 75.0% points percentage on home ice, both league highs. Their six home regulation losses are among the fewest in the NHL.

Rangers Home/Road Disparity

The New York Rangers have the largest venue-based split in the league. Their 9-17-7 home record (27.3% win rate) contrasts sharply with a 19-16-2 road record (51.4% win rate). No other team shows a home win rate more than 24 percentage points below their road win rate.

Dallas Road Strength

Dallas's 21-7-7 away record produces one of the highest road win percentages in the NHL at 60.0%. Their 70.0% road points percentage also ranks among the best. Combined with a strong home record (22-9-4), the Stars are one of the league's most consistent teams regardless of venue. Their puck line ATS record shows whether that consistency translates to covering the spread.

Vancouver Home Struggles

Vancouver's 8-23-5 home record (22.2% win rate) is the worst home mark in the league. Their 29.2% home points percentage sits at the bottom of the NHL. They have posted a better record on the road (13-17-3) than at Rogers Arena.

Worst Road Records

Calgary (10-22-3, 28.6% win rate) and Winnipeg (12-16-6, 35.3% win rate) own two of the worst road records in the NHL. Both teams show significantly stronger performance on home ice.
